Long-Baseline news, April 1996

*** LSND doubles oscillation signal, including 1995 data

In a 29 page paper submitted to Physical Review, the LSND collaboration reports their evidence for neutrino oscillations from muon decay at rest to be 22 events with a background of 4.6. If attributed to oscillations, this corresponds to an oscillation probability (averaged over the experimental energy and spatial acceptance) of (0.31 +0.11-0.10+- 0.05)%. Inclusion of the 1995 data doubled the exposure from what had been earlier reported. Analysis of the stopping muon data is still underway.

*** NSF report calls for new reviews

The "Report of the NSF Special Emphasis Panel on Experimental High Energy Physics", called the Cahn report by some, proposes that existing review procedures be supplemented by reviews of full collaborative programs. It has a suggested schedule of review dates, but in language which seems a bit strange to me, it states, "The long-baseline neutrino oscillation experiment at Fermilab would be reviewed once the collaboration's membership is established."

*** SNO milestone (Sudbury Neutrino Observatory)

SNO is being constructed in 4 parts; first the top half of the geodesic structure, then the top half of the acrylic vessel, followed by the bottom half of the acrylic and the bottom half of the geodesic. The upper two pieces were recently completed. The plan to finish construction in Jan. '97, and fill by May. Counters for nc events may deploy in November '97, after many cosmogenics decay, but the schedule will depend on exp. rates.

*** Homestake recalibrated counters

The Argon from the homestake solar neutrino detector has been measured in a number of proportional counters over the years, most but not all of which still exist. About 20 of the 30 counters have been recalibrated using Argon 37. The present result is 2.54 +- 0.14 +- .14 SNU. Using data only from counters which were recalibrated, the answer is 2.54 +- 0.20 SNU.

*** Gallex upgrades proposed

There is a proposal for a Gallium Neutrino Observatory (GNO) at Gran Sasso in 3 stages: GNO30, 1997-1999 would continue GALLEX by purchasing the gallium which is currently in use. It needs to be purchased by the end of 1996. GNO060, 1999-2001 would increase the gallium, probably in chloride form. GN100, 2001-2007 would get more gallium, possibly in metallic form.

*** Superkamiokande

With a flip of the switch, Superkamiokande turned on at 00:01 on April 1, 1996, as promised. During the fill, some dirt had gotten into the water, and the attenuation length is short, but it is growing as the water cleans up. They expect 8400 solar neutrinos per year with E.gt.5MeV. Only 1/3% of the inner tubes died during startup, and 2-3% of outer tubes. Mu rate is 3Hz.

*** Birds in California (San Onofre, now Phoenix)

The San Onofre experiment was finding obstacles in the way of constructing their facility near the reactor. One concern was that the construction would bother the San Onofre gnatcatcher, a bird which nests near the reactor, though there is less concern they are bothered by tanks which rumble by from an army base. The experiment has decided to move to the Palo Verde reactor near Phoenix. There they can build a deeper facility, which will reduce cosmic muon backgrounds, but with 3 reactors, only one of which is off at any given time, it will be a bit harder to compare reactor on versus reactor off data. Oscillation sensitivity remains constant as a result.

*** Bats in Minnesota (MINOS)

As part of the MINOS environmental assessment, Dan Taylor and a team from Bat Conservation International visited Soudan for 3 days to see if MINOS excavation might disturb the bat population. The dominant population (little brown myotis) prefers to hibernate in 40-44 degree F temperatures, which is not near the new MINOS hall. No problems are foreseen. Bat Conservation International is at http://www.batcon.org/
