Long-Baseline news, February 2024

*** Joint Fit between T2K and NOvA

https://indico.fnal.gov/event/62062/ The resulting fit for Delta m^2_32 has the smallest uncertainty compared to previous measurements; 2.429 +0.039-0.035 e^-3 eV^2 NO (normal mass order). Small preference for the IO, whereas each experiment prefers NO, but it reverts to a weak preference for NO adding constraints from Daya Bay. For CP, delta=pi/2 lies outside the 3-sigma interval for both NO and IO. A wide range of delta is allowed for NO, but for IO, CP conservation falls outside 3 sigma.

*** "Neutrinos from Home" 2024 23–24 April & 30 April–1 May

This is an online conference at the boundary between neutrinos and cosmology with a novel format, aiming to bring a real-world workshop experience online. There'll be pre-recorded talks, & both asynchronous & scheduled live discussions. A discussion space also facilitates ongoing and face-to-face discussions. Abstract submission will close on 27 March. Decisions will be made on a rolling basis and the total number of talks will be limited. https://neutrinos.cosmodiscussion.com/

*** NuSTEC Workshop: NuInt primer 11-13 April in São Paulo

A NuSTEC workshop that will take place immediately before NuInt24 at the same location. The workshop is mainly aimed at students and junior postdocs and will provide an overview of the current status of nu-nucleus scattering, highlighting both experimental and theoretical aspects of the field. The program will include lectures on nuclear models and nuclear effects in scattering, the connection between interaction studies and oscillation physics, and cross section measurements. Special attention will be paid to the use of generators, including sessions with hands-on exercises. https://indico.fnal.gov/event/59963/page/3440-satellite-event

*** NuINT 2024 15-20 April in São Paulo, Brazil.

NUINT 24 is the 14th in a series of workshops concentrating on neutrino-nucleus interactions. The goal of the whole series is to bring theorists & experimentalists from the nuclear & high-energy community together to address the many challenges of understanding these complex interactions. https://indico.fnal.gov/event/59963/

*** INSS 2024 3-14 Jun 2024 in Bologna

The 15th International Neutrino Summer School brings together grad students and postdocs, along with some of the best teachers and researchers in neutrino physics, to create a 2-week-long learning experience in an open and interactive environment that covers the full range of modern neutrino physics. It will be right before Neutrino 2024 in Milan. https://agenda.infn.it/event/39075/

*** Neutrino 2024 16-22 June in Milano

The XXXI International Conference on Neutrino Physics and Astrophysics will serve as a platform for the exchange of the latest results, innovative ideas and future perspectives in the fields of neutrino physics, astrophysics and cosmology. https://neutrino2024.org/ Registration is open

*** NPML 25-28 June in Zurich

The Neutrino Physics and Machine Learning (NPML 2024) Workshop, where cutting-edge research at the intersection of AI/ML and Neutrino Physics will take center stage. https://indico.phys.ethz.ch/event/113/

*** NuFACT 2024 16-21 September at Argonne in Illinois

The 25th International Workshop on Neutrinos from Accelerators aims to review the progress of current and future facilities able to improve on measurements of the properties of neutral & charged lepton flavor violation, and to search for new phenomena beyond the capabilities of presently planned experiments. Registration should open in mid April. https://indico.fnal.gov/event/63406/

*** SBND cryostat has been filled with liquid Argon

During February and through 5 March, the Short-Baseline Near Detector has been filled with liquid Argon. Tests of the circulation system are taking place. Note to those who leave out the hyphen, if the detector was short, the argon would spill.

*** DUNE will install Vertical Drift module first

(From FNAL Director highlights 26 Feb) The Far Detector/Cryogenics subproject is implementing in its schedule the DUNE Collaboration Executive Board decision to proceed with the installation of the Vertical Drift far detector (FD2) first in South Dakota, following handover of the underground detector cavern. This comes after an assessment by a task force from UK, France, Italy, US and CERN.
