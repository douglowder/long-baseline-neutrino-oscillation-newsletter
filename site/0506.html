Long-Baseline news, June 2005

*** MEYERS and BEIER ENQUIRE; NUSAG questions

The panel advising U.S. agencies on new neutrino programs asked the proponents of the new projects several questions. Reactor questions were due 6/30; 0nu-betabeta earlier, accelerator later. NuSAG has made available a public page with the May presentations: http://www.hep.net/nusag_pub/May2005talks.html

*** U.S. House ADMIRES neutrinos, Senate doesn't CONSPIRE

The House Appropriations committee added $22M to the President's DOE Science budget of $714M, with $11M "provided for upgrades to the neutrino research program". The Senate bill was for $717M with no mention of neutrinos. These things get worked out later.

*** ENTIRE universe seen by Auger with North proposal for Colorado

The Auger array is nearing completion and will hold a celebration 9-11 November 2005 in Malargue Argentina. Meanwhile, they chose a site in Southeastern Colorado for a Northern Hemisphere proposal. http://www.interactions.org/cms/?pid=1020465 ; http://www.rockymountainnews.com/drmn/state/article/0,1299,DRMN_21_3838724,00.html

*** Louis RETIRES "Old Red"; misses frequent FLYER miles

Louis Barrett, Western Washington Physicist who has been driving to Minnesota for Soudan 2 and MINOS experiments for several years, told people last year that he was going to "retire" his car after reaching 1/2 million miles, which he did at the end of last summer. Several were surprised this year to see him in the same '87 Prelude with 552,000 miles. It had new tires.

*** Fermilab Neutrino Department PERSPIRES

A hot week at Fermilab saw the establishment of the new neutrino department, led by Gina Rameika. It oversees four experiments - MINOS, MiniBooNE, MINERvA and NOvA. Fermilab's recently HIRED new DIRECTOR Pier Oddone spent his 1st day in Sweden. (Pier doesn't rhyme with plier.)

*** MIPP ACQUIRES NuMI target data

MIPP has started taking beam on the NuMI target. Particle production measured in MIPP may aid tuning nu Monte Carlos for MINOS. http://ppd.fnal.gov/experiments/e907/e907.htm

*** Kayser Neutrino lecture notes

As many competent particle physicists move from collider and B physics to neutrinos, they need a short but clear overview of the current status of neutrino physics. The writeup of neutrino lectures at the SLAC summer school in hep-ph/0506165 is such.
