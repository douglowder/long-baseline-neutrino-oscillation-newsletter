Long-Baseline news, March 2000

*** Neutrino Factory Study looks at long-baseline

Draft reports from the Physics group and the Design study group for a Neutrino Factory at Fermilab have been written, and can be viewed from http://www.fnal.gov/projects/muon_collider/index.html . Sections of the physics document describe the beam properties, oscillation physics, and non-oscillation physics. It is the nue component in the nu-factory that drives the oscillation physics program. A nue beam would a)enable basic tests of the oscillation framework, b) search for nue to nutau, c) determine the pattern of nu masses, d) check the MSW effect, e) measure or limit all mixing angles and f) measure or limit CP violation.

*** The sign of delta m^2

Delta m^2 = m1^2 - m2^2 could be positive or negative. Most oscillation experiments can't tell, but matter effects depend on the sign. Barger et al (below) explore the parameters and exper- iments needed at a nu factory to sort out the mass hierarchy.

*** The sine of delta m^2

Early results from K2K seem to show a big effect. They would get the biggest effect for delta m^2 near 8 10^-3 eV^2, but statistical errors cannot presently pin down delta m^2 well. On the low side, the size of the effect falls off as (del m^2)^2.

*** The sighing of delta m^2

The Kamiokande and Super-K "allowed" regions for neutrino parameters do not seem to overlap much in delta m^2. Many people find this curious, but a large amount of physicist intuition is based on the behavior of gaussian distributions. If atmo del m^2 fits were Gaussian, it would be curious. But atmospheric fits come from L/E distributions which are quite non-Gaussian.

*** MINOS scintillator contract

This will go to Itasca, and is somewhat below the baseline cost. Recent scintillator output looks good and light output exceeds the "standard candle" which was early Quick Plastics samples.

*** June 12 Meeting on Next Generation Underground Facility (WIPP)

The US Department of Energy (DOE) operates the Waste Isolation Pilot Plant (WIPP) near Carlsbad, New Mexico as the nation's first deep geologic repository for permanent disposal of defense-related transuranic waste. With an estimated 35-year operating lifetime and with very low primordial background radiation, WIPP may be used for experiments that require shielding from cosmic rays greater than about 2000 m.w.e. A Carlsbad workshop June 12-14 will consider possibilities. See http://www.wipp.carlsbad.nm.us/leptontown.
