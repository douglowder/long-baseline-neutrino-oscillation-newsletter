T2K is reporting seeing 3 e-like events in their anti-neutrino run on a background of 1.8 (the 1.8 includes 0.6 events from the wrong sign appearance channel). http://t2k-experiment.org/2015/07/first-look-at-antineutrino-appearance-from-t2k/

*** NOvA data coming soon

This off-axis long-baseline neutrino experiment from Fermilab to Northern Minnesota has gobs of data. An analysis of most of that data will be presented at a "wine and cheese" seminar at Fermilab on 6 August and at the DPF meeting in Ann Arbor the next day. It should be able to say whether or not it is seeing electron neutrino appearance, sensitive to theta_13, and muon neutrino disappearance, sensitive to theta_23. It will keep running as the NuMI power increases, and in a few years with gobs and gobs more data it can say something about the mass ordering if lucky.

*** MicroBooNE has "first light"

MicroBooNE is a large Liquid Ar Time Projection Chamber (LArTPC) neutrino experiment located along the Booster neutrino beam line at Fermilab. The experiment will measure low energy neutrino cross sections and investigate the low energy excess events observed by the MiniBooNE experiment. On 9 July, it reached a major milestone when filling of the LArTPC was completed, which required 9 tanker trucks and ~34,000 gallons of high purity liquid Ar. Commissioning of the detector is currently underway.

*** Ordering Neutrino parameters, NO and IO

We label the neutrino mass states according to the size of the nue i.e. by definition |U_e1|^2 > |U_e2|^2> |U_e3|^2. Nu_1 and nu_2 have a mass squared splitting equal to the ~7.6 10^-5 eV^2 and the order of these 2 was determined by the SNO experiment, m_2 > m_1. The mass eigenstate nu_3 has mass^2 splitting of ~2.5 10^-3 eV^2 from nu_1 and n_2 but whether it's mass is greater or smaller than m_1 and m_2 is undetermined. So if we order the masses from lightest to heaviest we have 2 possible orderings: m_1 < m_2 < m_3 (normal ordering NO) or m_3 < m_1 < m_2 (inverted ordering IO). Therefore this ordering question is important whether the masses are hierarchical or quasi-degenerate or anything in between. Mass ordering is the preferred terminology over mass hierarchy so as to avoid confusion with the another important question of whether the mass spectrum is hierarchical or quasi-degenerate.
