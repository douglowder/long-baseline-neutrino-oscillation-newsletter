Long-Baseline news, April 2024

*** ProtoDUNE NP04 argon filling started and completed

https://home.cern/news/news/experiments/protodunes-argon-filling-underway For 1 of the 2 prototype DUNE detectors, one with horizontal drift and one with vertical drift, filling was completed 2 May.

*** Minerva spokesperson

MINERvA has elected Rik Gran from the University of Minnesota-Duluth as its 5th co-spokesperson for the MINERvA experiment. The collaboration is grateful to Laura Fields from Notre Dame for her outstanding leadership during her seven years as spokesperson.

*** Status of JSNS2/JSNS2-II

https://j-parc.jp/c/en/topics/2024/04/19001324.html#NU This collaboration searches for short-distance neutrino oscillations by sterile neutrinos at J-PARC MLF. For JSNS2, the 4th long physics run was performed from Dec-2023 to Mar-2024 using a single near detector. 7.22e21 POT were accumulated for a total of 3.95e22 POT during 2020-2024 corresponding to 35% of approved POT (to 22 Mar 2024). This run continues to June 2024. The new far detector for JSNS2-II is under construction. The acrylic tank was installed on 14 Feb 2024. Three quarter of the veto PMTs (36 PMTs) will be installed, and the liquid scintillator will be filled within a few months. JSNS2-II aims to start the data taking soon.

*** Hyper-Kamiokande construction progress

The construction at the Kamioka mine is rapidly progressing. Last October, the ceiling part of the cavern, 69 m diameter & 21 m high was completed, and mining work will be completed within CY2024. A Program Advisory Committee was organized in March.

*** Boris Jules Kayser, 1938-2024

Boris Jules Kayser was a theoretical physicist, specializing in the study of neutrinos. He worked at the NSF for nearly 30 years before joining Fermilab. He retired from Fermilab in 2012. For 5 years, he was the editor of the peer-reviewed journal the Annual Review of Nuclear and Particle Science. He was a very commonly used theorist for a clear talk about neutrino properties, and an often-used fact checker for this newsletter.

*** DESI 2024 data and neutrino mass in arXiv:2404.03002

For the flat ΛCDM model with the sum of neutrino Sum(m_nu) free, combining the DESI and CMB data yields an upper limit Sum(m_nu) < 0.072 (0.113) eV at 95% CL for a Sum > 0 (Sum > 0.059) eV prior. I don't know about you, but my prior would be > 0.059 eV.

*** INFN school on Underground Physics (SoUP) 14-18 October

https://soup2024.ca.infn.it/ The 3rd school will be in the castle of Bertinoro, Italy. SoUP is an advanced school for PhD students, post-docs and young researchers willing to gain deeper knowledge in topics related to underground physics. It includes lectures and hands-on sessions on Physics Phenomenology, Detection Techniques & Instrumentation Technologies. Videos of lectures will be available on the SoUP Youtube Channel.

*** DUNE concreting and a video on DUNE excavation

The concreting activities for the DUNE North and South Caverns are 100% and 60% complete, respectively. The completion date for all concrete activities is early May 2024. See a presentation on the "colossal caverns": https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CGOzNGFXYFM

*** NuMI horn repaired

https://inside.fnal.gov/2024/04/raising-the-dead-repairing-numi-focusing-horn-ph2-02/ A NuMI horn that was removed for a water leak in 2020 has been repaired as a spare. To minimize beam down time, the NuMI team builds spare devices in anticipation of possible replacements. But after there was a horn stripline crack in December 2022, there was no horn 2 replacement readily available.
