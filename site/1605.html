Long-Baseline news, May 2016

*** ICFA neutrino panel report

http://icfa.fnal.gov/panels/neutrino_panel/ A Roadmap for the international accelerator-based neutrino program has been prepared by the neutrino panel of the International Committee on Future Accelerators. They provide 16 recommendations including that both DUNE and Hyper-K be pursued through established approval and funding processes. They have identified two "decision-points", at ~2020, to decide the future sterile and nu-scattering programs.

*** New result - Double Chooz has a high value of theta13

At Moriond Double Chooz presented its first theta13 measurement exploiting the combination of 2 years of single-detector data (since 2011) and 9 months of double-detector data (since Jan 15). The measured value for sin^2(2theta13) is (0.111+/-0.018), which is about 35% larger (1.6 sigma) than the most precise Daya Bay (0.082+/-0.004). https://indico.in2p3.fr/event/12279/

*** Lariat progress and prospects

LArIAT (Liquid Argon TPC In A Testbeam)doesn't observe nus, but is useful for argon experiments. The collaboration has recently reported preliminary results on the first total pion-Ar cross section measurement. LArIAT is designed for calibrating detector response in a charged particle beam in the energy range relevant to both Short-(microBooNE, SBND, ICARUS) and Long-Baseline(DUNE).

*** CAPTAIN MINERvA prospects at Fermilab.

CAPTAIN MINERvA plans to measure neutrino cross-sections and event signatures on argon in the resonance production and DIS cross-over regions with high statistics and good hadronic containment. After receiving stage one approval last summer, the team was planning to seek stage two approval at this month's FNAL PAC meeting. The FNAL director recently indicated he will not accept a proposal from CAPTAIN MINERvA due to a lack of funding model. The collaboration has petitioned to allow them to seek funds and return for stage 2 approval.

*** Three million Prospects for PROSPECT

http://www.eurekalert.org/pub_releases/2016-06/dnl-pes060116.php The Precision Oscillation and Spectrum Experiment PROSPECT will study the spectrum of reactor neutrinos 10 m from a core at the Oak Ridge High Flux Isotope Reactor. It was awarded $3M by DOE to determine if the difference in reactor measured/predicted rates has an energy dependence indicative of sterile nu models.

*** ANNIE Phase I Running, Funded for Year 1

The Accelerator Neutrino Neutron Interaction Experiment (ANNIE) has been awarded year 1 funding from the DOE through its Intermediate Neutrino Program. ANNIE is a new effort underway in the SciBooNE hall at Fermilab. It is designed to study the production of final state neutrons from neutrino nucleus interactions. ANNIE will also serve as the first implementation of Large Area Picosecond Photodetectors (LAPPDs) in a neutrino beam. Phase I of ANNIE, a measurement of beam induced background neutrons, is now installed in the hall and taking data.

** NOvA returns to power with recycler; needs to replace target

NuMI was able to return at full intensity three weeks after the transformer fire reported last month. After exceeding its expected lifetime, the NOvA target MET-01 has a probable helium leak. The graphite target core would oxidize in oxygen at high temperature sometime during its lifetime, so the target is kept in a helium atmosphere. This target will be replaced during the summer shutdown. Before the shutdown, the goal is to demonstrate 700 kW operation and then run three weeks with antineutrinos (~0.4e20 pot) by switching the horn polarity.
