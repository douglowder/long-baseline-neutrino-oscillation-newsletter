*** Reactor Theta-13 White Paper

An International Working Group has prepared a "White Paper" on the use of a nuclear reactor to measure theta_13. The 156 page document, prepared by 125 authors is available at http://www.neutrinooscillation.org/white.html Expect to see proposals within about a year.

*** MiniBooNE results in about a year

MiniBooNE has a committment of 5 10^20 protons on target, and have gotten 1.7 so far, with 2.5 10^19 in Jan 04. They expect significant improvements from a collimator system installed during the shutdown which will be deployed soon. The flux times cross section is 50% higher than a Monte Carlo. They'll open their "box" in their blind analysis in about a year.

*** Fermilab long range plan - proton driver 

Fermilab's planning process has identified the proton driver as a significant part of its future. It has a varied physics program, but the primary motivation is the long-baseline neutrino program. A project team led by Bill Foster will 
study technologies and project costs over the coming year in anticipation of a real proposal to follow. In parallel, Eric Prebys is leading an effort to identify short term improvments to the existing linac and booster aimed at maximizing the protons available to Run II, MiniBoone, and MINOS over the next several years.

*** Underground Lab site independent support group

A support/interest group for a US Underground Science and Engineering Laboratory was very recent started. This is a "nondenominational" effort, not connected with any site. The web page will endeavor to keep the community informed about all activities:

http://mocha.phys.washington.edu/NUSEL/ At this site you will find an electronic form. Fill it out to join the group and email list. The email list will be kept private, but will be available to any community member who has news about underground science or site developments.

*** Underground Lab Agreement for Homestake - is it enough?

According to articles January 13 and 14 in the "Rapid City Journal", S.D. Governor Mike Rounds signed a 6p agreement with Barrick Gold Corporation which details how the state would protect Barrick from liability for any donation of Homestake mine for an underground science lab. First there is a proposal for $3.5M for liability insurance, and $10M for initial construction, followed by a request to the NSF to guarantee bonds over the first 5 years of the lab. He is now planning to support a Lab with an estimated price tag of $114M (previously $150M).

*** Underground Lab Letters of Interest - SNOLAB

Here is a link to a request for Letters-of-Interest in staging experiments at SNOLAB, a facility for underground science presently under development at INCO's Creighton Mine in Sudbury Ontario, Canada. The request is accompanied by some guidelines for drafting your LOI. Also included is a descriptive on the status of SNOLAB development and the process to develop a scientific program at the new laboratory. http://www.neutrinooscillation.org/snolab.pdf
