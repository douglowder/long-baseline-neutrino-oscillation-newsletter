Long-Baseline news, March 2018

*** Stefan Soldner-Rembold elected DUNE cospokesperson

Stefan Soldner-Rembold, professor of particle physics at the University of Manchester, was elected to a two-year term as co-spokesperson of DUNE. He serves with Ed Blucher from the University of Chicago. Former spokesperson Mark Thomson will now serve as the Executive Chair of the Science and Technology Facilities Council, an important office in UK Science. https://www.symmetrymagazine.org/article/dune-collaboration-elects-new-co-spokesperson

https://stfc.ukri.org/news/new-stfc-executive-chair-announced/

*** Patricia Vahle elected NOvA cospokesperson

"Tricia" Vahle, professor of physics from the College of William and Mary, was elected to a two-year term as spokesperson of the NOvA experiment. She serves with Peter Shanahan from Fermilab. Former co-spokesperson Mark Messier from Indiana University has been writing meeting agendas as cospokesperson since 2006.

*** Rigged election disrespects neutrinos

https://www.symmetrymagazine.org/article/subatomic-smackdown An alleged "subatomic smackdown" had voters choose their favorite particle between the photon, proton, electron and neutron. This may have made sense in the 1950's. When I was in school first thinking about particle physics, I'm sure I would have assumed a fundamental particle is absolutely stable. Our standard model has 12 "particles" and an array of force carriers. But it is intuitively obvious to the most casual observer, that neutrinos are the most interesting on any list and should have been on the ballot. By the way, the photon "won". https://www.symmetrymagazine.org/article/photon-declared-champion

*** Lia Merminga selected to lead Pip II

The new project director of Proton Improvement Plan II, Lia Merminga has worked at SLAC, Jefferson Lab and TRIUMPH. PIP II will provide high intensity protons to the DUNE long-baseline neutrino experiment in South Dakota.

*** Don't walk on the floor with your SOX

Dark Matter DM direct detection experiments look for recoils of nuclei or electrons due to interactions with dark matter particles. But neutrino interactions from solar and other neutrinos provide a background. In the usual plot of dark matter cross section versus mass, this provides a "floor" below which it will be difficult to extract a DM signal. In arXiv:1803.08146 , Wyenberg & Shoemaker map that floor for DM-e experiments.

*** INFN cancels SOX experiment

The SOX (Short-distance nu Oscillations with BoreXino) project was canceled due to the impossibility of obtaining the required source. http://home.infn.it/en/media-outreach/press-releases/ The plan had been to obtain a Cerium source from Russia and look for oscillations in Borexino with a wavelength possibly suggested by the short-baseline anomalies. But the Russian source provider, Mayak Production Association, reported that it will not be able to deliver the source, and INFN reluctantly canceled the project.

*** Isotope cloud due to SOX?

Science magazine reported speculation that a low-level isotope cloud of ruthenium-106 may have been associated with the attempt to make the source for SOX. But the Russian company denies the report. See http://science.sciencemag.org/content/359/6377/729

*** Pontecorvo prize for Fogli and Lisi

Gianluigi Fogli and Eligio Lisi (from INFN, Bari) have won the 2017 Pontecorvo Prize for their work on global fits to neutrino oscillation data. Global fits have, among other things, been responsible for early indications of the large mixing angle solution to the solar neutrino problem, and a non-zero value for theta_13. http://www.jinr.ru/posts/pontecorvo-prize-for-2017/
