Long-Baseline news, August 1999

*** CERN proposals

Several Neutrino proposals have been turned in, including ICANOE, I216 and OPERA. (below) They will be discussed at a CERN meeting during September and decisions are expected in December.

*** K2K Horn failure

When running at maximum current (250kA) the KEK horn broke: the flexible twisted wire connection between horn and stripline (replaced with solid copper strips); the stainless steel water cooling manifold (replaced temporarily with rubber hose); the horn inner conductor piece which doubles as the target (will be replaced with a larger stronger piece. The end wall is also being redesigned to be stiffer.) A spare horn will run at reduced current until the redesigned horn is ready in a few months.

*** KEK neutrino beam workshop

Besides reports on KEK's neutrino success and hardware problems, there was discussion on horns for NGS at CERN and NuMI at FNAL which are under development. CERN is in the process of building a prototype horn for NGS, which will have a welded inner conductor. The design current is 150 kA, but the horns are still 6 m long. NUMI is building a prototype horn, to be ready next winter. (I=200 kA). NUMI horns are about half the size of CERN horns. Next nu beam workshop at FNAL sometime next year.

*** Confidence

Lincoln Wolfenstein is confident in Super-K results, but not in their confidence levels. John Ellis gives personal % confidence rates as follows: Nus mass(99), < 1 eV(90); Supersymmetry(80), LSP most of CDM (60); Lambda not 0 (90), Omega-Lambda big (50).

*** MINOS in budget which hasn't passed

$22M for the NuMI beam is in both the House and Senate Bills for FY00. When delivered, $41.8 of 76.2 TEC will have been appropriated. MINOS and Soudan funds are not line item but are "Other Project Costs". The budget plan which Fermilab says it will try hard to achieve shows $13.5M in FY00. This would represent $20.3M out of $59.9 OPC funds.

*** MINOS extraction

MINOS has been asked to consider switching from fast resonant extraction (1 millisecond) to Single Turn Extraction (10 microseconds). This could cause a neutrino rate problem in the near detector. Faster electronics is being designed. There would be savings in shielding around the extraction septa.

*** Neutrinos, Hilary Clinton love New York from Cornell to BNL

A recent meeting at the NSF focused on how the NSF and Cornell might get involved in a muon-collider/neutrino factory. NSF might have an interest in playing a major (~500M$) role in the neutrino factory. It could be located at Brookhaven.

*** NNN Program; (None of the N's are for New York.)

The program for the September 23-25 Stonybrook Workshop on the Next Generation Nucleon Decay and Neutrino Detector at Stonybrook is set. See http://superk.physics.sunysb.edu/NNN99 .

*** High Altitude Muons from balloons check nu fluxes.

HEAT has 10665 events, MASS has about 3000 and Caprice 4627. There are discrepancies with calculations at high altitude, but 3D calculations (in progress) reduce some discrepancies. Mu+/Mu- ratios vary from 1.2 to 1.6 but may depend on latitude (geomagnetic cutoff). Changes in the mu flux are more sensitive to many effects than the nu flux because neutrino detectors integrate over the height in the atmosphere.

*** Neutrino Telescopes; AMANDA, NESTOR, NEMO, ANTARES, BAIKAL

AMANDA has 16 atmospheric nu events in 85 days. The background is pretty small, but they need more Monte Carlo to be sure. NESTOR will be in the Ionian Sea, 3800m deep, 30km to counthouse. The cable installation has been delayed but should be in place with 12 modules in 1999. They hope for data in 2001. NEMO is a new nu telescope idea considering sites in the Ionian and Tyrrhenian Seas for km^3 arrays, 3300-3550 m deep. ANTARES near Marseilles will deploy a prototype in September '99 and finalize conceptual design next month. They need to position the array not too close to two unexploded bombs they found. Baikal has 196 tubes deployed and continues to take data.
