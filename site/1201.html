Long-Baseline news, January 2012

*** LBNE will design a liquid Argon far detector, not water

The process for a technology decision in the LBNE project and collaboration is now complete, and will be a liquid Argon TPC at the 4850 level of Homestake. This decision was the culmination of a long process including a CDR review, which concluded that both detector designs were sound and could be built and perform as required; the Science Capabilities Review, which concluded that both detectors "would make world-leading measurements relevant to all of the major science goals," but which unanimously concluded that the capabilities of the LAr detector exceeded those of the WCD; and the recommendation from the EC in favor of the WCD. The Project Manager Jim Strait, considered all the input and chose liquid Argon. This has now been agreed to by all parties. The U.S. Congress has asked for a report on LBNE progress by 1 April. To meet this, Office of Science Director Brinkman should decide no later than March whether to proceed at Homestake.

*** Theorists send support letter for a large detector

A group of particle theorists, wrote a letter to Dr. Brinkman to emphasize the urgent physics need for having a large underground detector in the US, linked to a long-baseline intense neutrino beam as would be available from Fermilab. http://www.neutrinooscillation.org/letter2012.pdf

*** Extension of Main Injector run to 30 April, 2012

The Fermilab Main Injector running has been extended from March 1 through 30 April, 2012. This will allow additional running for Minerva, and also the possibility that MINOS can install better clocks, fibers, GPS devices for an improved neutrino velocity measurement. MINOS prefers an HE beam, to get high statistics at the far detector for the v measurement. Minerva prefers the LE beam for its program to measure low energy neutrino interactions.

*** Pontecorvo award 2012 to Stan Wojcicki from Stanford

Wojcicki won the 2012 Pontecorvo prize. This prestigious prize was instituted in the memory of Bruno Pontecorvo in 1995. Awarded annually to an individual scientist, it recognizes "the most significant investigations in elementary particle physics", as acknowledged by the international scientific community. The prize will be announced by the JINR council on 17 February.

*** Double Chooz access issues

The Double Chooz experiment access is through a tunnel in which some rocks have fallen, temporarily affecting the ventilation. The experiment continues to take data, but some activities such as calibration have been postponed until remediation efforts finish.

*** Daya Bay progress

Two near detectors have been operating for several months, and at least 3 of the 4 far detectors are installed. All 8 detectors should be operating this summer. Results comparing the response of the two near detectors might be expected soon, and will confirm control of the systematic errors. Six reactors are now operating, 2 Daya Bay, 4 Ling Ao; (unit 4 started Aug 2011). Units 5 and 6 of Ling Ao phase III are under construction, due in 2016 (correction: I am told Ling Ao III no longer exists).

*** Superluminal workshop

A January workshop on Superluminal neutrinos at APC was mostly attended by theorists and a few experimentalists. There were many references to the OPERA result and the MINOS 2007 paper. Talks were mostly hypothetical stuff. Exotic possibilities are being considered: nu speed unequal to antinu speed; speed depends on direction; diurnal effect; Northgoing nu's(MINOS) subluminal, Southgoing nu's (OPERA) superluminal, EWgoing nu's (T2K) null. www.apc.univ-paris7.fr/APC/Conferences/APC_Meeting_on_Superluminal_Neutrinos/

*** Two neutrino double beta decay seen in EXO-200

In arXiv:1108.4193 , EXO-200 reports 2-nu double-beta decay in Xe-136 at 2.11+-.21e21 y, a second order Standard Model process not seen before. It is now confirmed by KamLAND-Zen in arXiv:1201.4664 2.38+-.14e21 y. Two previous lower limits by others seem wrong.
