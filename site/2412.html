Long-Baseline news, December 2024

*** No Main Injector running at Fermilab in FY2025 for NOvA

https://inside.fnal.gov/2024/12/main-injector-experiments-in-2025 A message from Chief Research Officer Bonnie Fleming mentioned the following: After an August fire at the Kautz Road substation and assessments of the transformers at Fermilab, it was determined that repair of 5 of the 8 transformers is required. Also the Main Injector Heat Exchanger system is in need of repair. Fermilab has announced that due to the challenges with the transformers and the heat exchangers, they are unable to provide beam for Main Injector programs in FY25, including the NuMI and SY120 programs. There will be beam in the Booster for the short-baseline nu program using SBND and ICARUS. The Booster Neutrino Beamline started on 10 December. This FY25 accelerator run will continue to July.

*** IMB site chosen as an APS historic site

In 2024, the Morton Salt Mine, the home of the IMB nucleon decay experiment was chosen as an historic site by the American Physical Society. At a ceremony in August, a plaque recognized "The Birth of Neutrino Astronomy" through the simultaneous measurement of neutrinos in the IMB detector in Painesville Ohio as well as the Kamiokande experiment in the Kamioka mine in Japan. https://www.aps.org/funding-recognition/historic-sites

*** JUNO is nearing completion

https://www.sciencenews.org/article/neutrino-detector-juno-experiment This experiment, using reactor neutrinos to determine the mass ordering of the three neutrinos (normal or inverted) plans to start taking data in the summer of 2025. The sphere will hold 20 kT of liquid scintillator, and be surrounded by a cylindrical pit of water. Filling of the water pit started 18 December.

*** Sunil K. Gupta elected President-Designate of IUPAP

I met Sunil working on a neutrino experiment at Fermilab. His 3- year term started 1 Jan. 2025. He is the 2nd Indian to be elected after Homi J. Bhabha, who served 1960–63. IUPAP, the International Union of Pure and Applied Physics, is an non-governmental international organization, founded in 1922 with a mission to assist in the worldwide development of physics, foster cooperation and help in applying physics toward solving problems of concern to humanity. https://iupap.org/

*** Neutrino-Nucleus worksop Mainz, Germany, 12-23 May

"Neutrino–Nucleus Interactions in the Standard Model and Beyond," will be a 2-week program at MITP in Mainz that will focus on advancing research in nu–nucleus interactions. The 1st week covers theoretical discussions and collaborations; the 2nd week features a workshop on nuclear modeling, cross section predictions, & their implications for precision measurements. Applications due 24 Jan. https://indico.mitp.uni-mainz.de/event/399/ *** Notable New References = Click last line for links
