Long-Baseline news, March 2010

*** Did NuMI switch back to Neutrinos from Anti-nu?

The NuMI beam for MINOS and Minerva ran with the horns set to focus negative particles from October 2009 through Mar 22 2010. 1.8e20 protons on target (POT) were delivered, to better study antineutrinos in the MINOS near and far detectors. Analysis is proceeding on the measurement of oscillation parameters with anti-nus, led by Donna Naples, Jeff Hartnell and Justin Evans.

*** Did Minerva finish installation?

The Fermilab Minerva cross section experiment finished installing its 120 track plus nuclear target modules in mid March. They expect to have a water target in May and a liquid Helium target by September. Their run plan is for 4e20 POT in the "low energy" beam, and 12e20 POT during the NOvA era. The fiducial volume is 3 ton CH,0.2t He, 0.15t C, 0.7t Fe and 0.85t Pb.

*** What does MINOS say about theta13?

MINOS previously published an analysis of electron nu appearance in the NuMI beam using 3.1e20 POT in arXiv:0909.4996. On 9 April, Lisa Whitehead will give a seminar on the analysis of 7e20POT at BNL; Gregory Pawloski at SLAC and Ryan Patterson at FNAL. On 12 April, Anna Holin will talk at CERN.

*** Does Borexino see Geo-neutrinos?

http://www.physorg.com/news187946006.html & arXiv:1003.0284 Geo-neutrinos come from beta decays of natural isotopes in the earth. I call them non-cosmic rays. With 0.25 kton-yr, Borexino sees 9.9 +4.1 -3.4 events, or an observation at 99.997%CL. The rate is 3.9 events/100ton-yr. 3TW core geo-reactor rejected @95%.

*** What is the RENO experiment?

RENO (REactor Neutrino Oscillations) is 1 of 3 reactor anti-nu disappearance experiments which hopes to take data in 2010 and increase the world's sensitivity to theta-13. It uses six reactors at Yonggwang South Korea located 1557, 1456, 1396, 1381, 1414 and 1490 meters from its far detector. A detailed Proposal and Technical Design Report is now available at arXiv:1003.1391.

*** More Questions; SPC report released for CERN/Europe

https://espace.cern.ch/EuCARD/WP3/NEU212/Documents/spc-e-940_c-2894Neutrinos.doc.pdf The Report of the SPC Neutrino Panel was presented to the CERN Council in March. The Council had asked questions, such as, "What is the view of the SPC on the importance of precise measurements of the neutrino oscillation parameters, in particular the CP violating phase and mass hierarchy?", "What is the view of the SPC on the overall value of the International Design Study for the future of (a neutrino factory)? Should CERN take a more active role in enabling the study to reach its goals, irrespective of where such a facility would be sited?" "What other high intensity neutrino facilities are technically possible and how would they address the measurements above?", "What should be the involvement of CERN in studies of those facilities, in particular with regard to the planned LHC upgrades?", "What is the view of the SPC on the merit of a European strategy in this phase of nu experimentation and whether it should have a place on the future CERN road map?"

*** Some Answers in SPC report for CERN/Europe

Answers in context are in the report, but here's some text: "After intensively studying hadron flavor physics for many decades, a comparable effort to study lepton flavor physics and determine all the nu oscillation parameters as precisely as possible is the logical next step." "It appears today very likely that without a nu factory several questions will remain unanswered." "A CERN- based program in direct competition with the Japanese and American ones would be of interest only if it can be timely or demonstrably superior in performance. Otherwise, participation by European(s) in those programs could be usefully supported at CERN." "No global strategy exists concerning accelerator-based nu facilities."

*** Some Recommendations in SPC report for CERN/Europe

  • Stronger CERN links with the European neutrino community.
  • Neutrino groups in PH and AB departments, if possible.
  • Regular workshops.
  • Support for costing superbeam, beta beam, nu factory options.
  • Development at CERN of a high power target facility.
  • Finish MICE & show feasibility to produce 18Ne.
  • Test beams for Detector R&D.
  • High Intensity nu source in Europe unrealistic before 2020.
  • Closely follow superbeams in Japan and USA.
  • Concentrate on R&D for neutrino factory and/or beta beam.
