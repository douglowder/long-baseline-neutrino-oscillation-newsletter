Long-Baseline news, April 1999

*** Massive Attack

No, it's not about the Balkans, and neutrinos would be useless in those mountains anyway. An article in the March 13th issue of New Scientist starts with neutrino mass overturning the Standard Model, but continues more cautiously. It goes on to call Mini-Boone "definitive", and describes long-baseline experiments in Japan and the US. Some things are unintelligible, though. Apparently "Massive Attack" is the name of a rock group, which is unknown to teens (or their parents) outside of the British Isles.

*** Workshop on Neutrino Factories

Given the long time scales associated with the development of new accelerators, now is the to time to begin work on a precision neutrino physics program. A workshop on neutrino factories based on Muon Storage rings is being held to explore and evaluate conceptual components and of the physics opportunities provided by its neutrino beams. See http://lyoinfo.in2p3.fr/nufact99.

*** Amanda sees atmospheric neutrinos

With the Amanda B array, they have analyzed 6 months of the 2.5 years of data. With a 50 GeV cut, they see 17 clean upgoing muons with no downgoing background. The angular resolution is about 2 degrees, as expected.

*** Relativity probably ok

One explanation of the neutrino experimental data which is not regular neutrino oscillations involves gravitationally induced oscillations. The suggestion is that the different flavors have different coupling to the gravitational potential (whatever that means.) Rather than the usual dependence on L/E, one finds the probability of such an oscillation depends on L*E and the solar and atmospheric neutrino effects collapse to the same area of parameter space. A new paper (Fogli et al, below) looked at the data with this idea and strongly constrains this possibility.
