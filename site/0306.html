Long-Baseline news, June 2003

*** Gran Sasso closed, state of emergency?

In a series of events difficult to interpret, its a good time to remind readers that everything in this newsletter should be taken with grains of salt. There was a spill by Borexino in August 02. This led to a local investigation, an alleged 10,000 page report and the closure of Hall C. A concern was identified to be the water-tightness of the drainage system, connected to the aquifer. All use of liquids (pseudocumene,...) was halted as a precaution. http://uk.news.yahoo.com/030604/12/e1jnx.html Now a state of emergency has been declared on June 27th by the President of the Council of Ministers. http://www.eurekalert.org/pub_releases/2003-06/indf-soe063003.php The Infn expressed satisfaction for the government decision, urged by the Institute and decided on June 25th as a consequence of the official request by the Abruzzo Region. The radical and prompt intervention established by the decree is an essential step toward the restarting of all the activities at the Gran Sasso Labs, in a trustful atmosphere with the citizens.

*** Opera restarted

On June 17th, the Competent Court of Teramo has partially accepted the requests of Infn, allowing the restart of preparations of the Opera experiment. OPERA and ICARUS are long-baseline neutrino experiments planned at Gran Sasso from CERN.

*** Homestake chosen for NUSEL

A geotechnical review panel for the NSF identified Homestake as "by far the most favorable site" for an underground lab in the US (over San Jacinto) with Soudan a possible back-up. A letter from the NSF Director of Physics Dehmer stated, "Nor does this determination suggest a probable successful funding decision following the outcome of further planning activities. Rather, this determination simply allows the science community and other interested parties to focus their planning and proposal efforts on the Homestake site." But Barrick turned off the pumps anyway, despite concerns about how this affects potential geomicrobiology.

*** NUSEL situation fluid, collaboration suspends efforts

The NUSEL collaboration reported June 18th the suggestion that its name is the National Underwater Science and Engineering Lab. The water is at 1.5 ft in the 8000 ft level, and slowly rising. It is estimated up to 2 years before it reaches the 7400' level. The executive committee of the National Underground Science and Engineering Lab issued a press release "Scientists Announce Intent to Suspend NUSEL-Homestake Efforts. An Open Letter to Our S.D. Friends". http://mocha.phys.washington.edu/NUSEL/release.html

*** MANET

Now that MINOS has installed its last plane, much "graffiti" painted on earlier planes has been covered up. That won't be the case with this plane, so a committee has been appointed. It's the "MINOS Art Nomination Evaluation Team" whose job is to sort through a hopefully large number of ideas for that last plane.

*** MINERVA

Minerva is the Roman version of Athena, but so far there is one Greek group among the 19 institutions on Minerva which just had its second collaboration meeting, and no Italians. Their physics topics include elastic scattering, the resonance region, nuclear effects & the transition region to deep inelastic scattering. Planning is for an on-axis detector made from solid scintillator approximately (2-3)m x (2-3)m x 2m. 8 of the groups are from nuclear physics. Their FNAL proposal will be submitted this fall.
