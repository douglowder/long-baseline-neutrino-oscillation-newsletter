Long-Baseline news, October 1998

*** NESTOR Institute Created

A Hellenic Republic Presidential Decree signed Aug. 6 has created the "NESTOR Institute for Deep Sea Research, Technology and Neutrino Astroparticle Physics". This is the first time that a National Laboratory has been created with the specific goal for High Energy Neutrino Astronomy. Lab Director L. Resvanis invites anyone who is interested to come to Pylos and try out their ideas (which might very well be orthogonal to their preconceptions).

*** Muon storage ring possibilities

People at CERN and elsewhere are considering muon storage rings to produce neutrino beams for oscillation studies. While the rings have an attraction for studying electron neutrinos (half of the neutrinos will be nu-e's and they will be tagged by sign, see Cline's paper below), they are not better suited for studies of numu to nutau oscillations than traditional horn-focused pion beams with a decay pipe, despite some claims to the contrary. Muon collider proponents would like to start with some intermediate facility, and neutrino physics is a plausible additional reason that can be used as an excuse, but be careful because some overblown claims are being made.

*** Neutrino Oscillation Length Resonance in Earth's Core?

Enhanced oscillations for particular parameters at small mixing would affect the interpretation of the results of the Super-Kamiokande experiment. Not MSW. (See below-Petcov).

*** NuMI review

This year's budget passed by Congress includes $14M in a line item for the Fermilab neutrino program, but during this next week there will be a "Lehman review" to determine if the project is ready to spend the money. This is standard now for all big US projects. One hint about Fermilab's future is that J. Dorfan who was originally to be on the committee dropped off since he is a rumored candidate for Fermilab Director.

*** A review of the review for NuMI

At the request of congress, the National Research Council issued a report in February 1998 on the need for independent project reviews in the US DOE. They chose NuMI and the Oak Ridge Spallation Neutron source to get "external independent reviews to focus on technical scope, proposed technologies, estimated life-cycle costs, schedules, underlying assumptions and supporting data, and the management, acquisition strategy, and reporting system effectiveness." After NuMI's Lehman review on election day, this review, which will be done by EG&G, will commence.

*** Neutrino election

Neutrino issues are not on the ballot as the United States gets ready to vote, but in a seminar by Barger at Fermilab last month entitled "The Nu Revolution", the hundreds of people there were asked, "Do you think neutrino mass is established?" About two people raised their hand. "Do you think that neutrino mass is not established?" Another two people raised their hand. Bob Dole once spoke about a poll on whether ignorance or apathy was the greater problem. The answer was "I don't know and I don't care."

*** Main Injector circulates beam; Intensity for NuMI updated

The Main Injector has circulated, but not yet accelerated any beam. The MINOS experiment was approved based on 3.7 10^20 protons per year. Some CERN comparisons have assumed that the intensity will be (much) lower than this. Fermilab management has just updated its realistic estimate of how many protons will be available for NuMI, and it's 3.7 10^20 per year.

*** JHF moves from Tsukuba to Tsuchiura

A new site has been proposed for the JHF, a 50 GeV hadron machine in Japan to be coupled with a spallation neutron source. This means that if it is to be used for a long-baseline neutrino experiment, a new beamline will have to be built. But it also helps the schedule for K2K. Earlier, K2K was going to have to pause while the PS was converted into an JHF injector.

*** FNAL neutrino exhibit

There will be a Special FNAL Director's Coffee Break on Friday, November 13, 1998 at 3:30 p.m. in the 1-East Gallery to mark the opening of a Neutrino Exhibit. Superman will be there.
