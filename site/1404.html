Long-Baseline news, April 2014

*** CAPTAIN to measure neutrons in Ar for supernova nu physics

"Captain", "chieftain" and "chef" are all the same french word borrowed by English centuries apart, and trace the diabolical evolution of French pronunciation k/ch/sh. Now CAPTAIN is a liquid argon cryostat being built at Los Alamos, a portable and evacuable cryostat holding 5 tons of liquid Ar with a 2,000 channel TPC and a photon detection system. CAPTAIN will be moved to a high-energy neutron beam at Los Alamos Neutron Science Center to measure cross sections of spallation products that are backgrounds to supernova nus. Later it will be moved to a neutrino source at SNS Oak Ridge or the on-axis NuMI beam.

*** Luk and Wang win 2014 Panofsky Prize

Kam-biu Luk from Lawrence Berkeley National Lab and Yifang Wang from the Institute of High Energy Physics have won the W.K.H. Panofsky Prize in Experimental Particle Physics from the APS for 2014. The citation is "For their leadership of the Daya Bay experiment which produced the first definitive measurement of the theta-13 angle of the neutrino mixing matrix." http://www.aps.org/programs/honors/prizes/panofsky.cfm

*** Big Bird wins a neutrino namesake, and a Mycenaean cow

Ice-Cube seems to like to name its neutrinos after Sesame Street characters, and the latest high energy event is called "Big Bird". https://www.sciencenews.org/blog/science-ticker/meet-big-bird-highest-energy-neutrino-ever-detected Minoan scholars struggle with Linear A, but Linear B was found to be a version of Greek. Did they find any interesting history when they deciphered Linear B? No, it was mostly tax records. There are no names of kings, but they do have the name of a cow.

*** Joe Grange's neutrino thesis wins multiple awards

Joe Grange's thesis, "First Measurement of the Muon Anti-Neutrino Charged Current Quasielastic Double-Differential Cross Section" will be published by Springer Theses Series, which comes with a 500 Euro award. It also wins the URA thesis award at FNAL.

*** Justin Evans wins IOP Group Prize

The UK-based Institute of Physics recently awarded the 2014 High Energy Particle Physics Group Prize to Justin Evans of the University of Manchester. The prize is awarded to Evans "for his world-leading contributions to the measurement of neutrino oscillations at MINOS using the Fermilab NuMI beam." http://www.iop.org/activity/groups/subject/hepp/prize/group/page_63058.html

*** Xin Qian and Lisa Kaufman win DOE Early Career Awards

Xin at Brookhaven is working on Detector Development toward precision measurements of neutrino mixing. Lisa is working on characterizing backgrounds for EXO. http://science.energy.gov/~/media/early-career/pdf/FY14_DOE_SC_Early_Career_Research_Program_Selectee_List.pdf

*** We all win as MIPP measures yields from a neutrino target

Jon Paley et al. have analyzed the data from Fermilab E907 measuring production of hadrons from 1.42e6 120-GeV protons hitting a NuMI target. Normalized charged pion yields were found in bins of longitudinal and transverse momentum between 0.5 and 80 GeV/c with combined syst/stat uncertainties 5-10%. http://arxiv.org/abs/1404.5882

*** Karol Lang wins MINOS+ cospokesperson election

U of Texas physicist Karol Lang is the new cospokesperson of MINOS+ along with Jenny Thomas from University College/London.

*** 1032 wins over 1033

The Fermilab PAC recommended approval of P-1032, a search for Dark Matter with MiniBooNE, and its request for 1.75e20 pot in beam dump mode. It did not recommend P-1033, MiniBooNE+, which requested 6.0e20 pot and the addition of 300 kg of wavelength shifting PPO to observe 2.2 MeV gammas from neutron capture and reduce NC background to nue appearance by half.

*** Mini MICE (Muon Ionization Cooling Experiment)

At a MICE review at RAL by DOE and STFC, the 2 funding agencies are leaning towards a "Minimal" MICE plan. Major hardware already delivered and to be delivered during the next 2-3 years would allow the demonstration of ionization cooling with re-acceleration. MICE would continue another 3 years (was 6) with DOE funding of $12M (was $30M). Overall it was a positive review.
