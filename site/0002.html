Long-Baseline news, February 2000

*** NuMI notice to proceed (or the FNAL/Gran Sasso connection)

The DOE announced that it has awarded a $30.5 million contract to S.A. Healy Co. to build the tunnel for NuMI. Construction of the NuMI tunnels and halls, including the decay pipe and the area for the MINOS near detector, will take about 18 months. The parent company of Healy is an Italian firm which worked at Gran Sasso.

*** The MINOS/SNO connection

The construction of the MINOS cavity at Soudan is proceeding, with a large drilling machine which is making the holes for the explosives. The machine was brought from Sudbury, Canada. One delay was caused when the crew couldn't find any metric bolts.

*** NEMO contract

A Contract for a Conceptual and Feasibility Study of a Deep-sea Astrophysical Neutrino Observatory, called NEMO (NEutrino Mediterranean Observatory), has been awarded to Sasp Offshore Engineering, Sonsub International and Saipem (ENI Group) by INFN. The Observatory will consist of ~104 optical sensors 3500 m deep.

*** Not enough adjectives

Amid a "historic science and technology budget" for FY01, the DOE line item budget for NuMI is adequate for the beam, but the detector budget is falling behind. The president's budget for FY01 cut the MINOS detector from $15M to $10M, but FNAL quickly determined that this was a mistake. NuMI management claims there are not enough adjectives in the English language to describe their reaction to the proposed FY01 budget.

*** UuNnO pronounced uno

A meeting at Irvine in February considered the construction of a 60 x 60 x 180 m^3 650 kton water cerenkov counter, the Ultra underground (or underwater) Nucleon decay and neutrino Observatory. Capabilities for nucleon decay, solar nus, super- nova nus, atmospheric nus and nu-factory targets were considered.

*** Super-K solar neutrinos waiting for Neutrino 2000

Super-K decided not to update its results on solar neutrinos every 6 months. 824 day numbers have been shown publicly, but won't be updated till a group meeting about 10 days before Neutrino 2000. One change in solar neutrino analysis is in the day/night effect, which was previously shown in 10 equal bins (5 day, 5 night). Bin N5 has now been changed to correspond to the core only. They are not seeing an enhancement in N5 only, but D-N/D+N is 1.8 sigma for 824 days. They are making progress in lowering the threshold. They are 95% efficient at 4.5 MeV and will soon show data down to 5.0 MeV and should go below.

*** SNO goes SuperNova while waiting for Neutrino 2000

SNO will wait for Neutrino 2000 to show its real results. They have some rate of flashing phototubes, which are easy to eliminate in offline analysis, but whose rates appear correlated to seismic activity. One physicist dropped a wrench on the outside platform and set off the supernova trigger.

*** K2K rates

K2K now expects to get 10^20 protons on target in 5 years rather than the earlier hoped for 3 years. So far they have about 1.5 10^19 pot. The neutrino event rate in the 3 near detectors is Data/MC ~ .84+-.01 in 3 detectors. Their far event rate is 3 contained fidvol events, expecting 12.3 +-1.8 (no osc). They also have 3 outside fidvol, expecting 5.5+-1.2; 2 events in the outer detector (crossing) expecting 4.2+-1.6 and 4 events contained in the OD, expecting 8.7+-3.3. They put 5.4 10^12 ppp on the target and expect a short term 5% increase. The pulse-to- pulse beam pointing is good to +-.5 mrad, probably dominated by small targeting errors on the current carrying target.

*** HARP experiment at CERN approved

This PS experiment will study hadron production for understanding potential muon yield for neutrino factories. The NA49 beamline and new apparatus are a possibility. E_max is 15 GeV/c.

*** A friend disappears

We deeply regret to report the death of Roland Barloutaud, founder and original director of the Frejus laboratory.

*** Neutrinos on the dark side

Matter effects change the region of parameter space that is allowed/excluded in the absence of matter effects for many experiments. These effects change depending on the sign of delta-m squared. This could be addressed in the sin^2-2 theta -- dm2 plot by folding over the negative solution above mixing of 1. But it turns out that tan^2 theta is a better variable to plot than sin^2 2-theta. This is done in Gouvea et al. (below)

*** Shrock shocked, Bethe blocked

At the neutrino factory workshop, Shrock said that he originally found the high mixing angle needed to explain atmospheric neutrinos "shocking". He wasn't alone. For a long time (still?) Bethe would only consider small angle MSW solutions. "Why do you prefer the small mixing angle solution?" Bethe was asked just 2 years ago. "Mixing angles are small." he answered.
