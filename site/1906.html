Long-Baseline news, June 2019

*** new NOvA results

The NOvA experiment showed new results with a larger antineutrino sample, based on 12.3e20 pot. There is 4.4 sigma evidence for nuebar appearance. Combining with neutrinos, it measures dmsq_32 = 2.48 +0.11 -0.06 e-3 eV^2, sin^2(theta_23) = 0.56 +0.04 -0.03, The data favors the normal mass order by 1.9 sigma and the upper octant for theta_23 by 1.6 sigma. arXiv:1906.04907

*** Updated 2019 Particle Data Group values for neutrino mixing

Listings are updated annually. In the 2019 version, there are new values for theta_13, theta_23, delta & dmsq_32, while theta_12 and dmsq_21 are unchanged. The new values are sin^2(q23)=0.536 +0.023 -0.028; delta = 1.37 +0.18 -0.16 pi rad; sin^2(q13) = 0.0218 +- 0.0007 and dmsq_32 = 0.002444 +- 0.000034 eV^2. http://pdglive.lbl.gov/Particle.action?node=S067

*** Vietnam Neutrino School

https://ifirse.icise.vn/nugroup/vson/2019/overview.html The "Vietnam School on Neutrinos" will start soon 7-19 July. The school is organized by Japanese physicists, Neutrino Group, IFIRSE & Rencontres du Vietnam, held at ICISE center, Quy Nhon. *** Gaurang Bhaskar Yodh, 1928-2019

https://umdphysics.umd.edu/about-us/news/department-news/1470-gaurang-yodh-1928-2019.html Gaurang was a notable cosmic ray physicist, but among his many accomplishments, he was a pioneer in using large neutrino detectors for their ability, between beam spills, to measure characteristics of ultra high energy cosmic ray air showers.

*** URA graduate thesis award to Jacob Todd for MINOS+ analysis

https://news.fnal.gov/2019/06/jacob-todd-receives-ura-graduate-thesis-award-for-work-on-neutrino-data-analysis/ Todd details a method for analyzing data from neutrino experiments in a way that removes bias present in traditional methods. It’s the first time that the method has been applied for long-baseline neutrino experiments — experiments in which two neutrino detectors are separated by a great distance.

*** Proposal to study tau neutrino production at CERN

In Aoki et al., arXiv:1906.03487 , the DsTau collaboration aims to use decays of D_s mesons produced in proton-nucleus interactions and measure the cross section in emulsion. A result would be a more accurate tau neutrino flux from a nuclear target.

*** Fewer Advisory Panels?

President Trump has ordered federal agencies to eliminate at least one-third of their non-statutory advisory committees created under the Federal Advisory Committee Act. The High Energy Physics Advisory Panel has been advising the government on the construction of particle physics facilities since the 1960s and oversees the Particle Physics Project Prioritization Panel (P5) process, which establishes a nationwide strategic direction for the field. P5 has scheduled an update.

*** First CAPTAIN Scientific Publication

CAPTAIN's first scientific paper has been accepted for publication by PRL. It is the first measurement of the neutron cross section on Ar from 100-800 MeV.
