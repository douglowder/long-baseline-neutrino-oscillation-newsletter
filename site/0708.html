Long-Baseline news, August 2007

*** Borexino measures Oscillating Solar Neutrinos

This experiment at the Gran Sasso in Italy has just made the first direct measurement of 7Be solar neutrinos. They get 47+-7(stat)+-12(sys) counts/day/100ton. The expectation based on the solar model plus LMA oscillations is 49+-4. Without oscillations it would be 75+-4. 7Be nus all have E = 0.862MeV. It is impressive that they achieved an excellent background level without any further but the initial purification. arXiv:0708.2251

*** What's in a name? Pursuing neutrinos with Project X

When cost of the ILC came out, US Under Secretary for Science Ray Orbach suggested the timescale for the ILC would stretch into the "middle or end of the 2020s" and that HEP needed new studies. http://www.science.doe.gov/hep/HEPAPORBACHremarks2-22-07-FINAL.pdf Fermilab appointed a steering committee, led by Y.K. Kim to study Fermilab's options and they have released a report. "...a central feature of the proposed Fermilab plan calls for building an intense proton facility, Project X, consisting of a linear accelerator with the currently-planned characteristics of the ILC, Fermilab's existing Recycler Ring, and the Main Injector." Similar to, but not exactly matching previous plans for the proton driver, or the High Intensity Neutrino Source (HINS) it was given a name which isn't a name, so the name will be chosen later. http://www.fnal.gov/directorate/Longrange/Steering_Public/report/Steering-Group-Internal-Report.pdf

*** More names for Fermilab intensity accelerator upgrades

  • ANU (Part of NOvA): 700 kW by slip-stacking 12 Booster batches into the recycler
  • SNuMI: (Super NuMI) 1.2 MW by stacking 18 Booster batches using both the Accumulator and the Recycler
  • Project X: 2.3 MW by replacing the Booster with an ILC-technology linace which feeds the recycler.

*** NOvA passes another in a series of reviews

NOvA passed a Fermilab internal Director's review in August and now has its CD23a Lehman review in October, where the final mass (15kT) associated with its $260M cap will be evaluated & approved.

*** Neutrinos in 2008, 2010, 2012 and 2030

Neutrino 2008 will be held May 26-31 at the University of Canterbury in Christchurch New Zealand. A web page is being developed at http://www.neutrino2008.co.nz/. Neutrino 2010 will be 14-20 June 2010 in Athens Greece. Neutrino 2012 will be in Kyoto Japan, and Neutrino 2014 will probably be in North America. The location for Neutrino 2030, the centennial of Pauli's letter, is still up for grabs.
