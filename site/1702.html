Long-Baseline news, February 2017

*** Milestone of 700 kW reached for Fermilab neutrino beam to NOvA

On 25 Jan. 2017, Fermilab reached the goal of the NOvA-PIP I program, 700 kW for the proton beam on target. With some beam to the switchyard it reached the "700 kW-equivalent" power of 635 kW. The NOvA long-baseline neutrino experiment is looking forward to extracting the physics potential that this record power presents. On 20 February, the horn polarity was changed for antineutrinos. http://news.fnal.gov/2017/01/fermilab-achieves-milestone-beam-power-neutrino-experiments/

*** T2K to upgrade its near detector

http://t2k-experiment.org/2017/02/t2k-near-detector-upgrade/ T2K has launched its Near Detector Upgrade project. The upgrade is targeted at reducing systematic errors in its search for CP violation. The current conceptual design will be developed into a technical design, leading to a full proposal, by the end of 2017. The collaboration aims to install the upgraded near detector around 2020, to fully benefit from the foreseen increase of the J-PARC MR beam power.

*** Disparaging the Neutrino masses

The low masses of the neutrinos seem appropriate to some of us, but Ballesteros et al. mention that the three masses for nu1, nu2 and nu3 are "disparagingly smaller than any physics scale in the SM." They go on to unify inflation with the axion, dark matter, Baryogenesis and the seesaw mechanism in PRL118, 071802 (2017), arXiv:1610.01639

*** Mobile Neutrino Laboratory

Before long-baseline neutrino beams were a reality, Bjorken proposed putting ionization detectors on trucks and driving them around Michigan to study the Fermilab neutrino beam. Now a mobile neutrino detector built by Virginia Tech is a reality, but to measure reactor antineutrinos. MiniCHANDLER uses solid scintillator which will allow it measure about 100 interactions per day. http://www.symmetrymagazine.org/article/mobile-neutrino-lab-makes-its-debut

*** Neutrino mass matters to the universe

My best guess is that the sum of the nu masses, m-nu1+m-nu2+m-nu3, is close to the sum of the square root of two measured delta-m^2, or 60 meV. The 9-year WMAP analysis made this assumption, but the pre-Planck ACT/SPT analyses did not. Calabrese et al. show this affects the best fit cosmological parameters by 0.25-2 sigma in their 2017 update. arXiv:1702.03272
