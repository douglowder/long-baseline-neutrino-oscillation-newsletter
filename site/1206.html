Long-Baseline news, June 2012

*** LBNE reconfiguration progress; feedback sought

LBNE has settled on a 10 kiloton far detector on the surface at Homestake. It is encouraged to get its baseline funding estimate (CD1) with a Lehman review in October/November. The interim report: https://indico.fnal.gov/getFile.py/access?resId=0&materialId=3&confId=5622 Public or private feedback is solicited by 20 July through the DPF newsletter. http://dpfnewsletter.org/?p=774

*** New results from Double Chooz at Neutrino 2012

Using the rate and energy shape, Double Chooz reactor antineutrino disappearance experiment now measures sin^2(2 q13) = 0.109 +- 0.030(stat) +-0.025(syst), & rejects no oscillations at 3.1 sigma. It has a week with both reactors off, useful to study backgrounds. [Web version note: talks can be found at http://kds.kek.jp/conferenceTimeTable.py?confId=9151#all.detailed ]

*** New results from Daya Bay at Neutrino 2012

The Daya Bay reactor antineutrino disappearance experiment showed an improved measurement with more than 2.5 times the data from their publication. Using the rates at near and far detectors, they see 0.944 +-0.007(stat) +-0.003(syst) of the expected rate at the Far Detector, and fit to sin^2(2 q13) = 0.089 +-0.010 (stat) +- 0.005(syst), the most precise measurement of theta13.

*** New results from Minerva at Neutrino 2012

Minerva has measured the the antineutrino Quasi-elastic cross section off scintillator & shown Q^2 distribution. The large axial-vector m value of 1.35 GeV is disfavored and there is a dip in the data at low Q^2. Data with nuclear targets are coming.

*** New results from EXO200 at Neutrino 2012

Using a 32.5 kg-yr of data (arXiv:1205.5608) and 22,000 2nu-2beta events, they measure a half life of 2.23 +- 0.017(stat) +- 0.22 (sys) 10^(21) yr for 136Xe. They observe no signal in the 0nu-2beta region of interest (5 events within 2 sigma, with 7.5 expected from the background fit.) They limit the lifetime to be greater than 1.6 10^25 years, and the effective mass to be less than 140-380 meV at 90% CL. Klapdor is mostly ruled out.

*** New results from Super-K at Neutrino 2012

Solar nu rate best fit flux is now 2.39 10^6/(cm^2 s) for SK-III. 1069 days of SK-IV has new electronics, a lower threshold & higher efficiency. The 3904 day analysis sees 57721.3 solar nus, with a best fit to .451 +- 0.007 of the unoscillated expected flux. The Day-Night Amplitude (-2.8+-1.1+-0.5%), "consistent with zero at 2.3 sigma". Also called a Day/Night hint. Atmospheric nus updated +1097 days to 3903 days and with theta_13 Delta msquared = 2.66 +.15-.40 10^-3 eV^2 at one sigma and sin^2(q23) = .391 to .691 at 90%CL. There is now 3.8 sigma signal for tau neutrino appearance with the reactor constraint.

*** New results from T2K at Neutrino 2012

Based on 2.56e20 pot collected by May 2012, T2K now has 10 events (6 before) for electron neutrino appearance in a muon neutrino beam in Japan. This is equivalent to a 3.2 sigma excess compared to no oscillations, and fits to sin^2(2 q13) = 0.104+0.060-0.045. The systematic error is now at the level of 10%. After the Mar 2011 earthquake, J-PARC/T2K restarted with 190kW beam power.

*** New results from MINOS at Neutrino 2012

With 10.71e20 pot nus, MINOS gets best fit delta m^2_32 = 2.41 + .11 -.10 10^-3 eV^2 and sin^2(2q) = 0.94 +0.04 -0.05. Systematics are still small. For antinus 3.36e20 pot, and delta m^2_32 = 2.48 +.22 -.27 10^-3 eV^2, sin^2(2q23) > 0.83 at 90%CL (best fit 0.97). For nue, expect 128.6 events, see 152 in nu, expect 17.5 antinu, see 20. With sin^2(2q13)=0.1, would expect 32.5 and 3.7 more. A joint fit with 37.9 kt-years of atmo nu data gives best fit 2.39 +0.09 -0.10 10^-3 eV^2, sin^2(2q23) > 0.90 at 90%CL (best 0.96).

*** New results from OPERA at Neutrino 2012

The OPERA experiment in the higher energy long-baseline beam has found a second event consistent with muon neutrino to tau neutrino oscillations. They have taken 14.2e19pot through 2011 and expect to have 18.9e19pot after the 2012 run, compared to 22.5e19pot in the proposal. They have found a second candidate tau event with 1 nuclear fragment and one primary track and a tau flight length 1.54mm which decayed into a 3 prong. They now have two events with 2.1 expected signal & 0.2 background. They have 19 electron candidates, 4 with E<20GeV (expect 1.1 signal, 3.9 background).

*** Congratulations to Higgs discoverers and to Higgs?

Since I congratulated my friends for the discovery of the Higgs at 125 GeV last December, I don't need to do it again. (I think Steven Colbert got it right on 7 March.) But does the Higgs give mass to the Universe? It accounts for 1% of the nucleon mass (rest glue) which makes up 4% of the Universe. Neutrino mass may be all Higgs (Dirac) or not (Majorana). http://www.colbertnation.com/the-colbert-report-videos/410170/
