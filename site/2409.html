Long-Baseline news, September 2024


NuFact 2024 Issue

*** Neutrino Oscillation Joint fit workshop

At a satellite workshop to the 2024 NuFact Meeting, Multi- experiment oscillation measurements and joint fits were discussed. https://indico.cern.ch/event/1453627/sessions/565029/#20240915 Results and lessons from the T2K/NOvA and T2K/Super-K analyses were covered and possibilities for DUNE/Hyper-K and JUNO/IceCube were discussed. The experience of the ATLAS/CMS Higgs Combination was also covered.

*** NEUT

NEUT is a nu-nucleus interaction generator for 100 MeV to TeV nus. It is maintained for T2K & Super-K. The source has historically not been public, but is available upon request. NEUT 6 is under development for Hyper-K with the aim to release it as open source before the end of 2024. https://indico.fnal.gov/event/63406/contributions/297535/attachments/181751/249331/NuFact2024_NEUT-2.pdf

*** GENIE

GENIE is a universal, self-consistent nu generator from MeV to PeV energy scales from a group of active core member which maintains a user group. The latest release is 3.04.02 and 3.06.00 is in development. See http://releases.genie-mc.org/ . GENIE covers physics from low-energy CEvNS to ultra-high energy DIS with large coverage in GeV region. There are some BSM processes as well. https://indico.fnal.gov/event/63406/contributions/297530/attachments/181713/249268/GENIE_developments_v2.pdf

*** NuWro

NuWro is an event simulation based on a factorization scheme. Nuclear effects are treated in the impulse approximation. The current version is 21.09.2. Version 24 is under development. eWro is a similar version for electrons. https://indico.fnal.gov/event/63406/contributions/297533/attachments/181661/249192/NuWro-NuFACT_2024.pdf

*** NuHEPMC

(See arXiv:2310.13211 )

To better compare results using different nu generators, NuHEPMC is a proposed set of agreed-upon minimal metadata beyond the true vector of particles involved in the event. Generator implementations are expected to store additional metadata. A draft will be put on the arXiv to solicit feedback. There are implementations for NEUT, GENIE, NuWro, Achilles, and NUISANCE.

*** Muon Collider (MuC)

https://indico.fnal.gov/event/63406/contributions/297172/attachments/181771/249372/NUFACT_talk.pdf A muon collider, which would be a prolific source of both muon and electron neutrinos, was an original motivation for the NuFact series. In 2023, the P5 panel recommended that the US should work on a collider with 10 TeV parton collision E, “In particular, a mu collider presents an attractive option..." The MuC community plans to self-organize. Join the mailing list at https://listserv.fnal.gov/scripts/wa.exe?SUBED1=usmcc-info&A=1

*** Boyarsky test

In a talk on neutrinos at CERN at NuFact 2024, Alexey Boyarsky said, "If you build an experiment, you're interested in any theory that can be tested at your experiment and you're not interested in any theory that can't be tested." A likely corollary is that theorists care most about results that can test their theories.

*** IceCube has a movable detector

The IceCube detector, which measures an atmospheric neutrino every six minutes, is sitting in antarctic ice which moves at the glacial speed of 10 m/y. The KM3NeT/ORCA detector with an energy threshold of few GeV is 20% complete and will be finished in 2028. KM3NeT/ARCA has measured the highest energy neutrino in the 10s of PeV which triggered 3672 PMTs. It was a degree above the horizon, as expected since the Earth is opaque to neutrinos above a PeV.

*** Mean Excitation Energy of Liquid Argon

If you use GEANT4, the mean excitation energy is 188 eV. This affects the stopping power of charged particles, and hence their energy determination in argon. After reviewing the history of previous measurements, Matt Strait remeasured it and found 201+-3 eV. Many older measurements don't quote any uncertainties. https://indico.fnal.gov/event/63406/contributions/298869/attachments/181858/249576/nufact2024-talk.pdf
