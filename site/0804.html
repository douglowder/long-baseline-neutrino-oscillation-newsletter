*** DUSEL Water up to 4850

The Sanford S.D. Lab at Homestake/DUSEL had hoped to get the pumps back on before the water level rose to 4850, where many new nu experiments are planned. A precise measurement in April found the water at 4,688. Pumps are now on to get it back down again. http://www.homestakelab.com/news.html

*** P5 Report and Neutrinos

The P5 report will be made public at the May HEPAP meeting http://www.science.doe.gov/hep/upcoming_meetings.shtm but the fact that I haven't read it yet won't keep me from speculating. I suspect it will say positive things about NOvA and Double Chooz and Daya Bay and Project X and a longer-term neutrino program from Fermilab to DUSEL. Of course, they can't predict resources in our field, but they can influence how those resources are divided for different scenarios, which in turn might influence the probability of each scenario

*** Bad funding scenario for neutrinos in High Energy Physics

One suggestion is that 73% of future money in HEP should go to Dark Energy Experiments, with 23% to Dark Matter Experiments, 4% to proton colliders, and much smaller amounts to electron machines and neutrino experiments. With this scheme, we would have been much better off when the universe was only 300,000 years old.

*** Status of RENO experiment in South Korea

The Korean/Russian collaboration for reactor nus plans to make its Technical Design Report available in May 2008. They are planning a near detector 290 m from the centerline of 6 reactors spaced at 1.3 km, and a far detector in a 380 m tunnel 1380 m distant. The $4M tunnel will be dug from May-Nov 08, & data taking scheduled to start in early 2010. Also a mock-up is under construction with 30 ten inch Hamamatsu phototubes scheduled to arrive in May.

*** Do Mossbauer Neutrinos Oscillate (take two)

I still haven't figure out the quantum issues yet, but the theoretical consensus seems to be that they do. The objection involves a claimed violation of the time-energy uncertainty principle. The neutrino wave function has here an extension which is much bigger than the size of the source/emitter, The kinematic details (emission, absorption and magnitude of momenta/energies) are a bit tricky, such that x=t does not apply. See ArXiV docs - arXiv:0803.1424 & arXiv:0804.3409

*** Neutrinos can be used to monitor nuclear reactors

A prototype cubic meter antineutrino detector has been used to monitor the relative thermal power of the San Onofre reactor to 3% within 7 days, potentially useful within the context of International Atomic Energy Agency's (IAEA) Reactor Safeguard Regime. See http://arxiv.org/abs/0804.4723
