Long-Baseline news, June 1999

*** K2K; First long-baseline (accelerator) event reported

This is the 88th monthly issue of long-baseline news, but the first for which an accelerator based long-baseline neutrino experiment actually has data. At Super-Kamiokande on June 19th, run 7436 event 1405412 a muon neutrino during a beam spill pointed back to the KEK accelerator. http://neutrino.kek.jp/news/990628.1stSK/

*** SNO - Sudbury's Now Operating

The Sudbury heavy water solar neutrino experiment has started taking data. Beautiful atmospheric neutrino candidates and solar neutrino candidates have been seen. Noise due to radioactivity is below expectation at this point. A problem they had with high voltage breakdown in a connector has been completely solved by regassifying the water with pure Nitrogen. It had been degassified to remove Oxygen. www.sno.phy.queensu.ca/event/event.html

*** Long-Baseline Plans in Europe - 1

A New York Times article described the Fermilab to Soudan project as being developed in Europe. There is some truth to this. There are three times as many European countries in the MINOS collaboration as North American countries. The British contingent of the experiment have recently appeared with a money and effort request in front of the PPESP. The requests will be forwarded to PPARC with formal approval expected in December.

*** Long-Baseline Plans in Europe - 2

Another European plan for long-baseline neutrino experiments is the NGS initiative, Neutrinos to Gran Sasso (from CERN). An NGS addendum has been issued (R. Bailey et al., report INFN-AE-99-05 and CERN-SL-99-034-DI). There is also a new web page with NGS information http://www.cern.ch/NGS. As always, such web pages are available at the Neutrino Oscillation Industry site, www.hep.anl.gov/NDK/Hypertext/nu_industry.html Proposals are due in October and a decision is seen by December. European long-baseline proposals include ICARUS, DAEDALUS, OPERA, AQUA-RICH, NOE and MONOLITH.

*** Atmospheric Sterile Neutrino "almost" ruled out

Since sterile neutrinos have no neutral current interaction, there is a difference in cross section between sterile and active neutrinos in the earth. This leads to an MSW like effect for sterile neutrinos. This effect is largest near the best fit parameters of Super-K for atmospheric neutrinos. It would distort the angular distribution of upward throughgoing muons and partially contained events. The ratio of vertical to horizontal events in these two categories disfavors sterile neutrinos at the 2+ sigma level. Great fits to all distributions can be obtained with if numu is oscillating to nutau.

*** Alice's Restaurant for solar neutrinos in Super-K

"You can get anything you want at Alice's Restaurant" or by analyzing Super-K solar nu data. There is now a 2 sigma effect for a day-night effect. From 825 days, D-N/D+N = -0.065 +- 0.031(stat) +- 0.013(syst). If real, it would be the Large Angle MSW solution. But then there wouldn't be a spectral distortion. Chi-squared for flat is 24.3 (18-1 d.o.f.) The shape is a fit for vacuum oscillations, but a change in the hep neutrino flux together with small-angle MSW also works. Finally, there is a hint for some seasonal variation, beyond what would be expected from the eccentricity of the earth's orbit.

*** Quark/Lepton Symmetry

Wolfenstein recently pointed out that Quark/Lepton symmetry is one reason for neutrino mass, but the symmetry is broken. "If you can't tell the difference between a quark and a lepton, you're in pretty bad shape." Ramond, "or a string theorist." Wolfenstein, "same thing."
