Long-Baseline news, January 2003

*** 2002 Anno Mirabilis Neutrino

Neutrino physicists, already with our heads in the clouds, had an extraordinary year in 2002. Previously we had evidence for neutrino mixing in atmospheric neutrinos. With the SNO results, the solar neutrino problem is no longer a problem for anyone, and there is clearly mixing of solar neutrinos. With KamLAND, there is mixing of reactor neutrinos. K2K has good "indications" for mixing of accelerator neutrinos.

*** Department of Energy Strategic Plan

DOE Office of Science Director Ray Orbach is assembling a 20 year vision for DOE science with input from High Energy, Nuclear and Basic Energy Sciences. HEPAP has assembled a committee to rank 13 possible future projects. Five of the projects are related to neutrinos: 3.) NuMI off-axis detector[$100-500M]; 4.) Proton Driver for a Neutrino Superbeam[$500M-1B]; 5) A next proton decay experiment[$500M-1B]; 6) Liquid Xenon double-beta decay detector [$100-500M] and 7) Muon Storage Ring Neutrino Factory [>$1B]. Prices are just estimates of specified ranges for the study.

*** LMA region bifurcates

Unless you care about history or CPT violation, you can forget about the SMA, LOW and Vacuum oscillation regions of parameter space now. But the LMA region now has two distinct solutions with a 2-nu analysis, called LMA-I and LMA-II by Fogli et al., at dm^2 = 7.3 10^-5 eV^2, sin^2q_12 = .315; and 15.4 10^-5; .300. In a 3-nu analysis with q_13 floating, a 3rd region, LMA-III appears: dm^2 ~ 2.5-3.2 10^-4 best for zero q_13. hep-ph/0212127

*** KamLAND error

By law, all Japanese reactor power levels are to be known to 2%. In addition, Korean reactors provide 2.5% of the event rate and the rest of the world 0.7%. What about geoneutrinos, which provide signal below 2.7 MeV from Uranium and Thorium decays? See Fiorentini et al, below.

*** Indian Neutrino Observatory

A collaboration has been formed and funded in India to study the possibility of a new large neutrino detector. A first phase would study atmospheric neutrinos, and a second phase would hope for beams from elsewhere. Two sites are under consideration, one in Southern India near Ooty, the Pykara Ultimate Stage Hydro Electric Project (PUSHEP) which is in a tunnel, and the other near the town of Rammam northwest of Darjeeling in the Himalayas. PUSHEP has better rock; at Rammam more overburden is possible. An MOU has been signed with ~12 groups & 40 people so far.

*** Ben Franklin Award to Bahcall, Davis, Koshiba

Just last week, an investment manager asked me "what good are neutrinos", and I gave the standard reply, "when Franklin was kite flying, he didn't know uses for electricity." This year's Ben Franklin award went to John Bahcall, Raymond Davis, and Masatoshi Koshiba for the discovery of solar neutrinos, furthering our understanding of the internal processes of the Sun and stars in general. See http://www.fi.edu/tfi/exhibits/bower/03/physics.html

*** K2K running

With the completion of the rebuild of Super-Kamiokande, the long-baseline neutrino oscillation experiment K2K has resumed running with beam from the KEK accelerator in Tsukuba. http://www.kek.jp/newskek/2003/janfeb/k2k-3.html (japanese only)

*** JHF/JPARC LOI for off-axis neutrino beam to Super-K

J-PARC has received 31 Letters of Intent for Nuclear and Particle Physics Experiments including JHF-nu. There's also a high-intensity mu source (PRISM) and a nu factory (NufactJ). A Committee to consider the LOIs will meet early spring. The 2003 "JHF-nu" LOI is at http://neutrino.kek.jp/jhfnu/ 139 people from 49 institutions in 11 countries signed.

*** NuMI Off-axis workshop(s)

A workshop to consider an off-axis experiment at Fermilab was held at SLAC. The goal is to submit a proposal by October. Several detector technologies were reviewed and a next workshop to narrow them down was set for April 25-27, 2003. Some diners at the SLAC cafeteria wondered if NuMI off-axis meant 90 degrees, so they could build a detector at SLAC.

*** Workshop on Radioactive Beams

Radioactive Beams (with helium 6 and/or neon 18) making nu-e's would be a powerful complement to superbeams(nu-mu), and may be feasible, after some R & D to increase the neon 18 production. A workshop on "radioactive beams for nuclear physics and neutrino physics" will be one of the march sessions at Moriond. At Les Arcs March 17-22. See http://www-dapnia.cea.fr/Spp/jb/index.html
