Long-Baseline news, May 2017

*** Baby MIND and WAGASCI to reduce uncertainties in T2K

The difference in target material between the T2K near detector (scintillator) and far detector (water) is the largest systematic uncertainty for oscillation analysis. A new ND with a water target (WAGASCI) has been developed. In order to measure the momentum and charge of outgoing muons, a magnetized iron detector (Baby MIND) is also proposed. arXiv:1705.10406 ; http://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/1742-6596/675/1/012030/pdf

*** Tau appearance in Super-K atmospheric neutrinos

When Super-K discovered neutrino oscillations in 98, they also had a consistent 2 sigma excess in tau neutrino appearance. Burt Richter didn't understand. They now report 338.1 +- 72.7 events of nutau appearance, a 3.3 sigma excess corresponding to a chance probability of 4.4e-4. This is 5326 days of SK-I to SK-IV.

*** Neutrino covfefe** in the U.S.

The first step in the US budget process is the President's Budget Request (PBR), which proposes a 17% cut of the DOE Office of Science for 2018 compared to 2017. It is 18% for HEP, For NSF the proposed cut is 11%, 10% for Math and Physical Sciences. Changes are possible, even likely, but some plans are made around these numbers. For LBNF/DUNE, there is $55M, $5M more than FY17 and $29M more than FY16, but below what's needed to stay on schedule. The winner of the 2017 URA thesis award for was Leonidas Aliaga Soplin for his work "Neutrino Flux Prediction for the NuMI Beamline", his 2016 thesis from the College of William & Mary, available at http://inspirehep.net/record/1450751/ He created a software tool called Package to Predict the FluX (PPFX). http://news.fnal.gov/2017/06/leo-aliaga-soplin-honored-2017-ura-thesis-award/

*** PIP-1+

When MINOS was proposed, the proton intensity of the FNAL Main Injector was projected to be 3.7e20 protons per year, though it took several years to reach that value. The Proton Improvement Plan (PIP) has now successfully raised that for NOvA to 7e20. PIP-1+ is not a project, like PIP-II, but a follow-on to reduce beam losses and conduct a new shielding assessment, to increase the rate, along with upgrading the NuMI target to take more protons per pulse. The PIP-II vision is 1.2 MW; "PIP-III" 2.4MW.

*** Follow the move of ICARUS across the Atlantic

The 760 ton (when full) ICARUS detector, which previously moved from Gran Sasso in Italy to CERN in Switzerland for upgrades, will soon begin its transport to Fermilab in the U.S. You can follow the journey at http://icarustrip.fnal.gov/

*** First neutrinos in the Fermilab muon area

The first beam has been injected into the Fermilab g-2 ring. Nobody has measured neutrinos from muon decay, but we know they are there. The g-2 physics run should start by calendar 2018.
