*** FASER sees first neutrinos at the LHC in emulsion

Collider experiments make less nus than fixed target experiments, but some are higher energy. A goal of CERN's FASER is to measure some of these TeV nus. As reported in arXiv:2105.06197, this detector using emulsions 480 m forward of the ATLAS experiment reported 6.1 (11.9) signal (mu background) nu interactions. https://phys.org/news/2021-11-physicists-neutrinos-large-hadron-collider.html

*** Panofsky prizes to Lundberg, Niwa, Rameika and Paolone for emulsion

The 2022 APS Panofsky prize was awarded to 4 recipients, "for the first direct observation of the tau neutrino through its charged- current interactions in an emulsion detector," including FNAL's Byron Lundberg, Nagoya's Kimio Niwa, & Pittsburgh's Vittorio Paolone and Fermilab's Gina Rameika. https://www.aps.org/programs/honors/prizes/panofsky.cfm

*** 2-gamma decay probes 0nu2beta decay nuclear matrix elements

There is uncertainty in predicted neutrinoless double-beta decay rates from matrix elements, and measurements from 2-nu decay does not pin it down. Future 2-gamma measurements might help. arXiv:2102.11101

*** And a reminder about OPERA

The Oscillation Project with Emulsion-tRacking Apparatus (OPERA) was a scientific experiment that detected 10 nutaus from numu oscillations. In 2018 they made their data public. https://home.cern/news/press-release/cern/opera-collaboration-presents-its-final-results-neutrino-oscillations-all

*** What would you do if you had $100M

At an SBND theory workshop at Fermilab, three theorists ranked 13 physics motivations, thinking about how they would divide up a hundred million dollars. The winner was the pseudo-scalar dark sector. No real money was spent in making slide 41 of https://indico.ph.ed.ac.uk/event/107/contributions/1461/attachments/931/1260/SBN_workshop_slides.pdf

*** James Webb Space Telescope overcomes >300 potential failures

After a successful launch, the telescope deployed is primary mirror, after testing, more testing, and even more testing a process with >300 single points of failure. We'll learn some more about nu mass. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pg-YI0T-4Mk

*** Uninteresting letters of Interest from >300 LOIs

A keen observer will be able to ascertain that there were no uninteresting letters of Interest submitted to Snowmass involving neutrinos. We've mentioned 80 of the more than 300. The deadline for "white paper" contributions to Snowmass is 15 March 2022, but so far only 3 have been submitted. We will soon switch from reviewing LOIs to reviewing neutrino white papers, which will be found at https://snowmass21.org/submissions/nf.

*** Interesting Letters of Interest; v16 - https://snowmass21.org/

  • Ling-095 "The Coherent Collaboration" This SNS collaboration is exploring new detector ideas for the Second Target Station STS.
  • HarryBool-191 "Scalable, End-to-End Optimizable Data Reconstruction and Physics Inference Techniques for Large-scale Particle Imaging Neutrino Detectors" This discusses Machine Learning challenges and opportunities, specifically for nus.
  • Wissel-064 "Ultra-High-Energy Neutrinos" This discusses >EeV neutrinos, higher energy than those seen already by IceCube.
  • Zsoldos-095 "Antineutrino detection at THEIA" THEIA is a proposed 10s-100 kton water-based liquid scintillator detector for geo- and reactor antineutrinos.
  • Ruiz-118 "Neutrino mass models at colliders in a post-ESU era" This focuses on collider experiments and what they might say about neutrinos beyond the timescale of the 2020 European Study.
