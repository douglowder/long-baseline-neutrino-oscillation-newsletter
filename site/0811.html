Long-Baseline news, November 2008

*** First SciBooNE paper submitted; Coherent pi production

The SciBooNE Collaboration has performed a search for charged current coherent pion production from muon neutrinos scattering on Carbon, \nu_\mu ^{12}C \to \mu^- ^{12}C \pi^+, with two distinct data samples. No evidence for coherent pion production is observed. They set 90% confidence level upper limits on the cross section ratio of charged current coherent pion production to the total charged current cross section at 0.67\times 10^{-2} at mean neutrino energy 1.1 GeV and 1.36\times 10^{-2} at mean neutrino energy 2.2 GeV. arXiv:0811.0369

*** GSI electron capture and neutrino oscillations (?)

A lively session at the 2008 International Conference on Particles and Nuclei was devoted to electron capture results which some interpret as neutrino oscillations. Kienle showed data from GSI on decays from 140Pr, 142Pr and new preliminary data from 122I which periodicity. Stuart Freedman reported on a "Search for Oscillation of the Electron-Capture Decay Probability of 142Pm using the Berkeley Gas Filled Separator" which did not see periodicity. The discussion covered phases, coherence, hyperfine splitting, the uncertainty principle, and the attitudes of particle & nuclear physicists. Also see 0811.1765 http://www.weizmann.ac.il/MaKaC/sessionDisplay.py?sessionId=70&slotId=0&confId=0#2008-11-11

*** Double Chooz veto tank installed

A steel vessel which will hold the inner veto for the Double Chooz experiment has been installed in the Chooz pit and is being welded. In order to position it after welding, it is currently sitting on a couple meters of water, like a boat. It will be sandblasted with 7 tons of sand.

*** Cute kid tests bucket for neutrino experiment

Double Chooz ordered 803 ten inch PMTs which have arrived (in Germany and Japan), been tested and cleaned and are ready to send to Chooz. *** OPERA ends 2008 run

The CNGS for the OPERA neutrino oscillation experiment ended 3 Nov (so that the LHC could fix itself), after starting 18 Jun. They recorded 10100 on-time events, & 1700 candidate interactions in the emulsion target. This corresponds to almost 1 tau expected. In the sample of events located, they have 2 charm candidates with an expectation ~2. The 2009 CNGS run aims to at least double the integrated intensity (1.7e19 pot) and get the 1st tau candidate.

*** SHINE hadroproduction experiment for T2K

Neutrinos in beams mostly come from pi decay and the pi mesons are created when protons hit targets. Thus to know your neutrino beam, you need to know hadron production rates. The HARP experiment at CERN helped tune the K2K Monte Carlo, the MIPP experiment at Fermilab is useful for MINOS. At CERN, the NA61 SHINE experiment is an experiment at the CERN-SPS to measure pi and K hadroproduction cross sections to characterize T2K. A pilot run was taken in 2007 and 50 days of beam were planned for 2008, but the LHC incident caused SPS extraction to the North Area to be stopped on very short notice. Commissioning of new systems was almost completed, but no physics run was taken. They hope to recover the the lost beam time in 2009. http://www.weizmann.ac.il/MaKaC/contributionDisplay.py?contribId=311&sessionId=46&confId=0
