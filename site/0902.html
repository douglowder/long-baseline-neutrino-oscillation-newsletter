Long-Baseline news, February 2009

*** Using MINOS to look for theta-13

MINOS showed its first analysis for numu to nue oscillations, which has sensitivity to theta-13, and a Fermilab seminar by Mayly Sanchez on February 27. MINOS observed 35 events with an expected background of 27 +- 5(stat) +-2(sys). MINOS measures nue-like background in the near detector, but has to extrapolate to the far detector allowing for numu to nutau oscillations. Analysis of 3.14e20 protons on target was carried out with two background extrapolation methods and two particle identification methods. sin^2 2-theta_13 < .29 (.42) for normal (inverted) hierarchy. For some values of delta, zero is barely excluded at 90% CL. MINOS already has another 3e20 pot.

*** Using a high energy nu experiment for low energy physics

A workshop to be held at Penn State will investigate the ways that the large Ice cube detector can be used for low energy physics, such as supernova detection. Ice-cube has a high Energy threshold for muons. http://gravity.psu.edu/events/LowENu_workshop/

*** Using a low energy nu experiment for high energy physics

A new paper by J. Learned et al. explores ways that that a liquid scintillator detector can be used as a megaton scale context for long-baseline neutrinos and nucleon decay. But a predictable feature of any future large detector that uses mineral oil will be a large contingency for the price of oil, given recent fluctuations in commodity prices. arXiv:0902.4009

*** HEPAP hears about liquid argon for long-baseline neutrinos

Bonnie Fleming described the R&D program and plan for Liquid Argon Time Projection Chamber Program at DUSEL for the Feb HEPAP meeting. MicroBooNE at Fermilab, and a 5 kiloton liquid argon detector are the first steps towards the needed big detectors. She covered the challenges, the R&D progress, and the reasons for modular detectors.

*** DUSEL long-baseline neutrino collaboration meets

80 physicists met at UC-Davis to continue planning an ambitious neutrino beam from Fermilab to Homestake SD. There are 3 phases in any project. Phase 1 is irrational exuberance. Phase 2 is work, problems, and overcoming setbacks. Phase 3 is success. This project will need significant funding from both DOE & NSF. 'S4' submissions for both water Cerenkov and Liquid Argon detectors were submitted in January. There is a DUSEL Experimental Development Committee (DEDC - pronounced dead sea). There were 25 S4 proposals altogether 8 non-physics, but many of them neutrino. At DUSEL, there are now 68 full time and 28 part-time employees, plus many contractors on site, involved with pumping, inspections, etc.

*** DOE and NSF to cooperate on long-baseline DUSEL (LBNE)

The 2 agencies are supporting each other to achieve their shared scientific goals. The model under consideration is for the NSF to lead the design, construction and operation of DUSEL, with support from DOE for individual experiments. The DOE will lead the design, construction and operation of the LBNE beam and detector, with support from NSF for elements of both. It is understood that efforts are subject to approval by respective agencies, the administration (OMB/OSTP) and Congress. Each agency will follow its own procedures and policies. For the DOE, this involves "Critical Decisions" CD0 etc. DOE and NSF will work together closely to coordinate their efforts, avoid duplication, and optimize their investments.
