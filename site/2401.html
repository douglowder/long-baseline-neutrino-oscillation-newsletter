*** New results from NOvA on the 2p2h cross section

Two analyses: 1) a sample with low hadronic energy focusing on the muons, and 2) a sample focusing on the hadron sector. The first selection enhances the QE (39.7%) and 2p2h (33.7%). The results were double differential numu CC cross sections near 2 GeV. Predictions tend to underpredict data in some kinematic bins. Two papers should be available on the arXiv soon.

*** T2K resumes neutrino beam running with 760 kW

The Japan Proton Accelerator Research Complex (J-PARC) resumed operations on 21 Nov. On 25 Dec. the MR/NU facility achieved a continuous operation at 760 kW which surpasses the initial design goal of 750 kW, a 50% increase compared to before the shutdown without an increase in electrical power consumption. https://j-parc.jp/c/en/press-release/2024/01/17001276.html

*** (2023) Summer shutdown at Fermilab almost over

The nominal "summer shutdown" for the NuMI (Neutrinos at the Main Injector) beam should come to an end in March and run through 8 July. The cycle time will improve (1.133s to 1.067s), leading to a 6% power increase to 1 MW. It will be an antineutrino run.

*** (2024) NuSTEC summer school at CERN 5-13 June

Aimed at PhD students and junior postdocs, the topics covered will include theoretical modeling of neutrino–nucleus interactions in various energy regimes, neutrino cross section measurements, Monte Carlo simulation, & physics at long-baseline neutrino experiments. https://indico.cern.ch/event/1331901/

*** NuINT24 to be in 15-20 April in São Paulo, Brazil

NUINT24 is the 14th workshop concentrating on neutrino-nucleus interactions. The goal of the series is to bring theorists and experimentalists from the nuclear & high-energy community together to address challenges of understanding these complex interactions. https://indico.fnal.gov/event/59963/

*** SBND receives Operational Readiness Clearance

The Short-Baseline Near Detector's cryogenics system is ready to start the gaseous Ar purge of the cryostat and then to fill with liquid argon. Then it will follow with detector commissioning and observing neutrinos in the Fermilab Booster Neutrino Beam.

*** Neutrinos from Jupiter visible in Borexino?

In arXiv:2401.13043 , S. Ansarifard & Y. Farzan imagine a 1.5 counts/day/100 ton signal from Jupiter in the 7Be flux to explain annual variation data due to the earth's eccentricity.

*** The forest as a neutrino detector

In arXiv:2401.14454 , S. Prohira points out that trees have been shown to be efficient broadband antennas, and wonders if they could be economically instrumented as a radio nu detector for energies above 10^16 eV. It is unexpected to find in the neutrino literature that the average spacing of lodgepole pine trees (Pinus contorta) in the Rocky Mountains is 2-5 m.
