*** NuSAG recommendations and rationale for neutrinoless double beta

In a report released by the NSAC meeting, Neutrino Scientific Assessment Group recommended that the US funding agencies support the search for neutrinoless double beta decay. They found 3 programs worthy of support. In alphabetical order, they were CUORE, EXO and Majorana. Lower priority for MOON and Super-NEMO. "Neutrino-less double beta decay experiments are needed to understand the absolute mass scale of neutrinos and to answer the fundamental question of whether neutrinos are their own anti-particles, that is, whether they are Majorana or Dirac particles. The laboratory precision beta decay experiments are limited in sensitivity to 0.2-0.3 eV. Neutrino-less double beta decay experiments considered in this report may have sensitivity to an "effective" neutrino mass an order of magnitude smaller. Observation of neutrino-less double beta decay requires that neutrinos are their own anti-particles, or Majorana type..."

*** Geoneutrinos, the discovery of non-cosmic rays?

The KamLAND group has been observing neutrinos and antineutrinos since Jan 02 with the Kamioka liquid scintillator antineutrino detector in Japan. Using data collected through Oct 04, KamLAND has detected electron antineutrinos produced by the decay of 238U and 232Th within the Earth, so-called geoneutrinos, for the first time. The number of geoneutrinos detected is consistent with the number predicted by a geophysical model. The radiogenic power from the decay of 238U and 232Th is essential to understand the dynamics, formation and evolution of the Earth. These results are published in the 7/28 issue of Nature. & see hep-ph/0508048 , http://www.nature.com/nature/journal/v436/n7050/pdf/nature03980.pdf

*** High Energy Neutrino Astronomy popular in India

Despite record rains and many cancelled flights to Bombay, 700 Cosmic Ray physicists in Pune heard the latest results from Hess, Auger, Amanda, etc. In the neutrino sessions, there were 6 papers on solar nus, 15 on atmospheric nus, 5 dark matter, 8 muons, 18 exotica and 53 on High Energy Neutrino Astronomy. The latter category featured Amanda/Ice-Cube, Antares, Auger, Baikal, NEMO, Nestor. Surprisingly, there were no papers about proton decay and none about supernova neutrinos.

*** Antares Progress

Antares tested "Line 0" in Spring 2005, recovering the string in May. There were no water leaks, but some optical transmission issues are being studied. The shore station is at La Seyne sur Mer, around 20 km from the ANTARES site. A sophisticated timing system with a goal tzero determination better than 0.5 ns has been developed. Deployment is scheduled for November 2005.

*** Seasonal Variations in Atmospheric Neutrinos

Cosmic Ray Muon fluxes vary seasonally by +- 2% each year as temperature variations in the upper atmosphere cause density variations. Amanda has modeled and measured this effect for atmospheric nus. Ger-ackermann-M-abs3-he22-poster 29th ICRC.
