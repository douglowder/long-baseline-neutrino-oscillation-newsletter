Long-Baseline news, February 1997

*** New DOE official pushes NuMI

Peter Rosen, the new Associate Director of High Energy and Nuclear Physics at the DOE was interviewed for the January 24 issue of Ferminews. After mentioning that his own personal interest had included work on neutrinos, he was quoted to say, "I'd like to see the NuMI project proceed with 'all deliberate speed' as they say."

*** Palo Verde site complete

As of Feb 1, 1997, the low background laboratory at Palo Verde has been completed. The neutron shielding water tanks are in place. The detector is being installed during February and March.

*** Chooz experiment waiting for neutrinos

The Chooz detector is together and has taken several background runs, which look good. They are now waiting for the reactor to turn on. This could be any day now, though past schedules for reactor turn-on have not been reliable.

*** SNO progress

The upper half of SNO's vessel is completed and is now suspended by ropes. The lower half is now under construction.

*** APT chambers for MINOS in CDF test beam

Gas chambers for MINOS, now dubbed "Aluminum Proportional Tubes" are being tested along with a Dubna electromagnetic calorimeter with D0 plastic planes, at the CDF test beam. Sharing the beam with CDF, the Dubna tests are first. In mid-March the 1 cubic meter calorimeter, with up to 25 planes (1, 2 and 4 cm segmentation) will be tested with positrons from 1 to 25 GeV. Results on plastic versus aluminum, proportional mode versus streamer mode and different gas mixtures will be obtained.

*** Why are pulsars moving?

The high peculiar velocities of pulsars (500 km/s) is a long standing problem. An anisotropic neutrino burst due to parity violation in the direction of the magnetic field can explain the velocities if m3 > 100eV. See the paper by Kusenko and Segre.

*** Intersections conference to include 4 neutrino sessions

The 6th Conference on the Intersections of Particle and Nuclear Physics will be held May 27-June 2, 1997 in Big Sky, Montana. There are four parallel sessions devoted to Neutrino and Non-accelerator Physics; organized around the topics "Non-accelerator Physics," "Solar Neutrinos," "Atmospheric and Cosmic Neutrinos," and "Neutrino Oscillation Experiments." The conference website is http://phnxmu.lanl.gov/intersections/.
