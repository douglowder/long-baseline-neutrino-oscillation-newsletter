Long-Baseline news, Special Edition Mid-November 2003

*** APS Neutrino Workshop Study Sat/Sun December 13-14 in Chicago

The APS Divisions of Particles and Fields and of Nuclear Physics, together with the APS Divisions of Astrophysics and the Physics of Beams, are sponsoring a year-long Study on the Physics of Neutrinos. The overarching purpose of the study is for a diverse community of scientists to examine the broad sweep of neutrino physics, and if possible, to move towards agreement on the next steps towards answering the questions that drive the field. The study will lay scientific groundwork for the choices that must be made during the next few years. A public initial meeting of the study will be held at Argonne on Sat/Sun 13-14 Dec. The study leaders are Stuart Freedman from UC-Berkeley and Boris Kayser from Fermilab. An organizing committee has been assembled. Working group leaders are being chosen, and will organize their groups during the workshop. The planned working groups include: ->Solar and atmospheric neutrino experiments ->Reactor neutrino experiments ->Superbeam experiments and development ->Neutrino factory and beta beam experiments and development ->Neutrinoless double beta decay and direct searches for nu mass ->What cosmology/astrophysics and nu physics can teach each other Each working group will include both experimentalists and theorists. In view of the many relations between the topics of the different groups, there will be considerable cross-talk between them. In addition, the theorists will be asked to consider how neutrino properties to be measured can teach us about the physics underlying neutrino mass and discriminate among models, and to consider the relation between neutrino behavior that can be observed today and leptogenesis/baryogenesis in the early universe.

*** Neutrino Physicist participation at Workshop Invited

The workshop will start Saturday morning with a meeting of the study organizers. Saturday afternoon/evening and all day Sunday the goals of the study will be discussed in a meeting to which the entire neutrino community is invited. This discussion of goals will include the organizing of the individual working groups on Sunday. Further details concerning this first meeting of the study, including details on access to Argonne, will be distributed very shortly.
See http://www.neutrinooscillation.org/studyaps/ The working groups will meet as necessary during the year, with the goal of completing their work in time for a final meeting of the study in late May or early June.

*** Neutrinos in 20 years Facilities Plan

The long awaited US Office of Science facilities plan was released by DOE Secretary Abraham at the National Press Club luncheon covered by CSPAN. The report is available at http://www.sc.doe.gov/Sub/Facilities_for_future/20-Year-Outlook-screen.pdf A double beta decay underground detector was ranked as a mid-term priority, 18th overall. A Super-neutrino beam (i.e. proton driver) was ranked as a far-term priority, 21st overall. Not included on the list, but under consideration, had been an off-axis experiment, a large proton decay detector, and a neutrino factory. A liquid xenon double-beta detector had been submitted as well and could be chosen. Programatically, the off-axis experiment can reasonably be linked to the superbeam, though the facilities plan was made, in part, to fit budgets.

*** What the facilities plan means

This is a matter of great discussion and speculation. Secretary Abraham made clear that we need to be flexible in the future, but he also emphasized rather strongly that these were the priorities and that changing them could not and would not be done lightly.
