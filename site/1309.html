Long-Baseline news, September 2013

*** MINOS+ sees neutrinos in the NuMI beam

http://www.symmetrymagazine.org/article/september-2013/upgraded-fermilab-neutrino-beam-back-in-business?email_issue=320 The first long-baseline neutrino observed in MINOS+ was observed 4 September as the NuMI beam started up. They are running with 2.5e13 POT/spill, 275 kW, and expect 2-3 e20 POT exposure in a year.

*** CERN approves neutrino work but not beam

In line with the approved European Strategy, an initial project is starting at CERN, with the aim to provide an effective platform for future neutrino research activities at and/or outside CERN. The proposal SPSC-P-347 (ICARUS-NESSiE) and the expression of interest SPSC-E-007 (LAGUNA) have been conditionally approved by the August 2013 CERN Research Board with the CERN code WA104 and WA105 respectively. Conditionally implies that at this moment in time the construction of a short-baseline neutrino beam is not granted and the focus is mainly on detector technology development and qualification in test beams.

*** CHIPS, its own little nowhere in the middle of nowhere

The idea is a water Cherenkov detector >60 m deep in the Wentworth Mine Pit, which is already in the NuMI beam. They were encouraged to formulate short term R&D plans that could improve prospects for future funding. This week instruments to study water currents will be deployed, using MINOS steel welded together as an anchor. A 3-5 year R&D proposal R&D proposal will be submitted this month to DOE/FNAL/STFC. Its vision is to join up the NuMI and LBNE programs to provide seamless access to highest intensity nu beam.

*** NuSTEC to improve neutrino generators

There is an effort to create a CTEQ-like collaboration including both theorists and experimentalists to study neutrino-nucleus scattering, called NuSTEC (Neutrino Scattering Theoretical Experimental Collaboration). Its 3 goals: 1) neutrino scattering physics studies that could lead to improvements of Monte Carlo generators, 2) organization of workshops, 3) a Neutrino Scattering Physics School aimed at advanced doctoral students and beginning postdocs. A further goal could be the eventual writing of a "Handbook of Neutrino-nucleus Scattering Physics" similar to the Perturbative QCD Handbook CTEQ wrote in the 90's. Contact with ideas/interest.

*** ICFA town meetings to discuss neutrinos

The ICFA Neutrino Panel was set up with the mandate to promote international cooperation in development of the accelerator-based neutrino-oscillation program and to promote international collaboration in the development a neutrino factory as a future intense source of neutrinos for particle physics experiments. They are organizing a series of Town Meetings to consult with the neutrino community: The Americas: 30 Jan - 1 Feb 2014 at Fermilab, Asia: 13 Nov 2013 at the Kavli IPMU, Kashiwa, Japan during NNN13, Europe: 8-10 Jan 2014 at the University of Paris Diderot. http://www.fnal.gov/directorate/icfa/neutrino_panel.html

*** RENO update

With a better estimate of the Li/He background, RENO updated their reactor neutrino disappearance result at TAUP and WIN2013. Using the same 403 day sample previously reported, sin^2(2q13) = 0.100 +- 0.010 (stat) as before, but the systematic error is reduced from 0.015 to 0.012. With 700 days of data so far, they have a rate plus shape analysis in progress.

*** 16 additions to the Long-Baseline Neutrino Experiment

The city of Snowmass is in Colorado, but the study took place in Minnesota. LBNE had its collaboration meeting in Colorado and added 5 groups from Brazil, 4 from UK, 7 from Italy. It went from >90% US to 20% non-US of its 455 members from 79 institutions. While rains which the U.S. weather service termed "biblical" pounded Colorado, LBNE responded by shortening its coffee break. http://www.fnal.gov/pub/today/archive/archive_2013/today13-09-26.html

*** T2K remains off due to May accident at J-PARC

http://j-parc.jp/en/topics/HDAccident20130812_02.pdf A report from J-PARC describes the material radiation leak at the Hadron facility. Due to a malfunction of the slow beam extraction, 2 10^13 protons were delivered to a gold target in 5 ms which is believed to have melted. Radioactive material was dispersed, but it was not realized/reported for about a day. A variety of improved safety measures are being implemented, and better safety for users and local residents is promised. No date is set yet. http://j-parc.jp/en/topics/20130726director_message.html
