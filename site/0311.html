*** Icicle Creek in Washington is NUSEL candidate

A Washington State Geologist and experts at the geotechnical firm Shannon and Wilson were asked to assess geotechnical suitability of 12 western sites. Two sites on Icicle Creek, under Cashmere & Cannon Mts., were outstanding candidates. The former, farther from the Leavenworth fault and somewhat deeper, with a strong bridge and an existing access road, became the top choice. There is the possibility of great cover 7400'; uniform, highly competent rock; and the existence of 2 long tunnels. The utility tunnel for the 8- mile New Cascade Tunnel is unlined, allowing an opportunity to assess the geology and hydrology. Other advantages include low-cost power and good visitor accommodations nearby.

*** Majorana White Paper

The Majorana Collaboration has produced a white paper about their Zero NuDouble-Beta Decay Experiment. The objective is to study neutrinoless 2-beta decay with an effective mass sensitivity <50 meV to characterize the Majorana nature, the mass spectrum, and the absolute mass scale. It will consist of a large mass of 76Ge in the form of high-resolution intrinsic germanium detectors located deep underground within a low-background shielding environment. Observation of a sharp peak at the endpoint is the signal. In addition to a modest R&D program, they show the scope, the low level of technical risk, and their readiness. nucl-ex/0311013

*** MICE (Muon Ionization Cooling Experiment) Approved at RAL

The goal is to realize ionization cooling in practice by showing they can design, engineer, build and measure in a beam a cooling channel capable of giving the desired performance for a neutrino factory. http://hep04.phys.iit.edu/cooldemo/
