Long-Baseline news, September 2023

*** Physics Graduate wins Nobel Peace Prize

https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/peace/ Narges Mohammadi won the 2023 Nobel Peace Prize for her fight against the oppression of women and her fight to promote human rights and freedom for all. She had been the winner of the 2018 Sakharov award from the American Physical Society. This July, the APS Committee on International Freedom of Scientists drafted a letter which the APS Board sent to leaders and judges in Iran, urging the release of Mrs. Mohammadi who is serving a 10 year sentence for criticizing the Iranian judicial system's treatment of minorities.

*** PRL Journal Club on FASER Collider Neutrinos

FASER is a forward detector near the ATLAS experiment at the LHC and was featured at an online and recorded session, which may be found at https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t67u-YoE3nk. They see 153 +- 2 (bkg) candidate neutrino events from the ATLAS interaction region, from an estimated 10^11 neutrinos which passed through the detector. They also tagged 64 million muons coming from the interaction region. These are the first interactions from neutrinos produced at a collider.

*** Antineutrinos fall in a gravitational field

https://home.web.cern.ch/science/experiments/alpha This is as expected, but how to test it has been long discussed. The ALPHA experiment at CERN concluded that antimatter, or at least anti- Hydrogen, falls in a gravitational field the same way as matter. I wonder if anyone has ever had a theory with the opposite conclusion, and if so, what it says about Majorana neutrinos.

*** CERN continues to support the Fermilab-hosted DUNE experiment

https://news.fnal.gov/2023/09/cern-moves-forward-with-support-for-fermilab-hosted-neutrino-experiment-dune/ On 11 Sep 2023, FNAL and CERN signed a project planning document to advance DUNE. Excavation of the large caverns of the Long- Baseline Neutrino Facility that will house the cryogenic vessels, detector modules and related infrastructure a mile underground in South Dakota is nearly 80% complete.

*** Reduced running for NOvA in 2023

https://www.directives.doe.gov/directives-documents/400-series/0420.2-BOrder-d/ There is a new DOE directive regarding the safety of accelerator operations. The Fermilab accelerators will not be allowed to restart until each system is in compliance with this directive. Beam may not return to the Main Injector until late February 2024.
