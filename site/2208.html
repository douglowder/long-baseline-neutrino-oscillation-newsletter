Long-Baseline news, August 2022

*** Hyper-K and DUNE will compete and complement each other

At the LBNF/DUNE-US CD-1RR DOE review, a key takeaway was strong support for the competitiveness and complementarity of DUNE and Hyper-K. "...If neutrino physics is more complex than can be parametrized in the PMNS matrix, this complementarity will become even more invaluable." The status of long-baseline experiments is at https://physics.aps.org/articles/v15/120.

*** LBNF/DUNE and the "CHIPS and Science Act"

This bill passed in both the house and senate. See p 211 https://science.house.gov/chipsandscienceact "The Secretary shall ensure that (LBNF) will provide at a minimum: (i) A state-of-the-art 800 MeV superconducting linear accelerator, (ii) Proton power of 1.2 MW at the start of LBNF/DUNE, upgradable to 2.4 MW, (iii) A flexible design to enable high power beam delivery to multiple users simultaneously and customized beams tailored to specific scientific needs, (iv) Sustained high reliability operation of the Fermilab accelerator complex. The Secretary shall, subject to the availability of appropriations, ensure full operations of (LBNF) starts before 31-Dec-2028.

*** Neutrino collaborators from outside Japan returning to JPARC

JPARC newsletter http://j-parc.jp/c/en/topics/project-newsletter/ A new 2nd horn was fabricated at U of Colorado, and has arrived for the final assembly work. Installation was done in July. Installation of a beam profile monitor was done in June with Canadian collaborators. Upgrade work of ND280 is in progress. Plastic scintillator cubes of the SuperFGD, a new tracking target detector, have been shipped to J-PARC. The HATPC, a new tracking detector surrounding the SuperFGD, showed some HV problems and a solution is being sought. Two Italian collaborators came to work on the renewal of the TPC gas system.

*** JSNS2

Experimenters at MLF searching for sterile neutrinos, are working on the blind analysis of the 1.45x10^22 POT data taken until June 2021. They resumed data taking from January 29, 2022, until May 31, 2022. The next step, JSNS-II, has started. A TDR was submitted and stage-II was recommended at the JPARC PAC meeting. A new detector 48 m from the target, outside of the MLF experimental hall, is under construction.

*** Snowwhite - Snowmass neutrino white papers; v5 -

https://snowmass21.org/submissions/nf

  • "A Gaseous Argon-Based Near Detector to Enhance the Physics Capabilities of DUNE", arXiv:2203.06281, A magnetized gaseous argon-based detector system (ND-GAr) is required for DUNE to reach its full physics potential.
  • "Testing neutrino flavor models", arXiv:2203.06219, We are entering the precision era of neutrino physics. But the question arises “How precise do we want to measure neutrino parameters?”. Flavor models can provide a compelling answer to this question.
  • "Tau Neutrinos in the Next Decade: from GeV to EeV", arXiv:2203.05591, This whitepaper, prepared as a part of the NuTau2021 Workshop, discusses the current and expected upcoming status of tau neutrino physics with attention to the broad experimental and theoretical landscape spanning long- baseline, beam-dump, collider, and astrophysical experiments.
  • "A Next-Generation Liquid Xenon Observatory for Dark Matter and Neutrino Physics", arXiv:2203.02309, A 2-phase Xe time-projection chamber an also study neutrinos through neutrinoless double-beta decay and through a variety of astrophysical sources.
  • "Neutrinoless Double-Beta Decay: A Roadmap for Matching Theory to Experiment", arXiv:2203.12169, This white paper focuses on the theoretical aspects of the neutrinoless double-beta decay program and lays out a roadmap for future developments.
