Long-Baseline news, May 2023

*** Hyper-Kamiokande Excavation of gigantic cavern has begun

The main underground cavity in which the Hyper-Kamiokande detector will be installed is 69 m diameter, 73 m tall cylinder with a 21 m high domed support. When complete, it will be the largest artificial underground cavity in the world. After excavation of a 1.9 km access tunnel, digging the cavern began in Nov. 2022. The Hyper-Kamiokande collaboration aims begin operations using the J-PARC accelerator and beamline upgrades in 2027. https://www-sk.icrr.u-tokyo.ac.jp/en/news/detail/684

*** DUNE excavation is proceeding

Excavation is about 65% complete overall; the central utility cavern has been fully excavated. A DUNE coordination office has been established at Fermilab, and is being led by Steve Brice.

*** Veniamin Sergeevich Berezinsky 1934-2023

The Russian physicist Veniamin Sergeevich Berezinsky, considered one of the founders of astroparticle physics and neutrino astronomy, passed away in April in L'Aquila Italy. https://globalhappenings.com/technology/344332.html

*** Stanley George Wojcicki, 1937-2023

We remember Stanford Professor Wojcicki as a force of nature, and not a weak one. He was the first spokesman of MINOS, which led to the revitalization of neutrino physics at Fermilab. In 2010, his daughter Anne and her then-husband, Google co-founder Sergey Brin, endowed a $2.5 million chair in experimental physics at Stanford in Stan's name. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stanley_Wojcicki

*** Petition to keep Fermilab open

For 50 years, Fermilab has been an open facility, welcoming to the community and research scientists from around the world. That has recently changed. Access restrictions make FNAL one of the harder places to visit and work. As usual, it took the initiative of some of the young members of our field to try to bring reason to the decision makers. A petition with > 2500 signatories so far to do just that can be found at https://www.reopenfermilab.com/.

*** Explanation for Fermilab access restrictions

https://news.fnal.gov/2023/05/from-lia-merminga-accessing-fermilabs-batavia-site/ Fermilab director Lia Merminga posted an open letter about FNAL access on Ferminews. While making openness a goal, the letter implies the difficulties and inefficiencies in working at Fermilab are needed for safety and security. I was disappointed that the letter didn't acknowledge that a heavy burden arising from new restrictions is borne by the international physics community. This is counter to the 2014 P5 advice to, "Reformulate the long- baseline neutrino program as an internationally designed, coordinated, and funded program with Fermilab as host."

*** rant: safety and security arguments

The wearing of badges doesn't provide security. A fence around Fermilab or the prevention of visitors coming on site doesn't keep anyone away from already access-controlled areas. Arguments about safety & security, when they are used as excuses rather than reasons, diminish safety and security, and run counter to the long-run interests of neutrino physics in the U.S. and the world.

*** Retired Neutrino Physicist on Mount Everest

https://www.telegraphindia.com/my-kolkata/news/the-telegraph-meets-a-senior-citizen-from-new-town-who-is-just-back-from-a-trek-to-the-everest-base-camp/cid/1941527 Naba Mondal, 1st leader of the India-Based Neutrino Observatory, made it up and back to the Everest Base Camp, 5364 m above sea level. It took 10 days and covered 130 km. In his thesis a few years back at the Kolar Gold Fields, he had measured atmospheric neutrinos. Does less atmosphere mean fewer neutrinos?

*** EPS award for 2023

The 2023 EPS High Energy and Particle Physics Prize is awarded to Cecilia Jarlskog for the discovery of an invariant measure of CP violation in both quark and lepton sectors; and to 2 of the 3 experiments which measured theta_13 using reactor neutrinos. The latter led to the conclusion that the lepton invariant is much larger than in the quark sector, and favored construction of the new round of long-baseline experiments, DUNE and Hyper-Kamiokande. https://eps-hepp.web.cern.ch/eps-hepp/prizes.php

*** New record power for NuMI beam at Fermilab

A 22 May power record for the Main Injector is 960 kW, by reducing the cycle time from 1.2 s to 1.13 s. The goal is 1.065 s. But the beam is down at the moment due to a water leak. NOvA's total expected beam by the 9 July shutdown is 3.75e20, below the base goal of 4.5 e20. NOvA will end at the LBNF long shutdown in late 2026. It hopes to have 60e20 pot by then.
