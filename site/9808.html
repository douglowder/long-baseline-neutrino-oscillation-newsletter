Long-Baseline news, August 1998

*** Discover of Neutrino, Fred Reines, passes away

Dr. Reines died last week in Orange California after a long illness. Working with Dr. Clyde Cowan, he used the Savannah River nuclear reactor to produce neutrinos and a tank of water as the detector in the 1950's. This won the 1995 Nobel prize. Reines' most recent paper was as one of 121 authors of "Evidence for oscillation of atmospheric neutrinos", hep-ex/9807003 .

*** NGS Report released by CERN

The CERN neutrino beam design effort is called "NGS" for the CERN Neutrino beam to Gran Sasso. The report (CERN 98-02 and INFN/AE-98/05) outlines the proposed facility consisting of a new neutrino beam directed from CERN to Gran Sasso and an underground area for neutrino physics 2 km from the source at the boundary of Geneva airport. The experimental area near CERN with a short-baseline experiment will complement the long-baseline program at Gran Sasso. The NGS draft schedule presented in the report assumes that funding would be available to start the civil engineering construction in October 1998. This is known not to be the case. A new draft schedule, with NGS beam staring early in 2003, is being prepared.

*** Hep neutrinos might explain the solar nu energy spectrum

If the low energy cross section for 3He+p -> 4He e+ nue is 20 times larger than the best (but uncertain) theoretical estimates, then the observed Super-Kamiokande spectrum agrees well with the MSW fits. This is quantified in hep-ph/9807525 . Other solutions are quantified in hep-ph/9807216; small angle MSW acceptable at 7%CL, (8% for sterile nu's), vacuum solution ok at 6%. Best MSW fit for actives is 5 10^-6 eV^2, 5.5 10^-3 mixing.

*** Macro Upward Muon results published

Submitted to Physical Review, hep-ex/9807005 reports that the ratio of the number observed to expected events integrated over zenith is 0.74 +- 0.036(stat) +- 0.046(syst) +- 0.13(theory). The shape does not fit no-oscillations (P~0.1%) The rate fits neutrino oscillations well with large mixing and dmsq ~ 2 10^-3 eV^2, but the shape doesn't fit well. Maximum P is for same dmsq but is only about 5%. P combined is 17%. They conclude the data favor neutrino oscillations but with unexplained structure in the zenith distribution.

*** Neutrino Oscillations and Charge quantization

The charge quantization problem depends on the conservation of the family lepton numbers. Since neutrino oscillation experiments search for family lepton number violation, they also indirectly probe the charge quantization problem. The Super- Kamiokande atmospheric neutrino data explain electric charge quantization provided that numu to nutau oscillations induced by a Majorana mass are their explanation. See Foot/Volkas ref below.

*** Is neutrino mass "a bit bizarre?"

Georgi and Glashow take six plausible "facts" and constrain the neutrino mass matrix to have a form that they find "both fascinating and a bit bizarre." With 1) 3 and only 3 Majorana masses, 2) Little atmospheric numu to nue, 3) large atmospheric mixing, 4) MSW or just-so solar neutrinos, 5) Neutrino masses just large enough to play a significant cosmological role (1% of the dark matter) and 6) no neutrinoless double beta decay. They are led by 5) (the least well-established "fact") to nearly degenerate neutrinos with interesting symmetries. See ref below.

*** What Is Measured in Real Neutrino Oscillation Experiments?

"A neutrino wave incident on a detector has a finite length - and passes the detector in a finite time interval. The square of the wave amplitude at time t gives the probability of detection at time t. But quantum mechanics tells us that the exact time of the detection within the time interval when the wave passes the detector is unpredictable! And measuring this exact time tells us nothing about the wave except that some piece of it was at the detector at that time. So all theories which try to use this time, transit time or proper time have nothing to do with the real world, and their conclusions are usually wrong!" For discussion at NOW98 .
