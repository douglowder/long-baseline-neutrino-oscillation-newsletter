Long-Baseline news, January 2014

*** J-PARC/T2K to restart after minor radiation leak

After a seven month shutdown, J-PARC has written several reports on safety and plans for how to avoid a reoccurrence of a similar accident. The governor of Ibaraki Prefecture has agreed with a plan to restart operations. Beam for the T2K long-baseline nu experiment may start in April, but budget cuts reduce 2014 beam.

*** Gerda joint fit further rules out alleged 0nu2Beta signal

Having ruled out Klapdor's alleged signal at 1.25 10^25 y, Gerda has combined its data (4 strings with 21.6 kg-yr) with IGEX and HdM, to further limit T > 3.0 10^25 y in Ge. They plan 100 kg-y with more Ge, better Pulse shape discrimination, cleaner material in the cables and holders, and to instrument the liquid Ar shield to veto cosmics. 2 nu lifetime is 1.84 +.14=-10 10^21 y. The background model is in arXiv:1306.5084 (& arXiv:1312.0562 ). In Xenon, EXO has 3.5 times their reported data from April 2012, and is working on nEXO, to cover the inverted hierarchy, in the SNOLAB cryopit.

*** Next 0nu2Beta experiments before new NSAC subcommittee

A standing NSAC subcommittee has been appointed to provide DOE & NSF guidance. It is chaired by Bob McKeown. The longest sentence of the charge requests that they assess the science-driven down-select criteria for arriving at the most promising approach to a second generation experiment, including a sensitivity goal that at a high level of confidence, based on present understanding, would be expected to answer the question of the Majorana vs. Dirac nature of neutrino for the inverted mass hierarchy scenario when combined with the results from other experiments that aim at establishing the hierarchy and masses of the three known neutrino flavors. First report is due April 2014.

*** MicroBooNE installs Time Projection Chamber in cryostat

http://www.fnal.gov/pub/today/archive/archive_2014/images/microboone-tpc-13-0430-24D.jpg The cryostat will be moved from D0 into the Fermilab Booster Neutrino Beam, filled with Liquid Argon, and help determine if the MiniBooNE low energy excess was due to photons or electrons.

*** ICFA neutrino panel meets in Europe

In January the ICFA neutrino panel met in Paris and discussed the current state of neutrinos and ideas for European facilities. They included NuSTORM at CERN, LBNO, ESSnuSB at the European Spallation Source, involvement in LBNE in the US and Hyper-K in Japan,and WA-104 and 105 as part of the CERN Neutrino Facility CENF. https://indico.in2p3.fr/conferenceOtherViews.py?view=standard&confId=8974

*** ICFA neutrino panel meets in the Americas

In January the ICFA neutrino panel met at Fermilab and discussed neutrinos again, along with accelerator R&D, Detector R&D, MAP & hadroproduction. LBNE was taken as a given. A session focused on international cooperation for previous and future programs. https://indico.fnal.gov/conferenceTimeTable.py?confId=7865#all

*** ICFA neutrino panel at the end of the beginning

Mimicking Winston Churchill, Ken Long said the neutrino panel was at the end of the beginning. They will write a short report on its three international workshops, and then engage with Lab Directors and Funding Agencies to refine its understanding of the factors that influence the development of an optimal scenario. Then a second report will be followed by a third year of community and stakeholder consultation while preparing a final report to ICFA.

*** NOvA nearing end of far and near detector construction

The last NOvA module has been strung at Minnesota for the (FD) far detector in Ash River, and the final block of the near detector is also in place. Watch a 7 min. video on the FD construction before it is old news. http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KxT529yRw_A ; http://chicagotonight.wttw.com/2014/01/14/fermilab-s-nova-experiment Meanwhile physicists are actively searching for the first FD nu(s) in the instrumented part, currently running in the NuMI beam. It's a narrow band beam tuned to the first oscillation maximum and below nu-tau CC threshold, so many nus have oscillated away!

*** Fermilab PAC considers several Neutrino proposals

http://www.fnal.gov/directorate/program_planning/phys_adv_com/index.html The Fermilab Program Advisory Committee advises the Director on the experimental program. In particular, the committee reviews proposals for new experiments at Fermilab, gives advice to the Director about the optimization of the ongoing experimental program, and about plans for the future program. In January it heard updates from NOvA, MINERvA, LBNE and MicroBooNE and proposals for MiniBooNE+, LAr1-ND (Testing Neutrino Anomalies with Multiple Argon Detectors at Fermilab), CHIPS (CHerenkov detectors in mine PitS along the NuMI beam), and ICARUS (moving it to Fermilab). http://www.fnal.gov/directorate/program_planning/Jan2014PACPublic/PAC_Agenda_Draft_Jan_2014.htm
