Long-Baseline news, September/October 2016

Catching up on notable references ***

*** New Fermilab responsibility at Sanford lab

On 1 October, Fermilab replaced Lawrence Berkeley Lab as the point of contact for the Department of Energy at the Sanford Underground Research Facility (SURF) which is owned and operated by the South Dakota Science and Technology Authority (SDSTA). It runs the underground lab where the Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment (DUNE) will be built. It is also home to LUX and Majorana Demonstrator. The final issue of the SURF monthly newsletter was issued for September/October. It includes a "Brief History of Underground Laboratory Efforts at Homestake" by Kevin Lesko. To keep up on activities at SURF, you should now subscribe to "Deep Thoughts", a weekly one page newsletter at http://www.sanfordlab.org/newsletters

*** J-PARC is back online after the summer/autumn shutdown

The accelerator resumed operation at the beginning of October and the neutrino program (T2K) restarted at the end of Oct. During the shutdown, T2K's first results on CP violation in the neutrino sector were presented at Neutrino and ICHEP. http://j-parc.jp/en/topics/2016/Press160808.html

*** Solar neutrinos, Oscillations or No-oscillations?

The Nobel prize in physics 2015 has been awarded "... for the discovery of neutrino oscillations which show that neutrinos have mass". While Super-Kamiokande (SK), indeed, has discovered oscillations, SNO observed effect of the adiabatic (almost non-oscillatory) flavor conversion of neutrinos in the matter of the Sun. See A. Smirnov in arXiv:1609.02386

*** SHiP, a proposed new fixed target facility at CERN

The Search for Hidden Particles (SHiP_ is a proposal at the CERN SPS accelerator. In five years, 400 GeV 2e20 protons would be dumped on a Molybdenum target. A detector downstream of the target will allow a search to made for long-lived particles with masses below O(10) GeV/c2. Another dedicated detector will allow the study of active neutrino cross-sections and angular distributions. The neutrino detector consists of an emulsion target, based on the Emulsion Cloud Chamber technology fruitfully employed in OPERA. http://ship.web.cern.ch/ship/
and see arXiv:1609.04860

*** Neutrino Exhibit to open at Fermilab 2 December

Artist Ellen Sandor has worked over the past 10 months with neutrino scientists at Fermilab to create artistic ways to show off neutrino research at Fermilab. A reception at 5 pm on Friday 2 December will open the virtual reality demos and displays.
