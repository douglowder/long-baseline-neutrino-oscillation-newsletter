Long-Baseline news, December 2016

*** Updated two detector results from Double Chooz

Before 2016, Double Chooz results relied on Gd selection and Bugey4 to provide effective near-detector-like normalization. The latest DC two-detector results rely on IBD neutron capture in all detector isotopes (H,C and Gd), thus expanding the FD target volume from 8 to 30 tons. The resulting FD IBD rate is 0.150 per day. The new best fit sin^2(2 theta-13) is 0.119 +/- 0.016 which is a 2.2 sigma effect compared to Daya Bay's latest result. A statistical fluctuation is ruled out >5 sigma, hence it is dominated by systematics. The DC systematic error is dominated by a conservative estimate of the proton number (0.012). A dedicated campaign for improved proton number estimation (including high redundancy) is envisaged during DC decommissioning (2018). See https://indico.cern.ch/event/548805

*** Reactor directionality, Combining reactor results for theta-13

DC has also reported the best IBD directionality results and the most precise rate+shape reactor spectral characterization, superseding, respectively, the previous best results from CHOOZ and Bugey3+Bugey4; DC provides now the most precise mean-cross- section per fission (0.564x10-42 cm^2/fission with a uncertainty: +/-1.1%) in good agreement (<1 sigma) with Bugey4. Discussion on the compatibility and eventual combining of results from Daya Bay, Double Chooz and RENO are ongoing. The 1st workshop took place in Oct 2016 (Seoul) and the next will be in mid-2017 (Paris).

*** Funding for LBNF/DUNE in 2017?

In November, our favorite congressman told me that a funding start for LBNF/DUNE this year looked promising. But Congress passed a "Continuing Resolution" through 28 April 17 which put "Science Funding in Limbo" according to the American Institute of Physics. In particular, it wrote, "the DOE Office of Science's request for funds to construct the new Long-Baseline Neutrino Facility at FNAL received support in both the House and Senate Energy-Water appropriations bills. Without full congressional approval of this appropriations legislation, the new construction project (won't) be able to proceed as planned during the course of the CR." https://www.aip.org/fyi/2016/science-funding-limbo-congress-extends-cr-through-april-28

*** NUISANCE package for studying nu cross section issues

Current and future nu oscillation experiments have stringent systematic error budgets which are considerably smaller than are currently acheivable. NUISANCE is a framework allowing detailed studies of nu interaction cross-section models implemented in MC nu event generators. It provides automated methods to perform comparisons of multiple generators to published data & each other. It allows the user to easily evaluate the impact of model parameter variations in the generators with data, or to tune the generator predictions to arbitrary dataset combinations. See arXiv:1612.07393 and/or http://nuisance.hepforge.org/

*** 2016 Review of Particle Physics from the Particle Data Group

Printing of the RPP by the PDG is performed by the lowest bidder. A few years ago, one company bid zero dollars. This sounds like a good price (attractive to the publisher because of the high impact factor), this leaves the PDG with no recourse if printing is late or out-of-spec. This year it is funding cuts which have delayed mailing of the 2016 books till around Feb. 2017. The entire book is available at http://pdg.lbl.gov. For many years I carried around the PDG pocket booklet, but with pdglive so easy to access, I no longer do. Similarly, schools have stopped teaching cursive.
