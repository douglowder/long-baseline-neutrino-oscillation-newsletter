Long-Baseline news, October 2000

*** Water in the Missouri River

The funding bill for the Department of Energy, which includes High Energy Physics, which includes Neutrino Experiments, was part of the energy/water bill, which President Clinton vetoed because of a disagreement about the level of the Missouri River. The bill has since been passed and signed. In the new FY01 budget, NuMI construction proceeds as planned.

*** Nestor in warm water

Nestor is ready to deploy 2 floors (28 pmt), but when the cable was laid this summer by a contractor (ALCATEL), not enough tension was applied and some fibers broke. They accepted responsibility and will fix it soon. Nestor will be a tower of 12 hexagonal floors of 32 m diameter at vertical interval of 20 m with highly sensitive photomultipliers at the corner points (168 for phase 1). When ready, the high bandwidth electro-optical cable will provide capabilities for several simultaneous projects. This is called LAERTIS (Laboratory in the Abyss of Europe with Real time data Transfer to shore for Interdisciplinary Studies)

*** Less Water

Kamioka was a water detector but KamLAND will use scintillator. KamLAND installed the last inner detector PMT in September and started the outer detector in October. The final pieces of the acrylic sphere are in place and the balloon will go in next Spring. If all goes well, good data in about a year.

*** More Water-UNO needs a new name

A proto-collaboration has formed to make a new ~20x Super-K water detector for nucleon decay and neutrino physics. It's been called UNO, UNNO, UUNO, ... but they are looking for a new name. Their first collaboration meeting is December 18-19 at Carlsbad NM. See http://superk.physics.sunysb.edu/uno/ .

*** JHF looking for a new name

The JAERI-KEK Joint Project for a new Hadron Machine in Japan was formerly known as the Japanese Hadron Facility. This project had a review in July and was strongly endorsed. They also recommended that priorities be set since there are many different elements and the cost is high. The entire facility is to be constructed within 10 years, while the first priority is the construction and completion of the accelerators (both 3 GeV at 1 MW and 50 GeV). The decision by the Agency of Finances is due in late December.

*** Chinese water, Long-Baseline from Tokai to Beijing

If built, a good distance for a CP violation study using a muon storage ring at Tokai is 2100 km, the distance to Beijing, where a detector would get 1300 events/100 kt-Yr. One idea is for a Water Cerenkov Calorimeter, using 10,000 tanks, each 1m x 1m x 10m, along with RPC's and magnets. Professor H. Chen, director of IHEP is considering for China to build a large detector for cosmic-ray physics now, later for JHF, and then the nu factory.
