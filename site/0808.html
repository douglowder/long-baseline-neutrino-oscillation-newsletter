Long-Baseline news, August 2008

*** MiniBooNE low energy excess

The MiniBooNE experiment was built to study possible solutions to the LSND anomaly. It found that the LSND excess could not be explained by 2 neutrino oscillations, but had a low energy excess of its own. They have further analyzed their data, including 1) going to lower energy (from 300 MeV to 200 MeV); 2) a refined treatment of pi flux errors from HARP; 3) additional hadronic backgrounds in MC, 4) additional cuts against nu interactions in the dirt and 5) more data. Some of these increase the effect, some reduce it, but the bottom line is that there is still an excess at low energy, more than 3 sigma, that remains a mystery. http://theory.fnal.gov/jetp/talks/polly.pdf

*** NEXT experiment approved for CanFranc (SPAIN)

NEXT (Neutrino Xenon TPC) is a new experiment proposed to the CanFranc scientific committee and has been approved to build a 100 kg gas-Xenon TPC at a pressure of 10 bar. It obtained 5+ million euro financing from a special grant. Gas is a competitive option for neutrinoless double beta decay, since one can observe the two tracks. NEXT hopes to be at the same time competitive and complementary to EXO (also using Xenon). It will be in an initial phase of R&D during 2 years to decide the technology (micromegas, APDs, PMT a la XENON...) and will construct a first prototype (NEXT-10 ) in 3 years, followed by NEXT-100 in 5.

*** Neutrissimos

Neutrissimos are not-so-heavy-neutral-heavy-leptons with potential mass between ~10 keV and ~100 GeV which can mix with the light neutrinos. They appear in many new models for neutrino mass. They may be difficult to observe at LHC, because of low production rates. They could be detected through a shift in the weak mixing angle measurements of neutrino-quark and neutrino-electron scattering experiments. Also, for the lightest neutrissimos, one can search for their direct production in meson decay using the signature of the neutrissimo decay. Francois Vannucci will discussed this search-method at a Fermilab Wine and Cheese 5 Sep.

*** SciBooNE finishes run

SciBooNE's 14 months of data-taking provided quantities of data that will lead to never-before-recorded measurements of neutrinos and anti neutrinos at low energies. These data will increase the efficiency of other neutrino research, including the next- generation experiment T2K in Japan. http://www.fnal.gov/pub/today/archive_2008/today08-08-18.html

*** NOvA FONSI

The NOvA long-baseline neutrino project at FNAL passed its environmental review. It was issued its Environmental Assessment and "finding of no significant impact" or FONSI, which indicates that environmental issues were studied & there are no big issues. http://www.ch.doe.gov/docs/adobe/fermi_pr_on_ea_of_nova_v6.pdf
