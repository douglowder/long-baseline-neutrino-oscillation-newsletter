Long-Baseline news, February 2008

Special Election Issue

*** Neutrino Physicist elected to US Congress - Illinois 14th CD

Former Fermilab Physicist Bill Foster won a special election caused by the resignation of former House Speaker Dennis Hastert. Bill had designed electronics for the IMB nucleon decay experiment which found the first evidence for the atmospheric neutrino anomaly, before dabbling in accelerator design and now politics. He has to run again in November. http://foster08.com/

*** Foster to look at Fermilab Funding

http://www.dailyherald.com/story/?id=150579 "Foster said he also intends to ask hard questions about exactly what was done to the budgets of Argonne National Laboratory and Fermilab `to see if there is some possibility of getting those mistakes rectified.`" Office of Science Director Orbach had told HEPAP that December's funding cuts were not decisions of staff, but of Congressmen that he had spoken with, and he emphasized that a compelling P5 report on HEP priorities was needed to turn things around. Meanwhile the House Science Committee says NOvA should proceed in the following pseudo-sentence: "The Committee also supports the High Energy Physics program moving forward with the planned neutrino experiment at Fermilab and the University of Minnesota until a final decision on the level of U.S. participation in the ILC is made." http://science.house.gov/docs/views_estimates_2009.pdf

On 10 Feb, the New York Times compared the President's budget to Earmarks, citing "$2.1M for a neutrino detector at the South Pole". http://www.nytimes.com/2008/02/10/washington/10earmark.html?ex=1203397200&en=651f84ccd3eee53c&ei=5070&emc=eta1

*** Neutrino Cancer Therapy

During the primary for the 14th CD there had been a debate (which Bill missed) and one question concerned science funding. A primary opponent (John Laesch) commented on how important science funding for Fermilab was, and used as an example that they had "invented neutrino cancer therapy." Usually I wouldn't interrupt a debate, but I called out "neutron". John didn't mind.

*** The Golden Years

At Neutrino '94 in Eilat Israel, the welcoming address was given by the President of Israel, who was celebrating his 70th birthday that week. He asked if anyone else there was over 70, and Leon Lederman raised his hand. Leon happened to be giving the opening physics address, and he started by mentioning that he was so old, he knew the dead sea when it was only just sick.

*** Golden Book for Project X; P5

http://www.fnal.gov/directorate/Longrange/Steering_Public/P5.html At a series of workshops, Fermilab prepared a Golden Book on "Physics with a High Intensity Proton Source at FNAL" featuring Neutrino and other physics for this new project at Fermilab. Whether to suggest this facility in advance of the still-highest- priority but further-in-the-future $21.9B ILC is one of the key decisions before P5, which heard quite a bit about neutrinos at its three meetings http://hepwww.physics.yale.edu/P5/. A neutrino subcommittee of P5 is Peter Fisher, Bill Marciano & Stan Wojcicki. Newsletter readers might remember that Bill Foster had resigned from FNAL in protest of the delay of the proton driver, an early version of Proj-X. http://www.neutrinooscillation.org/foster.pdf

*** Goldhaber-Grodzins-Sunyar Experiment Celebration

http://www.hep.anl.gov/ndk/hypertext/numeetings.html#goldhaber Neutrino Helicity is 50 years old. The 1-day BNL Symposium on 2 May will look back on this experiment and its impact on particle physics, and will highlight current worldwide activities and future prospects in neutrino physics.

*** UK projects ranked; T2K medium-high, MINOS lower

In a programmatic review motivated by a funding crisis (see http://www.scitech.ac.uk/STFCConsultation/comment.aspx and click prioritization) Neutron EDM was among 12 "High Priority", T2K and Super-Nemo among 19 "Medium-high", Auger and MICE exploitation among 12 "Medium Low" and MINOS among 26 "Lower priority" items. "As an approximate guideline, the funding that we anticipate being available for programs in the PPAN area could cover the High, Medium-High, & a significant part of the Medium-Lower categories."

*** Construction of Neutrino facility at JPARC

The Helium vessel of the Target Station (TS) was completed and buildings for utility (NU1, NU2) and loading (NC) are being build around the TS. The 3rd horn system is being built with real length bus bars and support system including remote maintenance system for cables and pipes. Assembly work of the Beam Dump (BD) for the neutrino beam line is started. Underground wall construction of the Neutrino Monitor (NM) pit was completed in Dec. The floor construction will be finished by the end of March.
