Long-Baseline news, September 2021

*** Single-photon production in MicroBooNE

MicroBooNE reported on their first test of the MiniBooNE Low-E excess (LEE) with a search radiative decay of NC Deltas(1232), a single photon with 1 or 0 protons and 0o charged leptons. With 6e20 pot, they measure 27 1 gamma 1p events, consistent with the expected signal of 5 and 21 background. An enhanced signal by 3.2 could account for the MiniBooNE LEE. With a 2-hypothesis test (expected/enhanced rate), they reject enhancement at 94.8% CL. https://microboone.fnal.gov/ Some electron results due 27 Oct.

*** Evidence the reactor antinu anomaly is from 235U flux

In arXiv:2103.01684, Kopeikin et al. measured e yields from 235U which indicated the 30-y old data used to predict reactor nu spectra are 5% high, in a way to almost eliminate the anomaly. See sec. 2.5 of arXiv:2107.03934. The 5 MeV bump remains.

*** New results from BEST on Gallium anomaly in arXiv:2109.11482

Another 51Cr test of the Gallium detectors at Baksan sees 20% fewer events than predicted, but no difference between Ga volumes at two average distances. To fit sterile neutrinos, this requires large mixing (sin^2(2q)~0.4) and high mass (Delta m^2 > 1 eV^2).

*** NuTools Report available at http://nutools.ornl.gov/

This report explores practical roles for nus in nuclear energy and security. It's the product of the Nu Tools study, commissioned in 2019 by the DOE's NNSA & DNN R&D. Among a number of findings and recommendations, it concluded that for most reactors under current IAEA safeguards, the safeguards community is satisfied with the existing toolset and does not see a specific role for neutrinos, but that advanced reactors present novel safeguards challenges and a case for possible use of neutrino monitoring.

*** Interesting Letters of Interest; v11 - https://snowmass21.org/

  • Adams-112 "Machine Learning Techniques and Software for Neutrino Physics", A product of Neutrino Physics & Machine Learning (NPML) workshops will be a white paper summarizing the survey of current R&D of ML applications & necessary resource for their development.
  • Jovanovic-140 "High-Resolution Multiphysics Reactor Modeling for the Antineutrino Source Term", State-of-the-art multiphysics reactor simulation codes could be used to provide a more detailed picture of the reactor antineutrino source to better support analysis and development of models for neutrino physics.
  • Akesson-091 "Electron-Nucleon Scattering at LDMX for DUNE", The LDMX (Light Dark Matter eXperiment) could provide (semi-) exclusive cross section measurements, with detailed information about final-state electrons, pions, protons, and neutrons.
  • Wissel-088 "The Beamforming Elevated Array for COsmic Neutrinos (BEACON)", BEACON aims to discover UHE tau neutrinos by searching for the radio emission from upgoing air showers generated by Earth-skimming tau neutrinos.
  • Peter_Meyers-018 "HUNTER: A Facility for a Trapped Atom Sterile Neutrino Search and Other Studies", HUNTER will mount a search for sterile neutrinos in the 20-300 keV mass range, based on total energy-momentum reconstruction of 131Cs electron capture decays.
