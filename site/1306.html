Long-Baseline news, June 2013

*** NuSTORM gets stage 1 approval at Fermilab

This is a proposal to build a mu storage ring & neutrino beam from 3.8 GeV muons + near & far detectors. It's part muon accelerator development and part sterile neutrino search. The FNAL Program Advisory Committee gave stage 1 approval at its June Meeting. http://www.fnal.gov/directorate/program_planning/June2013PACPublic/PAC-2013Jun-final.pdf

*** LBNE + LBNO

Discussions about joining forces are taking place between the mostly U.S. LBNE collaboration (Long-Baseline Neutrino Experiment) and the mostly European LBNO collaboration (Long-Baseline Neutrino Oscillation). Nobody has proposed calling it LBNI.

*** Daya Bay II evolves into JUNO

A new large reactor neutrino experiment could measure the energy distribution with a detector placed such that L/E was near the solar mass squared difference, corresponding to 60 km from powerful reactors. A plan for such an experiment, previously Daya Bay II, but now called JUNO is 60 km from reactors under construction at Taishan and Yangjiang, west of Hong Kong in China. A 20 kiloton liquid scintillator sphere would have an estimated signal event rate of 40 per day. It would use 15,000 20 inch PMTs. Designs are underway and a decision is expected in 2014-15.

*** RENO-50 evolves, but isn't RENO-47

RENO-50 is a similar project planned in South Korea, which had a June workshop to develop a proposal for a new reactor experiment. http://home.kias.re.kr/MKG/h/reno50/?pageNo=115, They have changed the detector design to 18 kton of unloaded LS with high QE 15000 20-inch PMTs with 67% surface coverage. A candidate site is found at the 450 meter high Mt. Guemseong, 47 km from the Hanbit (old name: Yonggwang) nuclear power plant. They are considering an inclined tunnel to obtain a deeper location for solar neutrinos and geoneutrinos.

*** Accident at JPARC delays T2K run

On 23 May, due to a malfunction of the beam extraction system of the 50 GeV synchrotron, proton beam melted a gold target. Radioactive material then leaked into the hadron experimental hall and some workers were exposed to radiation. Ventilation fans carried some material outside the facility, but radiation dose monitors showed no signatures. All J-PARC facilities have been shut since the accident. An investigation and review of safety practices is being conducted before the facility starts up again.

*** U.S. House and Senate offer language on LBNE

Appropriate committees have language about LBNE that may or may not ever be in a budget. House: "(This) includes $8M for project engineering & design activities of LBNE and its alternatives, $8M above the request. The recommendation includes no funding for long-lead procurements or construction activities for the LBNE project. The Committee recognizes the importance of this project to maintaining American leadership in the intensity frontier & to basic science discovery of nu & SM physics. However, (we) also recognize that LBNE construction must be affordable under a flat budget scenario. As such, the Committee supports the Office of Science's challenge to the HEP community to identify an LBNE construction approach that avoids large out-year funding spikes or to identify viable alternatives with similar scientific benefits at significantly lower cost." Senate: "The Committee also recommends $30M for the Long-Baseline Neutrino Experiment, which includes $10M for R&D and $20M for project engineering and design. Research in neutrinos represents the next frontier of particle physics and this experiment remains a top priority for the U.S. and international physics communities. The Committee restores funding for this project to mature the design, develop better cost estimates, and encourage international collaborators to make financial contributions." House has $12M for SURF; Senate $15M.

*** Hyper-K moving ahead, not tied to ILC?

A June meeting discussed plans for building a large new water Cherenkov detector for neutrino physics and underground physics in Japan. Quotes from the meeting: "It is often stated that we need a global strategy for the next generation neutrino experiments. In the world, we need both Hyper-K w/ ~300km baseline nu beam and a liquid Ar TPC w/ >1000km baseline." and "Both ILC and a big nu detector (Hyper-K) w/J-PARC are priorities. Note that we do not intend to select one between two. We want to push both."
