Long-Baseline news, January 2002

*** Evidence that Neutrinos are Majorana particles

H.V. Klapdor-Kleingrothaus et al., Mod Phys Lett A 16 (2002) 2409-2420, present "Evidence for Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay". A 3.1 sigma peak is found which fits to a best value of the neutrino mass of 0.39 eV and a half life 1.5 10^25 years. See hep-ph/0201231 and an analysis in hep-ph/0201226. Prior data analysis is in hep-ph/0103062. They're using 125 moles of Ge76.

*** Mini-BooNE gets ready to run

The Mini-BooNE tank is being filled with oil (45K gallons as of mid-January) and will be completely full by the Ides of March. The detector should be ready to run by April Fool's Day, while first beam is scheduled for around Memorial Day. A problem with the oil attenuation length turned out to be just bubbles, and went away after everyone's New Year's Eve parties were over (attenuation length improving to about 25 meters.) If the LSND signal is right, they'll get a >5 sigma effect in 1-2 years.

*** Super-K phototube studies; quick restart

Super-K showed a video of a tube breaking test at the HEPAP meeting. They broke 4 tubes on purpose near the bottom of their tank. The video showed the problem (a chain reaction) and one solution (1 cm acrylic bubbles). They plan to empty this month, redeploy tubes at 50% the previous coverage, and start filling again this fall. K2K will run again early next year.

*** JHF2K progress towards proposal

A study group has decided that the proposed neutrino beam from JHF-Jaeri to Super-K/Hyper-K should focus on the off-axis beam for a 5 year run from the JHF. They also have increased the decay pipe length from 80m to 130m for a 40% increase in the peak flux. The width of the decay pipe will accommodate +- 0.5 degree angle in the off-axis-beam. The front detector at 2 km reduces the far/near systematics.

*** Which Bora is the site for Hyper-Kamiokande

It's not the island Bora-Bora, or the caves at Tora-Bora. Ten kilometers south of the Kamiokande in the Mozumi mine, is the Tochibora mine, a possible site for Hyper-Kamiokande, where the rock is better. An off-axis beam from JHF could aim below both of them, and be 1.5 degrees off-axis both sites. The beam would come up in Korea.

*** Which Boring Machine isn't at NuMI anymore?

The tunnel Boring Machine finished its NuMI work December 8. Civil Construction scheduled end is August 2002. Installation of decay pipe scheduled to start late February.

*** SPL

At CERN, there is an idea to use the RF from LEP for a superconducting proton linac (SPL). The SPL could be used to run both a superbeam (numu) and betabeam(nu_e) together. The SPL itself could be staged, with a test of 3MeV H- injector first, then a 120 MeV H- linac in the PS South Hall. A design report with a cost estimate for the linac will be done in 2003.

*** UNO in Frejus?

A safety tunnel will be constructed at Frejus parallel to the highway. This provides an opportunity for a large detector 130 km from CERN (part of the motivation for NNN02-transparencies at http://muonstoragerings.cern.ch/NuWorkshop02/agenda.html)

*** Beta Beams

If you produce a radioactive ion with a short beta-decay lifetime, and accelerate it in the PS to high energy, and store the ion in a storage ring, then nu_e will be produced with single flavor, known spectrum and intensity. NE-18 and HE-6 considered, 15-30K neutrino events ~3/4 GeV at 130 km from CERN in UNO.
