Long-Baseline news, November 2011

*** First data from Double Chooz reactor neutrino experiment

One of three new-generation reactor neutrino experiments for theta_13, The Double Chooz experiment in France presented its 1st results from ~100 days of data at the LowNu meeting in Korea. A preliminary rate + shape analysis of the data indicated a hint for a positive value of theta_13: sin^2(2q_13) = 0.085 � 0.029 (stat) � 0.042 (syst) at 68% CL. Global fits which include MINOS, T2K, KamLAND, atmospheric and solar neutrinos indicate theta_13 isn't zero at more than 3 sigma, though the allowed range is fairly large. Only the far detector is currently on, but half of Double Chooz is not CHOOZ.

*** RENO Inauguration

LowNu2011 at Seoul National University November 9-12 served as the official inauguration of the RENO experiment. In the reactor experiment session on the first day, RENO gave a status report following the first result talk from Double CHOOZ and the Daya Bay status report. RENO has been taking data since August. On the last day, attendees were treated to a tour of YongGwang Nuclear Power Plant and both the near and far detector halls and control room for RENO. It was a long bus ride but well worth it! RENO will have a 3-sigma sensitivity to sin^2(2theta13) of ~0.05 with the data they will collect through March. Their goal is to release results next summer at Neutrino2012 in Kyoto.

*** Double Chooz off and off-off

A couple days after the Double Chooz result, there was a fire alarm which caused a power outage, and Double Chooz was off for about a day. There was no fire and no harm was done. Of more interest, about a week before the box was opened, there was a period of 23 hours in which both reactors were off (i.e. off- off), allowing a clean measurement of the background. So half of off-off is not off.

*** OPERA neutrino velocity paper submitted

In a subsequent test to the original OPERA neutrino velocity measurements, a dedicated CNGS beam had a single extraction of four bunches 3 ns long separated by 524 ns with 1.1e12ppp, or a total of 4e16 pot. 20 events passed cuts and the average time difference was 62.1 +- 3.7 ns in agreement with the 57.8 +-7.8 ns in the original analysis. This eliminates many suspected sources of systematic error associated with the beam. On 17 Nov 11, the paper was submitted to JHEP. arXiv:1109.4897

*** ICARUS looks for one form of evidence for superluminal nus

They looked at their energy distribution and found no deformation from what was expected, in contradiction to the idea of superluminal nus in the Cohen and Glashow model for a weak currents analog to Cherenkov radiation. arXiv:1110.3763

*** Neutrinos and the speed of light

In a water Cherenkov detector, MeV and GeV neutrinos travel at the speed of light, while light travels slower, due to the index of refraction. In loose analogy, one idea is that the dark- energy equation of state in an expanding Friedman-Robertson- Walker universe might cause nus to yield a truer measurement of Einstein's limiting speed. See Susan Gardner, arXiv:1109.6520

*** LBNE beam and detector decisions - half done

To move forward, LBNE needs to narrow the range of options that it is studying. This month, the Project Office, the Beam and the Conventional Facility teams decided the beamline configuration known as MI-10 Shallow would be the reference design for LBNE. This decision came after carefully studying both the MI-60 Deep and the MI-10 Shallow alternatives, including the technical risks and the cost differences between the two. The schedule is for LBNE to reach consensus on a far detector technology choice, between Liquid Argon and Water Cherenkov, before its collaboration meeting in December. http://www.hep.anl.gov/mcg/LBNEdec11.htm Vaia Papadimitriou is the Project Manager for the LBNE beam.
