*** Nishina Prize for neutrino work

T. Koboyashi (T2K spokesperson) and T. Nakaya (Hyper-K leader and T2K member) have received the Nishina Memorial Prize in Japan for "Discovery of electron neutrino appearance from muon neutrino beam." Since 1955, the Nishina Memorial Prize has been awarded annually by the Nishina Memorial Foundation. The Foundation was established to commemorate Yoshio Nishina, who was the founding father of modern physics research in Japan and a mentor of the 1st 2 Japanese Nobel Laureates, Hideki Yukawa and Sin-Itiro Tomonaga. http://www.nishina-mf.or.jp/NishinaMemorialPrize-E.html

*** First CHIPS prototype deployed

The CHIPS collaboration deployed its first prototype detector 60 m underwater in a mine pit in northern Minnesota on 1 Aug. The goal of CHIPS is to develop massive, low-cost neutrino detectors, using lake water as the structural support, and purified lake water as the target volume. The prototype "CHIPS M" took data, detecting cosmic rays and monitoring the attenuation length. The Experience will be used to inform the design of larger detectors. http://www.fnal.gov/pub/today/archive/archive_2014/today14-08-13.html
