Long-Baseline news, May 2006

*** CERN long-baseline neutrino beam start delayed (CNGS)

Scheduled for the 29 May 2006, the start of the CNGS beam commissioning is delayed due to problems with the powering of the PS accelerator magnet system. The AB Department's Power Converter Group is working flat out with Siemens to return the PS's power supply system to working order. A problem appeared on the insulation of the power cables of the rotor of the rotating machine (photo) which supplies power to the PS magnets. To prevent more significant damage to the rotating machine, the AB Department, with the approval of the CERN Management, decided to shut down the PS which had started running on 15 May. Everything is being done to restart the accelerator on 26 June. Presently, the latest DRAFT schedule foresees the start of CNGS on 10 July 2006.

Author of the EPP2010 report gave frank presentations at SLAC and Fermilab where he offered his opinion that Fermilab pursuing neutrino experiments such as NOvA was a mistake when it should be concentrating on the Linear Collider. http://today.slac.stanford.edu/feature/shapiro.ram For the impatient, go to 44 minutes into the program.

Given discussion of EPP2010, he argued that NOvA was lined up as a coherent international neutrino effort as recommended by EPP2010. *** Rob Plunkett elected MINOS cospokesperson

Fermilab's Plunkett was elected to a 3 year term leading MINOS. http://www.fnal.gov/pub/today/images06/RobPlunkett.jpg

*** MINOS back on after Fermilab shutdown

There had been a water leak which caused MINOS to limp along with its second horn for a short time just before the shutdown. The horn has been fixed and is as good as new. Spare horns are being built but are a few months away.

*** Daya Bay US meeting

On June 2nd, the Daya Bay US collaboration invited members of the US Double Chooz and Braidwood collaborations and others to consider joining the Daya Bay Reactor Neutrino Experiment in China near HK.

*** Double Chooz meets in a convent, gets surface facility

The June Double Chooz meeting will meet at the "couvent des recollectines", at Givet (near Chooz in France). This is so the God of Neutrinos will help. EdF recently allowed a building outside the tunnel to be used for scintillator storage, but the lack of U.S. support is delaying the project.

*** Colorado Senate supports DUSEL-Henderson surface facility

On 1-Jun the Colorado Senate Bill SB206-229 was signed by Lt. Governor Jane Norton who was the acting governor of the day. The bill authorizes $20 million for construction of a surface facility for Henderson DUSEL.

*** Super-K is refilling

They have replaced the phototubes missing since the accident. 2/3rds done. Pakvasa's prediction that the next Supernova would be while Super-K was off seems to have been evaded.
