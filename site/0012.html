Long-Baseline news, December 2000

*** Tau neutrino is the particle of the year

Two years ago, this newsletter declared the neutrino to be the particle of the year. In retrospect, we should have specified the eigenstate nu_3. This year the DONUT result that charged current interactions of the tau neutrino have been observed impels us to honor a different eigenstate, nu_tau.

*** Comparing Ue3^2 with numu to nue

One goal of the long-baseline experiments is to measure or limit numu to nue coupling at/near the Super-K value of dmsq. An existing limit from CHOOZ already exists. One point about presentation of these limits is not universally known. CHOOZ limits are usually shown in a two-neutrino plot, dmsq versus sin^2 2-theta_12. Let's imagine that plotted this way, a long-baseline experiment does twice as well after a given amount of running. If you then plot the same two results versus Ue3^2, they are the same. The long-baseline appearance experiment must include another factor of sin^2 theta_23 ~ 1/2.

*** Mass of the wrong neutrino in tau decays

At e+e- machines, one physics topic was to look at tau decays and use the kinematics of multi-pion decays to limit the "tau neutrino" mass. But this was usually done by taking the single event closest to the kinematic limit to constrain the mass, (a few tens of MeV). We now think that nu_tau is an approximately equal mix of nu_2 and nu_3. But for ANY mix, this procedure sets an upper limit only on the LIGHTER of the two mass eigenstates.

*** UNO will be called UNO

At its collaboration meeting in December at WIPP, UNO decided that it will be called UNO, Underground Nucleon decay and neutrino Observatory. WIPP is enthusiastic about the project, and excavation costs are being understood. Water handling issues are the next major study topic. Losing candidates for names included ARMAGEDDON (A Really Massive and Gigantic Experiment Detecting Decay of Nucleons), DIAMOND (Detector In A Mine Observing Nucleon Decays), PLUNGE (Proton Lifetime Underground Next Generation Experiment), and THUNDER (The Huge Underground Nucleon Decay Experimental Rig).

*** JAERI-KEK Project approved

Phase 1 of the JAERI-KEK Joint Project on high-intensity proton accelerators was approved for construction. Although the formal approval of the budget will be made by the Diet next year, the notice from the Japanese Government means that Phase 1 was practically approved: 1) 400 MeV Linac (normal conducting), 2) 3 GeV Proton Synchrotron (PS) at 1 MW, 3) 50 GeV PS at 0.75 MW, 4) major part of the 3 GeV neutron/meson facility, and 5) a portion of the 50 GeV experimental facility. The Phase 1 budget is 1,335 Oku Yen. Completion is planned in 6 years. (1 Oku Yen ~ $1 million) The cost including Phase 2 is 1,890 Oku Yen.

*** Neutrino Focusing

D. Monderen considers astrophysical situations which focus neutrinos in hep-ph/0011386. Beam designers will have to settle for horns, lithium lenses and hadronic hoses.

*** New Energy Secretary neutrino-neutral

Spencer Abraham, the designated new secretary of energy, once sponsored a bill to eliminate the energy department. But he is not the Michigan congressman who during a debate on funding the national weather service in the commerce department said, "We don't need the government; I get my weather from The Weather Channel." http://www.aps.org/WN/WN95/wn092295.html

*** Nuclear neutrino physicists meet, recommend

A group of 104 neutrino physics met and made a set of recommendations: #1 move new solar/supernova/beta-beta experiments through R&D Quickly and spearhead an effort to create a deep underground multipurpose lab; #2 support a neutrino bunker at the SNS beam stop, and #3, theory/computational initiative on supernova mechanism, and #4, support collaborators on projects funded primarily by others. http://arXiv.org/abs/nucl-th/0011014
