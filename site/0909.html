*** NuMI running antineutrinos for MINOS

After running for several years using neutrinos, the MINOS experiment has switched to running antineutrinos. This involved switching the polarity on the horn, and on the toroid magnets of the near and far detectors, to focus negative pions and kaons coming from the target rather than positive ones. There are less negative particles than positive ones, and the antineutrinos have a smaller cross section (by about two), so getting statistics is harder, but MINOS will be able to accurately check whether neutrino oscillation parameters are the same for neutrinos and antineutrinos. It will also help understand other beam issues.

*** Alternating Neutral Currents (III)

An electrical engineer tells me that Alternating Neutral Current is an appropriate description for power. The reason that neutral current is important in power distribution systems is mainly due to safety. If the neutral current doesn't equal the line current in a circuit, then that means that the difference must be flowing somewhere else, most often through ground, but potentially through someone who touches the wrong thing. This is the principle behind ground fault interrupters. They measure both line and neutral currents, and trip the circuit if there is a difference.

*** 4th Project X Workshop in 9-10 Nov; ICD-2

This Meeting will focus on a key element of Fermilab's future. http://www.fnal.gov/directorate/Longrange/Steering_Public/workshop-physics-4th.html The initial baseline design (ICD-1) of the Project-X accelerator complex can generate and handle the beam power required for the long-baseline neutrino program, however the high beam power available at 8 GeV is not readily usable by the rare-decay experiments. Further, the auxiliary accelerator concepts developed to handle and condition 8 GeV beam power for near-term muon experiments does not scale well with increasing beam power and precludes the use of the Debuncher and Accumulator for the anti-p research program. The challenge presented by the ICD-1 in serving the long-baseline neutrino program and rare-decay program has motivated consideration of the ICD-2 alternative design. The ICD-2 is based on a 2 GeV Continuous Wave (CW) linac, and its impact on the Project-X research program is the subject of a report found at: http://www.fnal.gov/pub/projectx/pdfs/ICD-2_Research_Program_Task_Force_v6.pdf
