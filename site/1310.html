Long-Baseline news, October 2013

*** P5

The Particle Physics Project Prioritization Panel (P5) is charged to develop a strategic plan for U.S. particle physics that can be executed over a ten-year timescale, in the context of a 20-year global vision for the field. It has a meeting schedule including Fermilab in November, Brookhaven and SLAC in December. The final report, which will represent the U.S. particle physics community's consensus roadmap recommendations for the next decade, is due in spring 2014 and will be delivered to HEPAP for review before it is officially submitted to the funding agencies. interactions.org/p5

*** The best baseline for a long-baseline experiment?

A study on the optimization of a long-baseline nu experiment for mass hierarchy and CP violation has been presented in arXiv:1311.0212. The authors find that a detector at a baseline of at least 1000 km is optimal for these studies.

*** Reorientation of Project X

Fermilab is reorienting the Project X program to provide > 1 MW of neutrinos on day one of LBNE operations. An affordable project would start by upgrading the linac, develop high power target capability and build a platform for a multi-MW future. Details of this new plan will be presented at the BNL P5 meeting in December.

*** Young Snowmass survey

There was a survey among the young physicists in the U.S. as part of the SNOWMASS summer study, and the results are tabulated in arXiv:1307.8080. LBNE was the experiment that the intensity frontier participants were the most excited about (Figure 35).

*** Neutron lifetime - it's a bit of a mess

The PDG value for the neutron lifetime is 880 +- 0.9 s. This is based on results from the "bottle" method. In contrast, the value in 2006 was 885.7 +- 0.9 s. The "beam" method had obtained larger values. A new determination made by recalibrating a previous beam monitor and using 2005 data gives 887.7 +- 2.3 s. This number impacts the size of the reactor neutrino anomaly. arXiv:1309.2623

*** The significance of significance

Some people treat the notion that we need 5 sigma about everything to increase our knowledge akin to one of Newton's laws. Louis Lyons in arXiv:1310.1284 suggests that other things matter. He says nu oscillations could have been discovered with 4 sigma, (and he wrote the book on statistics.) I think this misses the bigger problem, that people treat a-priori and a-posteriori tests the same. Also, we are trying to measure the mass hierarchy, not discover it. There is a mass hierarchy, with two possible values. A related issue covered in 1210.3651 is that the design of an experiment needs to deal with both its expected sensitivity, and the fluctuations that it is likely to encounter. Also 1310.4007.

*** LBNE 35 ton

LBNE is building a 35 ton prototype detector at Fermilab. The installation work for the 35t cryostat and cryogenics system is now complete. The goal of the 35t Phase I setup is to demonstrate operation with high purity for a system based on a membrane cryostat. The 35t cryostat is the first membrane cryostat in the US and the first cryostat built for scientific purposes. The budget calls for testing and operations for the cryogenics system from the beginning of October to the end of the year.
