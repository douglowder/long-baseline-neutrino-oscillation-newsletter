Long-Baseline news, August 2021

*** Are neutrino mixing parameters energy dependent?

Renormalization-group running of oscillation parameters between the nu-mass-generating scale and the weak scale has been long studied. But to be phenomenologically interesting, new physics associated with light degrees of freedom that couple to neutrinos. Babu et al. in arXiv:2108.11961 consider quantum corrections associated to the virtual exchange of the new degrees of freedom that modify nu production and detection. This can lead to observable consequences in long-baseline nu experiments, such as T2K and NOvA.

*** New neutrino parameters from NOvA

In M. A. Acero et al., "An Improved Measurement of Neutrino Oscillation Parameters by (NOvA)" the NOvA collaboration with a 50% increase in neutrino-mode beam exposure over the previously reported results. The additional data, combined with previously published nu and antinu data, are all analyzed using improved techniques and simulations. Delta m^2_32 = 2.41 +- 0.07 10^(-3) eV^2 and sin^2(q_23) = 0.57 +0.03 -004. See arXiv:2108.08219

*** Cosmology neutrino mass tests

Last month I complained that DES had ruled out three degenerate neutrino masses in a calculation that assumed degenerate neutrino masses. Some cosmologists have pointed out to me that the main cosmological observable is the sum of the masses and a degenerate spectrum should be regarded as an approximation that has no impact given the precision of current and likely future experiments. Sec. 7.1 of the CORE proposal ( arXiv:1612.00021 ) simulated CMB data assuming the normal nu mass order. They show that analyzing the data assuming a degenerate spectrum is a good approximation.

*** Curious referee comments on neutrinos

A referee comment on Ray Davis' 1955 first upper limit on neutrino production in the sun was, "Any experiment such as this which does not have the requisite sensitivity, really has no bearing on the question of the existence of neutrinos. ... one would not write a scientific paper describing an experiment in which an experimenter stood on a mountain and reached for the moon, and concluded that the moon was more than eight feet from the top of the mountain."

*** DOE Budget Proposal for Fiscal Year 2022 for LBNF/DUNE & PIP-2

The House and Senate would provide the requested funding of $176 M for DOE’s flagship Long-Baseline Neutrino Facility and DUNE project and $90 M for the associated Proton Improvement Plan II accelerator upgrade at Fermilab. Both proposals specify that DOE should allocate $30 million for work at the Sanford Underground Research Facility, where the DUNE detectors will be installed. Congress has not weighed in on the uncertainties currently surrounding the project’s total cost arising partially from shortfalls in international contributions. https://aip-info.org/1ZJN-7I349-E29E6Q-4JCWDX-1/c.aspx

*** Interesting Letters of Interest; v11 - https://snowmass21.org/

  • Friedland-085 "Deciphering explosion physics from the supernova neutrino signal", They outline the relevant physics issues regarding the development of the explosion in real time, during the first crucial ten seconds.
  • Shanahan-064 "Nuclear Matrix Elements for BSM Searches from Lattice QCD", The neutrino application is for matrix elements for neutrinoless double beta decay.
  • Gastaldo-198 "Reaching 0.02 eV sensitivity on absolute nu mass using low energy electron capture and beta decay" This LOI is for those interested in the development of next-generation m-nu experiments, particle & atomic physicists, and nuclear chemists.
  • Guenette-086 "High-pressure xenon gas time-projection chambers for neutrinoless double-beta decay searches", The NEXT collaboration claims they can cover the inverted mass order with HPXeGTPC technology. Exposures of 1 ton·year and backgrounds of < 1 event per year in the region of interest are required.
  • Gehrlein-114 "Testable neutrino mass models", A group of theorists list low-scale neutrino mass models and consider the phenomenology of the models that can be tested in a variety of experiments.
