Long-Baseline news, July 2014

*** Neutrino Summit held at Fermilab

A number of leaders of the international neutrino community were invited to a two-day summit at Fermilab to discuss the creation of a new international collaboration to fulfill the P5 goals for a long-baseline neutrino facility (LBNF). This process is the reformulation of LBNE. A next step is the creation of an interim international Executive Board (iiEB) which will appoint some working groups to help define the process and collaboration moving forward. https://indico.fnal.gov/conferenceDisplay.py?confId=8689 Rob Roser from Fermilab and Ken Long from Imperial College London are helping to guide the process.

*** NOvA dedication upon complete construction of far detector

At a ceremony in July, NOvA "cut the cake" to celebrate the completion of the 14 kiloton far detector to study neutrinos in a long-baseline experiment along the NuMI beam in Ash River MN. The near detector is also nearing completion and the first results are expected in early 2015. NOvA has now seen neutrinos in every "superblock" allowing it to check off the conditions for CD4. Then NOvA transitions from a construction project to operations. http://www.timberjay.com/stories/Big-device-examines-tiniest-known-particles,11615 See the progress of the construction, filling and electronics outfitting at http://www.hep.caltech.edu/~bckhouse/anim.gif.

*** APPEC statement from meeting on Large Neutrino Infrastructures

The agencies & lab directors gathered at the International Meeting on Large Neutrino Infrastructures agreed that the understanding of the neutrino sector is a worldwide priority promising physics in a unified theoretical framework that goes from the Electroweak Scale to the highest energy scales. Furthermore, this program is complementary to neutrino related measurements made in cosmology and provides crucial input to the knowledge of neutrinos. They supported the P5 vision proposing Fermilab host short- and long- baseline experiments. CERN recently approved a medium-term plan including hosting a neutrino platform for R&D and prototyping the next generation of neutrino detectors as the main CERN investment to the development of a worldwide program. http://www.interactions.org/cms/?pid=1033970

*** Hyper-K makes top 27, not top 10 (in MEXT draft roadmap)

Despite overlap with a Hyper-Kamiokande meeting in Vancouver, a number of their leaders attended the neutrino summit. They reported complementarity of physics and two large neutrino detectors makes the global neutrino program more fruitful. Previously, the Science Council of Japan had selected Hyper-K as a top priority project (27 chosen out of 192 in all of science.) In the MEXT draft roadmap, 10 of the 27 were selected for funding now, not including Hyper-K. They were asked to improve International Participation, Organization and their cost estimate. The next revision of the roadmap will be in 2017, which does not delay HK. The budget request was planned for 2017, and there is time to address the issues raised.

*** Fermilab Neutrino Division Created

In a reorganization by new Fermilab Director Nigel Lockyer, a Neutrino Division has been established to emphasize the importance of neutrinos to the future of Fermilab. There had previously been a Neutrino Department in the Particle Physics Division. The new division will be led by Gina Rameika. The Division will start small and grow as the short- and long-baseline program matures. https://indico.fnal.gov/getFile.py/access?contribId=9&sessionId=2&resId=0&materialId=0&confId=8765

*** LBNE statement supports P5

The LBNE collaboration issued as statement supporting the P5 recommendations and the evaluation of all options to optimize the scientific reach of a new international LBNF. http://lbne2-docdb.fnal.gov/cgi-bin/ShowDocument?docid=9368

*** JUNO International Collaboration established

The JUNO neutrino experiment will be located at Kaiping, a small city in the south of China, 200 km from Hong Kong. The 20kt liquid scintillator detector will be in an underground cavern at a depth of about 700 m. It will study the shape of the energy distribution leading to the mass hierarchy. The 200+ member collaboration has scientists from China, Czech, France, Finland, Germany, Italy, Russia and the U.S. Yifang Wang is the spokesperson and Marcos Dracos is chair of the Institutional Board. http://www.interactions.org/cms/?pid=1034020
