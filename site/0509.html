Long-Baseline news, September 2005

Many of this months items come from the 8th ICFA Workshop: http://chep.knu.ac.kr/ICFA-Seminar/program.php [click on ppt]

*** CERN plans for neutrinos

CERN is understandably focused on the LHC. The CNGS neutrino beam will be commissioned 4/06; 1st nus in May. The first target magazine was completed 7/05, and horn 1 was renovated and equipped with an automatic water connector. Robert Aymar has a "strategy group" to study options for the future. That group will meet near Berlin in spring 2006 and report to the July 2006 CERN Council meeting. Possibilities, along with ILC and CLIC include a multi-MW proton driver for a neutrino superbeam, a neutrino factory or a beta beam. No significant funding till 2010.

*** Fermilab plans for neutrinos

Neutrinos make up two of the five parts of Fermilab's current and future program: 1.) Tevatron, 2.) MINOS/MiniBooNe, 3.) LHC, 4.) NOvA 1 & NOvA 2, 5.) Linear Collider. The current experiments MiniBooNE and MINOS are taking data and should have results soon. Two scenarios for the future were considered. If the Global Design effort proposes a linear collider which seems affordable, that will become Fermilab's priority, and there will be no proton driver. There are VERY EARLY creative ideas on how to deliver 1-2MW at 120 GeV without a driver. If the linear collider seems likely considerably delayed (decision after 2012), then a proton driver would be proposed next year and ready by 2012. NOvA II refers to a liquid argon detector at the second oscillation max.

*** KEK/Japan plans for neutrinos

KEK stands for the Inter-University Research Institute Corporation High Energy Research Accelerator Organization (I always wondered about that.) K2K long-baseline neutrino project from KEK to Kamioka was terminated in 12/04 with 9.2 10^19 protons on target (pot). The 12 GeV PS will end 12/05. Neutrinos from JPARC/Tokai (T2K) will start in April 2009 at .75 MW. There are several options to increase the power of the beam (see slide 20 of Totsuka).

*** China plans for neutrinos

IHEP-Beijing is working on the Daya Bay reactor neutrino proposal, With the combination of 4 reactors (to be 6 by 2010), and high nearby mountains to provide overburden, it is an ideal site for a sensitive theta-13 experiment. The Nuclear Power Station is happy to cooperate with the project and a site study and tunnel design are underway. Chinese funding agencies agreed to support. Also a JPARC-Beijing long-baseline neutrino experiment is under study.

*** Other lab plans for neutrinos

SLAC is working on the Xe neutrinoless double beta decay experiment EXO, which can tag both the two electrons and the remaining Barium. DESY is working on Baikal and Ice-cube. Brookhaven is studying scintillator for reactor experiments and also a long-baseline neutrino beam to DUSEL in Homestake or Henderson.

*** RENO to measure theta-13 in Korea

In Korea, there are 17 MW reactor complexes at Uljin and at Younggwang. The RENO collaboration (Reactor Experiment for Neutrino Oscillations) is planning 20 ton (fiducial volume) near and far detectors, sensitive to sin^2 2-theta13 > 0.03.

*** Protons needed to make neutrinos at Fermilab

Before MiniBooNe, an estimate of the total number of accelerated protons in the history of Fermilab is 5 10^20. MiniBooNe has taken 6.3 10^20 pot to date and will continue with 2.0 10^20 protons per year during NuMI running. The NuMI/MINOS goal is 7.4 10^20 in 3 years or 25 10^20 in 7 years. The highest number that could be found on a NOvA slide is 150 10^20.
