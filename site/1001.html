Long-Baseline news, January 2010

*** Critical Decision Zero (CD0) for LBNE

The idea for a long-baseline neutrino experiment from Fermilab to the Deep Underground Science and Engineering Lab (DUSEL) got CD0 from the U.S. DOE in Jan 2010. CD0 is also known as Mission Need. The project includes a new beamline pointed at South Dakota and a detector at Homestake/DUSEL. It is currently called LBNE and has a web page at http://lbne.fnal.gov/. It does not include accelerator upgrades beyond those for NOvA, such as Project X. http://www.symmetrymagazine.org/breaking/2010/01/14/ground-breaking-neutrino-rd-gets-government-boost/ For comparison, here is NOvA CD schedule: CD0 in Nov 2005; CD1 in May 2007; CD2 in September 2008; CD3a in Oct 2008; CD3b - Oct 2009.

*** Critical Decisions One and Two for MicroBooNe

With a total project cost of <$19M, MicroBooNe, a joint NSF/DOE project, will be a 100 ton liquid argon detector in the Booster neutrino beam. Their CD1 review is in March and CD2 goal late this year. The MicroBooNE detector serves as a necessary next step in a phased program towards the construction of massive, kilo-ton range, LArTPC detectors. The goal of the R&D program is the construction of 20-kton detector for the Long-Baseline Neutrino Experiment, LBNE. http://www-microboone.fnal.gov/

*** LBNE Project Management Team

Jim Strait from Fermilab has been appointed as Project Manager for the Long-Baseline Neutrino Experiment (LBNE). Regina Rameika from Fermilab will be Project Scientist. The project manager for the new neutrino beamline is Vaia Papadimitrou from Fermilab. There will be a near detector, and two far detector technologies are being considered, and the project managers are Chris Mauger from Los Alamos (near), Bruce Baller from Fermilab (liquid Argon) and Jim Stewart from Brookhaven (water).

*** There is a hyphen in long-baseline neutrinos

Long-baseline neutrino news has come out since 1992. Some experiments, such as CHOOZ, K2K, MINOS and OPERA have been done with a long baseline. They are long-baseline neutrino experiments, with a hyphen, because the baseline is long, and not the neutrino or the experiment. The hyphen never caught on in High Energy Physics. "Most high energy physicists are tall or stoned."

*** Neutrinos in Europe; CNGS; OPERA

During the 2009 CNGS run 3.5E19 protons on target were delivered (to be compared with the 1.7E19 pot collected in 2008). This corresponds to 3692 neutrino interactions in the the OPERA emulsion target. The Collaboration has started a systematic decay search on located events.

*** Neutrinos in Europe; CNGS; ICARUS

This is a 600 ton liquid argon neutrino detector at Gran Sasso in the CNGS beam. There are two 300-ton modules in a cryostat of which one had been operated previously at Pavia. It is now being vacuum checked at 10^-4 torr, and will be cooled down 10 Feb. Filling is scheduled to start 26 Feb and go through 10 Mar. Thousands of CNGS beam events could start in April.

*** MINERvA at Fermilab finishes construction

MINERvA completed its detector construction in January, and as a result has started installation of the last 40% of its detector modules underground in the NuMI Beamline. MINERvA has been collecting anti-neutrino data since early November when the first 60% of the detector was installed, and will continue to take data while the upstream portion of the detector is added. Detector installation is expected to be complete in March.

*** Neutrinos and Life

My new word is enantiomerism. While not in my dictionary, I found it online in a medical dictionary, and it means the relationship between two stereoisomers having molecules that are mirror images of each other. A possible explanation for the imbalance between left-handed and right-handed amino acids on the earth and in the cosmos is given in "Supernovae and the Chirality of the Amino Acids" by Boyd, Kajino and Onaka arXiv:1001.3849. This idea involves the spin of 14N in the precursor molecules from which amino acids might be formed, and the nus together with the B field selectively destroying one orientation of 14N.
