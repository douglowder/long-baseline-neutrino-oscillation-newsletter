Long-Baseline news, February 2011

*** NSF DUSEL not in President's budget for 2012

The Administration proposes to eliminate NSF funding for planning and design for the proposed Deep Underground Science and Engineering Lab (DUSEL) because the construction and operation of such a large, costly, and complex particle physics facility is outside of NSF's core mission. See page 30 at http://www.whitehouse.gov/sites/default/files/omb/budget/fy2012/assets/trs.pdf

*** Pumps at Homestake to stay on

The NSF has found $5M to keep the pumps at Homestake/DUSEL/Sanford Lab on for the rest of the year. The President's Budget has $15M proposed for next year for the DOE to keep the pumps running through the end of Fiscal Year 2012. In the meantime, the DOE, which has stated it is committed to LBNE, has to figure out if it wants to maintain the lab at Homestake and what to include.

*** Marx Committee to study Homestake

The DOE has a new committee, chaired by Jay Marx (LIGO/Caltech) and Mark Reichanadter (SLAC) to help define cost-effective options for using Homestake. 8 scenarios for physics include LBNE-water at 4850 and LBNE-argon at 800 level. The other 6 involve double beta decay and dark matter at 4850, at 7400 and at SNO. The first meeting is 13 April at SLAC with a report due 25 May 2011.

The tests succeeded, but you won't care about that as you watch the videos! http://www.wired.com/wiredscience/2011/02/neutrino-photomultiplier-tubes/ & http://scienceblogs.com/brookhaven/2011/02/pop_goes_the_photomultiplier_t.php

*** Neutrinos for Europe in 2012 to meet in Paris

The 2nd annual meeting of the NEu2012 Network, "Neutrinos for Europe in 2012", will be May 10, at the CNRS Headquarters Paris in the context of the second Annual EUCARD Meeting (May 11-13). See http://eucard.web.cern.ch/eucard/index.html. The main aim of the meeting is to progress in the direction of the road map for long-baseline neutrino physics in Europe, in view of recent developments in the design studies EUROnu and LAGUNA LBNO and in view of the CERN strategy process to take place 2011 - Sep 12.

*** Leak in NuMI target so MINOS/Minerva off

They were currently running in antineutrino mode, but the beam will be off for at least eight weeks, it seems. MiniBooNE runs on.

*** Describing Neutrino Oscillations to the Public

It seems obvious to some people that if I start out with a muon neutrino at my source, and measure a tau or electron neutrino at my detector, that someplace in between it changed flavor. In the world of quantum mechanics, that is not what happened; rather the flavor is projected as part of measurement. We plot oscillation probabilities as a function of distance, but do not say that something happened in between. Popular writers often get this wrong, as in the last sentence on Double Chooz for Symmetry in: http://fermilab-today.blogspot.com/2011/02/fermilab-today-feb-24-2011.html

*** CUSEL opens in China

China's first deep underground lab is now operational for dark matter experiments. The vertical rock coverage of the lab, which opened in December at the Ertan Hydropower Plant in Sichuan China is 2400 m rock. http://iopscience.iop.org/1742-6596/203/1/012028 & http://english.peopledaily.com.cn/90001/90776/90881/7229358.html

*** Beyond Three Neutrinos

New neutrino projects are concentrating on the current 3-neutrino paradigm, and open questions include theta-13, the mass hierarchy, the masses themselves, CP violation, and the Majorana/Dirac nature of the neutrino. But surprises are possible, and there are an infinite number of ways the neutrino(s) could have properties out- side this paradigm. A BEYOND3NU workshop, which will study theory and experiment reasons to think about such things will take place at Gran Sasso in Assergi Italy from 3-4 May 2011. http://www.hep.anl.gov/ndk/hypertext/numeetings.html#beyond3nu Another workshop on possibilities for short-baseline nu experiments at FNAL will take place 12-14 May at Fermilab.
