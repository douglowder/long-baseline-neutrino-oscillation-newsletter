Long-Baseline news, March 2001

*** K2K Long-Baseline neutrinos by the numbers

First accelerator long-baseline publication, 150 authors from 20 institutions, 250 km source to detector, 28 events with 37.8+3.5 -3.8 expected, 10^-3 background, 2.29 10^19 pot. hep-ex/0103001

*** Pontecorvo Prize

On January 19, 2001, in Dubna, the Scientific Council of Joint Institute for Nuclear Research awarded the Pontecorvo Prize to George Zatsepin and Vladimir Gavrin for outstanding contributions in elaboration of the Ga method and investigation of the solar neutrino flux using this method at Baksan Neutrino Observatory.

*** THESEUS anesTHESEUSized

Voting 28-23, MINOS is turning off the Soudan 2 and mothballing it. There was also disagreement about both the desirability and possibility of turning it on again. Summer shutdown is expected.

*** Karmen Kaput

An era of short-baseline experiments is over with the end of Karmen which has stopped taking data on 8 March. With no oscillation excess, KARMEN still prefers a 3+0 mass scenario with no sterile neutrinos. The final results from Karmen2 are due this summer with global KARMEN/LSND analysis expected autumn. Karmen is already being dismantled.

*** MACRO moribund

The MACRO experiment is now shut down permanently as of 19 Dec 2000 15:08 (Italian standard time). Its data taking is now over.

*** CHOOZ choice chastised

The CHOOZ results are central when interpreting the Super-K data. Their early paper Phys.Lett.B420:397-404,1998, hep-ex/9711002 is often quoted, but their second article "Limits on neutrino oscillations from the CHOOZ" contains final and improved results and a more detailed analysis Phys.Lett.B466:415-30,1999, hep-ex/9907037.

*** New NuMass

BNL E952 aims to improve the numu mass limit by 20 (170 to 8 keV). It utilizes the g-2 storage ring as an extremely uniform one-turn spectrometer to compare the parent pi and daughter mu momenta from pi decay in flight. The experiment avoids scattering limitations by transforming the momentum measurement into a position one; the edge of the decay muon radial distribution referenced on an event- by-event basis to the parent pi depends on m^2 of the nu. http://www.hep.umn.edu/numass/

*** No fires planned at Snowmass this summer

A linear guy, a VLHC'er and a muon gal are meeting a funder when a fire breaks out. "Find me a C02 extinguisher!" the linear guy demands. "It is the modern high-tech way to put out fires". "A brute-force approach works better!" says the VLHC'er. "Get me a shovel to smother the fire using sand." "What you need to do is bring me a hose", chimes the muon gal. "It'll take time to connect but water is the best way to extinguish fires." Confused by the options, the funder hands a test tube of baking soda and one of vinegar to the linear guy, a spoonful of sand to the VLHC guy and an eye-dropper filled with water to the muon gal and asks them to all demonstrate their approaches on a small corner of the fire.

*** Is there a connection between neutrino mass and muon g-2?

g-2 depends on neutrino mass, but for conventional choices of mass, that dependence is phenomenologically uninteresting. Ma & Reidel (hep-ph/0102255) show that to explain the apparent discrepancy, threefold neutrino mass degeneracy near 0.2 eV is suggested. Also, interesting mu to e gamma and tau to mu gamma branching modes are predicted. Hisano and Tobe (hep-ph/0102315) discuss g-2 in the supersymmetric see-saw model. & hep-ph/0102304.
