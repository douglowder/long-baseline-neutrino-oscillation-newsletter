*** LBNF/DUNE and PIP-II in FY19-20 budget

According to AIP, funding for LBNF and DUNE is up from $131M to $175M. The estimated total cost of the project is $1.85B. An additional $30M is for infrastructure at SURF. Funding for the Proton Improvement Plan II, needed for LBNF/DUNEâ€™s nu beam at Fermilab, is up from $35M to $60M. That project is expected to cost about $720M. The total of $2.5B is within the June 2019 HEP project guidance for all DOE project costs of $2.6B. https://science.osti.gov/-/media/hep/hepap/pdf/201911/18-Merminga_LBNF_and_PIP-II.pdf

*** PIP-III would come after PIP-II

The 2014 P5 report called for a 1.2 MW beam power upgradeable to multi-megawatt power. Replacing the booster could get to 2.4 MW. A new working group co-chaired by Bob Tribble and Gianluigi Arduini will assess science opportunities and technology choices. Their goal is to submit a white paper before Snowmass 2021.

*** MicroBooNE results

MicroBooNE has published its first PRL, the first measurement of the double differential cross sections on argon. arXiv:1905.09694 They've also presented upper limits on the production of heavy neutral leptons decaying to muon-pion pairs: arXiv:1911.10545 .

*** New Neutrino Review in Review of Particle Physics (PDG)

http://pdg.lbl.gov/2019/reviews/rpp2019-rev-neutrino-mixing.pdf A new review of Neutrino Masses, Mixing and Oscillations now appears in the Review section of the PDG. It is written by M.C. Gonzalez-Garcia and M. Yokoyama.

*** Development of LiquidO, an opaque scintillator detector

The CHOOZ experiment ended when the attenuation length of light became too short. Double Chooz, Daya Bay & RENO succeeded by making Gd doped scintillator that kept a long attenuation length. Now Double Chooz spokesperson Anatael Cabrera is working on how an opaque scintillator & dense array of fibers could be advantageous. https://www.appec.org/news/the-novel-liquido-technology

*** Double Beta Decay APPEC Committee Report for Europe

https://www.appec.org/news/neutrinoless-double-beta-decay-report-from-the-appec-committee and https://arxiv.org/abs/1910.04688 . 4 recommendations involved neutrinoless double beta decay searches being a top priority and the need for support, underground space and theoretical assessment. Two others called for a multi-isotope program at the highest level of sensitivity should supported in Europe and that a program of R&D should be devised on the path towards the meV scale for the effective Majorana mass parameter.
