Long-Baseline news, October 2010

*** LAGUNA Long-Baseline Neutrino Oscillation Proposal

Since 2008, LAGUNA has studied large underground labs in Europe. The next challenge is to understand the costs of the detectors, in order to make a choice. This includes design work for 3 options, (scintillator, H20, Ar), the underground assembly, and operational costs. The LAGUNA-LBNO proposal will be submitted to the next FP7 infrastructure call DS 2011 (25 Nov 2010). A tentative structure:
- Management, Outreach, International relations
- Detector Design - Costs including Underground Construction and Assessment of Large Detector Magnetization
- Detector Operation, Safety and Risks - Costs including Lifetime Operation and Large Volume Liquid Processes
- Long-Baseline Neutrino Beams to LAGUNA - Neutrino Beam Designs and Near Detector Concepts
- Underground Science and Physics Potential
At Neu2012 at CERN the idea to send a nu beam to a LAGUNA site was seen by the community as an "interesting possibility" (see http://indico.cern.ch/getFile.py/access?resId=1&materialId=1&confId=106198). Participation in preparing the proposal is invited. Next meeting at CERN is Monday 22 Nov 14:30(CET). contact Andre Rubbia

*** T2K chooses logo

At ICHEP, T2K reported 33 neutrino events observed by Super-K from 3.23e19 POT, in which 23 satisfy cuts. Beam operation with higher power than 100kW starts mid November. A vote of all T2K members chose a new logo: http://www.t2k.org/news/logo/t2k_logo_medium.png

*** Double Chooz filling

The liquid filling of the Double Chooz far detector is underway. Concentric volumes are the outer veto with scintillator, the buffer with mineral oil, the gamma catcher with scintillator, and the target region with Gd loaded scintillator. The filling has reached the bottom of the target. Target filling involves four liquids at the same time.

*** NOvA filling

Filling of the NOvA Integration Prototype Near Detector (IPND) is now complete. This was quickly followed by the readout of the first cosmic ray muon tracks from the detector. The IPND provides an early deployment of the NOvA near detector at surface level, and a rehearsal for upcoming NOvA far detector integration. Later this Near Detector on the Surface (NDOS) will be moved underground to the same off-axis angle as the NOvA far detector.

*** MicroBooNE won't use MiniBooNE's building

Fermilab decided to build a new building for the MicroBooNE detector, allowing the possibility of continued running for MiniBooNE. Meanwhile, MiniBooNE's possible oscillation signal with antineutrinos is available on the arXiv:1007.1150.

*** The Indian Neutrino Observatory gets green approval

INO suffered a 2 year delay when the previous site at Singara was rejected. An alternative site in the Bodi Hills of Tamil Nadu has now received approval from the Ministry of Environment and and Forests. The project involves drilling a 2.1 km tunnel and building a lab for a 50 kton iron calorimeter with RPCs to study atmospheric, and possibly long-baseline neutrinos. http://www.thehindu.com/news/states/tamil-nadu/article836333.ece

*** Bimagic baseline is 2540

The baseline 7500 km is considered "magic" because the effects of CP violation cancel for both hierarchies and effects of the mass hierarchy are maximized, for all neutrino energies. In contrast, at 2540 km, the oscillation is independent of CP in certain energy ranges, around 3.3 GeV for inverted hierarchy and 1.9 GeV for the normal hierarchy. The implications are studied in arXiv:1011:1146 for Superbeams and arXiv:1009.1093 for Neutrino Factories. This distance matches BNL-DUSEL or CERN-Pyhasalmi. Since nobody knows what tri-bimaximal means, but it is now in our lexicon, "bimagic" works just as well.

*** MINOS/NuMI running antineutrinos again

Although the plan was to run neutrinos for the rest of the year, the NuMI beam was off for the last few weeks to replace the target which had a water leak and a helium leak. That was done and the beam is back up on 1 November running antineutrinos for MINOS and MINERvA.
