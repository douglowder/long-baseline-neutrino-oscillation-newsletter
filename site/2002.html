Long-Baseline news, February 2020

*** Hyper-Kamiokande project officially approved

The Hyper-Kamiokande project has been approved by the Japanese Diet, with operation expected to start in 2027. The project consists of the detector (8.4 times larger than Super-K) and a high-intensity beam from an upgraded J-PARC. The first year construction budget is 3.5 billion yen. http://www.hyper-k.org/en/news/news-20200212.html

*** Can just one neutrino be massless?

We know two delta msquared values and thus a minimum on the sum of the three active neutrino masses (~60 meV). And we know a maximum on that sum (~150-220 meV depending whom you believe). Consistent with that, m1 could be zero. That would have some interesting implications, as discussed at https://physics.stackexchange.com/questions/532896/.

*** First demonstration of ionization cooling by MICE

"Tertiary" muon beams have lower brightness than contemporary p, e and ion beams, but if they could be cooled (reduce momentum dispersion) and accelerated, a search for phenomena at E scales above the LHC might be feasible. The Muon Ionization Cooling Experiment (MICE) has published its results which show cooling two ways: 1) a smaller beam & 2) increased beam phase-space density. https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-020-1958-9 Data matches simulations, validating designs in which cooling is repeated to produce a larger effect.

*** Budget cuts for science in the U.S.; or maybe not

The DOE FY2021 Budget Request, aka "the President's budget", calls for a 22% decrease in U.S. funding for High Energy Physics. Over the last few years, proposed cuts were restored by congress, but this is the 1st step in the process. AIP reports the "anticipated cost of (LBNF&DUNE) grew last year from $1.85B to $2.6B, which is 40% above the top of the cost range (from 2015). The request attributes the increase to unexpected (SURF) excavation challenges and lower than expected contributions from international partners. It notes that should the project exceed (by 50%), DOE rules would require the project to revisit (CD1). The administration proposes to scale back work on the project from $175M to $102M in FY21." https://www.aip.org/fyi/2020/fy21-budget-request-doe-office-science

*** RANT - certain words don't belong in emails.

"Meeting today" is not a good subject line for electronic mail. You have no idea when someone is going to read the email. Likewise on SLACK, WhatsAp, Facebook Messenger, etc. Put words "today" and "tomorrow" in context with a date, day-of-week, etc.

*** Scattering and Neutrino Detector (SND) at the LHC

SHiP has a letter of intent to measure nus from pp at the LHC and search for feebly interacting particles. The detector target will use nuclear emulsion and fiber tracking and the muon system will use scintillating bars. arXiv:2002.08722
