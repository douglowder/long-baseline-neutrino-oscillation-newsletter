Long-Baseline news, July 2013

*** New results from T2K on theta-13

T2K has presented results for electron neutrino appearance with 6.39e20 protons on target, 2.1 times what was previously reported. They observed 28 events, with 4.64 background expected and 20.4 appearance expected (for sin^2 2q13=0.1, maximal q23 and delta 0). q=0 is excluded at 7.5 sigma. A new reconstruction algorithm removes 70% of the pizeros relative to the previous analysis. The T2K experiment expects to collect 10 times more data in the near future, including data with antinus for studies of CP. http://eps-hep2013.eu/press/pressReleases/T2K0719.pdf

*** First results from GERDA neutrinoless double beta decay

There was a previous claim to see 0nubeta-beta in Germanium in PLB 586-198(2004). GERDA is testing this claim with a low background Germanium detector in Gran Sasso. With 21.6 kg-yr of data, they see 3 (0) events in +-2 (+-1) sigma of the expected value, with 5.9 signal and 2.0 BG expected. They rule out the previous claim (aka Klapdor) with high probability. The lower limit on the half life is 2.1 e25 years (90%CL) corresponding to an upper limit on the effective electron nu mass "mee" < 0.2-0.4 eV (depending on matrix elements). http://www.mpi-hd.mpg.de/gerda/public . http://eps-hep2013.eu/press/pressReleases/GERDA0720.html

*** A question for Theorists about the neutrino mass hierarchy

I am asking the following question to a number of theorists and am getting a variety of nuanced answers. Any theorist who does or doesn't have an opinion, please let me know. The next thing we will probably measure is the neutrino mass hierarchy. It will either be "normal" or "inverted". The charged leptons and quarks have what we would call a "normal" mass hierarchy. If we measure an inverted neutrino hierarchy, is there anything we might glean from this, or are all theories agnostic? Would it be surprising?

*** SNO+, a million Isaacs

SNO+ reuse the SNO detector and look for neutrinoless double beta decay, among other things. They decided to switch from loading the scintillator with neodymium-150 to tellurium-130. This has a 34% isotopic abundance, so an initial 0.3% loading will be 810 kg. The ratio of two neutrino beta decay to zero neutrino is 50 times better. But the lower Q-value means that they need to reject some other backgrounds. They now think they can tag bismuth-214 decays using delayed coincidence to the 10^(-4) level. They will fill with water in 2013 and with scintillator in 2014. SNO was held up but they need to hold SNO+ down with a million Newtons.
