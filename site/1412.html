Long-Baseline news, December 2014

*** India-based Neutrino Observatory approved by cabinet

Indian Prime Minister Modi has approved setting up the India-based Neutrino Observatory (INO) in Tamil Nadu. At an estimated cost of $250M, this project aims at building a world class underground laboratory primarily to study neutrinos. Along with setting up the underground laboratory and the Institutional Centre for High Energy Physics in Madurai, IICHEP, they also approved the construction of a 50 kton magnetized Iron Calorimeter detector (ICAL) to study the properties of neutrinos such as the MH. http://www.thehindu.com/sci-tech/science/govt-approves-neutrino-project/article6757147.ece

*** ELBNF meeting 22-23 Jan at Fermilab

Fermilab is preparing to host the first long-baseline neutrino collaboration meeting Thu-Fri 22-23 January 2015. The current name is "Experiment at the Long-Baseline Neutrino Facility" but may evolve as the new collaboration makes decisions. Register at https://indico.fnal.gov/conferenceDisplay.py?confId=9209.

*** Letter of Intent for ELBNF - Sign by 11 January

The deadline for supporting the new collaboration by signing the LOI is Sunday 11 January. The document has been expanded to describe the science case for nus, nucleon decay and particle astrophysics as well as for the 1st 10kt module, ND & SURF. https://indico.fnal.gov/internalPage.py?pageId=0&confId=9090

*** Sergio Bertolucci interim Institutional Board Chair for ELBNF

CERN research director Bertolucci was chosen by Fermilab Director Lockyer to help establish the new collaboration. A schedule for new by-laws, elections, etc. is being worked out by a committee chaired by Chang Kee Jung from Stonybrook.

*** UK 3 year funding for long-baseline neutrinos

The UK funding agency (STFC) supported the future of long-baseline neutrino physics by funding 3-yr preparatory programs for future participation in LBNF and/or Hyper-Kamiokande. The LBNF funding supports 10 UK institutes across a broad research program for LBNF and related LAr detector developments at FNAL. In 2017, LBNF-UK intends to bid for construction resources for the LBNF experiment, including a significant investment in the LBNF far detector TPC.

*** DOE plan for Short-Baseline Experiments

At the December HEPAP meeting, a DOE process for short-baseline experiments starts with community ideas for small projects(<~$10M) at the 4-6 Feb Workshop on the Intermediate Neutrino Program http://www.bnl.gov/winp. Based on that, the DOE may announce a new funding opportunity FOA for such small projects later in 2015. http://science.energy.gov/~/media/hep/hepap/powerpoint/20141208/DOE_ShortBaselineNeutrinoExperiments_Crawford.pptx

*** Is the electron neutrino a tachyon?

It's suggested in http://arxiv.org/abs/1408.2804 which RELIES ON 20th century notation. But...nu_e is not a mass eigenstate. Hmmm.

*** ICARUS moves

The second of the two 300 ton components of the ICARUS detector which ran in the Gran Sasso laboratory in Italy has been moved to the European research center CERN on the board of France and Switzerland. It will be worked on there, and may move to Fermilab to become part of the Short-Baseline Neutrino program.

The DPF has honored the neutrino work of these two ladies.

The current Project Manager for LBNE got a fortune cookie: "Be prepared to modify your plans. Your flexibility is appreciated."

*** MicroBooNE achieves CD4

MicroBooNE, a new liquid Argon calorimeter in the Fermilab Booster Beam, had its CD4 review since the installation of the experiment is now complete. Next is the detector commissioning, being led by Bruce Baller and Matt Toups. Cooldown will take ~ 2 months.
