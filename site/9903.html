Long-Baseline news, March 1999

*** Intel scholarship winner studies neutrinos

Natalia Toro, a 14-year old high school senior from Boulder, Colo. won the Intel Science Talent Search (formerly Westinghouse) with a project on neutrinos. Her project was "Independent Analysis of Evidence for numu -> nutau Oscillations in the Super-Kamiokande Atmospheric Neutrino Data." She wins a college scholarship of $50,000 and hopes to attend MIT. She started nu research with the Research Science Institute at MIT summer '98.

*** Monica bad for neutrinos in UK

Work on MINOS in Oxford was interrupted on 11th March by unprecedented crowds in the centre of town caused by the presence of Monica Lewinsky autographing copies of her book at a local bookshop. But prospects for full funding of the MINOS effort in the UK by PPARC look pretty good. A PPESP presentation went well on 29th March, and PPARC decides this summer. UK responsibilities in MINOS include the rear-end electronics and the near detector.

*** New atmospheric oscillation signature at Super-K

Super-K quotes its R value from 3200 sub-GeV single ring events and 700 multi-GeV single ring events. There are also 2000 multi-ring events. In the two ring event sample, "2-e" events (with no pizero) are 59% nue-CC; "2-mu" & "1e-1mu" events are 89% numu-CC. With just these events, they see a numu deficit, and a zenith angle effect with a consistent value of delta-m^2. The pizero/e Ratio (NC/CC) is 1.11 +- 0.06(stat)+-0.02(MC)+- 0.26(syst) where the systematic error is dominated by cross section uncertainty. This ratio would be 1.00 for numu to nutau, and R(~.67) for numu to sterile neutrinos.

*** Transient neutrino flux?

One feature of the new 201 days of data from Super-K compared to their PRL is 5 or 6 standard deviations away from the earlier data. The overall event rate has dropped by about 12% and the electron rate has dropped by about 4 sigma. The measured mu/e rate in the new 201 days is 6 sigma higher.

*** Palo Verde

First results from the Palo Verde reactor neutrino oscillation experiment were presented at WEIN. With 70 days data, they measure an energy distribution as expected without numu to nue oscillations, and set limits on the mixing of .6 at high mass and 1.5 10^-3 eV^2 at maximal mixing. Signal:Background is 2:1.

*** SNO full

The Sudbury Solar Neutrino Experiment is now full and trigger studies show that at 30 hits, the detector is comfortable at 700Hz. Backgrounds due to U are below design goals, Th in H20 is at the goal, and a few times high in the D20. Some phototube bases have leaked water at a connector, 15% so far, or 4% of the total have some degree of breakdown. That's being monitored. Neutrino data will start in about 2 months, after Rn decays.

*** Neutrinos for the next thousand years

The NNN conference (Nucleon Decay, Neutrino Oscillations and Neutrino Astrophysics) will consider design of a much larger water detector than Super-Kamiokande. It will be held at Stonybrook from September 23-25th. Details will be at www.hep.anl.gov/ndk/hypertext/numeetings.html when I get them. There is a recent link to the meeting on Neutrinos for the Next Millenium, June 10-12 at Hopkins, a workshop to assess recent progress in neutrino physics. www.pha.jhu.edu/events/jhwsh99

*** Neutrino Security at Los Alamos

There have been reports in the popular press that some neutrinos from Los Alamos National Laboratory are missing. Officials are quick to point out that unlike the shortages of neutrinos in Japan, Gran Sasso, Homestake, Baksan and Soudan, Los Alamos actually has extra neutrino events. Nevertheless, security officials are busy with plans to surround Los Alamos with light years of lead.

*** MiniBooNe

MiniBooNe is in the process of preparing a technical design report. They have a Director's review in April.
