Long-Baseline news, December 1999

*** CERN Council endorses long-baseline (CNGS) program

On December 17th, CERN approved the nu program to Gran Sasso by a vote 17-3. The CERN director is quoted "The approval of the CERN Neutrinos to Gran Sasso Facility is a tremendous boost to Europe's strong community of neutrino physicists." The neutrinos will go from CERN below Mont Blanc, Aosta, Allesandria (where they cross the river Po), the Appenine Chain (where they reach their maximum depth of about 8km), Florence (at a depth of about 4 km) and Assisi. 2.5 milliseconds after creation they will arrive at Gran Sasso.

*** ICANOE

The SPSC committee was satisfied with the ICANOE proposal and impressed by the quality and amount of work presented. The realization of the T600 module remains an important milestone. The proposed modular structure allows for useful data in 2005.

*** OPERA

The SPSC committee appreciated the progress made in optimizing the detector design, cost estimates, and schedule. A full proposal is due during the summer of 2000. Data-taking in 2005 with a completed detector was deemed feasible.

*** What will you be doing May 15, 2005?

That's the day data taking for the experiments at Gran Sasso is planned to begin. Over half of the 71 million Swiss francs required for the project, in addition to the equipment valued at 22 million Swiss francs already existing at CERN, are being provided by INFN. Contributions from Belgium, France, Germany and Spain complete the funding.

*** Santa Claus found to have mass

For the second year in a row, the gravitational property of a fundamental object is the main scientific story of the year. Last years's story, the discovery of neutrino mass by Super- Kamiokande, has already made it to a college textbook (Modern Physics by Tipler & Llewellyn). Santa Claus' mass is probably related to the fact that he always accepts cookies, something he was told to do to get his web browser to work well.

*** Neutrino factory meeting in UK

On 7th December a 1/2 day meeting of the UK PP community was held in Oxford to discuss 'The Physics Opportunities of a Neutrino Factory'. The meeting drew a large attendance to hear speakers from CERN, the US and the UK on the status of neutrino oscillations and the physics potential of high intensity muon and neutrino beams from muon storage rings.

*** Another workshop on "Limits" at Fermilab

The CERN workshop on Confidence Levels this month is full, but if you are at Fermilab March 27-28, there will be another workshop, more at an instructional level. Methods for setting limits include the CL_s methods used by the LEP Higgs searches; Bayesian; Feldman-Cousins and modifications thereof; etc. Proponents of the various methods will speak.

*** Is the (atmospheric) nu mass scale cosmologically relevant?

Lesgourgues et al. argue that a 0.07 eV neutrino affects structure formation and microwave background anisotropies provided that a relic neutrino asymmetry exists.

*** Neutrino Anarchy

Does the mass matrix have an ordered pattern, perhaps governed by a flavor symmetry, or is it random? Hall et al, choose random.
