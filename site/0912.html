Long-Baseline news, December 2009

*** New neutrino intensity record for NuMI beam at Fermilab

Fermilab Today reports, "The new record for one day with most protons on target is 13.3e17, set on Dec. 26, 2009. Prior to the current running period, which began on Oct. 1, 2009, the protons on target record for a single day was 12.2e17, set on April 14, 2009. Since Oct. 1, that record has been broken 17 times, including 10 of the 14 days during the holiday period."

*** Neutrino Factory International Design Study meets 8-10 April

The fifth plenary meeting of the International Design Study for the Neutrino Factory (the IDS-NF) will take place at FNAL from 8-10 April 2010. The main goal for the IDS-NF this year is the preparation of the Interim Design Report (IDR). The meeting will focus on reviewing the status of the design work with a view to defining the content of the IDR. The WWW page for the meeting can be accessed from https://www.ids-nf.org/wiki/FrontPage or directly at the meeting page. https://www.ids-nf.org/wiki/FNAL-2010-04-08.

*** MINOS, New cospokesperson, & Deputy & rocks

After serving as spokesperson and cospokesperson of MINOS for 15 years, Stan Wojcicki stepped down in Dec 2009, but will continue as an active collaborator. Professor Jenny Thomas from University College London was elected to a 3 year term as cospokesperson, serving along with Fermilab's Rob Plunkett. Karol Lang from University of Texas-Austin was chosen as deputy. Previous beam results have been based on contained nu events, but one thesis (McGowan 07) studied neutrino oscillations using muons which started outside the fiducial volume including the upstream rock. Such rock event analyses are also being conducted to complement anti-neutrino studies.

*** NOvA spokesperson election & rocks

The term of Mark Messier from Indiana as cospokesperson of NOvA is up for election or reelection, to serve along with Gary Feldman from Harvard. Despite the cold weather up in International Falls Minnesota, progress is being made on the far detector building. One problem that has been encountered is that some of the rock which needs to hold up the buildings' walls has sheared away during the blasting of the underground part. Experts have been called.

*** MINERvA, new cospokesperson; & a hard plate.

Debbie Harris (Fermilab) has been elected to replace Jorge Morfin (Fermilab) as the MINERvA cospokesperson with Kevin McFarland from the University of Rochester. MINERvA is in the process of finishing the installation of its detector, which includes a number of large iron plates that had previously been assembled at the wide band photon lab on the other side of Fermilab. These plates, with a "strong back" attached, are 3 m x 7 m and weigh 6000 kg. They pose a traffic hazard as they extend 1 m beyond the sides of the truck. Orange flags warn other drivers of the wide load. A lead vehicle with yellow flashing lights escorts the flatbed truck during each transport as the truck moves across the Fermilab site. Some drivers chose to pass the truck even though there was insufficient road width to do so safely. If its you, stop it.

*** Long-Baseline Neutrino Experiment, 1st spokespeople; & rocks

The LBNE collaboration for a long-baseline neutrino experiment from Fermilab to Homestake in South Dakota, has elected its co-spokespersons. They are Robert Svoboda from University of California Davis and Milind Diwan from Brookhaven National Lab. Seismic tests and drilling on the Fermilab site started in December to collect geological information relevant for design of underground tunnels for the new beam. The most visible drilling in January will be near Kirk and Giese roads on the west side of the laboratory. At this location engineers will collect soil samples to 20m and rock samples to 220m.

*** 5 year plan for Muon collider/neutrino factory

There is a resurgence of interest in a muon collider as part of Fermilab's future. A draft of the 5-year R&D program can be found at https://mctf.fnal.gov/mapproposal-r2f-mutac-1.pdf A nice article about the possibility can be found in Science. http://www.sciencemag.org/cgi/content/full/326/5958/1342
