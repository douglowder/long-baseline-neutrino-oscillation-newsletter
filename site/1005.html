Long-Baseline news, May 2010

*** Opera no phantom, sees first long-baseline cc tau neutrino

It is not easy to distinguish the interaction of tau neutrinos and to measure enough neutrinos in a long-baseline experiment we need a lot of mass. The LNGS Opera experiment has done both, reporting their first charged current tau neutrino candidate from 1/3 of their data: http://neutrinooscillation.org/operafirstnutau.gif See press release at http://www.infn.it/news/newsen.php?id=576

*** The DED-C is dead, long live DURA

The Deep Underground Science and Engineering Lab (DUSEL) has the DUSEL Experiment Development Committee (DED-C) to advise it. Now it will transform itself into a User's organization, the DUSEL Research Association (DURA). The proposed charter for this new organization is available at: http://dusel.org/DURA.html

*** LBNE physicists meet in Deadwood South Dakota

130 members of the Long-Baseline Neutrino Experiment (LBNE) met in Deadwood SD to plan building large water and/or argon detectors for a new Fermilab neutrino beam as part of the LBNE project. (None of them were shot in the back, as Wild Bill Hickok had been in 1876.) The project includes two 100-kton Water Cerenkov Equivalent Detectors (20 kton of liquid Argon might be 100kton WCE). Physics groups are working on the capabilities for long-baseline nus, short-baseline nus, proton decay solar nus, geo/reactor nus, UHE nus. The project is writing a Conceptual Design Report for a November CD1 review.

*** Daedalus concept for CP violation at DUSEL

Daedalus would use a large Gd H20 detector & build several stopped pi beams. As described in ArXiv:0912.4079 one could use new high powered (2MW) low energy (2 GeV) low cost cyclotrons to search for inverse beta decay, due to numubar to nuebar oscillation. In phase 1, there would be 3 cyclotrons at 20 km, two at 8 km, and one at 1.5 km, using 9.4e22 POT per year. The design could match the sensitivity of an LBNE run with 30e20 nu plus 30e20 antinu.

*** ICARUS full and has tracks

Mythical Icarus plunged to his death. The ICARUS experiment has been more deliberate, & now both 300 ton modules at LNGS are full of liquid Argon. They turned on 12:14 27 May & immediately tracks were seen. T600 is triggered by the internal phototubes. Purity isn't yet optimal, but visible track lengths exceed several 10s of cm drift. The high voltage on the cathode was 40 kV and it is now brought to the 70 kV nominal, corresponding to the field of 500V/cm Both a single vertical track and a large horizontal shower have been observed. The data collection continues at the rate of several events/hour in half of the T600 mass.

*** Leon Lederman's Dead Sea Joke at Neutrino 1994

Neutrino physicists are heading to Athens and Neutrino 2010 promises to be an exciting conference. The President of Israel welcomed Neutrino '94 attendees in Eilat on his 70th birthday, and asked if anyone else was over 70. First speaker Lederman raised his hand, and started his talk saying that he was so old, he knew the Dead Sea when it had just been sick.

*** LSND effect rises from the dead

While the MiniBooNE neutrino data had a low energy excess, their previously shown antineutrino data has a small antineutrino excess at higher electron energy which might be consistent with LSND-like oscillations. See plot 26 at http://www-boone.fnal.gov/slides-talks/conf-talk/louis/aspen2010.ppt Additional antinu data from MiniBooNE could be shown in Athens.

*** Theta13 study with Super-K atmospheric neutrinos

Super-K has combined their atmospheric neutrino data and done a three neutrino study. In particular, they see if there is a distortion of the neutrino flux consistent with non-zero q13. For the normal (inverted) hierarchy they limit (sin theta13)^2 < 0.04 (0.09). Note the missing 2 which affects the number by 4. arXiv:1002.3471

*** Present and Future at Kamioka Laboratory in Japan

A new brochure for visitors at Kamioka describes the research program there, & includes past accomplishments and the present T2K long-baseline neutrino oscillation program. Three future projects are mentioned, 1) adding Gd to Super-K, 2) a future 1 Mton water detector and 3) a 10 ton liquid xenon detector (XMASS). *** Minos, Icarus and Daedalus Preparing for Neutrino 2010, here is a Greek mythology review. Icarus is the Son of Daedalus who dared to fly too near the sun on wings of feathers and wax. Daedalus had been imprisoned by King Minos of Crete within the walls of his own invention, the Labyrinth. But the great craftsman's genius would not suffer captivity. He made two pairs of wings by adhering feathers to a wooden frame with wax. Giving one pair to his son, he cautioned him that flying too near the sun would cause the wax to melt. But Icarus became ecstatic with the ability to fly and forgot his father's warning. The feathers came loose and Icarus plunged to his death in the sea.
