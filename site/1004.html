*** MINOS search for theta13; new limit

MINOS has presented improved constraints on numu to nue mixing with 7.01e20 pot. They observe 54 electron neutrino candidates with a background of 49.1 +-7(stat) +-2.7(syst). The excess is smaller than in the previous (3.1e20) analysis. It is a 0.7 sigma signal or give a limit of sin^2(2q13) < 0.12 (0.20) at 90% CL for the normal (inverted) hierarchy at delta=0, maximal mixing, and delta msquared 2.43 10^(-3) eV^2. This measurement represents the best constraint on theta13 for nearly all values of delta assuming the normal hierarchy and maximal mixing.

*** NOvA surface building complete

The NOvA near detector will be operated at the NuMI beam on the surface (NDOS) for about a year before being moved to the NuMI tunnel. The NOvA surface building at Fermilab has been completed, and nicely complements the MINOS surface building. http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fOz3RyDy7SE

*** NOvA blasting complete; concrete and fishing next

Most of the NOvA far detector building will be below grade. The granite blasting has recently been completed. An area measuring about 50 by 70 by 350 feet was cleared. Next is the pouring of concreted. Minnesota Gov. Tim Pawlenty will recognize the NOvA experiment during the state's annual fishing opener the weekend of May 14.

*** Neutrinos and Entanglement

H. Robertson suggests in arXiv:1004.1847 that the oscillation length for neutrinos from pion decay is off by 1.57. My favorite cocktail party conversation (when physicists are present) is to hypothesize a stopped pion beam with a super-accurate measurement of muon momentum. It is 2-body decay, so in principle could be a delta function. When neutrino mass is taken into account, the question becomes if you see 1 peak or 3? Whatever people answer, it is possible to argue for a long time; the only lesson being that most of us still don't really understand quantum mechanics. Could all accelerator based measurements of delta msquared be off by a factor of 1.57? People smarter than me tell me that when you use wave packets, and not infinite plane waves, it all works out to the usual answer, which must be lorentz invariant.
