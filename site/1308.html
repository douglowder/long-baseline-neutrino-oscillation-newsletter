Long-Baseline news, August 2013

*** New results on theta13 from Daya Bay

At NUFACT, Daya Bay presented a spectral analysis with 217 days of 6 detector running. The rate-only value is sin^2(2q13) = 0.089 +- 0.009. Their spectral analysis gives 0.090 + 0.008 - 0.009. The two-neutrino mass squared difference is measured to be Delta msquared_ee = 2.59 +.19-.20 x 10^(-3) eV^2 which corresponds to 2.54 for the normal hierarchy and -2.64 inverted. http://newscenter.lbl.gov/news-releases/2013/08/21/new-results-from-daya-bay/

*** Snowmass, P5

At Snowmass the community spoke loudly and clearly that Fermilab should have a flagship program and that it should be LBNE, and that it should be underground. The deadline for submission of Snowmass white papers is 30 September. Find neutrino papers at http://www.snowmass2013.org/tiki-index.php?page=Official+Snowmass+White+Papers The next step is a "P5" panel which is charged to "develop an updated strategic plan for U.S. high energy physics that can be executed over a 10-year timescale, in the context of a 20-year global vision for the field. The full charge can be found at http://science.energy.gov/~/media/hep/hepap/pdf/201309/P5_Charge_2013.pdf The report is due 1 May 2014.

*** Super-Kamiokande ten year plan

The Japanese funding agency MEXT gave Super-K one day to come up with a ten year plan. They have lots of plans so this was easy. They will improve the water system and load Gadolinium for the supernova relic neutrino system (GadZOOKs). The will upgrade the DAQ for continuous observation of nearby Supernova neutrino bursts, upgrade J-PARC for measurements of CP violation, study the mass hierarchy with atmospheric neutrinos, and search for sterile neutrinos, nucleon decay and dark matter.

*** NOvA beam starts up

After a long accelerator shutdown, the first bit of beam has started in the NuMI beamline. The first neutrinos have gone through the continuously expanding NOvA far detector, and a few have been detected in the Near Detector on the Surface (NDOS). http://www.fnal.gov/pub/today/archive/archive_2013/today13-09-05.html The near detector is under construction and the mu catcher is in. http://www.fnal.gov/pub/today/archive/archive_2013/today13-08-06.html

*** Reactor neutrino anomaly update, CeLAND and SOX

The reactor neutrino anomaly now stands as an observed to predicted average event ratio for 10-100 m distant detectors of 0.936 +- 0.024 or 2.7 sigma. It could be tested with a strong source in a scintillator detector. There are plans for CeLAND, a Cerium 144 source to placed in KamLAND. At Borexino, SOX-A would use an external Chromium 51 source, SOX-B an external Ce source and SOX-C an internal CE source.

*** Global fit to short-baseline oscillation experiments

Giunti et al. present the results of a global analysis of 7 short-baseline experiments, all the reactor experiments, and constraints for muon neutrino disappearance, in arXiv:1308.5288 . The case of no oscillations is disfavored at six sigma.

*** Why is this a hint and not a discovery of sterile nus?

Physicists confront their data with a-priori tests and a-posteriori tests. They do the former when they know what to look for; the latter when they see something unexpected. Both need to be done. The calculations are identical. The meaning is very different. Failure to make this distinction has led to the 5 sigma standard which I don't like.

*** Muon to e gamma update, The MEG Experiment

They reported a new limit B(mu-e gamma) < 5.7 10^(-13). An upgrade has been approved by PSI and the funding agencies which should reach a sensitivity of 5 10^(-14) with a larger Xenon volume, higher intensity and better position resolution, starting 2016.

*** Pontecorvo turns 100

In August the neutrino world celebrated the 100th birthday of Bruno Pontecorvo, who first suggested neutrino to antineutrino oscillations. It is followed by celebrations at meetings in Moscow, Dubna, Rome and Pisa. A 59' movie has been produced, "The history of Bruno Pontecorvo" by G. Mussardo & D. Cenetiempo. http://www.pilgrimfilm.it/pontecorvo_eng.htm#/intro
