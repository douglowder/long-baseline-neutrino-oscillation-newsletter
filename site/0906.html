*** SNO done, and SNO+ getting ready

The Sudbury Neutrino Observatory used its heavy water to measure (with a combined fit) theta-12 (34.4 +- 1.3 degrees) and Delta m^2 (7.59 e-5+-0.2 eV^2). arXiv:0905.4907 It didn't want to lose any D20 and it didn't lose any. I has given it all back to the AECL and it gave it back cleaner. But they kept everything else (acrylic, tubes, ...) Next a new collaboration will fill the detector with liquid scintillator. By dissolving 150 Neodymium, they can look for neutrinoless double beta decay. There are also reactor neutrino, geoneutrino and solar neutrino capabilities. http://snoplus.phy.queensu.ca/

*** Daya Bay Reactor Nu Experiment getting ready

One attraction of the Daya Bay Reactor Neutrino Experiment is the nearby large mountain which can provide a large overburden, and hence low potential backgrounds for a theta-13 search. The Daya Bay tunnels from two directions have joined up, and the civil construction is continuing towards the region for the far detector. The detectors will be built in a nearby lab and moved into the mountain in pairs. This moving of the detectors is independent of the idea to later move the detectors again by swapping them to reduce systematic errors. Some outsiders are skeptical that moving these triple volume detectors while filled with liquid can be reliably done. But the alternative plan of building the detectors in place and moving the mountain was rejected, and the idea of moving the reactors isn't practical either. Another neutrino lab will be built in China at Jinping Mountain in Sichuan Province. http://www.sciencemag.org/cgi/content/full/324/5932/1246

*** MINOS getting ready to switch to antineutrinos

The Fermilab NuMI beam is currently in a shutdown that is scheduled to end in September. The MINOS project will switch the sign of the horn and magnets in order to run with antineutrinos. This will allow MINOS to quantitatively check if Delta msquared is the same for neutrinos and antineutrinos.

*** New DOE official asks, "Why do neutrinos?"

William Brinkman, a senior research physicist in the Department of Physics at Princeton University, has been confirmed by the U.S. Senate as director of the Office of Science in the U.S. Department of Energy. The office is the single largest supporter of basic research in the physical sciences in the United States, providing more than 40 percent of total funding in this vital area. It oversees -- and is the principal federal funding agency of -- the nation's research programs in high-energy physics, nuclear physics and fusion energy sciences. The Office of Science also manages 10 national laboratories. His first question to the HEP community involved the neutrino sector, which is the key to future facilities in the last P5 report (Project X, new beamline, DUSEL, megaton scale detector.) A group of Fermilab administrators and theorists visited him and explained the rationale for neutrino research in the U.S.

*** Neutrino Predictions from String Theory (F-theory GUTs)

With string theory and two simple assumptions, a no-free-parameter first-order estimates can be made of the quark and lepton mixing matrices. The assumptions - 1) A GUT exists & 2) Particle physics is not changed in the absence of gravity (the Planck mass). The only parameter is alpha-GUT=0.04, determined from running the measured coupling constants to the GUT scale. Predicts CKM elements well; normal hierarchy, dmsq_32 = 30X dmsq_21 (data 31+-2); m1=0.002eV; q-23=q-12=27 deg (data 34+-1 and 45+-7) and q-13=12 deg. It also predicts delta. arXiv:0811.2417 ; arXiv:0904.1419 ; ArXiV:0904.3101 And a 10-100 MeV gravitino is the dark matter. arXiv:0906.0581
