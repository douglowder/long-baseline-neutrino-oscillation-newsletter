Long-Baseline news, January 2019

*** Pontecorvo School, September in Romania

The VIII Pontecorvo Neutrino Physics School will be in Sinaia, a beautiful mountain resort in Romania, 1-10 September 2019. The program of the School will cover modern topics of neutrino physics including neutrino experiments, phenomenology and theory. http://theor.jinr.ru/~neutrino19/

*** International Neutrino Summer School, August at Fermilab

The 12th International Neutrino Summer School will be held 5-16 Aug 2019 at FNAL. Information about applications, scholarships, curriculum, lodging, travel, & fees can be found at http://indico.fnal.gov/e/inss2019. The deadline for early acceptance is 17 Feb. Applicants who require a visa invitation letter should apply early. The final deadline is 30 April.

*** Baksan School, April in Baksan

The 16th Baksan school on astroparticle physics will take place 10-18 April 2019. It will be in the Baksan Valley, close to the neutrino observatory and Mt. Elbrus. The format will be “more a school, less a conference" with respect to previous meetings. http://inr.ac.ru/~school

*** Invisibles School, May at Canfranc

The Invisibles19 School will be 3-7 June at the Laboratorio Subterráneo de Canfranc in Huesca, Spain. It precedes the Invisibles19 Workshop in Valencia. Applications through 1 April. https://congresos.adeituv.es/school-invisibles-2019/ficha.en.html

*** Supernova workshop in May in Trento Italy

"SN Neutrinos at the Crossroads: Astrophysics, Oscillations and Detection" will be held at ECT* Trento 13-17 May, 2019 http://www.ectstar.eu/node/4437

*** NuFACT in Daegu Korea to b 26-31 August 2019

NuFACT2019, the 21st International Workshop on Neutrinos from Accelerators will be held 26-31 August in Daegu Korea. http://nufact2019.knu.ac.kr/ Working groups on muons, neutrino oscillations, accelerators, neutrino interactions and beyond PMNS will organize sessions. This year there will be an additional emphasis on advanced detectors and advanced analysis methods.

*** Neutrinos needed at Fermilab

The annual FNAL Family Open House will take place on Sun. 10 Feb. Fermilab is planning -- the Great Neutrino Hunt! Upon entrance, visitors will be given "detector cards" containing hints to spot the different kind of neutrinos disguised in the crowd. Once all the three types of neutrinos are found, the visitor will return the detector card and enter a raffle. Neutrino "volunteers will mingle with the crowd wearing a distinctive prop and be ready to answer the visitor's questions. Contact [email protected] for info.

*** Tau neutrino appearance in IceCube DeepCore

A challenge in finding tau neutrino appearance in atmospheric neutrino detectors is that one is looking for an excess of events that look like taus in the angular region where there is a deficit of numu events due to oscillations. IceCube excludes the absence of nutau appearance at 3.2 sigma in arXiv:1901.05366

*** Neutrino mistakes

In my article on neutrino mistakes for the nu history workshop, arXiv:1901.07068, I playfully attributed the increasing evidence for the normal mass order as god's mistake. New experiments on neutrinoless double beta decay are targeting the region suggested by the inverted mass order. But I added, "The more likely answer is that the normal mass order is just what we want and we aren’t intelligent enough to realize why yet." I have been pointed to arXiv:1608.01618 which points out that if the order is normal, we can have a chance to simultaneously pin down the two Majorana CP phases which is not possible if it's inverted.
