*** SURF is Underground

The Sanford Underground Research Facility, or SURF, or Sanford Lab, was officially dedicated 31 May. It hosts the LUX search for dark matter, and the Majorana Demonstrator project for neutrinoless double beta decay. The Yates shaft is now reopened for routine use to the 4850 level at SURF for the 1st time since 2003. http://www.symmetrymagazine.org/breaking/2012/05/31/ , https://docs.sanfordlab.org/docushare/dsweb/Get/Document-84363/

*** LBNE collaboration requests underground option

After being told that the LBNE reconfiguration committee was considering two surface options for LBNE, the collaboration executive committee and institutional board each passed unanimous statements asking that an underground option be included. One was. A report to the DOE is due before 1 Jul.

*** LBNE reconfiguration; Surface/Surface/Underground

The Long-Baseline Neutrino Experiment Reconfiguration subcommittee was tasked with finding viable options (i.e. <$700M) for a phase 1 physics program. Three options now being considered are a 30 kton Ash River surface liquid Argon (LA)detector in the NuMI beam, a 10 kton LA detector on the surface at Homestake with a new beam, and a 15 kton LA detector underground at Soudan using NuMI.

*** Pros and cons of LBNE options

The largest detector (30 kton) gives the best CP sensitivity. The longer-baseline option gives mass ordering reach for the full delta range, (though the mass ordering may be known before LBNE starts.) The underground option has the broadest phase 1 physics program including accelerator and non-accelerator physics.

*** Backgrounds for LBNE on the surface

A 10 kton surface liquid Argon detector with a 1.5 ms drift time will have about 3/4 billion muons traversing it during drift times associated with beam spills compared to 90 nue appearance events. Potential backgrounds come from em showers caused by muons, most of which are seen, but some of them aren't. Even if the muon is seen, the strategy for dealing with possible backgrounds is not trivial. Some people are worried about this; others are not.

*** Global fit to new data including theta_13

G. Fogli et al performed a global analysis of neutrino oscillation data in arXiv:1205.5254 , "...entering the era of leptonic CP violation searches". They find interesting indications for theta_23 < pi/4, possible hints for delta ~ pi, with no significant difference between the normal and inverted mass hierarchy.
