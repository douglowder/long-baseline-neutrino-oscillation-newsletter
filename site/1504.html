Long-Baseline news, April 2015

*** Lincoln Wolfenstein 1923-2015

http://www.post-gazette.com/news/obituaries/2015/04/04/Obituary-Lincoln-Wolfenstein-Internationally-known-physicist-who-taught-at-CMU/stories/201504040050 The W in MSW, he was an early subscriber to this newsletter and a strong advocate of the long-baseline neutrino program at Fermilab. Here are some of his not-quite-quotes as I remember them:
1. On quarks - For a long time I thought that quarks were a mathematical model and weren't "real" but when I found that I couldn't write a paper without mentioning quarks, they were real.
2. On neutrinos - As a student at Chicago, I walked next to Stagg Field where the test reactor CP1 in the squash court was pouring out trillions of neutrinos. Of course I didn't know, but if I had known I would have walked on the other side of the street.
3. On neutrino mass theories - We have thousands of theories, which is like having no theory at all.
4. To an experimenter at a talk on the CKM matrix - I invented rho & eta and I don't care what their values are; why should you?

*** Second Global Neutrino Infrastructures Meeting

http://www.appec.org/9-features/126-towards-the-realization-of-a-global-neutrino-infrastructure.html In the 2nd meeting, agency representatives were impressed by the rapidity, quality of convergence and momentum of the efforts of the community working on LAr TPCs to develop a credible program based on: an ambitious large infrastructure effort, consisting of a long-baseline beam and detector project (LBNF/DUNE) hosted at FNAL and SURF, based on previous design studies, but largely upgrading them, proposed by an international collaboration, very rapidly setting up its governance structure and preparing answers to an aggressive schedule of DOE critical design reviews; a medium scale program of short-baseline oscillation experiments (SBND, MicroBooNE and ICARUS) aiming to test the sterile neutrino hypothesis with unprecedented accuracy; a rich R&D and prototyping program in the CERN North Area, related to the above program along with other long-baseline efforts in the world (e.g. Hyper-K).

*** DUNE Technical Coordinator (TC) and Resource Coordinator (RC)

The Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment (DUNE) now has a fully functioning executive committee consisting of 10 members. Two important members are the TC (Eric James) and RC (Chang Kee Jung) chosen by FNAL's director and DUNE co-spokespersons. The TC monitors and coordinates the activities of all subsystems and is responsible for the overall technical performance, planning and scheduling. The RC is responsible for coordinating financial planning and other resources issues of the collaboration.

https://indico.fnal.gov/conferenceDisplay.py?confId=8903 NuFact15 is the seventeenth in a series that started in 1999 as an important yearly workshop with emphasis on future neutrino projects. *** NOvA wins U.S. DOE's Secretary's Award of Excellence

The NOvA project team successfully constructed the U.S.'s premier neutrino experiment by upgrading the neutrino beam at FNAL and constructing a 14-kt detector in MN, & a smaller 300-ton detector at FNAL. The $274M project will provide deep insight into the fundamental processes involved in the formation of the universe & understanding the differences between matter and antimatter. The project team effectively led a large number of partner institutions to fabricate and assemble an immense number of state-of-the-art technical components. Ernest Moniz March 2015

*** ICARUS move to Fermilab described in press release

A Fermilab press release tells how this large liquid Argon Detector, previously in Gran Sasso and now at CERN, will become part of the Short-Baseline Neutrino Program at Fermilab. http://www.fnal.gov/pub/presspass/press_releases/2015/ICARUS-20150422.html

*** NSAC meeting for nuclear physics planning in Kitty Hawk

A Long-range planning meeting in Kitty Hawk NC led to the drafting of 4 recommendations, not yet available. But for 0nu2beta, the nuclear physics community is supportive of a ton scale program in the next 10 years with a scale of $200M, and flexibility should be there for more than one experiment; e.g. one U.S. led ton scale experiment and participation in other experiments. By Nov. the NSAC Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay committee needs to evaluate the R&D necessary and specific milestones needed to make a down select. http://science.energy.gov/np/nsac/

*** Quenching and 0nu-2beta lifetimes

Matrix element calculations assume g_A=1.269. But it's known from single beta-decay and from 2nu-2beta that g_A is renormalized in nuclei. From measurements, it could be 0.5 to 0.8. It appears to 2nd order in NME and 4th order in 0nu-2beta rates. It is possible the rate limits we have now should be multiplied by 6-34, making ton scale experiments insensitive to the hierarchical inverted MH. https://indico.cern.ch/event/356320/contribution/15/material/slides/0.pdf and R.G.H. Robertson, Modern Phys. Lett. A 28, 1350021 (2013).
