*** Lehman panel promotes hadronic hose for NuMI

A review panel thought that the hadronic hose, a wire down the decay pipe which both increases the neutrino flux and makes the near and far spectra more similar, should be added to the project. One panel member even said it would be a waste of taxpayer money not to have the hose. They agreed MINOS should be 'rebaselined' to get money for the hose. Rebaselining does not change the Fermilab to Soudan baseline of 732 km.

*** MINOS and THESEUS joined.

The MINOS cavern at Soudan is completely excavated and they have broken through into the Soudan cavity where the Soudan 2/THESEUS detector resides (if THESEUS is approved.) Lakehead, the outfitting contractor, has begun pouring the MINOS cavern concrete floor. At FNAL, the beam excavation is starting slow because they are having a hard time getting workers. But the tunnel boring machine pieces are refurbished, on-site and will be ready to start in January 2001.

*** Matter effects in the Earth may explain IMB/Kamioka 1987a

IMB and Kamioka seemed to see different energy spectra of neutrinos from the same Supernova. Lunardini and Smirnov, show in hep-ph/0009356 . show that such distortions are naturally explained by the Large Mixing Angle (LMA) solution with matter effects. A paper last month which reached the opposite conclusion (Astro-ph/0010339) ignored matter effects.

*** Observing B-L violation at a neutrino factory

With intense neutrino beams, the possibility of searching for new rare processes opens up. In one such process, a neutrino interacts and makes 3 leptons in the final state, the "primary" lepton having the wrong apparent sign for its flavor. The diagrams are analogous to those for neutrinoless double beta decay. This is considered by Rodejohann and Zuber in hep-ph/0011050 . In another paper by Bueno et al., hep-ph/0010308 lepton flavor violating decays & lepton number violating processes are considered.
