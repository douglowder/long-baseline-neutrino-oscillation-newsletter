Long-Baseline news, May 2003

*** Borexino deals with seals

The Borexino detector at Gran Sasso is now installing the nested nylon scintillator containment vessels, and expects to begin the inflation and water filling process this summer. However, following a small (~50kg) accidental pseudocumene discharge last August, the experiment has since been prevented from performing needed scintillator purification tests. Due to environmental safety concerns with the laboratory's infrastructure, part of the Borexino experiment is currently under police seals. While the experiment has been able to finish ancillary construction, it will not be able to fill with scintillator until operating authorizations have been received from local authorities, and the investigating tribunal has removed the seals. Efforts to address the concerns of all parties and accomplish this are being vigorously pursued. On May 29th, Hall C at Gran Sasso was sealed by local authorities. The implications are not clear.

*** Minerva

or Main INjector ExpeRiment V-A. This collaboration wants to construct an off-axis near detector to measure neutrino cross sections on nuclear targets in the Few GeV Region with NuMI.

*** Finese

Fermilab Intense Neutrino Scattering Experiment. This is a proposal for a 10 ton detector 100 meters from the Booster source to measure the strange quark contribution to the nucleon spin.

*** MINOS at 99%

The last MINOS plane will be/was installed June 2 or May 29. The 2nd coil is scheduled to be powered for magnetized operations in July.

*** MiniBooNE rates up

MiniBooNE is up to half of its design intensity, a large recent improvement. Further improvements are coming with a collimator project and large-aperture RF cavities for the Booster. They have a pizero peak with minimal cuts, and a 23 MeV fitted width. With .73 10^20 pot (7% of goal) they have 80355 nus.

*** JHF2K or JHFnu or JPARC2K or JPARCnu or ...

On March 22, the first meeting of the Nuclear and Particle Physics Facility Committee (NPFC) was held at KEK. A detailed presentation of the neutrino LOI (whatever it will be called) was heard. The committee evaluated its scientific significance as very high and endorsed its early realization.

*** Fitting KamLAND

Alexei Smirnov pointed out that many people were analyzing solar data with KamLAND for mixing angle and mass. He noticed that even if you do something wrong, you get the same answer.

*** A table top long-baseline experiment; NOSTROS

In hep-ex/0303045 is an idea for NOSTROS (Neutrino OScillation Tritium Outgoing Source). Put 20 kgrams of Tritium in a 10 meter radius spherical gaseous Xenon detector with 1 bar pressure. (ok, its a big table.) Threshold neutrino energy of 200 eV for average 14 keV. There is sensitivity to theta_13 at the atmospheric delta m^2.

*** GADZOOKS

Only Mark Vagins could think up "Gadolinium Antineutrino Detector Zealously Outperforming Old Kamiokande Super". Add 100 tons of GdCl3 to Super-K and you can separate 16-O neutral current events from nu_e 16O --> e- F; providing a measure of burst temperature and oscillation angle. Some study is required first.

*** Neutrinos versus the bomb

Hep-ph/0305062 by Sugawara et al. is called "Destruction of Nuclear Bombs Using Ultra-High Energy Neutrino Beam". Aim a 1000 TeV neutrino beam at nuclear weapons, and the neutrino radiation hot spot will induce enough fissions for the bomb to destroy itself. More research on neutrino factories is needed first. Also see http://www.newscientist.com/news/news.jsp?id=ns99993734
