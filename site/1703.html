Long-Baseline news, March 2016

*** "42" or "Do we know the mass ordering yet?"

The ordering of the 3 neutrino masses is "normal" or "inverted". I don't know who chose these names, but based on them I have a strong Bayesian preference for "normal". In "Global constraints on absolute neutrino masses and their ordering," arXiv:1703.04471 , Capozzi et al. find a preference for the normal ordering from oscillation and cosmological data at approximately 2 sigma. That means 95%CL to me and I'd be willing to make a bet at 19:1 odds. In "Strong Evidence for the Normal Neutrino Hierarchy", arXiv:1703.03425 , Simpson et al., do a Bayesian calculation and infer odds of 42:1. But in "Comment on Strong Evidence for ...", arXiv:1703.04585 , Schwetz et al. attribute that evidence to the assumed prior "flat distribution in logarithmic scale" and not the data. My answer to the first question is Yes. What's yours?

*** What are Solar Atmospheric Neutrinos?

Studies of solar neutrinos made by fusion in the sun, and studies of atmospheric neutrinos made by cosmic rays interacting in the earth's atmosphere, have long been part of the stable of the neutrino oscillation industry. Two new papers this month address the separate issue of Solar Atmospheric Neutrinos, produced when cosmic rays interact in the solar atmosphere. C. Arguelles & al. in "Solar Atmospheric Neutrinos and the Sensitivity Floor for Solar Dark Matter Annihilation Searches," arXiv:1703.7798 and K. Ng et al., "Solar Atmospheric Neutrinos: A New Neutrino Floor for Dark Matter Searches", arXiv:1703.10280 consider this as a signal and as a background for Dark Matter.

*** T2HK and T2HKK: Does more matter matter?

The T2HKK idea is to move one of the two tanks of the HK detector to a site in Korea with a 1100 km baseline. In arXiv:1703.07136 , S. Raut compares these two possibilities in conjunction with data from DUNE (1300 km).

*** How can we remember the mixing angles (in degrees)?

Use a progression. theta_12 = 34. theta_23 = 45. For the last one we need the geometric progression 139. theta_13 = 9. Also, q_12 + q_23 + q_13 + delta_CP=0. Thanks to Carlos Wagner.

*** Do non-relativistic neutrinos oscillate?

While most treatments of oscillations assume that neutrinos are relativistic, 2 of the 3 mass states of relic neutrinos are probably non-relativistic now. If eV-scale sterile nus exist, they may be non-relativistic in some situations of practical interest. E. Akhmedov considers these issues in arXiv:1703.8169 and finds that coherence is lost except in the strongly mass- degenerate case. "Our results for the most part are in agreement with Hinchliffe's rule". That works half the time.

*** Second NOvA Analysis

Find "Constraints on oscillation parameters from nue appearance and numu disappearance in NOvA" at arXiv:1703.03328 . 33 nue candidates over a background of 8.2. No IH lower octant > 93%CL.

*** DUNE Near Detector

The baseline proposal for a DUNE Near Detector has been a high- resolution fine-grained detector. At a March DUNE ND workshop, there was an emerging consensus that it should include both a LAr-TPC & a high-res tracker. The next DUNE ND workshop will be 9-10 June at FNAL after the Users Meeting & 50th yr celebration.

*** New solar metallicity measurements

New solar wind measurements indicate a high-metallicity Sun. But the "solar modeling problem", i.e. disagreement between predictions of standard solar models and inferences from helioseismology, remains unsolved. arXiv:1703.10834
