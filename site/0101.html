Long-Baseline news, January 2001

*** Groundbreaking for CNGS

At 11:45 on 12 Oct, the CERN Director General dug the first earth for the CERN Neutrinos to Gran Sasso. Civil Engineering works are expected to continue until April 2003, first beam to Gran Sasso is scheduled for May 2005. But the civil engineering job for CNGS was considerably complicated by the CERN Courier (12/00) http://www.cerncourier.com/main/article/40/10/3/1/cernnews5_12-00 by painting an Italian instead of a French flag over the location of the SPS accelerator at CERN - now Italy is where the beam starts, then under Switzerland, but where is Gran Sasso?? CNGS has a snazzy new web page http://proj-cngs.web.cern.ch/proj-cngs/

*** Underground Facility Initiative

The NSF & DOE are supporting the work of a national committee to investigate whether the U.S. should develop a National Underground Laboratory and recommend a site. The final meeting will be Mar 3-4. The committee wants 1-2 page `letters of interest' from experimenters interested in using such a lab. For format, see http://www.hep.anl.gov/ndk/hypertext/underground.txt To be most useful to the committee, the letters must be received by John Bahcall by Friday, February 19th.

*** Wolfgang Pauli 70

The 70th anniversary of the neutrino provoked a response that the story of Pauli and his commitments from Dec 4th to 7th 1930 would make a good novel. The Zurich ball may have been a blind: no one in their senses could ever believe that Pauli would be indispensible at a ball; he would cause untold carnage. It was said that he was not able to travel because he could not miss his daughter's birthday. But this was possibly an excuse also, since he was very tentative about his neutrino hypothesis; apart from that letter he did not put anything down on paper for another 2 years ( after he had heard about Fermi's calculations?).

*** Enrico Fermi 34

The namer of the neutrino will be honored with a United States Postage Stamp on his 100th birthday, September 29, 2001 in Chicago. Such a stamp was one goal of a Fermilab UEC stamp campaign: http://www.fnal.gov/orgs/fermilab_users_org/stamp.html The image chosen shows Fermi at a blackboard with a few equations. Of course it will be a 34 cent stamp.

*** Ernest Rutherford 18

The 18th International Workshop on Weak Interactions and Neutrinos will be held in New Zealand on the campus of The University of Canterbury, Christchurch. This was the location of the undergraduate studies of Ernest Rutherford.

*** Neutrino Factory addition to Neutrino Oscillation Industry

http://www.hep.anl.gov/ndk/hypertext/nuindustry.html A long-realized defect of the nu oscillation industry web site has been addressed with the expansion of the "Future Neutrino Facility Initiatives" section to include nu factories. Suggestions on arranging the content are welcome. UNO and the Underground Physics site search are also included.

*** Neutrino preparations for Snowmass (?)

Neutrino physics has benefitted from support for many new projects recently. But this also means that many neutrino physicists have even less time to think about the long term future, where opportunities for further neutrino progress are probably large. Come on, neutrino community, lets get a little noisier as Snowmass approaches.

*** Reducing the speed of neutrinos to zero

The discovery of neutrino mass allows us to go into the rest frame of the neutrino, but no slow neutrinos are yet in evidence. With recent attention http://sciam.com/news/011901/1.html given to stopping light, slow nus might get some attention, but not soon. Primordial neutrinos might be non-relativistic.

*** George Bush and the case of the pilfered W's

Were they taken by miniBooNE scientists, who hope to see an increase in (W mediated) nu-e charged current interactions?

*** Site specific neutrino factory study at BNL

After study 1 at Fermilab, Study 2 for a neutrino factory at BNL is not the study-to-end-all-studies; but it is one step beyond a feasibility study. The cost estimate is expected early March, with a final report and closeout with BNL management in April.
