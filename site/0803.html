Long-Baseline news, March 2008

*** Daya Bay Having a Blast

Daya Bay started blasting on Feb. 19, 2008. They had passed their (US) CD2-3a review in January. The Daya Bay Neutrino Experiment is a neutrino-oscillation experiment designed to measure the mixing angle theta13 using anti-neutrinos produced by the reactors of the Daya Bay Nuclear Power Plant (NPP) and the Ling Ao NPP. http://dayawane.ihep.ac.cn/photo/civil200802/civil_200802.html

*** CHOOZ limit

MINOS will have the sensitivity this summer to improve upon the current CHOOZ limit on theta-13. But if you look through the literature, the CHOOZ limit can be found as 0.10, 0.11, 0.14, 0.15, 0.17 and 0.20 in sin squared two theta. In the PDG it is 0.19. There is a unique CHOOZ curve, as a function of delta msquared, but what dmsq to use? Using a best fit doesn't take into account the asymmetric nature of the curve. The current PDG limit is based on the one-sigma low Super-K dmsq, but the limit will go up if one uses the new MINOS dmsq.

*** P5 discussing future of neutrino physics and dancing

One possible ILC lesson is not to take too big a step at once. One question that P5 is asking itself as it crafts the future of HEP is whether a long-baseline beam to DUSEL should be the goal for neutrino physics. Such a plan would take 4 big steps: 1. DUSEL should exist; 2. Project X; 3. A new neutrino beamline and 4. A very large detector or detectors at Homestake. P5 is meeting in Washington to finalize its report, and one session will include a dance instructor teaching how to make 4 steps at the same time.
