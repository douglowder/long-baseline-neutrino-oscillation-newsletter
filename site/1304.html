Long-Baseline news, April 2013

*** Strategy for Neutrino theory meeting at Fermilab 20th of May

While the fraction of the US investment in experimental particle physics allocated to the so-called Intensity Frontier - and neutrino-related experiments in particular - continues to grow, the US neutrino theory community remains at subcritical levels. This imbalance will ultimately impact, perhaps severely, the returns from the anticipated healthy investment in Intensity Frontier research during this and the next decade. These issues will be discussed at a one-day FNAL no-talk no-fee meeting on Monday 20 May. Contact A. De Gouvea, A. Friedland, P. Huber.

*** MINOS global fit using beam and atmospheric neutrinos

With a combination of beam neutrinos and atmospheric neutrinos, MINOS can measure a new and better value of Delta msquared_32, for which it has the world's best measurement. In arXiv:1304.6335 the new best measurement is 2.41 x 10^-3 eV^2 + 0.09 - 0.10 with sin^2(2 theta_23) = 0.950 + 0.035 - 0.036.

*** CONNIE (COherent Neutrino Nucleus Interaction Experiment)

CONNIE is a multi-institution plan to use low noise CCDs to look for coherent nu nucleus scattering. Ten one-gram CCD targets were built at Berkeley based on a design for the Dark Energy Survey, assembled at Fermilab, and will be installed this June in a trailer 30 m from the core of the Angra II reactor in Brazil. They expect 1 event per 4 days with 0 background. A large water tank for monitoring the fuel in nuclear reactors will be installed in the trailer ~November. That project is known as Angra Nu.

*** What LBNE is and isn't

The reconfiguration process left several different documents which covered the physics reach of the Long-Baseline Neutrino Experiment describing different programs. The collaboration is clarifying that LBNE is a plan for a 34 kiloton liquid Argon detector underground at SURF with a fully capable near detector. The first phase which has CD1, for a 10 kiloton detector without a near detector is to be called LBNE10. It is a high priority goal to be placed underground.

*** International Neutrino Summer School 20112(INSS13)

INSS13 will take place from 6-16 Aug at the Beijing Shunxin Green Resort. The school is co-hosted by IHEP and Fermilab and will feature lectures & student-led problem solving and presentations. The school curriculum extends from "neutrinos in the Standard Model" to Leptogenesis to detector and accelerator technologies. The registration for the school is now open, and the deadline for registering is May 15. http://inss2013.ihep.ac.cn/

*** NOvA contingency

http://www.fnal.gov/pub/today/archive/archive_2013/today13-04-23.html In a director's corner, Pier Oddone reported that NOvA will be the third project in a row that FNAL has completed on budget and on schedule. A previous concern that remaining contingency was inadequate has been addressed. More work has been completed, nearly 14 percent contingency remains, and essentially all known remaining construction risks are retired except the price of oil.

*** ANU progress during Fermilab Shutdown

Work on the needed magnets, RF cavity and other equipment to increase the power of the NuMI beam during the Fermilab shutdown is progressing. When the beam returns in June, they plan to commission the Main Injector first at low power; 30 KW for 2 weeks and 150 kW by June 15th, 300 kW by July 1 and 500 kW in 5 months, using slip stacking in the Recycler. NuMI can expect 5e20 protons on target by July 2014, 1e21 by July 2015.

*** Why we went to the Intensity Frontier Workshop

Yuval Grossman started this Snowmass preparation workshop by asking "Why are we here?" and answering it, "We are here to find out why we are here." He also made use of all the vowels in his equation A + E + I = U .ne. O where A = Astrophysics, E = Energy, I = Intensity, U = Unity and O = zero.
