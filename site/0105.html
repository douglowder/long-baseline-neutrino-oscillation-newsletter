Long-Baseline news, May 2001

*** K2K run going well

They will run to ~July 10th and should double last year's flux. Then they plan to drain Super-Kamiokande, replace the broken tubes and do some upgrades. They will be able to run for some physics during the months when the tank is drained and refilled.

*** CERN to Frejus

Using the RF from LEP, Cern is considering a 2.2 GeV, 4MW 2mA Superconducting Proton Linac (SPL) which will cost about 350 MSF. (~$200M). Running at 75Hz, the machine will be 800 m long, and could aim at the Frejus tunnel 130 km away. A physics study at CERN for this intense superbeam is described in hep-ph/0105297 . The low energy helps keep nu_e's from K's quite low.

*** New Frejus tunnel?

The Mont Blanc accident is motivation for Frejus to build a 2nd "escape" tunnel, and they might build a large underground lab at the same time. The current (small) lab is 4600 mwe. Three locations for a 10^6 m^3 lab have been identified. One is French, one is Italian, and one is Franco-Italian. The reduction in border bureaucracy has depressed the local economy and it may qualify for some extra funding.

*** Senators Warner and Bingaman promote DOE Science Budget

In response to the President's budget which has large (~10%) cuts for DOE University research, Senators John Warner (R-VA) and Jeff Bingaman (D-NM) are circulating a letter calling for increases for the DOE's Office of Science: http://www.aps.org/public_affairs/issues/budget2.shtml .

*** Senator Jeffords and the Law of Unintended Consequences

His break with the Bush administration was not due to policy differences on future neutrino experiments. But there will be an effect. One is that Senator Daschle becomes majority leader.

*** Senator Daschle promotes Homestake Underground Lab

Senator Tom Daschle (D-SD) has taken a personal interest in promotion of a national underground science lab at Homestake, as recommended by a national committee of physicists. Operating costs that keep many miners after 1/1/2 ~$10M/year. http://www.senate.gov/~daschle/issues/Homestake/Homestake.html .

*** Senator Domenici promotes WIPP

Senator Pete Domenici of New Mexico believes that the WIPP site in New Mexico is a good site. "There is already strong concurrence that WIPP represents a superb facility which should be utilized for experiments requiring its unique features-a clean, safe environment with a radiation background 100 times lower than the Homestake mine," Domenici said. http://domenici.senate.gov/newscenter/record.cfm?id=176292 .

*** Congresswoman Mrs. Sonny Bono promotes San Jacinto

No, it is Mary not Cher. The San Jacinto site is being studied as a horizontal access site for a national underground facility. The escarpment is quite steep, so a tunnel could be built to a very deep overburden for about $150M. A 50m x 50m x 200 m cavern would be $82M. Operating costs about $2M/year.

*** Senators UNO losers -- SNO results are soon

The former is an anagram of the latter (don't read too much into it.) Long awaited SNO results are again rumored to be two weeks away. But they are GUARANTEED to be released before UNO measures solar neutrinos in 2012. Meanwhile, SNO has started adding salt to the detector, which will increase the neutral current cross section. hep-ph/0104166 & hep-ph/0104095 are recent predictions.

*** Heaviest neutrino 2.3 eV

Fodor et al hep-ph/0105064 assume that the highest energy cosmic rays come from "Z-bursts" due to extragalactic UHE neutrinos scattering from relic neutrinos at the Z pole. Using maximum likelihood they get 2.34 +1.29 -0.84 eV.

*** Heaviest neutrino lighter than 2.2 eV

DASI and Boomerang ignored neutrinos in their cosmological fits. Using their data now, Wang et al fit to everything they can think of (including neutrinos) and set an upper limit on the heaviest neutrino mass at 2.2 eV. astro-ph/0105091.
