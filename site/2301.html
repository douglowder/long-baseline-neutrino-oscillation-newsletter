Long-Baseline news, January 2023

*** Snowmass Report is the culmination of the 2020-2022 process

The 75p report from the 3-year Snowmass process is now available at https://inspirehep.net/literature/2627711. There are 3 levels of detail: an executive summary presents a vision for 10 years and beyond, high-level conclusions, and the projects that could contribute. Each frontier has a chapter. The Neutrino Frontier makes 5 recommendations (on page 43) for a diverse program.

*** Particle Physics Project Prioritization Panel (P5)

A bare-bones web page is at http://hitoshi.berkeley.edu/P5/. The 1st of 4 town halls will be 22-24 Feb at Lawrence Berkeley Lab. https://indico.physics.lbl.gov/event/2382/ Neutrinos will be one focus of the 21-24 March town hall at FNAL, with the 23rd at the beautiful campus of Argonne National Laboratory. Community engagement is crucial for the best science in the upcoming years.

*** A neutrino oscillation question for your next physics party

Suppose you detect both neutrinos from a Z decay. How are the oscillations of both neutrinos correlated? This is considered in "Mixed States for NC nu oscillation", arXiv:2301.07689

*** Elemental Accounting of the Solar Interior

The Borexino signal from the CNO cycle provides a direct measure of elemental abundances in the sun. A nice summary can be found at https://physics.aps.org/articles/v15/190.

*** DUNE civil construction half done

400,000 tons of rock have been safely removed from the Sanford Underground Research Facility so far. The second phase is now in full swing and is expected to be complete in 2024. https://sanfordlab.org/article/excavation-massive-underground-caverns-dune-halfway-complete

*** MINERvA measures axial vector form factor F_A with antinus

https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-022-05478-3. Using the plastic scintillator target, MINERvA is able to extract F_A for free protons and measure the axial charge radius to be 0.73 +- 0.17 fm. The extracted cross section data is available at https://minerva.fnal.gov/hydrogen-scattering-data-release-page/

*** STEREO 235U spectrum rejects sterile neutrino hypothesis

https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-022-05568-2.pdf With highly enriched 235U fuel used at ILL, they are able to make a measurement of the spectrum of neutrinos from 235U, in disagreement with the assumptions used that generated the reactor nu anomaly.

*** Neutrinos from Messier 77

IceCube reports evidence that the AGN NGC1068 is a source of TeV neutrinos. The excess from that direction is 79+22-20 events. https://www.science.org/stoken/author-tokens/ST-839/full

*** Neutrinoful - New word for the new year

If I google this word, and ignore their suggestion that I meant neutrinoless, I get arXiv:1405.0013, a paper which proposed a new Higgs field U(1)_B-L to explain neutrino masses, inflation, baryon asymmetry and dark matter in a cosmological context. But the word is only used in the title "Neutrinoful Universe" and probably just means that neutrinos are plentiful and important. I just ran across the word in the May 2023 INT program "New Physics Searches at the precision frontier" which describes one of its three thrust areas to be "neutrinoful and neutrinoless double beta decay". https://www.int.washington.edu/programs-and-workshops/23-1b Here the word seems to be synonymous with "two-neutrino", but nicely matches its companion term.
