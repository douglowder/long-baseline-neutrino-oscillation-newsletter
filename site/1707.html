*** Next Step on WA105; first particle tracks

Work is progressing on a dual-phase liquid Argon prototype at CERN, also known as WA105 and protoDUNE-DP. While an 11 m^3 detector is under construction, a 3x1x1 m^3 detector was made in 2016 and is being commissioned as part of the CERN neutrino platform. Two cosmic ray tagger panels have a 0.3 Bq rate. The cathode and field cage were powered to 50 kV. After 7 volumes of recirculation, good liquid Ar purity and long ms drift were achieved. On June 20, the first track was seen. http://www.symmetrymagazine.org/article/worlds-biggest-neutrino-experiment-moves-one-step-closer

*** Next Step for SBND; measuring booster neutrinos

A test installation of panels for the SBND Cosmic Ray Tracker has been completed in a way to see crossing particles generated by Booster Beam nus. The timing distribution clearly measures 1.6us spill of the Booster, as well as the 53 MHz proton structure.

*** First measurement of coherent nu elastic scattering

The signature of neutrino nucleus elastic scattering is a keV level nuclear recoil, a difficult process to measure. At the Oak Ridge Spallation neutron source, there is a high flux of decay- at-rest neutrinos. In order to measure this heretofore unmeasured process, the COHERENT collaboration installed a 14.6 kg CsI scintillator. The exposure was 153.5 live-days beam off and 308 beam on. Clear signals are seen in the energy and arrival time distributions matching expectation, with a 6.7 sigma excess. http://science.sciencemag.org/content/early/2017/08/02/science.aao0990

*** T2K updates electron neutrino appearance results

With 7.5e20 pot (nu) and 7.5e20 pot (anti-nu), they get 37 events of nue appearance, 4 in antinue appearance. This strengthens hints for non-zero leptonic CP-violation from before. The 90% CL region for delta is -2.95 to -0.44 (NO) and -1.47 to -1.27 (IO). The central 68% CL for dmsq32 is 2.54 (2.58)+-0.08 NO(IO)e-3 eV^2 and sin^2(q23) = .55+0.05-0.09. See details in arXiv:1707.1048 .
