Long-Baseline news, January 2016

*** Rumors continue to plague INO

India's government allocated US$225 million to construction when it gave the INO the green light last year, but the Madras High Court in Chennai brought the project to a standstill in March following a petition from local activists and politicians. The court said that the Tamil Nadu Pollution Control Board must give consent before construction can start. This is normally a 45-day step, but this hasn't happened yet after 9 months. http://www.nature.com/news/nuclear-rumours-delay-india-s-bid-for-neutrino-glory-1.19150

*** Long-baseline rumors; caveat from this newsletter's web site

Long-Baseline News is a newsletter which includes rumors and references about neutrinos. It should not be used as a reference. It can be used to follow-up rumors which may be of interest (and may or may not turn out to be true.) To help prevent people taking it too seriously, I do not put in retractions when the rumor turns out to be incorrect, but I will try to fix the web page version.

*** Short-Baseline Neutrino Experiment at JPARC

J-PARC E56, JSNS2, a search for sterile neutrinos at J-PARC, has received stage-1 approval. The plan is for a >100 ton liquid scintillator detector 24 meters from a mercury target. Next is preparation of a technical Design Report. arXiv:1601.01046

*** First NOvA oscillation papers available on arXiv

The first measurements of nue appearance and numu disappearance by NOvA were reported last summer. Two papers have now appeared on the arXiv: arXiv:1601.05022 and arXiv:1601.05037. The delay was due to correcting a misunderstanding in the survey location of the Near Detector, so that the data/MC hadronic energy scale difference is now smaller. The oscillation results are consistent but have a different look. The significance of nue appearance (3.3 sigma) is unchanged. The best fits for delta m^2_32 and sin^2 theta_23 are now 2.52 +.20 -.18 e-3 eV^2 and 0.38-0.65.

*** Fermilab Booster sets record

The Booster Neutrino Beam has been delivering excellent beam to MicroBooNE since October, 2015. Set a BNB intensity record on January 25 with sustained running of 5e12 ppp at 5 Hz. MicroBooNE has accumulated 1.3e20 protons on target since 15 Oct. http://news.fnal.gov/2016/02/booster-neutrino-beam-delivers-record-beam-intensity/

*** The NuMI beams "use by" date has passed

The NuMI neutrino beam was built in the early 2000's for MINOS, a 6-year experiment. The civil design lifetime was 10 years. Jim Hylen says the warranty has expired. But recent press reports say it is ok to use most food well after the package's "use by" date. On 15 Jan NuMI switched to 4+6 slip stacking and is currently running at 460 kW. It is now expected to reach 575 kW by the end of February and to demonstrate 700 kW before the summer shutdown.
