Long-Baseline news, September 1998

*** Joint CERN-Gran Sasso Long-Baseline Meeting

The scientific committees of the SPS at CERN and of the Gran Sasso laboratories will meet at CERN on 3-4 November to have a discussion of Neutrinos to Gran Sasso. They have called for ideas of experiments with this beam by documents up to 10 pages due October 10th. They have requested that documents for experiments with or without a near station show their exclusion curves as well as 4-sigma discovery curves, based on shared beam, 3 10^19 protons per year and 3 year runs. High and Low energy fluxes will be released by the end of September.

*** NOMAD finishes

NOMAD, the short baseline neutrino experiment at CERN, ended their run on September 19th. This year there were 1.8 E+19 protons on the neutrino target. Some of the time, the beam was set for antineutrinos rather than the neutrinos NOMAD (and CHORUS) usually ran.

*** Jurassic Pair

A proposal is being written to move NOMAD and a version of CHORUS together to the JURA mountains, where the present CERN neutrino beam already goes. It might be called JEDDO (Jura Experiment Dual Detector Oscillation) and would be sensitive to numu to nutau oscillations in the LSND region.

*** How many long-baseline beams should there be?

Some participants at the European neutrino oscillation Workshop NOW98 felt that there should be one high-energy long-baseline neutrino beam, NGS or NuMI, and not both. But this was the minority view. The complementarity of the two beams was discussed in a Nature article dated September 10, 1998. One scenario discussed in Amsterdam was that each program spend its first year confirming that oscillations exist, and then the two programs can split the tasks that will take long runs. One could use a narrow band beam to pin down oscillation parameters, while the other runs with antineutrinos to look for CP effects.

*** Best quote at NOW98: Bilenky

"Phenomenology can be done by theorists interested in experiment and by experimentalists who are interested in theory. Theory can only be done by theorists who are not interested in experiment."

*** Secretary of Energy Richardson finishes Main Injector

Richardson tightened the last four bolts on the final 20-ton dipole magnet installed in Fermilab's Main Injector on September 24th. Richardson said, "Fermilab's new Main Injector will open up extraordinary new opportunities for discovery in this basic field of research, not only for scientists from the United States but for the international physics community." Beam soon!

*** NuMI second in Future ER Facility Needs

In a 7 page September report from Director of Energy Research Martha Krebs, "Future Science Facility Needs," the "near term" HEP list was LHC, NuMI, BTeV and Non-Accelerator Facilities. The long-term wish list were new colliders; ee, VLHC and mumu. These were "large facilities (>$25 -$50 million) most needed by the scientific communities which look to the Office of Energy Research for sponsorship."

*** Palo Verde has data

The Palo Verde reactor neutrino oscillation experiment has gotten their first data, 19.6 days from July/August 1998. Results soon.

*** Another LSND "signal"

There is a report that LSND has a new analysis from DIF neutrinos of numu to nue oscillations. The look for Nue 12C to e- N12(groundstate) which decays to e+,nu,12C with a 15 MeV endpoint. This is the same oscillation signature that Karmen doesn't see. There is a correlated e+ and e- double tag within 30 cm and 5 milliseconds and a reported signal of 5 with 0.5 expected background.

*** Notable new references

  • R. Escribano et al., Boron abundance and solar neutrino spectrum distortion, hep-ph/9805238 . They stress here that a distortion of the spectrum at high energies is expected if the 8B abundance in the Sun is less than the standard prediction due to the the emerging role of the hep neutrinos.
  • M. C. Gonzalez-Garcia et al., Atmospheric neutrino observations and flavor changing interactions. They can fit the muon neutrino zenith angle distribution with new interactions but without oscillations. hep-ph/9809531 .
  • C. Giunti, "Statistical Interpretation of the null result of the KARMEN 2 experiment", hep-ph/9808405 and "A new ordering principle for the classical statistical analysis of Poisson processes with background" hep-ph/9808240 . The new principle minimizes the effect on the CL's when less background is observed than expected.
