Long-Baseline news, June 2004

*** Precise new results from KamLAND

At Nu04, KamLAND showed analysis from 545 days of data (previously it was 145). They also increased the fiducial volume 33%, cutting now at 5.5m. They observe 258 events. 365+-24 was expected without oscillations, plus 7.5+-1 background. Due to reactor issues in Japan, reactor flux was down in 2003 almost a factor of two. The energy spectrum now adds substantial information to the fit. The best fit to oscillations is delta m^2 8.3 e-5 and sin^2 2theta 0.83. A fit to a rescaled reactor spectrum is excluded at 99.89%CL. The best fit excludes LMA2, pins delta m^2 & agrees well with global fits. hep-ex/0406035

*** NOvA

In evaluating the new Fermilab long-baseline NOvA proposal, the PAC said that to establish a compelling case there were 4 criteria: Uniqueness, Competitiveness with T2K, Competitiveness with reactor experiments and capability for evolution. They BOLDLY declared that NOvA can meet these criteria if it is built in a timely manner. Steps to achieve stage 1 approval were: 1) finalize detector design, 2) complete near term R&D, 3) update proposal with the full physics case. They endorsed the R&D plan.

*** Snowmass Neutrino

The US Neutrino Community held a lively meeting in Snowmass to prepare a report on the future program of neutrino program in the US. Six working groups presented reports which included a number of ideas to aggressively exploit the recent progress in the neutrino sector. The details of the recommendations' format and content were vigorously debated, but the range of exciting opportunities in the neutrino sector in the next few years was universally agreed to. Presentations can be found at http://www.neutrinooscillation.org/studyaps/apsfinalprogram.html. Much attention focused on a program for theta_13 with expts at reactors & accelerators, and a double beta decay program.

*** Proton Intensity for Fermilab Booster up up up

In June 2004, the benefit of several improvements in the Fermilab Proton Source came to impressive fruition. A record of 9.73E18 POT (MiniBooNE) was achieved, and all four weeks were at least 10% higher than any previous week. During normal operation there were multiple periods of several hours each in which the MiniBooNE horn had to run at its limit of 5 Hz for the first time. The horn rate and the protons per pulse are about 80% of goals. All of these improvements are firmly based on controlling the residual radiation in the Booster. Further improvements in June are expected to reach the MiniBooNE goal 9E16 POT/hour.

*** EPP2010

The National Science Foundation and the Department of Energy have asked the Board on Physics and Astronomy (BPA) of the National Research Council to convene a committee that will undertake a study of the field of elementary-particle physics. In particular, the committee will be charged to: 1. Identify, articulate, and prioritize the scientific questions and opportunitites that define elementary-particle physics. 2. Recommend a 15-year implementation plan with realistic, ordered priorities to realize these opportunities. The BPA seeks to gather input on the makeup of the committee that will carry out the study. Send comments/suggestions to by 7/15. http://www7.nationalacademies.org/bpa/EPP2010.html

*** Fermilab to Beijing

Discussions are taking place about a possible long-baseline neutrino oscillation experiment from Fermilab to Beijing, a distance of 9400 km. The first oscillation minimum would be 17 GeV with a second minimum at 5 GeV. The beam would aim down at 48 degrees. A megaton detector is being considered.
