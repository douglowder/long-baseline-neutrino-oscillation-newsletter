Long-Baseline news, June 2015

*** Neutrinos at the June 2015 Fermilab PAC

Fermilab PAC presentations continue to be dominated by nu physics experiments & issues. During a planning exercise it was said that in 10 years, FNAL will be to neutrinos what CERN is to the Higgs. The lab specified four goals for the next 5 years:
* Support detector R&D and new initiatives in neutrino science
* Build the short-baseline neutrino program
* Deliver the world's highest power beams for neutrino science
* Partner internationally to host a world-leading long-baseline neutrino program.

*** Matt Toups gets 2015 Tollestrup Award

The award is made each year for outstanding work in collaboration with Fermilab scientists. Toups received this year's award for his contributions to the liquid-argon-based short-baseline neutrino program at Fermilab, including MicroBooNE and the Short-Baseline Near Detector. Two main contributions were to co-lead the commissioning of MicroBooNE and developing the UV light system. http://www.fnal.gov/pub/today/archive/archive_2015/today15-06-10.html

*** Mission Need for PIP II

The Proton Improvement plan II will provide 1.2 MW of power to the DUNE neutrino science program being developed at Fermilab. A review was held in June to provide support for the Mission Need Statement to be developed by the Office of High Energy Physics in the DOE. https://indico.fnal.gov/conferenceDisplay.py?confId=9939

*** Director's Review of LBNF and DUNE

DUNE/LBNF passed a director's review for planning its CD1 refresh, which is now planned for 14-16 July at Fermilab.

*** CERN SPC on DUNE prototype

After submitting an EOI to CERN in early October 2014, 214 members of DUNE from 43 institutes in 7 countries submitted a Proposal for a Full-Scale Prototype Single-Phase Liquid Ar Time Projection Chamber and Detector Beam Test at CERN in June which was heard by the SPSC h0ps://indico.cern.ch/event/397446/. In draft minutes, the Committee supports the motivation of the project & recognizes the importance of the engineering and physics goals expressed in the proposal. They noted with pleasure progress in defining the project and will review the technical implementation.

*** NuMI horn to be replaced

In June it was decided to replace the current horn "PH1-04" due to a severed stripline. The 1st horn capable of running at NOvA's design power of 700 kW, it accumulated 27 million pulses since Sep 2013. The failure is a severed stripline on 1 quadrant. The horn can't be pulsed. A new one will be installed during the shutdown. NuMI ran a few weeks horn off for MINOS+/NOvA/Minerva.

*** Ross Shaft rehab and Yates shaft maintenance at SURF

Rehabbing in the Ross Shaft has been taking place for a few years for Long-Baseline Neutrino Facility (LBNF) & DUNE. All excavated rock has to go through the Ross Shaft. They have reached the 2450 level, about halfway. Challenges arise as they remove old steel and replace it. A challenging section comes next -the Ross Pillar, a 1200-foot zone surrounded by a 3 ft thick concrete liner near where they had mined. Normal ground movement caused misalignment and bowed some steel. While Ross is being rehabbed, the Yates Shaft is undergoing top-down maintenance. Built 75 years ago, Yates was constructed of timber. Yates will serve DUNE and will continue to move people, equipment, and supplies. http://sanfordlab.org/sites/sanfordlab.org/files/newsletters/surf_monthly/%20SURF%20NL%20June15.pdf

*** Memories of David Cline

A professor of physics at UCLA and the first U.S. physicist to work on a large liquid argon neutrino detector, David Cline passed away in June. From Experiment 1 at Fermilab, to the new DUNE collaboration, he has long worked on neutrino experiments. He has also been a keen leader in the search for Dark Matter. I never met him when he didn't have a new idea. In 1993, he bet me $100 that the atmospheric neutrino anomaly wasn't due to oscillations. When Kamiokande reported an angular dependence in 1994, he said he felt like he already had $50 in his pocket. I never understand what he meant by that. He paid me in 1999.
