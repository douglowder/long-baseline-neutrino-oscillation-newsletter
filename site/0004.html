Long-Baseline news, April 2000

*** ICARUS progress

Icarus 600T cryostat has been delivered. Internal assembly of the first half-module is expected to be completed within 3 months when there will be a full test, with liquid Argon inside, up to read-out. Transportation to GranSasso will be sometime next year. Start-up operation with 14T prototype "baby Icarus" (10m^3) is going on near Gran Sasso. Liquid Argon filling took place in February 2000 and there were after we got beautiful tracks up to 4 meters long immediately. See http://pcicar1.lngs.infn.it/ . and also http://www.aquila.infn.it/icarus/activities/10m3. .

*** NuFact 00 in Monterey

A workshop on a muon storage ring for a neutrino factory is May 22-26 at the Naval Postgraduate School located in Monterey California. See http://www.lbl.gov/Conferences/nufact00/

*** Lownu workshop before Neutrino 2000

According to BP98, more than 97% of the solar neutrino flux is predicted to lie below 1 MeV. On the day preceeding the opening session of Neutrino 2000, thursday June 15, there will be a one-day workshop on experimental problems and techniques related to detecting solar neutrinos below 1 MeV. The workshop will take place on the campus of Laurentian University, Sudbury.

*** NANPino - Dubna neutrino meeting July 19-22

A topical international workshop on neutrinos and physics beyond the standard model, NANPino (Non-Accelerator New Physics in neutrino observations), will be held in Dubna, Moscow region, RUSSIA, on July 19-22, 2000. See http://nanp.dubna.ru/ .

*** Neutrinos are boring?

In April I attended a library convention with my wife, and at lunch was asked my field. I answered neutrino physics, and was told by a librarian from Pennsylvania "That's a conversation stopper." But it turned out the librarian next to her was from the county in South Carolina where neutrinos were discovered.

*** Boring for Neutrinos

At various points in time, it has been planned that the tunnel for the neutrino target, decay pipe and near hall at Fermilab be made with a Tunnel Boring Machine (TBM) or by drill/blast. The Healy contract was approved with drill/blast, but there is a TBM available for NuMI. A value engineering proposal to use the TBM has been conditionally accepted by Fermilab. It is now in Dallas where it was being used for the Dallas subway.

*** Neutrino Bourse at Neutrino 2000

Northern Canada can be cold, but Neutrino 2000 will be hot! New results on solar neutrinos from Super-K and first results from SNO. There will be more results from K2K and hopefully a clear nu-tau signal from DONUT. The program can be found at http://www.nrc.ca/confserv/nu2000/scientific-program.html.

*** Interfering with oscillations

Baillon (below) suggests that the deficit of atmospheric neutrinos may be due to coherent numu e to nue mu forward scattering, as an alternative to oscillations. One issue is whether orthogonal states interfere. Also, the scatterer size is much larger than the neutrino wavelength, so neutrinos behave like particles, not waves, and the interference terms wash out. The author's view is at http://home.cern.ch/b/baillon/www/cdftalk/cdfen.html
