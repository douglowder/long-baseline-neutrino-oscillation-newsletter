Long-Baseline news, January 2020

*** Hyper-Kamiokande moving forward

The Hyper-Kamiokande project is for a 190 kiloton fiducial mass water detector with 40,000 photosensors, and a plan to upgrade J-PARC to 1.3 MW. A 2019 supplementary budget of 3.13 BJPY for HK and .37 BJPY for J-PARC upgrade and FY20 .3BJPY (out of requested 1.8 BJPY) is proposed by the Japanese cabinet. Total cost = 72 BJPY. Construction will start in 2020 & operation in 2027. http://www.hyper-k.org/

*** Did a presidential candidate's grandfather work in a nu lab?

During a recent debate, Amy Klobuchar mentioned her grandfather worked underground in an iron mine in Ely Minnesota. Was it the Soudan mine, where the Soudan I, Soudan 2 and MINOS experiments studied neutrinos? Probably not. The 520 m deep Pioneer mine is a more likely answer. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pioneer_Mine

*** Number of neutrinos creeping toward three

The width of the Z boson at LEP convinced the community that there were three and only three light "active" neutrinos. However, the four measurements by ALEPH, DELPHI, L3 and OPAL were all two sigma below 3. But a recent analysis of beam-beam effects on the fully correlated luminosity measurement due to a bias of luminometer acceptance found a correction twice the previous systematic error. N_nu = 2.9918 +- 0.0081. arXiv:1908.01704 in PLB800 135068(2020).

*** Neutrinos are not illusive

Are neutrinos deceptive? That's the meaning of illusive. I don't think so. They propagate in well defined mass eigenstates & seem to interact with a calculable V-A interaction. But it is in a headline in SURF's "Deep Thoughts", which came from an article about John Marshall from Warwick and his work on DUNE, appearing in the UK's "Lab News". John used "elusive" in the article. The journal caught the mistake, but there was poor communication with production. The web version is now fixed. https://mailchi.mp/sanfordlab/bbcs-interview-with-jaret-heise-2576437?e=3a70dd9147 , http://www.labnews.co.uk/article/2030304/catching-the-illusive-neutrino

*** "Compressed Pulses from the ESS" in Uppsala 2-3 March

https://indico.cern.ch/event/849674/ The European Spallation Source (ESS) being built will be 5 MW of 2 GeV protons delivered in 14 2.86 ms long pulses per second. This Uppsala workshop is sponsored by ESSNSB (ESS Neutrino Super Beam). https://essnusb.eu/

*** Muon Collider Meeting at CERN 31 Mar-2 Apr 2020

https://indico.cern.ch/event/886491/ This working meeting will identify the critical steps in a design study towards an initial machine operating at 3 TeV center-of-mass energy. It aims to develop a new international collaboration towards this goal.

*** Gathering of Neutrino Theorists 11-13 May 2020 near D.C.

As the field of neutrino physics enters the precision era, nus are ubiquitous and yet retain many mysteries, key to Particle physics, Astrophysics, and Nuclear physics. The time is right for an interdisciplinary effort of theorists to analyze the status of the field and make a plan for the future. The inaugural PAN Neutrino Theory Symposium will be held Virginia Tech's Arlington Research Center. https://indico.phys.vt.edu/event/40/

*** RANT - Names on published papers

My brother-in-law once expressed surprise that an unreasonable fraction of papers in "Notable New References" were written by people whose last name began with "A". (I've since switched to listing collaborations when I can.) In the field of particle physics, many don't read a majority of their own papers. Each collaboration has their own arcane rules. This is a problem without a right answer, but it is a problem nonetheless.
