Long-Baseline news, July 2003

*** New value of Delta-msquared from Super-K

The new preliminary best fit for dm^2 from Super-K's atmospheric nu analysis is 2.0 10^-3 eV^2, a bit lower than before. Updates include parameters for neutrino interactions, such as M_A, light scattering parameters in water, outer detector PMT gains, fully automated data reduction with better background rejection, etc., but no new data (yet). At 90% CL, dm^2 is between 1.9 and 3.0 10^-3, and sin^2 2q > 0.9. At best fit, chi^2_min=170.8/170dof.

*** New value of theta_13 limit?

Chooz has the best limit, and Chooz hasn't changed. But if you look at the value of the limit for the new best fit dm^2, (Figures 55 & 58 of http://arxiv.org/abs/hep-ex/0301017it is 0.2 for sin^2 2q. It was ~0.13 for 2.5 10^-3 and ~0.11 for 3.0 10^-3. Limits should probably be calculated at the lowest dm^2 in the CL.

200 years ago, Lewis and Clark went across the country, looking for good sites for neutrino experiments (though they didn't know it.) With an original budget of $2.5K, they spent almost $39K.

*** COBRA 0-nu double beta decay idea

A Dortmund group is developing a new double beta decay detector using CdZnTe semiconductor detectors. An energy resolution of 2.2% FWHM has been measured at 2.6 MeV. See http://cobra.physik.uni-dortmund.de/

*** Yearlong US neutrino study

A yearlong neutrino study to provide input for future neutrino facility decisions is being put together by the DPF and DNP divisions of the APS and also may include the beams and astrophysics divisions. Office of Science Director Orbach, whose facility plan hasn't been released yet, told HEPAP, "The neutrino study this year is going to be of enormous value." A two-week meeting possibly in Madison or Santa Barbara was mentioned.

*** TEXONO at Kuo-Sheng reactor in Taiwan and 2 km away

The Taiwan EXperiment On NeutrinO was started to pursue a program in Neutrino and Astroparticle Physics. A high purity germanium detector was used to set a limit on the neutrino magnetic moment mu(nuebar) < 1.3 10^10 mu(Bohr) ( hep-ex/0307001). There's a road tunnel 2 km from the reactor, which might be useful for theta_13.

*** JPARC in Japan and 2 km away

The JPARC to Super-K neutrino program envisages a near detector at 280 meters and an intermediate detector at 2 km. The near detector would be scintillator. The intermediate detector water with a fine-grained component and muon identifier. The beam and the closer detector are in this year's budget request in Japan, but not yet the 2km detector and lab.

*** Neutrino performance measure as a 10 year goal.

The OMB has proposed 5 long-term performance measures for high energy physics, one on neutrinos, "Determine the masses of the neutrinos and characterize the mixing between neutrino species." http://doe-hep.hep.net/HEPAP/Jul2003/HEPAPPerformanceMeasures_crawford.pdf
