Long-Baseline news, June 1998

*** Clinton links neutrino mass to SSC

President Clinton's remarks to MIT graduates this June included: "Just yesterday in Japan, physicists announced a discovery that tiny neutrinos have mass. Now, that may not mean much to most Americans, but it may change our most fundamental theories -- from the nature of the smallest subatomic particles to how the universe itself works, and indeed how it expands. This discovery was made, in Japan, yes, but it had the support of the investment of the U.S. Department of Energy. This discovery calls into question the decision made in Washington a couple of years ago to disband the super-conducting supercollider, and it reaffirms the importance of the work now being done at the Fermi National Acceleration Facility in Illinois."

*** The Universe May Never Be the Same

Neutrinos are in the News all over. A front page story from the Chicago Tribune shows Bob Bernstein looking out of the NuMI stub. See www.hep.anl.gov/ndk/postscript/NUMI_TRIB_98.PS

*** MiniBoone Approved at Fermilab

The BooNE experiment has received Stage I approval at Fermilab. From Neutrino 98 and WEIN Conferences, there are now three results with an excess: LSND DAR (33.9 +/-8.0 events), LSND DIF (18.1 +/- 6.6 events) and Karmen I (7.3 +/- 7 events), and one experiment which sees less than its expected background: Karmen II (3 background events expected + 1.6 +/- 0.8 events expected based on LSND DAR and 0 seen). Phase I of BooNE (MiniBooNE) expects ~1000 events (a 10 sigma signal) if the LSND signal is numu -> nue, and thus will make a decisive statement either proving or ruling it out. They need about 3.5$M funds. Plans are to run early 2001.

*** Do new KARMEN limits push LSND effect? Showdown in Santa Fe.

There were heated discussions at WEIN '98 whether the new Karmen data "rules out" the LSND effect. As shown in "latest results" at www-ik1.fzk.de/www/karmen/karmen_e.html, the high delta m^2 limit from Karmen is 1.3 10^-3 for data through April '98 using the unified approach of Cousins and Feldman (though their sensitivity is 5.4 10^-3).

*** Tau neutrino candidate in DONUT

A good tau neutrino candidate has been reported by the DONUT experiment, the emulsion experiment searching for the direct observation of nutaus from taus from Ds decay in a beam dump at Fermilab. It has a decay after 4.5 mm in the ECC target. Three nutau candidates among the first 34 neutrino candidates is consistent with the expected rate.

*** New CHORUS and NOMAD limits

Chorus has analysed their 1 mu events from 94-96 and their 0mu events from 94-95. They have located 91,834 events so far. For large dmsq at 90% CL, their limit on the mixing angle is 1.3 10^-3. Their ultimate sensitivity when they are done scanning is 2 10^-4. For large dmsq at 90%CL, NOMAD's new limit on the mixing angle is 2.2 10^-3. This includes a partial analysis of their 1995-1997 data.

*** "Neutrino Oscillation Industry Page" feted

June 12 1998 Science named the Neutrino Oscillation Industry web page www.hep.anl.gov/ndk/hypertext/nu_industry.html, a "Hot Pick" for its information on neutrino hunting.

*** Neutrino shine during an eclipse?

If neutrinos decay to visible light, the center of the sun would shine during a solar eclipse. A team looked in February from Venezuela, and didn't see, as reported in May's CERN Courier.

*** SNO survives earthquake

Last month a drift collapsed in the Sudbury mine. No one was hurt, but the noise could be heard in Sudbury and registered 3.9 on the Richter scale. The detector was engineered to withstand 4.5 and held up with no problems at all.

*** First leg of Japanese Hadron Facility funded

Recently, the Japanese Government decided to provide an additional fund to supplement the JFY98 budget (starting 1 Apr 98). It includes construction of a high-intensity 60 MeV Linac. Note that the JHF accelerator consists of 200 MeV Linac, 3 GeV synchrotron and 50 GeV synchrotron. This does not necessarily mean approval of the entire JHF project. But it's a good start.
