Long-Baseline news, December 2006

*** Fermilab long-baseline nu beam and detector projects combined

At the prodding of the Office of Management and Budget (which cares about such things), the DOE is combining NOvA and the Proton Plan Phase II. NOvA is the off-axis long-baseline experiment at Fermilab to measure theta-13 and, if theta-13 is large enough, matter effects. The proton plan is a variety of measures to increase the Main Injector Intensity, mostly to provide "protons on target" (pot) for NOvA. Among other things, this bureaucratic move affects the schedule for reviews.

*** How to get a neutrino experiment from Deggendorf to Karlsruhe

KATRIN is an experiment to measure the neutrino mass by carefully measuring the electron energy at the endpoint of Tritium beta decay. By using a large spectrometer, it can improve the limit on (Sum (U_ei)**2 m_i) from 2 eV to 0.2 eV. The spectrometer was built in Deggendorf and needed to be moved to Karlsruhe, about 400 km away. However, it is too big to transport by truck, so it was moved by boat, through Austria, Slovakia, Hungary, Serbia, Romania, Bulgaria, Ukraine, into the Black Sea, through the Dardanelles, the Mediterranean, the Strait of Gibraltar, the Atlantic, the English Channel, into Holland and down the Rhine. http://www.neutrinooscillation.org/katrinroute.jpg

*** SNO completed

Over the months ahead, the heavy water in the core of the SNO detector will be removed and returned to Atomic Energy of Canada Limited and Ontario Power Generation. Modifications to the detector are planned and funding is being sought to adapt it to a new liquid scintillator core which will enable it to further sensitive measurements of neutrinos from the Sun and from the core of the earth, as well as studying a very rare and fundamental form of radioactivity .neutrinoless double beta decay. http://www.sno.phy.queensu.ca/sno/press_release/SNONewsReleaseNov15r.pdf

*** Reflector leak at CNGS

After the extremely smooth start of the CNGS run, the run was stopped Oct 27 when rapidly increasing water levels in a safety reservoir indicated a water leak in the CNGS reflector area. The 2nd horn at CNGS is called the reflector. It was decided to stop the CNGS run for 2006. The leak will be fixed by the time the emulsion target for OPERA is installed in 2007. http://spsschedule.web.cern.ch/SPSschedule/2006/Users_Meetings/last.html

*** ANITA launched

The ANITA balloon-borne high-energy neutrino telescope launched from McMurdo Antarctica on Dec 15, 2006, which is 95 y + 1 d after Amundsen was the first to reach the South Pole. The primary purpose of ANITA is to detect cosmogenic (GZK) neutrinos. Pictures and movies (Quicktime) of payload preparation and launch can be found at http://www.ps.uci.edu/~barwick/ANITA06/.

*** SciBooNE installs scintillator

The SciBar portion of SciBooNE was installed in December http://www.fnal.gov/pub/today/archive_2006/today06-12-14.html. SciBooNE is a neutrino detector that will inhabit a 23-by-16-foot pit about 300-feet downstream of the neutrino beam source, powered by the booster accelerator. It will have scintillator, an electron catcher, and muon range detector. See some entries in the SciBooNE logos contest at http://www-sciboone.fnal.gov/pictures/logos/
