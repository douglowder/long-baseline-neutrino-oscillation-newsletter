Long-Baseline news, August 2004

*** APS multidivisional Neutrino Study status

The draft report of the APS multidivisional neutrino study is called "The Neutrino Matrix": matrix n: 1. something within or from which something else originates, develops or takes form 2. the natural material in which something is embedded 3. womb 4. a rectangular array of mathematical elements. The current goal is to finish by an October 25 DOE/NSF briefing.

*** Proton driver Workshop at Fermilab October 6-9

There is a physics study for a 2 MW, 8 GeV proton driver with a new Linac and 2 MW at 150 GeV. Motivated by neutrino oscillation, but a group is formulating an ambitious neutrino scattering program. They will address the specific facility design challenges of a high-purity anti-neutrino beam, a narrow-band beam and fine-grained detectors incorporating LH2 and LD2 targets for both 8 GeV and 150 GeV protons on target. A report is due in December. Oct 6-9 Workshop of all working groups at Fermilab: http://www-td.fnal.gov/projects/PD/PhysicsIncludes/Workshop/

*** Do neutrinos oscillate in air?

MaVaNs are mass varying neutrinos as suggested by nelson http://133.1.141.120/%7enufact04_talks/talks/28_ple_nelson.pdf She suggests the neutrino oscillations might be matter dependent, so Super-K, Soudan and MACRO should reanalyze their atmospheric data not including the path lengths in the air. New reactor searches should be done with pathlength both in air and rock.

*** KamLAND polonium

Bi214->Po214 make double coincidences from the Q=3.27 MeV beta decay of Bi followed 164 usec later by a 7.7 MeV alpha from Po. The alphas are highly-quenched (factor of 13) so they appear as 0.6 MeV events, far below the 1.8 MeV cutoff for neutrons. Po210 gives a 5.4 MeV single alpha (quenched to 0.4 MeV) and goes almost 100% to Pb206 (small 10**-5 branching ratio to Pb206* at 0.8 MeV).
