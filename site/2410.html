Long-Baseline news, October 2024

*** Neutrino running at Fermilab

Main Injector operations at Fermilab have been negatively impacted by problems electrical and cooling infrastructure on the FNAL site. There is a reduced set of substation transformers which provide power to the experimental areas. Using three of the eight transformers, LINAC and Booster beam (for the short-baseline program) can take place, but substantial Main Injector operations (for NOvA) cannot be supported. The inside of the heat exchangers which are used to cool Main Injector components are corroded. A date for the return of NuMI beam hasn't yet been determined.

*** Cross Section Results from MicroBooNE

There has been some discussion/debate in the community about whether one can/should extract cross sections as a function of the true neutrino energy, as MicroBooNE did in Phys. Rev. Lett. 128, 151801 (2022), arXiv:2307.06413, Phys. Rev. Lett. 133, 041801 (2024), and Phys. Rev. D 110, 013006 (2024). A new paper, arXiv:2411.03280, describes the techniques we have used in those analyses in order to increase our confidence in the results, and reduces model-dependence of the extracted cross sections.

*** The neutrino force

The standard model predicts a long-range force between fermions due to the exchange of a pair of neutrinos. It is a loop effect rather than a classical boson exchange. At small distances, if falls as r^(-1) while at large distances it falls as r^(-5). In arXiv:2410.19059, Ghosh et al. study the effect for atomic parity violation experiments, and find a 4% effect for muonium and a 16% effect in positronium, with implications for measuring the weak mixing angle.

*** Memory Burden and neutrinos

Quantum effects may slow down the evaporation of primordial black holes. This is called memory burden and is equivalent to stating that the amount of information stored within a system contributes to its stabilization. Chianese et al. in arXiv:2410.07604 study the constraints on this from IceCube neutrino data and Zantedeschi and Visinelli also limit this using cosmic ray data.

*** Final Results of Majorana Demonstrator for six modes

76Ge could double-beta decay into three excited states of 76Se with zero or two neutrinos. In arXiv:2410.03995, the Majorana collaboration sets the strongest limits so far in all six modes. The halftime limit is > 1.5e24 years (90% CL).

*** Dueling views of quantum field theory

L. Jia in arXiv:2410.06604 associates the vacuum E of neutrino fields with dark energy and calculates m1 between 6 and 16 meV. But D. Cline in arXiv:2410.10522 write, "surprising proposals, which sound implausible at the intuitive level, are difficult to immediately debunk because they are expressed in language that is not quite the mainstream parlance." Another discussion can be found in arXiv:2410.03133, and arXiv:2410.05826.
