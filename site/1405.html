Long-Baseline news, May 2014

*** ICFA neutrino Panel issues Report

The preliminary report from the Neutrino panel of the International Committee for Future Accelerators (ICFA) was issued. http://www.fnal.gov/directorate/icfa/neutrino_panel.html You can download the report there, which starts with a neutrino manifesto, reviews the current program, describes opportunities in the near- and long-term, as well as next steps. You can also sign up for the ICFA-NEUTRINO-COMMUNITY email list.

*** HEPAP P5 subpanel issues Report

The report was presented to HEPAP & approved. Recommendations for the future US program focus on long-baseline neutrinos. There is no plan B. See http://usparticlephysics.org/p5/ and http://bigstory.ap.org/article/physics-panel-feds-beam-us-some-neutrinos

*** P5 report recommendations 12, 13, 14

Recommendation 12-In collaboration with international partners, develop a coherent short- and long-baseline neutrino program hosted at Fermilab.
Recommendation 13-Form a new international collaboration to design and execute a highly capable Long-Baseline Neutrino Facility (LBNF) hosted by the U.S. To proceed, a project plan and identified resources must exist to meet the minimum requirements in the text. LBNF is the highest priority large project in its timeframe.
Recommendation 14-Upgrade the Fermilab proton accelerator complex to produce higher intensity beams. R&D for PIP-II should proceed immediately, followed by construction, to provide proton beams of >1 MW by the time of first operation of the new LBNF.

*** Reformulation is unreconfiguration

The LBNE collaboration proposed and costed a 34 fiducial kt liquid argon detector underground at SURF with a fully capable near detector. The "reconfiguration" process led to CD1 for a 10 kt surface detector with no ND. P5 recommends the configuration that LBNE wanted. No new CD1 is required.

*** LBNA to LBNZ

I was told that the NBI corporation nbicorp.net chose its name from the acronym "Nothing But Initials". I don't know if that's really true. The choice of the P5 panel to call a liquid argon detector using a beam from Fermilab to SURF, the Long-Baseline Neutrino Facility, rather than LBNE has led to a number of jokes with at least 24 punchlines. The Colliding Detector Facility had to change name after 15y to the Collider Detector at Fermilab.

*** CHIPS R&D Plan approved by Fermilab PAC

After the approval of the CHIPS R&D program, the collaboration are ready to deploy a model detector in the Wentworth pit this summer. On 28 July it will deploy 5 Ice-Cube DOMs and monitor cosmics & light attenuation over the winter. The main goal is to understand mechanical issues associated with such a detector at 60 m depth.

*** More on CHIPS, RADAR, LAR1, NuSTORM, MAP (Reference p17 of P5)

These were called out by P5 as specific examples of large-scale projects not recommended for funding in any budget scenario. This doesn't apply to the CHIPS R&D program. There will be a DOE technical review in April 2015 to advise the agency and the principals on next steps. Depending on the R&D program, which is expected to take 3 years, and the size of any international contribution, CHIPS could be part of the "small experiments" portfolio endorsed by P5, or as a variant of the large water Cherenkov detector addition to LBNF endorsed by P5 under "C".

*** Towards figures-of-merit beyond the Standard Nu Model

Many neutrino reports of the last 10 y have content which is quite similar, naturally. Section 4 of the ICFA report does something valuable which is new. In 2 pages it tries to articulate the goals of the program and the figures-of-merit against which proposed contributions to the program can be judged. http://www.fnal.gov/directorate/icfa/files/ICFA-Neutrino-Panel-Rpt-1.pdf

*** NOvA Near Detector sees the beam

The NOvA collaboration at Fermilab had previously run a Near Detector on the Surface (NDOS) which took data for a couple of years, and is finishing construction of its 14 kiloton Far Detector, which has been measuring neutrinos from Fermilab since November 13. This month it turned on the first sixth of the new Near Detector, which is built at the same off-axis angle as the FD, and it has observed a timing peak from beam events.
