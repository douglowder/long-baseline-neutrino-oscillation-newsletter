Long-Baseline news, December 2015

*** DUNE/LBNF gets CD1R and passes review toward CD3a

Following the successful CD-1R review in July, DOE Under Secretary Orr formally approved LBNF/DUNE CD-1R. And in December, LBNF/DUNE achieved another major milestone with the successful completion of a DOE Independent Project Review. The purpose was to assess the readiness to begin early construction at the far site. Initial construction work needs to begin in FY17. This includes expanding the existing drifts, constructing systems to move the rock to the surface and completing other work necessary for the major excavation operations planned to start in FY18. That excavation work will move 800,000 tons of rock to create the underground laboratory space needed for the 40-kton fiducial volume DUNE. http://news.fnal.gov/2015/12/achieving-a-major-lbnf-dune-milestone/

*** LBNF construction included in U.S. 2016 budget

A U.S. Budget "compromise" passed Congress in late December. It includes $26M for construction of the Long-Baseline Neutrino Facility at Fermilab. See page 34 (located on page 73 of the PDF file) at http://aip-info.org/1ZJN-3X9L8-E29E6Q-1WUH4R-1/c.aspx.

*** The neutrino mass crash of 2015

Cosmological measurements are sensitive to nu properties. Some physicists ignore them, as they did the solar neutrino problem. Francesco Vissani has developed a Linked-In page tracking this: https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/2015-neutrino-mass-crash-francesco-vissani The assumptions need to be fully understood, but taking the results at face value, the hierarchy is normal.

*** Short-Baseline Neutrino Program Progress Review

There was a Dec. Director's Progress Review of SBN covering cost, schedule, management, ES&H and technical aspects. The committee commended the team "for the good progress made so far, and looks forward to a successful completion of the construction and a successful scientific program that it will enable." The schedule goal of completing SBND ready for cold commissioning by July 2018 and delivery of ICARUS to Fermilab is expected early 2017. http://www.fnal.gov/directorate/OPMO/Projects/SBN/DirRev/2015/20151215/Final_Report_SBN_DPR_2015.pdf

*** Presidential candidate might block nu oscillations

Waving a report from the TeV muon collider study that muon neutrino radiation could be lethal, one of the presidential candidates proposed banning all muon neutrinos from entering the borders of the United States. "We have to protect the homeland" the candidate stated during a rally in Iowa. When questioned about how to prevent muon neutrinos from going through the borders, the reply was "I'll put up a wall. If it has to be light years of lead, so be it." When asked by a reporter at a stop in New Hampshire how to deal with the fact that electron and tau neutrinos have been oscillating into muon neutrinos, the answer was that when president, "...that will have to stop, drastic times call for drastic measures."
