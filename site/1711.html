*** Progress Review for Fermilab's short-baseline program

An extensive document showing the motivation, design and schedule of the Fermilab short-baseline program is at arXiv:1503.01520 . The 3 detector program involved MicroBoone which was already approved, SBND which was to be built, and ICARUS which was to be moved from CERN. MicroBooNE is running, but while great progress has been made on the other 2 detectors, the original schedule called for detector cooling to finish this Dec. and that's not happening. A director's review in Nov. recommended a bottoms-up "Estimate to Completion " be developed. The physics goal is to resolve the short-baseline anomalies suggested by LSND and MiniBooNE. https://web.fnal.gov/organization/OPSS/Projects/SBN/_layouts/15/start.aspx#/SitePages/Director's%20Progress%20Review%20of%20SBN%2C%20November%201-2%2C%202017.aspx

*** Tau neutrino cross section with atmospheric nus

The Super-Kamiokande experiment expects about one tau neutrino charged current interaction from atmospheric neutrino oscillations per kiloton year. With 14.6 live-years of data in arXiv:1711.9436 they report 338 events, consistent with tau neutrino appearance. Since m_tau/m_p > 1, different structure functions matter compared to e/mu. Super-K excludes no-tau-appearance at 4.6 sigma, and reports the CC cross section averaged by the flux (3.5 to 70 GeV) to be 0.94 +- 0.20 e(-38) cm^2. This is 1.47 +- 0.32 expectation.

*** DOE Portfolio Review

The U.S. Department of Energy is conducting a review of currently operating DOE/HEP supported experiments. Glen Crawford reported at HEPAP that ".. it was realized that if the (2018 President's) budget was enacted as requested, it would require significant reductions to current HEP operating experiments and associated research, in order to preserve P5 projects at a much lower budget level than P5 envisioned." The 7 neutrino experiments among the 15 are Daya Bay, MicroBoone, Minerva, NA61/SHINE, NOvA, Super-K and T2K. The review covers running in FY2019-22. A subpanel report is due to HEPAP Apr 2018.

*** Ice-Cube reports earth shadow in neutrinos

Neutrinos are known to interact weakly, but the cross section increases with energy. A new paper by Ice-Cube, "Measurement of the multi-TeV neutrino cross section with IceCube using Earth absorption", arXiv:1711.08119 , published in Nature, gives evidence for neutrino absorption in the earth for the first time. Also see arXiv:1711.11043 .

*** Neutrino mass from enhanced clustering of galaxies

This group of authors, which includes one Nobel prize winner, fits Sloan data to neutrino mass, & gets the 3 neutrino mass sum to be 110 meV +- 30 meV. Needed assumptions are stated, and the best agreement with the inverted mass ordering is noted. They predict appreciable clustering enhancement in SDSS. But one cosmologist friend tells me robust structure formation limits on neutrino mass are still years away. See Emami et al. in arXiv:1711.05210 .
