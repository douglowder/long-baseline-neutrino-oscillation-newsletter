Long-Baseline news, March 2011

*** Note from KEK sent out just after the earthquake

"Japan experienced very severe earthquake on 11 Mar 11 at 14:46. J-PARC facility suffered damages for some extent. There are no reports of casualties and all staff, graduate students and foreign visitors have been located and as of evening 13 Mar all T2K members have been evacuated from Tokai. Fortunately the Tsunami tidal wave did not hit J-PARC. We will start the investigation of the facilities. We will updated the announcement as we learn the detail of the entire damage. Our present priority is to restore life-supporting infrastructure such as electricity, water supply and gas at J-PARC. It may take some time, but we promise the full recovery of the J-PARC accelerator and T2K experiment in the near future. We thank you for the messages of solidarity and sympathy." Director of the Institute of Particle and Nuclear Studies, KEK Koichiro Nishikawa & Spokesperson of the T2K experiment, Takashi Kobayashi

*** News about damage and progress at JPARC

The extent of the damage to J-PARC was updated on 29 Mar. See http://kds.kek.jp/materialDisplay.py?materialId=0&confId=6810 You will be asked to enter the ID/password shown pop-up. Although J-PARC and the neighboring nuclear reactor are along the beach, they were designed for a 10 m tsunami and it was 3 m there. The earthquake damaged roads, infrastructure and buildings at J-PARC, & there was a few cm of water in some places. Electricity for lights are on for the Main Ring, Neutrino, Hadron & MLF. A proposal for the "1st urgent budget request" to the government is done, and a 2nd request is due mid May after more assessment.

*** Can neutrinos be used to predict earthquakes?

Probably not, but the neutrino group sees an intriguing sharp drop in the vertical elevation at Tokai in the few days before the earthquake in two GPS units. No change is seen at Super-K. Look at the last slide in the previous item.

*** Fire in the Soudan shaft

The MINOS experiment is in a lab a half mile underground. A fire occurred on 17 Mar in the evening while no-one was in the lab http://news.dnr.state.mn.us/index.php/2011/03/18/fire-underway-in-mine-shaft-at-soudan-underground-mine-state-park/ The fire system automatically cut power to MINOS, which wasn't running due to a target replacement anyway. Foam was poured down the shaft to put the fire out. When staff returned to the underground lab, there was no visible fire or smoke damage. However, the fire doors to the MINOS Lab were pushed open and the MINOS Lab has significant quantities of foam and debris from the north end to the MINOS Detector. At the Detector, the foam reached as high as about a foot below the Detector core hole but was below the readout electronics. Power has been restored for pumps and lights. The cause of the fire will be announced after an investigation. Some infrastructure in the shaft needs replacement. http://news.dnr.state.mn.us/index.php/2011/03/23/power-restored-to-27th-level-of-soudan-mine-but-park-remains-closed-until-further-notice/

*** New neutrino oscillation parameter from MINOS

A new more accurate value of dmsq_32 from MINOS for 7.2e20 pot was presented at Neutrino 2010. The previous published result was 3.4e20 pot. The larger data set has now been submitted for publication, and global fitters should notice a change from the Nu10 value. Now 2.32 +0.12 -0.08 10^(-3) eV^2. arXiv:1103.0340

*** Nobel Prize winner Van der Meer designed the neutrino horn

Simon van der Meer, who recently passed away, got the Nobel Prize for Stochastic Cooling, but also invented the "horn", the focusing device used in neutrino experiments. I was originally planning to devote this newsletter to horn stories, but ran out of space. Please share with me any "horn" stories for a future newsletter.

*** MAP approved

The Muon Accelerator Program Management plan was signed on 14 Mar by Acting Associate Director for High Energy Physics Mike Procario. Steve Geer (FNAL) and Michael Zisman (LBNL) are codirectors for this accelerator R&D program in the U.S. http://map.fnal.gov/organization/MAP-Approval.PDF

*** Chang Kee Jung elected T2K co-spokesperson

Stony Brook's Chang Kee Jung was elected in February as International Co-Spokesperson of the T2K long-baseline neutrino experiment. He replaced David Wark from Imperial College. The (~500 member) T2K collaboration votes on this position.

*** Bruno Pontecorvo Prize awarded to Prof. Yoichuro Suzuki

The prestigious 2010 Bruno Pontecorvo Prize has been awarded to Prof. Y. Suzuki, the director of Kamioka Observatory, for his outstanding contribution to the discovery of atmospheric and solar neutrino oscillations in the Super-Kamiokande experiment. The Pontecorvo Prize has been awarded annually since 1995 by the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research(JINR) in Dubna to scientists who have achieved outstanding results in particle physics. http://www.ipmu.jp/node/967
