Long-Baseline news, June 2006

*** Daya Bay Press release

The Reactor Neutrino Experiment at the Daya Bay reactor outside of Hong Kong is now the largest U.S. - China collaboration ever. 11 U.S. groups have now joined this collaboration. Its goal is to measure the neutrino mixing parameter theta-13. http://english.eastday.com/eastday/englishedition/nation/userobject1ai2089796.html It was also mentioned as a priority for US-China in the NY Times- http://www.nytimes.com/2006/06/20/science/20hawk.html?ex=1151467200&en=077011b3a6a01a4a&ei=5070&emc=eta1

*** BENE report published for future neutrino experiments in Europe

The "Beams for European Neutrino Experiments" Networking Activity has issued its report as CERN Yellow Report CERN-2006-005 and as CARE-2006-009-BENE ECFA/06/242. It is an outline and a plea for a timely neutrino accelerator and detector R&D program. It is at http://people.na.infn.it/%7Epalladin/BeneMidtermReport.pdf and from thee BENE front Web Page http://bene.web.cern.ch/bene/

*** P5 (in Peru?) recommends NOvA long-baseline neutrino experiment

In Peru, every license plate has "P5" at the top. This stands for Plato Proceso para Peruano Principado (chiste). Peruvian Director of FNAL is pleased the Particle Physics Project Prioritization Panel recommended "the start of construction on the NOvA neutrino oscillation experiment using the NuMI beamline at Fermilab. This experiment is complementary to the other neutrino experiments on a worldwide basis and represents the next step for the U.S. in a phased international program aimed at measuring the remaining parameters of the neutrino oscillation matrix, determining the mass ordering among the neutrino mass eigenstates, and finding out whether neutrinos violate the CP symmetry."

*** DUSEL progress for underground lab

Conceptual Design Reports for Henderson and Homestake were turned in to the NSF in late June. TDR is expected in FY07 and $3M R&D funds are available in FY07 for physics. A Property Donation Agreement for Homestake was completed in April and the property formally transferred to South Dakota in May. There will be an early implementation program 2007-2012 with South Dakota funds.

*** MINOS new results on delta msquared_32.

An updated neutrino oscillation result was presented at Nu06 from 1.27 10^20 POT. Delta m^2_32 = 2.72 +.38 -.25(stat) +- 0.13 (sys) 10^-3 eV^2, and sin^2 2-theta_23 = 1.00 - 0.13(stat) +- 0.04(sys) http://neutrinosantafe06.com/downloads/Session7/7.8Nelson.pdf

*** Donut controversy

Fermilab Today used doughnuts to describe the collider energy, but it is well known this metaphor is more appropriate to neutrinos. http://www.fnal.gov/pub/today/archive_2006/today06-06-28.html

*** MiNERvA gets CD-0 and an MRI from the NSF

The Department of Energy (DOE) Office of Science has granted CD-0 approval to the MINERvA experiment. CD-0 approval is a statement detailing the scientific need for a DOE project. MINERvA is a low energy neutrino scattering experiment which uses the NuMI beamline at Fermilab. MiNERvA was also Selected by the National Science Foundation to Receive Major Research Instrumentation Grant in the amount of $777,485. http://minerva.fnal.gov/

*** Santa Fe Mayor addresses Neutrino 2006

The honorable David Coss, mayor of Santa Fe New Mexico is a biologist. But he carefully read the material that was sent to him about neutrinos and noted that it said, "Neutrinos have mass, but only under certain circumstances." This reminded him of many towns in Northern New Mexico, which have mass, but only under certain circumstances, i.e. when the priest shows up.

*** New law of Physics at Neutrino 2006

During the History session at Neutrino 06, Herald Kruse, the only surviving member of the Savannah River experiment which discovered the neutrino, reminisced on his experiences on the experiment and received an award. At dinner he commented that it was a new law of physics, outlive everyone else and you win an award.
