Long-Baseline news, May 2007

*** Report of the U.S. long-baseline neutrino experiment study

This study, led by G. Rameika and M. Diwan was sponsored by Fermilab and Brookhaven. The report is at arXiv:0705.4396 The study compares beam options - NuMI on-axis, NuMI off-axis 1st maximum, NuMI 2nd maximum, WBB to DUSEL and detector options Water Cerenkov Detector and Liquid Argon.

*** NOvA gets Critical Decision One (CD1)

The Neutrino Oscillation nu-e Appearance Experiment at FNAL had its CD1 Review last year, but it takes a while, sometimes, for bureaucrats to sign things. This involved, in part, the procedure for a far detector site in Northern Minnesota. A large 18 kton detector will be built there. The NOvA "Lehman" review for CD2/CD3a is scheduled for July 17.

*** NuSAG studying future accelerator neutrino options

http://www.fnal.gov/directorate/Longrange/Steering_Public/files/070514_NuSAG_Meyers.pdf With a charge, "Assuming a megawatt class proton accelerator as a neutrino source, please answer the following questions for accelerator-detector configurations including those needed for a multi-phase off-axis program and a very-long-baseline broad-band program." and the questions involved scientific potential, detector options, cost, schedule and international context. A report to HEPAP is due soon. One intriguing statement is "One strategic issue seems clear: can't start construction on Phase 2 without an observation of non-zero theta-13"

*** US DOE High Energy Physics Plan

http://www.science.doe.gov/hep/HEPAPlongtermassessmentreport.pdf A set of goals for High Energy Physics were approved by the HEPAP in Dec 06. The neutrino goal (1 of 6) is the most specific. It is "Determine the pattern of the neutrino masses and the details of their mixing parameters." & 1.) Confirm or refute evidence for additional neutrino species (referring to LSND & now probably 'met'); 2.) Confirm or rule out the current picture of atmospheric neutrino oscillations (already 'met'); 3) Measure Delta msquared-32 to 15% (needs 7.5e20 pot for MINOS); 4) Measure non-zero value for sin^2(2 theta13) or constrain < 0.06(90%CL)- (Double Chooz will meet by 2010 & Daya Bay will exceed by 2014).

*** Prediction for Theta-13

In "CKM and Tri-bimaximal MNS Matrices in a SU(5) x (d)T Model", arXiv:0705.0714 Chen & Mahanthappa find a sum rule tan^2(theta_12) = tan^2(theta_12,TBM)-theta_c/2*cos(beta), where the correction from the charged lepton sector (the second term) is related to the Cabibbo angle and it could account for the difference between the experimental best fit value and prediction of the tri-bimaximal mixing matrix. The prediction for theta_13 ~ theta_c / (3 sqrt[2]) ~ 0.05, may be accessible to the reactor experiments.

*** T2K (Tokai to Super-Kamiokande neutrino beam) progress

Construction of the Target Station (TS) is going on, which follows the 200m-long extraction beam tunnel and will contain the horn and target. The bottom part of the TS is being constructed. The installation of the frame structure which will serve as a Helium vessel will be installed in August. The 1st horn test, started Dec 06 has recently finished. This was excited by a 320 kA pulse peak current and total pulses reached 850,000. There was a small water leak (fixed). The 3rd horn was completed and delivered to KEK. This 3rd horn will be tested in series with the 1st one.

*** Beta Beam Progress (from Mandate at http://cern.ch/beta-beam)

A Beta Beam task force maintains a web page http://cern.ch/beta-beam. The acceleration of high intensity, radioactive ion beams to highest energies requires novel design solutions. The objective is to do a Conceptual Design Study (CDS), covering all accelerator physics aspects, to demonstrate the feasibility of a Beta Beam facility. The final report will contain: the optics design of the accelerator chain, beam dynamic simulations of critical processes, a loss management concept, first stage technical designs demonstrating the feasibility of critical hardware, a facility layout and a cost estimate. The combination of EURISOL with a Beta Beam would make optimum use of the enormous potential synergies and give a world class facility attracting not only the nuclear, but also neutrino and particle physics communities.
