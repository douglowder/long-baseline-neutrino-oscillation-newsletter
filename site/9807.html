Long-Baseline Neutrino News July 1998

*** Super-Kamiokande solar data may be "just-so"

With 504 days and 6.5 MeV threshold, their flux is .474 of the BP98 calculation for solar neutrinos. Looking for an energy distortion, the 3 bins above 13 MeV are high with large errors. This is suggestive of the "just-so" solar neutrino solution, vacuum neutrino oscillations with L_osc near 1-AU. No day/night effect is seen, particular in the "Night-5" bin which goes through the core. The seasonal data is consistent with 1/R^2 from the sun. The best "just-so" fit gives dmsq = 4.2 10^-10 and sin^2 2q = 0.93, but there is poor overlap with the regions from Hata/Langacker '98. "can't say anything conclusive". Nus from 5.0 to 6.5 MeV are also now seen with larger backgrounds.

*** Soudan 2 "high-resolution" nu data has good L/E resolution

At Vancouver, Hugh Gallagher reported on a new analysis of 3.9 kt-year data from Soudan 2 using events with good angular resolution. These are the low energy events with recoil nucleons, high energy quasi-elastic events, and multiprongs. R is reported to be .64 +- .11 +- .06. With the high resolution sample, R = .52 and L/E plots were shown. Values of delta-msquared above 10^-3 are favored.

*** The Richter scale shakes neutrinos at Vancouver ICHEP

Last month the Richter scale was mentioned for SNO in Ontario. In Vancouver Canada, nu's dominated ICHEP98. Following an excellent neutrino review by Janet Conrad, (after the speaker had been applauded and her microphone turned off), session chair Burt Richter made a skeptical remark about the use of 90% C.L.'s by neutrino physicists. About 20 of the 800 there applauded him. The rest of us were a bit confused, because taken literally, he implied one should never quote a 1-sigma error. (Color transparencies: www-e815.fnal.gov/~jconrad/icheptalk.html )

*** Arsenic is good for GALLEX's health

An Arsenic 71 exposure of Gallex was performed to test the chemistry response with dilutions at the 10-26 level to 1% in efficiency of extraction. They got perfect agreement, measuring .998+-.008 of the expected value, averaged over a variety of conditions. They measure 77.5 +-8 SNU; 60 +-7% of models.

*** Heat data heats up neutrino flux confusion

A new measurement of atmospheric muons suggests that the solar-min neutrino flux should be 30-50% lower. The energy distributions are in excellent agreement with the TARGET code, used for the Bartol flux. One possibility is that too high a primary proton flux was used in the calculation.

For non-maximal mixing nue to nutau and nue to numu oscillations are predicted that may be observable in future long-baseline experiments. (June 98)
