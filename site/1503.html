Long-Baseline news, March 2015

*** ELBNF renamed DUNE, Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment

The new long-baseline experiment at Fermilab, currently with a membership of 737 people from 148 institutions in 26 countries, has chosen the name "Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment" or DUNE. A contest for a logo (sand is ok, but no worms) will start soon. http://hep.colostate.edu/~dcherdack/Naming_Contest/Logo_Contest.html The first spokespersons have been elected and are Andre Rubbia from ETH-Zurich and Mark Thomson from Cambridge. A collaboration meeting is scheduled for 16-18 April at Fermilab. https://indico.fnal.gov/conferenceDisplay.py?confId=9740 The Dawn of Dune http://www.symmetrymagazine.org/article/march-2015/the-dawn-of-dune

*** LAr1-ND renamed SBND, Short-Baseline Near Detector

Several names were suggested for this element of the Fermilab short-baseline neutrino program, and the first-round winners were Booster Oscillation Near Detector, The neutrino Liquid Argon Near Detector and Short-Baseline Near Detector. Of more interest to some were some of the losers, such as TEFKAL (The Experiment Formerly known as LAr1-ND), LBNE (Liquidargon Booster Neutrino Experiment), NECROBooNE (NEar miCROBooNE), BANANA (Booster-Argon Neutrino Anti- Neutrino Apparatus), TURKEY (The Ultimate, Righteous, Koolest Experiment Yet), and LARP (Liquid ARgon Project).

*** Why physicists sometimes disagree

If data doesn't agree with the null hypothesis, and it also doesn't agree with the alternative hypothesis, some physicists say you need new data. Others say you need a new hypothesis.

*** J-PARC increases intensity for the T2K experiment

J-PARC has reached a great milestone, 1e21 protons on target toward Super-Kamiokande for T2K. And in February, Tsuyoshi Nakaya replaced Takashi Kobayashi as the spokesperson for T2K. For their work on neutrino physics, Kobayashi has been awarded the Yomiuri Gold Medal, and Nakaya together with Masato Shiozawa has been awarded the Yoji Totsuka prize. http://www.ipmu.jp/node/2151 ; http://t2k-experiment.org/2015/03/takashi-kobayashi-is-awarded-the-yomiuri-gold-medal/

*** Slip Stacking increases intensity for NOvA

FNAL is now running 2+6 slipstacking on all NuMI cycles. This has resulted in an increase of NuMI target power from ~325kW to ~360kW with the Fixed Target program running. It reached 453 kW for an hour and satisfied a goal of running more than 400 kW for 112 hours. http://www.theguardian.com/science/life-and-physics/2015/mar/29/neutrinos-rarely-interact-but-watch-them-hit-the-nova-detector-here

*** Neutral Current Quasi-elastic Scattering

(Thanks to nu-xsec newsletter) The neutrino community often calls it "NCEL", nu+A->nu+p+A' because we don't care about the final state of the nucleus. However, the final state is usually an excited nucleus, which is not "elastic scattering" in the nuclear physics sense. But T2K has measured de-excitation gammas from NC scattering, arXiv:1403.3140 so it may be the time to call it NCQE.
