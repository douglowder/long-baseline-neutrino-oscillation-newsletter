Long-Baseline news, May 2015

*** Hyper-K Memorandum

KEK-IPNS and Tokyo-ICRR have signed a Memorandum of Understanding for cooperation in the promotion of the Hyper-Kamiokande experiment. A proto-collaboration has formed with 240 members from 13 countries. KEK and ICRR have requested a design report which will be prepared for a budget request in 2015. A master plan from the Science Council of Japan and a roadmap from MEXT will be prepared in 2016-17.

*** First antineutrino results from T2K

The first running of antineutrinos in the Tokai to Kamiokande experiment shows a clear dip below 1 GeV as is expected for an oscillation signal. The best fit for sin^2(q) is 0.515 + 0.085 - 0.095 and for dmsq_32 is 2.33 +.27 -.23 e-3 eV^2. https://www2.kek.jp/physics-seminar/files2015/20150518_T2K.pdf

*** Testbeam at Lariat

Accelerator operators have begun using a revived beamline to send particles to LArIAT, Liquid Argon TPC in a Test Beam, which will advance particle detector technologies for neutrino experiments. http://www.fnal.gov/pub/today/archive/archive_2015/today15-05-05.html

*** MicroBoone

Shifts have begun on the MicroBooNE experiment, which has built a liquid argon detector in the Booster beamline. They have started cooling down and will start filling with liquid Argon soon. An increase in readout noise for ~1% of the wires led to need for inspection inside the detector. In April there were photos taken with a clever camera-mirror-lighting arrangement but all was found to be in order to proceed with filling. Meanwhile the horn in the booster beamline, which had run for years for MiniBooNE, has been replaced. https://www.facebook.com/MicroBooNE

*** White paper from INO

The upcoming 50 kt magnetized iron calorimeter (ICAL) detector at the India-based Neutrino Observatory (INO) is designed to study the atmospheric neutrinos and antineutrinos separately over a wide range of energies and path lengths. The primary focus is to explore matter effects by observing the energy and zenith angle dependence of the atmospheric neutrinos in the multi-GeV range. They outline the analyses carried out for the determination of neutrino mass hierarchy and precision measurements of atmospheric neutrino mixing parameters at ICAL, and give the expected physics reach of the detector with 10 years of runtime. They also explore the potential of ICAL for probing new physics scenarios like CPT violation and the presence of magnetic monopoles. arXiv:1505.07380

*** 25 June event to mark start of ionization cooling by MICE

On the 25th June 2015 the STFC Rutherford Appleton Laboratory is hosting an event to mark the start of the study of ionization cooling with the international Muon Ionization Cooling Experiment (MICE). Ionization cooling is the technique by which it is proposed to reduce the mu-beam phase space in a future nu factory or mu collider. https://eventbrite.co.uk/event/16827137421/

*** Public meeting 24 June for the DUNE/LBNF project (free)

The U.S. DOE is hosting a public meeting at FNAL on 24 June, 6:30- 8:30 p.m. to gather comments on the preliminary findings of the related environmental studies. It will provide information on the planned facility and the expected environmental impacts as shown in a Draft Environmental Assessment that's been prepared pursuant to the National Environmental Policy Act. There will be short presentations, posters, and the opportunity to ask questions and submit comments. The public comment period for the environmental assessment begins on 8 June and runs until 10 July. See http://lbnf.fnal.gov/env-assessment.html

*** Lee Grodzins postdoctoral award honors neutrino postdoc

Nominations are due 15 July for this award created to recognize Grodzins who performed groundbreaking measurements of the helicity of the neutrino with Maurice Goldhaber in 1957. http://web.mit.edu/lns/news/Lee_GRODZINS_prize/
