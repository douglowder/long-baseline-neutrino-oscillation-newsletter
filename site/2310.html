Long-Baseline news, October 2023

*** 2023 long range plan for nuclear science released

https://science.osti.gov/-/media/np/nsac/pdf/202310/NSAC_LRP_2023.pdf RECOMMENDATION 2 is "As the highest priority for new experiment construction, we recommend that the US lead an international consortium that will undertake a neutrinoless double beta decay campaign, featuring the expeditious construction of ton-scale experiments, using different isotopes and complementary techniques." The plan endorses CUPID, nEXO and LEGEND-1000. The report is a vision for nuclear physics for the next 5-10 y.

The first NuPHYS after the pandemic will take place at King's College in London. https://indico.cern.ch/event/1234324/ To encourage and facilitate the participation of early-career researchers and PhD students, a poster session will be a key activity of the meeting.

*** NuSTEC summer School, 5-13 June 2024 at CERN

The next installment of this summer school, focused on neutrino- nucleus scattering, will take place at CERN in June. See https://indico.cern.ch/e/nustec-school-2024.

*** MicroBooNE kinematic variable analysis for QE interactions

Using new kinematic imbalance variables, in arXiv:2310.06082, MicroBooNE has shown that GENIE version 2 is inadequate to describe the data. From 6.79e20 protons on target, 250,000 neutrino interactions on argon were studied. GiBUU agrees particularly well in areas of phase space where final state interactions have a very large effect.

*** MicroBooNE decommissioning completed

In October, the argon was all vented which was the last step in decommissioning. Samples were sent for analysis of contaminants and for radiological purity. Some parts are going to be used by SBND, ICARUS, and the DUNE 2x2 detector.

*** TRIDENT chooses site for neutrino detector

https://phys.org/news/2023-10-china-site-trident-neutrino-detector.html "The Tropical Deep-sea Neutrino Telescope (TRIDENT), nicknamed Hai-Ling in Chinese ('ocean bell'), is a planned 7.5 km^3 nu detector sited in the South China Sea near the equator. The detector contains 1,211 strings, each with 20 hDOMs separated vertically by 30 m, and ranging from ~ 2,800-3,400 m underwater. https://www.nature.com/articles/s41550-023-02087-6

*** Ice-Cube reconstruction Kaggle Competition results

https://www.kaggle.com/competitions/icecube-neutrinos-in-deep-ice Ice-Cube sponsored an open competition to predict a neutrino's direction based on data from the IceCube detector, which observes the cosmos from deep within the South Pole ice. 6,460 people registered, of which 901 from 74 countries turned in a valid solution. See arXiv:2307.15289. The top 3 solutions are shown in arXiv:2310.15674. A number of data science competitions are sponsored through https://www.kaggle.com/competitions.

*** Neutrino running at J-PARC to resume in late November

https://j-parc.jp/c/en/topics/2023/10/31001225.html#NU Beam had been disrupted due to a June fire in the Hadron Power Supply Building. There had been no leaked radioactive material, no radiation exposures, & no injuries. Meanwhile, upgraded ND280 detectors have been installed. 2 Time-of-Flight detectors were installed in July. The bottom High-Angle TPC was delivered to J-PARC in August and successfully installed in September. They the SuperFGD was planned for install in Oct. with 1st data soon.

*** Neutrino running at Fermilab to resume ?

At a safety all hands meeting, asked when the accelerator would resume, interim CSO Marc Clay said that they needed to update the Safety Assessment Document (SAD) & Accelerator Safety Envelope (ASE). There is a phased approach for document updates. The goal is to resume running in early February 2024. (Booster & 8 GeV)

*** Predicting the neutrino mixing angles with HVM

It would be nice if a theory could predict the mixing angles. Along the way, it would be nice to have some models which make such predictions. One which does is the Hierarchical VEVs model in arXiv:2310.12915 which predicts the sines of the angles as (12) 0.55 +- 0.00134, (23) 0.775 +- 0.00067, and (13) 0.1413-0.1509.

*** LBNF/DUNE Central Utility Cavern Excavation Complete

The Central Cavern, which will be used for power, liquid argon processing, etc. for the 4 DUNE modules, is complete. The cavern has a concrete slab which is in place at the 90% level. Detector installation will commence in 2024 with first science in 2028.
