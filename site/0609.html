*** Fermilab $26M plans for protons for neutrinos - "proton plan"

The "Proton Plan" represents a multi-year campaign to increase the proton intensity delivered to the 120 GeV & 8 GeV neutrino beams, with upgrades to the LINAC, Booster and Main Injector. Once the elements of this plan are completed, NuMI will accumulate ~3.2E20 protons/year. The 8 GeV beamline will receive ~1.8E20 p/year. The preliminary estimate for the total cost of this plan, including tasks & milestones to address the serious vulnerability issue with LINAC amplifier tubes. Costs are included for alleviating concerns for the availability of these tubes in the short-term, and for developing a plan for the long-term. Similarly, costs are included to determine the repetition rate limitation in the Booster and to develop a plan to exceed this limit, if appropriate. These two long-term plans, when accepted, will require significant addition to the present scope. DOE's FY06 NuMI goal of 1.0E20 p's was met.

*** Double Chooz reactor neutrino experiment press release

In a Joint press release from French agencies CEA/DAPNIA and CNRS/IN2P3, the initial construction of the Double Chooz reactor neutrino experiment was announced. Two identical detectors are to be installed near the Chooz nuclear power plant, in the Ardennes. http://www.neutrinooscillation.org/doublechooz6.doc

*** KASKA reactor neutrino experiment turned down

The proposed reactor neutrino experiment at the Kashiwazaki-Kariwa nuclear power plants has been turned down by the Japanese funding agencies. This is the site of seven nuclear reactors, and is the point on earth with the highest neutrino flux. Members of KASKA have joined the Double Chooz experiment. KASKA will continue the effort to realize a future reactor neutrino experiment in Japan, with a sensitivity to sin^2 w theta-13 better than .01, and thinks that participation in Double Chooz is useful for future projects.

*** International Scoping Studies on Neutrino Facilities

The final report of the Scoping Study (ISS) will be issued by the end of the year. The transition to International Design Studies (IDSs) of the different options, Superbeam(s), Neutrino Factory, Betabeam, is now the goal, as defined at Nufact06. Each design study should produce, over a few years: 1) a complete end-to-end feasibility study of its option, with a the level of engineering necessary for a conceptual design; 2) solutions of the most critical technical problems by means of a set of international R&D demonstration experiments like MUCOOL, HARP, MICE, MERIT... The next ISS/IDS meeting will be at CERN on 21-22 Feb. As emphasized by the recent EPP2010 report and CERN Strategy Document among others, this can only be a truly global effort. Collaborators are in all regions (U.S. Nu Factory Collaboration, European BENE Network, Nufact-J Working Group and others (India, Russia ...). http://www.hep.ph.ic.ac.uk/iss/iss-notes/ISS_Doc4_v06_28-8-2006.pdf

*** Borexino filled

Borexino has been inflated and filled with water. They hope to fill with scintillator early next year.

*** Super-Kamiokande filled again

The Super-Kamiokande rebuild is finished and they have filled the detector, calibrated it and are taking physics runs now. They are back to the phototube coverage they had before the accident.
