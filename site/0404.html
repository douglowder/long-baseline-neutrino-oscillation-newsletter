*** Where is the MINOS Near Detector?

Starting on March 31st, it is in the MINOS near-detector hall, when the first plane was installed underground and about a quarter of the 282 planes were installed during April.

*** What's the name of T2K?

The JPARC accelerator has been called JHF, HIPA and now JPARC. The long-baseline neutrino experiment has been called things like JHF2K and JPARCnu and other combinations, but is now commonly (though not officially) being called T2K (T is for Tokai) in apparent symmetry with K2K.

*** What's the Name of NoVA?

The "NuMI off axis" experiment, which Fermilab's PAC evaluated this month, chose the name NOvA, with the "v" being drawn as a neutrino when possible. This won a naming contest with nominees "CaNOE", "NEAT", "NOAXE" and "03 (pronounced "oh cubed"). Gary Feldman from Harvard and John Cooper from FNAL are spokespersons. NOvA received a favorable response after a FNAL PAC presentation. They wrote: "NOvAs expected sensitivity is approximately the same as that anticipated by T2K Phase I and could be accomplished on roughly the same time scale. However, because NOvA will have a longer baseline, it will be more sensitive to matter effects that may help to resolve the mass hierarchy. NOvA should be able to resolve the mass hierarchy for a small range of parameters."

*** If the target will melt, why not use a liquid target?

Neutrino factories and superbeams under consideration will put more power than ever onto a target. Some of the research involves innovative target designs, such as liquid Mercury jets.

*** What is theta_13?

nucl-th/0404059 unambiguously predicts 0 <= sin^2 2 q_13 <= 1! Other predictions can be found in hep-ph/0403305 & hep-ph/0404084

*** How Big is a Three Sigma Effect?

High Energy Physicists usually mean, "...corresponding to a chance probability for a 3 sigma Gaussian distribution, whatever the true probability distribution function." But is it significant? A hint? evidence? an indication? That's for the reader.

*** If nothing came before the big bang, what's before a Supernova?

If the precursor to a supernova is big enough and close enough, a detectable signal in a neutrino detector might show up from Silicon burning, giving an approximately two day 'warning'. Out to 2 kiloparsecs, GADZOOKS would get 3 sigma. astro-ph/0311012
