Long-Baseline news, November 1998

*** Main Injector is accelerating

Protons have reached 120 GeV. Some even 150 GeV.

*** Long-Baseline is now baselined

The MINOS/NuMI project passed its Lehman "baseline" review in early November, with a variance of less than 1%, which is considered too small to need to change anything. Thus the next steps, preparation of Title 1 for the beam, and putting out the bid to excavate the detector hall, are proceeding.

*** But those bats (not Sammy Sosa's and not Mark McGwire's)

The contract for blasting the MINOS cavity is ready to be put on the street for bids. We had hoped to start blasting in January, but >500 bats have started hibernating near the MINOS entrance, despite some anti-bat counter measures. That sample portends thousands nearby out of ~200,000 in the mine. Thus the excavation will probably start in May rather than March '99.

*** European long-baseline meeting

In early November, a joint meeting of the CERN and Gran Sasso Scientific Advisory Committees considered plans for neutrino physics in Europe. The SPSC concluded that it would make sense to do a neutrino experiment in Gran-Sasso. The timing depends on funding and will be left to the new Director General to sort out. This probably means that there will be no firm plans for a year.

***SNO half empty, half full

The SNO detector has just passed the half way point in filling with heavy water and the light water shielding. All but a few percent of the photomultipliers and associated electronics have been commissioned and measurements of radioactive background events in the detector have been proceeding. Preliminary measurements of gamma ray fluxes from the cavity walls are as expected and radioactive purity of the heavy and light water is already very near the design objectives. Water filling is proceeding steadily and should be completed early in 1999.

*** Auger has interesting neutrino capabilities

If Auger sees events at the horizon, they are probably from neutrinos interacting deep in the atmosphere. The timing signatures will make it easy to separate these from 1500 normal showers per year with zen angle > 70 deg. They are sensitive to 10^18 eV neutrinos which were predicted by early AGN models, but are likely ruled out by recent calculations of Bahcall & Waxman. hep-ph/9807282 The detector will have 10 km^3-sr H2O equivalent. Groundbreaking in Argentina will be the Ides of March, '99.

*** Notable new references

  • Gelb and Rosen, "Another Look at Just-So Oscillations", hep-ph/9809508, The pp neutrinos are reduced to half the standard model prediction and 7Be neutrinos make up a significant part of the gallium signal (in contrast to small-angle MSW). The 7Be neutrinos could show a large seasonal variation.
  • Smirnov "Towards the Solution of the Solar Neutrino Problem", hep-ph/9809481; also Akhmedov et al. in hep-ph/9808270
  • Hall et al, "Tau Appearance In Atmospheric Neutrino Interactions", hep-ph/9810468. If Super-K is numu to nutau, they should have 10 taus already. Looking at backgrounds, they conclude it may be possible to detect taus with 5-10 years data.
  • Janet Conrad, "Recent Results on Neutrino Oscillations", hep-ex/9811009, Plenary Talk presented at the International Conference on High Energy Physics (ICHEP), Vancouver, 1998
  • J Losecco, Bounds on Numu Oscillations from Atmospheric Neutrinos, hep-ex/9811008, An analysis of some IMB up/down data disputes the low delta-m^2 from Super-Kamiokande
  • Barger et al., "Neutrino Decay as an Explanation of Atmospheric Neutrino Observations", astro-ph/9810121 , They show that the observed zenith angle dependence of the atmospheric neutrinos can be accounted for by neutrino decay. Furthermore, it is possible to account for all neutrino anomalies with just three flavors.
  • Goldberg and Weiler, "Lower Energy Consequences of an Anomalous High-Energy Neutrino Cross-Section" hep-ph/9810533 Any anomalous neutrino strong-interaction at high energy, as hypothesized by some to explain the cosmic-ray events above the Greisen cutoff, results in an anomalously large ELASTIC nu-N cross section at much lower energy.
