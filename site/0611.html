*** "SNuMI (SuperNuMI) phase 1" renamed "proton plan phase 2"

NOvA is near CD-1 level approval. The project will be approved assuming increased neutrino fluxes resulting from upgrading the Fermilab accelerators and the NuMI beamline. A Super NuMI (SNuMI) plan to upgrade the proton intensity on target, the NuMI beamline, and target hall components following the shutdown of p-pbar was reviewed at Fermilab in November. This plan utilizes parts of the accelerator complex that will be available in that time frame and envisions upgrading power levels in two phases. SNuMI Phase I goal is 700 KW and the Phase II goal is 1.2 MW. A Conceptual Design Report has been prepared for Phase I describing the steps to be taken to operate the Booster at high rates, re-fit the Recycler as a proton preinjector to MI, and modify the MI and NuMI beamline for more intense beams. A conceptual strategy for incorporating the Accumulator for momentum stacking was discussed as a key component of achieving the Phase II power goal. The success of SNuMI depends critically on the success of the Proton Plan. Both Phases I & II have some technical risk for which accelerator physics simulations are recommended to address before the systems are built. The base costs (no G&A, Contingency, or escalation) in FY06$ were presented for Phases I & II as $33.4M and $53.6M. http://beamdocs.fnal.gov/SNuMI-public/DocDB/ShowDocument?docid=101

*** Neutrino renamed by PDG?

The nu_e, nu_mu and nu_tau aren't really particles. But we don't know everything about nu_1, nu_2 and nu_3 yet. We don't know, for example, if nu_3 is the heaviest or lightest nu. In the particle listings for the 2006 RPP, the neutrinos don't have names. In the new version of the popular Particles and Interactions chart, they are called, nu_lightest, nu_middle and nu_heaviest. http://www.cpepweb.org/images/chart_details/Fermions.jpg

*** NOvA occasionally named EvA

As NOvA prepares for CD2, and awaits the signing of CD1, CD0 had been given to the same project under the name of "Electron Nu Appearance" or Eva. This may have been motivated by a conversation in which U.S. Science Director Orbach asked "What is NOvA for?" answered "Electron neutrino appearance", and "Why don't you call it that?" together with Orbach's wife name of Eva. http://www.er.doe.gov/News_Information/Gallery/01.htm

*** Keeping names the same; MINOS/NOvA elections

Stan Wojcicki of Stanford has been reelected as co-spokesperon of MINOS. Gary Feldman of Harvard has been renominated without competition as co-spokesperson of NOvA.

*** Elections in Italy affect neutrinos

A new government in Italy seems keen to balance their budget and has proposed tax increases and spending cuts. This has squeezed the Italian funding agency INFN, which has had to discourage any new project starts. For example, significant Italian participation in NOvA has been nixed.

*** The neutrino vote, elections in New Mexico

One way to get votes is to arrange that two weeks before an election, you have your face on the cover of a magazine with a famous neutrino physicist. This is what New Mexico Governor Richardson did http://www.neutrinooscillation.org/governor.jpg with Neutrino 2006 organizer Tom Bowles. The ploy worked and Richardson was reelected 363926 to 170352. Now Richardson has 7% in a 2008 Democratic presidential preference poll.

*** MINERvA progress

MINERvA will have a (simultaneous) CD123a review in Germantown in December. Minerva, an experiment to be installed in the Fermilab NuMI beam, will make precise cross section measurements that are crucial for present and future long-baseline oscillation experiments. It will also study nuclear effects by using a variety of nuclear targets.
