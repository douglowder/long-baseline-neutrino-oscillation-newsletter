Long-Baseline news, February 1999

*** K2K beam to target

On Feb.3 16:00(JST), the proton beam was successfully extracted from the PS with newly installed fast extraction system and was transported along the entire beam line to the neutrino target point. The PS group and beam channel group did hard and excellent work.

*** LENS targets low energy solar neutrinos

If detecting neutrinos is considered hard, then detecting low energy (down to 250 keV) solar neutrinos, from the pp reaction, seems almost impossible. The LENS (Low Energy Neutrino Spectroscopy) Collaboration proposes to detect these low energy solar nus. The plan is to detect neutrino capture in which the result is a prompt electron and an excited isomeric state which then emits a photon. The trigger will be the electron with a delayed gamma. The possible target elements are Yb, Se or Gd.

*** NuMI Critical Decision 2

In an important milestone of the NuMI/MINOS project, Martha Krebs, Director of the DOE Office of Science, approved the NuMI Project Execution Plan on Feb. 17. This constitutes Critical Decision 2, Approval of Baseline, and marks the transition to the execution phase of the project. To follow the critical decision process (formally called key decision), see the September 1995 long-baseline newsletter.

*** And God created the earth and the sun

Found on the Borexino home page: "As Everybody knows, the earth and the sun were created for the sake of nu-oscillation experiments. The distance between them was chosen as vacuum oscillation length, the density inside the sun to fit the MSW effect and the sun was prepared as an ideal pure electron nu source. When all this was done, there were created Bruno Pontecorvo to invent idea of neutrino oscillations, John Bahcall to calculate the solar neutrino flux, Ray Davis to perform the first solar nu experiment and all the other solar-neutrino people to accomplish this difficult job." And of course the radius of the earth was chosen for the atmospheric neutrino effect, and the state of Wisconsin to be a neutrino drift space.

*** Majorana neutrino mass limit .2 eV

With 24 kg-yr of data, the Heidelberg Moscow neutrinoless double beta decay experiment sets a lower limit on the half life in 76Ge of 5.7 10^25 yr (90%CL), excluding an effective Majorana neutrino mass greater than .2 eV. This sets strong constraints on degenerate neutrino mass models. See Baudis et al.

*** Neutrino Dark Matter

The first two ( 1, 2,) papers listed below explore the neutrino experiment/astrophysics interface. Neutrino mass implied by atmospheric and solar neutrino experiments is not thought to be cosmologically significant, but adding LSND implies both a 4th neutrino and cosmological consequences.

*** High Mixing angle MSW solution and atmospheric neutrinos

If the solution of the solar neutrino problem is due to the high mixing angle MSW solution, numu to nue oscillations can affect the observation of low energy atmospheric nu_e neutrinos. The last two papers below (1, 2) seem to reach opposite conclusions on whether Super-K data agree with or rule out this possibility.

*** Notable new references

  • D. Caldwell, "Neutrino Dark Matter", hep-ph/9902219

  • J. Bordes et al., "A dynamical mechanism for Quark Mixing and Neutrino Oscillations", hep-ph/9901440. Known features of quarks and lepton mixing, including the large atmospheric neutrino effect, can be explained if fermion generations are given by a gauge symmetry plus a certain Higgs mechanism for its breaking. This predicts neutrinoless double beta decay 2-3 orders of magnitude lower than the present limit.
