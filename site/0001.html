Long-Baseline news, January 2000

*** K2K running in Y2K

The Long-Baseline Neutrino Experiment in Japan, K2K, has 115 days of running scheduled during calendar 2000 (see http://psux1.kek.jp/~kekps/beamtime/schedule.html ) Jan-July.

*** K2K results in Y1K

During last year's engineering run, there were 3 or 4 reported events, expecting about 11-12 (with no oscillations). Expected event rates (per pot) might or might not be lower than original estimates.

*** Homestake Cl experiment back, better

After being off for 2 years, the original solar nu experiment has DOE/NSF funding for an automated extraction system with a He driven refrigerator-cryostat system rather than N. This will permit more frequent/more complete extractions. They are also modifying the counters to eliminate both dead space and degraded pulses due to end effects. The combination should give an increased rate and smaller systematic uncertainties. The overall goal is to reduce the total combined error from 8% to about 5%. This reduction should have a significant effect on the 7Be limit after subtraction of the 8B flux (using either the SK or SNO values).

*** Deadline close for Aspen Neutrino Workshop

The nominal deadline for applications for this outstanding workshop Jun 26-Jul 17 is Feb 1. Apply for 1, 2 or 3 weeks at http://andy.bu.edu/cgi-bin/aspenapp .

*** KamLAND US approved

The US groups in the KamLAND reactor neutrino experiment underwent a successful review in January. The DOE will fund them at a level of about $4M and they will continue to seek US/Japan funds, etc. Phototube installation starts May 8th, scintillator fill starts in September and running on April 1, 2001. They will be sensitive to the Large Mixing Angle Solar neutrino solution.

*** Palo Verde Results

This reactor neutrino experiment has just submitted for publication results on a search for nue disappearance about 800 m from the three reactors at Palo Verde. The measured flux agrees with prediction. This provides a second direct result that disfavors numu to nue for the atmospheric neutrino channel.

*** MuMu 99

At the 5th meeting on Muon Colliders held Dec. 15-17, 1999 at San Francisco, there were extensive discussion of possible Neutrino Factories around the world (US, Europe, Japan). About 110 people were at the meeting which included talks by the US laboratory directors about the future of their laboratories. Proceedings will be published by AIP press.

*** Neutrino Factory Working group

An interesting workshop at Fermilab on Feb 17 and 18 is taking shape on neutrinos from a muon storage ring. See http://www.fnal.gov/projects/muon_collider/nu/study/two_day.html .

*** Super-Kamiokande

The 825 day data, shown last summer went down to 5.5 MeV threshold and the energy distribution (excluding where hep neutrinos might contribute) was fairly flat. This disfavors the small mixing angle solution. If the large mixing angle solution is correct, the 2-sigma day/night effect will grow with time.

*** MSW is an effect, not a theory

The realization that charged current neutrino-electron scattering introduces a term in the mass matrix that singles out e-nu's is known as the Mikheyev, Smirnov and Wolfenstein or MSW effect. This is currently associated with 3 of the 4 possible solar nu solutions, and also with "regeneration" in the earth effects. If the solar neutrino solution is "just-so" or vacuum oscillations, the MSW effect is irrelevant. Neutrino factories have one design goal to go far enough to see matter effects. It will help in the measurement of U_e3. It would be ironic to many of us if it turns out the MSW effect is irrelevant for solar neutrinos, but is then measured by accelerators.

*** Notable (twenty year-) old reference

In hep-ph/0001036, P. Ramond "predicts" large numu to nutau mixing and small numu to nue mixing. The paper, written in 1981, took awhile to make it to the Los Alamos archive, even though it is based on a talk given at Los Alamos. It's called "The case for neutrino oscillations".

*** Neutrinos in Ferment

Burt Richter's insightful (and incite-ful?) summary talk from Lepton-Photon makes a great read, both regarding neutrinos and everything else. It's "Assessment and Outlook", hep-ex/0001012 .
