Long-Baseline news, July 2006

*** CNGS commissioning starts

By my count, the third long-baseline neutrino beam started its commissioning on July 10th. This beam goes from CERN in Switzerland to the Gran Sasso Laboratory in Italy where the OPERA experiment will measure numu to nutau oscillations. The gradual commissioning of the installation should result in the production of a nominal beam during the month of August. Beam spots are visible at http://proj-cngs.web.cern.ch/proj-cngs/ Also see http://bulletin.cern.ch/eng/articles.php?bullno=29/2006&base=art#Article1 & for OPERA http://operaweb.web.cern.ch/operaweb/

*** MiniBooNE results soon?

If MiniBooNE sees numu to nue oscillations with the same 2nu mixing angle and mass difference squared as LSND (whatever that means), they will have an excess of electron-like events, compared to background, below 2 GeV. For "Neutrino 2006", they opened their "high energy box" and showed five 2-3 GeV data points where no oscillations would be expected. One point was a sigma high, one a sigma low, and the other 3 agreed even better. Their "box opening" had been delayed, in part, by a ~60% disagreement between the absolute normalization of the expected and observed numu events. This has been resolved by tuning the Monte Carlo to HARP data, using a sophisticated "boosted decision tree" analysis, and numerous other things. Box opening and results are hopefully less than a month away.

*** PDG turns 50; Neutrino Mixing Section Modernized

In July, the Particle Data Group turned 50. As they do every two years, a new issue of the particle listings, or "Review of Particle Properties" has been issued. The number one highlight is a complete rearrangement of the neutrino listings, including a neutrino mixing section that now contains measurements of the mixing angles and mass differences in the three-neutrino framework. http://pdg.lbl.gov/2006/html/highlights.html

*** Massive Neutrinos turn PDG's RPP into a phonebook

RPP 2006 grew in page length by only 10%. However, this publisher uses paper that is 25-30% thicker than other publishers. As a result this edition looks enormous. To make matters worse, they used paper that is significantly wider and taller than the previous edition. The result is that it looks like a thick telephone book.

*** Intelligent Design of Neutrino Parameters

http://www.usfq.edu.ec/psagenda/ S. Wojcicki showed neutrino parameters were designed in a way that has worked well for the current generation of neutrino physicists. Delta m^2_21 gives us a resonant MSW effect in the middle of the solar energy spectrum. Theta_12 is big enough to be seen in KamLAND. Delta m^2_32 gives full oscillation in the middle of the range of atmospheric neutrino distances. theta_23 is big enough so that oscillations can be easily seen. And theta_13 is small enough so as not to confuse the interpretation of the other four parameters. But the acid test is - will theta_13 be big enough to see CP violation and determine the mass hierarchy?

*** MINOS Horn fixed

After a valve problem, the cooling nozzle on the NuMI first horn was clogged with 1/2 mm resin beads. Luckily, this was just before the summer shutdown. After nearly a month with repair crews working 20-hour days, the problem was fixed. http://www.fnal.gov/pub/today/NuMIhorn.html

*** Neutrinos in the News

The August issue of Scientific American describes neutrinos as the future of Fermilab in "The Neutrino Frontier: At Fermilab, particle smashing yields to flavor changing." The July 21 issue of Science describes reactor neutrino experiments in "Neutrino Hunters go Nuclear to Tackle Antimatter Deficit".

*** Accelerator panel concerned about support for muon cooling

In its report to HEPAP, a subpanel on accelerators wrote "We are concerned that the support for muon cooling is below what is needed to sustain this program. Support for MICE leaves little for other R&D Without increased support, essential intellectual resources will disappear."
