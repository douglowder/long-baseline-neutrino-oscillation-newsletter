Long-Baseline news, February 2006

*** NuSAG report on neutrinos released

The NuSAG report on reactor and accelerator neutrinos has been presented to the Nuclear Science Advisory Panel (NSAC) and the High Energy Physics Advisory Panel (HEPAP). Both groups unanimously accepted the report. Experiments which might measure theta-13 were recommended. NOvA at Fermilab was recommended, as well as U.S. participation in two near detector projects for T2K in Japan. For reactor neutrinos, U.S. participation in Double Chooz (France) was encouraged, and a program at either Braidwood (Illinois) or Daya Bay (China). The full report can be found at http://www.neutrinooscillation.org/nusag_report2_final.pdf

*** NOvA Director's Review at Fermilab

The NOvA long-baseline nu experiment at Fermilab had a director's CD1 review (Feb-Mar) in preparation for Lehman CD1 review in April. A draft Conceptual Design Report has been prepared. The plan is for a construction start in 2008. 142 scientists and engineers are currently involved. The cost is dominated by plastic and mineral oil which are tied to the cost of oil. Bids have been received for an integration prototype which include options for the full detector. The TEC was $235M including contingency (FY06 dollars - burdened costs). The building is 19% of the cost and commodities are 52%. The schedule range is 3.4-4.8 years.

*** NOvA integration prototype

This will be built during 2007 in the MINOS service building at Fermilab. This is 75 mrad off axis in the NuMI beam. This location is a good one for testing the detector response, since muon neutrinos from K decay have an average energy of 3 GeV and electron neutrinos from K decay have an average energy of 2 GeV.

*** Fermilab Proton Driver off the table; or maybe not

The Fermilab off-axis electron neutrino experiment NOvA can make significant discoveries without the proton driver, but it has more sensitivity if a booster replacement is built. This new super-conducting proton linear accelerator at Fermilab is plan B if the International Linear Accelerator (plan A) is delayed or located out of the U.S. The large increases in both the proposed ILC and HEP budgets for FY07 have turned more attention to plan A. A CD0 for the International Linear Collider, likely at Fermilab, is expected soon. A CD0 for the proton driver is not.

*** G. William Foster resigns as Proton Driver Project leader

He also resigned from FNAL, and left high-energy physics. The cause was CD0 cancellation "on the grounds that it conflicts with the push for the Fast-Track ILC". Also, "The current strategy of 'give me $10B or give me death' may be the correct approach to take during the remainder of the Bush administration; or it may be suicidal...However technical reality must be respected in any strategic plan." He also refers to the Fermilab Directorate's new official position that we wait until ~2010 to decide whether to build the Proton Driver." http://www.neutrinooscillation.org/foster.pdf

*** Bubble Chamber in NuMI neutrino beam

A bubble chamber built to look for dark matter has been operating in the MINOS near detector hall. The one liter device was built at Chicago by the COUPP collaboration. By operating below threshold of alphas, it can search for dark matter/highly ionizing events. http://www-astro-theory.fnal.gov/Conferences/TeV/Sonnenschein.pdf

*** Workshop on Systematic Errors in Reactor Neutrino Experiments

About 30 attended a Paris workshop on systematic error details. Bugey, CHOOZ and KamLAND talked about previous experience, while Double Chooz and KASKA talked about future experiments. Subtle effects, such as calibration of spillin/spillout, were discussed.
