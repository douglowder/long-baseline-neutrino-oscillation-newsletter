*** JPARC Neutrino Beamline tunnel completed

The 200-m long extraction beam tunnel has recently been completed. This tunnel, with a nearly 90-degree arc, will be equipped with magnets and deliver a fast inward extracted proton beam from the synchrotron to the target. The civil construction of the target station has been started. This is the beam for T2K.

*** New unit for costing neutrino experiments - milliBarish

The International Linear Collider (ILC) global design effort has come up with a cost of $4.9B for a machine, $1.8B for facilities, + 13000FTE years, not including the detectors, contingency or escalation. This can then be described as a cost with numbers varying from $6.7B by the Chicago Tribune ("Cost of solving mysteries of universe: $6.7 billion" in the Feb 9 edition http://www.neutrinooscillation.org/ilccost.html) to $16B when crude estimates of detectors, contingency, manpower and escalation are included. Earlier banter estimates for the ILC peaked near $10B, with a cluster of guesses at $9.99B. Until a Lehman cost estimate is reviewed, this round number ($10B) will serve as a convenient unit of physics cost, or "Barish", with the more appropriate unit for neutrino experiments being $10M or mBarish. The milliBarishness of past, present and future neutrino experiments will be explored in this newsletter through the year.

*** DUSEL Soudan proposes Institute for Underground Science (IUS)

NSF received proposals from S. Dakota, Colorado, the U-Minnesota & U-Washington Jan 9 for a Deep Underground Science & Engineering Laboratory (DUSEL). Minnesota (http://www.soudan.umn.edu/) is proposing a multi-site DUSEL, The IUS will work with existing sites, including SNOLab, Soudan, Kimballton, WIPP & Homestake 4850. This approach is less risky & more cost-effective than a single- site. It exploits unique features: sedimentary rock at Kimballton, the NuMI beam to Soudan, infrastructure & low U-Th backgrounds at WIPP. It proposes to use the majority of the 1.5 mBarish available for DUSEL over the next three years to fund science and technology.

*** Burt Richter asks 4 Neutrino Questions at AAAS meeting

In "Charting the course for Particle Physics" he asks 1. Is there a sterile neutrino?, 2. theta-13=? 3. Neutrino is Dirac or Majorana? 4. Mass hierarchy? He spent the most time on #1, and he is not a fan of blind analyses. "We should consider the possibility that (MiniBooNE) will not have a conclusive result." He likes the idea of a new neutrino experiment at Oak Ridge. Referring to Double Chooz, Daya Bay and JPARC, he says "It would be wise to get the new results on theta-13 before embarking on other large and costly accelerator based neutrino experiments." http://www.slac.stanford.edu/cgi-wrap/getdoc/slac-pub-12345.pdf

*** How to explode a Supernova 20 years after SN1987a

http://sn1987a-20th.physics.uci.edu/Program1.htm SN models now actually explode. It takes 3-d. These 3-d models get asymmetric sloshing that make SN blow asymmetrically, even if there was no initial rotation. Good news for gravity wave guys and for making neutron stars jet around. The optical stuff on the remnant shows not only the triple-ring (which people are saying could come from the wind of a stellar merger, which would also explain the blue progenitor) but an asymmetric proto-crablike SNR forming. Still no compact object seen. There is a strong desire to build 4.4Mton detectors to catch ~dozens of events from (<10 Mpc) galaxies.
