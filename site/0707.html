Long-Baseline Neutrino news, July 2007

*** New MINOS measurement of Delta m-squared 32

Fermilab's MINOS experiment has presented its oscillation fit results with twice the previously published Luminosity (now 2.5 10^20 protons-on-target). The best fit is 2.38 +.20-.16 10^-3 eV^2 and sin^2 2theta = 1.00 -.08+.00. For energies below 10 GeV, a deficit of 38% compared to no-osc hypothesis is seen. The goal after the 2007 shutdown is to run stably at 2.5 10^13 ppp every 2.2 seconds, and in 2008-9 to improve the beam power 30-40% by stacking.

*** Neutrino Scientific Assessment Group recommendations 13jul07

NuSAG finds a continuing program designed to study CP violation in the neutrino sector and to determine the hierarchy of the nu mass spectrum is scientifically compelling. 1) The US should proceed to extend sensitivity to theta-13, 2) Beam R&D for upgrades to NuMI or a beam to DUSEL, 3) Water Cerenkov R&D 4) Liquid Argon R&D. "Planning and R&D should be ready for a technology decision and a decision to proceed when the next round of results on th-13 becomes available, which could be as early as 2012. A review of the international program in neutrino oscillations and the opportunities for international collaboration should be included in the decision to proceed." This is NuSAG's "final" report: http://www.science.doe.gov/hep/NUSAGFinalReportJuly13,2007.pdf

*** Theorizing the High Energy Neutrinos

If Astronomical TEV photons come from pion decay, associated with high energy protons, then neutrinos with the same energies must exist. If, instead, photons come from high energy electrons, there won't be (many) neutrinos. At ICRC, Francis Halzen reported checking astro-ph for the last year and the vote is 4-4. If you write the next paper, you can break the tie.

*** Looking for High Energy Neutrinos

A number of experiments are looking for or planning to look for Very High Energy and Ultra High Energy Neutrinos -- Baikal, Ice Cube, Antares, NEMO, NESTOR, RICE, ANITA, AURA, SHALON, Auger, Auriana, AMANDA, Telescope Array and HIRES using neutrino telescopes, radio, or acoustic signals or looking for horizontal air showers. There were extensive presentations at ICRC.

*** Looking for neutrinos/muons/Kaons/neutrinos

The MINOS experiment was built to study neutrinos, but it is the first large deep underground experiment with a magnet and has accurately measured the charge ratio of atmospheric muons which start near 1 TeV to be mu+/mu- = 1.374. Previous experiments from 100 MeV to 100 GeV all get close to 1.26. Phil Schreiner noticed that he could use Gaisser's muon spectrum equation (originally due to Zatsepin & Kuzmin) to fit this result and extract the pi+/pi- and K+/K- ratios in cosmic rays. see arXiv 0705.3815 & 0706.0538. Cosmic ray modelers are now looking into SIBYLL, QGSJET, etc. to see if retuning is needed. Above 1 TeV, most atmospheric neutrinos come from K decay, and the neutrino and anti-neutrino cross sections are different, so all the experiments listed above should care.

*** Consequence of the Super-Kamiokande Accident

Super-K III, running since the rebuild, now has a year of data. Due to the 2001 accident, Super-K II ran with less phototube coverage than Super-K I or III. To get the same performance in Super-K II they had to improve the reconstruction algorithm. Now they hope to lower the solar neutrino threshold in Super-K III from 4.5 MeV in Super-K I. The transition of solar neutrino oscillations between vacuum and matter-dominated oscillations occurs near 3 MeV.

*** DUSEL to Homestake, South Dakota

The NSF announced selection of a UC-Berkeley proposal to produce a technical design for a Deep Underground Science and Engineering Lab at Homestake. A 22-member panel unanimously picked Homestake from 4 proposals. The team could receive $5M/year for 3 years to develop the design. A decision to construct/operate DUSEL entails further approvals by NSF, the National Science Board, and Congress. http://www.nsf.gov/news/news_summ.jsp?cntn_id=109694&org=NSF&from=news

*** Is the neutrino mass growing?

The time evolution of a cosmological scalar field can be stopped by an increasing mass of the neutrinos. This leads to a transition from a cosmological scaling solution with dynamical dark energy at early time to a cosmological constant dominated universe at late time. The trigger for the transition is set at the time when the neutrinos become non-relativistic. The characteristic mass scale for dark energy is thus related to the neutrino mass, the only known particle physics scale in its vicinity. This explains the "why now problem" for dark energy. C. Wetterich, arXiv:0706.4427
