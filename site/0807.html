Long-Baseline news, July 2008

*** Long-Baseline in Europe - CERN CNGS Beam Restarted

The CERN long-baseline neutrino beam from CERN to Gran Sasso Italy has restarted as of June 18. By July 7th, they've reached 1.16E18 POTs http://cdsweb.cern.ch/record/1110531

*** Long-Baseline in America - NuMI Restarted

MINOS is running again with a replacement horn. A new "scenarios" committee will study the mix of nu/anti-nu running after 2010. The restarted NOvA project has once again passed its CD2 review.

*** Long-Baseline in Japan - Main Ring Circulation at Tokai

The T2K experiment will use a 50 GeV accelerator being built in Japan. In May, they injected beam from the 3 GeV ring to the 50 GeV ring for the first time. The beam was captured by the RF and, then, extracted outside the ring, but was not accelerated. They will accelerate to 30 GeV in Dec. For the nu beam, they've installed 10 of 14 magnet doublets, the beam dump, and the 900t near detector magnet. The 3rd horn was assembled at KEK. Assembling test of the target & 1st horn will be done in Aug.

*** Dr. Yoji Totsuka

Yoji Totsuka, the former director general of KEK and an outstanding contributor to great advances in neutrino physics, died of cancer at the age of 66 on Thursday, July 10. He had led the design and construction of the Super-Kamiokande Detector. http://www.kek.jp/intra-e/Introduction/column/

*** Predictions of Theta-13 (q13)

Some theories do make predictions and are testable. Datta arXiv:0807.0420 predicts sin(q13) related to the deviation of sin(q12) from 1/sqrt(3), while the devision of sin(q23) from its tribimaximal value is related to m_mu/m_tau. Porto & Zee's model of the private Higgs ( arXiv:0807.0612) U_e3 ~ 0.02. Jenkins and Manohar argue in arXiv:0807.4176 that any model with the exact tribimaximal mixing (q13=0) does not have leptogenesis, and to get "adequately large lepton asymmetry" one needs q13>0.01.

*** Measurement of the Neutrino Mass

The sum of the masses of the three neutrino mass eigenstates is now constrained both from above and below, and lies between 55 and 6900 meV. The lower limit is set by neutrino oscillations and the fact that masses are non-negative. The upper limit is set by laboratory measurements on the beta decay of tritium. The clustering on large scales in the universe depends on this quantity, and, within the framework of Lambda-CDM cosmology, favors a mass sum below about 600 meV. Robertson arXiv:0807.4258

*** Oxford English Dictionary Neutrino definition updated

The online OED was revised June 2008, presumably unrelated to last month's long-baseline news: "Each of three stable, uncharged leptons, associated respectively with the electron, the muon, and the tau lepton, which have very little mass and an extremely low probability of interaction with matter. Also: an antiparticle of any of these, one of which is produced (along with an electron and a proton) in the beta decay of a neutron." Better...
