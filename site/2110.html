Long-Baseline news, October 2021

*** Search for an excess of electron neutrinos in MicroBooNE

https://theory.fnal.gov/events/event/first-search-for-an-excess-of-electron-neutrinos-in-microboone-with-multiple-final-state-topologies/ They didn't find an excess. A usual interpretation of the MiniBooNE excess is the appearance in a nu-mu beam of nu-e due to oscillations involving sterile neutrinos. At a special 2-hour Fermilab seminar, MicroBooNE presented three analyses, comparing the predicted electron neutrino spectrum in the Booster Beam with one that accounts for the MiniBooNE low Energy excess (LEE). All three strongly favored the no-LEE choice. arXiv:2110.14054 , https://microboone.fnal.gov/electron_analysis_2021 The MC spectrum is based on a new theory-driven GENIE tune in arXiv:2110.14028.

*** MicroBooNE quasielastic analysis

https://microboone.fnal.gov/electron_analysis_1e1p_ccqe_2021/ The prediction was 29.0 +- 5.2(stat) +- 1.9(syst) events. There were 25 events observed, and in the eLEE model another 11.6 events would be expected. See arXiv:2110.14080.

*** MicroBooNE search for nue CC events without pions

https://microboone.fnal.gov/electron_analysis_1enp0pi_1e0p0pi_2021/ The prediction was 86.8 +- 8.8 +- 11.5 nue events with protons and no pions and 64 were seen. Also 30 +- 5.6 +- 4.3 with no protons or pions and 34 were seen. See arXiv:2110.14065.

*** MicroBoone inclusive electron neutrino analysis

https://microboone.fnal.gov/electron_analysis_1ex_2021/ There were 56 candidates compared to an expectation of 69.6 +-8.0(stat) +- 5.0(sys) without an excess and 103.8 +- 9.0 stat) +- 7.4(sys) if the MiniBooNE low-energy excess was nue's. arXiv:2110.13978. This analysis uses Wire-Cell, which is a 3D event reconstruction package which they have developed for liquid argon time projection chambers. It is described in arXiv:2110.13961. These analyses will be stepping stones for DUNE, SBND and ICARUS.

*** IUPAP neutrino panel white paper released

The IUPAP report was perceived by some in the community as another roadmap, but the mandate did not ask for a prioritization of projects. It was rather to consider the scientific opportunities that international cooperation in neutrino physics may afford. It aims primarily at demonstrating that neutrinos are very special particles with unique features and many exciting opportunities. https://www.iupapneutrinopanel.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/IUPAP_Neutrino_Panel_Report.pdf

*** Panofsky prize and Emily Noether distinction

https://www.aps.org/programs/honors/prizes/prizerecipient.cfm?last_nm=Rameika&first_nm=Regina&year=2022 FNAL physicist Regina Rameika is the 2022 W.K.H. Panofsky Prize in experimental particle physics recipient. She led the design of the NuMI beam, helped discover the tau neutrino, served as project manager of MicroBooNE, and is co-spokesman of DUNE. T2K physicist S. Bolognesi won the summer 2021 EPS Emmy Noether Distinction for Women in Physics. https://www.eps.org/page/distinction_prize_en

*** Interesting Letters of Interest; v14 - https://snowmass21.org/

  • Pattacina-076, "Astrophysical neutrino source investigation with RES-NOVA", It will hunt for supernova nus via coherent elastic nu nucleus scattering (CEνNS) using an array of archaeological lead based cryogenic detectors. The first detector will be (60 cm)^3.
  • Newby-109, "ORNL Neutrino Sources for Future Experiments", The High Flux Isotope Reactor (HFIR), an 85 MW research reactor, makes 10^19 antinus/s. The Oak Ridge Spallation Neutron Source (SNS) is the world’s most intense source of nus from stopped-pion decays. Both are planned to operate beyond 2030 with significant upgrades.
  • Fairbank-120, "Barium tagging for a nEXO upgrade & future 136Xe 0nu-2beta detectors", The focus is a road map of necessary R&D toward a nEXO upgrade using barium tagging to increase the sensitivity of a next generation 0nu-2beta detector.
  • Baudis-084 "Neutrino physics with the DARWIN Observatory", The 40 ton Darwin dark matter detector will measure the neutrino floor background which is also a signal for solar & SN nus.
  • Noemi_Rocco-090 "Microscopic approaches to neutrino-nucleus interactions", Good calculations of neutrino scattering on nuclei are essential for short- & long-baseline neutrino facilities. They require an understanding of all the reaction mechanisms in the broad range of E & p relevant to oscillation experiments.
