*** International Quantum Year

We are well into the International Quantum Year of 2025. See https://quantum2025.org/. While neutrino oscillations provide a manifestation of quantum mechanics on scales that are familiar (i.e. larger than atoms), our community doesn't seem to benefit since this doesn't seem relevant to quantum computers.

*** MicroBooNE sees ~2 sigma excess at low energies

In arXiv:2502.06064 , MicroBooNE does an inclusive search for an excess of single photon events from 6.3e20 protons on target as a possible source of the MiniBooNE low energy excess. For the full signal region, the data agrees well with the background estimate. In a subsample of events with no visible protons, there is an excess of 93+- 22(stat) +-35(syst) events mostly below 600 MeV. Studying kinematic distributions of the signal and sidebands, MicroBooNE has not identified a complete explanation for this excess. This motivates a future search using MicroBooNEâ€™s full dataset.

*** More MicroBooNE

In arXiv:2502.06091, MicroBooNE searches for NC Coherent single- photon production by neutrinos. They observe 34 events consistent with background and set an upper limit on the cross section. In arXiv:2502.05750 they search for NC Delta production in a neutrino beam, where the Delta radiatively decays to a photon and nucleon. They set limits compatible with an earlier analysis, and rule out the LEE in events with protons at 94.4% CL, but the MiniBooNE LEE is compatible if most LEE events have no proton. And a search for e+e- pairs in arXiv:2502.10900, motivated by a possible mechanism from the dark sector, sees 95 events with a background of 69.7 +- 17.3. The 95% CL upper limit excludes the majority of parameter space to explain the MiniBooNE LEE from a dark sector model.

*** KM3NET measures new record high-energy neutrino

An event which I first read about in the New York Times,but which is also described in Nature and arXiv:2502.08173. The estimated muon energy is 120 +110 -60 PeV, with a 90% CL range from 35 to 380 PeV. Using 335 days of livetime and the current size of the detector strings in the Mediterranean, they calculate a flux, but that exceeds current limits from IceCube and Auger. Some papers that consider the event are arXiv:2502.12986 , arXiv:2502.12070 , arXiv:2502.11434 , arXiv:2502.09548 , arXiv:2502.09545 , arXiv:2502.08508 , arXiv:2502.08387 , arXiv:2502.06944 , arXiv:2502.04508 , arXiv:2502.19245 , arXiv:2502.18256.

*** How Big is a neutrino (wave packet)

https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-08479-6 The paper arXiv:2404.03102 is now published in Nature. The STJ detector at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory use a K-capture isotope and measure the recoil of the daughter nucleus. Energy/momentum uncertainty (i.e. the width of the recoil energy distribution) is related via the Heisenberg uncertainty principle to delta T and delta X and can be used to limit the length of the wave packet.
