*** Signatures for Neutrino Magnetic moment

If the neutrino has a magnetic moment, this electromagnetic interaction increases as log E. Weak interactions increase at E, so searches should be at low energy. The MUNU experiment at the BUGEY reactor is searching using low energy neutrino electron elastic scattering. A preliminary new limit is < 1.9 10^-10 Bohr magneton. They will run the rest of 2001. Also, DONUT sets a limit 3.9 10^-7 for nutau's mu hep-ex/0102026

*** SNO tells us nothing twice

SNO was hoping to give their neutrino flux at the Washington APS meeting, but instead in two large talks told us the answer would come soon. They mostly measure nu d -> p p e which is CC, while Super-K measures nu-e elastic which is about 1 part NC to 6 parts CC. Active neutrino oscillations should be as low as ~ 0.30 of the SSM rate, while SMA & just so ~ 0.45. (See hep-ph/0103179 ) The problem may be agreeing on the systematic error. SNO's next collaboration meeting is this month.

*** Experimental Cosmologists ignore neutrinos twice

The DASI and Boomerang data now see the 2nd and 3rd peaks in the cosmic microwave background power spectrum! Both collaborations fit to 5% Baryons, 35% Cold Dark Matter and 60% Dark Energy. Asked about Hot Dark Matter, one speaker said it would be in their error budget.

*** Super-K now 1289.4 days, 79.3 kt-year

Sub-Gev R 0.638 +- 0.017 +- 0.050, Multi-Gev 0.675 +-0.033 +-0.08 Chisquared best fit is 132.4/137, while for no oscillations it is 299.3/139. stopping/through muons for Data/MC is 0.65 +- 0.04 +- 0.09. Best fit for dmsq is now 2.5 10^-3 eV^2 with the 90% CL between 1.7 and 4 with sin^2 2q> 0.89. Solar neutrinos are .451 +- .005 +- .016 compared to BP2000 with the 5.0-5.5 MeV bin.

*** Solar Constant variations

The solar constant is named because... Well it didn't change much. John Bahcall has adopted the agreed upon average value from all the solar radiation satellites of the solar luminosity. So the published version of BP2000 has a 2% lower 8B flux than given at NOW2001 (5.15 to 5.05 10^6 /cm^2-s). This will undoubtedly cause confusion when experiments compare to "BP2000".

*** Super-K daily and seasonal variations

For Day/Night, they have 636 effective days at night (?!). [N-D]/[(N+D)/2] = 0.033 +- 0.022. For seasonal variations, chisquared for eccentricity is 3.9/7 dof, for flat is 8.7/7 dof. Thus its the first observation of eccentricity using neutrinos.

*** Baikal up going neutrinos

Last year Lake Baikal neutrino telescope saw 35 neutrinos in 170 days with NT190.

*** NuMI costs going up

A DOE Lehman Review of the NuMI Project will be May 22-24 to consider increasing the baseline from $138M by about $30M.

Their web page is at http://projects.fnal.gov/protondriver/. A workshop is tentatively scheduled for June 6-7 at FNAL. 10 study groups.

*** Who runs the bar at Deep Space Ten?

You won't find the word neutrino in James Joyce. But the word is used as the name of a town in Kabardino-Balkaria Russia. Yes, it is near Baksan. Also, QNX has an operating system for embedded devices http://www.qnx.com/literature/nto_sysarch/intro.html and there is a band http://www.flyingneutrinos.com/neutrinoland.html A play in Theatre du Nouvel-Ontario called "Univers" muses on neutrinos and SNO (in French). And a climbing company in France sells carabiners named 'neutrino'. We can only hope they chose the name for its low weight not for its property to oscillate.
