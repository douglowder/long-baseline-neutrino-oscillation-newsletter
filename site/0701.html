Long-Baseline news, January 2007

*** Daya Bay Proposal for reactor neutrino experiment to DOE

The Day Bay nuclear power complex is one of the most prolific sources of antineutrinos in the world. Currently with two pairs of reactor cores (Daya Bay and Ling Ao), separated by about 1.1 km, the complex generates 11.6 GW of thermal power; this will increase to 17.4 GW by early 2011 when a third pair of reactor cores (Ling Ao II) is put into operation and Daya Bay will be among the five most powerful reactor complexes in the world. The site is located adjacent to mountainous terrain, ideal for siting underground detector laboratories that are well shielded from cosmogenic backgrounds. This site offers an exceptional opportunity for a reactor neutrino experiment optimized to perform a precision determination of theta_13 through a measurement of the relative rates and energy spectrum of reactor antineutrinos at different baselines. In addition, this project offers a unique and unprecedented opportunity for scientific collaboration involving China, the U.S., and other countries. http://www.arxiv.org/PS_cache/hep-ex/pdf/0701/0701029.pdf

*** Values of neutrino parameters in 2006 and 2007

The 2006 Review of Particle Properties from the Particle Data Group was the first to include listings for the 3 mixing angles and 2 values of mass squared differences from oscillations. (A new way to use RPP is pdglive: http://pdglive.lbl.gov/) Angles, expressed as the square of the sine of twice the angle are [23] > 0.92, [12] = 0.86 +0.03 -0.04 and [13] < 0.19. The dm^2 are [32] = 1.9 - 3.0 e-3 and [21] = 8.0 +0.4 -0.3 e-5 in eV^2. MINOS has improved [32] to 2.74 +0.44 -0.26 in hep-ex/0607088.

*** Improving neutrino parameter measurements

At WIN07, Sandhya Choubey mapped out how improvement in parameter measurements might take place. dm^2_21 is known to 12%. After 3 kT yr of KamLAND, a 6% measurement can be expected, which would improve to 2.4% with 5 years of Gd filled Super-K. sin^2(theta_12), known to 22% would improve with SNO-3 and 3kT yr KL and 12% after a pp solar nu experiment and 6% with SPMIN. dm^2_31, currently known to 23%, improves to 6% after 5 years of T2K and sin^2(theta_23), known to only 33%, improves to 20% after MINOS, CNGS and 5 years each of T2K and NOvA. http://www.hecr.tifr.res.in/MaKaC/getFile.py/access?contribId=13&sessionId=4&resId=0&materialId=slides&confId=14

*** Magic Baseline

Long-baseline accelerator numu to nue searches for theta_13 have clone solutions due to degeneracies in theta_23 and delta. However, at a "magic" baseline, delta-CP terms drop out and there can be a clean measurement of theta_13 and the mass hierarchy. This occurs at 7690 km. For the earth density profile, the largest matter effects will occur near 6 GeV neutrino energy. The distance between CERN and the Indian Neutrino Observatory (INO) is 7152 km, tantalizingly close to the magic baseline. See hep-ph/0610333 hep-ph/0610198 and hep-ph/0301257.

*** INO should be ONE

INO means little in Italian, but the Indian Neutrino Observatory is planning a 50 kiloton detector. ONE means big, so maybe it should be the Ooty Neutrino Experiment.
