*** Liquid Argon Purity Demonstration at Fermilab

The FNAL Liquid Argon Purity Demonstrator (LAPD) is designed to test whether the electro-negative purity necessary for drift distances of over 2 meters can be achieved in a liquid argon TPC without evacuation. It is located in PC4 at Fermilab, the same hall where the Upsilon was discovered. The first phase of the test is to show that the electron lifetimes can be achieved in an empty vessel, and the second phase will place materials similar to those planned for LBNE in the system and repeat the purity demonstration. The LAPD vessel will hold 30 tons of liquid argon and is scheduled to begin filling in the middle of November 2010.

*** Long-baseline Antineutrino Plan Delay (LAPD) delayed

In the NuMI beam, MINERvA wants neutrinos, MINOS anti-neutrinos. MINERvA has been given control of the beam tune until the end of the year, though there is a target leak now being fixed. When MINOS takes control it will spend most of the time in low energy anti-neutrino running. NOvA, which will soon be operating the near detector on the surface (NDOS) prefers neutrinos. A likely scenario is that NuMI will give NDOS 2E20 protons on target in neutrino mode and 2E20 protons on target in anti-neutrino mode. This delayed plan might be delayed, as in September the target for NuMI needed to be replaced. NuMI is off; probably till November.

*** Large Area Picosecond Photo-Detector R&D (LAPPD)

The LAPPD Project has recently entered the 2nd year of its 3y timeline. The goal is to leverage advances in material science to produce large-area microchannel plate detectors with sub-mm, sub- ns resolutions at low costs, scalable to neutrino and collider applications. The LAPPD has already demonstrated gains of 10^6 from 33mm MCPs made using its fabrication method, vacuum seals in 8 inch glass tubes, and time resolutions of a few picoseconds from its front-end electronics design. The time resolutions and fine spatial granularity within single detector modules have potential to improve tracking and particle ID capabilities in future Water Cherenkov-based neutrino detectors, and their high tolerance for magnetic fields and compact design might reduce some engineering challenges of conventional PMTs. See http://psec.uchicago.edu/.

*** New theta_13 fit from KamLAND (or KamLAPD if isospin is good)

arXiv:1009.4771 . A three-flavor analysis with theta_13 as a free parameter yields best fits on theta_12, dmsq_21 and sin^2 q13 = 0,020 +- 0.016. A global analysis with CHOOZ, atmospheric and accelerator data gives a nonzero value at 93% CL.

*** Large-area Avalanche Photo Diodes and neutrinoless beta decay

Neutrinoless double beta decay experiments need good energy resolution and large background rejection. The NEXT collaboration is building a 100 kg enriched 136Xe detector in the CanFranc Lab, possibly with multi-pixel photon counters (MPPC) and large area avalanche photodiodes (LAPD). EXO200 at WIPP in New Mexico also uses 136XE and has 516 200 mm^2 active area LAAPDs to detect scintillation light and provide a time-zero measurement. http://iopscience.iop.org/1742-6596/203/1/012068/pdf/1742-6596_203_1_012068.pdf , http://www.slac.stanford.edu/econf/C0805263/Posters/neilson_r.pdf

*** Los Alamos Physics Division http://www.lanl.gov/p/

They are heading a project (MiniCLEAN - Cryogenic Low Energy Astrophysics with Noble liquids) to make a direct search for WIMP dark matter. MiniCLEAN will exploit a 500 kg, 150 kg fiducial mass of LAr viewed by a spherical array of 92 PMTs and operating as a single-phase detector. The conceptual simplicity of the approach aims toward the development of a multi-ton experiment capable of interchanging massive targets of LAr & LNe. A related new program applies LAr technology to the cause threat reduction owing to the capability to discriminate nuclear recoil events like fast neutrons when swamped in a intense signal of gamma/e background.
