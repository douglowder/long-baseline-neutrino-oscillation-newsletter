Long-Baseline news, January 2017

*** Fermi's mistake?

In its article on "Why are we called Fermilab", you can see the famous Fermi picture with the wrong formula for the fine structure constant. http://news.fnal.gov/2017/01/why-are-we-called-fermilab/ My adviser, who worked for Fermi at the time, said UChicago wanted some publicity shots, and Fermi's office was too dark, so they moved to the conference room, where someone had written equations on the blackboard. When the post office issued a stamp for Fermi, Al Wattenberg checked it for "mistakes". As a result, part of the wrong formula was cropped out. But in the stamp ceremony, Nina Byers claimed this was clearly Fermi's handwriting.

*** LBNF anomaly and ITER

Nobody knows what Congress is going to do, but with the change of administration and the current Continuing Resolution, it is reasonable to speculate that there will be a CR all year. In that case, in order to start construction, LBNF needs an "anomaly", which has been advocated by congressmen from S. Dakota, Illinois and others. But other big projects in the Office of Science may be correlated. For example, in a May 2016 Report on the Continued U.S. Participation in the ITER Project, find, "The DOE Office of Science is considering several other projects of $1B or more, including Exascale computing, LBNF/DUNE, PIP, APS Upgrade and SNS 2nd target Station. Absent additional resources being made available, DOE would need to delay some of the projects listed above...but DOE is committed to funding the resources needed for ITER in the budgets through FY2018." https://science.energy.gov/~/media/fes/pdf/DOE_US_Participation_in_the_ITER_Project_May_2016_Final.pdf

*** U.S. Congress has a sense for neutrino research

https://www.congress.gov/bill/115th-congress/house-bill/589/text#toc-HB66DCA7804DC4DC68FF5F407DE2DE6F0 "The Director shall carry out research activities on rare decay processes and the nature of the neutrino, which may include collaborations with the National Science Foundation or international collaborations." This was part of the "DOE Research and Innovation Act" passed by the House (not yet law). The sponsor and science chair Lamar Smith said: "The committee will continue to prioritize basic research at the Department of Energy, and will work to limit spending on late-stage commercialization programs that distort the energy market. Critical reforms are needed to ensure the Department of Energy spends limited federal research dollars on discovery science that the private sector cannot conduct, not loan guarantees and subsidies." http://aip-info.org/1ZJN-4R0FZ-E29E6Q-2H9ACM-1/c.aspx

*** NOvA oscillation values

NOvA's publication preprint on muon neutrino disappearance covers the data shown at Neutrino 2016. arXiv:1701.5891 For the Normal ordering, delta msquared_32=(2.76 +- 0.11) e-3 eV^2 with two best fit values of sin^2(theta_23) = 0.404 +0.030 -0.022 and 0.624+0.022 -0.030 at 68%CL, disfavoring maximal mixing with 2.6 sigma.

*** NEOS - NEutrino Oscillation at Short Baseline

This is a very short-baseline reactor neutrino experiment in Korea designed to look for oscillations arising from a sterile neutrino explanation of the reactor neutrino anomaly. Their results so far don't find any. arXiv:1610.05134

*** Sterile Neutrinos, Fact or Fiction?

At the Washington APS meeting, Patrick Huber reviewed data concerning sterile neutrinos with this title. He concludes that disregarding the MiniBooNE low energy excess, an eV-scale sterile nu is a simple explanation for all observations. https://absuploads.aps.org/presentation.cfm?pid=13003

*** More on the APS April meeting and semantics

The APS meeting used to be called the Washington meeting, but it was in April and got expensive due in part to Cherry Blossoms. So it was moved to other cities, and later named the April meeting, though it was sometimes in May. This year the April meeting was in January in Washington. They told participants to use 16x9 "widescreen" slides, and not 4x3 "standard" slides, because "widescreen" is now standard and "standard" is non-standard.
