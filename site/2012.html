Long-Baseline news, December 2020

*** Daya Bay Completion Ceremony

The Daya Bay reactor neutrino experiment, which measured that the "small" neutrino mixing angle theta_13 was nonzero, has come to an end with a completion ceremony in December. http://www.chinanews.com/gn/2020/12-13/9361339.shtml or https://newscenter.lbl.gov/2020/12/11/scientists-say-farewell-to-daya-bay-site-proceed-with-final-data-analysis/?utm_medium=email&utm_source=FYI&dm_i=1ZJN,767M2,E29E6Q,T1QUK,1 and watch at https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DRJrcwqwRkQ

*** Short-Baseline Neutrino Program Multi-Institutional MOU

An MOU for collaboration by international partners on the FNAL- hosted SBN program is now being processed for signatures by all parties. It memorializes organizational, managerial, funding and participants' responsibilities to the program, as well as safety, environmental and radiological regulations of the host. It will be signed by FNAL, LANL, UNICAMP-Brazil, INFN-Italy, STFC-UK and Bern-Switzerland. It may be the model for a future DUNE MOU.

*** Neutrinos at Fermilab again in December

For the first time since the shutdown which was moved up at the beginning of the COVID shutdowns, the Fermilab accelerator complex is making neutrinos again, for NOvA, MicroBooNE, etc.

*** DOE Project Delays and Planning Delays

The pandemic situation has contributed to a number of DOE project delays, which in turn postpones the need for new input on planning from Snowmass and the next P5. Magnet issues have delayed mu2e. The CERN shutdown has affected accelerator, CMS & ATLAS upgrades. There are cryostat procurement issues for SuperCDMS. Some project rebaselining will be needed. The current DUNE/LBNF TPC is $2.6B; CD2 is still planned for 2021.

*** Snowmass2021 and P5 delay

After extensive community input, the Snowmass Steering Group, the Advisory Group, all conveners, & early career representatives met and decided to delay the Snowmass process by one year. Extending the timeline of the Snowmass and P5 process would enable HEP's scientific vision and the subsequent prioritization exercise, to be fully informed by another year's anticipated progress. The new timeline calls for preliminary topical group reports in the Spring of 2022, Frontier Reports in late spring, the summer study at UW- Seattle in summer 2022, and the Snowmas Book in October 2022.

*** Gerda neutrinoless double beta decay experiment ends

The GERDA experiment designed to prove the existence of zero-nu double beta decay is coming to an end. The collaboration has evaluated and published in PRL the full data. Though no signal was found, all goals set for the final phase of GERDA were achieved. The progress made since 2011 is now being incorporated as the search continues in the LEGEND experiment. https://www.mpp.mpg.de/en/news/news/detail/the-search-for-neutrinoless-double-beta-decay-continues-with-new-legend-experiment

*** Interesting Letters of Interest; v4 - https://snowmass21.org/

  • C.K._Jung-118 "3D-projection Scintillator Tracker (3DST) in SAND, a DUNE Near Detector Subsystem" The 3D-projection Scintillator Tracker is the proposed equivalent of SuperFGD within Hyper- Kamiokande for the SAND detector. 3DST will be made of scintillator cubes, each with three orthogonal holes for WLS fibers for 3D projectional readout, with a unique capability of event by event neutron detection.
  • Vieregg-222 "The Payload for Ultrahigh Energy Observations PUEO" The Payload for Ultrahigh Energy Observations is a NASA Long Duration Balloon that will launch in 2023 in Antarctica. PUEO is able to detect radio emission of high-energy cosmic particles, including high-energy neutrinos. Building off of the success of ANITA, PUEO will have a lower energy threshold and improved sensitivity to neutrinos.
  • -118 "Radio Detection of Cosmic Rays" Detecting high-energy neutrinos via radio detection allows for detection to not be limited to clear nights, using arrays of digital radio antennas.
  • Taboada-092 "Opportunities for multi-messenger observations with neutrinos and tests of fundamental physics over the next decade" IceCube-Gen2 will have five times the sensitivity for steady point sources and detect astrophysical neutrinos tenfold than IceCube. It would be monitoring approximately 8 gigatons of ice and including a 500 km^2 of radio array.
  • OceanBottomDetector-201 "Ocean Bottom Detector" This LOI describes the geoneutrino detector, geoscience, and ocean engineering efforts put into OBD. The OBD project has improved applied anti-neutrino science, and it is anticipated that a 1.5 kt detector will be deployed eventually,
