Long-Baseline news, March 2004

*** NOvA Proposal

The Proposal to Build an off-axis detector to study numu to nue oscillations in the NuMI beamline was submitted to Fermilab 3/15. 160 authors from 34 institutions signed the proposal. John Cooper (Fermilab) and Gary Feldman (Harvard) are cospokespersons. http://www-off-axis.fnal.gov/NOvA%20proposal%20March%2015%202004.pdf

*** APS Neutrino Study Web Page, & Astrophysics Group too

A study web page is now at http://www.interactions.org/neutrinostudy. Also the astrophysics/cosmology neutrino working group has established a page at http://home.fnal.gov/~beacom/NuStudy/

*** US Office of Science Goals

US HEP and Nuclear goals have been defined by a strategic plan with some dates between 2003 & 2026 (for estimated completion). http://www.sc.doe.gov/Sub/Mission/Mission_Strategic.htm Among 19 HEP goals are: 2005 Begin studies of neutrino mass differences and flavor mixing with NuMI/MINOS to clarify the neutrino's Standard Model role. 2011 Determine the pattern of nu masses and mixing parameters 2021 Using Super Neutrino Beam, begin measurements of matter/antimatter asymmetry in the lepton sector 2026 Measure neutrino masses from studies of double beta decay, helping to set energy scale for unification 2026 Use knowledge of neutrino mass to clarify role of neutrinos in dark matter and stellar explosions (supernovae) Among 27 Nuclear Physics Goals: 2006 Quantify neutrino mixing using neutrinos from the sun, cosmic-ray interactions, and nuclear reactors 2011 Establish an electron neutrino mass 2013 Begin experiments to look for nu-less 2-beta decay to provide essential information about the absolute scale of neutrino masses

*** Fermilab long-range plan and visions

Fermilab's vision is to build a linear collider. In case what you see isn't what you get, a second vision is to expand the neutrino program with a proton driver. To do this, Fermilab will prepare a case sufficient to achieve a statement of mission need (CD0) for a 2 MW proton source. There was a general feeling that specifics for this vision weren't clear at an FNAL presentation. http://vmsstreamer1.fnal.gov/VMS_Site_02/Lectures/LongRangePlanning/040311Montgomery/index.htm

*** MINOS has a beamline

On March 10, Fermilab accepted beneficial occupancy of the Service Building, the MINOS Shaft outfitting, and all the underground outfitting work downstream of the Target Hall area (the Target Area had been previously accepted in October 2003.)

*** CERN Workshop on neutrino beams May 25-27

The Workshop organized by ECFA/BENE & EURISOL intends to explore the opportunities offered by a multi-MW p beam of a few GeV, in particular, in the neutrino, slow muon, K sectors and for radioactive ion beams. http://physicsatmwatt.web.cern.ch

*** Three NSF solicitations for Underground Lab

As a next step for NUSEL/DUSEL, the NSF announced a process in which they would issue 3 solicitations for proposals and then would issue some grants to conduct studies for an underground lab. Solicitation I ; "Research & Associated Technical Requirements" would clearly articulate a science and engineering research program for the lab. It should be site independent and ideally involve everybody. Simultaneously, Solicitation II would involve site development and Conceptual Design for a lab, and 1-3 site specific proposals at $300,000 for 6 months could be supported. 90 days to respond. Later Solicitation 3: "Detailed Infrastructure Plan for Underground Science and Engineering".

*** Double CHOOZ approved

Well there are more hoops still to be jumped through, but the APC and Saclay each agreed that a new two detector experiment should be built at CHOOZ to measure theta_13. Other ideas for experiments which reported progress at the 3rd workshop on Future Low Energy Neutrino Experiments in Niigata Japan were Braidwood Illinois, Angra Brazil and Daya Bay China. http://neutrino.hep.sc.niigata-u.ac.jp/

*** Dips

The reported dip from Super-Kamiokande in the atmospheric neutrino L/E distribution is controversial to some dip seekers. http://www-sk.icrr.u-tokyo.ac.jp/noon2004/trape/10-ishitsuka.pdf. There were also some different ideas back in the 60's about what constituted a dip: http://www.neutrinooscillation.org/dip.jpg

*** Solar Neutrinos and the snow cover in France

The distinguished French Newspaper Le Monde reported that the French Power company, EDF, uses solar neutrinos to analyze the mountain snow cover, and hence predict its hydroelectric power potential each season. 40 geiger counters have been installed in the french mountains where "neutrinos...are absorbed by water molecules". Scuttlebutt is a journalism major got it a bit wrong. snow.txt
