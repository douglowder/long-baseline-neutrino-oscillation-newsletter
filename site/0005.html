Long-Baseline news, May 2000

*** A billion here, a billion there

One opinion about planning future HEP facilities is that projects costlier than $1B will not be funded. The strategy then should be to stage facilities in $1B pieces. The nu factory cost estimate is not yet public, but the ballpark guesses are that it could be built in one or two stages.

*** Next steps for neutrino factories

The FNAL Physics and Machine Study reports for nufactory are both available. http://www.fnal.gov/projects/muon_collider/index.html . The physics group has identified 3 next steps: 1) Compare the physics with the capabilities of an upgraded conventional beam, with emphasis on numu to nue (sin^2 theta 13), 2) Further study the polarization yields and 3) pursue detector possibilities and develop a desirable R&D program.

*** Radon implications workshop

A workshop on Radon (and its implications for neutrino experiments) will be held at nu-2000 on June 14th, before the LoNu workshop. Find links at http://www.hep.anl.gov/ndk/hypertext/numeetings.html .

*** NNN00-2

The next meeting for planning a Next Generation Nucleon Decay and Neutrino Detector (NNN) will be at Fermilab August 7-8. e-mail to for any inquiries. Working groups have been organized on 1) site, 2) detector, 3)photo-detectors, 4) nu-factory aspects and 5) physics/simulation

*** NuMI groundbreaking May 31st

It was wet but speeches were moved to Fermilab's auditorium and the shovel photo-op to the atrium garden. Healy is already blasting down in the dolomite. First neutrinos due Oct 1, 2003.

*** Sensenbrenner visits Fermilab

The Chairman of the House Science Committee toured Fermilab May 15th. He gave Fermilab some hope of getting the $33M shortfall in the fiscal 2001 budget put back before the budget is approved. Some of that money is needed for MINOS detector construction.

*** Fermilab PAC promises MINOS data before 2010

The FNAL PAC noted that "NuMI/MINOS is most at risk in the current budget difficulties. It is thus appropriate that the committee emphasizes that MINOS is likely to be the most important neutrino experiment of the decade..."

*** The sphinx's questions on Neutrinos

"What would be the most important experiment in the coming years which has anything to do with neutrino masses?" Minakata suggests that it is proton decay in hep-ph/0004249.

*** THESEUS near detector?

The 1 kT fine-grained Soudan 2 detector was built to search for proton decay. It's use along with the new 6 kT magnetic MINOS detector is called THESEUS. A working group is investigating costs, overlap rates, and physics potential for taking a few Soudan modules and putting them next to the MINOS near detector. But a consensus on this plan has failed to emerge so far. A THESEUS near detector is not in the MINOS project cost.

*** A THESEUS fan?

Speaking at Oxford, B. Richter opined "MINOS is too coarse-grained for the low energy neutrinos" and that "people should concentrate on finer-grained detectors". He was anti neutrino-factories and is pushing/suggesting long-baseline experiments using conventional beams of higher (x 10 -- 100) intensities which are cheaper.
