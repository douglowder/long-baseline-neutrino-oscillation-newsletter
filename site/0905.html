Long-Baseline news, May 2009

*** Why should neutrino physicists go to CERN? October workshop

A 3-day workshop is scheduled at CERN for October 1-3 to consider the future of neutrino physics with emphasis on the development work required for accelerator based neutrino physics. The workshop is being organized jointly by the CERN Management, the Scientific Policy Committee & the European Committee for Future Accelerators. This is in line with the conclusions of the European Strategy for Particle Physics "[4]... it is vital to strengthen the advanced accelerator R&D program; a coordinated programs should be intensified, to develop the CLIC technology and high performance magnets for future accelerators, and to play a significant role in the study and development of a high-intensity neutrino facility. [6]. Studies of the scientific case for future neutrino facilities and the R&D into associated technologies are required to be in a position to define the optimal neutrino program based on the information available in around 2012; Council will play an active role in promoting a coordinated European participation in a global neutrino program." (CERN strategy document, 2006). http://indico.cern.ch/conferenceProgram.py?confId=59378. Then the next IDS-NF meeting will be 12-14 Oct at TIFR in Mumbai.

*** Why we want less data

Most errors are proportional to the square root of the amount of data, so as the data grows, so do the errors. Luckily, neutrino cross sections are small, so we don't have much data or errors.

*** Why have theorists?

Theorists give us ideas and predict things, of the form, "If T, then B", and we experimentalists go look for B. But finding B doesn't prove T, and in fact we can't prove T. So why have theories? Luckily, there is no good theory of neutrinos. L. Wolfenstein once told me that we have 1000 neutrino theories, which is like having no theory.

*** Why do neutrinos oscillate?

Harry Lipkin always offers the rest of us provocative insights into a variety of physics topics, and he has been thinking about neutrino oscillations; in particular is the initial state an energy eigenstate? a momentum eigenstate? is it coherent? Some recent thoughts include "Why do neutrinos oscillate?" http://www.neutrinooscillation.org/lipkin1.txt; "breakthrough" http://www.neutrinooscillation.org/lipkin2.txt; "Theory of neutrino oscillations using condensed matter physics Including production process and energy-time uncertainty", arXiv:0905.1216; "Difficulties in using the sharp neutrino spectrum at short times" http://arxiv.org/abs/0904.4913 See also, "Paradoxes of neutrino oscillations" by Akhmedov and Smirnov, http://arXiv.org/abs/0905.1903

*** Why is the governor of South Dakota Happy?

The pumping of water out of the Homestake mine, the future site of the Deep Underground Science and Engineering Lab (DUSEL), has reached below the 4850-foot level, where many experiments will be located. Gov. Mike Rounds made the announcement May 13 09, "This is a benchmark day in the quest to convert the mine into a world- class laboratory for underground science," the Governor said. "The historic Homestake Mine yielded 40 million ounces of gold before it was shut down in 2001, but it will someday yield something more valuable than any precious metal: knowledge about the universe and the world around us".

*** Why revive a Tevatron Neutrino Program?

A working group has looked at the wide variety of physics that could be accomplished with a new TeV neutrino program. Two proton sources are potentially available in the future: the existing Tevatron at Fermilab, which can provide 800 GeV protons for fixed-target physics, and a possible upgrade to the SPS at CERN, called SPS+, which would produce 1 TeV protons on target. Topics include charm, tau neutrinos, standard model physics, sterile neutrino searches. arXiv:0905.3004

*** Why study atmospheric oscillations?

Matter driven theta13 effects are absent if theta13 is too small. Independent measurements of Delta31 and Delta32 can lead to hierarchy determination. The difference is Delta21, and one needs Delta21 L/E>1. This is satisfied by atmospheric neutrinos, but it is still hard. Gandhi et al., arXiv:0905.2382
