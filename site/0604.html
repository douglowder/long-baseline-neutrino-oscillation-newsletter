Long-Baseline news, April 2006

*** DOE will support the Daya Bay Reactor Neutrino Project

The DOE "received 3 strong proposals targeting R&D for neutrino experiment using reactors around the world. Although these proposals were well developed and the proponents well qualified, given limited resources (we) are only able to support one of them, the Reactor Neutrino Detector at Daya Bay, which has compelling scientific potential based on assessment by NuSAG, and with strong project planning and development, could be made technically feasible and affordable. We continue to be very interested in pursuing a world class reactor neutrino experiment as a single collaborative effort from the US." DOE also had R&D proposals from Double Chooz (DC) and Braidwood, and a full one from DC.

*** DOE discourages Braidwood

By rejecting the R&D proposal, DOE strongly implied a lack of future for the Braidwood neutrino project. Braidwood had been expecting a technical review, and was to present its plans at P5 the next week, though that was canceled.

*** HEPAP on Double Chooz - High Priority

Double Chooz will be a quick cheap experiment sensitive to sin^2 2-theta13 > 0.03. The U.S. request was for about 40% of the detector at $5M. Braidwood or Daya Bay aim to 0.01 if syst. errors are controlled, but at higher U.S. cost and longer time scale. (~$65M & $30M) NuSAG recommended an experiment sensitive to 0.01, but also encouraged participation in DC "with lower scientific priority". In contrast, HEPAP recommended it be high priority. More HEPAP members spoke to this than any other issue.

*** Europeans on neutrinos

Without U.S. support, Double Chooz will still proceed, but not as fast. In a list of main wishes of the European Neutrino Community, "The Double Chooz experiment should be strongly supported." http://www.slac.stanford.edu/grp/ppa/reviews/20060420-P5/talks/jacobs.ppt

Contingent on appropriate budget allocations and NSF approval, the NSF Physics Division is preparing to allocate funds in FY07 for detector-related R&D for underground, DUSEL-related physics experiments and related applications. For more information, see the url's below, which contain a description of the NSF Particle and Nuclear Astrophysics program, and a presentation given by the NSF DUSEL Program Manager for Physics, Jon Kotcher, during a recent visit to the two candidate underground sites. Deadline for proposals will be September 2006. More information will be distributed as it becomes available at: http://www.nsf.gov/funding/pgm_summ.jsp?pims_id=5633&org=PHY See Slide 14 http://neutrino.lbl.gov/Homestake/NSF_site_visit/NSF_site_visit.pdf or http://nngroup.physics.sunysb.edu/husep/KotcherDUSELOverviewMar06Sites.pdf

*** EPP2010 - a linear collider and what else?

The NAS decadal study on HEP, promotes a U.S. linear collider. It endorses the LHC program, and a buildup of particle astrophysics Plus neutrinos, proton decay and precision experiments. Clear advice under priorities is "Under no circumstances, therefore, should the committee's top 2 or 3 priorities be permitted to exhaust the entire available budget". http://www.interactions.org/cms/?pid=1024118

*** How did neutrinos do in EPP2010?

"*) A Phased program of searches for the nature of neutrino mass (using neutrinoless double beta decay) be pursued high priority *) DOE & NSF should invite international partners to initiate a multi party study to explore the feasibility of joint rather than parallel efforts in accelerator based neutrino experiments. Major investments in this area should be evaluated in light of the outcome of this process. *) Longer term goal should include experiments to unravel possible CP violation in the physics of neutrinos and renewed searches for proton decay. There may be a valuable synergy between these important objectives, as the nu CP violation measurements may require a very large detector that, if placed underground, will also be the right instrument for detecting proton decay." NOvA advocates worry that the 2nd point is aimed at them, though it could also be understood to refer to NOvA upgrades. Meanwhile, when the world price of oil declines, NOvA costs will plummet.

*** Scoping Study for Neutrino Factory Meets at RAL

The International scoping study and BENE (Beams for European Neutrino Experiments) met at Rutherford in April. http://www.hep.ph.ic.ac.uk/uknfic/iss0406/iss-beneprogr-plen.html An LOI to the FP7 for Particle Physics accelerator R&D projects was distributed for submission. The goal is to express intent to submit at least one proposal to undertake a design study and R&D for the next generation of neutrino oscillation facilities, in particular the Neutrino Factory and the Beta beam.
