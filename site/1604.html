Long-Baseline news, April 2016

*** Improving neutrino physics at J-PARC/T2K

A new facility in J-PARC to upgrade the Main Ring accelerator has been approved. This facility will house a new power supply system with which the repetition rate of the Main Ring will be doubled, resulting in 750 kW beam power, with the potential to exceed 1 MW. The T2K collaboration is considering improvements in physics sensitivity, especially for CP violation, with an extension of the running time with beam power of 1.3 MW. An Expression of Interest for the T2K extension was presented to the J-PARC PAC in Jan.

*** Fire at Fermilab recycler, no weasel or marten involved

On Shakespeare's Birthday, the recycler anode supply power transformer caught fire, causing a glitch that took out several other systems. To see the fire, go to slides 5 & 6 at https://indico.fnal.gov/getFile.py/access?contribId=0&resId=0&materialId=slides&confId=12023 Fermilab was able to quickly return beam to users in a few hours, but there is no recycler available for slip stacking, so the NuMI intensity is about half. They may be able to restart the recycler on 11 May. *** Booster Neutrino Beam at Fermilab exceeding flux goals

While the neutrino rate at NuMI is low for a while, the Booster neutrino beam has already far exceeded the FY16 integrated number of protons on target. See slide 3 of the previous Indico link. As of 1 May, they had 275 e16 protons, compared to 125 e16 design.

*** A phased array for radio detection of neutrinos

Looking for a radio signal of high energy neutrinos using the Askaryan effect, balloon flight ANITA-1 saw 1 event with a background of 1.1, ANITA-2 saw 1 with a a background of .97, and ANITA-3 in 2014 has a blind analysis underway. ARA has 3 test stations deep in the Antarctic Ice and will have more 2017-2018. But a phased array for PEV and UHE nus could see a correlated signal while the noise is uncorrelated. Phasing 400 antennas could provide good energy overlap with Ice-Cube while being sensitive to GZK neutrinos. They ran tests on antennas in existing boreholes in Greenland to improve GZK sensitivity by 100.

*** Two new Minerva results relevant for long-baseline searches

1) Evidence for NC diffractive production of neutral pions, a surprise process that is not in current generators but can be a background for nu_e appearance using a hydrocarbon target. 2) First measurement of CC K+ production. This is an irreducible background from atmospheric neutrinos for proton decay to a neutrino and a K+. arXiv:1604.01728 & arXiv:1604.03920

*** This month's new word: scotogenic

Well new to me. It means caused by dark matter. A scotogenic model in arXiv:1604.07777 gives maximal theta23, delta_CP is pi/2 or 3pi/2. Another scotogenic model is found in arXiv:1604.05569 .

*** Latin American Neutrinos

A workshop at Fermilab focused on past and future Latin American contributions to neutrino physics, with a focus on DUNE. https://indico.fnal.gov/conferenceDisplay.py?confId=11392 Representatives from eight countries were involved.

*** Civil Construction progress for FNAL Short-Baseline program

MicroBooNE is running, but buildings are under construction for the short-baseline program near detector (SBND) and far detector (ICARUS). They will be complete at the end of calendar year 16. To increase the intensity of the booster beam, a longer horn & refurbished power supply could double the event rate. It costs $6.5M; with design starting now it could be in operation in 2019.

*** Hyper-K open meeting 10-11 July, Queen Mary U in London

The Hyper-Kamiokande project is being designed to be the next decade's flagship experiment for the study of nu oscillations, nucleon decays, and astrophysical neutrinos. An International Open Working Group Meeting for Hyper-K will be held 10-11 July. http://indico.ipmu.jp/indico/conferenceDisplay.py?ovw=True&confId=94
