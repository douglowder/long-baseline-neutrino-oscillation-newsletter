Long-Baseline news, February 2004

*** Direct Oscillatory evidence from Super-K

At NOON2004, Super-K reported seeing a "dip" in their L/E analysis of atmospheric neutrinos for the first time. Though expected for oscillations (versus decay or decoherence, for example), the L/E resolution limited this. The method involved selecting events with the best L/E resolution. A dip is clearly seen at 600 km/GeV. This also measures delta m squared to be between 1.9 and 3.0 10^-3 eV^2 at 90% CL. "See Oscillations" can now be crossed off the neutrino physics to-do list.

*** DONUT results on tau neutrino

The DONUT collaboration discovered the interaction of the tau neutrino with 4 events in 1999. They have about 900 neutrino interactions in their emulsion, and they continue to scan and analyse their data. They presently have identified two more trident events, for a total of 6 candidates, 6.3 expected. They also have 8 charm seen, 6.9 expected.

*** JPARC budget

The approval of the neutrino project in J-PARC for a five-year construction includes a total budget of 16 billion Yen approved by the cabinet in Japan. Although the final official decision on any budget matter must wait until the approval by the House at the end of March, 2004, a change is unlikely. The construction of the neutrino beam line will start in JFY04 that starts 1-APR-04. The JPARC budget is now 151 billion Yen.

*** Reactor Neutrino Experiment to study NuTeV anomaly?

Experiments are being designed to measure theta_13. Another idea, being developed by Janet Conrad & others is to collect enough nu-e elastic scatters in the near detector (and separate them from the inverse beta decays) to measure the Weinberg angle. http://apsreactor.uchicago.edu/meetings/chicago/conrad.pdf
