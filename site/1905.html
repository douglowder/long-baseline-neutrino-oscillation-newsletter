Long-Baseline news, May 2019

*** Fermilab neutrino running curtailed

The FNAL operations budget is strained as funding to PIP-II, LNBF/ DUNE, mu2e, and other projects ramp up. So beam will be severely curtailed for the rest of the year. Beam is now 5 days on and 9 days off until the start of the summer shutdown in July. This changes NOvA expectation from 6e20 to 5.3e20 POT this year. It also affects the beam for the NOvA test beam detector which just started taking data.

*** J-PARC neutrino running curtailed

On 24 Apr., it was observed that the field strength dropped by 2% in one of bending magnets (B15D). Upon investigation they found insufficient insulation between conductors in coils of the B15D. They suspended main ring operations. User operation will be resumed in the Nov., after the coil replacement in Sep.

*** Limits on sterile neutrinos from beta decay

In arXiv:1904.06787, Bryman and Shrock look at various particle decays, as well as superallowed nuclear beta decays, to set improved limits on Ue4^2 in the MeV to GeV mass range.

*** White Paper from USQCD on neutrino-nucleus scattering

This program of lattice-QCD calculations is relevant to neutrino experiments, becoming increasingly important on the timescale of LBNF/DUNE and Hyper-K. The US LatticeQCD collaboration classifies the problems into 3 groups; straightforward, challenging, & holy grail. The holy grail for nu-N scattering is controlled QCD calculations of the axial form factors, resonance transition form factors, and nuclear PDFs for 40Ar.
