Long-Baseline news, November 2001

*** Super-Kamiokande accident

While refilling, a tube, most likely on the bottom, imploded and set off a chain reaction destroying much of it to about a depth of 2 m below the current (~2/3) water level. Waveshifter plates are shattered, cables perhaps shredded, etc. The water is full of antimony and other hazardous chemicals. About 7000 of the 11000 22-inch phototubes (~$3000 each) were broken. See pics at http://www-sk.icrr.u-tokyo.ac.jp/doc/sk/photo/pmt-damage/

*** Super-Kamiokande to rebuild

Super-K will rebuild the detector. "There is no question." The strategy may be the following steps, which will be proposed and discussed by the Super-K and K2K collaborations: 1. Quick restart of the K2K experiment.
(1) clear the safety measures suggested by all committees;
(2) reduce the density of the photomultiplier tubes by ~1/2;
(3) use the existing resources;
(4) resume K2K, goal ~ 1 year;
2. Preparation for the JHF-Kamioka experiment.
(1) Restore the full detector using state-of-the-art techniques;
(2) The detector will be ready by the time of JHF commissioning;

*** Bin Laden lauds neutrino detector blast

Bakhrubabad, Afghanistan - Speaking from his hidden mountain stronghold, Osama bin Laden praised the destruction of the Super-Kamiokande neutrino detector. The terrorist leader said neutrinos are "...an abomination on the face of God," and termed the search for neutrino mixing "...idolatry, which will be smashed beneath the fist of righteousness."

*** Particle Production Experiment 907 approved.

This is a proposal to measure Particle Production in the Meson Area Using Main Injector primary and secondary beams. One goal is to measure particle production off NuMI targets using 120 GeV/c protons with sufficient accuracy to predict the NuMI neutrino spectrum. Similar efforts at CERN are NA49/HARP.

*** MICE

A letter of intent for an International Muon Ionization Cooling Experiment has been submitted. This is a next step for a neutrino factory based on a muon storage ring. The draft is at http://proj-bdl-nice.web.cern.ch/proj-bdl-nice/cool/loi/draft1/loi-draft1.pdf Alain Blondel is spokesman.

*** CERN workshop Jan 16-18 on neutrinos and proton decay

A workshop on "Large Detectors for Proton Decay, Supernovae and atmospheric neutrinos and low energy neutrinos from high intensity beams" will be held at CERN 1/16-18. A low energy beam from the Cern Superconducting Proton Linac (SPL) is among topics. See http://muonstoragerings.cern.ch/NuWorkshop02/welcome.html .

*** S17 (relevant to solar neutrino Boron flux error).

A new measurement of S17, the 7Be(p,gamma)8B cross section, appears in Junghans et al., nucl-ex/0111014. They report S17(0)=22.3+-0.7(expt)+-0.5(theor) eV-b. This is a bit higher than the value in BP calculations, and indirect measurements are significantly lower; ~17.8 ( nucl-ex/0104003 & nucl-ex/0101010 ) &

*** K2K data

Through this summer, K2K, the one long-baseline neutrino experiment with data ( hep-ex/0110034), has observed 44 events versus 63.9 expected in the absence of mixing. (Don't just say 63.9 events are expected. Mixing is expected now.) Fogli et al, hep-ph/0110089, show the consistency with neutrino oscillations from Super-K's atmospheric results and combine results.

*** NuTEV gets high Weinberg angle.

Or everyone else got a low Weinberg angle. Or some new neutrino physics... NuTeV measures the neutral current to charged current rate for neutrinos and ant-neutrinos Their sin^2 th-W = 0.2277 +-.0013(st)+-.0009(sy) is 3 sig > world average. hep-ex/0110059.
