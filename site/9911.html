Long-Baseline news, November 1999

*** K2K resumes running

K2K data taking started again on 30 October at 01:00 am JST, right on schedule. Initial conditions were: Horn current 250 kA, proton intensity was 5 10^12 ppp. In previous running they have one fidvol event, expecting about 5.5 (no oscillations).

*** Fermilab rejects Emulsion R&D proposal

The PAC and Fermilab Director are not convinced that the emulsion method would lead to a compelling experiment. "The case for hybrid emulsion R&D is not strong enough to justify the investment of already-scarce Laboratory resources."

*** PPARC approves MINOS

UK participation/funding for MINOS was approved enthusiastically.

*** Aspen summer neutrino meeting

A workshop titled "NEUTRINOS WITH MASS, THE NEW PARADIGM IN PHYSICS AND ASTROPHYSICS" will be held at Aspen 6/26-7/17/00. In addition to updated results from many reactor, accelerator and underground experiments, there will be new experimental results from K2K, SNO, and SuperK. There are other experiments in preparation such as GALLEX, KamLAND, MINOS, and detectors in Gran Sasso which we hope will produce results in the next few years. Future new projects could test theoretical predictions from different scenarios. Email or see http://andy.bu.edu/aspen/workshops00.html#neutrinos.

*** MINOS blaster likes work

The MINOS cavity blasting has finished the entrance tunnel and is ramping up to the ceiling of the MINOS cavity. A front page article about the blasting in the Tower "Timberjay" reports that "Logistics, unique rules make work complicated for MINOS cavern contractor". It wasn't clear if one of the unique rules was conservation of lepton number. 22,000 cubic yards of greenstone are to be removed, under a $5.1 Million contract. A jumbo driller makes 110 holes for each blast in two shifts. Photos at http://131.212.67.203/albums/MINOS/Weekly_Photos

*** Interest growing in sub-dominant numu to nue

The numu to nutau explanation of the Super-K results implies that numu to nue should be seen with the same delta m-squared, but with strength proportional to MNS matrix element U_e3. Chooz limits this to under 5%. There is potential for confusion because the relevant parameter differs by a factor of 2 from the usual two-component mixing angle expression.

*** Neutrino ponderomotive force

Neutrino oscillations in dense matter produce collective forces, which are maximal for resonant conversion in the adiabatic region. See Grasso, below

*** Should the km^3 detector be put on Uranus?

Escribano et al. (below) consider the possibility of neutrino lensing by astrophysical objects. Nus can cross a star's core which results in better focalization. The Earth-sun distance is too small for a sizable effect to take place, but a neutrino telescope on Uranus would notice the enhancement of distant neutrino sources whenever they are aligned with the sun.
