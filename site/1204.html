Long-Baseline news, April 2012

*** LBNE reconfiguration

After deciding that LBNE which was being developed and had a $1.4B estimated price could not be supported, Office of Science Director Bill Brinkman asked Fermilab for a report on a phased approach. That cost included a new 700kW beam, a 34 kT liquid Argon Far Detector underground at Homestake, a Near Detector, and some costs for that lab. Fermilab appointed a steering committee with a timeline determined by the need to provide detailed information on plans to Congress for its consideration of the President's FY2013 Budget Request and for the planning of the Office of Science budget proposals for 14. A draft report will be delivered to Oddone by 1 Jun and the final report by 1 Jul. 20 members of the steering committee are led by Y.K. Kim. A 15 member Physics Working Group is led by M. Shochet. A 10 member cost group is chaired by M. Reichanadter & J. Strait. http://www.fnal.gov/directorate/lbne_reconfiguration/

*** LBNE reconfiguration recapitulation

178 physicists attended a two day meeting at Fermilab to consider the physics and options for long-baseline neutrino oscillations using the NuMI beam and new detectors at Soudan or Ash River, and a new beam with smaller detectors at Homestake. 50 stayed with 50 others for an LBNE collaboration meeting. Surface detectors cost less, but have less physics, if any. Budget guidance for phase 1 is $600M. The committee will choose acceptable Phase 1 options, have a dialog with DOE-OHEP, and prepare a report. https://indico.fnal.gov/contributionListDisplay.py?confId=5456

*** LBNE appropriation recalculation

A congressional subcommittee added back $16M for LBNE to the $10M in the President's budget for FY2013, = $26M.

*** MINOS running rescission (i.e. end)

I started working on calculating neutrino oscillations using a beam from Fermilab to Soudan in 1987. This newsletter, with a focus on that experiment started in 1992. The MINOS collaboration submitted its proposal in 1995. The first neutrino from Fermilab interacted in MINOS in 2005. The last neutrino arrived last week. MINOS+ will continue after the shutdown, but MINOS as originally proposed has now ended with 15.6e20 total protons on target. http://www.fnal.gov/pub/news_update/2012/update_04-27_04-30.html

*** RENO neutrino disappearance revision

Shortly after releasing its result for theta_13 from 229 days of running with 2 detectors and 6 reactors, the RENO collaboration released a revised result. The ratio of observed to expected numbers of antineutrinos in the far detector is now 0.920 +- 0.009(stat) +- 0.014(syst). From this deficit, they now find sin^2(2-q13) = 0.113 +-0.013(stat) +-0.019(syst), 4.9 standard deviations from zero. arXiv:1204.0626v2
