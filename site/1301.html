Long-Baseline news, January 2013

*** India-based Neutrino Observatory going ahead

The 50 kton atmospheric nu detector has approval from the state government. Site work in the West Bodi Hills to start in Feb. http://www.thehindu.com/news/states/tamil-nadu/neutrino-observatory-site-work-to-kick-off-in-february/article4326609.ece

*** 78 Neutrino Whitepapers led the big parade

The 2013 Snowmass Neutrino working group is part of the Intensity Frontier Working Group and itself is divided into 7 study groups. There was a call for one-page whitepapers on topics of interest to the community, and 78 were received. Topics range from summaries of recent analyses to new ideas for experiments. See: http://www.snowmass2013.org/tiki-index.php?page=Received+Whitepapers and participate in the 6-7 March SLAC meeting.

*** with 110 Young Scientists close at hand

http://www.hep.anl.gov/ndk/hypertext/snowmassdoensf.pdf A memo from the DOE and NSF was sent to clarify the Snowmass process and other planning efforts for HEP. It wrote: "...the DPF-led Snowmass process has been established to identify compelling science opportunities over an approximately 20 year time frame." Some of us don't have 20 years of neutrino studies left in us, so the studies are most relevant to the younger members of our community. If you (think you) are young, visit http://snowmassyoung.hep.net/ and/or contact Jonathan or Roxanne .

*** Rows and rows of other studies; Office of Science Facilities

Each 10 years, the DOE Office of Science lists large new projects. This time it is called "Brinkman's Facilities Panel". Large means >$100M. Input from the 6 advisory panels is due 22 March. http://science.energy.gov/~/media/bes/besac/pdf/2012-1220-brinkman-to-advisorycommittees.pdf

*** Rows and rows of other studies; NSAC

https://twindico.hep.anl.gov/indico/getFile.py/access?resId=0&materialId=slides&confId=1291 Fundamental Symmetries and neutrinos are an important part of the mission of the Division of Nuclear Physics, as emphasized in a recent report to NSAC. *** Rows and rows of other studies; Europe

https://indico.cern.ch/getFile.py/access?resId=0&materialId=0&confId=217656 . An update of the European Strategy for Particle Physics has been released, but needs to be ratified by the Cern Council in March. The draft states, "Rapid progress in neutrino oscillation physics, with significant European involvement, has established a strong scientific case for a long-baseline neutrino program exploring CP violation and the mass hierarchy in the neutrino sector. CERN should develop a neutrino program to pave the way for a substantial European role in future long-baseline experiments. Europe should explore the possibility of major participation in leading neutrino projects in the US and Japan."

*** Alfred K. Mann 1920-2013

http://articles.philly.com/2013-01-23/news/36508061_1_neutrinos-eugene-beier-particles The first idea that I ever heard for a long-baseline neutrino experiment came from a Fermilab Seminar by Al Mann. His work appeared in http://prd.aps.org/abstract/PRD/v15/i3/p655_1, "Neutrino oscillations and the number of neutrino types" in 1977. At the time of the Long-Baseline Neutrino Workshop in 1992, he proudly told me that this was the first paper in PRD with a map. A tireless advocate for a US Underground Lab, he will be missed.

*** George Tzanakos 1948-2013

A long-time neutrino researcher, George participated in DONUT, MINOS, MINERvA and NOvA. He was a member of the International Neutrino Commission and organized Neutrino 2010 in Athens Greece.

*** Liquid Argon videos

These videos from Terry Tope of the Liquid Argon Purity Demonstrator show that liquid argon is a very clear colorless cold liquid. One video shows a sub-cooled state with no boiling. The other is the transition to boiling as the pressure is lowered. http://dbweb3.fnal.gov:8080/ECL/lartpc/E/file/6747/subcooled_surface.mov ; & http://dbweb3.fnal.gov:8080/ECL/lartpc/E/file/6746/subcooled_to_boiling_transition.mov

*** NOvA, the next long-baseline neutrino experiment, progress

http://minnesota.publicradio.org/display/web/2013/01/11/daily-circuit-nova-far-detector-neutrinos NOvA is being built in Northern Minnesota block by block, and is being filled. Some will be ready for Fermilab beam this summer.
