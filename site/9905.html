Long-Baseline news, May 1999

*** K2K taking data

The first long-baseline neutrino experiment continues to take data. In a run ending in March they saw about 100k events in the near detector. This allowed them to study the neutrino beam relative to various beam monitors and horn current settings. The direction of the neutrino beam has now been confirmed by the front-detector profile. Although they have seen 0 events in Super-K, data from monitors and the front detector are being analyzed to predict the expected number of events per proton. They are taking more data in May-June before the summer shutdown.

*** CERN moving forward

While no formal action on long-baseline plans are expected at CERN before December 99, NGS proponents in Europe (for a CERN to Gran Sasso neutrino beam) expect strong encouragement from meetings in June. The US continues to urge collaboration.

*** Main Injector dedicated by big shots.

A governor (Ryan), energy secretary (Richardson), senator (Fitzpatrick) and Speaker of the House (Hastert) all converged at Fermilab June 1 to dedicate the Main Injector. They each got 10^13 neutrinos to take home.

*** MINOS

NuMI/MINOS passed its second "Lehman" Review and received CD3B approval, which allows authority to start constructing the beam at Fermilab. Meanwhile, the excavation contract for the new cavity at Soudan was won by Lametti, a Minnesota firm. Excavation will start slowly during the tourist season. The new NuMI project manager at Fermilab is Dixon Bogert, replacing Tom Fields.

*** ICARUS

ICARUS is the only detector which is already "approved" to run in the CERN to Gran Sasso beam. They are building a 600 ton detector and planning for the physics capabilities of a 30 kton Super-Icarus. A 10 m^3 prototype of the cryogenic and purity systems was tested during February and March. All goals were reached, in particular temperature uniformity (which affects the tension on the wires), liquid Argon purity, and low noise.

*** DAEDALUS

The distances from CERN to Gran Sasso and Fermilab to Soudan are both, coincidentally, 730 km. In their 730 km flight from Crete to Sicily, Daedalus made it and Icarus did not. Ikaria, where Icarus reputedly tumbled into the sea, is only 250 km from Crete, the same baseline as KEK to Super-Kamiokande. Daedalus is a new design for a 15 kt fine-grained liquid scintillator long-baseline nu detector which relies on good identification of quasi-elastic events. See Doucet et al., below.

*** THESEUS

THE Second Experiment Underground at Soudan (THESEUS) is Soudan 2, a fine-grained one kiloton tracking calorimeter which will be in the NuMI-MINOS beam when it turns on. It will be part of the MINOS experiment, but is separate from the new MINOS magnetic detector. Soudan 2 has operated as a nucleon decay and atmospheric neutrino experiment for about ten years. The designation THESEUS distinguishes its MINOAN role as a long-baseline detector from its non-accelerator function.

*** New NOMAD Limit

NOMAD has improved its numu to nutau limit by a factor of 3.5 over previous published limits and almost a factor of two from what was reported at Neutrino '98. The data corresponds to 950,000 numu CC interactions, and they see no signal for nutaus, setting a limit sin^2 2-theta<1.2 10^-3 for delta-m^2>1.0 eV^2. See Astier et al., below.
