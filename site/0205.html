Long-Baseline news, May 2002

*** Solar Angle is not maximal

The most recent solar fits point to the Large Mixing Angle solar Solution (LMA) but apparently not maximal mixing. For example, a fit by Smirnov gives a best fit to (Dm^2)_12 = 6.2 10^-5 eV^2 and tan^2 theta_12 = 0.40, ruling out maximal mixing more than 3 sigma.

*** Using SNO data

Global analysis by de Holanda & Smirnov is in hep-ph/0205241. Other analyses using new SNO data are in hep-ph/0204194 , hep-ph/0204173 , hep-ph/0204253 , astro-ph/0203107, hep-ph/0204265 , hep-ph/0204286 , hep-ph/0204305 , hep-ph/0205053 & hep-ph/0205061

*** New results and results we knew (at Neutrino 2002)

Neutrino conferences happen every two years. But most results in between are reported in between. Most of us heard the exciting new SNO neutral current result before. One new result involves dark matter, Edelweiss ruling out most all of the DAMA allowed region.

*** K2K fits to Delta m-squared

This long-baseline neutrino experiment in Japan finds a best fit to oscillations with maximal mixing and (Dm^2)_23 = 2.8 10^-3 eV^2.

*** Two sources, one solar neutrino flux.

Reporting the ratio of measured to expected phi_pp, Kirsten was asked where we get expected phi_pp. His answer was, "from god or John Bahcall." It was pointed out that some do not believe in god. During a later talk, Bahcall reported this was the first neutrino conference in 40 years where there is greater belief in the audience about the solar model than there was on the podium.

*** Two numbers from KamLAND, no answer yet.

They showed 11 anti-nu_e candidates, with R^2< 5m^2. These are excellent candidate events from faraway nuclear reactors. The also now have ~1600 hours of livetime. The two numbers don't correspond to each other, though.

*** Two Homestake letters, one with intent

Ken Lande received a letter dated April 8th requesting he remove the historic Chlorine detector from the mine. Data taking has ceased and all counting of previous data is now terminated, but negotiations to keep the detector in place continue. He also proposes a 10x larger detector with more extractions. An LOI for long-baseline from Brookhaven to Wipp (2880km), Soudan (1770km), Homestake (2540km) and others is at hep-ex/0205040

*** Two beta decay, no neutrinos

At Nu2002 there was widespread skepticism about the reported indication for 0nu double beta decay. The discussion after "Neutrinoless double beta decay: review and future" was lively & interesting, in part because it included much periphery (such as calling people "commissars" and delays in publication). A reply to a comment on the result is hep-ph/0205228. It ends "We think it remains useful and inspiring to have informed the neutrino community about our evidence for a 2.2-3.1 sigma result ..."

*** Two experiments, one numu to nutau limit

A joint Chorus/Nomad analysis high dm^2, sin^2 2q < 1.7 10^-4.

*** Two detectors in one (NuMI) beam?

*** Continued work toward neutrino factory

The Muon Collaboration discussed a number of issues at Shelter Island, May 8-15. New developments include a planned proposal for a Muon Ionization Cooling Experiment (MICE), which will be sent to Rutherford Lab, new results on radiation produced in rf cavities, and tests of windows for liquid hydrogen absorbers. Simulations with realistic 6D cooling channels that generate longitudinal cooling are giving good results - for different geometries. Work on targetry and cost reduction is also proceeding.
