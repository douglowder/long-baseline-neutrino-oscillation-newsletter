Long-Baseline news, October 2018

*** Hyper-Kamiokande to start construction in 2020

On 12 Sep 2018, during the Hyper-Kamiokande general meeting at IPMU (Kashiwa, U.Tokyo), Professor T. Kajita, Director of the Institute for Cosmic Ray Research at the University of Tokyo and 2015 Nobel Laureate in Physics, on behalf of the University of Tokyo, announced the start of construction of the experiment in 2020. The proto-collaboration that is currently writing the Technical Report for the far detector to finalize its design, as well as working on the high power beam and near detector configuration, was excited by this ground-breaking news and welcomes more international partners. http://www.hyperk.org/?p=387

*** European Neutrino meeting 22-24 Oct at CERN

https://indico.cern.ch/event/740296/ Panels on oscillations (convener P. Huber), Beyond PMNS (O. Fischer), Nus & the Universe (M. Shoposhnikov) and Ancillary measurements (F. Sanchez) are getting ready. This is related to the update of the European Strategy for Particle Physics, a 2-year process aiming at developing a common vision for the future of particle physics in Europe. The process is expected to be concluded in May 2020. To inform this process, written input is invited 18 Dec 2018. https://indico.cern.ch/event/765096/

*** Neutrino replaces Higgs as #1 in Review of Particle Physics

Order RPP @ http://pdg.lbl.gov/2018/html/receive_our_products.html or you can download individual reviews and print them yourself. All 127 reviews from 2016 were downloaded more than 1000 times. The top one was "Neutrino Mass, Mixing and Oscillations" by Petcov and Nakamura with ~69,000 downloads. In the 2014 edition, Higgs Boson Physics was downloaded the most. There is a new review on Neutrinos and Cosmology in 2018.

*** theta23 values in PDG

In the 2018 edition, the PDG quotes 4 values of sin^2(q23), depending on the mass order and angle octant. For normal order NO Octant 2 is is 0.597 +0.024 -0.030; and 0.417 for NO,O1; 0.592 for IO,O2, 0.421 for IO,O1. See pdgLive

*** New PDG listings for double beta decay

Previously, results from two neutrino double beta decay and neutrinoless double beta decay were listed together. For 2018, 2nu-betabeta lifetimes are in 1 node, 0nu-betabeta limits another.

*** New release of Genie 3.0.0 Neutrino generator

This is a major release designed to help the users to select the best configuration for their analysis purposes. The main difference, from the user point of view, is the concept of tune, which is a set of consistent models and parameters. With v3 the collaboration will support numerous tunes out of the box, whose brief description can be found at http://tunes.genie-mc.org/.

*** Using geoneutrinos to search for "second continents"

So-called second continents are upper crustal materials subducted below 300 km and gravitationally stabilized near 650 km depth. Seismological techniques can't image them. But Roskovec et al. show in arXiv:1810.10914 "Testing a proposed 2nd continent under Eastern China using geoneutrinos" that land and ocean-based geonu detectors deployed at strategic locations could find Australia sized 2nd continents and test models of mantle/plate dynamics.
