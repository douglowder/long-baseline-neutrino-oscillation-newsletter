Long-Baseline news, November 2013

*** Neutrino Discussions for Europe at CERN

https://indico.cern.ch/conferenceDisplay.py?confId=285170 At a CERN meeting, several possible future scenarios for support of nu research in Europe were considered. From a summary talk about the CERN plans, CERN offers a platform for neutrino detector R&D, will support this platform in an active way and will help WA104 & WA105 and others in this initial phase. CERN will construct a large neutrino test area (EHN1 extension) with charged beam capabilities, available in 2016. It will continue detailed studies of a short-baseline neutrino beam at CERN and will assist the EU neutrino community in its long term common plans. The European neutrino community will again discuss its plans at a meeting of the ICFA neutrino panel in Paris in January. http://www.apc.univ-paris7.fr/APC/Conferences/ICFA_Neutrino_European_Meeting_2014/Home.html

*** MicroBooNE progress

MicroBooNE will be a 170 ton Liquid Argon neutrino detector in the FNAL Booster beam which will determine if the MiniBooNE low energy excess is due to photons or electrons. It is also a step toward the construction of massive LAr TPCs. The wireplane and cryostat are being assembled in the Dzero building. The PMTs were installed in Aug/Sep, and a fiber calibration system Sep-Oct. The wireplane will be finished and put into the cryostat soon. The building for MicroBooNE has been finished and will be ready to receive the cryostat when it is done. https://www.facebook.com/MicroBooNE ; http://www-microboone.fnal.gov/news/index.html

*** Muon Ionization Cooling Experiment (MICE) progress

Muon ionization cooling is an essential technique for future intense stored-muon-beam facilities such as nu factories and mu colliders. An old idea, it has yet to be put to practice. MICE aims to demonstrate 10% ionization cooling of a muon beam by its interaction with low-Z absorber materials followed by restoration of longitudinal momentum in short RF linacs. "Step IV," including the 1st LH2 or LiH absorber cell sandwiched between two particle tracking spectrometers, will constitute the first experimental test of the technique and will provide important validations of the physics models used in cooling-channel design simulations. The first of two large superconducting spectrometer solenoids needed for Step IV has recently been trained, mapped, and delivered to RAL, and training of the second is under way, as is that of the Focus Coils needed to provide the desired optics at the absorber. Step IV running is planned for 2014-15. In MICE Step VI, including three absorbers and two short linacs, the full range of ionization cooling optics and parameters will be tested, towards the end of this decade.

*** TallBo tests of photon detectors

A team from Indiana University has successfully conducted tests of their latest design for an ultraviolet photon detector system, as the first users of the new "TallBo" liquid argon dewar facility at Fermilab. This PD prototype is intended for the LBNE far detector, where scintillation light will provide valuable timing information for event reconstruction in a TPC. The team collected many days' worth of cosmic ray events to evaluate the performance of their system and help steer decisions for the final detector design.

At a hearing of the Subcommittee on Energy of the House Committee on Science, Rep. Randy Hultgren (R-IL) posed several questions to Patricia Dehmer, Acting Director of the Office of Science. Dehmer assured him that Fermilab will be at the forefront in working at the intensity frontier. She added, "we're looking forward to a very good future for High Energy Physics and (FNAL)". Hultgren spoke of the importance of the Long-Baseline Neutrino Experiment.

*** PIP II

Pip is a cabin boy who goes crazy in Moby Dick. It is also the initials for the Proton Improvement Plan, i.e. efforts by FNAL's accelerator division to increase the proton intensity for Fermilab Intensity Frontier Experiments. A less costly plan than Project X for improving the intensity of the Fermilab Main Injector for future neutrino experiments will be described at the Brookhaven P5 meeting in December and named PIP II.
