Long-Baseline news, May 1998

*** New analysis of SuperKamiokande atmospheric neutrino data

A neutrino oscillation analysis/announcement regarding SuperK data is expected during Neutrino '98. A special edition of long-baseline news will probably be sent out next week.

*** NOW98-Neutrino Oscillation Workshop in Amsterdam in September

A 2.5 day workshop at NIKHEF will be 7-9 September. Information can be obtained at http://www.nikhef.nl/pub/conferences/now98

*** wet SNO

There is about eight feet of water in the cavity, but the fill was stopped to assess how many tubes are likely to be dead (not many). The serious fill started May 19 and there should to be heavy water in by the end of the month. There are more than 7000 phototubes cabled in to the electronics, the last 1000 are just about ready to go. It will probably be another six weeks before all channels are connected. The fill will take two and a half months.

*** Baltay panel reviews MINOS

A subpanel of the Fermilab PAC reported that the physics case for MINOS is stronger now than 3 years ago. A small region of allowed parameter space may not be covered, but we should proceed and continue to be flexible. Emulsion R&D should be encouraged.

*** French neutrinos met

80 people attended the "Journees Neutrino" workshop to discuss where French neutrino physics should be going. Topics included solar neutrinos, reactor neutrinos, astrophysics, Fermilab 's BOONE, DONUT and MINOS, the short-baseline proposals at CERN, and the entire Gran-Sasso program including 5 long-baseline proposals (ICARUS, NOE, NICE, OPERA and AQUARICH). If CERN approves the long-baseline at the end of '98 and civil engineering tenders went out in Spring '99, they would have a beam in 2003. All 5 are in the range of 50M sf, and the beam is around 70M sf; sans near detector hall.

*** Evidence for oscillations in IMB up mu data?

When an experiment stops taking data, analysis does not stop, but it may slow down. There was an oscillation limit from a comparison of the upward muons (from nus) in IMB with the Volkova flux. But an unofficial and rough re-analysis of the shape of the zenith angle distribution of those up mu's agrees with oscillations (dmsq ~ 0.005) and disagrees with no-oscillations at the 5 sigma level. See www.phys.hawaii.edu/~jgl/post/imb_upmu_osc.html. If true, this might imply that the flux calculations were low by 20-40%. But improved cross section calculations, (Lipari et al Phys. Rev. Let. 74,4384, 1995) have made this inconsistency disappear.

*** First long-baseline neutrino Ph.D?

David Petyt successfully passed the viva-voce examination for his D. Phil at Oxford University in May. As far as long-baseline news knows, this is the first D. Phil purely on an accelerator long- baseline experiment. His next stop is the RAL Soudan/MINOS group.

*** Baikal Neutrino Telescope NT200 completed

On April 6, 1998, NT-200 in Lake Baikal was completed. This is the preliminary end of a long journey, starting in 1980 with first site surveys at the Lake and getting a concrete form with a 192-PMT array called NT-200 in 1989/90. With a dozen nu events identified (1994-1997), the goal is now to record neutrinos with a 2000 m^2 detector and a 10-15 GeV threshold (approximately 1 event/day induced by atmospheric neutrinos). In parallel, an upgrade towards a >10 000 m^2 detector is envisaged. The Baikal project is a Russian-German collaboration.

*** Notable new references

  • Xing, A Phenomenological Interpretation of Atmospheric and Solar Neutrino Oscillations, hep-ph/9804433
  • S. Pakvasa, Some Phenomenological Aspects of Neutrino Physics, hep-ph/9804427 techniques to distinguish amongst various oscillation scenarios from atmospheric neutrino data.
  • G.L.Fogli et al., "Zenith distribution of atmospheric nu events and nu_e mixing", hep-ph/971142 to appear in PLB; They point out observable effects of small nu_e admixture (within Chooz limits) on top of nu_mu<--->nu_tau oscillations of atmospheric neutrinos. A peculiar effect is studied, which is present only for genuinely three-flavor mixing.
  • G.L.Fogli, E.Lisi, and D.Montanino, "Discriminating MSW solutions to the solar neutrino problem with flux-independent information at SuperKamiokande and SNO", hep-ph/9803309 They analyze flux-independent tests of MSW oscillations at SuperK & SNO and their interplay. Includes 374 day SuperK data.
  • A. Geisser, "Pseudo-Dirac neutrinos as a potential complete solution to the neutrino oscillation puzzle", CERN-EP/98-56. A small Majorana mass splits nus into two almost degenerate states with maximal nu-nubar mixing. Handles solar, atmo and LSND.
  • Faid et al., "Vacuum oscillations and variations of solar neutrino rates in SuperKamiokande and Borexino", hep-ph/9805293 They analyze flux-independent tests of vacuum oscillations at SuperK and Borexino, and their interplay. They also generalize the Fourier expansion of the signal to three-flavor oscillations.
  • E. Akhmedov, "Parametric resonance of neutrino oscillations and passage of solar and atmospheric neutrinos through the earth", hep-ph/9805272
  • Y. Fukuda et al., "Study of the atmospheric neutrino flux in the Multi-GeV energy range", (SuperK), hep-ex/9805006
  • Argyris Stassinakis "A Study of the Atmospheric Neutrino Flavour Content Using the Soudan 2 Detector", Ph.D. thesis http://webnt.physics.ox.ac.uk/Neutrino/stassina.htm
