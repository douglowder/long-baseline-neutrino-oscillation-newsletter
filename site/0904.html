Long-Baseline news, April 2008

*** T2K beam commissioning, First neutrinos

The first neutrinos have been produced for the T2K "Tokai to Kamioka" long-baseline neutrino experiment from the new JPARC accelerator. It is using a new beamline to point at the Super-K detector to search for theta-13. See the 1st scope (23 April) trace: http://www.neutrinooscillation.org/t2kscop.png from the muon monitors. The blue curve is the silicon signal and the red curve is a scintillation counter installed for commissioning. The beam commissioning team is proceeding to establish the optimum orbit and beam manipulation method.

*** First Neutrinos in MINERvA

The first pieces of the fine-grained MINERvA experiment in the Fermilab NuMI beam have detected their first neutrinos. The first portion of the detector consists of 24 modules each 3.5 cm thick that sit next to each other like slices of bread. When the detector is fully assembled in 2010, it will have 108 modules with 30,000 channels. http://minerva.fnal.gov/news/MINERvA-Sees/minerva-sees.html

*** NOvA groundbreaking, First Dirt (Neutrinos already there)

Ground was broken on May 1st for the new long-baseline NOvA experiment (Neutrino Oscillation neutrino Appearance) near Ash River Minnesota, which will place a new off-axis experiment in the NuMI beam to search for theta-13. Actually, the ground was pretty hard, so the dirt was brought in. The American Recovery and Reinvestment Act is providing $40.1 million. FNAL has committed an additional $9.9 million in stimulus funding to purchasing key high-tech components for the project from U.S. companies, allowing those firms to retain and hire workers. 180 scientists and engineers from 28 institutions in 7 countries are building the 15,000-ton detector.

*** MiniBooNE running extended

They wanted more antineutrino data, and the March recommendation by the Fermilab PAC is to get it. "The Committee recommends continued running of MiniBooNE for up to an additional 5e20 POT, provided no significant expense is incurred by Fermilab for this extra running. The Collaboration has requested to use the additional POT solely for antineutrino running. Given the low- energy excess in neutrino mode, the Committee recommends the Collaboration should address the optimal use of the additional POT."

*** Neutrino Experiments to Share computing resources?

A workshop was held at Fermilab to bring together present and future neutrino experiments to share information on infrastructure and offline computing. Several issues were discussed from various hardware needs to possible sharing a CVS repository for code. http://nova-docdb.fnal.gov/cgi-bin/ShowDocument?docid=3739 Another workshop is scheduled for March 2010.
