Long-Baseline news, May 2021

*** FNAL's MicroBooNE finishes beam run

The MicroBooNE experiment at Fermilab has concluded its beam run and is analyzing its data to better understand MiniBooNE's low-E excess in the Booster beam and if it's due to e's or gammas. The detector is still operating and collecting specialized data. R&D includes collecting scintillation light in new ways, making grounding measurements, & raising the drift HV. Some studies are due to requests from SBN & DUNE. No additional nu data will be proposed, unless repeat measurements seem needed after unblinding.

*** Neutrino memory

General Relativity predicts that the passage of matter/radiation from an asymmetrically-emitting source should cause a permanent change in the local space-time metric. This phenomenon, called the "gravitational memory effect", hasn't been observed, but supernova nus are a promising avenue for its future detection. New gravity wave detectors (DECIGO & BBO) will be sensitive to neutrino memory for supernovae at typical distances in our galaxy and beyond. See M. Mukhopadhyay et al. in arXiv:2105.05862.

*** Interesting Letters of Interest; v9 - https://snowmass21.org/

  • Paley-173 "The EMPHATIC Table-Top Spectrometer: Enabling Hadron Scattering & Production Measurements for Improved Beam Simulation" An upgraded EMPHATIC device, in FNAL's test beam, could measure hadron yields and reduce uncertainties in the calculation of nu fluxes by a factor of 2. It will combine thin target measurements with those using a replica NuMI target & spare NuMI focusing horn.
  • Hedges-153 "Measuring Inelastic CC and NC Antineutrino-Nucleus Interactions with Reactor Neutrinos" The high flux of reactor antinus makes cross section measurements attractive, but there needs to be improvements in detector technology, understanding of nuclear matrix elements, and computational power.
  • Rosner-045 "THREE STERILE NEUTRINOS IN E6" E6 could be a satisfactory framework for including sterile nus.
  • Arguelles-155 "Neutrinos as Probes for Lorentz and CPT Symmetry" Upcoming neutrino experiments provide new sensitivity to search for both Lorentz and CPT invariance.
  • Wagman-177 "Connecting QCD to neutrino-nucleus scattering" A coherent set of theoretical frameworks is required to describe neutrino interactions on nuclei with the level of accuracy needed for the success of DUNE & other precision oscillation experiments.
