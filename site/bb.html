Baseball companion to Long-Baseline news, September 2015

The September 2015 Long-baseline neutrino newsletter, the "Back to the Future" Cubs world series issue, has numerous references to the game of baseball and to the movie "Back to the Future". Here is an explanation of some of those references

  • Back to the Future

    This was a set of 3 movies that came out in 1985. In the second movie, the hero, Marty McFly was in the future year at the time, 2015, and, heard that the Cubs had just won the world series. The last time the Cubs won was in 1907, so it has been a long time, and the Cubs have also not won in the 30 years since the movie came out. This motivated Marty to buy a sports results book, which Biff stole and took back to 1955 and changed history, since the young Biff could bet on winning sports teams.
    As I write this (1 October 2015), the Cubs are one of ten teams that have made it to the playoffs, so they might not win the 2015 World Series, but they did in the movie, so my working assumption is that they will.

  • Baselines

    These are lines which run from home plate to first base and third base. the lines also continue to the right and left field walls where there are "foul poles". A ball hit between these two lines is fair and in play, while a ball hit outside the line is a foul and everyone returns to base, unless the ball is caught in the air (an out).

  • Sox

    The 30 professional baseball teams have names such as Yankees, Dodgers, Cubs, etc. One of the teams is known as the Red Sox, and one of the teams in known as the White Sox. There was a scandal involving the 1919 White Sox and this is known as the Black Sox.

  • Hitting for the cycle

    This is when a player gets 4 particular hits, a single, a double, a triple and a home run in the same game. It is rare.

  • Big Papi

    This is the nickname of a player from the Boston Red Sox

  • Grand Slam

    This is a home run with other runners on all three bases. It scores 4 runs and is the best you can do in one play.

  • Green Monster

    This is the left field wall at Boston's Fenway Park, and is rather unique part of a baseball park.

  • World Series

    This is an annual contest between the best team in each of the two baseball leagues in the United States. Although there is one team from Canada, it is called the World Series because the first one was sponsored by the New York World Newspaper. But people like the rather prententious name so it has been kept.

  • Rookie

    This is a player in his/her first year on a major league baseball team. Years spent in the minor leagues do not count.

  • Pitcher

    The player who starts a play by throwing the ball to the batter

  • Left Fielder

    One of nine fielding positions

  • Umpire

    The individual who rules on plays, such as balls, strikes and outs.

  • Harry M. Stevens

    Designer of the first scorecard and possible inventor of the hot dog.

  • Scorecard

    A sheet of paper on which you can keep track of everything important that happens in a baseball game. There is usually a list of players and their numbers. They wear the numbers on their shirts which and can be identified that way.

  • Yogi Berra

    A famous baseball player who died last month (September 2015) and was known for his famous quotes, such as, "Nobody goes there anymore, it's too crowded" and "It's deja vu all over again" and "It's not over till it's over." Here are some more Yogi Berra quotes

  • hits it out of the park

    If you hit the ball over the fence, it is a home run, and you and anybody on base scores a run. The goal in baseball is to score more runs than the other team.

  • An asterisk in the record book

    This was a myth, but involved what many thought was the most important record in baseball, the yearly home run total. Babe Ruth held the record for many years with 60, but in 1961, the length of the baseball season increased from 154 games to 162 games. Roger Maris hit 61 homers, but after the 154th game, so some people claimed he didn't really break Babe Ruth's record, and that there should be an "asterisk" in the record book.
