Long-Baseline news, March 2006

posted not long before 01:02:03 04/05/06 (UTC-GMT/Zulu)

*** Delta msquared from MINOS

University of Minnesota physicist David Petyt presented first MINOS results from the NuMI beam at Fermilab last week. MINOS sees a shift in the energy distribution between its near and far detectors. The best fit to delta-msquared_32 is 3.05 +0.60 -0.55 (stat) +-0.12 (syst) 10^-3 eV^2 and sin^2 2 theta = 0.88 +.12 -.15 (stat) +- 0.06 (syst). This is for 0.93 10^20 pot out of 1.4 to date. http://www-numi.fnal.gov/talks/results06.html

*** Delta msquared for maximal mixing in MINOS

If a fit is done constraining the angle to maximal mixing, the result is dmsq_32 = 2.70 +0.263 -0.305 10^-3 eV^2.

*** Doug Michael Memorial Grove

5 trees were planted 31-Mar in honor of Doug Michael at the NuMI service building. Among many other accomplishments, Doug had worked successfully to assure that early MINOS running would have close to the promised proton intensity from the Main Injector.

*** New K2K result on theta-13

sin^2 theta_mu-e < 0.13 at 90% CL. The theta-13 limit is 0.26. hep-ex/0603004.

*** Adding more neutrinos to the roadmap

The P5 committee in the U.S. is meeting to add more ambitious neutrino objectives to the High Energy Physics Roadmap for the next 10 years. Their charge mentions, "while all routes on the roadmap should be interesting, not all will be taken. [Your] prioritization should therefore recommend ... the relative priority and time ordering of projects." A meeting at Fermilab on April 18-19 will consider neutrino and dark matter proposals. http://www.fnal.gov/directorate/program_planning/P5/P5_Apr2006/Meeting.html

*** NOvA mass

The DOE has expressed a desire to keep the total cost for NOvA (EvA) around/under $200M. NOvA has re-optimized its baseline mass to 25 kilotons from 30 kilotons for $225M. A detailed costing "Lehman Review" is taking place."

*** Tritium at Fermilab

NuMI water is safe enough to drink, but there are measurable levels of tritium in it. On March 20, Fermilab received a Notice of Violation (NOV) from the Illinois Environmental Protection Agency (IEPA). Fermilab is maintaining a web site with information about this at http://www.fnal.gov/pub/about/community/IndianCreek.html

*** Tritium at Braidwood

The situation with some water spills at the nuclear power plant in Braidwood has had extensive coverage in the Chicago area press for several weeks. There is no connection between this and the possible neutrino experiment to measure theta13 at Braidwood. Exelon's web site about this is at http://www.braidwoodtritium.info/
