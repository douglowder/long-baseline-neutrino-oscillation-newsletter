Long-Baseline news, December 2002

*** KamLAND; 86.8+-5.6 expected (no oscillations), 54 events seen

This confirms the LMA (large mixing angle) solution for solar neutrinos. See hep-ex/0212021 for "First Results from KamLAND: Evidence for Reactor Anti-Neutrino Disappearance"

*** K2K; 80.1 +6.2-5.4 expected (no oscillations), 56 events seen

Long-Baseline accelerator experiments were proposed to look for neutrino oscillations. The first published oscillation analysis has appeared and will be in PRL. It is "Indications of Neutrino Oscillation in a 250 km Long-baseline Experiment", hep-ex/0212007

*** Neutrino Phases for Pedestrians

Harry Lipkin hep-ph/0212093 reminds us that Stodolsky's theorem simply and clearly resolves the issue of neutrino phases. Neutrino oscillations are experimentally observable only as a result of interference between neutrino states with different masses and THE SAME ENERGY. All interference effects between neutrino states having different energies are destroyed by the interaction between the incident neutrino and the detector.

*** George Marx; started International Neutrino Conferences

Prof. George Marx, a leading figure in Hungarian astro-particle physics died at the age of 76 on 2 December 2002 in Budapest. Beyond his personal contribution to the discovery of lepton number conservation in 1953 and the derivation of an astrophysical upper bound to neutrino mass in 1972, Marx played a key role in starting the series of Neutrino Conferences (the 1st was at Lake Balaton).

*** Answer to (Boris Kayser's) homework

See section 2 of hep-ph/0211134 . The essence of the puzzle is not the Majorana character of the neutrinos, but the chirally- handed character of the charged weak currents. Here is a DIRAC version: Suppose a DIRAC numu interacts with some target in whose rest frame (t) it has - helicity, and makes a mu. Now run faster than the nu. In your frame (s), the nu has + helicity. One frequently hears Dirac nus with +("right handed") helicity do not interact. If that were really true in all frames, then in frame s nothing would happen. Answer: The amplitude for the nu interaction is indeed Lorentz invariant, so if that interaction yields a mu as seen in frame t, it yields one in s too. The Lorentz-invariant amplitude is a dot product of 2 4-vector currents: a leptonic current, and a target/recoil current. In frame s, the leptonic current is highly suppressed relative to its size in frame t, as naively expected when a nu has + helicity. However, in frame s the target/recoil current is correspondingly enhanced, to just such a degree that the dot product of the 2 currents is the same in frame s as in frame t.

*** Plato's fire

The non-Abelian discrete symmetry A4 is also the symmetry group of the regular tetrahedron, one of five perfect geometric solids known to Plato who associated it with fire. In hep-ph/0211393, Ma discusses implications for the neutrino mass matrix.
