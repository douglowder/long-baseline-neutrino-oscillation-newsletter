Long-Baseline news, October 2004

*** Shaking up the Neutrino World

The APS Neutrino Study was presented to the funding agencies and was well received. Nearly final drafts are available on the working group pages, and the final draft will soon be posted at http://www.interactions.org/cms/?pid=1009695. Recommendations: 1. as a high priority, that a phased program of neutrinoless nuclear double beta decay be initiated as soon as possible. 2. as a high priority, a comprehensive U.S. program to complete our understanding of neutrino mixing, to determine the character of the neutrino mass spectrum, and to search for CP violation among neutrinos. This program should have the following components: a) an expeditiously deployed multi-detector reactor experiment with sensitivity down to sin^2 2q = 0.01. b) a timely accelerator experiment with comparable sensitivity and sensitivity to the mass hierarchy through matter effects. c) a proton driver in the megawatt class or above and neutrino superbeam with an appropriate very large detector capable of observing CP violation and measuring the neutrino mass-squared differences and mixing parameters with high precision. 3) the development of a spectroscopic solar neutrino experiment capable of measuring the energy spectrum of neutrinos from the primary pp fusion process in the sun.

*** Shaking up a few (reactor) neutrino physicists

There was a recent magnitude 6.8 quake in Japan. The epicenter of the quake was only 30 km from Kashiwazaki-Kariwa nuclear power station. The quake caused derailment of the 200 km/hr Shinkansen train which took 2km to completely stop after the safety system operated. Very fortunately (it can be said it is a miracle) no one was injured. Around the epicenter, more than 100,000 people were evacuated. The KASKA group was in charge of the boring study in the power station and had just finished digging the 70m depth 6.6 cm diameter hole 1 day before the quake. 3 KASKA collaborators stayed in the area to prepare the cosmic-ray and gamma ray detectors to put in the hole. At the time of the first quake, they were in a hotel near the power station and felt the strong quake. A power outage took place. They were evacuated and stayed outside until midnight being afraid of the aftershocks. (many were magnitude 5-6). KASKA was concerned that the boring hole might be collapsed by the quake. But it survived! They put in the detectors feeling some aftershocks, and measured background data.

*** Braidwood R&D Proposal for Reactor Neutrinos

The Braidwood collaboration includes 68 people from 13 institutions. They have submitted a 1 year site-specific R&D proposal for $1M and plan to submit a full proposal in one year. The cost estimate is $66M. It's led by E. Blucher & M. Shaevitz.

*** CHOOZ-US proposal for Double-CHOOZ Reactor Neutrinos

A group of 20 physicists from 7 U.S. Institutions has proposed a $5M 3-year program to join Double-CHOOZ to measure theta-13. If fully approved in 05, far detector would start 07. It's led by R. Svoboda & M. Goodman. hep-ex/0410081

*** Brookhaven Proton Driver Proposal

Brookhaven has sent a letter to the DOE as a preliminary proposal to construct a Super Neutrino Beam at BNL,using the upgraded AGS as a 1 MW proton driver. This follows the design for a Very Long-Baseline Neutrino Oscillations Experiment. The Total Estimated Cost (TEC) is $407M. A conceptual design is available: http://www.bnl.gov/henp/docs/bnl73210-04.pdf

*** Fermilab Proton Driver Workshop

The Proton Driver workshop was an initial workshop, and should be regarded as the start of a process. Though some of the discussions were around well-known issues (e.g. neutrino physics), it included how proton driver physics will fit into the global HEP program. Some new ideas included a low energy muon program for both fundamental and applied research, and the problem of achieving the long spill beams needed for kaon physics. Currently, achieving regular slow spills from the Main Injector while controlling activation remains an issue. One idea is to use the Tevatron instead. The present 9 month effort will result in a report in late 04 and workshops are expected to continue.
