Long-Baseline news, June 2008

*** Latest SNO results improve precision on theta-12

In the 3rd and final phase of the Canadian Sudbury Neutrino Observatory (SNO), 36 strings of 3He proportional counters were used to measure neutrons from neutrino neutral current interactions in the D20. When the box was opened just before neutrino 2008, a signal of 983+-77 neutral current events was observed, over a background of 185+-25. They fit their data together with KamLAND to obtain theta_12=33.8+1.4-1.3 degrees, an improved precision of 40%. see arXiv:0806.0989

*** Early stages of T2K beam in Japan

T2K will use a 3 GeV injector to a 50 GeV main ring. In May, the 3 GeV beam was injected into the main ring for the first time. At the end of Feb 08, the beam power per pulse reached 5 kW/pulse. Since the 3 GeV ring will operate at 25 Hz (25 pulses per second), this power/pulse is equivalent to 130 kW per second if 25 pulses were accelerated.

*** NOvA funded in supplemental US budget bill

A supplemental funding bill for the Iraq war included funding for science which allowed Fermilab to cancel a round of planned involuntary layoffs. There was also $9.5M for the neutrino experiment NOvA. NOvA also got mentioned in the 2009 budget markup. Everyone expects a continuing resolution starting October 2008, but the markup might become the basis of a real budget in 3/09.

*** Non-zero theta13?

A global analysis including recent results from Neutrino 2008 finds two converging hints for theta_13 > 0, an older one coming from atmospheric neutrino data and a newer one from the combination of solar and long-baseline reactor data. sin^2(2 theta_13) = 0.064+-0.040 See Fogli et al arXiv:0806.2649 (While many think a 1.6 sigma effect should be ignored, remember a 90% CL upper limit is the absence of a 1.29 sigma effect.)

*** New vision for US HEP in P5 report

The vision for the future of High Energy Physics in the United States includes a large detector in the proposed Deep Underground Science and Engineering Lab (DUSEL) and a high intensity neutrino source at Fermilab. A proto-collaboration to work on the long-baseline neutrino experiment is forming. They met in June at Fermilab, again 14 Aug, and before UDIG (next) at BNL.

*** DUSEL UDIG, October 16-17, at Brookhaven

A Workshop on Underground Detectors Investigating Grand Unification (UDIG) will consider topics in Nucleon Decay, Supernovae and Oscillations. An LB-DUSEL Collaboration Meeting will be held 15 Oct as a prelude to the workshop. http://www.bnl.gov/udig

*** Lewis and Clark

The DUSEL long-baseline detector might be water or argon, for which the Dakota-appropriate acronyms LEWIS and CLARK could work: Large Experiment Water cerenkov for Interdisciplinary Science (LEWIS) and Cryogenic Liquid ARgon 100 Kiloton scale (CLARK).

*** MicroBooNE approved at Fermilab

MicroBooNE, a proposal for a liquid Argon Detector in Fermilab's booster beam has received stage 1 approval; recommended by the FNAL PAC and approved by the director. It also received an NSF MRI for the TPC and PMT array for the detector. The experiment will address the low energy excess observed by the MiniBooNE experiment, measure low energy neutrino cross sections, and serve as the necessary next step in a phased Ar detector program.

*** Let's be careful with the language of science

The 2nd edition of the 20 volume Oxford English Dictionary defines "neutrino" as "Either of two stable, uncharged leptons, (associated respectively with the electron and muon) which have zero or negligible mass and an extremely low probability of interacting with matter". I showed this to a joint English/ Physics major who said "That is wrong in so many ways." http://dictionary.oed.com/
