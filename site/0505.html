Long-Baseline news, May 2005

*** Neutrino Assessment

The Neutrino Scientific Assessment Group met in the DC area and considered 5 neutrinoless double beta decay experiments, 3 reactor neutrino experiments, and 2 accelerator programs. The next meeting is July 17. A first report is now expected in late July, with an opinion on 0nu betabeta experiments first. Members: http://www.science.doe.gov/hep/Subpanel%20List.shtm

*** Should the Tevatron turn off to run NOvA sooner?

The P5 advisory committee for DOE-HEP has a charge to study the remaining programs at Fermilab's Tevatron and SLAC's B factory, which are scheduled to cease operations in 2009. An earlier start to a Fermilab off-axis experiment was one example of what might be done if something turned off any earlier during a presentation by Robin Staffin at Fermilab.

*** Deep Neutrinos

There were 8 proposals for the DUSEL lab, and some winnowing is going on, with depth being one of the issues. So far, Mark Felt denies that he is the source of any neutrinos.

*** MINERvA project

MINERvA is now a project at FNAL with Debbie Harris as Project Manager and Nancy Grossman and Ron Ransome (Rutgers) as Deputies.

*** Cuorcino result

The Cuorcino experiment has set a new limit on the neutrinoless betabeta decay of 130Te of 1.8 10^24 years. With uncertainties from matrix elements, this implies an upper bound on the effective neutrino mass .2-1.1 eV. This is comparable to the sensitivity of Ge experiments. Reminder a claim for a signal at 0.4 eV exists. hep-ex/0505045 & hep-ex/0501034

*** Cuore search for neutrino mass

This is a proposal for a 0nu betabeta experiment searching for 130Te to 130Xe using .78 ton of TeO2. It will be bolometric, using the source as the absorber, and run at 10 milliKelvin. It has been approved by the LNGS policy committee, and US interest includes Livermore, LBL, Berkeley and South Caroline.

*** EXO search for neutrino mass

EXO is a fully funded 200 kg enriched Xenon experiment to search for the neutrinoless double beta decay of Xe136. There is also a research program to study the possibility of Barium tagging, which would make this a virtually background free search. It will operate at WIPP. A one ton detector in 5 years could be sensitive to 50-68 meV and a 10 tons to 11-15 meV. US groups include SLAC, Stanford, Alabama, Colorado State, UC Irvine and Cal Tech.

*** Majorana search for Neutrino Mass

The proposal is for 180 kg of Ge76 and to keep backgrounds at or below 1 event per ton-year in the energy region of interest. US groups include Brown, LBL, LLNL, LANL, New Mexico State, Oak Ridge, Pacific Northwest, TUNL, Chicago, South Carolina, Tennessee and Washington. The planned Major procurement total is $21.7M with a $38.7M Total estimated cost. Majorana has signed a cooperative agreement with GERDA to share expertise.

*** MOON search for neutrino mass

4 experiments can be done using 100Mo, a search for solar nus and 0nu betabeta decay. This group, which includes Washington, is asking for no money and a proposal would be prepared around 2007.

*** NeMO 3 and SuperNEMO search for neutrino mass

NeMO 3 has been running since February 2003 at Frejus. They have 7 sources in foils imbedded in wire chambers, Two of them Mo and Se are 0nu betabeta searches, with limits set so far at 4.6 10^23 years and 1.0 10^23 years corresponding to neutrino mass limits .66-2.81 eV and 1.75-4.86 eV. SuperNEMO would use 100 kg of Se. Mount Holyoke, Texas and Idaho Falls are interested US groups.
