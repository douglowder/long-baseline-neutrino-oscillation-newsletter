Long-Baseline news, October 2009

*** SNO low energy solar neutrino data

SNO is over but is still presenting interesting new results. in arXiv:0910.2984 they have lowered the kinetic energy threshold to 3.5 MeV and measure the total flux of active-flavor solar neutrinos from Boron decay with uncertainty more than a factor of 2 smaller than before. theta_12=34.06 +1.16 -0.84; dmsq_21 = 7.59 +0.20-0.21e-5 eV^2. As others before, they do a global fit and get sin^2(theta_13) = 2.00 +2.09 -1.63 e-2. In Figure 29, the LMA prediction is for an upturn at low E. The data is noticeably low instead, but errors are large.

*** NOvA gets final construction approval; CD-3b,

On October 30th, the DOE gave final approval for CD3b; which allows for a start of full construction on the NOvA project. This required a tremendous amount of work on the part of the project team -- John Cooper and Paul Derwent in particular.

*** MicroBooNE receives CD-0

The MicroBooNE experiment at Fermilab will use a time-projection chamber filled with 100 tons of liquid argon to look carefully at low-energy neutrino interactions. CD-0 is a designation from the Department of Energy which establishes "mission need", i.e. that we want to know more about low energy neutrinos. A final cost (less than $20M) will be developed to ask for CD-1.

*** LBNE, The best you can get for $900M (CD-0 soon)

LBNE, the (about to be renamed) experiment for a new long-baseline beam/detector from Fermilab to DUSEL, has yet to get CD-0. (With a hyphen in the name, it is hard to go negative.) But there is hope for CD-0 by the end of the year. Recent experience is that projects over $1B receive undesirable added scrutiny by those above the funding agencies, so the new beam/detector combination may end up with a price cap near $900M.

*** SciNOvA - new idea with an Expression of Interest at FNAL

Not even CD-minus-two (perhaps). This is an idea to re-instrument the SciBar detector (15 tons of solid scintillator, segmented in 1.3 cm x 2.5 cm strips used recently by the SciBooNE experiment) and deploy the detector in front of the NOvA near detector. It will be presented to the Fermilab PAC in November by Messier & Tayloe.

*** CERN Workshop on Neutrino Strategy summary

The European Strategy for Future Neutrino Physics program is kept at http://indico.cern.ch/conferenceDisplay.py?confId=59378. It was only a beginning of a process. Neutrino physicists in Europe have a program of R&D projects (SPL, MERIT, MICE, EMMA) Design Studies (EUROnu for beams, LAGUNA for underground sites) and networking (NEu2012) which aims at providing inputs for decisions to come. The SPC neutrino panel is preparing its report for December, and CERN management will hold a "retreat session" end of November to review the upgrade of the LHC injectors. The European accelerator neutrino community will continue its interaction with management to narrow down options only to realistic beam and detector configurations. The option to revive the old PS Gargammelle neutrino beam, devoting it to accelerator/detector R&D, is also being discussed.

*** MICE beam and experiment gets money & "Transnational Access"

The Science and Technology Facilities Council in the UK has awarded 222K Euro for Transnational Access to the MICE (ionization cooling) infrastructure. This aims at supporting experimentalists from EU to investigate muon ionization cooling or to perform neutrino detector tests with high quality low energy beams of muons, electrons, protons or pions. For information or to apply, see http://eucard.web.cern.ch/EuCARD/activities/access/

*** Are there Neutrino Acoustic Oscillations of cosmic neutrinos?

Some big bang neutrinos may be non-relativistic. So the point of last scattering is closer for the heaviest cosmic neutrinos than for the lighter ones, or for light. arXiv:0907.2887 and http://focus.aps.org/story/v24/st15 Even if one could measure cosmic neutrinos, it appears we can't detect Neutrino Acoustic Oscillations.
