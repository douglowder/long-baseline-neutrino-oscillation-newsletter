Long-Baseline news, October 2019

*** T2K indication of CP violation in the lepton sector

A value for delta_CP other than 0 or pi would enhance numu to nue oscillations for neutrinos in the normal mass order and suppress antinu oscillations. In arXiv:1910.03887 , T2K favors a large enhancement of the neutrino oscillation probability. The 3-sigma CL (on a -2pi to 0 scale) is -3.41 to -0.03. Likelihood surface data will be available at http://t2k-experiment.org/

*** Neutrinos in European science book

The European briefing book, arXiv:1910.11775 was prepared by the European Strategy for Particle Physics Preparatory Group as part of the process to formulate the European Strategy Update for the consideration & approval of the CERN Council. Chap. 6 covers nus. It discusses the current long-baseline experiments NOvA and T2K in the context of the future DUNE & Hyper-Kamiokande projects. JUNO, IceCube & ORCA are mentioned, as well as the ENUBET and nuSTORM proposals. Cosmology, Neutrinoless double beta decay, direct mass experiments and searches for new states are also covered. It concludes, "A balanced support to this world-wide effort from Europe will allow to secure the determination of the oscillation parameters, aim at the discovery of CP violation and test for possible deviations from the 3-nu framework."

*** What if KATRIN had measured 1.1 eV?

Recently ( arXiv:1909.06069 ) the KATRIN experiment reported a limit on the effective nu mass in tritium beta decay of 1.1 eV. What if they had measured that value? In the 3-nu paradigm, & with the 2 known values of delta msquared, it would mean that all 3 masses do not differ from 1.1 eV by more than 0.1%. The uncertainty from experiment is much larger. The tritium spectrum is an incoherent sum of 3 spectra with eigenmasses & are fit simultaneously. The eigenmasses are close, so the fit is the same for 1 mass as all 3.

*** Is the normal mass order just what we need?

It seems to make the 0nu-2beta experiments, and hence determining the Majorana nature of neutrinos harder. But S. Ge & J. Zhu ( arXiv:1910.02666 ) argue it opens the opportunity to determine the solar octant and measure two Majorana CP phases.

*** Neutrino conversations

There are not many spouses at neutrino conferences. Lucky for me, mine has been an exception. Once I accompanied her to a meeting of the American Library Association in North Carolina and attended lunch as a spouse. "What do you do?" I was asked by a seat mate. "I'm a neutrino physicist." I answered. "That's a conversation stopper" was the reply. But it turned out that the next librarian at the table was from the city in South Carolina where neutrinos were discovered, she knew it, and a long conversation followed.
