*** Earthquake near Gran Sasso

A serious earthquake in Italy damaged parts of L'Aquila which is close to the Gran Sasso lab. We wish the best for our neutrino friends there. http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/in_pictures/7984969.stm

*** Neutrino 2008; Archiving old Meeting web sites

Meetings now post transparencies on their website and this has made printed conference proceedings functionally obsolete. (Some editors like to have them anyway.) However, not all websites get maintained. Many older meetings at the neutrino meetings page give bad links. Spires will archive meetings, and Neutrino 2008 has done so: http://www.slac.stanford.edu/econf/C0805263/. This link should be good for many many years.

*** Neutrino Interactions Meeting 18-22 May in Barcelona

Nuint09 is the 6th in a series of workshops concentrating on low energy neutrino interactions. It will be in Spain this Spring. http://nuint09.ifae.es/Welcome.html

*** WIN 2009 Meeting to be at Gran Sasso in September in L'Aquila

The 22nd International Workshop on Weak Interactions and Neutrinos (which alternates with Neutrino Conferences) will be held in L'Aquila Italy from 14-19 Sept. 2009. http://win09.lngs.infn.it/

*** Neutrino Summer School Meets in July 6-17 at Fermilab

The Summer School aims to cover the full breadth of neutrino physics and will present an opportunity for participants to concentrate on the many aspects of the field that our community will be addressing over the next few decades. They encourage young researchers, either currently engaged in neutrino physics, or those considering it to attend the School. The school is aimed at advanced graduate students and recent post-docs. http://projects.fnal.gov/nuss/announcement.html

*** Neutrino Factory Workshop Meets in Chicago in July 20-25.

Just after the summer school is the 11th in a series of workshops to study future accelerator-based neutrino facilities. It will be at the Illinois Institute of Technology in Chicago (where less is more), except the first day will be at Fermilab. Early registration fee till 1 May at http://nufact09.iit.edu/

*** NNN09: Meeting on Nucleon Decay/Neutrinos October in Colorado

NNN09 will be the tenth of a series of workshops to discuss experiments that go beyond the reach of current projects, as well as the theory work that motivates and describes such advances. New detectors will explore proton decay lifetimes over 10^35 y, and advance our understanding of the neutrino sector, including mass hierarchy, additional oscillation parameters, CP violation, supernova dynamics, and high energy cosmic neutrino sources. http://nnn09.colostate.edu

*** Neutrino Meeting at Luxor (no not Las Vegas)

In my travels, I would rate the Valley of the Kings as the most interesting place I've ever been, despite the lack of neutrino physicists there. That will be rectified in November, when the CTP International Conference on Neutrino Physics in the LHC Era will be held in Luxor Egypt. http://ctp.bue.edu.eg/workshops/Neutrino/Neutrino_index.htm

*** Low Emittance Muon Collider Meeting June 8-12 at Fermilab

This 4th workshop focuses on the development of high-luminosity muon colliders using extreme muon beam cooling, where many constraints on muon collider designs are alleviated with beams of smaller emittance and lower intensity. The workshops covered topics related to proton drivers, cooling, cooling demonstration experiments, bunch recombination, muon acceleration, collider lattices, interaction-point design, site boundary radiation, and detector concepts for energy frontier and Higgs particle studies. The workshops are sponsored by Fermilab's Technical Division and Muons, Inc. http://www.muonsinc.com/lemc2009/

*** New Fermilab Lecture Series "Extreme Beam" Meetup

A new lecture series at Fermilab will explore myriad intensity opportunities. The intensity frontier offers a unique path for 21st-century particle physics. Lectures will address topics including neutrino physics, muon physics, hadron physics and innovative accelerator and detector technologies for the intensity frontier. http://www.fnal.gov/ExtremeBeam/

*** Meeting on Supernova Physics and DUSEL 16-17 Sep at UCLA

If you want to see a Supernova underground, you should probably use neutrinos. This workshop will consider supernova dynamics, neutrino properties and supernova, the detector of nus at DUSEL, and the DUSEL lab and supernova detector design/strategies. http://www.physics.ucla.edu/hep/supernova/

*** Neutrino Champagne Meeting in Reims France 19-21 October

This 5th International Workshop on Low Energy and Solar Neutrinos has the best URL name. http://neutrino-champagne.in2p3.fr/
