Long-Baseline news, May 2018

*** New results being presented at Neutrino 2018 in Heidelberg

Follow as they appear: https://www.mpi-hd.mpg.de/nu2018/programme See the June newsletter for a summary. Find some neutrino PR at https://www.symmetrymagazine.org/article/game-changing-neutrino-experiments

*** new (first neutrino beam physics) result from MicroBooNE

MicroBooNE will someday chime in whether the low energy excess is due to electrons, photons or both. In the meantime they have compared the multiplicity distributions observed to GENIE model predictions in arXiv:1805.06887, submitted to PRD. They used 5e19 800 MeV protons on target (pot). Kinematic distributions agree well with GENIE, but there is an indication of fewer high (>3) multiplicity events in the data than in GENIE.

*** New results from MiniBooNE, large excess of e-like events

MiniBooNE had previously presented results from 6.l4e20 (11.27e20) pot with nu(antinu). Now with 12.84e20 pot the excess of e-like events is 381.2 +- 85.2 (4.5 sigma) E_nu between 200 & 1250 MeV. Combining LSND/MiniBooNE the excess is 6.1 sigma. The excess above 475 MeV (the original blinded cut) is low. arXiv:1805.12028

*** New and Final results from OPERA long-baseline experiment

https://home.cern/about/updates/2018/05/opera-presents-its-final-results-neutrino-oscillations They now have ten tau-nu candidates appearing in the LNGS beam.

*** Call for proposals for projects at the CERN nu Platform

The CERN Neutrino Platform supports generic detector R&D for large nu detectors. There may be opportunities for new projects after the 2018 long shutdown. https://cds.cern.ch/record/2314101?ln=en

*** HEPAP approves DOE-HEP portfolio review -- Nus shine

The DOE reviewed 13 non-LHC running experiments in its "portfolio review". The highest priority was given to nu experiments NOvA & T2K and also DES and eBOSS. https://science.energy.gov/~/media/hep/hepap/pdf/201804/PGrannis_HEPAP_201805.pdf

*** DOE funding

While the president had proposed a 14% cut for the Office of Science (which funds HEP in the US), the house (senate) bill currently proposes a 5% (6%) increase. A proposed amendment from a former neutrino physicist would add $50m. See simplicity: https://amendments-rules.house.gov/amendments/FOSTER_103_xml_Office%20of%20Science%20Amend530181032203220.pdf

*** Hyper-Kamiokande Design Report

See arXiv:1805.04163 (333 pages). The Hyper-Kamiokande detector will be hosted in the Tochibora mine, 295 km away from the J-PARC accelerator research complex in Tokai, Japan. J-PARC is being steadily upgraded to reach a MW beam by the start of Hyper-K.

*** NuFACT registration open, August in Virginia

Registration is open for the 20th International Workshop on Neutrinos from Accelerators (known as NuFACT due to previous names for the workshop.) It will be 12-18 August in Blacksburg Virginia. https://nufact2018.phys.vt.edu/ If you are not familiar with Virginia, don't assume that it is near Washington DC, because it is not. Fly to Roanoke.

*** Neutrino History registration open, September in Paris

Registration is now open for the International Conference on the "History of the Neutrino" (5-7 September 2018) in Paris http://neutrinohistory2018.in2p3.fr/. If you are not familiar with Paris, you should be.

*** Monoenergetic muon neutrinos measured by MiniBooNE

They come from Kaon decay-at-rest. arXiv:1801.03848 and see https://physics.aps.org/articles/v11/35

*** neutrinooscillation.org expires

As part of the neutrino oscillation industry website, http://www.hep.anl.gov/ndk/hypertext/, for which the job sites, meetings, newsletter, and neutrino poetry are still maintained, the URL neutrinooscillation.org had been used for many years. Subdomains, such as minos.neutrinooscillation.org had been defined for most experiments. But it never became widely useful or used. I have let the domain expire, and at some point is should become available again for another user.
