Long-Baseline news, September 2011

*** 2012 RPP contains late neutrino data

The Review of Particle Physics, which many of us recently received, contains 1526 pages of almost everything you want to know about particle physics. It doesn't have the Higgs mass right yet, but three post-deadline papers from Double Chooz, Daya Bay and RENO were included so that you can find the value of theta-13. Use pdglive: http://pdglive.lbl.gov/listings1.brl?quickin=Y

*** Daya Bay finished construction

This reactor neutrino disappearance experiment has now installed the last 2 of its eight antineutrino detectors (AD) watching the 6 reactors to measure theta_13. One AD was calibrated in 3 dimensions. This is part of a comprehensive calibration for the spectral shape analysis. The current measurement is sin^2(2theta-13) = 0.089 +-0.010(stat)+-0.005(syst) and the estimated precision is 5% after 3 years. Also 5% on Delta m^2_31.

*** Daya Bay II being planned for mass hierarchy, theta-12

A large reactor neutrino experiment with good energy resolution can be used to determine the mass hierarchy. The current default parameters for the Daya Bay II idea is 20kt, 3% Energy resolution, 36 GW and 58 km baseline. Oil and Water detectors are being considered, and a mix of 8 and 20 inch tubes gives good coverage & good timing. PMTs with microchannel plate are being developed. An overburden of 1350 mwe is being considered. A possible time schedule is for a proposal to the government in 2015 and construction 2016-2020. Also see arXiv:1208.6473 & arXiv:1209.2227

*** Should we measure theta-12 better?

We currently know sin^2(theta-12) to 5.4%. A goal of upcoming 0nu double beta decay experiments is to cover sensitivity to all of the inverted hierarchy. The 3-sigma uncertainty in theta-12 leads to an uncertainty in the lower lifetime of a factor of 2, which affects the sensitivity (or cost) of these experiments by 16.

*** TREND, Tianshan Radio Experiment for Neutrino Detection

This is a a Sino-French project for the radio-detection of Extensive Air Showers induced by high energy cosmic particles. It is high in the mountains in northwest China where there is a very clean radio environment above 20 MHz. TREND 50 (50 antennas) has been in stable operation since March 2011. In 104 live days, they have 33 EAS candidates with zenith less than 65 degrees.

*** NOvA first block raised into place at far detector

The NOvA long-baseline neutrino experiment in Ash River is being built with plastic extrusions. First several layers are stacked horizontally in a "block" and then the block is raised. See: http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gFpK00WJl90&feature=share&list=UUD5B6VoXv41fJ-IW8Wrhz9A

*** Viet nus 17-22 December

The Viet Nus meeting will be held in Qui Nhon in Vietnam, 17-22 Dec 2012 under the auspices of the 8th Rencontres du Vietnam. The main idea is to bring together experienced researchers with young adepts and mix ideas with actual work. The web site is http://www.hep.utexas.edu/VietNus2012/

*** LBNE passes review

The Fermilab Long-Baseline Neutrino Experiment had a successful September Director's Review. The closeout reported, "The review committee is confident that the LBNE Project will be ready for a successful DOE CD-1 review in one month. The recent reconfiguration of the project scope was well executed yielding a revised scope that satisfies the performance requirements recommended by the LBNE reconfiguration steering committee."

*** No LSND anomaly in a long-baseline experiment

If neutrinos oscillate in a short-baseline, they are still mixed in a long-baseline experiment. M. Antonello et al. (ICARUS Collaboration) report a search for a nu_e signal due to a LSND anomaly at 730 km. using 0-30 GeV neutrinos, and limit the numu2e oscillation probability to <5.4e-3 at 90%CL. arXiv:1209.0122
