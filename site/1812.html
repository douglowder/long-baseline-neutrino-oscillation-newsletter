Long-Baseline news, December 2018

*** No ILC in Japan

A committee of scientists in Japan recommended against building an ILC to the government there. When it was seriously discussed, I wondered whether or not there was any anti-correlation with Hyper-Kamiokande. I was told the funding sources would have been different. But both were more expensive (though different scales) than previous science projects. https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-018-07833-9 and https://mainichi.jp/english/articles/20181219/p2a/00m/0na/028000c

*** de Gouvea wins physics slam with neutrinos

A physics slam is a lecture during which physicists use 10 minutes to describe their work to the general public. At an FNAL slam in November, Andre de Gouvea used his 10 minutes to describe neutrino oscillations and why we care. At the end of the evening, the audience declared him to be the winner of the 2018 Physics Slam. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UY1QQr-PZOg&feature=youtu.be

*** A You Tube connection to neutrinos

The number of Youtube links to neutrino stories in this newsletter is increasing. An interesting business news article describes the career rise of the CEO of Youtube and one of Google's founders. It was founded in 1998, the same year that Super-K conclusively discovered neutrino oscillations. The CEO, Susan Wojcicki, is also the daughter of longtime MINOS spokesperson Stan Wojcicki. https://www.businessinsider.com/susan-wojcicki-youtube-ceo-bio-career-life-2018-12

*** PDG encoding of neutrino parameters

Neutrino physicist Alessandro Bettini has joined the Particle Data Group and is helping to update the neutrino sections in the Review of Particle Physics (RPP). One interesting issue is that experiments are quoting oscillation parameters for 2 mass orders (normal and inverted) & 2 octants for theta23 (upper and lower). Should they choose errors from the global or local best fit? Experiments are making one choice, some global fitters the other.

*** Conclusions from the European Neutrino Town meeting

The document "Future Opportunities in Accelerator-based Neutrino Physics" addresses i) the role of CERN and its neutrino platform, ii) the importance of neutrino cross-section experiments, and iii) the capability of fixed target experiments as well as colliders to search for manifestations of nu mass generation mechanisms. It makes 6 recommendations. arXiv:1812.06739

*** New GENIE Release for neutrino simulators

The GENIE collaboration has a new release of both Generator and Reweight. Reweight (v1.0.0) restores compatibility with the new series (v3*) of Generator releases AND supports all comprehensive model configurations & tunes currently available in the Generator. A Generator revision (3.0.2) to supports Reweight and includes minimal changes in physics models. https://github.com/GENIE-MC
