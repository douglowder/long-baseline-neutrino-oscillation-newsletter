Long-Baseline news, February 2012

*** Daya Bay compares first two detectors

Two near detectors at the Daya Bay reactor neutrino experiment have been operating since 23 Sep 2011. A comparison of two months worth of data, mostly from the two Daya Bay reactors, shows that the detectors are functionally identical, and that detector-related systematic uncertainties are well under control. arXiv:1202.6181

*** T2K long-baseline neutrino experiment run resumes in Japan

http://t2k-experiment.org/category/news/ The first T2K beam nu event since the Mar 2011 earthquake was seen in Super-K (T2K's far detector) on 26 Jan 2012. It has a muon which was made when the beam interacted with a nucleon in Oxygen.

*** The neutrino mass hierarchy; the next big thing

We've likely measured a non-zero theta-13. Our collider friends aren't positive if there is a standard model Higgs, but if there is, we know its mass. Whether the neutrino mass hierarchy is "normal" or "inverted" (i.e. if m3 > m1, m2 or if m3 < m1, m2) is the next neutrino property we might know. Sensitivity to the hierarchy can be had in charge sensitive atmospheric nu angular distributions, matter effect differences in electron nu and antinu appearance measurements, supernova energy spectra in some models, comparison of delta_msquareds measured by accelerator and reactor experiments, and neutrinoless double beta decay. Here's a difference; with non-zero theta-13 or the Higgs mass, we still want to measure them more accurately. When the hierarchy is determined, we'll be done with that task, and there is nothing to do better.

*** LBNE moving forward in US and budget issues

http://news.sciencemag.org/scienceinsider/2012/02/at-doe-body-blows-to-fusion-nucl.html Progress in putting together the LBNE experiment may be slowed but not stopped in the proposed 2013 administration budget (PBR) which would cut spending from $21M this year to $10M next, despite the strong support of the DOE Office of High Energy Physics. Recently Fermilab gave a briefing on LBNE to Bill Brinkman, the Head of the DOE Office of Science and acting Under Secretary for Science, to discuss a path forward towards a decision on LBNE. An exchange between Congressman Hultgren and OSTP Director Holdren on LBNE is found in AIP-FYI: http://www.aip.org/fyi/2012/030.html.

*** GLADE idea for liquid argon detector at NOvA

A new idea for an off-axis detector seeks to foster international collaboration with significant European Involvement. The Global Liquid Argon Detector Experiment (GLADE) would be on the surface at Ash River, 810 km from FNAL, in the same hall as NOvA. An LOI sent to Fermilab shows how a 7 year run (2 with neutrinos, 5 with antineutrinos) combined with T2K and NOvA, would help define the mass hierarchy. UCL's Jenny Thomas is the contact person.

*** OPERA statement on velocity rumor

"The OPERA Collaboration, by continuing its campaign of verifications on the neutrino velocity measurement, has identified two issues that could significantly affect the reported result. The first one is linked to the oscillator used to produce the events time-stamps in between the GPS synchronizations. The second point is related to the connection of the optical fiber bringing the external GPS signal to the OPERA master clock. These two issues can modify the neutrino time of flight in opposite directions. While continuing our investigations, in order to unambiguously quantify the effect on the observed result, the Collaboration is looking forward to performing a new measurement of the neutrino velocity as soon as a new bunched beam will be available in 2012. An extensive report on the above mentioned verifications and results will be shortly made available to the scientific committees and agencies." http://www.nature.com/news/flaws-found-in-faster-than-light-neutrino-measurement-1.10099 A rather specific rumor says the problem was a loose connector. http://news.sciencemag.org/scienceinsider/2012/02/breaking-news-error-undoes-faster.html
