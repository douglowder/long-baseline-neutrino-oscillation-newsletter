Long-Baseline news, August 2009

*** Cern neutrino workshop 1-3 October 2009

The aims are to begin the process of establishing a European road map, examine techniques to improve precision, devise R&D plan for decisions by 2013, bring to light synergies between nus and other areas, consider CERN's role, & to establish procedures for Europe coordination. Registration deadline is 25 Sep. The deadline for abstracts (send to ) is 14 Sep. http://indico.cern.ch/conferenceDisplay.py?confId=59378

*** The Perfect Neutrino Conference

The Local Organizing Committee of NuFACT09 decided to allow a free afternoon for participants rather than plan an excursion. High on the list of suggested activities was to attend a White Sox (baseball) game, 3 blocks from the IIT conference site. The many participants who went saw the 18th ever perfect game (by Mark Buerhle) http://whitesox.mlb.com/mlb/news/buehrle_perfectgame/ in the history of baseball. One European told me, "They only told us this was an exciting game in the 9th inning. They should have told us in the 6th inning." The fireworks momentarily disrupted the SPC/LOC/Convener meeting where suggestions for NuFACT10 were being discussed. That will be at TIFR in Mumbai India in late August 2010, where cricket is never perfect.

*** Alternating Neutral Currents (II)

My mention of Alternating Neutral Currents in the last newsletter in an item about Gargamelle's award for discovering neutral currents elicited the largest number of corrections for one item ever. They all ascribed the phrase to a competing experiment, HPWF. As a first year graduate student in 1974, (before I knew that particle physicists had personalities), I had heard the phrase and I remembered it, perhaps incorrectly, in conjunction with the whole discovery of neutral currents. If I google "alternating neutral current" or "alternating neutral currents" I get 13 hits. I am most surprised that one of them involves capacitors and has no relation to neutrinos. http://journal.esrgroups.org/jes/papers/1_4_2.pdf?acr_id=197

*** Can Neutrinos make us secure?

A white paper on the Nuclear Security Applications of Antineutrino Detectors has been made public: A. Bernstein et al., arXiv:0908.4338 . It considers near-field (<100m) monitoring of PWR's as part of IAEA regime; mid-field (1-10km) monitoring; and far-field (10-500km) monitoring. Several recommendations regard antineutrino R&D and evaluation of alternative methods to monitor reactors as part of treaties.

*** Can Neutrinos make the economy recover?

Funds from the American Recovery and Reinvestment Act (ARRA) are used to build neutrino experiments; NOvA in particular. See p9 at http://science.doe.gov/National_Laboratories/Recovery_Gov/09newsletter/SC_Recovery_Act_Newsletter_Jul_09.pdf

*** PASAG set up by HEPAP; some astrophysics is neutrinos

HEPAP formed a subpanel, the Particle Astrophysics Scientific Assessment Group (PASAG). The charge and membership can be found at http://www.science.doe.gov/hep/panels/subpanel_list.shtml, and summary slides can be found linked to the most recent HEPAP agenda http://www.science.doe.gov/hep/agendas/HEPAPAgendaMay2009.shtml. The subpanel has been at work gathering information from projects in the areas of Dark Matter, Cosmic Ray/Gamma Ray/VHE Neutrinos, Dark Energy, and the Cosmic Microwave Background.
