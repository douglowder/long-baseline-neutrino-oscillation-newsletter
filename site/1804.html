Long-Baseline news, April 2018

*** Neutrino collaboration at Fermilab and in India

U.S. DOE secretary Rick Perry and India's Atomic Energy secretary Sekhar Basu signed an agreement that will expand collaboration on neutrino experiments in the U.S. and India. This includes the India-based Neutrino Observatory (INO) in India and the Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment (DUNE) in the U.S. http://news.fnal.gov/2018/04/u-s-india-sign-agreement-providing

*** Neutrino collaboration at Fermilab and in Canada

There is a new partnership between Fermilab and York University in Toronto Canada. They have made a joint appointment of Deborah Harris who will lead York's participation in DUNE. http://news.fnal.gov/2018/04/canadas-governor-general-julie-payette-commends-new-partnership-between-fermilab-and-york-university-in-support-of-the-deep-underground-neutrino-experiment/

*** Neutrino collaboration for Latin American and UK physicists

The initiative's goals are to establish a network of UK-LA collaborative efforts in cutting-edge areas of advanced computing/ deep learning, high-speed electronics and light detection technology, to provide training opportunities for early career scientists in Latin America in these three areas and to define a 10 y GCRF program, building on the networks established in this 1 y proposal. https://indico.fnal.gov/event/16950/

*** Name a neutrino meeting conference room (15 May deadline)

The Long-Baseline Neutrino Facility Project, DUNE Office and Neutrino Division are moving to a newly renovated Wilson Hall 13 at Fermilab. This new space includes 7 conference rooms which they want you to help name! Fill out the Conference Room Name Form and give us between 1 and 7 suggestions for names by 15 May. https://indico.fnal.gov/event/17078/registration/register

*** Neutrino Theory Network (15 May deadline)

To strengthen the neutrino theory community and its impact, with a special emphasis on the connection with the experimental neutrino program, the DOE has provided some funds to support the growth of a network. Deadline is 15 May. http://ntn.fnal.gov/

*** Neutrino Physicists on Youtube

Fermilab has published some profiles of some of the neutrino physicists at FNAL on Youtube. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DO5umyPikBI for Thomas Warburton; and https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N5gGnMW4jMM for Jessica Esquivel. And read about Miriama Rajaoalisoa and Herilala Razafinime at http://nus2surf.dunescience.org/2018/04/madagascar-students-tau

*** Neutrino day X (10) July 14, 2018

Neutrino Day X has a neat logo on its web page. If you are in South Dakota then, volunteer to help and get a free t-shirt. https://www.sanfordlab.org/neutrinodayx

*** NuFACT registration opens

The 20th International workshop on Neutrinos from Accelerators (NuFACT) will be held at Virginia Tech 12-18 August 2018. Registration is now open at https://nufact2018.phys.vt.edu/

*** European 2020 study requests input by 18 December

The CERN Council has set itself the objective of updating Europe's Strategy for Particle Physics by May 2020. To achieve this, it has established a Strategy Secretariat to organize it. Two major events will be an "Open Symposium" in May 19 and a "Strategy Drafting Session". Send written input by 18-Dec-18. Guidelines: https://gallery.mailchimp.com/1c2ffbf7f7d8f7439923005ce/files/7e6884b8-47c6-49ad-a385-56336040665e/Guidelines_input_call_Feb2018.pdf?utm_source=Units&utm_campaign=d99a1779a9-DPF+030718&utm_medium=email&utm_term=0_a5eb4215e8-d99a1779a9-106930045

*** miniTimeCube: the world's smallest neutrino detector

In Star Trek, Gordy's V.I.S.O.R. could detect neutrinos. But that is fiction. A 2 liter neutrino detector ran at NIST 2014-2016, but didn't detect neutrinos. Read the lessons learned in Vlacheslav Li's Ph.D. thesis. http://inspirehep.net/record/1666321
