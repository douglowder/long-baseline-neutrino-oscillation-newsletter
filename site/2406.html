Long-Baseline news, June 2024

*** NuFACT 2024 in September at Argonne National Laboratory

Abstract submission and registration are open for NuFACT 2024, the 25th International Workshop on Neutrinos from Accelerators at Argonne Lab 16-21 September. A lively program of neutrino, muon and accelerator physics is planned. The registration fee is low for a conference these days. https://www.anl.gov/event/NuFact2024 and https://indico.fnal.gov/event/63406/

*** Satellite NuFact Workshop on multi-exp oscillation analyses

On Sunday 15 Sep. there will be a satellite workshop as part of NuFact24 on multi-experiment oscillation measurements, with an eye towards the DUNE and Hyper-K era. Information will be put on the NuFact website or contact Mark Scott at [email protected].

*** Inaugural muon collider meeting 7-9 August at FNAL.

The inaugural meeting for the US Muon Collider community will be 7-9 Aug. at Fermilab. This meeting will be open to the community with the goal to engage broader participation in the muon collider effort, familiarize new groups with the current status of physics, accelerator and detector developments, discuss ways they can contribute to the effort, and take the next steps in forming a US Muon Collider collaboration. https://indico.fnal.gov/event/64493/

*** Neutrino 2024 highlights; 2026 and 2028 plans

A large number of exciting new results were presented in Milano at Neutrino 2024. See https://agenda.infn.it/event/37867/timetable/ There were 71 scientific talks and 460 posters chosen from 616 abstracts (66% of which were submitted in the last 3 days). There were 855 registered participants, but only 828 made it to Milano. 31% were students or within 4 years of their Ph.D. Neutrino 2026 will be at UC Irvine, chaired by Mu-chun Chen and Michael Smy. Neutrino 2028 will be in Beijing China, chairs Jun Cao & Wei Wang.

*** NNN 2024 in Rio Registration open

The 23rd International Workshop on Next Generation Nucleon Decay and Neutrino Detectors will be 28 Oct - 1 Nov in Rio de Janeiro Brazil. https://indico.ggte.unicamp.br/event/4/

*** SBND is successfully taking data now

The Short-Baseline Near Detector (SBND) Time Projection Chamber reached full voltage on 3 July in the Fermilab Booster Beam. The detector has been operating stably since then, seeing its first neutrino events, and will collect neutrino data through the end of this year’s accelerator run.

*** Daya Bay Hydrogen analysis

Reactor neutrino experiments use Gd to capture neutrons and tag inverse beta decay. But they have found that Hydrogen capture is also effective. In arXiv:2406.01007, Daya Bay showed their data on H capture from 1958 days. Combining H and 3158 days of Gd, they got sin^2(2-q13) = 0.0833+-0.0022, 8% better than Gd alone.

*** NOvA adds considerable statistics for neutrino oscillations

NOvA doubled their neutrino data since their previous release 4y ago, including adding a new low-E sample of nu-e's. The new data are consistent with previous NOvA results with improved precision. NOvA favors the “normal” ordering of neutrino masses more strongly than before. NOvA will continue running through early 2027. https://news.fnal.gov/2024/06/new-nova-results-add-to-mystery-of-neutrinos/

*** EuCAPT Astroneutrino Theory Workshop in Prague 2024

The European Consortium for Astroparticle Theory is holding its second Astroneutrino Theory Workshop in Prague, Czech Republic 16-27 Sep. 2024. The scientific agenda of the Workshop is focused on the role of nus in astroparticle & cosmological phenomena. In particular it will focus on the New Neutrino Interactions proposed beyond the Standard Model. https://indico.utef.cvut.cz/event/39/

*** NuMI Breaks 1 MW!

On 26 June, the Fermilab Neutrinos at the Main Injector (NuMI) beam set a new 1-hour power record of 1.011 MW due to a faster cycle time. NuMI won't be operating at this power continuously since the MI beam is shared with other experiments.

*** Jim Kerby is the new LBNF/DUNE-US Project Director

He'll be responsible for managing everything supporting execution of the project, including program leadership, strategic planning and general oversight. He will serve as primary point of contact to external stakeholders and collaborators. He has worked on the LHC in Geneva and ANL's Advanced Photon Source Upgrade project.

*** SIREN: An Open Source Neutrino Injection Toolkit

In arXiv:2406.01745, A. Schneider et al. present a new tool for nu phenomenology and experimental searches (SIREN), which enables accurate interaction and detector geometry modeling without the overhead of detailed detector response simulations.

*** Tau neutrinos seen in IceCube

In arXiv:2403.02516, using 9.7 years of data, IceCube observes 7 astrophysical tau neutrinos with a background of about 0.5 events. The energies range from roughly 20 TeV to 1 PeV, mean of 200 TeV. https://icecube.wisc.edu/news/research/2024/03/icecube-observes-seven-astrophysical-tau-neutrino-candidates/
