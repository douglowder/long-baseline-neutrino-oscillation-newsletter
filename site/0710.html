Long-Baseline news, October 2007

*** CNGS beam from CERN to Gran Sasso restarted

After fixing a water leak in the horn and reflector, the CNGS beam was restarted. Beam was extracted to the target 21 Sep. The 1st Opera event was recorded 2 Oct. Bricks, made of lead & emulsion, containing ten events were immediately removed from the detector and dispatched to the various laboratories of the OPERA Collaboration, which are equipped with automatic microscopes required to read these special pictures and to measure the relevant quantities. http://www.infn.it/news/newsen.php?id=441

*** NOvA passes its cost review

NOvA had its "Lehman review" for CD2, and it concluded that the project is ready for CD2. The project was presented at 15kt for $260M, including detector and accelerator improvement costs. The model for escalation (i.e. inflation) might be optimistic, and one option is 13 kton. Next is an External Independent Review (EIS).

*** Oil costs going up and down

NOvA needs 3.1 Million gallons of oil and tons of energy intensive PVC, so the cost of NOvA is closely tied to the cost of crude oil. In particular Conoco/Phillips Gulf Coast 70N Group II as tracked in the Lube Report http://www.lubereport.com/ "base oil.." Oil recently had a dramatic price spike. NOvA's TDR calculates a contingency based on a historical model that has accurately covered future oil prices 2 years out, 8 times out of 8. (http://www-nova.fnal.gov/nova_cd2_review/tdr_oct_23/tdr.htm)

*** Groundbreaking of Daya Bay reactor neutrino civil construction

The Daya Bay reactor neutrino experiment broke ground for civil construction, which is expected to be completed in 2009. Data 2010 "I believe the Daya Bay Reactor Neutrino Experiment will achieve full success with the efforts and wisdom of the scientists", remarked Jinpei Cheng, Vice Minister of Ministry of Science and Technology. Robin Staffin, (now former) Director of the Office of High Energy Physics of U.S. DOE said, "At the ground where we now stand, scientists will measure with the world's greatest precision one of the most important parameters of nature". http://neutrino.physics.wisc.edu/dayabay/groundbreaking/

*** Selecting Homestake for DUSEL

A Deep Underground Science and Engineering Lab is currently the highest priority for a new start in NSF physics. A 22 member site selection panel had unanimously picked Homestake by secret ballot from 4 choices. A $15M 3 year development contract has been made to UC Berkeley led by Kevin Lesko. This is a commitment to site, design team & facility design work, not to facility construction.

*** DUSEL not yet a done deal

As a possible future Major Research Equipment & Facilities (MREFC), DUSEL will require approval by the National Science Board, the NSF director and an administration and congress. A rough planning target is $500M split evenly between facility and experiments, with a 7-8 year construction, constantly interleaving experiments as ready. Water is coming into Homestake at 700 gal/min, and has filled the shaft from 8000 feet to 4996 as of 2 Nov 07. If pumps aren't restarted soon, it is expected to reach 4850 level in February 2008. Their goal is to beat the water to the 4850 level as efficiently and safely as possible.

*** Indian Neutrino Observatory (INO) Approved

INO has been approved by the Dept of Atomic Energy (DAE). DAE is the funding agency for large high energy/nuclear physics projects in India. Accordingly the Planning Commission of the Government of India has allocated the necessary funds for INO in its current Five year plan allocation. To use the construction money, the INO collaboration is now required to submit a detailed project report & funding request. It needs the final approval of the central ministry before using the funds. INO is planning a center at Mysore. It plans to start generic detector R & D, online & offline software development and physics simulation & data analysis. With the major funding for the project is now assured INO welcomes international collaboration.

*** Merit Makes progress (http://cern.ch/merit/)

On 26 October at CERN, the MERIT experimental team produced the first proton beam/Hg Jet interaction in a magnetic field. The principal parameters were: Hg Jet 15m/s; Solenoid Field 5T, Proton Beam intensity 10TP.

*** MicroBooNE proposal for Fermilab

There is a new proposal for a large liquid Argon Time Projection Chamber at Fermilab in the Booster beam and also off-axis in the NuMI beam. It will address the low energy excess observed by the MiniBooNE experiment, measure low energy neutrino cross sections, and serve as the next step in a phased program towards liquid Ar detectors. http://www-microboone.fnal.gov/
