Long-Baseline news, April 2003

*** Homestake pumping water

The Barrick Corporation, which owns the Homestake mine, announced its intention to turn off the pumps. Al Mann and Ken Lande flew to Lead to protest and the mayor obtained a restraining order from the state's 4th judicial circuit court to keep the pumps running. The NUSEL-Homestake Executive committee pointed out that flooding of the 8000 ft level will irreversibly damage plans for a geomicrobiology program. SD Governor Rounds got Barrick to agree pumping into June. The NSF site selection will occur soon.

*** NuMI not always pumping water

The civil construction of the NuMI tunnels is done, but all of the infrastructure, such as backup pumps have not yet been installed. So far, there have been a few floods, up to 8 inches, as the one set of pumps have not always worked. No damage done.

*** Flamanville could pump water again

One possible source for a reactor neutrino theta13 experiment is a 8.3 Gwth (total) reactor complex at Flamanville near La Manche. Its the site of a previous iron mine with levels at 90m and 150m under the reactor, great potential locations for a near detector. Maps are needed to determine if an appropriate far detector site can be found. The mine had been drained and filled 10 times. It was most recently drained after World War II but has been full of water for 40 years. It could be drained in 8 days.

*** K2K-II Water Detector Rededicated with Dr. John Marburger

In April, K2K was rededicated as K2K-II at a ceremony held at KEK after successful rebuild of its far detector Super-Kamiokande. Leading the American delegation was Dr. John Marburger, Director of the Office of Science and Technology Policy and science advisor to President Bush. He also toured the K2K beamline and near detector along with other facilities at KEK, where Y. Totsuka, leader of the original Super-Kamiokande project, assumed the position of Director General in April. http://www.kek.jp/en/topics/k2k-IIe.html

*** Boundary Waters

The NuMI off-axis experiment could use a detector in Northern Minnesota (~700 km) or in Southern Canada (~960km). A possible site for the latter is in Kenora; for the former at LTV. The distance can effect the amount of matter effects. As the proposal is being written, more than one site for the detector will be considered.

*** Salty Water

SNO has provided two measurements of the neutral current rate of solar neutrinos and they will soon provide a third. They have recently shown data of the energy spectrum and isotropy since they have added the salt. A blindness parameter has been added. The distributions look as expected.

*** More Water - UNO proton decay experiment

The P5++ panel wrote that the physics of a next generation nucleon decay experiment in the US is "absolutely central" The UNO collaboration is formalizing itself in order to move forward with a design and proposal and choosing a site. http://superk.physics.sunysb.edu/nngroup/uno/

*** TESCOE - where bargains are found for off-axis detector

A committee has been established to consider the detector technology choices for a NuMI off-axis experiment. Options being considered include liquid and solid scintillator and resistive plate chambers. There is also a cost committee, Costco, to go along with Tescoe (a word that's the UK version of Walmart).

*** Using Reactors to measure theta13, an amazing thing happens.

The best limit on th_13 comes from a reactor experiment (Chooz). No such experiment is currently running because they are limited by systematic errors. Two detector experiments are being considered to limit systematic errors. In hep-ph/0303232 Huber et al. show that high luminosity experiments (>1000 ton-Gigawatt-years) return to statistical improvement in theta_13 sensitivity when the statistical error on the flux at the far detector measures the flux better than the external input! sin^2 2q <0.005 possible.

*** The moment of conception and neutrinos

About two hundred neutrino physicists received an email idea from a New Jersey attorney that the moment of conception for humans might be related to solar neutrinos. Several answered him pointing out some of the flaws in this idea.

*** LVD as part of CNGS

Rock muons from CNGS into the LVD experiment may be a useful beam monitor according to Aglietta et al., in hep-ex/0304018. 150 events per day.
