*** Muon Cooling Workshop at CERN 25-27 October.

A neutrino factory based on a muon storage ring will study neutrino oscillations, including leptonic CP violation. It is also the first step towards muon colliders, but relies on muon cooling. The aim of the workshop is to explore at least 2 versions of an experiment based on existing cooling channel designs.

*** Off angle beams

Brookhaven proposal 889 was the first proposal for an off-axis neutrino beam. Today's beam designer finds that high energy proton beams make more low energy neutrinos than low energy proton beams, but the "high-energy tail" of the neutrino distribution can compromise the physics. Off angle beams greatly reduce these high energy tails, and are being intensively studied.

*** Wrestling with SUMO

The bottom of Lake Superior is off-angle to the upcoming NuMI beam line, and the fine Cerenkov properties of water are well-known. Larry Wai is developing an outline of a shallow underwater Main Injector Neutrino Oscillation Experiment (SUMO). 6250 pmt's would cover 5 sides of a 156 kt cube, 568m^2 sides and could push Ue3^2 down to .003 at 5 10^-3 eV^2.

*** MINOS first light, first muons

With eight planes up, MINOS is now measuring cosmic muons tracks.

*** Is Super-Kamiokande's atmospheric neutrino analysis wrong?

An impromptu session at the Hamburg ICRC for "Comprehensive Discussion Around the Super-Kamiokande (Atmospheric Neutrino)" focused on complaints that e/mu separation was inadequate and calculations of angular distributions were not "exact". Of the 30 who came, there were no Super-Kamiokande members present to defend the experiment, but none were needed. Criticism was based on inappropriate cross sections and oscillation calculations were wrong. Meanwhile, new Super-K results exclude nu decay at 99% CL.

*** The Lessons of the SSC

As the HEP community deals with how and whether to ask for the NLC, it is important to keep in mind the lessons of the SSC, but we don't seem to agree what they were. Unanimity is one request of us. A member of the B&B panel asked me why the neutrino community didn't get together and say what we wanted. 335 of us signed a letter asking for new experiments after MINOS and MiniBooNE, and the Snowmass group agreed to focus on a proton driver for a superbeam. Other new ideas will continue to come forward. Do we just need a marketing strategy?

A person involved in shutting down the SSC is now of great value. A "good" California lawyer is also on board.

*** The Theory of the SSC

Are there astrophysical sources of TeV or greater E neutrinos? Models for such sources do exist. TeV photons are being seen from an increasing number of nearby AGN's (new is 1426+428) but it is unknown if protons or electrons are being accelerated there. Protons would create pions and yield neutrinos, but the synchrotron self compton (SSC) model of electron acceleration is the increasingly accepted explanation of the visible photons.

*** Neutrino Search for Extra Terrestrial Intelligence?

This was reviewed by Pakvasa in hep-ph/0105127. The idea is that an advanced galactically dispersed civilization needs to accurately synchronize its clocks, and it would use Z decays, the fastest known process. This provides a source of 45.6 GeV nus.
