Long-Baseline news, June 2010

Minoan/Crete Issue

*** New MINOS Delta msquared_32

The world's best value of Delta msquared_32 came from the MINOS previous analysis of 3.4e20 pot comparing the E of charged current events in the near and far detectors. At Nu2010, Patricia Vahle showed that with 7.2e20 pot, MINOS has 1986 events (expected 2451 if no oscillations). The shape fit gives Dm^2_32= 2.35 +0.11 -0.08 10^-3 eV^2. The previous value was 2.43 +- 0.14. The new best fit is maximal mixing, and sin^2(2-theta) > 0.91 at 90%CL. Nu2010 links: http://indico.cern.ch/conferenceTimeTable.py?confId=73981#all.detailed MINOS talk at: http://indico.cern.ch/getFile.py/access?contribId=201&sessionId=1&resId=0&materialId=slides&confId=73981

*** ICARUS sees first neutrino event

When Theseus left Crete, Minos got mad at Icarus, who then left. The ICARUS liquid argon detector is being commissioned in the Cern Neutrino to Gran Sasso (CNGS) beam. On May 28 19:54 the first long-baseline neutrino event in liquid argon was seen. It has a leading muon, two charged tracks undergoing hadronic interactions, and two photons converting 14 and 16 cm from the vertex. They showed three other CNGS neutrinos as well. http://indico.cern.ch/getFile.py/access?contribId=218&sessionId=7&resId=4&materialId=slides&confId=73981

*** MiniBooNE antineutrinos seem a bit different

In my drive to "Knossos", the site of Minos' alleged Labyrinth, I saw Greek signs spelling it with two sigmas and spelling it with three sigmas. They can't agree whether the site contains a two sigma effect or a three sigma effect. MiniBooNE, which previously showed its neutrino data was inconsistent with a two-neutrino oscillation interpretation of LSND, updated its antineutrino data with 5.66e20 pot, and showed an LSND-like excess, E>475 MeV. The match to null is 3%. It's consistent with LSND at 99.4% relative to null. In nus, a 2-nu fit is inconsistent with LSND at 90%CL. http://indico.cern.ch/getFile.py/access?contribId=208&sessionId=3&resId=0&materialId=slides&confId=73981

*** MINOS antineutrinos seem a bit different

MINOS had previously shown an analysis of antineutrinos using a neutrino beam. At Athens, they showed an analysis of 1.71e20 antineutrino running (where negatives are focused in the horn and positives in the detectors). The no oscillation prediction was 155 events and 97 were observed, disfavoring no oscillations at 6.3 sigma. Parameter space has poor overlap with neutrinos. MINOS has requested 2.5e20 more running in antineutrino mode.

*** MINOS neutral currents

In MINOS, the neutral current event rate should not change between the near detector and far detector with 3-nu oscillations. An FD deficit could indicate mixing to sterile nus. MINOS expects 757 events, observes 802 and sets a limit of 22% (40%) on oscillation fraction to sterile nus at 90%CL without (with) nue appearance.

*** New MINOS paper on electron appearance

The Minoans left great sites for archaeologists, but documentation was nil, Linear A being undeciphered. MINOS has documented much, from Lorentz invariance arXiv:0806.4945 , to nus arXiv:0806.2237 , to seasonal variations of cosmic rays arXiv:0909.4012. A new paper has been submitted on the search for electron appearance with a new limit on theta13 arXiv:1006.0996. The observation is 54 candidate far detector nu_es with a background of 49.1+/-7.0(stat.)+/-2.7(syst.) leading to 2sin^2(2theta_{13})sin^2(theta_{23})<0.12 (0.20) at 90% C.L. for the normal (inverted) mass hierarchy at delta_cp=0.

*** Neutrino name origin and destiny in Neutrino 2010 summary talk

The legends of Minos et al. arose not from Minoans but Greeks who came later. In Lisi's theoretical summary at Nu 2010, he traced the origin of the word Neutrino. Fermi's "little neutral one" came from the Italian Neutro "neutral" from Latin ne-uter "not either" and uter is from Ionic Greek Koteros (Sanskrit Kataras) "Which of 2" from Indo-European Ka or Kwa. Declining "Koteros", he asked: Which of the two? - (mass hierarchies) Which of the three? - (see-saw types, if any) Which of the many? - (scales of new physics) http://indico.cern.ch/getFile.py/access?contribId=273&sessionId=26&resId=0&materialId=slides&confId=73981

*** Theta-13 in Athens

There is considerable graffiti on Greek roads, and the most popular graffiti in Crete is "Theta-13". Whether it was put there by gate 13 soccer fans or physics hooligans, it was mentioned by many experimenters and all theorists at Neutrino 2010. Many theorists showed tri-bimaximal mixing(TBM), and said that theta_13 was zero or maybe more. One theorist actually showed a prediction based on corrections to TBM related to the Cabbibo angle, and said Ue3 = 0.0583, the first prediction I remember good to 4 digits! http://indico.cern.ch/getFile.py/access?contribId=221&sessionId=9&resId=0&materialId=slides&confId=73981

*** ArgoNeuT progress

When the Argonauts were going home and passed Crete, a bronze giant threw rocks at them. At Athens, Mitch Soderberg showed the tremendous progress on Liquid Argon Activities in the past few years at Fermilab, including results from ArgoNeuT and plans for the Liquid Argon Purity Demonstrator, MicroBooNE and LBNE. ArgoNeuT acquired 1.4e20 pot in mostly antineutrino mode, and saw 5110 CC numu, 5490 CC antinumu, 142 Nue and 4266 Neutral Current. They'll use data to measure the effectiveness of dE/dx particle ID and obtain cross section measurements. http://indico.cern.ch/getFile.py/access?contribId=219&sessionId=7&resId=0&materialId=slides&confId=73981
