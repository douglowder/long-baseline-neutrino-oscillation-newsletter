Long-Baseline news, November 2009

*** T2K sees first neutrinos

Events from the new neutrino beam at J-PARC have been detected in the T2K near detector. They were seen in "Interactive Neutrino GRID detectors" (INGRID), a neutrino beam monitor for the beam profile which was installed in August. These first events were seen 24 Nov 09. T2K expects to begin physics running in January, using the Super-Kamiokande (far) detector. The director of KEK correctly stated, "Researchers around the world must be jealous that once again neutrinos seem to like to reveal new properties in Japan!" http://www.kek.jp/intra-e/press/2009/J-PARCT2K2.html

*** SciNOvA Expression of Interest at November FNAL PAC

They propose to re-instrument the scintillator from SciBooNE and deploy the detector in front of the NOvA near detector where it will be exposed to the 2 GeV NuMI narrow-band beam. The detector would consist of 15,000 scintillator bars that would provide a fine-grained detector to measure quasi-elastic scattering, neutral-current elastic scattering, neutral-current pizero production, and enhance the NOvA measurements of electron neutrino appearance and muon neutrino disappearance. The Fermilab PAC responded favorably to the EoI and recommends the proponents clarify/expand on the complementarity and synergy with MINERvA and how it would help the NOvA understand its off-axis beam.

*** INO has to find a new site for its neutrino observatory

The Indian Neutrino Observatory (INO) collaboration has received a letter from India's minister of environment and forests telling them to find an alternate site for the underground laboratory. Trucks involved in construction at the site would have had to drive through a wildlife corridor and therefore a number of conservationists had objected to the project. http://www.nature.com/news/2009/091124/full/462397b.html

*** Muon collider for Fermilab gains momentum

A muon collider might be harder to build than an e linear collider, but one advantage is that it could be built in stages with physics opportunities at each stage. One such step is Project X, another is a neutrino factory. Fermilab has submitted a DOE proposal to form a national muon-accelerator project, starting at $15m/year. Nature describes some of the motivation and challenges that this entails: http://pubweb.bnl.gov/~gallardo/NATURE/natureb.pdf

*** Reactor neutrino experiments and Baseball

New reactor neutrino experiments have three volumes of liquid, including the middle "gamma catcher" which has scintillator but no Gadolinium, to catch the electromagnetic energy from photons and positron showers in the target.
