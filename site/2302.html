*** FY23 United States DOE Office of Science Budget Outcome

LBNF/DUNE will receive $176M and PIP-II $120M next year, matching the requested amounts. They also had received $125M & $10M through the IRA. Schedule estimates for LBNF/DUNE have stabilized with installation slated to begin in 2024 and the start of full science operations targeted for 2031. LBNF/DUNE’s total cost to DOE is now expected to exceed $3B, and PIP-II almost $1B. Those estimates do not include the > $1B in international commitments.

*** Bethe prize to Princeton Physicist Frank Calaprice

Calaprice, an emeritus professor of physics at Princeton, will get the 2023 award “for pioneering work on large-scale ultra-low- background detectors (Borexino) measuring the complete spectroscopy of solar neutrinos, culminating in observation of CNO neutrinos, thus experimentally proving operation of all the nuclear energy driving reactions of stellar evolution.”

*** NuMI beam returns to NOvA

A December horn stripline failure prevented the Fermilab neutrino program from taking beam in 2023 until now. But they have taken advantage of machine studies and may be able to make up the 2023 beam goals. Reducing the repetition rate to 1.2 s represents a 16.7% increment in beam power. As there is more high power (>900 kW) operation, they will enlarge the beam spot from 1.2 to 1.5 mm.

*** Here Comes the Sun: Solar Parameters & Long-Baseline neutrinos

In arXiv:2302.08513, P. Denton and J. Gehrlein discuss how long- baseline experiments (NOvA, T2K, DUNE, Hyper-K) can determine the solar neutrino parameters, and thus all 6 oscillation parameters. While sensitivities are not competitive with existing constraints, they are orthogonal and provide a consistency check of a less probed sector of the three-flavor oscillation picture.

*** Final result for Majorana Demonstrator

It set a lifetime limit for 76Ge of 8.3e25y (90% CL). Background reduction techniques will be combined with those from GERDA to achieve even lower levels in LEGEND. PhysRevLett.130.062501 https://www.ornl.gov/news/researchers-publish-final-word-majorana-demonstrator

*** Neutrino Structure Functions from GeV to EeV energies

In arXiv:2302.08527, A. Candido et al.'s approach combines a machine-learning parametrization of experimental data with pQCD calculations based on state-of-the-art analyses of proton and nuclear parton distributions (PDFs). The "NNSFnu" determination is made available as fast interpolation LHAPDF grids and can be accessed through a driver code at https://nnpdf.github.io/nnusf/ or directly interfaced to neutrino event generators such as GENIE.

*** RANT: hyphens again

As a stickler for the correct use of hyphens in long-baseline experiments, I have been puzzled by the practice that many put a hyphen in cross section when it is not being used as an adjective. There exists guidance that you should not. But Nature magazine does. To avoid ambiguous pronunciation, English does not allow compound words with 3 consecutive identical letters (skull-like or bee-eater) so there is a hyphen. But nobody writes crosssection. https://english.stackexchange.com/questions/488780/when-to-use-hyphens-in-compound-words & https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/GPO-STYLEMANUAL-2008/pdf/GPO-STYLEMANUAL-2008-8.pdf
