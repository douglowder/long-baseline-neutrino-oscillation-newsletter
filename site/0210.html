Long-Baseline news, October 2002

*** Muon neutrino - 1/4 nu1, 1/4 nu2, 1/2 nu3

Back in the days of bimaximal mixing (not SO long ago), we wrote that numu was 1/4 nu1, 1/4 nu2 and 1/2 nu3. We now think this isn't quite right, and we're learning the fractions better. If KaMLAND confirms the LMA (on 11/9 or later), we'll continue to measure the MNS matrix even better. But it gave the Nobel prize committee an idea.

*** Nobel Prize - 1/4 for Davis, 1/4 Koshiba, 1/2 someone else

The prizes for neutrino astronomy this year (and not oscillations) was food for many lunch table conversations. One theory is that this leaves room for a prize for oscillations. Another theory is that this was meant to be remembered as the prize for oscillations, even though it wasn't.

*** Nobel prize for Neutrino Theory?

The New York Times stated, "No matter how worthy the winners, the selection process is inherently unjust." It mentioned that the citation left out John Bahcall, Vladimir Gribov, Bruno Pontecorvo, Stanislav Mikheyev, Alexei Smirnov and Lincoln Wolfenstein. Folklore states that mixed experiment/theory prizes are rare. Pontecorvo and Gribov have passed away hence ineligible.

*** BooNE Prize for Neutrino Theorist Kayser

Boris Kayser won the unprestigious BooNE prize, awarded for most closely guessing the number of phototubes in their 1st nu event. MiNIBoone already has more neutrino events than LSND. But the p intensity & hence the event rates are lower than planned because of Booster losses, which should be reduced soon.

*** 2002 Cultural Prize for Totsuka

Yoji Totsuka, leader of the Super-Kamiokande collaboration which showed conclusive evidence for neutrino oscillations, won the 2002 Cultural Prize. http://www.kek.jp/newskek/announce/bunka2002.html

*** New Detectors in the NuMI Beam

An extensive study on detectors for future neutrino experiments in the NuMI Beam will be presented to the Fermilab PAC. Some detector R&D would be useful for RPC's, phototubes and Liquid Argon. http://www.hep.anl.gov/mcg/detrd/detectrd.pdf

*** Neutrino Scattering in the NuMI Beam

An EOI signed by 17 institutions is thinking of high precision neutrino studies in the near detector region of the high intensity NuMI beam at Fermilab. Neutrino cross sections, nuclear effects, and a wide variety of other particle and nuclear physics topics could be addressed. http://home.fnal.gov/~morfin/midis/midis_eoi.pdf

*** An Off-axis Near Detector in the NuMI Beam

A group from Rochester has expressed interest in a new off-axis near detector to measure cross sections in anticipation of a future off-axis far detector. http://www.pas.rochester.edu/~ksmcf/numi-near/eoi.pdf

*** Neutrino Homework

Assume Majorana nus. A left-handed numu beam interacts with a target producing negative muons. The same experiment is observed by an observer moving faster than the nus. In this reference frame they are right-handed. They are, therefore, called antineutrinos by the observer and their CC interactions are expected to yield positive muons. Sign of the electric charge is a Lorentz invariant, hence both observers must observe the same sign, and the negative sign is the answer. How does the moving observer explain this? Does it prove that nus cannot be Majorana?
