Long-Baseline news, June 2014

*** Open LBNE meeting 30 July-1 August at Fermilab

As LBNE (The Long-Baseline Neutrino Experiment) prepares to transition into LBNF, in a not-yet-defined process, it welcomes all interested parties to attend its collaboration meeting 30 July - 1 August at Fermilab. It will be preceded by a workshop on near detector parameters 28-29 Jul. Agendas & Register at https://indico.fnal.gov/conferenceDisplay.py?confId=8125

*** Open Hyper-K meeting 19-22 July in British Columbia

The 5th Open Meeting for the Hyper-Kamiokande Project will be held 19-22 July in UBC, Vancouver Canada. First time outside Japan. http://indico.ipmu.jp/indico/conferenceDisplay.py?confId=34

*** Driving Nu Physics, MicroBooNE moved into place

The 30 ton MicroBooNE detector was driven 3 miles and moved into place in the Booster Beam on 23 June. It will resolve if the low energy excess of MiniBooNE is due to photons or electrons. Video: http://www.fnal.gov/pub/presspass/press_releases/2014/MicroBooNE-Move-20140623-images.html

*** Sterile neutrinos at Neutrino 2014

Kopp described the usefulness of sterile nus for SM&phenomenology: https://indico.fnal.gov/getFile.py/access?contribId=295&sessionId=25&resId=0&materialId=slides&confId=8022 A standard model singlet fermion can be leftovers of extended gauge multiplets. It helps explain the small nu mass, baryon asymmetry of the Universe, dark matter and various anomalies. But a global fit shows "severe tension" with 3+1/2/3, 1303.3011. Some new limits were presented by MINOS & others. http://www.symmetrymagazine.org/article/june-2014/minos-result-narrows-field-for-sterile-neutrinos

*** Excess in reactor neutrino fluxes at 5 MeV

At Nu 2014, RENO reported a "new reactor component at 5 MeV". It looked statistically compelling, but with systematics was quoted as a 3.6 sigma effect. It is consistent with measurements from CHOOZ, Double Chooz and others. See slide 33 of the review at https://indico.cern.ch/event/303475/contribution/11/material/slides/0.pdf#page=33 It is probably unrelated to the fits to theta_13 or to the reactor neutrino anomaly, but that needs some thought. More likely it is an interesting or uninteresting nuclear physics issue.

*** T2K running again; to concentrate on antineutrinos in 2014

While J-PARC was off for a radiation incident, the Linac was upgraded, 3 horns were replaced & a new beam monitor installed. T2K has restarted and seen its first antinu during an antinu test. It's interesting that comparing T2K data so far with reactor data, the best overlap is for normal hierarchy and delta=270. Lucky!? https://indico.fnal.gov/getFile.py/access?contribId=268&sessionId=18&resId=0&materialId=slides&confId=8022

*** Updated results from Daya Bay

The reactor nu experiment in China reported 621 days with 6 and 8 running detectors + 6 reactors. The best fit of sin^2 theta13 from Gd is 0.084 +- 0.005. Using H captures, 0.083 +- 0.018. They presented stringent sterile limits in the low mass region.

*** David Wark's view of cosmological fits to Neutrinos

Will cosmological data measure the hierarchy before long-baseline experiments? In Dave Wark's experimental perspectives view: "Cosmological data is rare and precious stuff for determining the properties of the early Universe. We shouldn't waste it measuring Nu properties. (They) should be inputs to cosmology, not outputs. That statement is (to me) independent of the quality of the cosmological data."

*** PIP-II status

The US strategic plan called for Fermilab to aggressively move forward on the Proton Improvement Plan II, to provide > 1 MW for long-baseline neutrino experiments. It is a complete, integrated, cost effective concept that also leverages U.S. superconducting rf investment, attracts international partners and provides a platform for the long-term future of Fermilab. Summary/Details: https://indico.fnal.gov/getFile.py/access?contribId=12&sessionId=8&resId=0&materialId=slides&confId=8126, , & https://indico.fnal.gov/conferenceOtherViews.py?view=standard&confid=8365

*** US appropriations committees appreciate P5 report

2300 Physicists signed their support for the 10 year plan. https://usparticlephysics.pa.msu.edu/authorlist/show.php . In its appropriation bill, the house committee stated, "The Committee applauds the (DOE) for (P5) and continues to support a clearly articulated vision under realistic budget scenarios for each of the Science programs." Regarding LBNE, "The Committee recognizes the importance of this project to maintaining American leadership in the intensity frontier and to basic science discovery of neutrino and standard model physics." $12M was added for LBNE over the President's budget by both the House and Senate.
