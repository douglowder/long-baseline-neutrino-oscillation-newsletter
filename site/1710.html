*** First results from CUORE on 0nu-beta-beta from Te

CUORE started running in March with 988 TeO_2 crystals (34% 130Te abundance) and has 86.3 kg-yr (natural Te). The best fit for a signal in the region of interest(2165-2575 keV) is negative. The Bayesian lifetime limit is >1.3e25y, for an m_(beta-beta) < 100-400 meV. Running at 10 mK with the coldest cubic meter in the known universe ( arXiv:1410.1560 ), they commissioned the cryostat 2014-Mar 2016, installed through 26 Aug 2016, saw their 1st pulse 27 Jan 17, and a "science run" started 24 April. arXiv:1710.07988 .

*** CUPID

CUORE will run 5 more years. The next generation of experiment is CUPID (CUORE Upgrade with Particle IDentification). It would be difficult and expensive to reduce backgrounds further with better radiopurity. Instead, the idea is to instrument each crystal with a photodetector and use particle identification to separate background alphas from signal betas. arXiv:1610.03513 .

*** LBNF beam optimization

A recurring tradeoff in neutrino experiments is how much effort to put into beam design versus how much effort to put into detector design. An October review studied an optimized design for the LBNF neutrino beam. The Experiment Facility Interface Group (EFIG) has concluded that the optimized beamline offered compelling improvements to the physics reach and should be implemented. The optimized target and horn design leads to a 36% improvement in the flux within the important 1-4 GeV neutrino energy range. The optimized design has implications for other systems, such as conventional facilities and remote handling. Additional costs and schedule delays have been estimated and are not large.

*** The other hierarchy

Our collider friends (sometimes) worry about their own hierarchy problem, i.e. why the Higgs mass isn't near the Planck mass. One solution is supersymmetry, which explains this one number but doubles the number of parameters. In the "Neutrino Option", by I. Brivio in arXiv:1710.01007 , the Higgs mass is explained as a radiative correction, while there is a "new challenge of generating PeV Majorana masses while suppressing the tree-level scalar potential in the UV".

*** MICE considers move to Protvino

Since mid-September, the Muon Ionization Colling Experiment (MICE) at Rutherford has been taking high quality data with its liquid Hydrogen absorber. It had previously tested ionization cooling with a Lithium hydride absorber. They have developed a novel emittance analysis technique to compensate for the loss of a superconducting coil in the downstream spectrometer solenoid. With at most a few more months of data to be taken at RAL, they are considering a more ambitious follow-up experiment at Protvino.
