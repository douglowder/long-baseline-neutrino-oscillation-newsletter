Long-Baseline news, June 2001

*** [2 weeks] since SNO results were on Nightline

Last month rumors abounded that SNO results would be out in 2 weeks. They measure a CC rate which is 0.347 +- 0.029 the prediction of BP2001. Combining their measurement of CC with the Super-K combination of NC+CC, it is shown from the NC rate that the total flux of neutrinos (5.4 10^6 /cm^2/s) is in close agreement with solar models. This strengthens the conclusion that the low solar CC rate is due to neutrino oscillations. nucl-ex/0106015 & http://www.sno.phy.queensu.ca/sno/first_results/

*** [Several] Accidents delay NuMI Project

The NuMI projects was stopped and restarted in late June as the result of several lost-time accidents. The most recent, (6/21) an employee of Layne-Western, a subcontractor of S.A. Healy, was injured as a result of equipment failure while operating a drilling rig. Because of the string of accidents that have occurred on NuMI, Fermilab director Mike Witherell a halt to NuMI construction until there were assurances the project would proceed safely. A phased restart of NuMI was approved 6/29. http://www.fnal.gov/pub/presspass/press_releases/numiinjury.html

*** [T600] ICARUS sees tracks

Half the 735 ton (Argon) module in Pavia is full & sees cosmicray tracks up to 18 m long. Nominal HV is 75 kV. Impurities are less than 1 ppb O equivalent, (design 0.1) Attenuation >1.5m. After commissioning, the module will be taken to Gran Sasso.

*** [<300,000 km/s] in a long-baseline experiment.

MINOS plans to measure the oscillations in the energy distribution of nu_mu from Fermilab. A new idea is to improve timing to look for time-of-flight delays. It is the mass eigenstates nu_1, _2 and _3 which would travel with a fixed velocity. Oscillations arise from wavepackets of e.g. nu_2 and nu_3 being shifted but overlapping. The time spread of the nu emission precludes an interesting time-of-flight measurement. But if MINOS did measure a time of flight difference between neutrinos, it would not measure any energy oscillations.

*** [>300,000 km/s] & NuMI delays

numidelay.html . Besides accidents, NuMI is faced with cost overruns and schedule delays. A Lehman review "rebaselining" to redefine the schedule wasn't approved in May but might be in Aug. The overrun is ~$30M which increases the project cost from ~$140M to ~$170M, but the detector is on schedule while the delays and additional costs affect the beam. The steel for the first far detector plane was delivered to Soudan last week. On the bright side, if the detector is finished on schedule, and runs for two years or so before the beam turns on, that will increase the parameter range sensitivity to beam tachyons(?). Well, at least cosmic rays.

*** [$2.2 Billion] for Homestake gold; neutrinos are free

Toronto-based Barrick Gold Corp said Monday that it is acquiring Homestake Mining Co., one of the oldest U.S. mining operations and owner of the legendary Homestake mine in the Black Hills of South Dakota. In a $2.2B stock deal Barrick, one of the largest gold producers in the world, said it would take over Homestake and create a combined company with a market capitalization of $9B, double its nearest rival. The National Underground Science Lab (NUSL) was brought up during negotiations, and no one objected to staying on course for the transition.
