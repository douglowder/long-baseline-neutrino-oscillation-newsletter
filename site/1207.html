Long-Baseline news, July 2012

*** LBNE reconfiguration complete

The LBNE reconfiguration process is complete and the project has a green light to move forward. CD1 review which baselines the cost is scheduled for later this year. The plan is for a new beam from Fermilab to the Homestake mine with a 10 kton liquid Argon TPC on the surface. Additional funding is sought from sources outside the DOE which could build a near detector and/or put the detector underground. http://www.fnal.gov/directorate/lbne_reconfiguration/

*** Hyper-Kamiokande open meeting 21-23 August in Tokyo

Hyper-K is designed to study nu oscillations, nucleon decay & astrophysical nus. An open meeting for those interested will be http://indico.ipmu.jp/indico/conferenceDisplay.py?confId=7

*** FNAL director's Letter to Europe for 10-12 Sep Krakow meeting

Oddone wrote to the European Strategy Preparatory Group, "FNAL's intensity frontier program includes 2 major projects that work synergistically: LBNE that will send a new neutrino beam to Homestake (1300 km baseline); and the Project X accelerator and experimental program that includes megawatt-class beams at several energies. Both projects provide unequaled opportunity for scientific discovery at the intensity frontier. International collaborators would derive the maximum scientific benefit from participation ... since a large fraction of the necessary infrastructure will be provided." http://espp2012.ifj.edu.pl/

*** NOvA lays and glues first planes for far detector

NOvA, the next long-baseline neutrino experiment, has started construction in place of its far detector. The first planes of extruded plastic have been laid down and glued together.

*** New OPERA paper on neutrino velocity

There is a new version of the OPERA paper on neutrino velocity: arXiv:1109.4897v4. This is the update version of the OPERA original version of September, already updated in November 2011 (but still with the wrong result on the neutrino velocity). This latter version has been resubmitted to JHEP and it should be referenced for any citation of OPERA results on neutrino velocity.

*** Phased Ice-cube Next Generation Upgrade (PINGU)

PINGU is a plan to add 18-20 higher density strings in IceCube/ DeepCore to lower the threshold to about 1 GeV. The effective volume would be 10 Mton at 10 GeV, & this would make the South Pole experiment sensitive to matter effects & oscillations.

*** Plans for NuSTORM, workshop 21-22 September at FNAL.

NuSTORM (Neutrinos from STORed Muons) is an idea for a single baseline lower energy entry-level neutrino factory. It requires no cooling and thus no new technology. A 60 GeV proton beam at FNAL could hit a 100kW Target and feed a 3.8 GeV/c 150 m long muon decay ring. Short-baseline neutrino anomalies could be studied. A collaboration-building workshop at FNAL will be 21-22 Sep. https://indico.fnal.gov/conferenceDisplay.py?confId=5710

*** Community questions from NuFACT

Each year, the oscillation community asks itself questions at NuFACT. For 2013: 1) How long should T2K & NOvA run? 2) What are the sensitivities & systematics for future facilities? 3) Can we do precision without a ND? 4) What significance for hierarchy comes from atmo & cosmo experiments? 5) What do we learn about Majorana nature of nus from hierarchy + 0nu-bb probes? 6) Can we reoptimize design of future facilities for large theta13? 7) Are off-axis beams interesting for large theta13? 8) What is the deviation of theta23 from maximality at future facilities? 9) What is the useful target precision for each mixing parameter?

*** A comment on staging (neutrino experiments) from NuFACT

See: https://www.jlab.org/indico/contributionDisplay.py?sessionId=15&contribId=95&confId=0 In 1248, Konrad von Hochstaden, the Archbishop of Cologne started a civil engineering project to build a Gothic cathedral. In 1517 M. Luther announced a "new theory" and construction came to a halt. In 1824, citizens raised $1B equivalent to resume construction; finished in 1880. Lessons: need to transcend day-to-day politics; phase n does not imply phase n+1; re-assess program based on new developments; need community involvement.

*** NNN meeting 4-6 October at Fermilab

The 2012 international workshop on Next Generation Nucleon Decay & Neutrino Detectors NNN12 will discuss future large-scale detectors for nucleon decay, neutrino physics, and related topics. The meeting will focus on next-generation detectors and strategies for advancing research on proton decay, accelerator, atmospheric, solar, supernova, and reactor neutrinos as well as developments in studies of neutrino interactions and searches for 0nu-bb decay.

*** Venus's transit of the sun and neutrino pop quiz

The best answer to "During the Venus transit of the sun, were more solar neutrinos absorbed in Venus, or focused toward us by gravity?" came from U Chicago's Matt Strait: "Since Venus didn't transit the core of the Sun, it didn't absorb any nus."
