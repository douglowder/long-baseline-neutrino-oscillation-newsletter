Long-Baseline news, September 2011

*** Borexino sees pep solar neutrinos

They measured the rate of pep solar nus to be 3.13 +- 0.55(stat) +- 0.23 (syst) per day per 100ton and constrained CNO nus to <7.6 at 95%CL. They can reject the dominant background in the 1-2 MeV region which is cosmogenic 11C. Assuming the LMA, this is a solar nu flux 1.6+-0.3 x 108 cm^-2s^-1 and < 7.4 (95%CL) in agreement with SSM. These represent the first measurement of pep nus and the strongest constraint of the CNO solar nu flux to date.

*** Neutrino Physicists celebrate the end of the Tevatron 30 Sep

The Tevatron turned off to a big party, even as the NuMI run continues. Neutrino physicists will get more protons. In particular, the ANU project will use the recycler to raise the power from about 250 kW to 700 kW for NuMI. The short term gain for NuMI may be only 6%, due to shortening the cycle from 2.2 s to 2.06 s. The long shutdown is scheduled for Mar 5, 2012 but there are rumors for extensions to May or June. Read or share Tevatron memories in the guest book: http://www.fnal.gov/pub/tevatron/guestbook.html.

*** MINOS ends Antineutrino run 15 Sep

A new target (NT07) was installed in the NuMI beam, and the priority was switched from MINOS to Minerva on 15 Sep, which resulted in a switch from focusing for antinus to focusing nus.

*** Hyper-Kamiokande Letter of Intent

A description of a possible 0.99 million metric ton water Cherenkov detector (0.56 fiducial) is on the arXiv:1109.3262. It would be 8 km south of Super-K, 295 km from J-PARC, and use 99,000 20-inch PMTs. A neutrino physics program to study theta-13, mass hierarchy, CP violation, along with nucleon decay, supernova burst neutrinos, supernova relic neutrinos, dark matter searches and solar flare neutrinos is described.

*** Senate appropriations bill and LBNE

"The Committee provides no construction funds for the Long-Baseline Neutrino Experiment. The Committee is concerned that this project is not mature enough for construction because a location for this experiment in an underground laboratory has not yet been selected and the decision of the National Science Foundation to discontinue construction funding for the Deep Underground Science and Engineering Laboratory in South Dakota has created uncertainty about the future of the project. In addition, the Office of Science has not yet selected a technology, which affects where the experiment can be located and total cost."

*** Superluminal neutrinos - What OPERA saw

They measure position with geodesy, time with good clocks, and fit the shape of the 10 microsecond proton pulse with a fast BCT in the SPS with the shape of the neutrino pulse in the Gran Sasso. Nus appear 60.7+-6.9 +-7.4 ns early. (v-c)/c = 2.48+-0.28+-0.30 e-5. arXiv:1109.4897

*** Superluminal neutrinos - What MINOS saw

With geodesy and a not-so-good clock, MINOS in 2007 fit the shape of the nu pulse in the near and far detectors (v-c)/c=5.1+-2.7e-5. arXiv:0706.0437. A task force is looking into new clocks, etc.
