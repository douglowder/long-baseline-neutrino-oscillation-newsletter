*** Super-Kamiokande starts observations with Gadolinium

The rare earth gadolinium has been introduced into SK, starting a new period of observations. The addition of Gd helps SK’s ability to observe supernovae bursts in our galaxy. It will also improve discrimination of atmospheric neutrinos from antineutrinos and reduce backgrounds for nucleon decay searches. http://www-sk.icrr.u-tokyo.ac.jp/sk/news/2020/08/sk-gd-detail-e.html

*** Snowmass Letters of Interest

In August many "Letters of Interest" were submitted for the Snowmass 2021 HEP planning exercise, though more may dribble in. LoIs are informal documents for the 1st stages of the study. They will help conveners to prepare the 5-8 Oct 2020 Planning Meeting. LoIs contain opinions, interests and proposals for further study. https://www.snowmass21.org/docs/files/?dir=summaries/ Many nu LoIs will be summarized in future LBNN newsletters.

*** MicroBooNE soon

We should soon hear from MicroBooNE whether the low energy excess is due to electrons or photons, but I don't know when. Possible answers include no excess, all electrons, all photons, and a mix with a rate the same, lower, or higher than MiniBooNE.

*** Muon g-2 answer soon

We should soon hear an answer from the FNAL experiment measuring the muon's g-2, but I don't know when. Possible answers include a value consistent with theory, an answer consistent with BNL E821, a value in between, or a value larger or smaller than both. https://muon-g-2.fnal.gov/. In preparation, a four year international collaborative effort of theorists has updated the Standard Model prediction in arXiv:2006.04822 . They get (g-2)/2 = 116591810(43)e(−11), 3.7 sigma smaller than the BNL measurement. https://news.fnal.gov/2020/06/physicists-publish-worldwide-consensus-of-muon-magnetic-moment-calculation/?utm_source=newsletter&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=ft-200612 An outlier number in arXiv:2002.12347 has recently been revised a bit. I don't know any nu connection of all this, but who knows.

*** New word for my spell checker - unobservability

In a nice talk on 0nu2beta decay at Nu2020, Petcov used the phrase "well of unobservability" for the dip in the plot of the effective mass measured in 0nu2beta experiments vs the smallest neutrino mass. The two small masses and Majorana phases would conspire to make it difficult to measure this small or vanishing term. Note that the effective mass is not the mass of anything. The neutrino was once thought to be unobservable, so we shall see.

*** 2020 edition of PDG/RPP updates neutrino parameters

The new Review of Particle Physics from the Particle Data Group is available as P.A. Zyla et al. (PDG), Prog. Theor. Exp. Phys. 2020, 083C01 (2020). PDG values of neutrino mixing parameters are [angles are sin^2(), mass differences Delta m^2 in eV^2] theta12 = 0.307 +- 0.013, theta23 = 0.545 +- 0.021, theta13 = 0.0218 +- 0.0008, dm_32 = 2.453 +-0.034 e-3, dm21 = 7.53 +- 0.18 e-5. delta = (1.36 +- 0.17) times pi. https://pdglive.lbl.gov/Particle.action?node=S067&init=0 Compared to 2019, theta23 and dm_32 went up a little while delta went a bit down.

*** Supernova power outage

For a brief amount of time, a supernova has an impressive power output, mostly neutrinos. Just before the big blast, there is a possibly measurable flux of pre-supernova neutrinos, that could be seen by KamLAND. They send out a "presupernova significance" signal which might indicate to other potential supernova neutrino detectors to keep their livetime high, but due to a power outage at KamLAND it was off for part of a weekend in August.
