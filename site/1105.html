*** Recovery from the fire for MINOS

The fire at the Soudan mine did not directly affect MINOS, but a power cable was damaged and the foam used to put out the fire did enter the MINOS lab and bring dirt with it. At the end of May, the MINOS Far Detector magnet coil was returned to full current and is operating normally. Staff at the Soudan Lab together with personnel from the Minnesota Department of Natural Resources and private contractors worked diligently in the last month to clean the lab and get the detector working. However, the NuMI target NT06 failed before MINOS was back on, and only NT01, a restored target which might not take full power is available now. MINOS was running antineutrinos before the fire, while Minerva wants neutrinos, and Fermilab management has announced that MINOS is requested to open the box on anti-neutrinos. MINOS wanted 2e20 more pot after 1.7 shown previously, but now has less than before.

*** Recovery from the NSF decision on DUSEL

The Marx committee, appointed by the DOE to help determine if the DOE should manage the Homestake mine for LBNE in view of the NSF's decision not to proceed with DUSEL, finished their report and gave it to the DOE's Bill Brinkman at a briefing on 1 June. I don't know what it says. Glenn Crawford reported to the Fermilab Users meeting that the briefing "went well" and the result will be made public on 15 June. The DOE has $15M in its FY12 budget for pumping at the mine. See http://www.aip.org/fyi/2011/063.html.

*** DOE secretary Chu, "The recovery is underway."

Appearing at Fermilab and Argonne last week, Secretary of Energy Stephen Chu quoted John Kennedy's message to Congress 25 May 1961. http://www.jfklibrary.org/Research/Ready-Reference/JFK-Speeches/Special-Message-to-the-Congress-on-Urgent-National-Needs-May-25-1961.aspx Much of the speech could be given again today, but a bottom line was to go to the moon. Quotes include, "The first and basic task confronting this nation this year was to turn recession into recovery...The recession has been halted. Recovery is under way." And "I believe that this nation should commit itself to achieving the goal, before this decade is out, of landing a man on the moon and returning him safely to the earth." In Congress, Chu said the NSF decision on DUSEL was "disappointing". http://news.sciencemag.org/scienceinsider/2011/05/chu-calls-nsfs-decision-to-aband.html?ref=ra

*** 2011 Neutrino Summer School in Switzerland

The International Neutrino Summer School 2011 (INSS11) will be hosted 18-30 July 2011 by University of Geneva and CERN in the Geneva countryside. The school curriculum extends from "neutrinos in the Standard Model." to introductions to the most advanced detector and accelerator technologies. The School is open to all neutrino students, undergraduates, graduates or advanced physicists who want to know more. You can request support to participate in the school by following instructions on the web site. See http://dpnc.unige.ch/NeutrinoSummerSchool2011/.

*** MiniBooNE protons

The MiniBooNE experiment, which uses protons from the Fermilab Booster, recently had their best week ever, with 0.13e20 pot in 1 week while running antineutrinos. They have previously shown an anomaly (excess events in the 2 neutrino LSND region with anti-nus with 5.66e20 pot and will show the result from 8.5e20 pot later this year. They expect 10e20 by next year's shutdown.
