Long-Baseline news, August 2000

*** More K2K data

At ICHEP and NNN, K2K showed data for 30% more running than at Nu2000, 2.6 10^19 pot. They report data/no-osc-expect for fully contained 27/40.3; out fidvol 16/17.2 Outer-D contained 10/30.1; Outer-D crossing 13/14.4. The run ended June 22 and will resume January 10, 2001. Earlier data can be found at hep-ex/0008047

*** K2K Near Detector errors

K2K has three near detectors with vastly different systematics and factor of 50 variation in statistics. It is interesting to note that the errors on the predicted event rate from the three detectors are quite similar. They are 40.3 +4.7-4.6 (1kton water), 41.4 +6.2-6.4 (Mu counters); and 40.0 +5.2-5.5 (scint fibers).

*** US Nus and World Report (WIPP)

A similarly named weekly magazine reported on the Carlsbad meeting on page 62 of its June 26 issue, and the possibility of putting an underground physics lab down in the salt mine with nuclear waste. The neutrino background from the 6.2 million ft^3 of waste is negligible. They also hope that a project which demands low radiation will allay public worry about the waste.

*** Is the neutrino better off today than 8 years ago?

It's convention season, so conventional neutrino beams are being looked at. Most calculations suggest that high intensity conventional beams fall short of the capabilities of neutrino factories, but Burt Richter comes to the opposite conclusion in "Conventional Beams or Neutrino Factories: The Next Generation of Accelerator-Based Nu Experiments", hep-ph/0008222 . Another look is Dick et al., "A comparison of the physics potential of future long-baseline nu oscillation experiments", hep-ph/0008016

*** What neutrino mass do you measure/limit in tau decay?

Presumably it's a mixture of nu-2 and nu-3. The same thing is limited in mu decay 100 times better. The PDG plans to clean things like this up by 2002.

*** Push down Ue3 limit in reactors?

The Chooz/Palos Verde reactor limits (~10%) on Ue3 are themselves limited by systematic errors. In a proposal for a two-reactor experiment at Krasnoyarsk (250m & 1100m), many systematic errors cancel and the ratio is controlled down to 0.5%. hep-ex/0008046
