Long-Baseline news, November 2010

*** NOvA reaches milestone

The NOvA Project has met the Level 1 Milestone "Integration Prototype Near Detector (IPND) ready to take data". This milestone was met on November 24, 2010, when the NOvA group was able to synchronize all 6 Data Concentrator Modules in the IPND to the current time, and obtain synchronized cosmic ray track data through the multiple IPND detector blocks. This milestone represents important progress for the NOvA detector, and an intense effort on the part of the NOvA group which will continue as they move into the initial data taking phases. The group is now able to proceed to work with beam triggers and will begin searching for neutrino events in the off-axis NuMI beam.

*** NSF funding delay/denial for 2011 DUSEL

On 2 Dec, the National Science Board's Committee on Program and Plans voted not to recommend the proposed DUSEL bridge award to the full NSB. National Science Foundation staff will be working with their counterparts at DOE, Berkeley, and other stakeholders to assess next steps. This money is $29M in bridge funding to partially cover DUSEL expenses until the start of full NSF funding. The rumored reason is the status of the stewardship model between NSF and DOE. That means who is responsible for each physics component (Neutrino oscillation, double beta decay, dark Matter) when the lab is open. An article in Science before the action, with the colorful title "Scientific Gold Mine or Dicey Money Pit" discusses some of the challenges facing DUSEL. http://www.sciencemag.org/content/330/6006/904.full. An interview with 1 NSB member confirms his main concern is operations costs. http://www.bhpioneer.com/articles/2010/12/09/breaking_news/doc4d011dd5a1965197722158.txt

*** New limit on delta msquared_32 in Particle Data Book

The 2010 Review of Particle Physics (RPP) by the PDG has been published and delivered to most, along with the small pocket book. The best value of dmsq_32 is 2.43 +-0.13 10^-3 eV^2, compared to 1.9-3.0 in 2008. The new value will be replaced in 2012 when the MINOS result shown at Neutrino 2010 is published in a refereed journal. Get the latest http://pdglive.lbl.gov/Rsummary.brl?nodein=S067

*** New limit on theta_13 from Chooz

The limit on theta_13 in the 2010 RPP is sin^2(2-theta_13) < 0.15, compared to .19 in 2008. I've been at meetings where 4 different numbers were called the CHOOZ limit. No, the limit doesn't change but it depends on dmsq_32, and its error. Some quote the limit at a "best fit" dmsq, but since the dependence is asymmetric, that means there is a 50% chance that the true 90% CL limit is worse. The problem was dealt with in the 2008 RPP by quoting theta_13 at the 1 sigma low value of Super-K's dmsq, and putting the best fit limit in a footnote. In 2010, the RPP uses the bet fit value, and the 1 sigma low value is in a footnote. New results, which have to deal with delta and hierarchy, only analyze at one dmsq. The right way would be a global fit; PDG doesn't encode global fits.

*** SNIF: A Futuristic Neutrino Probe for Undeclared ... Reactors

The SNIF (Secret Neutrino Interactions Finder) concept proposes to use a few hundred thousand tons neutrino detectors to unveil clandestine fission reactors. Beyond previous studies it provides estimates of all known background sources as a function of the detector's longitude, latitude and depth, and we discuss how they impact the detectability. arXiv:1011.3850
