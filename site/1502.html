Long-Baseline news, February 2015

*** Nakaya and Kobayashi win Nishima prize

Professor T. Kobayashi (KEK) and professor T. Nakaya (Kyoto) were awarded the 2014 Nishina Memorial prize for "Discovery of electron neutrino appearance from muon neutrino beam", in T2K. http://www.nishina-mf.or.jp/index_e.html

*** TOE wins Oscar, XOE loses naming contest for ELBNF

Eddie Redmayne won the best actor Oscar for the portrayal of Stephen Hawking in the movie "The Theory of Everything". But XOE, or eXperiment Of Everything, was one of the 100 names not chosen as a finalist to replace ELBNF, at the new Long-Baseline Facility. Other losers were TEFKAL (The Experiment Formerly Known As LBNE/ LBNO), NERDS (NEutrino Research DetectorS), I90 (for the highway in South Dakota), HEArT (Homestake Electron appearance using an Ar Tpc), and E1062. The final 10 are ALADIN, ATHENA, BISON (with two acronyms), BLADE, DUNE, GOLDEN, LEONARDO, LOLA and NEBULA.

*** Shanahan wins NOvA election for cospokesperson

Peter Shanahan from Fermilab has been elected co-spokesperson of the NOvA experiment as it takes long-baseline neutrino data using the NuMI beam. He replaces Harvard's Gary Feldman who is stepping down and will work with Mark Messier from Indiana University.

*** The winners for ELBNF cospokesperson will be

2 of six candidates, Sanjib Mishra from South Carolina, Milind Diwan from BNL, Chang Kee Jung from Stony Brook, Andre Rubbia from Zurich, Mark Thomson from Cambridge, and Bob Wilson from CSU.

*** It will be a win-win at WIN 2015

The Weak Interactions and Neutrinos Workshop will be 8-13 June in Heidelberg. http://www.mpi-hd.mpg.de/WIN2015/ This workshop, held alternately with the International neutrino conference, will examine the crucial issues of our time; i.e. neutrino physics and related fields. The program is posted with parallel sessions on Electroweak, Neutrino, Flavor, and Astroparticle Physics. Those interested should contact the conveners listed on the web page.

*** Fermilab Short-baseline program wins Stage 1 approval

The Fermilab PAC wrote: "The Committee recommends Stage 1 approval for the SBN program, which incorporates LAr1ND and ICARUS with MicroBooNE towards a coherent SBN program." It also recommended approval for ANNIE, to put a small detector with LAPPDs in the SciBooNE hall for neutrino induced neutron measurements. While the PAC did not endorse NESSIE, it did endorse the physics goals of the CAPTAIN-MINERvA and CAPTAIN-BNB initiatives to use a liquid argon tank built by Los Alamos in the booster and NuMI beams. http://www.fnal.gov/directorate/program_planning/Jan2015Public/PAC-2015Jan_final.pdf

*** Majorana or Dirac?

Of more interest to the 2015 neutrino community is whether the neutrino is Majorana (its own antiparticle) or Dirac. Neutrino Oscillation Experiments cannot distinguish this property, but neutrinoless double Beta decay experiments can. If the neutrino ordering is inverted or degenerate AND if the neutrino is Majorana, this process will happen at predictable rates (up to nuclear matrix elements) and could be seen soon. If the neutrino is Dirac, it won't happen. If the mass hierarchy is normal, it may be too small to be seen for the foreseeable future.

*** Majorana in South Dakota

The Majorana Demonstrator project, funded by DOE Nuclear Physics and the NSF aims to establish the feasibility to construct arrays of Germanium detectors and demonstrate backgrounds low enough to justify a ton-scale experiment in neutrinoless double beta decay. It's located at the 4850 level of SURF and will deploy 10 kg of natural Ge and 30 kg of 86% 76Ge. Phase I of 7 strings were deployed last year, and Phase II with 7 more strings should be up this year. Gerda Phase II will also take data in 2015.
