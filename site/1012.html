Long-Baseline news, December 2010

*** CERN yellow book 2010-03 on neutrinos

The proceedings of the CERN workshop on European Strategy for future neutrino physics held on Oct 1-3 2009 is now out. See: http://cdsweb.cern.ch/record/1240330/files/cern-2010-003.pdf It includes also the summary published by the Neutrino Panel of the CERN Scientific Policy Committee in March 2010. This is an excellent source to understand the current state of neutrino physics and how Europe might contribute in the future.

*** Antineutrinos in NOvA

NOvA has seen its first neutrinos in the NDOS (Near Detector on the Surface) which is running in the way-off-axis NuMI beam. In 2013 the far detector will be commissioned in Ash River Minnesota, and a near detector at a similar off-axis angle will be running underground at Fermilab.

*** Antineutrinos in Double Chooz

The Double Chooz far detector is now filled and running. Here is a link to a press release on the subject. http://doublechooz.in2p3.fr/Status_and_News/Press/Press_release_Dec2010.pdf

*** Neutrinos in the completed Ice Cube detector

On the 18th of December, just before 6 PM South Pole local time, IceCube installed its 86th and final string of photomultipliers deep in Antarctic ice. This ended a long journey that began with the installation of the first string in January 2005. Each string of the completed array holds 60 PMT's. 80 of the strings have nominal 17m PMT spacings, while 6 (the Deep Core subarray) are more densely packed. IceCube plans to take data for 15 years.

*** Neutrinos in CNGS/Opera

In 2010 the SPS delivered 4.03e19 protons on target to the CNGS. The overall efficiency for the accelerator complex was 82%. The OPERA detector ran smoothly with an efficiency close to 100% and collected 4250 events in the target during 187 days of run. The 2011 run is expected to be Mar-Nov. Extrapolating the 2010 performance, OPERA should collect in 2011 a number of pot larger than 2010. The analysis of the 08-09 data set is in progress.

*** More Antineutrinos for MiniBooNE

The request for continued MiniBooNE antineutrino running was granted by the Fermilab PAC. No exact POT goal was given, but it is open ended, so they will run as long as taking the extra data is deemed useful, i.e. 10E20 POT or more. Their goal is 15e20 pot by Feb 2013, and 10e20 is likely by the 2012 shutdown. http://www.fnal.gov/directorate/program_planning/Nov2010PACPublic/PAC_Report_Nov_2010.pdf

*** LBNE Project solves 2 problems; faces 3 more

The LBNE executive committee recommended that the reference design for CD1; i.e. one 200 kt Water Cherenkov Detector plus one 17 kt Liquid Argon Detector was the preferable one for physics. Also, a document representing a consensus view of collaboration management, collaboration leadership, lab management and the DOE has been reached on the prioritized physics goals. The primary objectives are 1.1 Search for an precision measurement of numu to nue oscillations, including theta_13, phase delta and mass ordering; 1.2 Precision measurements of theta_23 and Delta msquared_32; 1.3 proton decay; 1.4 neutrinos from a core collapse supernova in our galaxy, if there is one. Secondary objectives, additional secondary objectives, and primary and secondary objectives of the near detector are also specified. But LBNE faces 3 near-term challenges: 1.) Issues related to DUSEL funding and NSF/DOE cooperation; 2.) The cost estimate. Detailed costings of the beam and detectors exceed the upper bound of ~$1B given by the DOE. Value engineering/ cost cutting while maintaining the scientific capabilities are in progress. 3.) There is no FY2011 budget yet, and this may (or may not) get coupled with a "no new starts" this year.

The 2011 Neutrino summer school, originally scheduled for LNGS on 18-30 Jul, is moved to somewhere in Switzerland, a small country. *** Suspicious Neutrino Conference

In even numbered years, there is a neutrino meeting sponsored by the International Neutrino Commission. In odd-numbered years there is a Weak Interactions and Neutrino (WIN) workshop. But a web page http://www.waset.org/conferences/2011/rome/icnpa/ has appeared from a publishing company about an "International Conference on Neutrino Physics and Astrophysics". (WASET) It looks like a for-profit meeting, similar to several I see in unwanted email blasts. There is no link to any scientific secretariat who knows anything about neutrinos. So I won't link it to the neutrino meetings page unless someone vouches for it. The paper submission deadline has passed anyway.
