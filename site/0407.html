*** MINOS getting closer

The MINOS experiment has been taking cosmic ray data with its far detector & is in the process of completing its near detector and (NuMI) beam line. The experiment showed 37 contained and 48 throughgoing nu induced events at Neutrino 2004, and hopes to measure the charge ratio soon. With atmospheric muons, there is a sharp moon shadow. Near detector installation has 260/280 planes installed, and will be done during the Olympics. The Main Injector has run with 6 batches and 2.7 10**13 ppp.

*** THESEUS dies again

THE Second Experiment Underground at Soudan, aka Soudan 2, was an early player in the long-baseline neutrino planning process. MINOS recently considered again the costs, merit and manpower requirements for running this 1 kiloton fine-grained calorimeter in the NuMI beam. Having decided no, modules are being scrapped.

*** ADONIS new for supernovae

A new collaboration is considering a supernova experiment using lead and scintillator, A Detector Of NeutrInos from Supernovae. Proposed: 6x6x6 m**3, 466 ton lead, 81% fiducial volume for supernova nus. The signal will be nu-e CC and neutron(s) from NC. Previously only nuebars have been seen from Supernovae.

*** ICARUS

This 600 ton liquid argon experiment to be installed in the Gran Sasso is part of the Cern Neutrinos to Gran Sasso (CNGS) program and also called CNGS2. 27,000 cosmic ray triggers were collected during an 01 run. At the LNGS Director's mandate, a working group reviewed cryogenics, safety, installation, commissioning & operation of T600 in the lab along with a risk analysis of possible failures. They recommended improvements to the cryogenics and concluded the overall risk linked to LNGS activities will not increase when T600 becomes operational. The schedule is a dry test 9/04 and filling 10/05.

*** Athens in MINOS

The University of Athens joined MINOS in 1998 (after COSMOS was cancelled) and has participated in various MINOS activities: 4-plane prototype readout and analysis, initial design efforts of mu monitoring for the nu beam, testing and characterization of Hamamatsu M16 PMTs for the MINOS FAR detector (in collaboration with UT Austin), and MINOS FAR detector commissioning. The Athens group is currently participating in the commissioning of the MINOS NEAR detector, while preparing for physics analysis, an effort involving developing software for neutrino event classification in the MINOS detectors, using Artificial Neural Network techniques.

*** NESTOR

The Neutrino Extended Submarine Telescope with Oceanographic Research has 745 atmospheric muons at a depth of 3800 m in its site near Pylos Greece. They used one NESTOR star with 12 optical modules in March 03. They plan to run 4 floors in autumn 2004 and full tower deployment in autumn 2006.

*** ANTARES

This array of phototubes in the Mediterranean is located near Toulon France at a depth of 2500 meters. A prototype sector line with 5 storeys of optical modules operated in 2003, along with a Mini Instrumentation Line with various devices to study the water. They recovered the prototype and will redeploy in 2004. Full detector will be deployed in 2005-2007. Presently the detector design has 10 strings, each 400 m high with 30 storeys spaced vertically by 16 m, and 60 m between strings.

*** MiniBooNE horn sets world record; then fails

After 100,000,000 pulses, MiniBooNE's horn suffered a ground fault July 24. It pulsed an order of magnitude more than any other! They may take horn-off data til an available replacement is used.
