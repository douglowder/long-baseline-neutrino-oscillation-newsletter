*** DES measures some neutrino parameters

DES has its 3 year data where it analyzed large-scale structure combining three two-point correlation functions (3x2pt). DES alone doesn't distinguish between the sum nu mass and the matter density. Combining with DES 3x2pt data, other low-redshift data, and Planck CMB, they find s=m1+m2+m3 < 130 meV (95%CL). See the recent preprint in arXiv:2105.13549.

*** rant - understanding neutrino parameters from cosmology

In the normal (inverted) mass order, s=m1+m2+m3 > 59 (100) meV. If as DES says, s < 130 meV, degenerate solutions are impossible. But DES "...model(s) massive neutrinos as three degenerate species of equal mass." It's been 34 years since we suspected neutrino masses were different and 23 years since that was accepted. Why don't cosmology "measurements" of neutrino parameters do it right?

*** MINERvA Data Preservation Project

MINERνA has a huge amount of data, different nuclear targets etc., so it has started a dedicated effort in order to preserve the collaboration’s data so it remains usable into the DUNE era. It involves a master AnaTuple: A unified ROOT file that contains low- and high-level reconstruction objects used to reproduce MINERνA’s results, and MAT, a framework with functionalities that allow easy cross-section extractions centralized across an experiment.

*** Comment on "Observation of a Nu Burst from the SN1987A"'

It is 34 years after the fact, and we hear about possible deadtime caused by tape writing errors in Kamiokande from Y. Oyama in arXiv:2108.01783 . It's interesting but he doesn't mention that all of his probabilities are a-posteriori (not really meaningful) & he combines them by multiplying them together which is wrong.
