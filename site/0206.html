Long-Baseline news, June 2002

*** New results from K2K

The KEK to Kamioka Long-Baseline Neutrino Oscillation Experiment recently reported updated results which provide increased confidence in the existence of neutrino oscillations. The new results represent all data accumulated by K2K from April 1999 to June 2001. http://neutrino.kek.jp/news/2002.06.12 (in Japanese), k2k0206.pdf (in English)

*** The next step in Neutrino Oscillation Measurements

A Fermilab neutrino study group came up with a physics statement which attempted to represent a consensus among neutrino physicists about our goals for future experiments. The text is in http://www.fnal.gov/projects/muon_collider/nu/study/fnal-nu-exp/new2.ps In summary, theta_13, theta_13, theta_13.

*** Neutrino Facilities Advisory Committee Roadmap

The National Science Foundation is considering support for Ice-Cube, but many other future neutrino facilities, such as NUSL and others are also under consideration. A panel was appointed to get the neutrino picture and they met June 24th to look at double beta decay experiments, the underground lab, long-baseline experiments, neutrino telescopes, etc. Originally to be chaired by George Trilling, Barry Barish ran the meeting. A workshop (next item) and this NRC study will provide information needed for funding agencies, OSTP, OMB, and Congress to develop a road map for this research in the context of planned investigations worldwide.

*** NESS 2002

The International Workshop on Neutrinos and Subterranean Science, NeSS 2002, will be held in Washington D.C., 19-21 Sep 02. NeSS 2002 will be a major workshop designed to review and integrate the results of many of the recent developments in neutrino, low background and geo-science investigations requiring great subterranean depth. The genesis of the workshop is a statement found in the fiscal year 2003 budget request for the NSF.

*** Yang Neutrino Workshop 11-13 October 2002 at Stonybrook

A conference on Neutrinos and Implications for Physics Beyond the Standard Model will cover experimental and theoretical neutrino topics, including oscillations but also grand unified models & extra dimensions. Links to these and other meetings can be found at http//neutrinooscillation.org/numeetings.html

*** MINOS dedication July 2

A grand opening ceremony for the Soudan Underground Lab, in part to thank the State of Minnesota for its support of the MINOS cavity construction, is taking place 2 July. 1/2 of the MINOS far-detector planes are up (248 mounted 6/24), & more than half of the magnet windings for the 1st supermodule are installed. At the FNAL end, Chicago Bridge & Iron, NuMI Decay Pipe subcontractor to S. A. Healy, transported the 56th and final piece of the decay pipe underground on 6/27/02. It is all welded together.

*** Is solar chi-squared too good?

Global solar neutrino fits favor the LMA, but SMA and LOW do not have bad chi-squared. The LMA best fit chi-squared may be too good. This may be because experiments overestimate systematic errors. What is P(every experiment reports their errors right)? (e.g. hep-ph/0205241 ) Also see Fogli et al., hep-ph/0206162

*** Totsuka wins Fujiwara Prize

Prof.Y.Totsuka won the 2002 Fujiwara prize for "Discovery of Nu Oscillation from Observations of Atmospheric and Solar Neutrinos." The Fujiwara prize is an annual prize given since 1960 by the Fujiwara Science Fund to a scientist who made an outstanding contribution to the development of science and technology. The fund was established privately by Ginjiro Fujiwara, the "paper-manufacturing king" of Japan.
