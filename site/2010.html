Long-Baseline news, October 2020

*** Sanford Underground Research Facility selected historic site

The APS added SURF to its list of historic sites for Ray Davis' work on solar neutrinos. From 1962 to 1994, he built and operated the first successful detector for solar neutrinos using John N. Bahcall’s theoretical model and working with William A. Fowler, Maurice Goldhaber, and numerous engineers and crew members on the 4850 Level of the Homestake Mine. That location is now the Davis Campus at the Sanford Underground Research Facility. https://www.aps.org/programs/outreach/history/historicsites/

*** Super-Kamiokande expands fiducial volume

For two nucleon decay modes, Super-K increased the mass that was analyzed 22.5 to 27.2 kton, with a small increase in potential background. This could help other analyses. arXiv:2010.16098

*** Panofsky award to Sobel and Kearns

Henry Sobel at UC Irvine and Edward Kearns at Boston University were awarded the 2021 Panofsky Prize in Experimental Particle Physics. "For pioneering and leadership contributions to large underground experiments for the discovery of neutrino oscillations and sensitive searches for baryon number violation."

*** Moore award to Strigari and Martoff

Louis Strigari from Texas A&M and C. Jeff Martoff from Temple University were recipients of the Gordon & Betty Moore Foundation Fundamental Physics Innovation Awards for their neutrino work. https://www.aps.org/programs/innovation/moore/recipients.cfm

*** neutrino road

In July 1962, L. Lederman et al. published in PRL "Observation of High-Energy Neutrino Reactions and the Existence of Two Kinds of Neutrinos", showing their understanding of hyphens and showing the existence of the muon neutrino. On 7 Oct. 2020, the Chicago City Council passed ordinance S02020-107 authorizing and directing the Commissioner of Transportation to take the actions necessary to honorarily designate 33rd Street from State Street to LaSalle Street as, "Leon Lederman Way" where it crosses the IIT campus.

*** old neutrinos

While we expect 330 neutrinos per cubic centimeter of the universe left over from the big bang, there should be a smaller number of supernova relic neutrinos from the O(10^18) supernovae that have gone off in the visible universe to date. The current background dominated limit from Super-K is about twice the most optimistic expected flux. By tagging neutrons, Super-K Gd and Hyper-K Gd should be able to greatly improve that search. See: https://indico.fnal.gov/event/46258/contributions/201118/attachments/136789/170260/nakajima_2020-10-29_snowmass.pdf

*** Interesting Letters of Interest; v2

- https://snowmass21.org/

  • SNOWMASS21-062 "The ESS neutrino Super Beam Design Study ESSnuSB and the High-Intensity Frontier Initiative HIFI" With a 400 m circumference accumulator ring to compress the duration of each ESS pulse, it proposes that an intense neutrino superbeam could be utilized to improve systematic error in measuring CP violation with a detector at the 2nd oscillation max.
  • THEIA-1l78 "Long-Baseline Neutrinos at THEIA" It proposes a water-based liquid scintillator detector at SURF able to simultaneously detect scintillation light and Cherenkov light. It could be the 4th detector at DUNE.
  • Phil_Barbeau-067 "Far-Future COHERENT physics program at the SNS" COHERENT plans to have further high-statistics configurations. Recently, they had success with their first 2 CEvNS measurements with CsI and Ar. They plan to add detectors within the Second Trial Station (STS) at the SNS.
  • LauraFields-165 "Nu Scattering Measurements on H & Deuterium" It proposes a 1 ton hydrogen and/or deuterium based detector focusing on measuring neutrino-nucleon amplitudes to be built at DUNE. It would be a secondary target to search for new physics. Several ideas for experimental configurations are discussed.
  • SNOplus-186 "Detecting Antineutrinos from Distant Reactors using Pure Water at SNO+" Using the impressive efficiency of SNO+ to detect neutrons, it can measure three Canadian nuclear reactors' operations through identification of inverse beta decay at this water Cherenkov detector currently running. ~6 IBDs per year.
