Long-Baseline news, October 2011

*** First data from ArgoNeuT

This 47 x 40 x 90 cm^3 TPC has its first result on the measurement of the inclusive muon neutrino CC cross section on Argon. arXiv:1111.0103.

*** INO gets land from Tamil Nadu

The India-based Neutrino Observatory has passed another hurdle, getting a land donation from the state of Tamil Nadu for the cavern and its surface laboratory. They also need to arrange for the purchase of some land for an institute at Madurai.

*** Invisibles... for neutrinos, dark matter & dark energy physics

Invisibles is a new European ITN project (FP7-PEOPLE-2011-ITN), which focuses on Neutrino and Dark Matter phenomenology and their connection. Experimental and theoretical aspects are also encompassed. The network involves nodes in seven European countries and its associated partners extend to seven non-European countries. http://www.invisibles.eu/

*** Blog site for high intensity workshop (30 Nov-2 Dec Rockville)

To enable discussion of opinions and ideas for neutrinos at the intensity frontier in advance of the Rockville meeting, we have set up a discussion website: http://neutrinowg.phy.duke.edu/ If you want to initiate a post, please contact one of the neutrino working group conveners (Andre De Gouvea, Kevin Pitts, Kate Scholberg or Sam Zeller). Anyone can make comments on the posts, although comments will be moderated. They will publish all non-anonymous comments from the community.

*** HEPAP news

Neutrinos were a big part of the October 2011 U.S. HEPAP meeting. http://science.energy.gov/hep/hepap/meetings/previous-meetings/hepap-agenda-october-2011/ A. Lankford reported on the NRC DUSEL study, which looked at the physics value of LBNE, dark matter and 0nu 2beta experiments. He reported that "The value of the whole is greater than the sum of its parts" and that "The cost of the whole is less than the sum of its parts." H. Weerts reported on the upcoming Intensity Workshop.

*** LBNE report and technology decision

The 2010 Physics Working Group Report prepared by the (now 320 member) LBNE collaboration and edited by Bob Wilson (Col State) has appeared on the ArXiV. http://arxiv.org/abs/1110.6249 More recent "case studies" detailing the water and argon choices for the technology decision will be released soon. Those documents focus on the physics capabilities. A description of the full experiment including costs is being developed by the project as the CD1 report. The current schedule calls for the technology choice to be made in December 2011. http://lbne.fnal.gov/project/milestones.shtml

*** More on neutrino velocity

According to the New Scientist, OPERA will repeat its neutrino velocity measurements with shorter pulses from the SPS before it submits its abstract for publication http://www.newscientist.com/article/dn21093-fasterthanlight-neutrino-result-to-get-extra-checks.html

*** Call for Neutrino Astrophysics papers

A Special Issue on "Frontiers in Neutrino Astrophysics," will be published in Advances in High Energy Physics in July 2012. You can find the Call for Papers for this Special Issue at http://www.hindawi.com/journals/ahep/si/fna/ and the deadline for submission is 6 Jan 2012. The Special Issue is open to both original research articles as well as review articles.

*** Raju Raghavan 1937-2011

Raju was a creative and outstanding physicist most recently known for his ideas on solar neutrino detection in LENS (Low Energy Neutrino Spectroscopy) See http://cnp.phys.vt.edu/cnp-bin/raju.pl
