Long-Baseline news, December 2023

*** Report from The Particle Physics Project Prioritization Panel

https://www.usparticlephysics.org/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/P5Report2023_120723-DRAFT_single-pages.pdf P5 was charged with developing a 10-year strategic plan for US particle physics, in the context of a 20-year global strategy & 2 constrained budget scenarios. The report was organized into 3 science themes and 2 science drivers/theme. In the baseline budget scenario, for 2023-27, funding is as assumed in the 2022 CHIPS Act of 2022, followed by 3% increases each year. The less favorable scenario assumes 2% increases per year through 2033. The Neutrino Frontier suggests that physicists endorse the P5 report. Go to https://forms.gle/SGceKyyR11ccci7g6.

*** P5 DUNE

The highest recommendation was to reaffirm the conclusions from the previous P5 on the first phase of DUNE and PIP-II, as well as NOvA, SBN and T2K. DUNE was called the centerpiece of our program to reveal the mysteries of neutrinos. In the constrained scenario, elements of DUNE would be delayed or descoped. This would negatively impact the US's international reputation in HEP. The 2nd recommendation was to plan and start a list of programs of which the 2nd priority was a re-envisioned second phase of DUNE with an early implementation of an enhanced 2.1 MW beam, a third far detector, and an upgraded near detector. Besides the six "recommendations", there were 18 "area recommendations", two of which supported addressing Booster reliability risks through the 1st 10 y of DUNE operation, and DOE funding of SURF expansion.

*** P5 Ice-Cube

Ice-Cube-Gen2 was the 5th priority for study of nu properties using non-beam nus complementary to DUNE, and for indirect high mass Dark Matter searches using neutrinos as a tool.

*** P5 Muon Collider

To invest in the future, Rec #4 proposes to support vigorous R&D toward a cost-effective 10 TeV pCM collider based on proton, muon, or possible wakefield technologies, as well as a plan to improve the FNAL accelerator complex including neutrinos, flavor, and a 10 TeV pCM collider.

*** A theory with predictions for m1 and delta_CP

It is nice when a theory actually makes predictions and multiply nice when it makes multiple predictions. In arXiv:2312.14096, Babu, Mohapatra & Thapa suggest a GUT based on the gauge symmetry SU(5)_L x SU(5)_R with parity interchanging the two factor groups. It predicts the CP phase to be ±(130.4 ± 1.2) deg and m1 = (4.8 − 8.4) meV. There is no 0-nu 2-beta decay nor need for an axion.

*** Countries sign MOU to contribute to the construction of DUNE

Brazil, Canada CERN, France, Italy, Switzerland and the UK have signed a memorandum of understanding to provide hardware for the 1st 2 modules of DUNE. Also expected are Spain & Czech Republic. https://news.fnal.gov/2023/12/international-science-organizations

*** Rant: Authorship issues: Authors without borders

Experimental collaborations in particle physics each have a set of authorship rules which, in my experience, tend to be egalitarian. However, given the size and productivity of our colleagues, I suspect that in our field on average we have not read a majority of our own papers. The Scientific Research Honor Society Sigma Xi has prepared a study, "Authors Without Borders: Guidelines for Discussing Authorship with Collaborators", which addresses many of the resulting issues. https://online.fliphtml5.com/oaiev/cipo/

*** DUNE cavern construction nearly complete

https://listen.sdpb.org/science/2023-12-20/massive-caverns-for-dune-neutrino-project-nearly-excavated With 800,000 tons of rock out of the way, two large caverns (20 m wide, 151 m long, 28 m tall) for 4 ten kiloton fiducial detectors should be complete in January 2024. Cryostat installation is expected to take two years each.

*** Vertical Drift Technology for DUNE's second cryostat

The plan DUNE's 2nd detector is to use vertical drift technology. The technical design (TDR) for a liquid argon detector using this technology is available at arXiv:2312.03130. A design report for the cryostats can be found at arXiv:2312.09104.
