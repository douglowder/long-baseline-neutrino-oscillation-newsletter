Long-Baseline news, April 2017

*** NuEclipse 21 August 2017

A workshop on physics that can be done with neutrino experiments at the Spallation Neutron Source at Oak Ridge National Laboratory will take place 20-22 August in Knoxville TN. They have also arranged for a nearby total eclipse of the sun on 21 August, and will have an excursion to view it on that day. No flash photos. https://sites.duke.edu/nueclipse/

*** Neutrino Day 8 July 2017

South Dakota isn't on the path of the eclipse, but if you attend the Neutrino Day activities at Sanford Underground Research Facility on 7-8 July, you can then drive into the path of the August eclipse. See http://www.sanfordlab.org/neutrinoday2017

*** Neutrino Shadow?

When the moon is in front of the sun, will that affect any solar neutrino experiments? Not hardly. I was asked this month if the shadow of the moon had ever been seen in a neutrino experiment. I quickly answered no. The earth is opaque to UHE neutrinos, but the flux is small.

*** NuFACT 25-30 September 2017 in Uppsala

If you've not won a Nobel prize, you want to book your ticket to Stockholm anyway for the 2017 International Workshop on Neutrinos from Accelerators (NuFACT2017). Expect a full neutrino program. Registration is open at https://indico.uu.se/e/nufact2017

*** KATRIN first light Back in October, the KArlruhe TRItium Neutrino (KATRIN)

experiment guided its first electrons through the 70 m beamline. It will be sensitive to a combination of neutrino mass and mixing angles at 350 meV. http://www.kit.edu/kit/20624.php (German)

*** When does DUNE reach 1000 people?

A thousand is an arbitrary milestone for a physics collaboration, but it an unprecedented one for a neutrino experiment. There are currently 966 DUNE collaborators. But the number of names in the DUNE database currently stands at 1001, including people involved in the collaboration who are not collaborators. Plus there are another 143 former collaborators who have moved on since the beginning of the collaboration in early 2015.

*** Neutrino Question of the week

The Fermilab Neutrino Physics Center is launching the "Neutrino Question of the Week". The first question is "Why does the sun produce neutrinos while nuclear reactors produce antineutrinos?" Answer at http://npc.fnal.gov/question-week-page/

*** LArSoft Tools and Technology Workshop 20 June

LArSoft is a software framework for liquid Argon detectors that current and future neutrino experiments will use. A free one-day workshop on Tue 20 June at Fermilab will include lectures, demonstrations and tutorials as well as new tools in LArSoft. http://larsoft.org/register/

*** U.S. budget for HEP for the rest of 2017; more for LBNF

There have been many numbers publicized recently for HEP and the Office of Science in the US DOE's budget. There was President Obama's proposed budget, a house budget, a senate budget, a Trump budget proposal, all for fiscal year 2017, as well as proposals for 2018. But in the final approved 17 budget, LBNF received "$5 million more than requested..." according to AIP's FYI: http://aip-info.org/1ZJN-4X3S5-E29E6Q-2LZ1QS-1/c.aspx
