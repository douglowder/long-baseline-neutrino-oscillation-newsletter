Long-Baseline news, June 2020

*** Virtual Neutrino 2020 great success

Neutrino 2020, originally scheduled for downtown Chicago with an estimate of 800 participants, took place online with (halfway through) 4260 registrants. That dwarfs the 2476 current subscribers to this newsletter. Registration was open to the public. Future meeting organizers will have to gauge the relative value and costs of remote, in-person and hybrid meetings. Popular features were the Slack channel for questions and comments, and virtual reality rooms. Slides and videos are available at https://indico.fnal.gov/event/43209/timetable/#20200622.detailed if you registered. Thanks to organizers Sam Zeller, Marvin Marshak & Stephen Brice!

*** New at Nu2020 - Final three-flavor results from MINOS/MINOS+

Final results on neutrino mixing parameters from MINOS and MINOS+ were presented at Nu2020 and in arXiv:2006.15208 . (It was for MINOS that this newsletter started in May 1992.) With 21e20 protons on target and 61 kt-yr of atmospheric neutrinos, it reports Dm^2_32 = 2.40+0.08-0.09 (2.45+0.07-0.08) 10^(-3) eV^2 and sin^2(2q_23) = 0.43+0.20-0.04 (0.42+0.07-0.03) at 68%CL for the normal (inverted) neutrino mass order.

*** New at Nu2020 - Solar cycle CNO neutrinos seen for first time

Borexino presented the first evidence for solar CNO neutrinos at Nu2020 and in arXiv:2006.15115 . This process, whose nus were previously unmeasured, is expected to provide 1% of the sun's energy, with a theoretical uncertainty of 28%. The measured flux of CNO nus was 7.0 +3-2 e8 cm^-2 s^-1. To establish the signal, control of backgrounds from Bismuth were needed: best fit 7.2 cpd/100 ton.

*** New at Nu2020 - supernova relic neutrino limit from Super-K

At Neutrino 2020, Super-K provided a new limit on the flux of supernova neutrinos from the sum of all previous supernovae in the universe of < 2.7 cm^-2/s (90%CL) for E > 16 MeV. There were many new limits presented, but this one is notable because the model prediction of 1.7 cm^-2/s is within a factor of two. It is also called Diffuse Supernova Neutrino Background, but we need a signal before it can become a background. See nu2020 poster 231. This result will improve when SK starts running with Gd. The addition of Gd to Super-K, which will tag neutrons, just started.

In particular, they should continue to collaborate with partners towards the successful implementation of LBNF and DUNE. https://home.cern/sites/home.web.cern.ch/files/2020-06/2020%20Update%20European%20Strategy.pdf

*** Underground construction of DUNE area begins

On 23 June, the first blast took place 3650 feet below the surface at the Sanford Underground Research Facility. 3000 tons of rock will be removed to place a drill, which will be used to remove the 800,000 tons of rock at the 4650 level. https://www.symmetrymagazine.org/article/DUNE-moves-to-the-next-stage-with-a-blast

*** DUNE papers

The physics potential of DUNE, as detailed in the TDR, is being provided in several publication-quality papers which are starting to appear in the arXiv. arXiv:2006.16043 covers the long-baseline physics potential. There's a paper on CVN in arXiv:2006.15052. Papers will come soon on Supernova nus, nucleon decay/beyond-SM physics, and ProtoDUNE.

*** Xenon1T observes excess of low-energy electrons

The Xenon1T dark matter experiment ( http://www.xenon1t.org/) sees an excess of electrons below 5 keV in 227 days of data. It might be a background but more interesting hypotheses include solar axions or nu magnetic moment. They both give good fits, but with parameters in tension with other observations. arXiv:2006.09721

*** Comments on Neutrino-4 reactor-based sterile nu "signal"

The results of Neutrino-4 in arXiv:2005.05301 finding a 7 eV sterile nu were the subject of skepticism in arXiv:2006.13147 The authors of that preprint describe aspects of both the data and analysis method that might lead to an oscillation signature arising from a null experiment. Neutrino-4 answers in arXiv:2006.13639 .
