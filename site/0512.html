Long-Baseline news, December 2005

*** In Memoriam, Doug Michael

Doug Michael passed away on Christmas Day after a one year battle with cancer. A cospokesman of MINOS, he was a tireless dedicated physicist, whose loss is that much greater because it seems he is too young to report our loss of him. I heard someone describe him as a jet plane, while we are traveling in helicopters. Instead of flowers, donations have been requested for the LYMPHOMA Research Foundation in memory of Dr. Douglas Grant Michael. http://www.lymphoma.org/site/pp.asp?c=bfIKIVMIG&b=39065

*** CD0 for double beta decay, reactor and long-baseline neutrinos

In response to the interest expressed in the critical design 0 on 3 neutrino physics topics, the DOE has established a web page: http://www.science.doe.gov/hep/newinitiatives.shtm The long- baseline CD0 options include 1) NOvA, 2)T2K, 3) do nothing. Reactor options are 1) Double Chooz, 2) Daya Bay, 3) Braidwood, 4) do nothing. Request for CD0 for a high-intensity neutrino beam for neutrino CP-violation experiments will shortly follow.

*** MINERvA is working on CD1 & 2

DOE gave responsibility for MINERvA's CD1 to Fermilab in lieu of having a "Lehman" review. After a successful Fermilab CD1 Director's Review in December, MINERvA is preparing to submit the other documents required for CD1 early in 2006. There will be another Director's Review in April 06 and a DOE CD2/3 June 06.

*** Daya Bay Reactor Neutrino Project

Daya Bay has begun bore-hole drilling which should take about two months to complete. The Institute of High Energy Physics and several Chinese Institutions have awarded $2 million Chinese dollars from NSFC for carrying out R&D activities for the Daya Bay project a few months ago. This grant is good for one year.

*** RENO funded in Korea for $9M

In December 2005, the Congress in Korea approved the $9M that was requested for RENO, the Reactor Neutrino Oscillation Experiment. The collaboration so far consists of 10 institutions from Korea.

*** Combinations of Reactor and Accelerator Neutrino Measurements

When theta_13 is measured by both reactor and accelerator experiments, it will tell us much more about nu mixing than precise measurements by 1 technique alone. A recent preprint by Mahn and Shaevitz shows some examples. hep-ex/0409028

*** Fermilab reports low tritium in NuMI water to neighbors

Fermilab surface water ponds look different this year, as they prepare to deal with a problem with zebra mussels, which is driving the timing of the next accelerator shutdown. In early December, the lab notified neighbors of a small tritium release, well below regulatory standards, into Indian Creek. Pumping from the NuMI tunnel brings tritiated water to the surface, protecting the underlying aquifer. Fermilab has taken measures to bring Indian Creek back to a level so low that it cannot be detected.

*** ICARUS T1200 canceled

A 300 ton liquid Argon detector was tested at Pavia in 2001 (T300) and ICARUS had planned a physics program based on the 600 ton detector and 2 units of 1200 tons (T3000). After the Borexino accident, all Argon activities were suspended till Sep 04. T600 will proceed with installation in March 2006. Major orders on T1200.1 have been canceled and T1200.2 is postponed. INFN had proposed 20M euro for 85% of T1200 for CNGS2, but now ICARUS is unable to fulfill the deliverables. T600 may continue as an INFN supported technical tool for advance tests.

*** First neutrino seminar at Olin, new engineering college

What would you do with $464M? In a December 20 Wall Street Journal Article, the new Olin Engineering College is described. That college had its first seminar on neutrinos in November. One of many things that makes OLIN unique is that 2/3 of the physics faculty is currently pregnant. neutrinooscillation.org/olin.html
