Long-Baseline news, February 2021

*** Two good candidates for DUNE spokesman

There was just an election for DUNE cospokesperson between Regina Rameika and Mary Bishai. Rameika was elected and will serve with continuing cospokesperson Stefan Soldner-Rembold. This was the first time I voted in a preferential election with two candidates. Thanks to Ed Blucher for his work on DUNE.

*** Four good candidates for NOvA cospokesperson

The collaboration is choosing among Jeff Hartnell, Alex Himmel, Mayly Sanchez and Louise Suter. The winner will serve together with Tricia Vahle. Thanks to Peter Shanahan for his work on NOvA.

*** No good candidates for nucleon decay

The discovery of neutrino oscillations came from experiments set up to look for GUTS-inspired nucleon decay. Super-K continues to set new limits in a variety of potential modes, and there aren't many candidates to provide reasonable hope that they will stick above the background anytime soon. Early in the search, Maurice Goldhaber pointed out that he would judge each candidate by its background. Do some voters prefer candidates with no background?

*** 28th International Workshop on Weak Interactions and Neutrinos

WIN 2021 offers a significant opportunity to assess the status of the field and to initiate collaborative efforts to address current physics questions. Following up workshops in Heidelberg, Irvine & Bari, WIN 2021 will be online Mon-Sat 7-12 June, with sessions between 1300 and 1700 GMT. Required no cost registration and additional information are now available: http://win2021.umn.edu. Topics are 1) Neutrino Physics, 2) Electro-Weak Symmetry Breaking and Higgs, 3) Flavor Physics & 4) Astroparticle Physics. There will be Plenary highlight talks, recorded talks posted in advance (asynchronous talks), a live discussion panel and a Virtual poster session. Abstract submission and registration are open.

*** A neutrino experiment at the LHC - FASER

The ForwArd Search ExpeRiment at the LHC FASER is searching for light long-lived particles that are made at the ATLAS interaction point, move along the beam axis, and decay within FASER. It will also study TeV nus. See arXiv:1908.02310 & http://faser.web.cern.ch

*** power record for NOvA experiment

The NuMI beam used by NOvA set a new hourly record beam power of 775 kW. SY120 and g-2 were off with 1.2 sec rep rate. A recent week saw 21.5e18 pot delivered to NuMI

*** Interesting Letters of Interest; v4 - https://snowmass21.org/

  • Batell-084 Fixed-Target Searches for New Physics with (10 GeV) Proton Beams at FNAL, The beam dump target station being proposed can handle up to 1 MW of power. SBND in the Booster beamline could search for sub-GeV dark matter, as MiniBooNE had done.
  • Toups-099 Fixed-Target Searches for New Physics with (1 GeV) Proton Beams at FNAL, Similar to the previous LOI, this studies sub-GeV dark sector models, and explore BSM physics & CEvNS.
  • Callaghan-071 CHESS; Each unit consisting of an upper and lower cosmic tag, target, propagation medium, and a PMT array, the CHESS (CHErenkov/Scintillation Separation) setup is an LBNL experiment. Dichroicons are used to separate Cherenkov & scintillation light, The setup provides a sensor geometry for sorting out the photons.
  • Doped_Scintillator-180; Continued Development of Organic Scintillators with PSD and Li-doping; Using Organic Scintillators with Pulse Shape Discrimination and Li-doping can identify reactor
  • Dallavalle-081; Tau-neutrino Production at a multi-TeV Lepton Collider; This LOI suggests that tau neutrino interactions should be further studied, as hints at abnormalities in the tau sector have accumulated. Muon colliders are ideal for investigating tau neutrino production in W+W- boson interactions.
