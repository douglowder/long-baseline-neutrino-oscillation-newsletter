Long-Baseline news, October 1999

*** EOI for Neutrino Factory submitted to NSF

An Expression of Interest for a Neutrino Factory Based On a Muon Storage Ring was submitted to the National Science Foundation by the Neutrino Factory and Muon Collider Collaboration on 11/1/99. The Collaboration home page is http://www.cap.bnl.gov/mumu/ 195 signed the document (see URL below.)

*** Ultimate Neutrino Page moves across Finland

Juha Peltoniemi's popular "ultimate neutrino page" has moved with him from Helsinki to Oulu. It can now be found at http://cupp.oulu.fi/neutrino/

*** MINOS growing

In the last year, MINOS has grown with groups from Wisconsin, Pittsburgh, Northwestern, Elmhurst, BNL, Chicago, James Madison, Athens and South Carolina. Despite the large number of neutrinos expected, the people will be limited to 6 Billion.

*** AquaRICH now for atmospheric nus and possibly pdk.

AquaRICH, once an NGS long-baseline proposal, is now concentrating on a large water detector (about 1 Mt) either in a deep pit (for atmospheric alone) or the sea (for atmospheric and proton decay). They are building and instrumenting an 8 m diameter prototype with 50 HPD's of 10" diameter each with 2048 pixels. They already have made 5" and 10" HPDs (with 2048 pixels) and are embarking to make 20" and 40" versions (with 512 pixels).

*** Oak Ridge's ORLanD project changes name, keeps acronym

Despite a cut, Oak Ridge's SNS got some funding. The proposed LSND-like neutrino oscillation experiment, ORLanD has changed its focus from an experiment to a facility. They changed their name from Oak Ridge Large Neutrino Detector to Oak Ridge Lab for Neutrino Detectors. The plan calls for building a laboratory which could house up to 4 200 ton detectors. Frank Avignone is the spokesman for this effort. The 2 Megawatt source will create 1.4 10^16 neutrinos per second.

*** Solar "LOW" region can be addressed by day/night, seasonal

Fogli, Lisi, Montanino, Palazzo (below) show that the so-called ``LOW'' MSW solution to the solar nu problem can not only be tested through night-day rate variations in BOREXINO, but also through seasonal variations in GNO. The net winter-summer difference (6 months averages, eccentricity effects removed) amounts to 4--6 SNU in GNO, within the LOW region.

*** NuMI to get hosed

The MINOS collaboration endorsed the idea of a "hadronic hose", a current carrying wire down the center of the decay pipe to focus more hadrons in the direction of the detectors. The device, proposed and designed by Jim Hylen, will increase the flux and reduce possible systematic errors due to near/far differences.

*** NuMI might get plugged

The NuMI beam group is considering a 1.5 meter graphite plug just after the 2nd horn. Initial simulations show that this would reduce the high energy tail by more than a factor of 3 on the low energy beam without reducing the peak intensity much (10%).

*** NuMI costs and possible delay

The NuMI tunnels and Halls project received 6 proposals on 10/26. Prices are confidential, but presumably above the engineer's estimate of $24.9M. The project will proceed after discussion on scope and contingency utilitization, but the $25M figure represents the threshold that kicks in yet another review, likely to delay the project start at least 2 months from Jan 00.

*** Neutrino lobby prevails in US Senate

The Senate rejected the Comprehensive Test Ban Treaty (CTBT) so that neutrino physicists could propose neutrino oscillation experiments using nuclear weapons. Physicists prefer to do these experiments with a long-baseline than with a short-baseline. Bombs are a prolific source of neutrinos, but the detection of nus from nuclear weapons does not appear in the literature.
