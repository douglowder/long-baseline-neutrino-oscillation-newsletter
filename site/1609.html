Long-Baseline news, August/September 2016

*** LBNF/DUNE reaches CD3a milestone

The Department of Energy has formally approved CD3a, which enables construction at Sanford Lab in South Dakota of the first two large underground caverns. One cavern would fit the first DUNE detectors and the second would house the cryogenic systems and other utilities. Funding is included in the President's budget, and is working its way through the house and senate.

*** Neutrinos from PIon Beam Line, nuPIL

A conventional neutrino beam consists of a proton beam hitting a target followed by one or more magnetic horns. An alternative option for LBNF has been developed based on a sign-selected wide acceptance (transverse and in momentum) pion beam line. A design was made using a 200 meter straight section with 27 quadrupole magnets based on work from NuSTORM. Possible advantages over the LBNF baseline design is the much lower wrong sign components, most of the Kaons would decay in the bend, the hill is not needed, and beamline instrumentation could better constrain the flux. https://indico.fnal.gov/conferenceDisplay.py?confId=12553

*** LBNF/DUNE evaluates NuPIL

https://indico.fnal.gov/conferenceDisplay.py?confId=12553 The NuPIL idea was presented at the last DUNE collaboration meeting, and has been evaluated by the Experiment-Facility Interface Group (EFIG). In order to concentrate efforts on preparing a TDR for the beam by June 2019, it has been decided not to pursue NuPIL or other alternative studies to the optimized LBNF wide-band beam design.

*** LBNF/DUNE supported by Illinois and South Dakota

Two Lieutenant Governors, Matt Michels from South Dakota and Evelyn Sanguinetti from Illinois got together to issue a joint declaration of support for the Long-Baseline Neutrino Facility and Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment. http://news.fnal.gov/2016/09/joint-proclamation-support-lbnf-dune-illinois-south-dakota/

*** Ice-Cube sees no evidence for sterile neutrinos

A global fit to explain the short-baseline anomalies gets dmsq = 1 eV^2 and sin^2(2q_24) = 0.1. These sterile nus would produce distinctive energy-dependent distortions of the measured zenith angle distributions in IceCube, cased by resonant matter- enhanced oscillations during neutrino propagation in the Earth. Using 20145 muons from 344 days, IceCube expects a large depletion for the global 3+1 best fit point, dominated by the parametric resonance. The absence of this depletion allows them to exclude that point at 99% CL. See arXiv:1605.01990 (recently updated).

*** Ice-cube fails to resolve or explain short-baseline anomalies

Taking all data at face value, 3+1 sterile nus as an explanation of the short-baseline anomalies has been ruled out for some time. But what is the explanation? It is probably not statistical fluctuations. It may be systematics or interesting new physics.
