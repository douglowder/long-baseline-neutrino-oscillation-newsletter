Long-Baseline news, December 2001

*** Lower neutrino funding at CERN

In its efforts to produce a budget for 2002, the CERN management is considering now to cut the budget of R&D for high intensity proton machines and Neutrino Factory down to... 0.3 MCHF! This is down by a factor 5 with respect to the 2001 budget and by an even larger factor below the level that a healthy R&D programme would require. Even if the R&D on future neutrino sources is to be shared with other laboratories in Europe, this cut will make it difficult to set-up these collaborations. A manifesto on the subject has been developed at the web page http://nufact.cern.ch/NUFACT-RD/NUFACT-RD.html (not working today.) See http://nfwg.home.cern.ch/nfwg/nufactwg/nufactwg.html

*** Homestake Deal Blasted in New York Times

In a front page article in the January 3 New York Times, the Homestake "deal" releasing Homestake Mining Company of liability for the possible future site of the National Underground Physics Lab is described as a $30-40M giveaway to the company. It is not in the interest of good environmental policy. The government will "assume any and all liability relating to the mine and laboratory, including liability for damages, reclamation, the costs of response to any hazardous substance" and mine closing.

*** Mining for pork in South Dakota

The bill for the Homestake lab was described as a "sweetheart deal" and a "stealth amendment" in an editorial in January 4th's Chicago Tribune. They accused Senator Daschle of "hypocrisy" for previously arguing in favor of environmental protections and against limitations on liability. To make clear that they didn't like the bill, they said it "stinks." edittrib.html

*** Highlights of NNN-Baton Rouge

NUSL will start with small projects. Searches for neutrinoless double-beta decay and dark matter "absolutely compelling" motivations for initial operation. Also, progress with KamLAND almost ready for data, and Borexino coming along, with several other approaches for p-p solar neutrino detection under development. Theoretically, there is renewed interest in neutron-antineutron oscillations as well as proton decay. There were many ideas for low-energy, off-axis, or narrow-band neutrino beams for oscillation studies. There are several ideas for detection of supernova neutrinos with the goal of extracting the neutrino temperature for the different species.
