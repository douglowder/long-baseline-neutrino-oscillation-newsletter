Long-Baseline news, July 2011

*** South Korea's RENO reactor neutrino experiment filled

As of 5 July 2011, both the near and far detectors of RENO were filled with all liquids including the Gd loaded liquid scintillator target. Dry runs had checked the PMTs and DAQ during March to May. Commissioning shifts & calibration efforts are currently underway, and physics data is expected to begin in August 2011.

*** NRC report on long-baseline next step for LBNE

Proceeding with three physics experiments, including LBNE, planned for DUSEL would provide an "exceptional opportunity" for major scientific advances & give the U.S. a leading role in underground science, says a new port by the National Research Council. They wrote, "The long-baseline neutrino oscillation experiment would provide a great advance in the study of neutrino properties, particularly when coupled with a neutrino beam produced at FNAL using a new high-intensity proton source under development." The DOE's director of the Office of Science William Brinkman is expected to react to the Marx panel report on costs and the NRC report on physics and make a decision about DOE involvement at Homestake soon. http://sites.nationalacademies.org/BPA/BPA_058955 , https://download.nap.edu/catalog.php?record_id=13204

*** New NuMI working group

There are several experiments using the NuMI beam line at FNAL: MINOS, MINERvA & NOvA; along with previously ArgoNeuT & future MINOS+. A joint beam group is being formed to share both technical and intellectual knowledge about the beam flux and spectra, including geometry, modeling, stability and cross sections. If interested, contact Alfons Weber

*** More antineutrino results from MiniBooNE

MiniBooNE, which previously showed antineutrino results from 5.66e20 protons on target, have shown results from 8.58e20 pot. The LSND-like excess persists, but is reduced; for E>475 MeV, oscillations are favored over background-only at 91.1% CL. However, a greater excess is now seen at low energy, looking like the low energy excess seen by MiniBooNE using neutrinos. For E<475 MeV, the excess is 38.6 +- 18.5 events. They will continue running with a request of 15e20 pot.

*** More antineutrino running, results for MINOS

After the replacement of the NuMI target with a previous very hot target (NT02), MINOS has resumed low energy high-intensity antineutrino running on 29 July, known as Run IX. Previous results on antineutrino oscillation parameters were shown for 1.71e20 pot. Results from an additional 1.24 pot are expected to be shown at Lepton-Photon Mumbai.

*** OPERA Status; The expected rate is now with oscillations.

Opera has run long enough to see 1.65 expected tau. Previously experiments mentioned the expectation in the absence of oscillations. Now we quote the "expectation" in the presence of oscillations. They see one tau candidate, with 5.3e19 pot analyzed out of 12.6e19 collected and 22.5e19 expected by the end of 2012 or so. They got extra beam since there were problems at CERN in the Compass beam line. They are continuously improving scanning performance & predicted background. In particular, the background for the tau decay to muon is very low. See arXiv:1107.2594.

*** Long-Baseline neutrino experiments at short baselines

LBNE plans a 1300 km baseline, and other long-baseline proposals vary from hundreds to thousands of km. At NuFACT11, an argument was made for a 200 km experiment, calling it a "short-baseline experiment".

*** The small neutrino mixing angle is large

Last month, T2K and MINOS reported evidence for non-zero theta-13. T2K's paper was mentioned last month, and MINOS now has a paper on the subject: Adamson et al., "Improved search for muon-neutrino to electron-neutrino oscillations in MINOS", arXiv:1108.0015 . In the tri-bimaximal mixing matrix, theta-13 is zero, but the new best fit is in the upper half of the CHOOZ allowed region. Many theory papers now use "large" to describe this value, e.g.: arXiv:1107.0696 , arXiv:1107.0738 , arXiv:1107.1097 , arXiv:1107.1589 , arXiv:1107.3728 , arXiv:1107.0221 .
