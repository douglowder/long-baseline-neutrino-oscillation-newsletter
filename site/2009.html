Long-Baseline news, September 2020

*** Tensions between T2K and NOvA

Collaborators on these two large neutrino experiments get along very well. And both experiments, analyzed alone, seem to favor the normal neutrino mass order (m3>m2>m1). But differences in the delta and octant determination lead to the situation that a joint fit of both experiments seems to favor the inverted mass order (m2>m1>m3). See, for example, K.Kelly et al. in arXiv:2007.08526.

*** Dual phase ProtoDUNE concludes 7 Sep

The dual phase prototype of ProtoDUNE ended running after testing several aspects of this novel technology. One problem was an apparent short between an HV extender with a ring connected to the field cage. A mitigation exercise was done in June to remove some of those connections. An mp4 video of this procedure is available at https://cernbox.cern.ch/index.php/s/9HXtdxxw8YI2RbB. The photodetector system measured light changes with and without Xe & N_2 injections. R&D on HV and other issues is proceeding in preparation for phase II of ProtoDUNE-DP.

*** If Betelgeuse goes boom...

This red giant is the 10th brightest star in the night sky, and is a candidate for a supernova. It is 700 light years distance, and would be a prodigious source of neutrinos if that were to happen. https://news.fnal.gov/2020/10/if-betelgeuse-goes-boom

*** Basic Research Needs (BRN) Detector R&D report available

The goal of the BRN study was to assess the present status of the HEP physics and technology landscape, and to identify strategic technology areas, aligned with the strengths of the US community, that future long-term R&D efforts should focus on in pursuit of the HEP science drivers identified in the P5 report. Neutrinos are one of the five listed science drivers. https://science.osti.gov/-/media/hep/pdf/Reports/2020/DOE_Basic_Research_Needs_Study_on_High_Energy_Physics.pdf

*** Interesting Letters of Interest; v1

Between now & the 31 Jul 2021 deadline for Snowmass contributions, this section will feature some of the 205 two-page neutrino LOIs submitted for the study to define the most important questions for the field of particle physics and to identify opportunities to address them. https://snowmass21.org/ Links on the web version.

  • Nigel_Smith-037, "IUPAP Neutrino Panel Report", The International Union of Pure and Applied Physics has convened an international panel to promote international collaboration in the development of future neutrino programs to establish the properties of neutrinos and the physics of neutrino sources.
  • J.J._Gomez-Cadenas-199, "The NEXT-SABAT R&D for Barium Tagging" R&D aims to utilize a fluorescent bicolor indicator (FBI) that chelates Ba²+ cations and then tag barium using sensors fully dispersed on a cathode and scanned by a movable microscopy system. A prototype is called pBOLD.
  • HyperK-187, The "Hyper-Kamiokande Experiment", Hyper-Kamiokande is the next generation underground water Cherenkov detector that builds on the highly successful Super-Kamiokande experiment. Starting in 2027, it will obtain 3.8 Mt-yr of exposure over 20 years and measure nus from the upgraded JPARC beam.
  • OceanBottomDetector-201, "Ocean Bottom Detector", Currently, continental detectors KamLAND and Borexino are measuring traits of geoneutrinos, 70% of which are from the earth's crust. An ocean bottom detector would focus more on nus from the earth's mantle. Development tests will begin in 2022.
  • Aurisano-154, "Atmospheric nu-tau Appearance in the Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment", They could potentially access 800 nu-taus with a coordinated reconstruction effort.
