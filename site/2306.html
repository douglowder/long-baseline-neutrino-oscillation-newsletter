Long-Baseline news, June 2023

*** IceCube sees Milky Way Neutrinos

https://physics.aps.org/articles/v16/115 With 60,000 nu cascades above 500 GeV, they obtain an excess of events in the galactic plane at the level of 4.5 sigma. For E_gamma > 1 GeV, photons from neutral pion decay in the plane of the galaxy are clearly visible. Nus from charged mesons are expected. 87% of the events are atmospheric nus, 6% atmospheric muons, & 7% the astrophysical nu signal. https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.adc9818

*** Don Perkins, 1925-2022, "A force of nature"

I just became aware that Don Perkins, an early prominent advocate of long-baseline experiments, passed away last October. Among many other things, he played a leading role in Gargamelle, which helped establish that neutrinos interact using neutral currents. https://www.theguardian.com/science/2022/nov/22/donald-perkins-obituary

*** Don Perkins, an original subscriber to long-baseline news

https://www.hep.anl.gov/ndk/longbnews/0307.html He often commented on the newsletter, such as the July '03 comment that Lewis & Clark were looking for good sites for neutrino experiments, though they didn't know it. "Your message about Lewis & Clark's search for neutrino sites reminds me of a paper by Goldhaber and Nieto in Rev Mod Phys (1971), about physical constants etc. They pointed out that the fact that Magellan's ships got around the world set a limit on the photon mass around 500 years ago only about a factor 10**10 poorer than today's limit (~10**-27 eV). A finite photon mass would have meant a deviation (a la Yukawa) from the inverse square law, to be compensated by ring currents around the equator which would have certainly screwed up Magellan's compasses. Also, I could remark that, if the geomagnetic field had been only 4 or 5 times its present value, Kamiokande would not have found atmo nu oscillations, since their cut-off would have reduced fluxes by an order of magnitude, and neutrino energies would have been higher and the oscillation length much longer. So the evidence would have had to rest with SOUDAN 2 & IMB at northerly latitudes. Since IMB didn't support oscillations, God knows what would have happened!"

*** Don Perkins, the Higgs and theta-13

In December 2010, the final Oxford MINOS collaboration meeting took place, and I ran into an ever-ebullient Don Perkins at the elevator. "How are things?" he asked. Trying to match his enthusiasm, I said, "Great! In the coming year we'll find the Higgs and measure a non-zero value of theta-13." He pointed his finger at me and said "I'm going to hold you to it." Now history will record that both of these accomplishments happened in 2012, but by October of 2011, many of us thought we knew the value of theta-13 and the Higgs mass. In March, before the July CERN announcement, even comedian Stephen Colbert knew the Higgs mass. https://www.cc.com/video/3df60s/the-colbert-report-higgs-boson-humor
