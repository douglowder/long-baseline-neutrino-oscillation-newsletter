Long-Baseline news, November 2018

Who knew new nu?

*** long-baseline pioneer, Ko Nishikawa 1949-2018

Koichiro Nishikawa, the founding spokesperson of the K2K and T2K experiments, passed away on November 28th, 2018. Nishikawa-san was the pioneer of the accelerator long-baseline neutrino experiments. He proposed and carried out the world first long-baseline neutrino oscillation experiment, K2K, that firmly confirmed nu oscillations discovered in atmospheric neutrinos. T2K found the first evidence of nue appearance in the numu beam. With these scientific achievements, Nishikawa-san received several awards: Nishina Memorial Prize, Breakthrough Prize, and Bruno Pontecorvo Prize. He was the professor emeritus in KEK, and served as the director of Institute of Particle and Nuclear Studies, KEK.

*** Large Enriched Ge Experiment for Nu-less 2beta Decay (LEGEND)

Building on the successes of the MAJORANA DEMONSTRATOR and GERDA, the LEGEND collaboration has been formed to pursue a ton-scale 76Ge experiment, with discovery potential at a half-life beyond 10^28 years. See arXiv:1810.00849

*** STRings for Absorption length in Water (STRAW)

STRAW is a pathfinder for a deep ocean neutrino telescope in the Pacific. It was deployed in June 2018 at 2600 m in the Cascadia Basin, a site operated by Ocean Networks Canada (ONC). It will measure the scattering length and ambient background to determine suitability for a km^3 detector. arXiv:1810.13265

*** Giant Radio Array for Neutrino Detection (GRAND)

This is a planned large-scale observatory for high energy (E > 10^8 GeV) cosmic rays (CR). It would study radio emission from extensive air showers in the atmosphere. It plans 10,000 radio antennas over 10,000 km^2 in radio-quiet locations. UHE CR's have a GZK horizon at 100 Mpc, but nus don't. arXiv:1810.09994

*** Tritium endpoint study of Cosmic Neutrino Background (CNB)

The concept of neutrino capture on beta-decay nuclei to detect the CNB was laid out by Weinberg in 1962 and refined by Cocco, Mangano and Messina in 2007 in view of the finite neutrino mass discovered by oscillation experiments. A transverse drift electromagnetic filter in PTOLEMY studying Tritium decay provides a step toward the required energy resolution. arXiv:1810.06703

*** Pyhasalmi mine

The Pyhasalmi mine had been chosen as the site for two projects, LENA and LAGUNA-LBNO that did not go forward. But it is an available fresh air underground site at 4100 mwe. There is the Experiment with Multi-Muon Array (EMMA) studying the CR mass composition in the knee region at 75 m depth. See arXiv:1810.00909 .

*** ProtoDUNE-SP finishes beam running

Beam running from 0.3-7.0 GeV with pi, proton, e+ & K events at each momentum has concluded. A proposal about further running of ProtoDUNE-SP and -DP after CERN's long shutdown 2 has been submitted to CERN. http://cds.cern.ch/record/2647182/files/

*** NOvA shifts chosen by Monte Carlo

Two years ago at NOvA shift sign up time, all shifts were taken and some people didn't get any. I never understood why this was a problem, A new system was put in place where everyone selects preferences & a computer algorithm assigns shifts. The algorithm is chaotic in that it is sensitive to a random number seed. It is run for 10^6 random seeds and the best solution is based on a “chi squared” using quotas and preferences. This time there were two best solutions, the 1st one being the 113947th seed.
