Long-Baseline news, June 2011

*** T2K sees excess of events consistent with theta_13

http://jnusrv01.kek.jp/public/t2k/sites/default/files/t2k-nue1st.pdf Results from 1.43E20 pot (before earthquake) has 6 electron signal observed and 1.5+/-0.3 BG expected. This is 0.7% probability to \theta_13=0 (significance: 2.5 sigma) and the 90% allowed region for the Normal hierarchy is 0.03 < sin^2(2\theta_13) < 0.28 with a best 0.11. Inverted: 0.04 < sin^2(2\theta_13) < 0.34, best 0.14. The experiment is described at arXiv/1106.1238.

*** MINOS sees excess of events consistent with theta_13

http://www.fnal.gov/pub/presspass/press_releases/minos-electron-neutrinos-20110624.html MINOS showed data from 8e20 pot and with an improved particle ID (called Library Event Matching LEM), gained 30% in sensitivity. The data was 62 events with 49.5+-2.8+-7 background. Best fit to sin^2(2\theta_13) 0.04(0.08) normal(inverted) & excludes 0 at 89% CL. See http://theory.fnal.gov/jetp/talks/MINOSNue_2011June24.pdf

*** Global fits to theta13 by Fogli et al

"Our global analysis of all the available data provides >3 sigma evidence for nonzero theta(13), with 1-sigma ranges sin^2 theta_13 = 0.021+-0.007 or 0.025+-0.007, depending on reactor neutrino flux systematics." arXiv:1106.6028; [Note - sin^2(q), not sin^2(2q)]

*** Marx committee report on underground science shown to HEPAP

( http://science.energy.gov/~/media/np/pdf/Review_of_Underground_Science_Report_Final.pdf The DOE Review of Options for Underground Science was released. It gives price ranges for DOE management at Homestake for LBNE, 0nu- beta-beta(DBD) & Dark Matter experiments & compares to SNOLAB for the last 2. No recommendations but 11 "major conclusions": 1) accuracy level of cost estimates; 2) TPC is $1.2-1.5B for LBNE, 0.1B for each DM, 0.2-0.3 for each DBD, $18-23M/yr for operations; 3) If LBNE is not at 4850, add $0.15B; 4) Standalone experiments at Homestake are not cost effective; 5) 7400 ft level experiments prohibitively expensive; 6) Shafts need modernization; 7) DM & DBD at SNOLAB appears cost effective; 8) The LBNE, DBD & DM time scale is 20 years; 9) Locating a facility in the U.S. would help to promote U.S. leadership in these fields for the foreseeable future; 10) there is a very significant strategic benefit to making the LBNE technology choice (water/argon) as soon as possible; 11) There are advantages to the 1+1 (water+argon) option.

*** Appropriations committee report has Homestake language

The House Energy and Water Committee for DOE High Energy Physics reported out $797.2M compared to $795.4M in FY11 and $797.2 requested by the Administration. Language in the bill included "As the Department weighs alternatives, the Committee cautions the Department against taking over the construction and long-term management of DUSEL. Adopting management of yet another laboratory site would add budgetary and management burdens to an already stressed program. However, the Committee supports the use of funding to maintain the viability of the DUSEL underground lab, including dewatering and maintaining security, in order to preserve it as an option while the Department weighs the alternatives. Further, the Department is directed to report to the Committee an assessment of alternatives to DUSEL and its recommendations for moving forward." 2 sides got words in there.

*** DOE & Homestake - next step

The National Research Council will come out with a report on underground science. Then Bill Brinkman will decide the DOE's approach to managing Homestake for LBNE, DBD & DM.

*** MINOS+, MINOS+, so good they named it twice

A large subset of the MINOS collaboration has proposed to Fermilab to continue running the two MINOS detectors in the NuMI beam for 3 years starting 2013. The "medium energy" setting will allow the Far Detector to collect 10,000 charged current and 3,000 neutral current events 4-10 GeV and make stringent tests for non-standard interactions, sterile nus, extra dimensions, time-of-flight, etc.
