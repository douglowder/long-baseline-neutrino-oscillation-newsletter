Long-Baseline news, August 2014

*** LAGUNA-LBNO turns in design study

Slides from an open meeting marking completion of the design studies and transition to the realization phase of LAGUNA/LBNO are at http://laguna.ethz.ch/indico/conferenceDisplay.py?confId=59. Several work product documents were delivered on 31 August, and will be made public on the arXiv. There are more than 4000 pages and a 478 MB pdf file. The next step beyond the LAGUNA-LBNO design is the realization of the 1:20 scale "demonstrator" called LBNO-DEMO, endorsed (so far) by 120 from 22 institutes. WA105 is scheduling an October meeting at CERN.

*** IIEB for a Long-Baseline Neutrino Facility

Fermilab is actively responding to the P5 call to create a new international collaboration to fulfill the long-baseline vision in that report. An "interim International Executive Board", IIEB, has been appointed to guide preparations for a letter-of-intent (LOI). There are roughly 20 leading neutrino scientists from around the world together with several funding agency representatives. They plan to meet in September. A working group of funding agency, lab and other people has been formed to draft an international project governance plan for LBNF.

*** LBNE continues to grow

http://www.fnal.gov/pub/today/archive/archive_2014/today14-08-07.html When a new international collaboration comes into existence, it is expected that most everyone on LBNE will join. But meanwhile there is work to do and LBNE does not want to interfere with new partners, so it is not using the name LBNF. (Let's hope there is a new name when the time comes!) LBNE how has 537 collaborators, making it the largest neutrino collaboration to date.

*** T2K near detector sterile neutrino tests

https://indico.cern.ch/event/300521/session/2/contribution/116/material/slides/0.pdf In a search for electron neutrino events as a function of energy in the T2K near detector (FGD water target and TPC tracking chambers) they see less events than expected in a way that is consistent with nue disappearance. dmsq_41=2.14 eV^2 for the best fit. Fitting the energy distribution, the null hypothesis is rejected at 94%, i.e. the data does not fit no-oscillations well in this test motivated by the sterile neutrino hypothesis. This matches MiniBooNE's sterile nu delta m-squared, but it's strange that a nue disappearance signal in a numu beam can support a nue appearance signal in a numu beam.

*** NOvA Near Detector Completed

In August, a team from the NOvA collaboration led by Louise Suter and Marco Colo finished instrumenting the final block of the NOvA near detector, with all electronics turned on. It is 350 feet underground on the Fermilab site, the 330-ton NOvA near detector is filled with liquid scintillator. Previously, NOvA had run a Near Detector on the Surface (NDOS) and recently also completed its 14 kiloton Far Detector in Ash River Minnesota. This near detector is located at the same off-axis angle as the far detector and will be used to measure the unoscillated beam.

The Summer School will be 17-28 August at the ICTP South American Institute for Fundamental Research in Sao Paulo. The INSS will, curiously, happen in Winter (as well as NuFACT) so, please, get your swim suit, sun protection and surf board ready.

*** Do you know the location of your slide rule?
