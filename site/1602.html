Long-Baseline news, February 2016

*** Can the LIGO result be explained by neutrinos?

No. They would travel between Livingston Louisiana and Hanford Washington in the same time as gravitational waves. But they wouldn't affect a Michelson Interferometer. Could neutrinos come along with the gravitational waves? Maybe some could be emitted during a black hole merger. But 1.4 Billion light years is a lot of one over rsquared. arXiv:1602.08436 Why not look (a statement, not a question.) arXiv:1602.05411. Antares and Ice-Cube looked within 500 s of the 14 Sep event and found 0 and 3 events, none pointing to GW150914 and consistent with atmospheric background.

*** Neutrino Statistics? A meeting in japan 30 May - 1 June.

Neutrino rates used to be low. Now we're either better or smarter or both. But with higher rates come more statistical issues. PhyStat-nu will address statistical issues in the broad range of modern neutrino physics, including making measurements of model parameters, setting limits, discovery criteria, discrete choice of models, Bayesian vs. frequentist inference, and combining experiments. There will be a 2nd workshop at Fermilab 19-21 Sep. http://conference.ipmu.jp/PhyStat-nu

*** DUNE prototypes

A 35-ton liquid Argon prototype detector which was built at FNAL has been filled with liquid Argon. Contamination is low so the e lifetime is long, tracks have been seen, and shifts are continuing to study noise rates and other issues. A dual-phase liquid Argon prototype at CERN, formerly known as WA105, is now part of DUNE, Dual-phase protoDUNE. There is also work going forward for a Single-phase protoDUNE. The EHN1 building extension for these is underway. http://indico.cern.ch/event/502868/

*** Notation change for mixing angles?

Stephen Parke writes, "Why do we label the mixing angles 12, 13 and 23?" Theta_13 isn't the mixing between physical mass eigenstates nu1 and nu3; it's the mixing between nu_e and nu_3. He suggests:
theta_e2 instead of theta_12
theta_e3 instead of theta_13
theta_mu3 instead of theta_23
Then U_e2 proportional to sin theta_e2 & similarly for e3 and mu3! There is the cos theta_e3 in two of them. It would be hard to make notation changes but it's at least worth a discussion.
