*** rant: In defense of stovepiping

Well, not really. But there was a great deal of discussion at the neutrino frontier workshop about how funding boundaries, such as between NP and HEP were frustrating researchers as they try to put together exciting research programs. An oft-mentioned example is how the measurement of theta-12 in solar neutrinos was defined to be nuclear physics, while the measurement of theta-13 in reactor and accelerator physics was high-energy physics. But the field now has good measurements of both parameters. The fundamental constraint is that there will always be more good ideas for new experiments than money, even if 100% of GNP were available. When a funding agency has to say no, people want reasons, and it is easier to say "We don't do that," than "Your good idea was not as good as another good idea." Multidisciplinary projects do happen.
