Long-Baseline news, August 2019

*** J-PARC neutrino program strategy Recommendation

It makes sense that while Hyper-K will be under construction, T2K should operate, but likely budget allocations are not sufficient. T2K had requested a beam extension to 20E21 POT. A panel formed to discuss a J-PARC neutrino program strategy has recommended that if Hyper-K is approved, beam operation of 10E21 POT take place in parallel with beam and near detector upgrades for T2K. http://www.hyper-k.org/doc/2019/Panel-recommendation_final.pdf

*** ProtoDUNE-DP LAr filling complete at CERN neutrino platform

In the dual-phase detector, the electrons drift upward to deposit charge on a plane of anodes. Amplification electronics are above the liquid argon in a gaseous argon environment. The 2nd or 3rd DUNE 10-kt module will likely use this technology. See the filling at: https://cernbox.cern.ch/index.php/s/wwc1aqvLD5jtQNp

*** DUNE TDR

A draft multiple-volume technical design report describing the detectors, physics, beam, etc. for the Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment is being reviewed by the Long-Baseline Neutrino Committee (LBNC), which reports to Fermilab management. The plan is to submit the document near the end of calendar 2019.

*** New results from NOvA using neutral currents in antinus

A new search for evidence of sterile neutrinos in NOvA using NC Disappearance with antineutrinos was presented by Michael Wallbank at DPF 2019. A predicted NC rate of 122 with 78% purity was compared to an observed rate of 121 +- 11 +- 18. It was consistent with oscillations in the 3-flavor framework. http://nova-docdb.fnal.gov/cgi-bin/ShowDocument?docid=39502

*** NNN 2019 in Colombia

The International Workshop on Next Generation Nucleon Decay and Neutrino Detectors (NNN2019) for the first time will be in Latin America at the University of Medellin, Colombia, on 7-9 Nov. There will be parallel sessions on neutrino detectors and also Systematics and Analysis Techniques. http://www.nnn19medellin.com

*** RANT Hyphen almost always needed in long- and short-baseline

If we do an experiment with a long baseline, long is an adjective and baseline is a noun. There is no ambiguity and no hyphen is needed. If I do a long-baseline neutrino experiment or facility, or work on a short-baseline program, it is the baseline that is long or short and not the neutrino, the experiment, the facility or the program. The hyphen removes ambiguity. Long-baseline experiments will likely take a long time, & be long long-baseline experiments. And if Ken Long from Imperial College ever builds a Nu factory, that will be Long's long long-baseline experiment.
