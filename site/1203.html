Long-Baseline news, March 2012

*** Daya Bay measures theta_13

arXiv:1203.1669 Using six reactors and six detectors, the Daya Bay reactor neutrino collaboration reported a 5.2 sigma measurement of electron antineutrino disappearance. With 43,000 GW-ton-days, they see 80376 neutrinos at their near detectors; they also see 10416 events in their far detector which is 0.940+-0.011+-0.004 of the rate expected if theta13=0. Their best fit is sin^2(2-q13) = 0.092+-0.016(stat)+-0.005(syst) in a 3 nu framework. This is with rate only; they will have 2 more detectors, and more exposure.

*** Magic number for theta_13?

In China the word for "eight" (Cantonese "fa", Mandarin "ba") sounds like the word for prosper or wealth. Superstitious people take this seriously, e.g. the license plate number 888888 sells for millions. The Daya Bay result for theta_13 in degrees is 8.8 +- 0.8. http://www.neutrinooscillation.org/theta13table.html

*** RENO measures theta_13

Reno detects antineutrinos from 6 reactors at 2 locations, 294 m & 1383 m. In a 229 day run, the they see 17102 events in the far detector and 154088 in the near detector, or .922+-0.010+-0.008 of the expected rate for theta_13=0. The best fit is sin^2(2q13) = 0.103+-0.013+-0.011 from rate only. Submitted to PRL arXiv:1204.0626

*** Top 9 consequences of the new measurements of theta_13

1.) Future long-baseline experiments need to redo all of their sensitivity plots; 2.) They need to switch from log theta13 to linear in theta13; 3.) They need to go above 0.1 if they didn't before; 4) Nobody needs to add tribimaximal to their spell checker anymore; 5) Muon fed neutrino factories need a new reason to exist 6) People should stop calling theta_13 the last unmeasured nu mixing angle; soon it will be the best measured angle. 7) We need to think of a way to define the mass hierarchy as an angle. 8) It is too late for a theorist to "predict" theta_13. 9) Global fitters need to get back to work on mass hierarchy and delta.

*** LBNE "decision" is no as-is; but a version of LBNE will emerge

After testifying before congress and telling us that LBNE was too expensive, http://www.aip.org/fyi/2012/043.html Office of Science Director Brinkman sent a letter to Fermilab Director Oddone: "... we cannot support the LBNE project as it is currently configured. This decision is not a negative judgment about the importance of the science, but rather it is a recognition that the peak cost of the project cannot be accommodated in the current budget climate or that projected for the next decade." He asked for a phased approach and consideration of alternative configurations to LBNE. http://www.neutrinooscillation.org/brinkman2012.pdf

*** Fermilab Steering Committee to meet 25 & 26 April

A new committee will look at a phased approach and consider alternative configurations to LBNE. On 25 April, possible new ideas for using the NuMI beam will be considered, while on 26 April, ways to split the current LBNE (far detector at Homestake, near detector, beam) will be considered. I've heard that the sessions which are at Fermilab will be open.

*** LBNE holds director's review

Even after the Brinkman letter, the LBNE project presented its review of the project as-is, to the Fermilab Director, in advance of a CD1 review. By understanding the costs in detail, it can help to develop reasonable staging options.

*** NuTURN Gran Sasso 8-10 May, http://nuturn2012.lngs.infn.it/

With the large theta_13 from Daya Bay and others, we have an opportunity to study the leptonic mixing matrix, addressing the major challenges in this field. The NuTURN2012 workshop will evaluate the "large" theta_13 scenario. It will target the opportunities offered by the existing and new facilities, and in particular, by the long-baseline experiments. A principal aim is to clarify which type of neutrino beams, detectors and which laboratories are best suited to investigate the neutrino properties, in order to outline a realistic European roadmap.

*** MicroBoonE gets CD3B

This short-baseline liquid argon experiment will resolve if the MiniBooNE low energy excess is electrons or photons. It is also a step along the way to larger liquid argon detectors.
