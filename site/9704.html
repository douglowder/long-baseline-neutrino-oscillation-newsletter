Long-Baseline news, April 1997

*** Too few nu-e; SuperK results on Solar neutrinos

With 201.6 days livetime, and comparing to the BP95 solar flux, Super K sees 0.400 + 0.013 - 0.012 (stat) + 0.020 - 0.014 (syst) of the standard Solar model flux with a threshold of 6.5 MeV. Above 7.0 MeV the result is 0.409. This is based on 3216 events, as opposed to 390 in Kamioka (where the ratio was more like 0.5). In and independent analysis, the results were 0.379 +- 0.017 +- 0.019 and 0.394 +- 0.018 +- 0.019. An earlier day night difference has gone away.

*** Too few nu-mu; SuperK atmospheric results

Two independent (onsite and offsite) analyses confirm an atmospheric neutrino deficit in SuperKamiokande. There are two analyses. The offsite analysis gets 1977 events in 196.3 days. The onsite analysis gets 2126 events in 208.5 days. Looking at single ring events only below 1.33 GeV only, in one analysis they see 476 (mu-d), 467(e-d), 5566(mu-mc), 3666(e-mc) for a ratio of ratios 0.67 +- 0.05 +- 0.06. The Monte Carlo is obviously for a larger exposure. The other analysis gets 466, 459, 1072, 676 for 0.64 +- 0.04 +- 0.06. As a function of fiducial volume, the ratios are flat, so the effect is not due to neutrons. In the "multi-GeV range" they get 0.52 +- 0.09 +- 0.06.

*** CHORUS/NOMAD results

Nomad has new limits on numu to nue and numu to nutau. For numu to nutau, they have zero events in tau--> e and tau-->pi. They set a limit on P of 0.002 or 0.004 on sin^2 2theta for high mass. nu-e's are about 1% of the beam. Using the measured numu, numubar and nuebar spectra they can predict the nue contributions from K+'s, mu+'s and K-longs. They can then use the difference in predicted and observed nue energy distributions to limit oscillations. They get sin^2 2theta less than 0.0015 above 10eV^2, which contradicts LSND's result except at lower masses. CHORUS has no nutau candidates from 200 kg of emulsion, leading to a mixing angle limit 0.008. When all of their data is scanned, their limit can approach 0.0003.

*** CERN neutrino considerations

The CERN SPSC considered a LOI (the proposal will be next step) from the combined CHORUS/NOMAD groups to do a new short baseline experiment with 3 tons of emulsion in the UA1 (NOMAD) magnet. The timescale would be technical run in 2001 and data taking 2002-2004 and projected sensitivity 2X COSMOS.

*** New mu to e gamma limit

The previous mu to e gamma limit is 5 10^-11. But this is based on a result with 50 candidates. MEGA presented a new analysis based on 20% of their data, with no candidates and a limit on B of 4.2 10^-11. This result is sensitive to the same lepton violating processes as neutrino oscillations, though a sub-eV neutrino coupled with no new physics until the GUT scale leads to a B of ~ 10^-50.

*** CHOOZ starts up; SNO Still Not Operational

One of the reactors at CHOOZ has been started and has reached 50% power. They have 5 tons of Gd loaded scintillator in a tank. Palo Verde is planning to start with 12 tons of segmented detector in late 1997. SNO is carefully building their tank, with expected data in 1998. A piece of their sphere had to be replaced because, "...if it breaks, you have 8 kilotons of not-so-heavy water, some unhappy Canadians, and no experiment".

*** LSND Decay-in-flight results due soon

When completed, the LSND mu decay in flight analysis will have different systematics and backgrounds from the decay at rest analysis. The energy is higher, but the distance is greater and the statistics is lower, so a new result will be sensitive to a similar region of delta-msquared for the two analyses. A paper is expected in 2-3 weeks and a presentation at the Big Sky conference on nuclear/particle physics intersections.
