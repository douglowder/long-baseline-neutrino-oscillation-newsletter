Long-Baseline news, April 2020

This web page has no known viruses.

*** T2K CP studies appear in Nature

Details of T2K CP studies released in October ( arXiv: 1910.03887) using nus and antinus have been published in Nature and generated much publicity. https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-020-2177-0 A recent CERN seminar is at https://indico.cern.ch/event/900928/. Matter and Antimatter matters matter.

*** T2K and the press

Many press reports discuss the matter-antimatter asymmetry in the universe and CP violation. Neutrino physicists are aware that leptogenesis provides a plausible connection, but the story isn't over. In translating that story for the press, misstatements abound, which mostly don't matter. In a provocative blog, I was struck by "few people care about CP violation in the nu sector." http://backreaction.blogspot.com/2020/04/no-physicists-have-not-explained-why.html better is https://www.nytimes.com/2020/04/15/science/physics-neutrino-antimatter-ichikawa-t2k.html

*** Fewer neutrinos during pandemic

During the "work at home" days, the major labs are closed and beams are shut off. At FNAL for example, the beam is off for the rest of the fiscal year. But some experiments are keeping their detectors going, such as NOvA for supernova & cosmic ray studies.

*** Neutrino 2020 and 2030

Neutrino 2020 will be a virtual conference, starting 22 June and extended to run Mon-Thu over 2 weeks with each day shortened to 4.5 hours. International advisory committees overwhelmingly supported an online conference over a conference postponed by a year. Abstracts for posters were delayed until 8 May. As of 1 May, over 550 abstracts had been received. Registration details (which will be free) will be posted on the web site soon. https://conferences.fnal.gov/nu2020/ Neutrino 2030 in Zurich will be a live meeting for the Pauli Centennial Celebration, except for those of us whose presence is required at a ball.

*** NuFACT2020 and ICHEP

ICHEP 2020 will be a virtual meeting, with parallel sessions 28-31 Jul & plenaries 3-6 Aug. http://ichep2020.org/ ICHEP 2024 will be in Prague. NuFACT2020 in Sardinia, on the other hand, will be held on 21-26 June 2021. It is OK to call it NuFACT2021.

*** Neutrino backgrounds

With meetings at home on zoom, ... we get to see in the background what people have on their walls and bookshelves. Books such as "Neutrino" by Frank Close can be seen, as well as classics such as "The structure of scientific revolutions" by Thomas Kuhn, and the Feynman lectures. Some choose to use a virtual background, (arrow next to video in zoom) & a large neutrino detector is an obvious choice. Please share with me any good neutrino backgrounds.

*** Neutrino Day goes virtual 11 July 2020

A Sanford Lab tradition, this year's neutrino day: a matter mystery will be a virtual experience, with online chats, hands-on activities, games, art activities, scavenger hunts and a science comedian. https://www.sanfordlab.org/article/neutrino-day-goes-virtual

*** ICARUS filling with liquid Argon

The ICARUS detector, which came to Fermilab from LNGS, is part of the short-baseline nu program to follow up on unexpected results from MiniBooNE. It started filling with liquid Argon in February https://news.fnal.gov/2020/04/liquid-argon-delivery/ and is now full with 760 tons of liquid Argon. https://icarus.fnal.gov/

*** RANT - how to write a date

The progression day-mon-yr has always made more sense to me than mon-day-yr. The inferior scheme is ingrained in many people, and isn't going away. When you make a hotel reservation on many websites, be careful that you are choosing the dates you want!

*** Neutrino Interaction drawn by hand

It has been a while since physicists recorded their data with a pencil & paper. But Third-grader Maria Esperanza Castro Torres of San Cristóbal, Venezuela showed it's still a good skill to have. https://news.fnal.gov/2020/05/hand-drawn-particle-interaction/

*** Neutrino Music by David Ibbett

In the “Particle of Doubt,” scored for soprano, violin, viola, cello, piano and electronics, you can almost hear an oscillation. https://news.fnal.gov/2020/04/giving-voice-to-neutrinos-fermilab-guest and https://www.multiverseseries.org/blog
