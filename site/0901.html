Long-Baseline news, January 2009

*** Neutrino Mixing - The Leader of the PACS

When you write your paper, and it gets published, you or someone assigns it a PACS code, for the Particle and Astronomy Classification Scheme. http://www.aip.org/pacs/ Section 14 is for particles and there are 40 codes. For the 2008 statistics of 75 journals in the SPIN database, 1460Pq Nu Mixing was the runaway top code with 265 papers, followed by 1480Cp, non- standard Higgs with 124 and 1420Dh proton and neutron with 79. Last was 1465-q Light quarks - only 3 papers. The other neutrino codes are 1460Lm, Ordinary Neutrinos, which had 46 papers, and 1460St, non-standard-model neutrinos with 57. With apologies to The Shangri-Las http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6WKP8-Ni-m0

*** Workshop on Neutrino Technology July in Trieste

A workshop to discuss possible practical applications of neutrinos will be held 13-17 Jul 09 at ICTP in Trieste. It will consider areas of neutrino physics which can potentially lead to such applications. These include neutrino geophysics, Earth tomography, communication systems, control of atomic reactors, creation of new neutrino sources and detectors, use of coherent neutrino interactions and the neutrino Moessbauer effect. http://cdsagenda5.ictp.trieste.it/full_display.php?ida=a08170

*** Electron Neutrino Appearance in MINOS

MINOS has an electron neutrino appearance analysis group which has been doing a blind analysis of this channel, sensitive to theta13, for it's first 3.14 e20 protons on target. The sensitivity has been shown previously. The group will open its box and is tentatively scheduled to show the result at Fermilab late in February. One advantage of a blind analysis is that it helps the group think carefully through the consistency checks that need to be made before looking at the result, as well as discouraging over-analysis after the box is open.

*** J-PARC accelerates to 30 GeV

The new Japanese main ring at Tokai has accelerated protons to 30 GeV and extracted to the abort dump, as of 23-Dec-08. They plan to extract to the hadron beamlines in February. The T2K long=baseline neutrino experiment will use this beam.

*** NOvA and Budgets

FRA-Fermilab must become certified in Earned Value Management System (EVMS) budget procedures. NOvA is the first Fermilab project to go through this process. There still isn't a 2009 Federal Budget, but House stimulus bill contains extra DOE money for science, and if so, NOvA might be expedited. How do I explain to my grand-children that NOvA was delayed, almost canceled, and then expedited, all for the same reason (bad US economy)? The CD-2 baseline with $278M was approved. CD-3a is approved for a limited $19M set of critical path/long-lead time items. Project has spent $16.8M through FY08. CD-3b Review maybe this summer.

*** Five Year plan for muon collider R&D at Fermilab

There is a Neutrino Factory & Muon Collider Collaboration (NFMCC) and Fermilab Muon Collider Task Force (MCTF) planning a possible future neutrino program involving a neutrino factory. In Dec 08, they submitted a five-year R&D plan to the Department of Energy. It includes a study to demonstrate the feasibility of building a muon collider by 2013. In parallel, they complete the MICE experiment and participate in the the International Design Study (IDS) to deliver a neutrino factory reference design report by 2012. https://mctf.fnal.gov/annual-reports/ To join NFMCC, go to http://www.cap.bnl.gov/mumu/info/join.html.

*** Indian Neutrino Observatory and Elephants

The next step for the underground atmospheric neutrino experiment in the hills of India is an environmental permit from the Tamil Nadu state government. Some groups have opposed this project because trucks will go on an existing road in the habitat where elephants live. The neutrino physicists themselves presumably do not pose a threat in this country of 1.3 billion. According to an article in Science, a program to help elephants is planned. http://www.sciencemag.org/cgi/reprint/323/5911/197.pdf
