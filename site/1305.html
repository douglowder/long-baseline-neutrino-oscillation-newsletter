Long-Baseline news, May 2013

*** New Minerva results on quasi-elastic scattering

Minerva has measured neutrino and antineutrino quasi-elastic scattering on a Hydrocarbon target. Good agreement is obtained with a model where m_A (the axial mass) is 0.99 GeV/c^2, but modifications are made to account for the cross section for the exchange of transversely polarized photons in electron-nucleus scattering. m_A doesn't need to be increased. They also observe effects consistent with multiple protons in the final state. arXiv:1305.2234 and arXiv:1305.2243.

*** What LBNE is and isn't

The reconfiguration process left several different documents which covered the physics reach of the Long-Baseline Neutrino Experiment describing different programs. LBNE is clarifying that LBNE is a plan for a 34 kiloton liquid Argon detector underground at SURF with a fully capable near detector. The 1st phase which has CD1, for a 10 kiloton detector without a near detector is called LBNE10. It is a high priority goal to be placed underground.

*** Ice cube sees a new source of astrophysical neutrinos

By examining events with large energy loss, Ice Cube has found astrophysical neutrinos at higher energies than are expected from atmospheric neutrinos. They reported 2 events in arXiv:1304.5356. A contained vertex search has 28 events over an atmospheric neutrino background of 10.6. https://events.icecube.wisc.edu/getFile.py/access?contribId=76&sessionId=41&resId=0&materialId=slides&confId=46 They have also been able to measure atmospheric neutrino oscillation parameters: http://wipac.wisc.edu/news/view/128.

*** Snowmass neutrino update

There is a call for Snowmass white papers, besides the nu 1pagers: https://www-public.slac.stanford.edu/snowmass2013/submissionform.aspx The purpose of 1 pagers was to define the SLAC meeting program. *** International Symposium on Underground Physics for Snowmass

A pre-Snowmass meeting considered the advantages of placing LBNE at SURF underground (most likely at 4850 level). These include the ability to search for nucleon decay, atmospheric neutrino oscillations and the possible detection of supernova neutrinos (as opposed to antineutrinos). All agreed that it should be underground. A document will follow. http://neutrino.physics.ucdavis.edu/indico/conferenceTimeTable.py?confId=0#all

*** Glashow and precision

I've heard several people mention Sheldon Glashow's view of the High Energy Physics field in 2013 (arXiv:1305.5482) so I will do so. In his section on neutrinos, he mentions three potentially transformative experiments, and one astrophysical challenge, that could be part of the U.S. program. Two relate to the mass hierarchy, and the detection of CP violation. He would also like to see the reactor neutrino disappearance measurements of theta-13 confirmed by accelerator experiments using electron neutrino appearance. But he concludes that we must avoid the pursuit of accuracy for its own sake.
