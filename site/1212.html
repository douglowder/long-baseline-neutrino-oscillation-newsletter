Long-Baseline news, December 2012

*** Our second Neutrino Giant award to Fred Reines

Frederick Reines was an American physicist who co-discovered the neutrino with Cylde Cowan and won the Nobel Prize in physics in 1995. Reines sought to measure neutrino interactions as proposed by Pauli in the Hanford and Savannah River laboratories in 1956. Reines became the head of the Case Institute of Technology physics department. There, he built an experiment in South Africa which observed atmospheric neutrinos. As a member of IMB, he was able to detect and study a stream of neutrinos emitted by Supernova SN1987A. Reines grew up in North Bergun, New Jersey and had a strong love for science as a child. He also had a passion for singing and later performed in the Cleveland Orchestra Chorus. http://www.hep.anl.gov/ndk/hypertext/#Reines

*** The Finnish finish LAGUNA (for now)

The Finland ministries of Education and Culture, Employment and Economy, and Transport and Communications considered whether Finland should host the LAGUNA project, a Large Apparatus for Grand Unification and Studying Neutrino Astrophysics. A favored site for this was the Pyhasalmi mine in Finland, using nus from CERN. The host country would be expected to contribute 20-50% of the 0.9-1.6B euro construction cost. The Ministries consider the LAGUNA project to be scientifically interesting, but in view of the large costs, it is not possible for Finland to commit to host the project. http://www.minedu.fi/OPM/Tiedotteet/2012/12/Laguna.html?lang=fi

*** Siegrist Sermonette (to LBNE critics if there are any)

At HEPAP he said, "The HEPAP/P5 strategic plan has been successful at: developing a prioritized, executable program plan that has been sufficiently flexible to respond to major changes, conveying the interest and importance of HEP to non-HEP audiences, framing the program in an easy-to-understand context. And yet, some members of the community do not seem to know what the plan is, much less communicate it effectively to non-HEP types; worse other members of the community will gladly substitute their own personal vision for the community plan with little or no prompting. This does not convey the impression that we as a community know what we want, which makes implementation of anything very challenging. It also undermines our international standing. For example, implementation of reconfigured LBNE seems to suffer from this phenomenon. This does no credit to US HEP at an important fiscal juncture."

*** NOvA fills first modules with liquid scintillator

When completed, NOvA will be the world's largest plastic structure. It will be 28 "blocks" of 384 modules. On 11 Dec, the first two modules were filled about 236 gallons each in 3 hours. 10,750 more modules to go. NOvA was featured in the Economist Magazine article, "Know your neutrinos." http://www.economist.com/news/21566416-next-big-thing-physics-will-be-lightest-matter-particle-all-know-your-neutrinos
