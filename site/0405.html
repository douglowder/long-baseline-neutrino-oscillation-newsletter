Long-Baseline news, May 2004

*** SNOWMASS neutrino meeting for APS study June 28-30.

What do neutrino physicists want to do? What do neutrino physicists need to do in the US? The APS neutrino study will hold a meeting at Snowmass to discuss these issues and write a report, as a conclusion to its yearlong study. http://www.interactions.org/cms/?pid=1011988

*** Double CHOOZ proposal

The best current limit on theta13 comes from looking at the CHOOZ limit at the Super-K delta msquared. More sensitivity than CHOOZ requires more Gigawatt-ton-years and better control of systematic errors. Hence an obvious solution is to go back with a bigger detector, a near detector, and a longer run. That's just what Double CHOOZ proposes to do. A letter of intent is available at hep-ex/0405032 Already approved in France, this is working through the funding agencies in Germany and elsewhere.

*** Theta13 from solar

At lower values of delta-msquared, CHOOZ limits nothing. But if theta13 were large, solar results are affected. Global analyses are required, such as in hep-ph/0309130 by Maltoni et al. The best fit is actually sin^2 theta13 = 0.006, but values greater than 0.1 are ruled out at all dm^2 (Figure 8.) hep-ph/0405172

*** Theta13 from reactors

Besides Double CHOOZ, reactor theta13 experiment ideas include Angra (Brazil), Braidwood(Illinois, US), Daya Bay (China), Diablo Canyon (California, US) and KASKA (Japan).

*** Theta13 from the atmosphere

A non-zero value of theta13 will affect the atmospheric numu to nue ratio, but only through matter effects, and it is more apparent if theta_23 is large (larger than 45 degrees). Large exposures (1500 kt-years) are needed. See hep-ph/0312038

*** Theta13 from supernovae

Matter conversion resonances can affect neutrino fluxes measured from a supernova, and there is sensitivity to theta13. Dighe and Smirnov discussed this in Phys Rev D62, 033007, hep-ph/9907423

*** Theta13 at accelerators

Ideas for exploring theta13 at accelerators include using the long-baseline experiments, building superbeams and/or off-axis experiments (T2K and NOvA), neutrino factories and beta beams. hep-ph/0405081 looks at the possibility to exploit a high energy beta beam without a massive detector. With a gamma of 2500, a search for nue to numu could be done using the Gran Sasso rock as the target and detecting the muons in a 15 x 15 m^2 detector.

*** Theta13 and Ue3

Luckily theta13 is a fairly common notation and doesn't have too many other names. (Glashow calls the angles theta1, 2 and 3, and says that others talk too much, but luckily he is in a shrinking minority.) But even with only one name, limits are quoted for theta13 in radians, in degrees, sin^2(2 theta13), sin^2(theta13), and sin(theta13). A possibly useful table for conversions is at http://www.neutrinooscillation.org/theta13table.html.

*** sin theta13 = 0.13

If so, the angle is aptly named. This prediction is based on what Harrison and Scott call "The Simplest Nu Mass Matrix" and is derived from the measured delta m^2. hep-ph/0403278

*** Beyond theta13

Joe Formaggio is leading a working group interested in interesting additional physics that can be uniquely probed by low energy neutrinos produced in reactors, called "Beyond theta13". http://faculty.washington.edu/josephf/beyond_theta13.html

*** European joint lab for Frejus & Gran Sasso?

Ideas were reviewed at a workshop at CERN in May. http://physicsatmwatt.web.cern.ch/physicsatmwatt/ "DSM, IN2P3 and the INFN agreed to prepare the design of a very Large Underground Laboratory in the new Frejus tunnel, with complementary features with respect to the Gran Sasso laboratory, to be submitted as a joint proposal to the French and Italian governments. The institutions aim at associating the Frejus and Gran Sasso laboratories in a single entity, a European joint laboratory, open to the world scientific community, to carry out advanced experiments in particle, astroparticle and nuclear physics in the coming decades, on topics such as matter stability, neutrino mixing and mass, stellar collapses and nuclear astrophysics."

*** The Neutrinoless Universe?

Nicole Bell won the Tollestrup Award for her work on the relic neutrino abundance and neutrino mass. Adding neutrino oscillation to the calculation of the chemical potential of relic neutrinos was a big improvement. But if there is coupling to a new light boson, all relic neutrinos could have annihilated.
