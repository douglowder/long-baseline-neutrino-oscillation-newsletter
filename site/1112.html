*** Does a 125 GeV Higgs couple to Neutrinos?

Maybe the Higgs has been discovered at about 125 GeV. Could nus get their masses from the Higgs mechanism like all the charged fermions? Neutrinos couple to some Higgs field, which may or may not be the same one that gives all charged fermions masses. Neutrinos could couple to the same Higgs field in the same way. It does imply the existence of right-handed neutrino fields, otherwise the left-handed neutrino fields cannot couple to the Higgs condensate. This does not seem like a big change to the Standard Model particle content. But right-handed nus are gauge singlets, which means they are allowed to have masses independent from the left-handed neutrinos, and independent from the Higgs. "Anything that is allowed is mandatory." We expect these right- handed neutrino Majorana masses to exist and, since they have nothing to do with the Higgs, we have no idea what their values are. By combining the neutrino-Higgs interaction with the right- handed neutrino Majorana masses, we end up with all neutrinos as massive Majorana fermions.

*** The $\nu$ Standard Model

We have a new Standard model with Dirac or Majorana masses, but we don't know what it is yet. Right-handed neutrinos may not have their own mass parameters. In this case the neutrinos end up as Dirac fermions. If these right-handed neutrino masses are not present, they must not be allowed. In order for that to be the case, there must be some new symmetry we either did not know about or that we thought was only accidental. One example of the latter is lepton number. If neutrinos turn out to be massive Dirac fermions, we will have learned something we do not know today. For the scenario where all the neutrinos are Majorana, a curious thing happens regarding couplings of neutrinos to the Higgs boson. All charged fermions couple diagonally to the Higgs boson (b-bar{b} or s-bar{s}, but not bbar{s}), and the coupling is proportional to the mass. But for massive neutrinos, the Higgs can decay to nu_1 nu_1, nu_1 nu_2, nu_3 nu_4, etc in all kinds of off-diagonal ways. The couplings are also proportional to elements of the PMNS matrix.

*** LBNE technology recommendation

The Long-Baseline Neutrino Experiment, a proposed new beamline from Fermilab to SURF in South Dakota, has done R&D on two possible technology options, water Cherenkov and liquid Argon. In December, the executive committee recommended to the project manager that a water Cherenkov detector be built for the far detector. The Project will make a decision in consultation with Fermilab management and the DOE. Then on to a CDR and CD1 reviews.

*** Interim design report on a Neutrino Factory

The IDS-NF mandate is to deliver the Reference Design Report (RDR) for the facility on the timescale of 2012/13. In addition, the mandate for the study requires an Interim Design Report to be delivered midway through the project as a step on the way to the RDR. This document, the IDR, has two functions: it marks the point in the IDS-NF at which the emphasis turns to the engineering studies required to deliver the RDR and it documents baseline concepts for the accelerator complex, the neutrino detectors, and the instrumentation systems. arXiv:1112.2853

Careful readers of this newsletter have probably noticed that this does not get the attention it deserves. This is due to my incomplete understanding of the issues, and not any lack of appreciation. arXiv:1111.7264 seems relevant.
