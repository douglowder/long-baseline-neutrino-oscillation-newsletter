Long-Baseline news, October 2003

*** JPARC neutrinos setback

Japanese Council for Science and Technology Policy, Cabinet Office (CSTP) recently evaluated the J-PARC neutrino project as a rank C project (the lowest priority in the four steps of evaluation, S, A, B, and C). C means "needs re-consideration." 3 reasons given: 1. A question remains whether suitable prior evaluation was made about the advance of the J-PARC second phase plan. (J-PARCnu had been classified as one of the J-PARC second phase projects.) 2. In view of the present severe financial situation of Japan, a question remains about whether a large amount of investment is justified only from a viewpoint as basic research. 3. Concerns if there exists a domestic similar plan or domestic facility where similar research can be conducted. - There was better news from a subsequent meeting of Koshiba & the panel. The chair of the panel stated that if a concrete plan for overcoming these difficulties is formulated, they'll reconsider.

*** DESY interest in JPARC-nu

Whether or not a linear collider is built at DESY, there is a gap in their physics program after the possible turn off of HERA. At a workshop to study new projects last month, there was intense interest in contributing to the 2km detector for JPARC-nu.

*** NuMI target area completed

Ragnar Benson completed the Target Area civil construction 10/20. This includes the MI-65 Service Building, the outfitting of the below grade pre-target area, the below grade support rooms, the Target Hall, and the Target Shaft. Fermilab took beneficial occupancy of the area. Installation work has begun. MINOS area completion is due 1/31/04.

*** Abraham gives Fermi prize to Bahcall, Davis

WASHINGTON, DC - On Wednesday, October 22, Secretary of Energy Spencer Abraham presented the 2003 Presidential Enrico Fermi Awards to neutrino physicists John Bahcall and Raymond Davis, Jr. and to Seymour Sack. This is one of the government's oldest and most prestigious science and technology awards. The winners receive a gold medal and a citation signed by the President and Secretary of Energy. Drs. Bahcall and Davis each received $93,750, whose sum is suspiciously close to 186,282, the speed of light in miles/second.

*** Abraham to unveil DOE facilities plan on November 10th?

It has been reported that the Orbach facilities plan of the Office of Science for the next 20 years will be released by the Secretary of Energy on November 10th, followed by the signing of Critical Decision 0 (permission to think) for one or more projects. Several neutrino-related projects are on the HEP and NP lists.

*** Neutrinos at Reactors in the Land of Abraham (Lincoln)?

A Letter from Fermilab and Argonne Directors to the Chairman of Exelon (formerly Commonwealth Edison Nuclear) sought permission for development of a reactor experiment (for theta_13) by a group spearheaded by Jonathan Link. They agreed to help develop one. Ill. reactors under consideration at Byron, Braidwood, Lasalle.

*** Neutrinos at a Reactor on the coast of Brazil?

Angra, located on the coast at Angra dos Reis (3 hours south of Rio), contains a 4GW reactor and is surrounded by 400-600m hills. The Brazilian community is enthusiastic and is working on a proposal and cost estimate. There is a hope that the civil construction could be about half the cost of a similar US project. There is already good communication with the reactor company so funding and construction could occur by 2006.

The October Issue of Lufthansa Magazin includes several pages of particle physics event displays as art. Only one was a nu event. Each nu experiment is encouraged to send me an event picture for a collection to be put on http://www.neutrinooscillation.org/.

*** Neutrino Marketing

"Most people look for the man in the moon. Some look for neutrinos," starts a bike advert on p39 of September's Atlantic Magazine. The ad mentions neutrino 4 times. It even tells us what one is (similar to an electron) but not much about the bike.

*** SAUND - looking for noisy neutrinos

A neutrino induced shower with enough energy will make some noise that microphones can detect. A Study of Acoustic High Energy Nu Detection (SAUND) is underway in the Bahamas using AUTEC, the Atlantic Undersea Test and Evaluation Center, using 7 hydrophones. In 211 days, dolphins have been detected, but no clear nus. http://hep.stanford.edu/neutrino/SAUND/
