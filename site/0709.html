Long-Baseline news, September 2007

*** NuMI vacuum decay pipe switching to Helium

After a shutdown, NuMI/MINOS is ready to start again. Concern about possible long term corrosion damage to the vacuum window of the decay pipe has led to a plan to fill the decay pipe with He. This will reduce stress on the window and make a catastrophic failure less likely. A 3% loss in neutrino flux is expected.

*** MiniBooNE run over but still running

MiniBooNE was only approved to run until the present shutdown, but after the shutdown is over, SciBooNE is running, so MiniBooNE will continue to get beam. They also have submitted a proposal to be considered by the PAC for a further 3E20 in anti-neutrino mode. This should take a further 2 years and enable them to perform a search for anti-nue appearance with 'similar' sensitivity to nue.

*** MiniBooNE Low energy excess more excessive at lower E

MiniBooNE did not see an excess of electron neutrino events which could be interpreted as oscillations with the same 2-neutrino parameters as LSND. But they did see an excess of electron neutrino candidates at low energy. They have further studied the low energy region and haven't identified the explanation of the excess. If they lower the energy threshold of the analysis (to 200 MeV), the excess grows. See slide 25 of http://www-boone.fnal.gov/slides-talks/conf-talk/tayloe/S04_12.pdf One idea is muon internal brems - arXiv:0709.4004. They say no: http://www-boone.fnal.gov/publicpages/low_e_responses/radcorr/

*** CARE07 meeting 29-31 October at Cern: http://cern.ch/CARE07

The main objective of the CARE project - Coordinated Accelerator Research in Europe - is to generate a structured and integrated European area in the field of accelerator research. The program includes advanced scientific & technological developments relevant to accelerator research for Particle Physics. It is articulated around 3 Networking Activities and 4 Joint Research Activities. The main objective of BENE NA is to coordinate and integrate the activities in a worldwide context, towards achieving superior neutrino beam facilities for Europe.

*** Fermilab Steering group choices on theta-13

A final report of the Fermilab steering committee on the future of FNAL is now out - http://www.fnal.gov/pub/directorate/steering/ A Fermilab discovery plan is mapped out, depending on such things as the value of theta-13 and the schedule of the ILC. Chapters 4 & 5 deal with neutrinos as the "Intensity Frontier". Neutrino fluxes are estimated for 1) now, 2) proton plan, 3)NOvA, 4)SNuMI and 5) Project X, ranging from 176 to 2314 kW at 120 GeV. Appendices deal with issues such as the international neutrino program, neutrino science at 8 GeV and 800 GeV, and mu e conversion.

*** The ILC can't be delayed.

The date of various projects is often compared to the technically driven ILC schedule put out by the GDE, but that doesn't include time for convincing Congress, getting money, international agreements, passing treaties, etc. Therefore, the ILC doesn't have a real schedule, so it can't really be delayed. Some ILC proponents may be worried that Project X will delay the ILC.
