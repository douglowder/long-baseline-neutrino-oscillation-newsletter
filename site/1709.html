*** Both Chooz reactors are off

Since the neutrino cross section is low, most neutrino experiments try to maximize both the number of neutrinos and the detector size. Reactor neutrino experiments want the most flux possible, which is proportional to reactor power. Daya Bay, for example, sits near six powerful reactors. But background uncertainty can be a source of systematic error, so it is a good thing for Double Chooz, which sits near two reactors, if both reactors are off and the background can be experimentally measured. In a six reactor site, this is unlikely for economic reasons. Double Chooz previously recorded a week of "off-off" for its far detector and will use the current data to study near detector backgrounds.

*** Another subscriber wins Nobel Prize

Barry Barish is the fifth recipient of this newsletter with a Nobel Prize. The subject was gravitational waves, but in addition to that and his work on the linear collider, he has long been a neutrino physicist. He was spokesperson for 7 neutrino proposals at FNAL, and later played a major role in helping to define the NuMI beam, which is still running for NOvA and MINERvA.

*** Optimizing the neutrino/antineutrino split

CP violation implies a difference between antineutrino and neutrino oscillations. But the CP-violation parameter delta_CP could be extracted from two experiments at different baselines just running neutrinos (in the 3-nu paradigm). Parameters from reactor and accelerator nu experiments are studied by Lindner et al. in arXiv:1709.10252 delta_CP seems to be near negative pi/2 for which the optimum nu:nubar running isn't 1:1 but rather 2:1.

*** Neutrino/Antineutrino parameter differences

In the 3-nu paradigm, the oscillation probabilities might differ for nu and nubar, but the parameters should be the same. With CPT violation they might not be. The ability of DUNE and Hyper- Kamiokande to discover or limit this is studied in de Gouvea and Kelly, arXiv:1709.06090
