Long-Baseline news, July 2016

*** Neutrino Woodstock and projector oscillations

Welcoming the participants to Neutrino 2016, Stephen Parke dubbed the 700 participant meeting "Neutrino Woodstock" (a reference to a 4 day music festival in 1969 and metaphor for a lively happening.) It was the largest meeting of neutrino physicists ever. Closing the meeting, he pointed to future conferences in Heidelberg 2018, Chicago 2020, Seoul 2022, & Zurich 2030, 100y from Pauli's letter. The first day of the meeting, someone kept banging his head on the projector, causing the screen to oscillate. The rest of Nu2016 in London was flawless. http://neutrino2016.iopconfs.org/programme

*** Nobel Prize lectures at Neutrino 2016 in Session 1

Highlights this year included Nobel Prize winners Arthur McDonald describing the measuring of solar neutrino neutral current events in SNO, and Takaaki Kajita reviewing the discovery of atmospheric neutrino oscillations in Kamiokande and Super-Kamiokande. Related events were a reception at the Canada House kicking off an exhibit featuring SNO-lab, and a talk in session 4 (not uploaded) on photodetector development by Hamamatsu for SNO and Super-K.

NuFACT 2017 will be in Uppsala Sweden, probably 25-30 September and most will fly to the nearby Stockholm airport. NuFACT 2016 starts soon in Vietnam. http://vietnam.in2p3.fr/2016/nufact/

*** NOvA indicates non-maximal theta-23

With 6.05e20 protons on target, NOvA measures muon neutrino disappearance. 473+-20 events are expected with no oscillation and it sees 78. best fit at sin^2(2q)= 0.40+0.03-0.02 or 0.63 +0.02-0.03. Maximal mixing is excluded at 2.5 sigma, based on the shape of the energy distribution. dmsq_32=2.67+-0.12e-3 eV^2.

*** T2K prefers maximal mixing for theta-23

T2K's disappearance results continue to be consistent with maximal mixing. sin^2(2q) = .532+.044-.060(NH) and rule out NOvA's best fit at more than 90%CL. dmsq_32=2.545+.084-.082e-3.

*** More evidence for neutrino CP violation

T2K and NOvA both see more electron neutrino appearance than might be expected using the reactor theta-13 and delta_CP=0. And with low statistics, T2K sees less electron antineutrino than expected. This all points to delta ~ 270 degrees, the best possible value. T2K alone excludes CP conservation at 90% CL. A fit from Marrone excludes it at > two sigma. In the concluding talk, M. Mezzetto called it the first robust indication of CP violation in the lepton sector. http://neutrino2016.iopconfs.org/IOP/media/uploaded/EVIOP/event_582/14.00__6__Mezzetto.pdf

*** What about the Mass Ordering?

If you are an accelerator-based neutrino physicist, you say the normal mass ordering is favored. If you work on neutrinoless double beta decay, you say the inverted mass ordering is disfavored.

*** Super-Kamiokande and Daya Bay update results

Daya Bay showed data from 1230 day; sin^2(2q13)= 0.0841+-0.0033. Delta m^2_32 = 2.45(-2.55)+-.08 e-3 eV^2 NH(IH). Super-K now has 4.6 sigma effect for tau neutrino appearance in the upward going atmospheric neutrinos. They have updated SK-IV to 2520 days and report 3 neutrino fits for 0.33 Mt-y of data. Solar neutrinos are updated to 2365 days of SK-IV. They now have a 3 sigma effect in the day/night difference, asymmetry = -3.3+-1+-0.5%, E>2.5 MeV.

*** Warm Dark Matter is a hot topic

A workshop summary of the Chalonge - de Vega Workshop "WDM in Astrophysics in Agreement with Observations and keV Sterile Neutrinos" argues the solution matches cosmology and galaxy data better than lambdaCDM. They argue that 7 keV sterile neutrinos would give a 2 keV thermal relic. chalonge.obspm.fr/Highlights_and_Conclusions_CIAS2016.pdf

*** Pontecorvo prize to Gianpaolo Bellini

The award is for his outstanding contributions to the development of low-energy neutrino detection methods in Borexino and the important solar & geoneutrino results obtained in this experiment. http://www.jinr.ru/posts/b-pontecorvo-prize/

*** MINOS decommissioning

Most work is the disassembly of the steel and scintillator. It will be taken apart destructively. A few modules may be salvageable, and be treated as government property. The veto shield will become part of SBN at FNAL. Other equipment will most return to Fermilab. Pictures can be found at http://www.soudan.umn.edu/decom/ Contact Rob Plunkett for questions and details.
