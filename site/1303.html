Long-Baseline news, March 2013

*** OPERA sees third tau neutrino

The long-baseline emulsion neutrino appearance experiment has reported a third tau neutrino from numu to nutau oscillations in the CNGS beam. After 5 years they have 1.8e20pot (protons on target), 20% less than the proposal of 2.25. They also have 19 electron neutrinos and 50 charm events. http://www.infn.it/news/newsen.php?id=663

*** New results and a new challenge for RENO

RENO showed new results on electron neutrino disappearance from 6 powerful reactors in Korea at the Neutrino Telescopes meeting. With double the statistics and improved analysis and background rejection, they measure sin^2(2 theta13) = 0.100 +- 0.010 +- 0.015 with 400 days data. But on 13 Oct 2012, a tiny fraction of a Cf calibration source dissolved in the liquid scintillator, in part due to a loose O ring. The CF is at the bottom of the target, as shown by the vertex distribution. The prompt energy distribution is wide, until Cf events are removed with a "multiplicity cut".

*** Planck

Planck is a European Space Agency mission, accepted in 1996 to map the Cosmic Microwave Background with much higher resolution than WMAP. The CMB provides constraints on the sum of neutrino masses, this constraint is coming closer to the ~0.1 eV level expected from oscillation scenarios. Planck's CMB data-only constraints set the sum of neutrino masses to be less than 0.66-0.85 eV (depending on the choice of CMB data used). BAO results help to break the geometric degeneracy in the angular-diameter distance to last scattering (which is sensitive to the neutrino mass), and improve the limit. Including these, the 95%CL upper limit is 0.23 eV (assuming spatial flatness). The effective number of nu species, N_eff, is measured by Planck (plus other CMB data) to be 3.36 +/- 0.7, adding BAO pulls it down to 3.30 +/- 0.5, adding H_0 moves it up to 3.62 +/- 0.5. The Planck team appears to give most weight to N_eff~3.3. In any case, Planck argues for no compelling evidence against the canonical N_eff=3.046. arXiv:1303.5062 ; arXiv:1303.5076

*** NOvA award and events

The NOvA Far-Detector Laboratory in Ash River, Minn., has earned a National Recognition Award in the American Council of Engineering Companies 2013 Engineering Excellence awards competition. The building has a large span but also some overburden to shield cosmic rays. The award will be given in DC in April. Meanwhile, NOvA's far detector has recorded its first 3D particle tracks: http://www.fnal.gov/pub/presspass/press_releases/2013/NOvA-20130328.html

*** Lev Mikaelyan passes away in Moscow

We are sad to report the passing of Lev Mikaelyan, a long-time neutrino physicist and the first one that I heard propose a new generation of reactor neutrino experiments to measure theta-13, http://arxiv.org/abs/hep-ex/0008046. Before Double Chooz, Daya Bay and RENO, the International Working Group on Reactor Neutrinos was considering Krasnoyarsk, an underground site, and Lev arranged a visit, only to have it canceled at the last minute by local authorities. He wrote us "Better schreckliche Ende than Schrecken ohne ende," i.e. better a disastrous end than endless disasters.

*** Two new results from Borexino

As Kepler knew but Galileo didn't, the earth is in an elliptical orbit of the Sun. This should give a 6.8% seasonal modulation peak to peak of the solar neutrino flux. Using a Lomb-Scargle analysis of the Beryllium 7 data, Borexino excludes no seasonal effect at more than 3 sigma. With 613 ton-years of data, they measure 14.3+-4.4 geoneutrinos after background subtraction, corresponding to 38.8 Terrestrial Neutrino Units (TNU) from U and Th in the earth's crust. arXiv:1303.2571
