Long-Baseline news, February 2014

*** NOvA on the go I, first far detector neutrinos

In some language, "nova" means "it will not go". But the NuMI Off-axis electron neutrino Appearance experiment aka NOvA, has seen its first neutrinos from the NuMI beam in the far detector. It had previously run for two years with a Near Detector on the Surface (NDOS) and seen many thousands of neutrinos at Fermilab. Using the time distribution of the NDOS events, NOvA showed that it sees events which look like neutrinos at the right time in the Far Detector. NOvA is also building a near detector at the same off-axis angle as the far Detector. http://www.fnal.gov/pub/presspass/press_releases/2014/NOvA-20140211.html

*** NOvA on the go II, beam and detector

In February the last plastic modules were shipped from the U of Minnesota module factor, the last of its 28 "blocks" of plastic extrusion detector was installed, which took 19 monhts. Many of the far detector modules still need to be filled with scintillator & electronics installed. The reconfigured Recycler successfully sent 12 batches of slip stacked beam to the NuMI target. http://www.fnal.gov/pub/today/archive/archive_2014/today14-02-19.html , http://www.fnal.gov/pub/today/archive/archive_2014/today14-03-04.html

*** Fire and Radiation incident at WIPP; EXO result

Two seemingly unrelated incidents have put the EXO neutrinoless double beta decay experiment on hold. In early February there was a fire in a salt truck which led to the evacuation of the Waste Isolation Pilot Plant. Later in the month, some low but unexplained levels of radiation showed up on some monitors. EXO has a supply of valuable xenon, but the recovery system worked and the xenon is all safe. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rS3HhEHbFpw , http://www.currentargus.com/ci_25232120 EXO has shown a new result with two years of EXO-200 data. Limit is 1.1 10^25y, based on 99.8 kg yr of Xe-136 exposure. arXiv:1402.6956

*** Three neutrino flavors, not just two

While the two neutrino formula works surprisingly well, it is only an approximation and there are three neutrinos. MINOS, which has the world's best value of delta msquared_32, has a new paper with a three neutrino analysis combining beam and atmospheric neutrinos. arXiv:1403.0867 Also see T2K in arXiV:1403.1532

*** Sterile neutrino report I from astrophysics

Two papers arXiv:1402.2301 & arXiv:1402.4119 discuss a unexpected possibly monochromatic x ray line seen in XMM data (European x-ray telescope). One paper combines data from Andromeda and Perseus, the other stacks 73 clusters of galaxies correcting for redshift. They see a 3.5 keV line which might be due to 7 keV nu decay.

*** Sterile neutrino report II from astrophysics

Some recently published papers show that some tension in Planck data with the lambdaCDM model can be alleviated with sum of nu mass about .46eV and a .43eV sterile. arXiv:1307.7715, arXiv:1312.4947 and http://www.sciencemag.org/content/343/6173/826.full.pdf?sid=34872020-0041-4c07-ac85-1e18565370f2

After working on Ice-cube and Double Chooz, Michelangelo D'Agostino spent a year working on software for the Obama 2012 campaign. Now he does data mining for Braintree. http://www.symmetrymagazine.org/article/february-2014/statistically-significant?email_issue=427

*** A neutrino physicist thrives

Lisa Whitehead, who has worked on T2K, Daya Bay, MINOS, MINOS+ and LBNE, was named Woman Physicist of the Month by the American Physical Society for March 2014. http://www.aps.org/programs/women/scholarships/womanmonth/2014.cfm

*** A neutrino physicist skeptical of sterile nus changes his view

I used to be skeptical of accelerator and reactor based sterile neutrino interpretations. One argument is that almost any mistake in a neutrino experiment can be interpreted as a sterile neutrino. I must now widen that point-of-view to include astrophysics.

*** UK neutrino physicists join LBNE

The Long-Baseline Neutrino Experiment (LBNE) is gaining international support, including the membership of 41 people from 10 institutions in the UK. Some in the UK are also interested in CHIPS and Hyper-K. http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/science-environment-26017957

*** LBNE Cryostat success

LBNE has exceeded a purity goal using a prototype 35 ton membrane cryostat. The oxygen goal is less than 200 parts per trillion of Oxygen, which translates to an electron lifetime of 1.5 ms for the 3.5 meter drift. The measured electron lifetimes were > 2.5 ms. http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/science-environment-26017957
