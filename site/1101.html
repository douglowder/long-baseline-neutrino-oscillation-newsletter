Long-Baseline news, January 2011

*** LAGUNA now LAGUNA LBNO; for long-baseline

LAGUNA is the design of a pan-European Infrastructure for "Large Apparatus studying Grand Unification, Neutrino Astronomy and Long- Baseline Neutrino Oscillations." In particular, they have studied & costed deep underground Science Facilities for a next generation neutrino observatory in Europe. 7 technical site-dependent design reports are available, and a joint report on potential European sites will be available soon. 3 sites are the main options for long-baseline: CN2FR (Frejus, 130 km from CERN), CN2PY (Pyhasalmi, 2288 km) and CNGS (Umbria, 658 km). The site in Italy has the advantage that the beam exists, but the disadvantage that a near detector is unlikely. The next LAGUNA meeting is 3-4 Mar at CERN.

*** DUSEL & LBNE

The 300 member LBNE collaboration is planning a new long-baseline neutrino experiment from Fermilab to the Homestake mine. At the Jan 2011 LBNE meeting, it was affirmed that DOE HEP is continuing to pursue LBNE even though the future of DUSEL is up in the air. The mission need for LBNE was independent of whether the NSF was constructing DUSEL. The project is going forward and locating the far detector at Homestake with or without DUSEL is possible. The DOE needs to know the cost of Water Cherenkov and Liquid Argon options including infrastructure assuming DOE bears the full cost.

*** DUSEL Preliminary Design Report

Although NSF funding for DUSEL is problematic for the rest of the year, the DUSEL project office recently finished and froze the Preliminary Design Report for the Laboratory. There are ten volumes of material including Cost, Science, Facility Design and Operations Plans. A comprehensive internal review is scheduled for March 2011, based on $875M. A DUSEL fact sheet is available at http://www.dusel.org/html/factsheet.html.

*** Deep Lab in the Andes? Workshop 11-14 April in Argentina

The construction of the Agua Negra tunnel between Argentina and Chile gives the scientific community a unique opportunity to build an underground laboratory 1750m deep under the Earth surface in the southern hemisphere. The plans for the ANDES underground lab have to be finished before mid 2011 in order to be included in the tunnel excavation. Its opening would be in 2018. The First International Workshop for the Design of the ANDES Underground Laboratory will study how to plan the laboratory according to the needs of the underground physics community. http://www.hep.anl.gov/ndk/hypertext/numeetings.html#andes

*** Improved Reactor Flux calculation; 3% shift

Mueller et al., arXiv:1101.2663 have recalculated the reactor antineutrino spectrum including the latest information from nuclear databases and a detailed error budget. The error budget is comparable to previous work, but the normalization for 235U, 239Pu and 241Pu has to be corrected by 3%.

*** The reactor antineutrino anomaly

Previous reactor experiments were compared to an earlier reactor flux calculation; Bugey-3, Bugey-4, ROVNO, Goesgen, Krasnoyarsk-I, ILL, Krasnoyarsk-II, Palo Verde and CHOOZ. With the new flux, all experiments have a deficit of neutrinos, as shown in Mention et al., arXiv:1101.2755 . Since some are closer than 100 m, A neutrino oscillation explanation implies a new oscillation length, corresponding to delta msquared > 1.5 eV^2.

*** Will Project X get CD0 Soon?

There is a need for higher intensity for future neutrino experiments, and other "Intensity Frontier" experiments as well. CD0 is sometimes referred to as "mission need", but as with all else, schedules are hard to make when (US) budgets are uncertain.
