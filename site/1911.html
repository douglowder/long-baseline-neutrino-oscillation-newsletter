Long-Baseline news, November 2019

*** T2K measures electron antineutrino appearance

In arXiv:1911.07283 , they present a measurement of 15 events over a background of 9.3. They present two analyses, one showing that the data prefer electron antinu appearance over no appearance. They also free the rate and show it is compatible to the rate expected using previously measured parameters of the PMNS matrix.

*** Groundbreaking at Fermilab for LBNF near site

At a ceremony at Fermilab on 14 November 2019, there was groundbreaking for the near site of the Long-Baseline Neutrino Facility. The ceremony, at the site of the future hill for the LBNF beam, included remarks by LBNF/DOE/FNAL management and international partners. Local officials from Warrenville, West Chicago and Batavia also participated in the groundbreaking. https://news.fnal.gov/2019/11/fermilab-international-partners-break-ground-on-new-beamline-for-the-worlds-most-advanced-neutrino-experiment/ Site work is starting for DUNE-related construction at Fermilab. https://news.fnal.gov/2019/11/prep-work-to-start-for-dune-related-construction-at-fermilab/?utm_source=newsletter&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=ft-191113

*** Award for LBNF Director Chris Mossey

In October, Fermilab Deputy Director Chris Mossey was elected to the National Academy of Construction. They recognized Mossey for his leadership in the military and private sectors, his commitment to conservation, sustainability and the environment, and his support of female, minority and young engineers through mentoring. https://news.fnal.gov/2019/11/fermilab-deputy-director-chris-mossey-elected-to-national-academy-of-construction/?utm_source=newsletter&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=ft-191112

*** Mossey Mountain?

In his groundbreaking remarks, DOE's Associate director of Science for High Energy Physics Jim Siegrist suggested that Fermilab have a contest to name the hill that will built for LBNF. He first heard about the proposed hill (which will be 1/3rd the height of the high rise) from former FNAL director Oddone, and he suggested possible names of Mt. Oddone, Mt. Lockyer and Mt. Mossey.

*** Congressman Foster's inside neutrino joke

Taped messages for the groundbreaking came from Senator Durbin, Congressman Underwood and Congressman Foster. Durbin & Underwood showed their understanding of the purpose of LBNF and DUNE. But Congressman Foster described an experiment he worked on looking for neutrino oscillations as "...something so crazy that only Larry Sulak would design an experiment to look for it."

*** Neutrinos lead to discovery in basic math

https://www.quantamagazine.org/neutrinos-lead-to-unexpected-discovery-in-basic-math-20191113/ That is, if you consider linear algebra to be basic. (When I went to M.I.T, linear algebra was 18.06 with a prerequisite of 18.02). In arXiv:1907.02534 , Denton et al. presented a new method of calculating neutrino oscillations in matter. Along the way, they derived an identity using eigenvectors and eigenvalues. See: https://terrytao.wordpress.com/2019/12/03/eigenvectors-from-eigenvalues-a-survey-of-a-basic-identity-in-linear-algebra/

*** DUNE "Module of Opportunity" Workshop

A 4th DUNE "module of opportunity" would need to be specified by mid-2024 to start installation in mid-2027, in a technically limited schedule. A workshop at BNL discussed many ideas for the technology. The next step is writing concept papers which specify physics capabilities, needed R&D, prototype plans, requirements and rough costs. https://indico.fnal.gov/event/21535/other-view

*** NuFACT 20 to add detector working group

A unique feature of the NuFact series is that it brings together neutrino experimentalists, theorists and accelerator physicists. Given the large effort in neutrino detector R&D currently underway and with new initiatives planned in the years ahead, a new working group on detectors is created. While there is certainly overlap with existing groups on neutrino oscillations, neutrino cross- sections, muon physics and beyond PMNS searches, (in order of WG#) the goal is to enlarge the participant base, as well as the topic base, of the meetings. A search for conveners is underway. The 2020 NuFACT meeting will be 24-29 August in Sardinia, Italy. https://indico.cern.ch/event/855372/

*** Program to visualize neutrino oscillations

Xianguo Lu from Oxford has developed some tools to visualize neutrino oscillations. The interactive mode can be found at http://www-pnp.physics.ox.ac.uk/~luxi/visos/im/

*** SBND cryostat completed

The construction of the cryostat steel structure for the Short- Baseline Neutrino Detector has been completed. This was done by INFN, CERN and FNAL crews. A collection of daily progress and pictures is at http://dbweb5.fnal.gov:8080/ECL/sbnd/E/index.
