Long-Baseline news, April 1998

*** Official Opening of Sudbury Neutrino Observatory on web

The official opening of the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory will take place on April 28 and 29, 1998. Cambridge University Physicist Dr. Stephen Hawking will be in Sudbury for the official opening, which will be available by webcast, starting on the 28th at 6:30 pm Eastern Daylight Time (UTC - 4). See sciencenorth.on.ca/sno/ for information on the webcast.

*** Auger presented again

The scaled-back AUGER proposal was presented to the SAGENAP panel April 14th in DC. Some SAGENAP members were leery of the finances and physics potential. Overheard in the lobby, "...the way things are going, its going to be canned."

*** SuperKamiokande - USA proposal

The US part of the SuperK collaboration is proposing a $1.1M upgrade to the outer detector. 156 of 1885 tubes have died and the loss rate is 1/8.5 days. In mid 1999 during a drain of Super-K there will be an opportunity to maintain the tubes.

*** New CERN numu to nue experiment - I216

This proposal to check LSND involves refurbishing the PS neutrino beamline. A rough cost of 3-4 MCHF is estimated. The required 2.5 10^20 protons takes 3 of the 12 cycles of the PS for 2 years. Information is now available under "Short Baseline" on the .NuOsc Industry page,

*** Seeing nu_taus from Active Galactic Nuclei (AGN's)

VHE neutrinos produced in sources, (AGN's or gamma bursts) are nu_e/nu_mu. Above 1 PeV nus are absorbed by CC interactions in the Earth. But the Earth never becomes opaque to nu_tau since taus produced in a CC nu_tau interaction decays back into nu_tau before losing much energy. This gives an experimental signature for oscillations. Vs. zenith angle, these Nu_tau's would be flat up to the highest energies. Mixing sensitivity would be dmsq > 10^-17 eV^2 (for the farthest sources). This also provides the opportunity for neutrino astronomy well beyond the PeV cutoff, possibly out to above 10^20 eV. See Halzen/Saltzberg paper below.

*** All ideas for renaming the neutrino are losers

Last year, long-baseline news issued a call for renaming the neutrinos, at the request of DOE's Peter Rosen. Suggestions included Microsoft, IBM and Toyota; Father, Son, Holy Ghost; vanilla, chocolate, strawberry. None of these will be forwarded.

*** Et tu, Malcolm

At a 65th birthday party for Malcolm Derrick in Lamont Illinois, it was noticed that three attendees were from one town in England, (Hull) and that all three lacked Latin in their high school career. Should we name the neutrinos Brian Musgrave, Don Perkins and Malcolm Derrick? Malcolm started his career by writing an award winning essay at Birmingham University on Neutrinos. Referring to early neutrino experiments, Don Perkins pointed out that almost anything you did was interesting, but that most of what you did turned out to be wrong.

*** Notable new references

  • The KEK_PS Long-Baseline Neutrino Oscillation Experiment E362. By K2K collaboration Sakuda et al. KEK-PREPRINT-97-254 Feb 1998. Also see hep-ph/9803014.
  • Paolo Lipari and Maurizio Lusignoli, Comparison of nu_mu to nu_tau and and nu_mu to nu_s oscillations as solutions of the atmospheric neutrino problem, hep-ph/9803440 .
  • Gibbons et al., A Four-neutrino mixing scheme for Observed Neutrino Data, UMD-PP-98-98; a theory paper on sterile neutrinos: postulates a new mass texture that links the LSND and atmospheric neutrino anomaly to only one parameter. This ansatz has a new testable prediction in a shortline \nu_e-\nu_tau oscillation expt.
  • J P Bowes and R R Volkas, Maximal mixing neutrino models UM-P-97/09, hep-ph/9804310, They account for the solar and atmo nu problems by introducing maximal mixing between conventional and sterile neutrino partners. Using an extended seesaw mechanism they introduce a new U(1) global symmetry together with an extended Higgs sector.
  • Francis Halzen and David Saltzberg, Tau Neutrino Appearance with a 1000 Megaparsec Baseline, hep-ph/9804354
  • Osamu Yasuda, Three flavor neutrino oscillation analysis of the Superkamiokande atmospheric neutrino data", The allowed region including 414 days data is given and the implications to the long-baseline experiments are discussed. hep-ph/9804400
  • Inclusive nu scattering in C12: Implications for numu to nue oscillations, Singh, Mukhopadhyay & Oset, nucl-th/9802059
  • Detectors for Neutrino Physics at the First Muon Collider, Harris & McFarland, hep-ex/9804009, High fluxes make possible low-E nu oscillation experiments on a "tabletop" scale.
  • A limit on the mass of the Tau Neutrino, CLEO, Mar 1998, hep-ex/9803031. m < 30 MeV from 5 million tau pairs. Best is 18.
  • Neutrino Oscillations in the Dualized Standard Model, Bordes et al. Reasonable agreement found with atmospheric and long wave-length solar solution, but not MSW, hep-ph/9802420
