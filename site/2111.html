Long-Baseline news, November 2021

*** NOvA and T2K working on a joint fit

The NOvA and T2K collaborations have formed a working group to prepare a joint fit of NOvA and T2K data for neutrino oscillation parameters in the 3-nu paradigm. The analysis breaks degeneracies between oscillation parameters due to different baselines and energies of the two individual experiments. The combined result will exploit the full sensitivity of the experiments using correct statistical treatment.

*** NSAC considers neutrinoless double beta decay

At the 16 Nov. 2021 NSAC meeting, Tim Hallman, Associate Director Office of Science, reported on the progress and prospects for ton- scale neutrinoless double beta decay experiments. He wrote: "Despite all our earnest efforts and total technical success there is a substantial chance after 20 years and $1.0 B in investment that we will only observe background down to the limit of 10^28 y. That puts a big premium on being able to verify any claimed signal at that limit. A serious campaign would benefit from having >1 ton-scale experiment & different systematics, especially given the uncertainty in the nuclear matrix elements. https://science.osti.gov/np/nsac/Meetings/202111

*** Status of the LEGEND proposal

This high-purity Ge crystal experiment will build on developments by GERDA, MAJORANA, and LEGEND-200. It will have low backgrounds, excellent resolution, and topology discrimination to allow for an unambiguous discovery of 0νββdecay at 3 sigma of 1.60 e28 y , and 12 meV for median mbetabeta. The DOE cost is $257M including 56% contingency. International collaborators contribute 40%.

*** Status of the nEXO proposal

Scaling of a Liquid Xe Time Projection Chamber is favorable for background reduction. Radon mitigation is understood and the relevant radon background components do not have a spatial distribution like the signal. The DOE TPC is $350M out of $406M total. The 3-sigma discovery for the half-life is 1.35 e28y and 11 meV for mbetabeta.

*** Status of the CUPID proposal

This scintillating crystal bolometer will be sensitive to a lifetime of e27 years at 3 sigma and m2beta at 15 meV. The DOE TPC is $35M out of $64M and will be at LNGS in Italy.

*** MicroBooNE turned off

Following a successful year-long R&D run and after collecting ~0.5M neutrino interactions in argon, MicroBooNE has put the detector in dormant mode after completing data taking on 25 Oct.

*** Interesting Letters of Interest; v11 - https://snowmass21.org/

  • Szydagis-163 "Extracting Physics from Natural Neutrinos with G3 Liquid Xenon Detector", This LOI considered the capabilities of the next-generation liquid-xenon direct-detection experiments to study neutrinos from natural sources such as solar, supernovae and atmospheric nus.
  • Billard-039 "Astrophysical neutrinos & dark matter experiments", Neutrinos are a background to direct DM experiments. This LOI considers how to characterize that background.
  • Zennamo-138 "Improving Large LArTPC Performance Through the Use of Photo-Ionizing Dopants", Anti-correlation between the energy deposited in scintillation and ionization leads to an energy smearing without the accurate utilization of both signals. Dopants will help deal with this, but always more R&D is needed.
  • Gramellini-137 "Multi-modal Pixels for Noble Element Time Projection Chambers", This LOI proposes development of multi-modal pixels for the simultaneous detection of VUV scintillation light and femtoCoulomb ionization charge. This would boost the sensitivity of noble element TPCs for physics at the low energy.
  • Arnquist-006 "An Ultra-low Background Facility to Support Next Generation Rare Event Physics Experiments", Next-generation ultra-low background experiments will require significant and collaborative efforts to achieve target radioactivity levels in order to reach required sensitivity. They propose a facility to deal with these issues.
