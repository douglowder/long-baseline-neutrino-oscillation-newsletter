Long-Baseline news, November 2002

*** Vision for long-baseline experiments at Brookhaven

A Report from the BNL Neutrino Working Group is now available. Informal Report BNL-69395 http://nwg.phy.bnl.gov/preprints.php An intense wide band neutrino beam (.5-7GeV) from a 1MW AGS proton source and 500 kT or larger water detector are envisaged. The main conclusions of the report are that the bold program is technically feasible and economically attractive.

*** NuMI goes from contractor to contractor

S.A. Healy, contractor for the NuMI tunnel, is now finished, and they have transferred the site to Ragnar Benson, contractor for the Service Buildings and Outfitting. The actual delivered product is remarkably close to what was imagined and planned for a decade. D. Bogert commented the site "almost sparkled".

*** PDG reviews its reviews

There are a number of useful reviews in the Particle Data Group's RPP (available in the US by request, hard to get in Europe, also on web at http://pdg.lbl.gov/) Reviews focus on neutrino flavors, the number of nus, 0nu double beta decay, flavor change, 2 flavor oscillation limits, 2-flavor plots, Solar nus. Reviewing the PDG, a committee suggested unifying the oscillation reviews and figures and moving them into the Review section at the front of the book.

*** Two neutrino superbeam experiments are better than one

The issue is resolving parameter degeneracies, and these are the kinds of issues facing future experimental proposals. Barger et al. have quantitatively addressed this in hep-ph/0210428.

*** Misusing quantum mechanics

From time to time, people write down the 2-neutrino probability of oscillation, improperly compound approximations, and get the wrong answer by two. This has been done again in "...The Gribov- Pontecorvo phase for neutrino vacuum propagation is right" hep-ph/0211199. Similarly in hep-ph/0208086. But Okun et al. show that Pontecorvo got the correct standard expression in late publications, and in the earlier ones, based on the same assumptions, there was either a simple math mistake or misprint. hep-ph/0211241.

*** Bi-Large Mixing

Now that Bi-maximal mixing is fairly well ruled out, theory still needs to explain why theta_23 and theta_12 are large. One ansatz which works is the Fritzsch texture (see hep-ph/0210276). Though neutrinoless double beta decay would be too small to be observed, CP violation could be measured in new long-baseline experiments, and theta_23 should not be maximal.

*** Anarchy or Hierarchy?

Which form for model of neutrino mass is more likely? According to Altarelli et al., ( hep-ph/0210342) hierarchy wins. Small mass squared differences among nearly degenerate nus are hard to get.
