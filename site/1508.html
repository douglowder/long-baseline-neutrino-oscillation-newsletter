Long-Baseline news, August 2015

*** NOvA sees numu disappearance and nue appearance

The 1st results from the NOvA experiment were presented in August. http://nova-docdb.fnal.gov/cgi-bin/ShowDocument?docid=13883 With a "full-detector-equivalent" exposure of 2.74e20pot, 33 numu CC events are identified, with 201 expected in the absence of oscillations (including 3 background). The best oscillation fits (which are good) are for dmsq_32 = 2.37 +0.16 -0.15 e-3 eV^2 (NO), and -2.40 +0.14 -0.17 (IO) and sin^2(q_23) = 0.51+-0.10. Appearance was measured using two id algorithms, Likelihood ID (LID) & Library Event Matching (LEM). Prior to unblinding, it was decided to show both results and to use LID as the primary result. For both PIDs, 6 events are expected for NO, 2 for IO, and 1 BG. NOvA measured 6 events for LID (3.3 sigma for appearance) and 11 events for LEM (5.5 sigma). Data should double by next summer.

http://natmonitor.com/2015/08/08/scientists-stunned-after-witnessing-oscillating-neutrinos-could-unlock-secrets-of-big-bang/ When you're excited about your results, you try to share that excitement as widely as possible. It is certainly possible to be misunderstood. One reporter translated the first results from NOvA on neutrino oscillations as the first ever observation of neutrino oscillations. This might be a good time to point out the caveat on the homepage of this newsletter. Please tell me if/when I report something wrong.

*** Can neutrino models predict oscillation parameters?

In arXiv:1508.06063 , Rodejohann and Xu investigate the stability of neutrino mass matrix predictions on important and currently unknown observables. They show how perturbations change the predictions. A larger neutrino mass (e.g. 0.1 eV) makes things unstable. It does not mean that models cannot predict anything, it is just supposed to illustrate that corrections to models need to be taken into account before predictions are claimed.

*** IceCube Neutrinos highlight of Cosmic Ray Conference (ICRC)

The 34th Annual Cosmic Ray Conference added "The Astrophysical Conference" to its name. http://icrc2015.nl/ It was great to sit for a week and hear hundreds of references to neutrinos without discussion of the Higgs. A clear highlight of the conference this year was the IceCube astrophysical neutrinos, with other searches, possible explanations, and proposed studies. Neutrinos are a robust leg of the "Multi-Messenger" approach to understanding cosmic rays. See e.g. arXiv:1508.01180 , arXiv:1508.01315 , arXiv:1508.01493 , arXiv:1508.03009 , arXiv:1508.01698 , arXiv:1508.02500 , arXiv:1508.03681 . arXiv:1508.04091 , arXiv:1508.06089 .

*** J-PARC upgrade could triple T2K beam flux in Japan

http://kds.kek.jp/conferenceOtherViews.py?view=standard&confId=19079 (The password is in the window that comes up.) At a workshop on JPARC, an upgrade of the accelerator to 1.3 MW is planned. The Main Ring has a design power of 750 kW in 2018-19 and will reach 1 MW with new power supplies after 2020. It will be able to operate at 1.3 MW before 2026 with improvements. The integrated number of protons on target could reach 21D21 before 2026, 3 times the current T2K, and could measure nu CP violation with > 3 sigma.

*** Gadolinium approved for use in Super-Kamiokande

http://www.symmetrymagazine.org/article/july-2015/underground-plans?email_issue=812 The idea to add Gadolinium, to help in the identification of final state neutrons in low energy neutrino events, originally dubbed GADZOOKS!, has been approved, but the name will be SuperK-Gd. Tests with EGADS (Evaluating Gadolinium's Action on Detector Systems) worked well. The next step is to drain the tank, check for leaks and fix them, replace bad phototubes, and then refill.
