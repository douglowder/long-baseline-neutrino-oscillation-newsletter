Long-Baseline news, July 2001

*** Neutrino consensus for US High Energy Physics

Many drafts of a "consensus" of the US HEP community circulated at Snowmass. Early drafts did not even include the word neutrino, but the final statement listed "the elucidation of neutrino masses and mixing" as one of 6 outstanding issues to be addressed by HEP. Strong support for the existing neutrino program was also mentioned, but endorsing a linear collider will be the most quoted conclusion. The neutrino subgroup indicated support for a neutrino superbeam the #1 priority.

*** Neutrino awareness in Utah

While at Arches Utah, I wore a "Cosmic Ray Conference" shirt, while my daughter had on a "Soudan, proton decay" t-shirt. One day we were asked "What are cosmic rays?" The next, "What does proton decay mean?" As we started to explain, we were asked each time, "Does that have something to do with neutrino mass?"

*** LANNDD

A massive Liquid Argon Detector for Proton Decay, Supernova and Solar Neutrino Studies and a Neutrino Factory Detector. If future large nucleon decay detectors are background limited, it might take a fine-grained detector to discover nucleon decay. This idea (from Cline, Learned and McDonald) is for a 70 kT active volume liquid argon TPC immersed in a magnetic field. astro-ph/0105442. It could go in Carlsbad.

*** 335 sign neutrino letter of intent

A letter of intent for neutrino experiments beyond MINOS and Mini-Boone has attracted 335 signatories. It can be found at http://www.fnal.gov/projects/muon_collider/nu/study/loi/loi.ps

*** Soudan 2 turned off

This nucleon decay and atmospheric nu detector began data taking in `89; reached 5.90 kt-years when turned off this month.

*** Fermilab Hadron Hose dropped

Another casualty of NuMI funding priorities is the hadronic hose, a wire down the beam pipe which would have increased the low energy flux and decreased near/far systematic effects.

*** Tunnel Boring Machine wet, then dry

A pump failed and the tunnel boring machine digging the NuMI tunnel got wet. But it dried out quickly and is boring again. Meanwhile, the first MINOS plane is installed. See http://www.sudan.umn.edu/Albums/MINOS/Weekly_Photos/index.html .

*** KamLAND filling

KamLAND, a reactor neutrino experiment in Japan which is expected to confirm the Large Mixing Angle solar neutrino (LMA) solution is about half full of scintillator. They hope to be running in October and present results at the Sendai Conference 02/02.

*** Super-Kamiokande unfilling

At the end of July, the K2K run ended and Super-Kamiokande started draining the tank in order to replace a few inner and a few more out phototubes. They will be able to run in super-nova mode at night with most water levels. The process of draining fixing and filling will take about six months.

*** Novel nu-e beam idea

Helium 6 has a lifetime of 0.8 seconds. Zucchelli suggests a relativistic beam of Helium 6 to make a pure nu-e beam with a perfectly known energy spectrum. See hep-ex/0107006 .

*** House funds ICE Cube, Senate Homestake

The Committee has included $15M to initiate the IceCube Neutrino Detector physics/astronomy project recently approved by the National Science Board. This project, building on the successful AMANDA demonstration, is designed to more fully develop knowledge of the origins of the universe as well as the fundamental nature of physical matter using its unique polar telescope. A house committee also recommended 11.4M for NuMI, the same as the budget request. The senate "encourages" Ice-Cube but earmarks $10M for Homestake, "a world-class underground laboratory for physics, geology and extreme biology." No extreme physics?

*** Will a Brighter Booster Help Fermilab Neutrino Experiments?

Yes. MINOS needs it to achieve originally projected nu rates.

*** Small Mixing Angle Solution Ruled out by SNO plus Super-K

It depends who you talk to, but interpretations of SNO are filling the preprint server. hep-ph/0106258 , hep-ph/0106264 , hep-ph/0107022
