Long-Baseline news, November 2024

*** Progress regarding neutrino mass limits from cosmology

According to arXiv:2411.14524, "As cosmological datasets grow, the nu mass will soon be either measured or a bound in clear conflict with oscillation experiments will be placed." That paper separates 2 cosmological nu mass effects: background evolution related to the neutrino energy density, & kinematic perturbations related to nu free-streaming. Limits Sum(m) < 50 meV is the strongest limit compared to Sum > 60 meV from oscillations. A new DESI limit in arXiv:2411.12022 gives Sum(m) < 71 meV at 95%CL, which disagrees with the inverted mass order. arXiv:2411.06000 uses SPT-3G polarization data to conclude Sum(m) < 75 meV. Stay tuned.

*** CEvNS - Standard meme for Coherent Neutrino Scattering

In the literature, CNS, CNNS and CENNS can be found. The "E" is for elastic, which is valuable since otherwise it can and does get confused with coherent pi production at GeV energies, a totally different process. The pair NN reminds many nuclear physicists of nucleon-nucleon scattering. CE$\nu$ENS works in word but in a plain text environment is just plain ugly. So the community has settled on "CEvNS", pronounced "sevens". Spread the meme!

*** Gerry Garvey 1935-2024

https://www.funerals.coop/obituaries/gerald-garvey He introduced nucleon correlations to neutrino physicists, all after he retired in 2001. When you hear someone say the words 2p2h or MEC as if these are household names, smile and think of Gerry Garvey. It was a long road of understanding to thankfully get to where we are now and Gerry was right at the front showing the way. He connected the keywords and showed that the enhancement of nu cross sections around 1 GeV is real. He noticed the enhancement due to np-nh in the Martini model (2010) https://inspirehep.net/literature/833905, although he regretted dismissing the idea at first.
