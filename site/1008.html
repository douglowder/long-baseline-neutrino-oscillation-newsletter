Long-Baseline news, August 2010

*** Our first value for the CP violating phase delta

A full 3-flavor Super-K analysis presented at Neutrino 2010 had a normal hierarchy delta between 140.8 and 297.3 degrees at 68%CL, with a best fit near 220. It was pointed out to me that Chen & Mahanthappa, in two Phys. Lett. papers predicted 227 degrees, and 9 parameters predicting well 22 quantities. See arXiv:0904.1721 - http://indico.cern.ch/getFile.py/access?contribId=224&sessionId=9&resId=1&materialId=slides&confId=73981

*** NOvA passes DOE review

NOvA is spending money, so it gets an annual DOE progress review, until it is done (CD-4). CD-4 deliverables will be to upgrade the Fermilab proton source from 320kW to 700kW, build a new Far Detector Hall and a 14 kiloton Far Detector, build a 222 t Near Detector underground after an Integration Prototype Near Detector takes data on the surface. The committee provided the usual findings and comments, but overall things are going well.

*** LHC trouble --> Tevatron extension? --> NOvA delays?

The Fermilab Program advisory committee met 27 Aug to consider a 3 year Tevatron extension for 7pb^(-1) (beyond Sep 2011). http://www.fnal.gov/directorate/program_planning/Aug2010PACPublic/Aug2010PACReport.pdf They advised yes, whether there was additional funding or not. This opportunity didn't seem to exist a few years ago looking at LHC schedules of the time. But opportunities have costs. With extended Tevatron, NOvA construction remains on schedule, but the NuMI beam power will be limited to 400 kW during Collider operations. The ramp up to 700 kW would be delayed until the Recyler Ring recommissioning is accomplished. Gary Feldman showed the PAC the NOvA impacts, which include delaying the first results by 2 years; final results by 1.5 yr, & add 3.7M$ to cost. http://www.fnal.gov/directorate/program_planning/Aug2010PACPublic/Feldman.pdf

*** Unlikely role for solar neutrinos

http://news.stanford.edu/news/2010/august/sun-082310.html , They find evidence for solar periodicity rates in nuclear decay data. Do solar neutrinos affect radioactivity rates on earth? It doesn't sound right. That makes it intriguing. arXiv:1007.3318 , & 1006.4848

*** T2K Progress and Status

T2K accumulated 3.35e+19 POT through 26 June, and 22 neutrino events were observed by Super-Kamiokande through mid May. There is a shutdown and maintenance period for the neutrino beam facility and the near detector ND280 until the end of October, and beam operation with higher power (>100kW) is planned from November. Maintenance will be carried out to achieve a more stable operation of the beam. The barrel ECALs, which are the remaining ND280 sub-detectors, are being installed.

*** Lorentz Violation

I'm hoping that the phenomenon of Lorentz violation doesn't exist, so that we don't have to explain the phrase to non-scientists. But a workshop on this subject elicited many ideas that might be of interest to neutrino physicists. There is "A quantum field model for tachyonic neutrinos with Lorentz symmetry breaking" arXiv:1007.5418, "Lorentz & CPT violation in neutrino oscillations" arXiv:1008.0411, "Test for Lorentz and CPT violation in MiniBooNE", arXiv:1008.0906, and "A New Lorentz-Violating Model of Neutrino Oscillations", arXiv:1008.0105; to name a few.
