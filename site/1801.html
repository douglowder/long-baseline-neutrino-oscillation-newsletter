Alexander the Great issue

*** Alex Radovic presents new NOvA results at Fermilab

Now with 8.85e20 POT (14-kton equivalent), NOvA has 50% more beam than in the 2016 analysis. They have improved the analysis by tuning energy bins, retuned cross section modeling, improved simulation and used data driven NuMI flux estimates developed by MINERvA. They expect 763 numu events (no oscillations) and see 126. Delta m^2_32 is 2.444e-03 eV^2 +- 0.08. The upper octant is preferred at 0.2 sigma, sin^2(q_23) = 0.558+0.041-0.033 upper, 0.475+0.036-0.044 lower, octant. The previous analysis had suggested non-maximal mixing. Rejection of maximal has changed from 2.6 sigma to 0.8 sigma. For electron neutrino appearance, NOvA sees 66 events with 20.5 BG. 48 signal would be expected for delta = 1.5pi normal order; 20 for 0.5pi, inverted. The inverted order is almost rejected at 2 sigma. NOvA is currently running with antineutrinos. http://nova-docdb.fnal.gov/cgi-bin/ShowDocument?docid=25938 Live stream at http://vms.fnal.gov/asset/detail?recid=1952641

*** Alex Sousa proposing NOvA test beam

Alex Sousa from the University of Cincinnati is leading an effort to put two 2x2 31 plane NOvA blocks in a test beam at the Fermilab Test Beam Facility. A tertiary beam with momenta 0.2-2 GeV/c could improve models of hadronic and em response and reduce energy scale uncertainties. A November presentation estimated running could take place at MC7 Jan-Jun 2019.

*** Record neutrino power at J-PARC

(From J-PARC Newsletter 69) The neutrino experimental facility ran October - December for T2K. Recently the beam power reached a new record value, 475 kW. The beam is stable in the operation with anti-neutrino mode. The accumulated POT is 3.8x10^20 as of Dec. 20. In the spring to summer 2018 run they aim to double the antineutrino statistics for electron neutrino appearance studies.
