Sports Issue

*** Borexino sees the CERN beam.

If you put a neutrino detector into a neutrino beam, it sees neutrinos. Last year Borexino filled with about 300 t of water in their detector with 2240 photomultiplier tubes looking in. During two brief periods of CNGS running, they obtained 5 events when 5 were expected, and twelve events when 10 were expected. During normal operation, they expect 315 events/day total and 47 CC+NC events with a vertex in the detector. Every neutrino that passes through OPERA will pass through Borexino first. In December the first 11 m^3 of scintillator were added to the inner vessel. They are now simultaneously draining the water and adding the scintillator, and they plan to complete in May.

*** MiniBooNE and boxing

Results of a short-baseline neutrino oscillation search from the MiniBooNE experiment at Fermilab are scheduled to be discussed at a seminar at Fermilab on April 11. MiniBooNE is sensitive to numu to nue oscillations at the same value of L/E as a reported signal from the LSND experiment at Los Alamos. I expect them to show the presence or absence of an excess of low energy electrons (below 2 GeV). The "blind" analysis has been done with a large amount of neutrino running. MiniBooNE is currently running with anti-neutrinos, and they got to do systematic checks with two unexpected antineutrino beam configurations for a few months. Ten metal plate were designed to provide an opportunity to shorten the length of the decay pipe and two of them fell down, inadvertently partially blocking part of that pipe. Repairs will be done 4/11.

*** Unified Reactor Neutrino Calculations and the Final Four

Since the International Working Group on Reactor Neutrinos starting considering a new experiment to measure theta_13, many reactor sites have been considered. There are now four experiments moving ahead: Angra in Brazil, Daya Bay in China, Double Chooz in France and RENO in South Korea. A detailed quantitative discussion of the theta-13 reach of these four experiments is presented by Mention et al. in arXiv:0704.0498 (Beware the new ArXiv numbering scheme.) Sensitivities depend on assumed systematic errors, backgrounds, run times, and reactor power assumptions, in addition to detector layout as proposed. 90% CL limits are calculated (sin^2 2 theta-13) for Double Chooz (0.0278), Daya Bay Mid with LAII off (0.0410), Daya Bay Mid with LAII on (0.038), Daya Bay Far (0.011) and RENO (0.0213). Completion dates are not specified.

*** Models, Super-models and the neutrino mass from cosmology

Maybe theorists should take a page from the Sports Illustrated Swimsuit issue and call their best models - super-models. The WMAP data can be analyzed with different models and the neutrino mass or limits obtained vary. This model dependence was called a systematic error at WIN07, but I don't think that's right. Hannestand & Wong have looked at realistic assumptions about neutrino mass sensitivity from future high redshift galaxy surveys, and get 0.05 eV at 95% CL for a minimal 8-parameter cosmological model. In a more general model framework, 3 times worse. astro-ph/0703031
