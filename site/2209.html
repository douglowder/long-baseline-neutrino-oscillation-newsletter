Long-Baseline news, September 2022

*** Early funding for ton scale 0nu2beta decay

https://science.osti.gov/-/media/np/nsac/pdf/202209/Hallman_DOE_NP_NSAC_Meeting_09282022.pdf At the Sep. 2022 NSAC meeting, Tim Hallman, Associate Director of the Office of Science announced that >$8M will support early work on a neutrinoless double beta decay experiment.

*** Clash of the Titans - DUNE and Hyper-Kamiokande

With a conclusion that "The unexpected may be a long shot worth betting on," Science magazine compared in detail the competition and contrast between the two large long-baseline experiments being developed to measure leptonic CP violation in the U.S. and Japan. https://www.science.org/content/article/showdown-two-huge-neutrino-detectors-will-vie-probe-matter-s-origins

*** P-ONE, Pacific Ocean Neutrino Experiment

P-ONE is a proposed km^3-scale nu telescope to be installed in the Pacific in collaboration with the optical communications and deep- sea data network operated by Ocean Networks Canada. They are an international collaboration of researchers with plans for an array of km-long mooring lines around 2660 m deep in the relatively flat Cascadia Basin, to monitor optical bioluminescence and for other oceanographic and marine research. See arXiv:2209.14710.

*** Induced bioluminescence

Phototubes which measure neutrinos in the otherwise dark deep ocean also see faint glowing creatures which emit light like fireflies. But you don't need a phototube to see the light in 3 "biobays" in Puerto Rico, the brightest of which is on the nearby island of Vieques. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VwfG9s1A8pI If you visit this amazing place, pick a night before moonrise.

The Photon Detector System is now installed as part of the SBN Program's near detector, the Short-Baseline Neutrino Detector.

*** Neutrinos at the LHC

https://www.symmetrymagazine.org/article/catching-neutrinos-at-the-lhc Experiments FASER and SND@LHC are running and should see 1000s of neutrinos. New proposals include: FASERv2, Advanced SND, FASER2, FORMOSA and FLArE.

*** Snowwhite - Snowmass neutrino white papers; v6 -

https://snowmass21.org/submissions/nf

  • "Beyond the Standard Model effects on Neutrino Flavor", arXiv:2203.10811, Topics described include additional neutrino states, new interactions, neutrino decay and tests of fundamental physics principles.
  • "The Selena Neutrino Experiment", arXiv:2203.08779. This summarizes the broad program of a large imaging detector with a 10-ton target of 82Se.
  • "Neutrino Scattering Measurements on Hydrogen and Deuterium", arXiv:2203.11298. There is motivation for new measurements of neutrino scattering on H and D for nu-N scattering amplitudes.
  • "Low Background kTon-Scale Liquid Ar Time Projection Chambers", arXiv:2203.08821. It is possible to increase sensitivity to low energy physics in a DUNE-like module with careful controls over radiopurity. Sensitivity to supernova and solar neutrinos can be enhanced with improved MeV-scale reach.
  • "The Sanford Underground Research Facility", arXiv:2203.08293. The capabilities at SURF for a wide range of technology and science applications are reviewed. Details about facilities and plans for possible future expansion are described.
