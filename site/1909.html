Long-Baseline news, September 2019

*** First KATRIN result on tritium beta decay

The long-in-development KATRIN direct mass experiment has given an upper limit on the effective nu_e mass from tritium beta decay of 1.1 eV at 90% CL based on the first four week science run. See arXiv:1909.06048 . Note that this is not the mass of any particle but a mixture of the 3 nu masses and angles in the 3-nu paradigm. The expected ultimate sensitivity is 0.2 eV as described in the companion paper on KATRIN operation in arXiv:1909.06069 . See https://www.sciencemag.org/news/2019/09/massive-blimplike-experiment-lowers-weight-limit-neutrino

*** Neutrino 2020 registration opens

The International Conference on Neutrino Physics and Astrophysics (Neutrino 2020), will be held 21-27 June 2020. It will take place at the Hyatt Regency Chicago (downtown). Poster abstracts open 1 January. Registration is open at https://conferences.fnal.gov/nu2020/.

A new result on CP violation from T2K appears in arXiv:1910.03887. This September issue is late. More about this T2K result in October's newsletter.

*** DUNE near detector workshop 20-23 October 2019 at DESY

This is an open workshop concentrating on the international contributions to the DUNE Multi-Purpose (Near) Detector. https://indico.fnal.gov/event/21340/

*** DUNE Module of Opportunity Workshop 12-13 Nov at Brookhaven

A preliminary technology choice has been made for 3 of the 4 10-kT modules for the 40-kT DUNE detector. This workshop will be a unique opportunity to shape the technology used for the 4th DUNE module. The Collaboration invites the broader particle physics community to participate. https://www.bnl.gov/dmo2019/

*** Neutrinos at the Fermilab art gallery

The current exhibit in the Fermilab art gallery is Cosmic Design by Shanthi Chandrasekar through the end of October. She wrote, " I would sometimes imagine myself as a neutrino, racing through the cosmos at speeds close to that of light, wondering where I came from and where I am headed." She made ink drawings of neutrino pathways, appropriately three of them. https://news.fnal.gov/2019/09/behind-the-cosmic-design/

*** Rant about name badges at workshops and conferences

Most large physics meetings use name badges, with either lanyards around the neck or clips to attach to clothing. I don't like lanyards, but I realize that many others don't like what clips do to their clothes. A very common problem with lanyards is that 50% of the time, the name-tag is turned around and you can't see any name. A very few clever conferences print the name on both sides.

*** LBNF funding status

FY19 funding for LBNF was $130M. The president's request for FY20 was $100M, but the House and Senate bills were $171M. But no bill is yet signed and the U.S. government is operating on a continuing resolution through 21 November 2019. https://www.aip.org/fyi/federal-science-budget-tracker#tabs-section-doe-science
