*** Masatoshi Koshiba 1926-2020

Koshiba was the instigator of both the Kamioka nucleon decay experiment (Kamiokande) and Super-K. He won the Nobel Prize in 2002 "for pioneering contributions to astrophysics, in particular for the detection of cosmic neutrinos." His instincts made him pursue the atmospheric neutrino anomaly, which was explained by neutrino oscillations, when many others dismissed cosmic ray results as being too untrustworthy. https://www.nytimes.com/2020/11/16/science/masatoshi-koshiba-dead.html

*** Samoil Bilenky 1928-2020

Samoil Bilenky, from JINR, Dubna, Russia, has passed away 5 Nov. He was a founder of modern neutrino physics and had a leading role in its evolution to its present mature state. In a fruitful collaboration and co-authorship with Pontecorvo, which started in the 70's, he developed the theory of nu oscillations in vacuum and laid the foundations of the phenomenological theory of nu mixing, on which every theoretical model of neutrino mass generation (including GUTs) is based. In the early days of his career he left his mark in the field of polarized scattering phenomena obtaining important results on the relation between the particles intrinsic parities and the polarization effects in scattering processes. He was an excellent teacher, mentor and inspiration for many young researchers in the field of elementary particle and nu physics and had collaborators and friends in many countries. His human warmth and benevolent personality embodied the best humanistic & cultural traditions of the Russian Intelligentsia to which he belonged.

*** Interesting Letters of Interest; v3 - https://snowmass21.org/

  • Marco_Vignati-061, "BULLKID: Low-threshold Kinetic Inductance Detectors for neutrino and dark matter searches" Applications of cryogenic sensors are being used to detect CEvN). Utilizing the capabilities of Kinetic Inductance Detectors, BULLKID could Dark Matter interactions close to the neutrino floor.
  • David_Curtin-184 "Recent Progress & Next Steps for MATHUSLA LLP" This proposed detector anticipates placement at CERN near CMS. It would improve the detection of hadronically decaying Long-Lived Particles (LLP) and has a sensitivity towards low-scale LLP models like SM+S and Right-Handed Neutrinos.
  • Kyle_Leach-055 "Lab-Based keV-Scale Sterile Nu searches & BeEST" Beryllium Electron-capture with Superconducting Tunnel junctions (BeEST) would track 7Be→7Li reactions via superconducting quantum sensors. By measuring the low-E recoiling atom in an electron capture decay. HUNTER is a similar ongoing experiment.
  • Speller-182 "Toward Sensitivity to the Neutrino Normal Hierarchy with Quantum Calorimetry" CUPID-1T is a potentially multi-site, highly-segmented calorimeter with ≈1 ton of isotopic mass with a focus on providing coverage of the Inverted Hierarchy region. It uses cryogenic calorimetry and includes a robust readout system for large macrocalorimeter arrays.
  • Orebi_Gann-089 "Astrophysical neutrinos at THEIA" THEIA, A large hybrid optical neutrino detector, could leverage both Cherenkov & scintillation light in a single detector and could measure phenomena caused by astrophysical nus. It has a high sensitivity for SN activity and adequate sensitivity for DSNB identification.
