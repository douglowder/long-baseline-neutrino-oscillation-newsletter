*** ProtoDUNE-SP filled and working

The single phase prototype (i.e. liquid argon) for DUNE's first detector is ProtoDUNE-SP, which has been filled with 800 tons of liquid argon. Built in the CERN Neutrino Platform for studies in a charged particle test beam, the purification for the argon is ongoing, so the first tracks are close to the wire plane. http://news.fnal.gov/2018/09/first-tracks-in-prototype-for-neutrino-experiment/ For a detailed look at ProtoDUNE-SP, see 28 Sep Wine & Cheese http://docs.dunescience.org/cgi-bin/ShowDocument?docid=10464

*** ProtoDUNE-DP high voltage tests

The two phase ProtoDUNE-DP, also known as WA105, has also built a prototype in the CERN Neutrino Platform. The idea is to drift electrons in liquid argon, but read out the hits in gaseous argon at charge readout planes (CRP) where there is electron amplification. between the CRP and the electric field of the Large Electron Multiplier (LEM) planes. The first cold test was June/July. A few problems were fixed and in a second test, all the LEMs reached 32kV/cm. A photon detector system is ready for installation. https://www.symmetrymagazine.org/article/a-dual-phase-dune

*** Neutrino-4 reactor neutrino oscillation results

This reactor experiment looks at the flux and spectrum 6-12 m from a reactor core. Sterile solution of reactor and gallium anomalies is excluded at 99.7%CL. But an oscillation effect with dmsquared = 7 eV^2 and sin^2(2theta) = 0.35 is observed, also with 99.7% CL (presumably a-posteriori). arXiv:1809.10561
