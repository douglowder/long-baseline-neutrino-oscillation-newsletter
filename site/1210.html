Long-Baseline news, October 2012

*** ICFA Neutrino Panel

ICFA has set up a neutrino panel with the following mandate: "To promote international cooperation in the development of the accelerator-based neutrino oscillation program and to promote international collaboration in the development a neutrino factory as a future intense source of neutrinos for particle physics experiments." The panel will have 15 members; five each for Asia, the Americas and Europe. In addition to the geographical balance, the panel should have a balance of expertise ranging from theory and phenomenology, neutrino experiment and accelerators. Send nominations for US members to the intensity frontier conveners, De Gouvea, Pitts, Scholberg &/or Zeller. [email protected]

*** Further Exploitation of NuMI, new email list for study group

A 6 month study interested in further use of the FNAL NuMI beam will see whether there are sensible ideas. Examples: GLADE, a Cherenkov Detector in Lake Superior or a mine pit, and a 5 kt Cherenkov detector in the MINOS hall. The focus is delta-CP. While none of the baselines are long enough for the MH, its likely that anyway MH will be uncovered by measurements over the next decade. A 200 kT Cherenkov detector can get the delta CP to between 20-50 degrees error across all delta (GLOBES simulations under way). Join the email list by sending to [email protected] with no subject and 1 line in the body: subscribe e-numi.

*** Important election starts Monday November 5

The LBNE cospokesperson election will run from 5 to 16 November. The candidates are Chris Mauger from Los Alamos and Robert Wilson from Colorado State University. Only collaborators can vote.

*** LBNE has surface operation review

Simulations indicate a combination of passive shielding and the precise pattern recognition provided by LAr can reject backgrounds to at most a few events per year. An independent review concluded the analysis is sound and risk is manageable.

*** LBNE has CD1 review,

LBNE's cost review at Fermilab was the day after Hurricane Sandy hit the east coast and, among other problems, made travel from Washington, where several reviewers were located, and from Long Island, where several reviewees were located, difficult. But the review went well, and LBNE has been recommended for CD-1, subject to dotting a few i's. The cost estimate is $867M with a range of $687-$945M. CD3A is now planned for 4/2015 and CD2 for 4/2016. http://www.fnal.gov/directorate/OPMO/Projects/LBNE/DOERev/2012/10_30/FullCloseout.pdf

*** MicroBooNE construction begins

Construction has started on the MicroBooNE Liquid Argon neutrino detector at Fermilab. In spring of 2013, the collaboration will have finished building the LArTPC and will move it from its current location, the DZero assembly building, to its permanent home at the eventual Liquid Argon Test Facility. http://www.fnal.gov/pub/today/archive_2012/today12-10-04_tpcReadmore.html

*** Stewardship of double beta decay in U.S. DOE

One unanswered question about neutrinos is whether they are of a Dirac or Majorana nature. The way to test this is with a zero neutrino double beta decay experiment. There are several ideas for experiments, such as Majorana, Gerda, CUORE, EXO, etc. http://www.hep.anl.gov/ndk/hypertext/double_beta_decay.html At the Community Planning Meeting, it was pointed out that in the U.S., this would be funded by nuclear physics section of the DOE, and not the high energy physics section. If there are hints for the inverted hierarchy, more HEP people will get interested.

*** Statistics of the mass hierarchy determination

Since the mass hierarchy has 2 possible values, some statistical issues differ from measurements of a continuous parameter. This and related issues are discussed in X. Qian et al. arXiv:1210.3651
