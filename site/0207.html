*** Long-baseline neutrino parameter degeneracies

If you make a measurement of something like an event rate, and it maps into two possible values of a physics parameter, this is called a degeneracy. In extracting neutrino mixing angles and CP phases it is increasingly apparent that such degeneracies are possible and must be kept in mind. Mena's discussion at NUFAC02 http://www.hep.ph.ic.ac.uk/NuFact02/Scientific-programme/files/Wednesday/wg2/A05_mena.ps It focuses on Theta_13 and CP-delta degeneracies. This emphasizes the need for experiments at many L/E's. Also see Burguet-Castell et al., (hep-ph/0207080) which states, "Superbeams and Neutrino Factories are not alternative facilities for exploring neutrino oscillation physics, but successive steps.

*** MiniBooNE Full

On Friday May 3 saw the last of 250,000 gallons of pure mineral oil pumped into the MiniBooNE detector. This successfully concludes a process which started on 7-Dec-2001 when the first two railcars of oil were delivered to the site.

*** 3M

Visit www.3m.com and you'll learn "anything is possible with enough duct tape". This proposal is for 10 100kt H20 detectors at NUSL for proton decay, supernova neutrinos, UHE neutrinos from astrophysical sources, and long-baseline neutrino oscillations. A SAGENAP presentation focused on the latter, though that is outside their charge (Scientific Assessment Group on Experimental Non-Accelerator Physics, a committee chaired by Eugene Loh (NSF) and James Stone (DOE). SAGENAP didn't see anything to approve or disapprove, but urged cooperation among groups seeking to establish a large underground detector program for the US. http://doe-hep.hep.net/SAGENAP-Report-2002_final.pdf

*** Super-Kamiokande Repair funded in US

Super-K is in the process of redistributing its unbroken PMT's for 50% of the original inner photocathode density, and replacing the broken out tubes. The outer tubes were a traditional US responsibility. SAGENAP strongly endorsed the Super-K repair proposal, noting "... the funding of $2.1M to the US groups must be one of the historic bargains in science funding." For 2003, KEK will extend the run from 6 months to one year for K2K.

*** Where are all the nuclear powered submarines today?

Detwiler et al. (hep-ex/0207001) look at the effect of naval nuclear reactors on the study of oscillations. The presence of naval reactors at unknown locations and times may limit the accuracy of future very-long-baseline reactor-based neutrino oscillation experiments. But use of a large Russian Icebreaker for precision experiments is also possible. The possibility of running the reactors at full power with the ship stationary needs to be investigated.
