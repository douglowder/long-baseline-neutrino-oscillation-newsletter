Long-Baseline news, June 2007

*** Borexino Starts search for neutrinos

Borexino, a large tank of liquid scintillator at the Gran Sasso Laboratory in Italy, finished replacing the water in its tank with scintillator on May 15th. As of May 16, the first run for solar neutrinos with the full detector has begun. http://borex.lngs.infn.it/

*** SciBooNE starts search for anti-neutrinos

SciBooNE, a calorimeter upstream of MiniBooNE in the 8 GeV FNAL neutrino beam, started taking data on May 30th. This is ahead of their original construction schedule. The 8 GeV beam is currently tuned for antineutrinos. It may go back to neutrinos after the end of August. Their goal is cross sections.

*** ICARUS installed in tank at Gran Sasso in Italy

ICARUS T600 consists of two 300 ton cryostats which will be filled with liquid argon in the CNGS beam at the Gran Sasso. The 2 tanks were recently put into a larger cooling vessel which holds both tanks in preparation for filling with liquid argon later in 2007. One of the tanks operated previously at Pavia. Filling should be complete for the CNGS beam which may start May 1, 2008.

*** For Fermilab, in Lieu of the ILC; neutrinos

What should Fermilab do if the ILC in its future is more "future" than anything else? A steering committee is investigating Fermilab's "intermediate" future, and neutrinos are a big part of the planning. Six of 16 "one page proposals" involved neutrino experiments, and two more involve large proton decay detectors which also serve as neutrino detectors. The two proton decay ideas are UNO, a multi-cubical water Cerenkov detector 60x60x180 m^3, and an unnamed 500-1000 kton water Cerenkov detector at Homestake DUSEL. http://www.fnal.gov/directorate/Longrange/Steering_Public/community_letters.html

*** One melodic idea for Fermilab - NuSONG

NuSONG, one of the 1-page Fermilab ideas, stands for Neutrino Scattering On Glass. Its main purpose is the study of neutrino-electron scattering. The plan is to use a beam which is similar to NuTeV, but with higher rate, and a detector inspired by Charm II, but with more segmentation and tonnage. http://www.fnal.gov/directorate/Longrange/Steering_Public/files/OnePager_Conrad_Fisher.pdf

*** New LNGS2 site for MODULaR Lab

Italy/INFN is looking at a new laboratory for housing a large (20 kton) liquid argon detector. The lab would be off-axis of the CNGS beam, not in the National Park or water table, or tunnel. Sites on both sides of mountain are being considered, with an excavation costing 50-60 M Euro.

*** MODULaR Detector Physics Goals

A new CNGS detector could compete with T2K and NOvA if built in 4 years. The baseline is the same as NOvA and meson production scales with the beam energy - 400 GeV. Compared to NOvA and T2K, neutral current backgrounds to nu-e appearance could be significantly reduced, but intrinsic nu-e would be similar.

*** Future CNGS beam possibilities

Key to future CNGS neutrino experiments is the possibility to upgrade beam from the SPS for higher neutrino fluxes. During some of the time, the LHC will certainly have priority. An analysis of cycle time, beam sharing, power limitations and possible new hardware can be found in CERN AB-2007-013(PAF) & http://cryodet.lngs.infn.it/agenda/Talk%20Cryodet%202007/06-14_Afternoon/CNGSProtonFlux140607.ppt

*** Extrusion success for NOvA neutrino experiment

The planned off-axis long-baseline experiment in Minnesota using the NuMI beam will use horizontal and vertical plastic extrusions filled with liquid scintillator. These 15.5 m long walls have to be highly reflective. The vertical walls will be thicker to provide structural support. Recent tests at Extrutech Plastics Inc. in Wisconsin successfully made thick-walled verticals and thin-walled horizontals using the same custom PVC formula.
