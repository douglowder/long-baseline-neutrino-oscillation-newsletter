Long-Baseline news, September 2008

*** CNGS running long-baseline nu beam from Switzerland to Italy

The 2008 CERN neutrino run has 5e18 protons on target (pot) up to 28 August. They are now running at the design of 2e13 protons per pulse & 2 pulses per cycle, but they have only half the expected pot (but the slope is increasing). OPERA finished installing 146621 bricks in June, but the supplier ran out of lead and 5000 more will be installed at the end of the year. They have 399 events on target so far, and expect 1 tau event by the end of the year. Borexino expects 315 events/day. They see both prompt events (from muons) as well as a secondary peak from neutrons. LVD is seeing the beam as well, and ICARUS now expects to fill with liquid argon in October.

*** CNGS finds earth's magnetic field

CNGS started running in 2006. One anomaly was an asymmetry in the horizontal muon counters, and a smaller one in the vertical counters. When they corrected for the earth's magnetic field, including attenuation in the decay tube stainless steel, the distributions were well explained. Several problems found during the 2007 run have been fixed for 2008. They had a leak in the water outlet of the cooling circuit of the reflector after 4e5 pulses. This was traced to a design fault in ceramic insulator brazings which have all been replaced. Cracked busbar flexible connections to the reflector were replaced with continuous plates. A large fraction of electronics were in a tunnel area where the ventilation system failed due to radiation effects after 7.9e17pot. The electronics has now been moved and shielded.

*** Borexino sees two solar neutrino signals

Borexino has presented the first simultaneous measurement of solar neutrinos from the vacuum dominated region (7Be) and from the matter enhanced. They also have the first measurement of 8B in real time below 5 MeV. They find the 8B neutrinos in the region from 2.8 to 16.3 MeV, where they find 0.26 +-0.04(stat) +-0.02(sys) count/day/100ton after cutting and subtracting several backgrounds. Non-oscillation is excluded at 4.2 sigma. Assuming the high-Z BP07 flux, P(ee) is 0.35 +- 0.10 at 8.6 MeV mean energy. In the future they'll study the CNO, pep and high energy pp neutrinos, as well as geoneutrinos; and hope for a supernova. See arXiv:0808.2868

*** LAGUNA scientists studying 7 underground sites in Europe

Large Apparatus for Grand Unification and Neutrino Astrophysics The European ApPEC roadmap recommended a new large European infrastructure be put forward, as a future international multi- purpose facility on the 10^5-10^6 ton scale for improved studies of proton decay and of low-energy nus from astrophysical origin. The LAGUNA structure was proposed and accepted Nov 2005. Mature design and proposals should emerge in 2010. 100 scientists from 11 European countries are studying 7 sites: Boulby UK; CanFranc, Spain; Frejus, France, Pyhasalmi, Finland; Sieroszowice, Poland; Slanic, Romania; to be determined, Italy. Baselines for neutrino beams from CERN from 130 to 2300 km are available.

*** Platonic solids and Neutrinos at NOW workshop

http://www.ba.infn.it/~now/now2008/Program/program_video.html A good review of neutrino physics took place at NOW2008 Neutrino Oscillation Workshop. Not only are the slides available from the program web site, but streaming video too. The first 4 sessions (hexahedron, octahedron, dodecahedron, icosahedron) were organized in terms of increasing energy; the 5th session was associated with all energy scales (tetrahedron).
