*** Did Einstein say that? Facebook says yes.

A book for newly elected officials in Illinois quotes Albert Einstein as saying, "the only reason for time is so everything doesn't happen all at once". That didn't sound like him, so I looked online, finding at checkyourfact.com that a search of 80,000 Einstein archive documents found no mention of this quote. https://checkyourfact.com/2019/08/22/fact-check-albert-einstein-reason-time-everything-doesnt-happen-once/

*** Who named the neutrino?

A popular answer is Fermi, who did popularize the term. But I believe the correct answer is Amaldi, though Fermi was there. I learned this from Lincoln Wolfenstein, who could dramatically roll his rrrrr as he pronounced it. It is documented in Physics Reports, Volume 111, pp1-331, September 1984, note 277.

*** Dedication of Stagg Field and Site A as APS Historic Sites

On 14 July this year, the American Physical Society dedicated Stagg Field at the U of Chicago, and Chicago Pile Site A (AKA Red Gate Woods) as APS historic sites. CP1, the first self- sustaining chain reaction, though only 200 W, was also the first manmade (anti)neutrino source. Lincoln Wolfenstein was a Chicago undergraduate at the time, who walked by Stagg Field every day. He once said that if he'd known that so many neutrinos were pouring out of Stagg Field, he would have crossed the street. https://www.aps.org/programs/honors/history/historicsites/index.cfm

*** The NuFACT 2024 meeting will be in Europe

NuFACT 2023, the 24th International Workshop on Neutrinos from Accelerators, is taking place in Korea, which is in Asia. http://nufact2023.snu.ac.kr/ Meetings alternate among continents, and NuFACT 2024 will be in Europe. Candidate sites are in Liverpool and Tel Aviv. Despite the 2016 non-binding referendum, Liverpool is in Europe. And Israel is on the CERN council, so that is in Europe too. I've never understood why Europe and Asia are separate continents, geographically or geologically. And Africa and America are named after Europeans, while Europe is named after a mythical goddess from Phoenicia, which is in Asia.
