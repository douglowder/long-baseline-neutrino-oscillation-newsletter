Long-Baseline news, July 2005

*** Underground Lab narrowed to Henderson & Homestake

The National Science Foundation (NSF) selected two sites for its "Deep Underground Science and Engineering Laboratory (DUSEL) Site and Conceptual Design." Homestake (South Dakota) and Henderson (Colorado) will receive $0.5M to produce a conceptual design for a possible DUSEL. The awards result from the 2nd stage of a 3-stage process that is providing input for NSF's future decision on DUSEL.

*** Scoping Study for Future Neutrino Facility from NUFACT meeting

A Scoping Study for a future Neutrino Facility which would incorporate a Nu Factory and/or high intensity Superbeam was launched at NuFact05. It will elaborate the physics case, define the baseline options for such a facility and its detectors and identify the R&D program to lay the foundations for a complete design. Comparisons will be made with other options e.g. beta beams. A report will be done Sep 2006. Contact: Yori Nagashima (Physics Group), ; Mike Zisman (Accelerator Group), ; Alain Blondel (Accelerator Group), ; Peter Dornan (overall) . See http://hepunx.rl.ac.uk/uknf/wp4/scoping/

*** Numerous P5 meetings ahead

The Particle Physics Project Prioritization Panel (P5) has been reconstituted. The chairman is Abe Seiden and the other members are at http://www.science.doe.gov/hep/NewP5MembershipList.shtm. They've been asked for a new roadmap for the field which "integrates various projects into a coherent plan based on scientific promise, cost, and technical and budgetary constraints." It will cover LHC, Linear Collider, and input from subpanels on neutrino physics, dark matter and dark energy.

*** More on patenting neutrino light

http://www.patentstorm.us/patents/6891310.html Last months' Neutrino light patent generated lots of email. Views ranged from "It doesn't matter" to "This should be challenged because a claim could be made against a real neutrino detector." It claims to use "microwaves to create neutrino force differentials on piezoelectric material." The patentor also writes "When applied to the universe and then to neutrinos, surprising theories emerge." Not too many neutrino detectors are patented, though Joe Weber patented "Method of and apparatus for generating neutrinos and/or antineutrinos, a neutrino modulation method, and a neutrino beam generator" Link
