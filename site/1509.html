Long-Baseline news, September 2015

"Back to the Future" Cubs World Series edition

*** Long-Baseline experiments and baseball oscillations

Long-baseline and short-baseline experiments have the etymology of their name from baseball diamonds where the baselines run from home plate to the left and right field foul poles. Baselines at the 30 major league fields range from 92 m to 108.2 m. The one team on the metric system chose exactly 100 m. Chicago's Wrigley Field has the longest baselines, explaining why I am a Cubs fan.

*** SOX (SOurce eXperiment) to test sterile nus in Borexino

SOX is scheduled to begin by the end of 2016. The schedule is driven by the source delivery from Mayak. A transportation route for it has been worked out. The licensing of the containers and other paperwork are ongoing. The pit under Borexino where the source will be deployed has been retrofitted with the rail tracks to wheel the source in place. Finally, the 2.2 t tungsten shield is under production by Xiamen in China. They recently conducted tests on a smaller sample to check it structural soundness and brittleness in case of drop. Borexino will conduct an extensive calibration campaign early next year to calibrate outer areas of the detector (not used for solar phase) in preparation for SOX.

*** Harry Lipkin's Final World Series (1921-2015)

A theoretical physicist famous for "pedestrian" lectures making many issues of particle physics accessible to experimental grad students, Harry spent his last few years addressing issues in neutrino oscillations. He criticized a familiar derivation of the oscillation formula by pointing out that nobody measures time. arXiv:1005.4183 , arXiv:1003.4023 , arXiv:0905.1216 , arXiv:0904.4913 , arXiv:0805.0435 , arXiv:0801.1465 , arXiv:hep-ph/0505141 , arXiv:hep-ph/0312292 , arXiv:hep-ph/0304187 , arXiv:hep-ph/0212093 , arXiv:hep-ph/9907551 , arXiv:hep-ph/9901399 , http://www.deadfamous.info/harry-j-lipkin-israeli-nuclear-physicist-died-at-94

*** Derby England and a Radio Neutrino Experiment Scorecard

https://www.facebook.com/pages/Neutrino-Supplements/421382384608051?sk=info&tab=overview Derby is home to "Neutrino Supplements", a health food store and also the birthplace of Harry Stevens, the inventor of the hot dog and designer/seller of sport's first scorecard, which he sold with the phrase, "you can't tell the players without a scorecard." 1. ANITA: Antarctic Impulse Transient Antenna - http://www.phys.hawaii.edu/~anita/new/html/science.html

3. Arianna: Antarctic Ross Ice-Shelf ANtenna Neutrino Array - http://www.ece.uci.edu/~stuartk/Kleinfelder_Home/Research/Entries/2011/6/19_The_ARIANNA_Neutrino_Experiment_2.html
