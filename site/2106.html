Long-Baseline news, June 2021

*** Robots for Liquid Argon - Argonaut

Neither Richard Branson nor Jeff Bezos will ever be Argonauts. But that appellation will be used for a robotic system being developed to work inside liquid argon detectors, such as DUNE, MicroBooNE, ICARUS, SBND and others. Components will have to work in both a cryogenic environment and around high voltage. https://news.fnal.gov/2021/05/argonaut-project-launches-design-effort-for-super-cold-robotics/

*** Data for NOvA module in charged particle test beam

NOvA has placed two 31-plane 2.6 m x 2.6 m blocks in a new FNAL tertiary charged particle test beam. The purpose is to measure particle response in NOvA modules & a check of energy calibration. There are electrons, muons, pions, protons and kaons with known momentum in the beam. There has been two data-taking periods with >5k events in year 1 and >16K in year 2. A problem with underfilling of the top horizontal cells due to a slight tilt was fixed during year 2 and analysis of the data is ongoing. An additional beam run in early 2022 is being considered.

*** Current NOvA beam sets new record

A new NuMI beam intensity record, 843 kW average power for 1 hour, was set on 15 June. The summer shutdown started 27 June. During 2021 the average power was 680 kW.

*** Future NOvA beam running

NOvA is expected to run till January of 2027 when there will be a long shutdown for LBNF and PIP-II. NOvA has analyzed 26e20 pot of the ~37e20 pot taken through the shutdown. Through 2027 NOvA expects a total of 60-70 e20 pot, about double the current total.

*** Interesting Letters of Interest; v11 - https://snowmass21.org/

  • Sehgal-016 "CMB-HD: An Ultra-Deep, High-Resolution mm-Wave Survey Over Half the Sky", It would measure the number of light particle species that were in thermal equilibrium with the known standard-model particles at any time in the early Universe, i.e. Neff, with a 1-sigma uncertainty of 0.014.
  • Liu-039 "Lattice Calculation of Neutrino-Nucleon Cross Section" The (chi)QCD Collaboration (part of the the USQCD Collab) plans to carry out a lattice calculation of the nu-nucleon scattering cross section for the neutrino energy < 7 GeV, in the range for DUNE.
  • Paley-068 "The NOvA Near Detector Physics Program", For an exposure of 63e20 pot, NOvA will use its near detector to measure many nu cross-sections. For multiple E & angle bins for 12 final states, the LOI spells out expected stat & syst uncertainties.
  • Sanchez-133 "Physics Opportunities in ANNIE", ANNIE's measurement of the neutron abundance in nu interactions could lead to a reduction of systematic uncertainties and other improvements in signal-background discrimination for future nu detectors.
  • Coherent-111 "COHERENT Sensitivity to Dark Matter (DM)", Detectors made to study CEvNS are capable of constraining DM mass near 15 MeV/c^2. They outline strategies to search for a DM flux consistent with the cosmological DM concentration.
