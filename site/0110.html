Long-Baseline news, October 2001

*** Neutrinos in HEPAP Subpanel report

The Draft report on US HEP planning was encouraging or discouraging with respect to its 84 mentions of neutrinos, depending on expectations. http://fnth37.fnal.gov/hepap.pdf The 20 year "roadmap" includes a neutrino superbeam, a neutrino factory, and a muon collider. But you don't go everywhere on a map. Icecube and a national underground science lab are endorsed. Specifically, in A.2.1 and A.2.2, a neutrino superbeam cost was put at $500M, and a neutrino factory "significantly more than $1B." More generally, it states, "The future neutrino program will be shaped by the results from the present generation of experiments."

*** HEPAP subpanel urges NuMI completion

In Draft Section 2.5.1, the subpanel urged "projects under construction, such as LHC and NuMI/MINOS be completed as scheduled." Didn't anyone tell them?

*** Neutrinos for National Security

Spencer Abraham, US Sec. of Energy, says the mission of the DOE is "national security". And "(science) programs and projects that fall outside this mission will not receive my support for funding without a clarity of mission and compelling circumstances."

*** Homestake Underground Lab moves up

At the NUSL meeting in Lead South Dakota, the issue of prior liability seemed to be a hang-up for Homestake mining company to turn over its mine for a national underground science laboratory. This issue was resolved after a meeting between SD Senator Daschle, Governor Bill Janklow and Homestake President Jack Thompson. The indemnification agreement that would limit civil liability for environmental problems at the mine to $75M needs congress' nod.

*** 3M

This would be a Megaton Modular Multi-Purpose Neutrino Detector, made from 10 (50-m)^3 cylinders at 6950 ft deep at Homestake, for pdk, UHE nu's, atmo nus and long-baseline. $10M construction and about $30M for phototubes each.

*** KATRIN Letter of Intent

With the compelling evidence for massive neutrinos from recent neutrino-oscillation experiments, one of the most fundamental tasks of particle physics over the next years will be the determination of the absolute mass scale of neutrinos. The absolute value of nu-masses will have crucial implications for cosmology, astrophysics and particle physics. A next Generation Tritium Beta Decay Experiment with sub-eV Sensitivity for the Electron Neutrino Mass has written a letter of intent, hep-ex/0109033. And see http://www-ik1.fzk.de/tritium/

*** CPT violation

If CPT is violated, there could be 6 neutrino masses, (rather than 3) and hence many more interesting delta-msquareds. CPT violation would motivate many more long-baseline experiments. See hep-ph/0110175 (and previously listed hep-ph/0108199 ).

*** New and Old Tests for Neutrino Mass

Two new preprints hep-ph/0109027 and hep-ph/0110061 discuss the time between gravity waves and supernova neutrino bursts. It is noted that one of these two was published in 1981.
