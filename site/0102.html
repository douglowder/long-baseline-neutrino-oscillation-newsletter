Long-Baseline news, February 2001

*** Observation of nu_tau appearance by Super-K

If the atmospheric neutrino result is due to nu_mu to nu_tau oscillations, there should be about one nu_tau interaction per kiloton year of data. Super-K now has evidence for these events, which have characteristics different from most nu_mu and nu_e interactions. One program gives 31% efficiency for nu_taus and only 2.1% for nu_mu and nu_e. They have done three highly correlated analyses (likelihood, neural net, event shape) and get (1.5, 1.6, 1.9) sigma results, corresponding to about 40 events.

*** Neutrino Day was February 23rd

The Homestake solar neutrino experiment counted about one neutrino conversion per day. The city commission of nearby Lead South Dakota declared February 23 Neutrino Day.

*** 250 km Tsukuba to Mizumi

The currently running K2K experiment from KEK in Tsukuba to Super-K has a baseline of 250 km. Running was scheduled to resume in January 2001. Through June 2000 2.3 10^19 protons.

*** 295 km Tokai to Mizumi

The recently approved JAERI site at Tokai is 295 km from Super-K. Data could start in 2006-7.

*** 730 km Geneva to Assergi

http://proj-cngs.web.cern.ch/proj-cngs/cngs.htm shows a crane over the shaft head. Neutrinos due in May 2005.

*** 731 km Batavia to Soudan - (Longer is Better)

The NuMI target hall excavation is complete, and the tunneling continues. Neutrinos will stream under Wisconsin in April of 2004. http://www-fess.fnal.gov/engineering/NuMI/start.htm

*** 1700 km Upton to Soudan

A site specific neutrino factory study is underway at BNL. http://www.cap.bnl.gov/mumu/studyii/. BNL doesn't want to dig down into the water table, so a neutrino factory aimed at Soudan could be built up on an 8 degree sloped man-made hill. A 20 GeV muon storage ring would yield 2150 nu_mu events per kiloton year, plus 960 nu_e's. The beam would be 39 degrees from MINOS.

*** 2900 km Upton to Carlsbad

The hill would tilt 13 degrees, and would yield only 740 nu_mu and 330 nu_e events per kiloton year. This is a preferable distance for separating matter effects and CP violation.
