*** EPS Prize for Gargamelle Awarded for neutrino neutral currents

This year's EPS-High Energy and Particle Physics Prize was awarded to the Gargamelle Collaboration for the discovery of the weak neutral current(NC). This was key for our standard model. On 3 Sep 1973 the 59 scientists published 2 papers in the same issue of Phys. Lett.: 1) NC involving electrons, 2) NC interactions with hadrons (p&n). The race with other experiments became known to some as "alternating neutral currents". http://www.symmetrymagazine.org/breaking/2009/07/07/gargamelle/

*** Neutrino Giant Prize from Long-Baseline News for Pontecorvo

A "neutrino giant" is not an oxymoron (jumbo shrimp). Henceforth Long-Baseline News will annually add a name to a list appearing on the www.neutrinooscillation.org site for "Neutrino Giants", important contributors to the burgeoning neutrino physics field. In 1948 Bruno Pontecorvo proposed a radiochemical method for neutrino detection. He discussed advantages of the Cl-Ar reaction. (cheap liquid, ease to extract Ar etc). He pointed out possible nu sources (reactors, the sun, radioactive materials prepared in a reactor). In 1947 he discovered that the constant of muon capture is the same as the Fermi constant and he was the first who put forward the idea of muon/electron universal weak interactions. In 57-58 he proposed an idea for neutrino oscillations. At that time only one type of neutrino was known. He considered antinu --> nu (sterile) oscillations. Thus, he invented not only oscillations but also sterile neutrinos. He considered a possible experiment with reactor antineutrinos (like KamLAND). He pointed out that experiments with nus from accelerators are possible in 1959. As a 1st problem, he proposed to look at whether the electron and muon neutrinos were the same or different. In 1967 he pointed out that (due to oscillations) the observed flux of solar neutrinos can be half the prediction, thus foreseeing the solar nu problem.

*** More Atmospheric Neutrinos in Super-K

Super-K has data from 518 days of SK-III. No surprise, the atmospheric neutrinos oscillate. The best fit for the L/E analysis is mixing 1.0 and dmsq 2.2 e-3 eV^2, or 1.9 to 2.6 at 90%CL. The best fit for nutau appearance is now 138 +- 48 (stat) +14.8-31.6(sys) which is 2.4 sigma and consistent with expectation 78.4+-26. A quadrant analysis including the solar term (for theta-23) found sin^2(theta) to be from 0.410 to 0.585 at 90%CL (i.e. no observed deviation from 0.500.)
