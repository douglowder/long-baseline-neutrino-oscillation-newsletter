Long-Baseline news, July 2000

*** Donut reports discovery of tau neutrino

The beam dump experiment at Fermilab reports 203 neutrino events, of which 4 events survive tau cuts in the Long decay search. The expected number based on the calculated cross section and efficiencies is 4.1 +- 1.4. The background is .41 +- .15 events, half charm and half hadronic interactions. The preliminary report of 5 events at Nu2000 included a short decay analysis. See http://fn872.fnal.gov/presentation/complete/2000/WC2000.pdf

*** Kolar Event type anomaly in NuTeV

NuTeV put a Helium bag in front of their detector to search for verticizing events from Neutral Heavy Lepton or Neutralino decay. A "blind" analysis found 3 dimuon events (0 others), with expected background of only 0.04 dimuons, or 0.30 events total in 2 charged particles. But the asymmetry in the 3 events fits Deep Inelastic Scattering, and <0.5% likely from decay. See http://www-e815.fnal.gov/NuTeV.html

*** New K2K data

K2K showed their deficit in OD (outer detector) events but didn't include that deficit in quoting a statistical significance. Their position is that OD events are useful for checking the consistency with FC events, but they are less well understood and the systematic error, due to OD timing cut, may be large, when they start discussing oscillations quantitatively. Exiting events with vertex in fiducial volume will be helpful after ICHEP(OSAKA).

*** Large Ue3?

If Ue3 is 10^-3 or less, a neutrino factory won't see much CP violation or matter effects. Chooz constrains Ue3 to <~0.2 , but long-baseline experiments will increase the search sensitivity. A recent paper by Akhmedov et al. suggests that in the absence of fine-tuning of the neutrino mass matrix, the LMA solar neutrino solution points towards a nicely large value, 0.05-0.15. (PRL 84/16 p3535) This is also discussed in hep-ph/0007029 and hep-ph/0006281 . Others impose new symmetries so Ue3=0., as in hep-ph/0007011 .

*** MINOS lowers baseline initial beam Energy

Probably not in response to Michael Feldman's concerns, MINOS has chosen to switch to the "low energy beam" rather than the "medium energy beam" as the probable initial beam energy. For favored values of delta m^2, this centers the oscillations more on the beam spectrum. The parabolic horns can be moved to focus differing energies, but other hardware such as monitoring need specifications which depend on beam energy.

*** South Pole Daily Nus

I don't know what they are reading in Antarctica, but the AMANDA experiment now shows data from 1997 run of AMANDA-B with signal and background for 18 cut levels, trading off efficiency (for source searches) for background rejection (atmospheric neutrino flux.) The rate of analyzed atmo nus is about one per day.

*** High Intensity Neutrino Beams

A new physics study is being conducted on the neutrino physics potential of the first stage of a neutrino factory, an intense hadron beam together with a conventional neutrino beam. Calling them "superbeams", the study is being led by FNAL's Debbie Harris.

*** LOI for JHF and Super-K

A JHF Neutrino Working Group organized by K. Nishikawa has submitted a Letter of Intent for a long-baseline neutrino oscillation experiment using the JHF 50 GeV machine and Super-K. The JHF is planned for Jaeri, 295 km from Super-K. With 10^21 protons on target (per half year) they expect 6000 neutrinos with a Wide Band Beam, 1500 with a NBB, both much more than K2K.
