Long-Baseline news, June 2018

*** New results from T2K at Neutrino 2018

T2K is operating steadily with 485 kW beam power for 2017/18. They've collected 3.16e21 POT evenly split between nus and nubars. They've analyzed 1.49e21 nu and 1.12e21 nubar & will release full data set analysis in late summer. For nuebar appearance, they expect 6.5 events for no appearance, 11.8 events for the 3-nu best fit, and they see 9 events. The data prefers the normal mass (89%) order over the inverted, (11%). Upper octant for q23 is 77%. http://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.1286751

*** New results from NOvA at Neutrino 2018

NOvA has 6.9e20 POT nubar and 8.85e20 nu. NOvA expects 30-75 events of nue appearance (depending on delta and q23) and 10-22 nuebar. It measures 58 nu and 18 nuebar. The nuebar signal includes an expected 5.3 background (3.5 beam, <1 cosmic and 1 wrong sign nue) giving >4 sigma evidence for nuebar appearance. Best 3nu fit to appearance + disappearance is delta = 0.17 x pi, normal order, sin^2 (q23) = 0.58 +-0.03 (UO) and delta m^2_32 = 2.51 +0.12 -0.08 e-3 eV^2. It rejects maximal mixing at 1.8 sigma & no evidence for mixing with sterile nus in nu or nubar using NC. http://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.1286757

*** New results from MINOS+ at Neutrino 2018

With 25e20 POT (total), plus 60.8 kt-yr of atmospheric nu, MINOS/ MINOS+ have a best fit delta m^2_32 = 2.42e-3 eV^2, sin^2 q23 = 0.42. At 90% CL, delta m^2_32 = 2.28-2.55 (-2.60 to -2.33) normal (inverted) order. Using a new 2-detector fit technique, MINOS+ sets leading limits on sterile neutrino mixing, especially in the critical 1 - 10 eV2 region. http://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.1286759 (Other new results at https://www.mpi-hd.mpg.de/nu2018/programme)

*** US & Italy to cooperate on the Short-Baseline Neutrino (SBN)

http://news.fnal.gov/2018/06/united-states-and-italy-sign-agreement-to-collaborate-on-sterile-neutrino-research/ On 28 June, representatives of the U.S. Department of Energy and the Italian Embassy in DC signed an agreement for collaboration on research using FNAL's SBN program. SBN comprises 3 nu detectors in the booster beamline, SBND (Short-Baseline Near Detector), ICARUS (Imaging Cosmic And Rare Underground Signals) and MicroBooNE (Micro Booster Neutrino Experiment).

*** First mention of neutrinos in a medical physics article

In the first mention of neutrinos that I've seen in a medical physics context, Pravikoff et al. use Ge detectors "developed (for) neutrino experiments" to compare brain samples from Alzheimer patients to those with tumors. arXiv:1806.09539

*** potential for increased LBNF/DUNE budget

From AIP: The House & Senate both specify that much of the (HEP) funding increase should go to PIP-II & LBNF/DUNE. The House (Senate) proposes increasing the budget for PIP-II from $25M to $41M ($35M). They propose ramping up the LBNF/DUNE budget from $95 million to $175M ($145M). LBNF/DUNE is targeted for completion in 2030 with a total cost of between $1.3B and $1.9B, excluding contributions from international partners. https://www.aip.org/fyi/2018/fy19-appropriations-bills-doe-office-science?utm_medium=email&utm_source=FYI&dm_i=1ZJN,5PUOX,E29E6Q,M9NL8,1
