MINOS is again taking data after a slightly extended shutdown. Work included repair of a water pipe section for horn #2. http://www.fnal.gov/pub/today/NuMIhornrepairmockdrill.html

*** Cooperative Agreement between DOE & U of M for NOvA lab

The Department of Energy Office of Science has awarded a $45M, 4-year cooperative agreement to the University of Minnesota's School of Physics and Astronomy to build a new physics laboratory near the Ash River, about 60 km SE of International Falls, Minn. http://www.ur.umn.edu/FMPro?-db=releases&-lay=web&-format=umnnewsreleases/releasesdetail.html&-RecID=36917&-Find

*** Minerva CD stuff

On November 8, 2007, Dennis Kovar approved MINERvA to begin construction as soon as funds are available. This approval (affectionately referred to as "CD-3") means that the design and engineering are essentially complete, and a final design review has been performed. CD-3a (approval for a limited number of items) was granted Mar 07 and the full CD-3 ("CD-3b") in November. The DOE has requested funding for construction of MINERvA in its FY08 budget request. MINERvA plans to go full speed ahead as soon as the Continuing Resolution is over. MINERvA will measure nu interactions in unprecedented detail on a variety of nuclear targets, which, while being interesting in its own right, is also a key ingredient in long-baseline oscillation experiments.

*** Helmut Faissner, 1928-2007

Helmut Faissner, a pioneer of neutrino experiments at CERN died in August of this year. He conducted the first neutrino experiments at CERN in 1963, separating electron and muon nus. He played a key role in identifying neutral currents at Gargammelle.

*** Three mixing angles

There are 3 mixing angles in the MNS matrix, and these are commonly known as (q for theta) q12, q23 and q13. These numbers are labels, they could have been called anything, and the order of the pair is of no consequence.

*** Three neutrino mass names

The mass eigenstates have to be defined in some way. This is similar to naming the electron, muon and tau. For neutrinos, we define the eigenstates 1,2,3, with masses m1, m2, m3 such that: a) m1 < m2. b) if the mass hierarchy is normal, m3 > m2,m1, c) if the mass hierarchy is inverted, m3 < m2,m1,

*** Why isn't m3 the heaviest?

We could define the states such that m3 > m2 > m1. In this case, however, the relation between mixing angles and the different oscillation "channels" would be hierarchy dependent. For the normal hierarchy, the "solar" angle is q12, the "atmospheric" angle is q23, and the "CHOOZ" angle is q13 as usual. On the other hand, if the hierarchy were inverted, we would need to relabel the mixing angles. If one sticks to the PDG angle parameterization: a) tan qsolar = sin q13 / (sin q12 * cos q13) b) sin qchooz = cos q12 * cos q13 c) tan qatm = something very messy -- a horrible choice

*** 6 mass squared differences - sign matters

With 3 masses, there are 6 (ordered) differences of the squares of the masses(dmsq). Order matters, e.g. dmsq13 = - dmsq31. 3 are the negative of the other 3, and each 3 sum to zero, so only 2 are independent. The PDG uses dmsq21 and dmsq32. At the moment, we know the sign of dmsq21 (positive) but not dmsq32. dmsq32 and dmsq31 are different, but it will be some time before they can be measured separately.

*** Neutrino Sudoku

With 3 neutrino mass eigenstates, 3 neutrino flavor eigenstates, and 3 intermediate vector bosons, we can create neutrino sudoku. http://neutrinooscillation.org/sudoku/ prepared by Travis Mui and Matthew Castillon from the Illinois Math and Science Academy

*** Sakurai prize for Mikheyev and Smirnov

A. Smirnov & S. Mikheyev have been awarded the 2008 Sakurai prize by the APS "for pioneering and influential work on the enhancement of neutrino oscillations in matter, which is essential to a quantitative understanding of the solar neutrino flux". http://prizes.ictp.it/pio/words/news/2007/smirnov-awarded-sakurai-prize

*** Intensity Frontier widens

A Project X workshop, with well over 200 participants, explored the potential science that the unprecedented intensities of Project X would make possible. If super-symmetry is discovered at the LHC, one of the most compelling challenges will be to connect super-symmetry to neutrino physics via the study of charged lepton flavor violation. Besides neutrinos, there was interest in muons, kaons and anti-protons.
