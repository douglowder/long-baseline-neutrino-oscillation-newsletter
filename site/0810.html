*** Theory White Paper on DUSEL physics seeks signatures

A theory workshop last spring has led to a Theory White Paper supporting the wide range of physics, including neutrino physics, which can be carried out at a new large underground detector. It is now available and they are seeking additional signatures. "The theory community is excited by the prospect of DUSEL, since this would create a U.S. center for studies of proton decay, neutrino oscillations and astrophysics, dark matter, and the very nature of the neutrino and the neutron and other possible forefront experiments probing the properties of nature". To read and/or support it, see http://www.ccapp.osu.edu/whitepaper.html. & arXiv:0810.4551

*** Fermilab Beam to DUSEL

A new working group at Fermilab under Jeff Appel strives to understand the optimum parameters, costs and possible schedule for a neutrino beam from Fermilab to DUSEL. They are discussing beam optics, extraction, new civil, targeting, near detector hall, instrumentation, etc. The new plan is that NuMI-Minnesota will be upgraded to 700 Kw, but 2MW will be for NuMI-South Dakota. See http://beamdocs.fnal.gov/SNuMI-public/DocDB/ListTopics

*** It costs extra to be the flagship

It will not be appropriate to take the level of risk associated with the NuMI project for FNAL-DUSEL, if this is to be the flagship of the US HEP program, as suggested by the P5 report. But the beam design can take advantage of "lessons learned" from the NuMI experience, which include 1) tritium production and mitigation; 2) decay pipe window corrosion and 3) lack of early spare target/horns.

*** Dewatering Homestake for DUSEL

Homestake is pumping out water more slowly than originally hoped. This is partly due to dissolved iron in the water, which must be treated before it is released. Unfortunately, this water cannot be used to make a magnetized water Cerenkov detector. The current schedule is to reach the 4850 level by July 09.

*** SNO elastic scattering measurement with He counters seems low

The SNO experiment has measured the solar neutral current rate three ways now, and they all agree. They had a heavy water phase, a salt phase, and a helium counter phase. CC rates agree too. They also measure the elastic scattering cross section (which is a mixture of NC and CC). The recent ES measurement seems low, compared to the salt phase or the more precise Super-K result (PRD73,112001,2006] They measure 1.77 +.24-.21(stat)+.09-.10(sys) 10^-6/cm^-s, based on 171 events, with 2.4 expected. A statistical fluctuation is a likely explanation.

*** Terrestrial Neutrino Units

Solar Neutrino Physicists wanted to work in convenient units, so they invented the Solar Neutrino Unit or SNU. 1 SNU = 10^(-36) captures per target atom per second. Even though the earth is closer to most neutrino detectors than the sun, it is only slowly decaying and not burning (thank goodness), so the convenient unit is smaller. The Terrestrial Neutrino Unit or TNU = 1 event per 10^32 protons per year. KamLAND measured 57 TNU (+33-31) and geology models and heat energy balance give predictions.

*** MiniBooNE Anti-neutrinos

MiniBooNE has results for muon nu and anti-nu disappearance. They observe no disappearance, reducing some of the region allowed by 3+2 models. The anti-neutrino results are in a new region of phase space. Maybe anti-nu appearance results in Dec 08.
