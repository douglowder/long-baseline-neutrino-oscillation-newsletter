Long-Baseline news, January 1998

*** NuMI gets environmental ok

A FONSI (Finding Of No SIgnificant Impact) doesn't mean that no-one cares about neutrinos. It means that the DOE has determined that the neutrino project at the main injector has considered the appropriate environmental issues in its design, and no environmental impact statement is required.

*** Lower Omega leaves less for neutrinos?

Astronomers are now more loudly saying that Omega-total is about 0.2, rather than 1.0 as many astrophysicists have long said. There still needs to be dark matter, but not as much. The argument against all of the dark matter being neutrinos is still that one could not account for structure formation. One theorist thinks this argues against more short-baseline experiments.

*** COSMOS looking for emulsion

The emulsion group which provided the emulsion for COSMOS at Fermilab and TOSCA at CERN has pulled out of both short-baseline experiments, perhaps due to increased interest in long-baseline experiments. COSMOS is now scrambling to find a new target.

*** BooNe Proposal has first hearing at Fermilab PAC

The MiniBooNE proposal was well received by the FNAL PAC Jan 9th. "The MiniBooNE collaboration has persuaded the PAC that their experiment would have the sensitivity to definitively confirm or rule out the LSND signal. The anticipated signal size assuming the LSND oscillation parameters would be ~1000 events. This large number of events would allow a first estimate of Dm2 and sin22q from the shape of the energy distribution of observed events....From a scientific point of view, the Committee feels that the experiment is fully justified." Consideration for approval will be in May.

*** Neutrinos in Aspen, January, June and July

Transparencies from the Aspen winter meeting, which focused on neutrinos and skiing will soon be on the web. From June 15 to July 12, there will be a workshop on "Neutrino Physics and Astrophysics: from Solar to Ultra-High Energy Neutrinos" at the Aspen Center for Physics. This resulted from one proposal covering neutrino physics at low energies and another High Energy Neutrino Astrophysics workshop Applications are at http://andy.bu.edu/aspen/application/

*** Super-Duper Kamiokande trigger

When the Superconducting Super Collider was cancelled a few years ago, it was predicted that the next accelerator would not have "super" in its name. But SuperKamiokande has survived this verbal curse and is providing some of the most beautiful neutrino data right now. In order to see more solar neutrinos, they now have a "super-low energy trigger" at 4.6 MeV threshold. This superseded the 5.5 MeV threshold in June 1997.

*** Auger to measure neutrinos?

As reported in New Scientist, Chan Hong-mo suggests the neutrino cross section rises with E enough to be a candidate explanation for the 10^20 eV energy events seen by air shower experiments. Those same events are part of the motivation for Auger, since it is hard to explain the highest energy cosmic rays due to a lack of close Active Galactic Nuclei, coupled with the Zatsepin-Greisen cutoff for protons from far AGN's. However another paper by Burdman et al (hep-ph/9709399) rules this out based on a s-channel unitarity.

*** Auger to measure missiles?

In a November 1997 SAGENAP report, Auger was rejected. However it will be revised and resubmitted. Meanwhile an unarmed cruise missile fired from a B-52 bomber at the Dugway Proving ground north of the proposed Auger site hit a trailer used by a Japanese cosmic-ray observatory. No one was hurt, as the area is routinely evacuated during missile tests, but the missile was in the wrong place.

*** Amanda finishes third hole; Ice-cube workshop

Three deep strings are now deployed, along with 2 television cameras 2000 m deep in the ice. It has been promised that live events will be available from their web page. An intense two day workshop to initiate the conceptual design of ICECUBE - a kilometer-scale neutrino facility based on AMANDA will be held at UC Irvine March 27-28 1998. See http://www.ps.uci.edu/~icecube or email

*** Notable new references

  • S. Bilenky et al., Long-baseline neutrino oscillation experiments and CP violation in the lepton sector, hep-ph/9712537
  • Barger et al., Four-Way Neutrino Oscillations, hep-ph/9712495 They present a 4-nu model (one sterile) which has maximal numu to nutau oscillations of atmospheric neutrinos and explains solar and LSND results. It predicts nue to nutau and nue to numu in long-baseline experiments with L/E > 1 km/GeV.
  • B. Armbruster et al., KARMEN Limits on nue->nutau Oscillation in 2-neutrino and 3-neutrino Mixing Schemes, No positive evidence in three analyses. hep-ex/9801007, submitted to Phys. Rev. C
  • D. Wagner and T. Weiler, Boxing with neutrino oscillations, hep-ph/9801327
  • Mannochi et al., A Test of numu to nux oscillations and determination of the dmsq parameter of atmospheric nus using L/E, hep-ph/9801339, January 998
  • Teshima, Sakai and Inagaki, "Analysis of neutrino oscillation in three flavor neutrinos", hep-ph/9801276, A combination of high angle solar solution (dmsq = 3 * 10^-5 eV^2) and the Cardall Fuller solution, matches nicely the new SuperKamiokande angular distributions.
