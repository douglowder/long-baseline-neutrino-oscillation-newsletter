Long-Baseline news, February 2019

*** Pontecorvo award to Francis Halzen

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bruno_Pontecorvo_Prize The JINR Scientific Council at its 125th session has approved the recommendation of the International jury to award the 2018 Bruno Pontecorvo prize to Prof. Francis Halzen (University of Wisconsin) for his leading role in the construction of the IceCube detector & experimental discovery of very high energy cosmological neutrinos. http://indico.jinr.ru/conferenceDisplay.py?confId=734

*** QuarkNet and Neutrinos

QuarkNet, in collaboration with MINERvA, is launching its 1st nu- based masterclass this Spring. High school students around the world will become neutrino physicists for a day, performing an analysis in which they identify quasielastic-like events and use transverse kinematic imbalance to measure the size of the carbon nucleus. They will video conference with other classrooms that have completed the same exercise and with 2 FNAL physicists that will engage them in conversations about the exercise, nu physics, and life in science. They'd welcome participation in this program by hosting a masterclass at your institution for local high school students -- contact Rob Fine (mailto:[email protected]).
https://quarknet.org/content/minerva-masterclass-documentation-2019

*** NOvA record running protons on target (pot)

In January, NOvA received a weekly record 21.7e18 pot from the Main Injector. It has obtained 12e20 pot in the reverse horn current mode, featuring antineutrinos. At the end of February, the NuMI beam switched back to Forward Horn Current (neutrino) running.

*** Bounds on long-range interactions of neutrinos

A universe full of electrons together with IceCube results leads to an unprecedented sensitivity and limits on new interactions. See Bustamante & Agarwalla in Editor's Suggestion PRL 122, 061103 (2019) and arXiv:1808.02042.

*** New release of GIBUU

The GiBUU project provides a unified theory and transport framework in the MeV and GeV energy regimes for elementary reactions on nuclei, including current and future nu oscillation experiments. The new release 'GiBUU 2019' is available for download from http://gibuu.hepforge.org/.

*** PIP-II groundbreaking

PIP-II is a key project to support future high intensity neutrino running at Fermilab. On Friday 15 March, an official groundbreaking ceremony for PIP-II will take place at FNAL. http://pip2.fnal.gov/

*** Argon Cube 2x2

ArgonCube proposes an advanced approach for building Liquid Argon Time Projection Chambers (TPCs). The principal novelty of ArgonCube is a modular design which allows for the scalability to a large active detector mass without losing a significant amount of active volume. The ArgonCube design will be applied in DUNE, a next generation neutrino experiment. https://argoncube.org/
