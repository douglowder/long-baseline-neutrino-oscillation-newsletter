Long-Baseline news, September 2003

*** Salty SNO Box Opened

The salt phase of SNO is now complete. 254 days of data have been analyzed with 150 to go. Data qualitatively agrees well with before. LMA I is selected, with LMA-II only remaining at 99.7%CL. Maximal mixing ruled out at 5.4 sigma, the best fit now at 7.1 10^-5 eV^2 and theta 32.5 degrees. See nucl-ex/0309004

*** Year Long US Neutrino Study

The Division on Nuclear Physics (DNP) and Division of Particles and Fields (DPF) of the APS along with the Division of Astrophysics (DAP) and Division of Particle Beams (DPB) are jointly sponsoring a neutrino study for the next year.  Boris Kayser and Stuart Freedman will be leading the study, which will aim at identifying the upcoming physics challenges for neutrino physicists. A report will presumably be written by the end of next summer.

*** KamLAND rates down

KamLAND is (presumably) measuring less neutrino events than earlier, not because neutrino oscillation has increased over time, but because many Japanese reactors have shut down for inspection as a result of concerns over safety lapses and inaccurate information. In May of 2003, the Japanese nuclear industry was operating at 37% of total capacity.

*** Studying Supernovae in Tennessee

The SNS**2 workshop was for Supernova Neutrino Studies at the SNS. They are investigating putting some liquid or solid detectors 21 m from the beam stop. They could measure neutrino cross sections from a variety of nuclear targets, of interest to supernova dynamics. A lead target would be of interest to the OMNIS project.

*** Universe might end, NuMI to continue

Some superstitious cosmologists pointed out back in 1998 that the prospect of a Cubs-Red Sox World Series (baseball) coupled with the curse of the Billy Goat and the curse of the Bambino meant that the universe must end before the final out of the 7th game October 28th. The project manager for Neutrinos at the Main Injector has decided not to let the current frequentist probability of such a match-up (1/16~6%) delay the project.

*** NuMI proton intensity

The MINOS proposal calls for 2 yrs of running NuMI at 3.7x10^20 pot, although only 2 x10^20 pot/yr is now expected for the 2005 start. MINOS has proposed that Fermilab spend ~10M$/yr to upgrade the beam intensity to 7.2x10^20 pot by 2009, yielding 25x10^20 for a 5 yr run beginning in 2005. This can possibly be achieved by multi-bunch stacking in the Main Injector and faster cycle times with increased magnet and RF power. These issues are being studied by a Fermilab Committee chaired by David Finley.

*** Particle Physics with Particle Board

The P929 NuMI Off-Axis collaboration is writing a proposal for the fall Fermilab PAC meeting. A committee recently made a working decision to write the proposal with liquid scintillator as the baseline for the active detector, with resistive plate chambers as the alternative. The passive part of the detector will be (~7") particle board. A 50 kT detector is envisaged.

*** Seesaw meeting, Paris June 10-11, 2004

Arrive early for Neutrino 2004 and for an international conference on the Seesaw Mechanism and the Neutrino Mass at Institut Henri Poincare, Paris, June 10-11, on the occasion of the 25th anniversary of the seesaw mechanism. http://seesaw25.in2p3.fr/

*** NOVE II - Venice December 3-5, 2003.

The Second International Workshop on: NO-VE "Neutrino Oscillations in Venice" will be held in Venice at the "Istituto Veneto di Scienze, Lettere ed Arti", to discuss the impact of recent results and to chart the direction of future research.

*** JPARC delayed, neutrinos accelerated

The Japanese Particle Research Center, JPARC, also known as JHF, is being funded by JAERI (nuclear funding) and KEK (particle funding). Due to JAERI's tight budget, the Minister of MEXT (Ministry of Education, etc. and Technology) proposed: A. to complete J-PARC by the end of JFY07 (one year delay) and B. to start the neutrino program from the next FY (5 year funding term). This is positive for the neutrino program which wasn't previously approved as part of Phase-I of JPARC.
