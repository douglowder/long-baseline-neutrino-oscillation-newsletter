Long Baseline / P822 news, September 1992

Special Edition

(Courtesy Doug Michael from Cal Tech/CERN)

A workshop on using CERN neutrino beams for studying neutrino oscillations over long baselines is being planned. The dates have now been set for November 20 and 21 at CERN. I apologize for this late notice which has resulted from the very tight schedule of the director of CERN. I hope that this will not make it too difficult for you if you are interested in attending. More details will be sent at a later date but I wanted to alert people now about these dates so travel arrangements can be made.

The purpose of this workshop is to discuss neutrino oscillations in the specific context of what can be accomplished with CERN beams and how that compares with other possible plans. I expect that the schedule will include discussions on the expected beams, possible experiments for the Gran Sasso underground laboratory, possible lake or sea experiments, Super Kamiokande with a CERN beam, short baseline experiments already planned at CERN, plans and progress for Fermilab Main Injector proposals, summaries of the present state of experimental and theoretical understanding of the neutrino oscillation picture, and more.

If you have questions, feel free to send me a note. If you are interested in giving a talk at the workshop, please let me know. I can be contacted at CITHEX::MICHAEL or MICHAEL@CITHEX. My phone numbers are 818-356-6669 at Caltech and 39-862-437-212 or 39-862-437-224 at the Gran Sasso. E-mail is probably the easiest way to contact me.
