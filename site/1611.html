Long-Baseline news, November 2016

*** Hyper-Kamiokande with a detector in Korea

It's been considered before, but the appeal of a second detector at 1100-1300 km baseline from J-PARC is again being studied now that theta13 is known. The range of OA angle in Korea is 1 to 3 degree, in which there are many mountains over 1 km high. A detailed look at this configuration is available in "Physics Potentials with the Second Hyper-Kamiokande Detector in Korea" by the Hyper-Kamiokande proto collaboration. arXiv:1611.06118

*** NOvA overheats due to a snowstorm

Due to a heavy snow on 18 November, the lab at Ash River Minnesota lost power. After power was restored, the temperature in the computer room got too high and triggered an overhead sprinkler. There was water throughout the computer room, but the detector was not affected. Far detector data was interrupted for about a week, but due to heroic efforts from the lab crew and others, data acquisition at the far detector has now resumed.

*** Neutrino pioneer MGK Menon, 1928-2016

The former director of TIFR (the Tata Institute of Fundamental Research), Professor Menon set up the deep underground physics laboratory at the Kolar Gold mine, the world's deepest. Last year was the 50th anniversary of the first reported atmospheric neutrino from KGF. The laboratory was also used to search for nucleon decay, magnetic monopoles and for cosmic ray muon studies. http://economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/science/mgk-menon-remarkable-polymath-scientist-leaves-behind-big-void/articleshow/55645467.cms

*** Ross Shaft at Homestake Mine

The rehabilitation of the Ross Shaft at the Sanford Underground Research Facility is almost done. Started in 2013, they have removed the old steel and installed new, while cleaning out decades of debris. So far 4269 feet of shaft have been fixed, with 900 to go; using 6 million pounds of steel and almost 50,000 rock bolts. Completion is scheduled September 2017.

*** Solar non-oscillations 2

Last month long-baseline news mentioned a reference that pointed out that the adiabatic crossing in solar neutrinos wasn't oscillatory. That paper was motivated by a couple of words in the Nobel prize citation, and wasn't meant to be groundbreaking. Previous works on this subject include Nunokawa et al., "What fraction of boron-8 neutrinos arrive as a nu(2) mass eigenstate?" arXiv:hep-ph/0601198 . hep-ph/0601198,; L. Wolfenstein, Proc. of the 8th International Conference on Neutrino Physics and Astrophysics - Neutrino 78 (ed. E.C. Fowler, Purdue University Press), p. C3; and S. Petcov, "On the Oscillations of Solar Neutrinos in the Sun" Phys. Lett. B 214 1988 139.
