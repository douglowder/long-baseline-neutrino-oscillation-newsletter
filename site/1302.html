Long-Baseline news, February 2013

*** NOvA Far Detector sees first light

Several blocks have been constructed; A kiloton of detector is full of scintillator. Some has now been instrumented & read out. Steve Magill is now shift coordinator and shifts will start soon. A radio story on NOvA is available from Minnesota Public Radio. http://minnesota.publicradio.org/display/web/2013/01/11/daily-circuit-nova-far-detector-neutrinos

*** Daya Bay II approved

In February 2013, Daya Bay II was approved by the Chinese government. This is roughly equivalent to CD1 in the US. It will be a large detector requiring good energy resolution to measure the wiggles in the oscillation spectrum and sensitive to the mass hierarchy.

*** KEK roadmap includes Hyper-K

Hyper-K was recommended by Japan's HEP future projects committee last year. The Science Council of Japan will soon update the Master Plan for large scale projects for all fields of science. Hyper-K will be resubmitted March 2013. The Master Plan is expected to be an important input for the Japanese government. The next Hyper-K working group meeting will be 21-22 Jun in Tokyo. http://www.jahep.org/office/doc/201202_hecsubc_report.pdf , http://kds.kek.jp/getFile.py/access?sessionId=1&resId=0&materialId=0&confId=11728

*** New CERN experiment, ICARUS & NESSiE at CENF.

A 6x6x6m^3 LAGUNA LAr prototype will be built at CERN in a new neutrino beam in the north area. Actually several components are forseeen in the newly-named CENF (CErn Neutrino Facility). While LAGUNA LAr prototype will be housed in the near site (460 m from the target), a two site experiment will be setup in the near site and in a far site (1600 m away form the target) for neutrino sterile search. The system is done by coupling LAr and Muon Spectrometer detectors. ICARUS moved from Gran Sasso will be used in the far LAr detector and a cloned 150 ton Liquid Argon Detector in the near site. Two Spectrometers a' la OPERA together with a new magnet conception for very low momentum muon measurement will be placed behind the LAr's. p.s. Of course there have previously been many CERN Nu Facilities.

*** Cosmology results on neutrinos, so far and next month

The number of (effective) neutrinos and the sum of the nu masses are parameters with sensitivity from current and future cosmology experiments. See arXiv:1212.6423 , arXiv:1212.6943 , arXiv:1301.0816 , arXiv:1301.0824 , arXiv:1301.3119 , arXiv:1301.4791 , arXiv:1301.6640 , arXiv:1301.7102 , arXiv:1302.0014 , arXiv:1302.5086 . A new better result is expected from Planck by the end of March.

*** International Symposium on Underground Physics 24-27 May

This meeting's goal is to discuss the motivation for large underground nu experiments in a global context. A particular goal will be to find a plan/money/way to build LBNE at Homestake underground rather than on the surface. Other additions to the neutrino meetings page include: ANT13 conference on Advances in Neutrino Technology at Davis in May, NNN13 in Tokyo in November, a symposium in honor of the centennial of the birth of Bruno Pontecorvo in Pisa in September, and WIN2013 in Brazil in Sep. http://www.hep.anl.gov/ndk/hypertext/numeetings.html#isoup13
