Long-Baseline news, November 2012

*** Fermilab's Raaf: a hands-on neutrino physicist

Fermilab's Jennifer Raaf works on MicroBooNE and LBNE. She was featured in the DOE Pulse, a newsletter of Science and Technology Highlights from the DOE National Laboratories. http://www.ornl.gov/info/news/pulse/no375/profile.shtml

*** Physicists brave hurricane Sandy to keep LBNE on schedule

The CD1 review was held at Fermilab during hurricane Sandy, so many physicists from BNL and reviewers from DC could only connect remotely if at all. But the meeting went on and the review was done. http://www.symmetrymagazine.org/article/november-2012/east-coasters-brave-hurricane-sandy-to-keep-neutrino-project-on-schedule

*** LBNE gets closer to CD1

During a cost and schedule review last month, LBNE presented the new long-baseline neutrino beam from Fermilab to South Dakota with a 10 kiloton Liquid Argon detector on the surface. DOE has since raised the cost guidance/profile to the presented $867M.

*** KamLAND fire

There was a fire in the parking lot of the KamLAND experiment (underground in Japan) which disrupted power for at least a week. There was no damage to the experiment.

*** New LBNE co-spokesperson

Robert Wilson from Colorado State University is the new cospokesperson for LBNE for 2013-2014, joining Milind Diwan from Brookhaven. He is no relation to Fermilab's founder of the same name.

*** NOvA Near Detector Hall Excavation Completed

Started at the beginning of Fermilab's current shutdown, excavation of the NOvA Near-Detector Cavern is complete. Workers have installed a new concrete floor over the exposed bedrock to be level with that of the existing facility. Workers will next install the infrastructure necessary to support the future near detector, including steel platforms and ceiling, electrical and mechanical utilities, fire doors & walls and other safety devices.

*** Double Chooz Near Detector Hall Excavation Completed

Blasting rock near the site of an operating nuclear reactor is problematic and caused delays, but the site for the second Double Chooz detector is done and outfitting is in progress. http://www.hep.anl.gov/mcg/doublechooznear.jpg

*** Contingency for NOvA

The 14 kiloton NOvA experiment is finishing the construction of its 5th block out of 28 at the far detector and hopes to start filling soon with scintillator. But the contingency is being used up faster than desirable, and the DOE wants NOvA to identify how to get $10M more in contingency through some savings, but without giving up detector mass. One idea that has been mentioned is to fill up downstream modules with water and not instrument them, for a mu catcher, but this gives a smaller fiducial volume for electron neutrino identification than just having a smaller detector.

*** Contingency for Sequestration?

A change in the US funding of everything might happen next month, with the obscure name "sequestration", which basically means cuts in all spending which of course includes U.S. neutrino physics. The press can't handle a long word, so they call it "fiscal cliff". Up to now, there has not been contingency planning, because it was thought we wouldn't need it. One idea that has not been mentioned is to fill our offices with water.

*** NuMI exploitation, GLADE, CHIPS and Lake Superior

A group interested in further ways to use the NuMI beam has started meetings. The scope and transparencies from the first meeting are accessible at http://www-hep.physics.wm.edu/~vahle/enumi/. Ideas include GLADE, a liquid argon detector behind NOvA; CHIPS, a water Cherenkov detector in a mine pit along the Mesabi Range of Northern Minnesota; and a large water detector in Lake Superior. Also NOvA upgrades and MINOS++.

*** Stuart Freedman 1944-2012

Berkeley's Stuart Freedman had a distinguished career in experimental physics. Among many other accomplishments, he helped to resolve the 17 KeV neutrino claims. He worked on KamLAND and CUORE and helped to establish the current generation of reactor theta-13 experiments. A Freedman fellowship for a top graduate student is being established at UC Berkeley. Donations are welcome. http://www.photonics.com/Article.aspx?AID=52411 ; http://newscenter.lbl.gov/news-releases/2012/11/16/

*** Messing with Texas

Neutrino physicists are pouring into Texas. The December 2012 collaboration meeting of LBNE was in Houston; The January 2013 meeting of NOvA is in Dallas and MINOS+ is meeting then in Austin. I asked a friend from Texas if he was upset that the Higgs was not found in Texas. He said yes. There is also the issue, as discussed by Riva et al., "Is the 125 GeV Higgs the superpartner of a neutrino?" arXiv:1211.4526
