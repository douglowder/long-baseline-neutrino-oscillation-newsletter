Long-Baseline news, January 2025

*** Fermilab summer shutdown

The temperature 2 weeks ago in International Falls Minnesota, near the location of the NOvA far detector, reached -35 degrees F. https://www.timeanddate.com/weather/usa/international-falls/historic?month=1&year=2025 (Just a reminder that at -40, the Fahrenheit and Centigrade scales unify.) The 2024 summer shutdown of the NuMI beam at Fermilab is continuing. However, NOvA is seeing some off-axis events in the Booster Neutrino Beam.

*** S8

Several papers this month use nus to address the quantity S8, which is used to quantify the amplitude of matter density fluctu- ations in the universe. L. Zu et al. in arXiv:2501.13785 "Can nu-DM interactions solve the S8 discrepancy?"; P. Shah et al. in arXiv:2501.19117 "It's not sigma8: constraining the non-linear matter power spectrum with the Dark Energy Survey Year-5 supernova sample"; and M. Yarahmadi and A. Salehi in arXiv:2501.07860 "Using the Kaniadakis horizon entropy in the presence of neutrinos to alleviate the Hubble and S8 Tensions". One more is 2501.06611.

*** Coherent neutrino scattering using Germanium

The CONUS+ collaboration has reported their first observation of coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CEvNS). It uses reactor electron antineutrinos and Ge detectors with recoil thresholds as low as 160 eVee. arXiv:2501.05206. See arXiv:2501.17843 "Implications of the first CONUS+ measurement of coherent elastic nu-nucleus scattering". Also see A. Chattaraj et al. in "Probing Standard Model and Beyond with Reactor CEνNS Data of CONUS+ experiment" and M. Alpizar-Vernegas et al in arXiv:2501.10355, "Exploring the Standard Model and Beyond from the Evidence of CEνNS with Reactor Antineutrinos in CONUS+".

*** T2K operation status

T2K resumed operation in neutrino mode at the end of November. From mid-December, operations were switched to anti-neutrino mode, and stable operation is being conducted at 810 kW. The ND system at 280 m downstream of the target, which was upgraded with a new Super-FGD, High-AngleTPC and Time-Of-Flight detector, is running. https://www.j-parc.jp/c/en/topics/2025/01/24001453.html#NU

*** JSNS2-II

The JSNS2/JSNS2-II collaboration searches for neutrino oscillation with a short distance by sterile neutrinos at J-PARC MLF. For JSNS2, the 5th long physics run using one ND is being prepared. For JSNS2-II, the new far detector is under its calibration phase. The gains and timing responses of 172 PMTs located in the inner stainless-steel tank was calibrated in-situ using the fast-timing LED system installed in the detector. JSNS2-II aims to start the commissioning data taking with beam soon, in parallel with the next physics run of the near detector.

*** Could neutrinos have long-range interactions?

A plethora of long-range neutrino interactions will be probed by DUNE and T2HK. See arXiv:2501.14835. Also arXiv:2501.12662 and arXiv:2501.12171.

*** Neutrino Platform at CERN work temporarily halted

An oxygen deficiency hazard safety incident occurred at CERN in Geneva, Switzerland, in Building 182, which focuses on neutrino platform activities. Two members of the CERN personnel lost consciousness 17 Jan. while working in a confined space used to contain Ar. https://www.isssource.com/two-workers-injured-at-cern/
