Long-Baseline news, August 2006

*** First observation of CNGS beam Neutrinos

This was 18 Aug 06 at the OPERA experiment in the Gran Sasso, using the neutrino beam made at CERN. There are 31 target planes each in 2 supermodules, a total of 206336 bricks or 1766 tons. From August 18 to August 21, they took 3 x 10^17 protons on target. This resulted in more than 100 beam correlated events with a clean time distribution from GPS of about 10 mu-s. Tracking detectors in OPERA are taking data with practically no dead time.

*** OPERA next step

In addition to the beam events, they have also measured thousands of cosmic ray muons. The next step for the OPERA experiment is to observe neutrino events in the Emulsion Cloud Chamber Bricks. The information in the emulsion will be used to separate numu events from nutaus. http://operaweb.web.cern.ch/operaweb/ ; http://operaweb.web.cern.ch/operaweb/OPERA-starting.pdf

http://nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/physics/laureates/1988/ His nobel prize is for co-discovering the muon neutrino. PRL 9, 36 (1962). http://prola.aps.org/pdf/PRL/v4/i6/p306_1 He also was the first to calculate how to measure neutrinos with an accelerator PRL 4, 306(1960). *** Scoping Study scopes out future nu opportunities

Results from the International Scoping Study on Neutrino Factory & Superbeam was one of the highlights of NuFact06. The Workshop endorsed the proposal of the Programme Committee of the ISS to proceed now to the preparation of complete International Design Studies for Betabeam, Neutrino factory and Superbeam(s) options. The final report of the ISS should be available within 2006. The next ISS/IDS meeting will be at CERN in February. Most likely Feb 21-22. It will set ways of the transition from ISS to IDS's. http://www.hep.ph.ic.ac.uk/~longkr/tmp/ISS_Doc4-v06_28-8_2006.pdf
