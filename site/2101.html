*** PIP-II Performance Baseline Approved

The performance baseline has been approved for PIP-II, the accelerator project that will increase the beam power for the DUNE long-baseline neutrino experiment. The total project cost will be $978M with CD4 scheduled for 2028. Beam has been accelerated in the first two PIP-II cryomodules to 17 MeV. The mission is to provide 1.2 MW, with a roughly 4 year ramp from CD4, and upgradable to 2.4 MW compatible with continuous wave capability.

*** FY21 appropriations for LBNF/DUNE and PIP-II.

The budget for the flagship LBNF/DUNE project is flat at $171M, while the associated PIP-II budget increased from $60M to $79M. Congress also directed at least $30 million for the Sanford Underground Research Facility in South Dakota, where DUNE will be installed. Appropriators did not comment on the 40% increase in the TPC for LBNF/DUNE, which stemmed from increases in civil construction costs and shortfalls in partner contributions. https://www.aip.org/fyi/2021/final-fy21-appropriations-doe-office-science

*** T2K extension approved

The proposed T2K extension, T2K-II, to accumulate POT of 20x10^21 for a 3 sigma establishment of CP violation and the precise measurement of mixing parameters, has been officially approved at the 30th J-PARC PAC. Number one of Nature's ten remarkable discoveries of 2020 was T2K's paper on CP violation in the nu sector. https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-020-03514-8.

*** Roaring Neutrinos in the Roaring 20's

Due to the lack of a year zero, the decade of the 20's arguably just started. I make these neutrino predictions for the decade:

1. We'll learn that m3 is the heaviest and that theta23 > 45^o.

2. Cosmological measurements of the sum of neutrino masses will have interesting upper and non-zero lower limits.

3. The value of delta_CP will not be zero or pi.

4. The MiniBooNE low E excess will be explained.

5. The LSND excess will not be explained.

6. DUNE will mostly be on time.

7. 0nu2beta limits will improve but there will not be a signal.

8. At least one of these predictions will be wrong.

9. There will be "nu" questions for the 30's.

*** Relic neutrinos seen from earth

There are three particles called neutrinos, nu1, nu2 and nu3. We live in a gravitational potential, called a galaxy, caused by dark matter. The nu3's are nonrelativistic, so there are more near us. Nu3 has less nue in it, so the effective flavors locally are not equal. This is considered by Baushev in arXiv:2101.11405 .
