Long-Baseline news, May 2020

*** Saruhashi prize to Atsuko Ichikawa for long-baseline work

T2K spokesperson Atsuko Ichikawa from Kyoto University has been awarded the the 40th prestigious Saruhashi Prize for "unraveling the nature of neutrino(s) using an accelerator-based long-baseline neutrino experiment". You can find details about the prize at https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saruhashi_Prize. Also see https://www.asahi.com/articles/ASN5R3SD7N5MULBJ00P.html

*** Review paper on K2K and T2K long-baseline experiments

https://link.springer.com/article/10.1140/epjc/s10052-020-7906-x This review on long-baseline neutrino oscillation experiments with accelerators in Japan has appeared in European Physics Journal C, v80 (2020) and is a memory of Nishikawa-san (1949-2018) who deceased during the writing of this review.

*** Registration for Neutrino 2020 Meeting 22 Jun - 2 Jul 2020

Registration is required for the online Neutrino 2020 conference even if you had previously registered for the inperson conference. There is no fee to attend. Please register for the online event before 8 Jun. The deadline to upload posters is 12 June and the deadline to optionally upload poster videos is also 12 June. A vibrant agenda is posted. https://conferences.fnal.gov/nu2020/

*** NNN20 postponed to 2021

The International Workshop on Next generation Nucleon Decay and Neutrino Detector, originally scheduled for 1-3 October in Hida city Japan has been postponed until 2021. They will decide the date of NNN21 when the situation improves substantially. At the workshop next year there will be tours of the sites of Super-Kamiokande and Hyper-Kamiokande.

*** Neutrino Physics and Machine Learning (NPML)

The neutrino physics community has made great use of new tools in machine learning. There will be 2 NPML workshops soon. On 17-19 June, a satellite meeting to Neutrino 2020 will have "lightning talks". https://indico.slac.stanford.edu/event/377/ Nu2020 poster presenters are encouraged to give a 10+5 minute oral talk. A main workshop 7-17 July will have longer & more detailed discussions. https://indico.slac.stanford.edu/event/371/

*** Neutrinos at Snowmass

The entire international neutrino physics community is invited to participate in Snowmass Neutrino Frontier activities. A wiki can be found at https://snowmass21.org/neutrino/ where information about each of the ten topical groups is listed. Note that there are many connections and overlaps between groups, as well as between Frontiers. Submitted Snowmass LOIs will be considered in all groups. The conveners are working on identifying liaisons who will make sure there are no gaps and that there is good communication. Regular topical convener meetings have begun and minutes are available. https://indico.fnal.gov/category/1101/ An FNAL listserv mailing list, SNOWMASS-NEUTRINO-FRONTIER will be used mainly for announcements. We encourage community members to participate in discussions on Slack, in the #neutrino channel, or in one of the topical group channels. A zoom Neutrino Town Hall is planned, tentatively 17 July 10am-1pm US Eastern. An in-person Neutrino Frontier Meeting may be scheduled in Feb/Mar 2021.

*** 3D Supernova simulations

Neutrino energy deposition gives that extra energy trying to revitalize the explosion. arXiv:1909.04152 details the largest collection of sophisticated 3D SN simulations ever performed. https://www.anl.gov/article/largest-collection-of-3d-supernova-simulations-leads-to-new-insights-on-explosion-dynamics
