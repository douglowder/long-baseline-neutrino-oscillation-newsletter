Long-Baseline news, June 2000

*** Civil Contract approved for Cern to Gran Sasso Beam

The CERN financial committee approved a contract for the civil construction of the neutrino beam from CERN to Gran Sasso. The beam will be paid for with a substantial contribution of Italian money, so the 6 bidders for the contract had to show substantial Italian participation in the consortia that were formed. The contracts should be signed in about 3 weeks, and a 32 month construction schedule is planned.

*** New K2K data

With 2 10^19 protons on target, they see 17 observed events in the fiducial volume, while they expect 29.2 in the absence of oscillations, a two sigma effect. For out of fidvol events, the numbers are 9/12.4, outer detector contained, 8/21.8, and crossing 10/10.4. Some more running will be analysed by the Osaka meeting.

*** Large Mixing Angle favored for solar neutrinos

With 1117 days of data (compared to 825 last presented) the The threshold is lowered to 5 MeV, a unified analysis for the entire energy region, the Monte Carlo retuned, and re-evaluation of systematic errors. Day/Night effect in Super-Kamiokande's solar nu results has gone down from 1.8 sigma to 1.3 sigma. D-N/(D+N)/2 = -0.034 +- 0.022 +- 0.013. The energy spectrum has flattened out, chi^2 for flat 13.7/17dof. This rules out the small mixing angle and vacuum solutions at 95%CL, also the lower half of the LMA, leaving LOW and part of the LMA. Great for KamLAND! Sterile nus are ruled out at 95% with global fits.

*** Fly's Eye winks

The University of Utah's $14 million cosmic ray observatory at Dugway Proving Ground was shut down by the Army last month pending a review of national security concerns. Dark moonless running is greater in the winter, so we'll hope its on by then. An article in the June 7 SLC Tribune tells part of the story.

*** New Atmospheric neutrino data

From 70.5 kt-years of Super-K; R=.652 +- .019 +- .051 for subGeV events and .668 +- .034 +- .07 for Multi-GeV. Best fit is now maximal mixing, dmsq 3.2 10^-3 eV^2. Numu to nusterile is excluded at 99% CL. They can get a handle on more exotic solutions by fitting the data to sin^2 c*L*E^n and they get n=-1.06+-0.14, which disfavors gravitationally induced neutrino oscillation explanations, and some other exotic ideas.

*** How is this data different from all other data

It wasn't a seder, and John LoSecco was not the youngest at Neutrino 2000, but he persisted to ask 4 questions about atmospheric neutrinos, mostly involving consistency with other data. Hank Sobel's answers showed the wisdom of Solomon.

*** SNO works, GNO works, SAGE fails in one attempt

A scaled Boron-8 distribution matches the SNO charged current nhit distribution above 60 hits, where the backgrounds are small. They conclude that based on the energy, direction and location, the data in the region of interest appears to be dominated by B8 solar neutrinos detected via the charged current and elastic scattering reactions, with very little background. SNO and GNO are the 6th and 7th experiments to detect solar neutrinos. GNO saw 65.8 +10.2-9.6+-3.5 SNU, consistent with Gallex and SAGE. SAGE was unsuccessful in using a bottle of vodka to find out the SNO scale factor.

*** Quo Vadis LSND?

In a reanalysis of LSND, the average probability of oscillation shifted a bit lower, to 0.25 +- .06 +- .04%. Karmen 2 data with 7160 Coulombs rules out the LSND effect as it was in 1993-95, but not all of the 1993-1998 favored region. Karmen 2 achieved its anticipated sensitivity, and will take data till April 2001. Neutrino summary speaker asked, "Quo Vadis LSND?". MiniBooNe. The Karmen timing anomaly went away with new data.

*** Quo Vadis CERN?

God wanted first generation long-baseline experiments at 730 km, so she put Soudan 730 km from FNAL and Gran Sasso 730 km from CERN. In agreement with other neutrino factory proponents, A. DeRujula likes 3000 km for a neutrino factory baseline, but drew circles that distance from CERN and found Greenland, Siberia, Chechnya, Iraq, Sudan and Mali. He concluded that there was no "peaceful technologically reasonable non-freezing place 3000+-1000 km from Geneva(CH) except the Canary Islands".

*** Snap and Pop for Neutrinos

A "name the neutrino" contest suggested 3 years ago by Peter Rosen only had losing entries, one of which was "Snap, Crackle and Pop". The designer of the briefcase for NU2000 used a rather loud plastic clasp, so at the end of the last speaker's talk at each session, the hall erupted in a cacophony of snaps. It's a nice case, so you may hear it at other meetings.
