Long-Baseline news, September 2014

*** First meeting of LBNF interim International Executive Board

The 2014 HEPAP P5 sub panel has recommended that the US form a new fully international collaboration to design and execute a highly capable Long-Baseline Neutrino Facility (LBNF). To guide this process an interim International Executive Board has been formed, and it met for the first time at Fermilab on September 23-24. The presentations from this meeting are available at https://indico.fnal.gov/conferenceDisplay.py?confId=8937. The meeting concluded with broad agreement on the science goals of the project as well as the configuration using a broad band intense neutrino beam and a high granularity liquid argon time projection chamber as the far detector. Within the next month, the board will draft an LOI describing the experimental parameters and the initial collaboration membership. Next meeting 20 October.

*** ArgoNeuT measures coherent neutrino pion production in Ar

ArgoNeuT has measured coherent pion production from Argon for the first time, arXiv:1408.0598 . Theoretically, "coherent" applies only when the target nucleus is in its ground state after the interaction. They identify events with just a muon and pion observed. A lively discussion has ensued in [email protected] about the interpretation of the result, which agrees with the Rein-Sehgal model. MINERvA paper in arXiv:1409.3835 .

*** Connie CCDs for coherent neutrino elastic scattering

Four CCDs arrived in Brazil and will be installed this month by the CONNIE collaboration in a box 30 meters from the ANGRA 2 reactor. Then polyethylene and lead shielding will be installed later this year. They expect 0.3 events/day in each 10g CCD. arXiv:1405.5761. describes the CCDs, used previously in a Dark Matter search, which could measure the nuclear recoils.

*** The COHERENT collaboration at the Oak Ridge SNS

A newly formed collaboration called COHERENT proposes to measure nu-nucleus coherent elastic scattering (NES) using neutrinos from stopped pions created by neutrons at the Spallation Neutron Source. The goal is to unambiguously measure the N^2 dependence. http://fsnutown.phy.ornl.gov/fsnufiles/positionpapers/Coherent_PositionPaper.pdf

*** Decoherence of neutrino oscillations

Nus are produced in flavor eigenstates and propagate in mass eigenstates with different velocities. If the wave packets get far enough apart, they decohere and we get mixing without oscillations. As far as I know, only supernova neutrinos are far enough away to decohere from measured sources of nus.

*** Coherent program of Short- and Long-Baseline Neutrinos

The P5 report called for a coherent program of long-baseline and short-baseline neutrino physics at Fermilab. I have been asked what you can do in short-baseline experiments that you cannot do in a near detector of a long-baseline experiment. The physics goals are different. The LBL program is trying to measure parameters within the three neutrino paradigm. The SBL is looking for new physics outside that paradigm largely motivated by certain anomalies. But also, the short-baseline program would like to get going with existing beams, should put detectors at more than one baseline, and sites on the surface are cheaper than deep locations in the NuMI or new LBNF beams. There will be synergies, of course, in the development of liquid Argon technology.

*** NOvA achieves CD4; celebrate with a baseline talk

On Wednesday 8 October FNAL will have an all-hands celebration of the NOvA successful construction completion. Now operating in the Fermilab NuMI beam is a far detector at Ash River Minnesota and a near detector at Fermilab. Both detectors are off-axis from the direction of the NuMI beam which was built for MINOS and is now running for Minerva and MINOS+. At 9AM on the 8th there will be a seminar on "Baselines and Nu Oscillations" by Mary Bishai in WH10NW, addressing baseline's role in CP & MH. The NOvA celebration starts at 10 in Ramsey Auditorium and a buffet dinner with beer/wine and jazz is 4:30-6:00 in atrium.
