Long-Baseline news, February 1998

*** NOE changes available

Recent changes in the configuration of the NOE detector, (proposed for the CERN to Gran Sasso long-baseline experiment) the addition of a TRD detector and improved performances are described in a status report, available at http://www1.na.infn.it/wsubnucl/accel/noe/noe.html

*** Los Alamos Science focuses on neutrinos

A beautiful edition of the publication "Los Alamos Science" focuses on "Celebrating the neutrino". 16 articles tell some of the stories about neutrinos today. Send requests for Number 25 to Los Alamos Science, MSF656, Los Alamos, NM. 87545.

*** NuMI in President's budget

For fiscal year 1998-99, the president/DOE is asking for $14.3M for the NuMI project at Fermilab. There will also probably be $1.5M in the Fermilab capital equipment budget. This total is less than the $20M in last year's suggested funding profile. MINOS and NuMI have a "Lehman" budget review scheduled in April.

*** Draft Gilman Panel report endorses MINOS

It wrote: 'The Subpanel endorses the importance of the long- baseline neutrino oscillation program at Fermilab. The question of neutrino mass and flavor mixing is of fundamental interest for particle physics. The 1995 HEPAP Subpanel on Accelerator-Based Neutrino Oscillation Experiments recommended that "the Fermilab program should remain flexible to react to new information." Consistent with this, we recommend that Fermilab carefully evaluate the configuration of the NUMI/MINOS facility in the light of results becoming available from experiments elsewhere. We further recommend that the role of the short-baseline COSMOS experiment be reviewed.'

*** NRC Report on Particle Physics endorses neutrino mass

'Neutrino oscillations: Many experiments now give hints that a neutrino of one family can change into one of another family. One of the most important discoveries possible in the next decade would be the confirmation of any one of these hints.'

*** HANUL prototype under construction

The High-energy Astrophysics NeUtrino Laboratory is a design for surface neutrino detectors, using a sandwich of magnets between water Cherenkov detectors. If a prototype works as expected, 25 modules covering 1000 m^2 will be built, according to the February 6 issue of Science. But all is not blue sky. The article concludes, "...it could also inspire millions of young people to consider becoming scientists."

*** Lab directors discuss Long-baseline, workshops

Fermilab's Director and CERN's director-designate are on the phone from time to time discussing common interests like long- baseline neutrino oscillation experiments. One idea is a pair of workshops to be held in Italy and the US. The first one might be in the fall. P. Strolin and R. Bernstein are investigating.

*** ORLaND encouraged to write proposal

There is $157M in the FY99 President's budget to start the SNS, the Spallation Neutron Source (SNS) to be built at ORNL. The ORLaND proponents visited the DOE in February and presented a case for a neutrino facility add-on. They were encouraged to submit a proposal by this coming summer. This proposal is to be reviewed by HEPAP and most likely by NSAC as well.

There will be an International Workshop on Nuclear Emulsion 12-14 June 1998 in Nagoya Japan. *** Notable new references

  • Proceedings of the Workshop of Main Injector, FERMILAB-Conf-97/279. G. Bock and J. Morfin. There are several reports on long-baseline neutrino experiments.
  • Proceedings of the Fourth International Solar Neutrino Conference, Edited by W. Hampel, Max-Planck-Institut fur Kernphysik, Heidelberg, ISBN 3-00-002064-0
  • Albright, Babu and Barr, A Minimality Condition and Atmospheric Neutrino Oscillations hep-ph/9802314, A structure for the mass matrices breaking SO(10) predicts a large mixing angle between \nu_{\mu} and \nu_{\tau}, as suggested by atmospheric neutrino data. The mixing angles of the electron neutrino are predicted to be small.
  • T.K. Gaisser and Todor Stanev, Path length distributions of atmospheric neutrinos, Phys. Rev. D 57 (1998) 1977. For downgoing atmospheric neutrinos, the uncertainty in the path length due to the unknown production height in the atmosphere is larger than most detector resolution effects.
  • Bordes et al., Post-GZK Air Showers, FCNC, Strongly Interacting Neutrinos and Duality, hep-ph/9711438
  • M. Gonzalez-Garcia et al., Update on Atmospheric Neutrinos, hep-ph/9801368. They take the lower dmsq seriously and suggest long-baseline experiments maybe should be redesigned
