*** New co-spokesperson for T2K

Federico Sánchez, from University of Geneva, was just elected International Co-Spokesperson of the T2K long-baseline neutrino experiment. He joins Atsuko Ichikawa from Kyoto University.

*** End of MINERvA operations in February

https://news.fnal.gov/2019/04/minerva-successfully-completes-its-physics-run/ The MINERvA experiment finished its physics run at the end of Feb. after 10 years of recording neutrino interactions in the NuMI beam using several nuclear targets. After receiving its full request for protons on target in both nu and antinu modes in the ~6 GeV on-axis NuMI beam, MINERvA is turning its attention to extracting the most out of these data sets. Some habits die hard though: MINERvA is running minimal operations of the MINOS Near Detector to provide NOvA with an on axis spectrum, and of MINERvA to keep components ready for possible re-use by ProtoDUNE-ND.

*** First Captain result in arXiv:1903.05276

The first result from the Mini-CAPTAIN detector is a measurement of the neutron cross section on Argon in the E range 100-800 MeV. LAr TPC neutron response is important for E_nu reconstruction.

*** NOvA has an issue with a GPS rollover

https://techcrunch.com/2019/04/06/gps-rollover-is-today-heres-why-devices-might-get-wacky/ The GPS system had a "rollover" in April, a problem reminiscent of the Y2K issues. The 10-bit "week number" resets to zero every 1024 weeks. NOvA knew about the issue but documentation indicated the hardware connected to the GPS receivers would handle the issue. Only two days of data were lost as the engineers and op team quickly rallied to implement hardware and firmware updates.

*** Neutrino mass guarantee

Science doesn't come with many guarantees. I recently looked as some slides I wrote in 2008 guaranteeing a measurement of nonzero theta13 in upcoming reactor neutrino experiments. The Astro2020 White Paper ( arXiv:1903.03689 ) more reasonably states, "Our understanding of the clustering of matter in the presence of massive neutrinos has significantly improved over the past decade, yielding cosmological constraints that are tighter than any laboratory experiment, and which will improve significantly over the next decade, resulting in a guaranteed detection of the absolute neutrino mass scale." The future sensitivity on the mass sum is given to be 14 meV on a number between 57 and ~250.

*** Neutrino spacecraft boosted by NASA

NASA has given the neutrino space-craft project a green light & $500K to work on a Phase-2 project, and estimate a final mission cost along with a prototype for lab testing; Simulations and scoping out the science was Phase-1. Marshall SFC engineers will do the mission studies from launch, orbits, communications, flight computer, controls and 5 y of operation; they are thinking of a Super Falcon Heavy with 3 side boosters instead of two for $130 M launch. A "small explore class" mission has to be under $350 M.

*** Sterile neutrino search wins thesis award

Jacob Todd was selected as the recipient of the 2018 URA Thesis Award, for his thesis on the "Search for Sterile Neutrinos with MINOS and MINOS+”. This recognizes his work in producing a very innovative analysis and documenting it in a high-quality thesis. It impacted the results that MINOS/MINOS+ recently published as an Editor’s Suggestion in PRL. Jacob will get the award and present his work at the Users Meeting. https://uec.fnal.gov/
