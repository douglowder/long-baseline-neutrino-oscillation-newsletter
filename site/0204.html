Long-Baseline news, April 2002

*** SNO sees neutral current using neutrinos from the sun

Previously SNO has measured the charged current CC rate from the sun, and the neutral current rate was inferred from Super-K data which was a mixture of CC and NC. You might ask whether SNO measures the same NC rate. The answer is yes, and as before, it suggests that the total number of nus from the sun is larger than the number of nu_e's, evidence for oscillations. They have measured 576.5 NC events, along with 1967.7 CC and 263 elastic scatters. An NC event is nu-D ->p n nu, and the n capture gives a 6.25 MeV gamma ray. The flux of numu + nutau is 3.41 +-.45 +-.48 10^6 cm^-2 s^-1; 5.3 sigma above 0. Nothing tells us that they're from the sun, but agreement with the model is very good.

*** It's looking like the LMA

Fitting the new SNO data through May 2001, with their NC and updated CC fluxes (5.09 and 1.76 10^6 cm^-2 s^-1), and other solar neutrino data, the LMA is favored (Large Mixing Angle Solution). LOW is ruled out at 90% CL, though allowed at 99.7%. SMA is ruled out at more than 99.7% confidence level. More NC data since the salt was added is analyzed blind for now.

*** Hence, HLMA

KamLAND will probe the LMA with nuebar's from nuclear reactors. If however the Delta M^2 > 10^-4 eV^2 (named the HLMA region), it will only observe a rate suppression but no spectral distortion and hence it will not measure the mixing parameters. Schoenert et al. propose a new medium baseline reactor experiment HLMA located at Heilbronn (Germany) to pin down the precise value of the solar mixing parameters. hep-ex/0203013

*** National Research Council backs underground Lab

The NRC committee stressed the need for a deep laboratory in a report presented at the APS Albuquerque meeting. The chairman, Michael Turner, noted that such a laboratory would allow the US to leap to the head of international efforts in underground science. The report calls for the NSF & DOE to cooperate in financing experiments the laboratory would house. http://www.eurekalert.org/pub_releases/2002-04/uow-nrc042202.php

*** Two Senate candidates on same side of the neutrino issue

Both Senator Tim Johnson (D-South Dakota) and Rep. John Thune (R-South Dakota) are seeking voter recognition for their efforts to locate the National Underground Physics Lab in SD. Barrick Corporation, the new owner of the mine, remains engaged, and is keeping the mine dry. It has also rehired some workers to do further cleanup in the underground areas. Despite the lack of an agreement for land transfer, there is some reason for continued optimism. http://www.aip.org/enews/fyi/2002/042.html

*** How many neutrinos in your collection?

The FY 2003 President's NSF budget includes $2 million for "research on neutrino collectors." There was $10M in 2002 funding for the "State of South Dakota to maintain the physical integrity of the Homestake Mine in preparation for the potential development of a major research facility on that site."

*** MINOS collects first neutrino

It's not from Fermilab. (The beam isn't quite done yet), but the growing MINOS detector saw it's first atmospheric neutrino generated upward muon on March 26th. Installation (through April 28th) is now over 194 planes. This is 2.2 kiloton, (1.6 fiducial) and MINOS is 40.1% complete. It is reading out 192 planes, or 6 crates.
